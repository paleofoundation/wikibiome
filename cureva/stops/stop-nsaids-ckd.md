# STOP: NSAIDs in Chronic Kidney Disease

## The Conventional Approach

CKD patients take NSAIDs for pain and inflammation, often without awareness of the triple harm mechanism.

## Why This Is Counterproductive

NSAIDs damage CKD patients through three convergent pathways:

1. **Direct nephrotoxicity**: COX inhibition reduces prostaglandin-mediated renal vasodilation, acutely lowering GFR. Renal failure observed in 1.3% of NSAID adverse drug reactions.
2. **Dysbiosis amplification**: NSAIDs reduce protective Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium while increasing Gram-negative bacteria, worsening the saccharolytic → proteolytic shift already present in CKD.
3. **Beta-glucuronidase reactivation**: Gut bacteria deconjugate NSAID glucuronides via beta-glucuronidase, reactivating them in the intestinal lumen. This causes local mucosal damage and increases gut barrier permeability — amplifying the endotoxemia that drives CKD inflammation.

## Alternative

Acetaminophen with renal dose adjustment. Anti-inflammatory dietary approaches (omega-3, polyphenols). Topical NSAIDs for localized pain.
