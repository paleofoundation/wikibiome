## Mechanism

**Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT)** delivers 100% oxygen at pressures above atmospheric, dramatically increasing tissue oxygen tension. This disrupts the **hypoxic niche** that obligate anaerobic pathobionts depend on for survival and biofilm maintenance. Elevated oxygen also enhances neutrophil bactericidal activity and promotes tissue repair.

## Rationale

The endometriosis ecosystem is characterized by localized hypoxia that favors obligate anaerobes and biofilm-forming pathobionts. HBOT directly targets the **oxygen state as ecological determinant** (Primitive 9) — rather than attacking specific organisms, it changes the environmental condition that enables their dominance. Restoring aerobic conditions shifts the competitive landscape in favor of facultative aerobes and the host immune response.

## Evidence Status

- **Animal model**: HBOT reduces endometriotic implant volume and peritoneal adhesions in rat models.
- **Case series**: Limited reports of symptom improvement (pain, inflammation) in endometriosis patients receiving HBOT as adjunctive therapy.
- **Clinical**: No RCTs specific to endometriosis-HBOT.

## Open Questions

- Optimal HBOT protocol (pressure, duration, frequency) for endometriosis.
- Whether HBOT effects on anaerobic pathobionts are sustained post-treatment.
- Interaction with hormonal therapies and surgical management.
- Risk of oxidative stress in repeated HBOT sessions.
