# Liu et al. 2018 -- Gut Microbiome and Rosuvastatin Lipid-Lowering Effect

## Key Claims

- [[Rosuvastatin]] therapy had different blood lipid-lowering effects on [[hyperlipidemia]] patients, and the [[gut microbiome]] exhibited variation in community composition, diversity, and taxa in association with treatment efficacy
- Patients with high rosuvastatin efficacy (Group I: lipids normalized by week 4) showed significantly different Shannon diversity (p<0.05) and distinct microbial community composition compared to low-efficacy patients (Group II: lipids not normalized by 8 weeks)
- Group I (high efficacy) was dominated by *Bacteroides* (16.93%), *Prevotella_9* (15.07%), *Dialister* (6.70%), *Pseudobutyrivibrio* (3.98%), and *Faecalibacterium* (3.32%)
- Group II (low efficacy) was dominated by *Bacteroides* (21.35%), *Prevotella_9* (16.53%), *Dialister* (4.42%), *Pseudobutyrivibrio* (3.12%), and *Faecalibacterium* (2.99%)
- Phyla [[Firmicutes]], *Fusobacteria* were negatively correlated with LDL-C level, while *Cyanobacteria* and *Lentisphaerae* had positive correlation with LDL-C

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Clinical trial (ChiCTR-ORC-17013212)
- **Population**: 64 patients with primary hyperlipidemia (55-75 years, 45 men, 19 women) at First Affiliated Hospital of Dalian Medical University, China
- **Intervention**: Rosuvastatin 10 mg/day for 4-8 weeks
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA gene sequencing (V3-V4 regions); Illumina HiSeq; QIIME analysis; LEfSe for differential abundance
- **Lipid results**:
  - Group I: TC -41.9%, LDL-C -58.5%, both normalized
  - Group II: TC -26.6%, LDL-C -26.6%, not reaching normal levels
  - TG decline was not significant; HDL elevated slightly in both groups
- **Microbiome findings**:
  - At phylum level: Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes most abundant (54.71% and 45.27% in Group I; 38.38% and 44.75% in Group II)
  - 42 differentially abundant OTUs identified by LEfSe (LDA score >3.0): 29 enriched in Group I, 13 in Group II
  - Gender and age influenced gut microbiome distribution: females in Group I had higher Firmicutes, Tenericutes, and Ruminococcaceae
  - Age 65-75: significantly more *Fusobacteria* and lower *Bacteroidaceae* and *Peptostreptococcaceae* in Group I
