# Key Claims

- The gut microbiome and sex hormones have a **bidirectional relationship**: sex hormones shape microbiome composition, and the microbiome regulates sex hormone levels via metabolites and enzymatic activity
- Bacterial beta-glucuronidase catalyzes the deconjugation of estrogen from conjugated to deconjugated forms, affecting the amount of active estrogen in circulation -- a key mechanism for estrogen-driven diseases
- Sex differences in microbiome composition are well-documented: male mice show higher abundance of *Porphyromonadaceae*, *Vellionellaceae*, *Enterobacteriaceae*, *Lactobacillaceae*, and *Bacteroidaceae* compared to females
- Androgen supplementation can eliminate gut microbiome deviations between gonadectomy and sham mice, demonstrating hormone-dependent microbiome regulation
- Post-menopausal women's microbiota does not differ from male microbiota, supporting the role of estrogen levels in maintaining sex-specific microbial diversity
- The gut microbiome influences ovarian cancer pathogenesis through estrogen receptor-mediated effects; *Enterococcus hirae* translocation from small intestine to lymphoid organs may contribute to cyclophosphamide efficacy
- In PCOS, *Bacteroides vulgaris* is significantly elevated, and the gut microbiome may contribute to hyperandrogenism and insulin resistance

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of bidirectional microbiome-hormone interactions
- **Diseases covered**: Ovarian cancer, postmenopausal osteoporosis (PMOP), polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), type 1 diabetes
- **Key table**: Summarizes cell, animal, and human studies on gut microbiome-sex hormone interactions with specific bacterial changes and mechanistic findings
- **Mechanisms**: Beta-glucuronidase pathway for estrogen metabolism; brain-gut axis; androgen-mediated immune protection in T1D (NOD mice); SCFAs and Treg cell regulation
- **PCOS findings**: High androgen levels alter gut microbiome; reduced microbial diversity in PCOS; FMT from PCOS women to mice transferred metabolic phenotype
