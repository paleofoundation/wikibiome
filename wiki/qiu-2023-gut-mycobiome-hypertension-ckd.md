# Key Claims

- HTN+CKD patients displayed distinctive fungal composition with increased richness and diversity compared to both HTN-only and healthy controls, while HTN-only patients showed minimal fungal differences
- In HTN+CKD patients, *Apiotrichum*, *Cystobasidium*, and *Saccharomyces* were increased, while *Bjerkandera*, *Candida*, and *Meyerozyma* were decreased compared to controls
- 13 fungal genera showed significantly different abundances between HTN+CKD and control groups; *Candida* showed negative associations with GRO-alpha, LIF, and TNF-alpha cytokines
- *Saccharomyces* displayed positive correlations with multiple pro-inflammatory cytokines (IFN-gamma, IL4, LIF, SCF, SDF1-alpha), suggesting immune-modulatory roles
- *Apiotrichum*, *Saccharomyces*, and *Septoria* exhibited positive associations with eGFR, linking fungal genera to renal function
- *Septoria* was positively correlated with systolic and diastolic blood pressure; *Nakaseomyces* and *Saccharomyces* were positively associated with diastolic blood pressure
- The HTN+CKD group had significantly higher concentrations of IL2R-alpha, IL18, and TNF-alpha, and lower IL9 and TNF-beta compared to controls

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional case-control study with ITS sequencing
- **Population**: 150 participants -- 50 healthy controls, 50 HTN-only, 50 HTN+CKD (age-matched, 50% male each)
- **Methods**: Fungal DNA extraction (DNeasy PowerSoil Pro); ITS1F/ITS2 primers; Novaseq 6000 sequencing; QIIME V1.9.1; Vsearch for OTU classification (UNITE 12_11 database)
- **Statistical analyses**: PCoA, PERMANOVA, Pearson correlations, linear regression; Bio-Plex cytokine panel (48-plex)
- **Dominant phyla**: Ascomycota (50-60%) and Basidiomycota (31-44%) across all groups
