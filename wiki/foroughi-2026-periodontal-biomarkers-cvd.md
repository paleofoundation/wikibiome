# Key Claims

- Periodontal disease is independently associated with increased risks of coronary heart disease, stroke, and atherosclerosis, beyond shared traditional CVD risk factors
- Periodontal pathogens (*P. gingivalis*, *A. actinomycetemcomitans*) and host immune responses promote systemic [[inflammation]], endothelial dysfunction, and atherogenesis through multiple mechanisms
- Key biomarker categories for the periodontal-CVD axis: (A) local oral biomarkers (IL-1-beta, IL-17, TNF-alpha, MMP-8/aMMP-8, 8-OHdG), and (B) systemic biomarkers (IL-6, CRP/hs-CRP, fibrinogen, serum amyloid A, MPO, MDA, Lp-PLA2, ICAM-1, VCAM-1, *P. gingivalis* antibodies, bacterial DNA in plaques, HSP60, eNOS surrogates)
- Bacterial DNA (including *P. gingivalis*) can be found within human atherosclerotic plaques, directly implicating oral microbial involvement in plaque development
- Molecular mimicry between bacterial heat-shock proteins (HSP60) and host endothelial HSPs elicits autoimmune-like cross-reactivity, contributing to vascular injury
- Periodontal therapy has been associated with reductions in systemic inflammatory markers and improvements in endothelial function, although large randomized trials remain limited
- Comprehensive biomarker table catalogs local and systemic markers with their biological significance, detection methods, and sample matrices (GCF, saliva, serum, plasma, arterial plaque)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review of studies published 1990-2025 (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science)
- **Inclusion**: Clinical, epidemiological, experimental, reviews, and meta-analyses examining periodontal biomarkers and CVD
- **128 studies** initially identified; 74 articles met inclusion criteria for final synthesis
- **Table 1**: Comprehensive catalog of 20+ local and systemic biomarkers with detection methods, sample matrices, biological significance
- **Key mechanisms**: Systemic inflammation, bacteremia/endothelial dysfunction, immune cross-reactivity/molecular mimicry, oxidative stress/lipid peroxidation, matrix metalloproteinase plaque destabilization
