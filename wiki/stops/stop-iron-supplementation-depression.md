conventional_rationale: "Low serum iron and low hemoglobin are commonly documented in MDD. Iron is a cofactor for tryptophan hydroxylase (the enzyme that converts tryptophan to serotonin), creating a plausible rationale for iron supplementation to support neurotransmitter synthesis."
why_counterproductive: "The low serum iron in MDD reflects active nutritional immunity — hepcidin-mediated iron sequestration in response to Enterobacteriaceae enrichment — not a deficiency. Iron supplementation: (1) feeds the Enterobacteriaceae already elevated in MDD dysbiosis; (2) increases LPS output, which upregulates IDO (indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase) and diverts tryptophan away from serotonin toward the inflammatory kynurenine pathway — the opposite of the intended effect; (3) worsens neuroinflammation via the gut-brain axis; (4) undermines the very neurotransmitter synthesis the clinician is trying to support."
