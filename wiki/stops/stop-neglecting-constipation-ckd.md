why_counterproductive: "Severe constipation quadruples ESRD risk (HR 4.41, 95% CI 3.61-5.39) with a clear dose-response. Prolonged intestinal transit increases contact time between gut bacteria and substrates, amplifying uremic toxin (IS, PCS, TMAO) production and absorption. Many CKD medications actively cause constipation — iron supplements, calcium-based phosphate binders, opioids for CKD-associated pain, anticholinergics — creating an iatrogenic vicious cycle."

alternative: "Active constipation management as a core CKD care component: (1) dietary fiber supplementation (low-potassium sources in advanced CKD); (2) adequate hydration within fluid restrictions; (3) review and minimize constipation-causing medications (switch iron supplements to lactoferrin, use non-calcium phosphate binders); (4) physical activity; (5) osmotic laxatives if needed. Consider constipation severity as a CKD progression risk factor worthy of monitoring."
