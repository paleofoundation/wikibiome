conventional_rationale: "Anemia is common in older adults with Alzheimer's disease; low serum iron is interpreted as iron deficiency requiring repletion"
why_counterproductive: "Brain iron accumulation is a hallmark feature of Alzheimer's pathology — elevated iron is found in amyloid plaques, tau tangles, and affected neurons. Serum iron may appear low due to hepcidin-mediated nutritional immunity sequestering iron away from systemic circulation as a host defense response, not true deficiency. Oral iron supplementation increases gut iron availability, feeding siderophore-producing pathobionts enriched in the AD dysbiosis (E. coli, H. pylori), and may increase iron delivery to the brain through neuroinflammation-associated disruption of the blood-brain barrier."
