In children, 56.2% on PPI+placebo developed gut dysbiosis vs. 6.2% on PPI+probiotics. PPIs promote Candida gastric colonization (96.9% detection rate). PPIs increase risk of C. difficile, Campylobacter, Shigella, and Salmonella infections. PPIs do NOT change esophageal microbial composition — they reduce inflammation but leave the Type II (Gram-negative) dysbiosis intact. 54.1% of long-term PPI users still have persistent symptoms."

## Overview

**Harm potential: GREEN** — PPIs remain appropriate first-line therapy; this STOP recommends co-administration of probiotics, not PPI avoidance.
