conventional_rationale: "CKD patients develop hyperphosphatemia requiring phosphate binders. Iron-based binders (SFO) are preferred for lower pill burden and calcium-sparing properties."
why_counterproductive: "SFO notably enriches iron-tolerant species (e.g., Streptococcus salivarius) and elevates serum indoxyl sulfate and p-cresyl sulfate despite minimal overall microbiome diversity changes. Iron availability feeds siderophore-producing Enterobacteriaceae — already enriched in CKD and causally linked to disease progression. This paradoxically worsens the gut-derived uremic toxin burden that drives CKD progression."
