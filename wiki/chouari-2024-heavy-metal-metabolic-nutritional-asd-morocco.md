# Chouari et al. 2024 -- Heavy Metal Intoxication and Metabolic/Nutritional Profiles in ASD Children (Morocco)

## Key Claims

- 95% of the 20 Moroccan ASD children studied showed heavy metal intoxication (As, Pb, Cr, Cd) in hair analysis
- 100% showed intestinal dysbiosis and 100% showed disruption of mitochondrial activity, establishing near-universal co-occurrence
- Heavy metal intoxication was significantly associated with intestinal dysbiosis (chi-square p = 0.003), neurotransmitter production disruption (p = 0.041), mitochondrial dysfunction (p = 0.017), and oxalate excess (p = 0.001)
- 70% of children showed reduced production of neurotransmitters like dopamine and noradrenaline due to nutritional deficiencies of phenylalanine and tyrosine
- Competition between toxic metals and essential metals like [[zinc]] produces cellular pathologies including [[oxidative-stress]], lipid peroxidation, and axonal demyelination

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Exploratory cross-sectional study
- **Population**: 20 children with ASD (80% boys, 20% girls, ages 2-9) from 9 urban regions in Morocco
- **Methods**: Hair analysis for toxic/essential metals; stool microbiology; urine organic acids; nearly 100 biological parameters assessed; chi-square statistical testing
- **Data collection**: September 2019 to July 2023
- **Key findings**:
  - Heavy metal intoxication: 95% of cases (As, Pb, Cr, Cd detected)
  - Intestinal dysbiosis: 100% of cases -- gut microbiota dysregulation universal
  - Elevated oxalate levels: 80% of cases -- linked to oxidative stress and inflammation
  - Mitochondrial activity disruption: 100% of cases
  - Neurotransmitter production deficit: 70% of cases (dopamine, noradrenaline)
- **Statistical associations (chi-square)**:
  - Heavy metal intoxication <-> dysbiosis: Cramer's V = 1.000, p = 0.003
  - Heavy metal intoxication <-> neurotransmitter production: Cramer's V = 0.717, p = 0.041
  - Heavy metal intoxication <-> mitochondrial dysfunction: p = 0.017
  - Heavy metal intoxication <-> oxalate excess: Cramer's V = 1.000, p = 0.001
- **Severity distribution**: 45% severe, 35% moderate, 20% mild ASD
- **Limitations**: Small sample size (n = 20), exploratory/descriptive design, no control group
