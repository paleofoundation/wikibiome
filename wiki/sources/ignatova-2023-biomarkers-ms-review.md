# Biomarkers in Multiple Sclerosis: Analysis of the Present Advantages and Look to the Future

## Key Claims

- No single biomarker is sufficiently specific for MS diagnosis, disease activity, or treatment response; a multi-biomarker approach combining clinical, imaging, and molecular markers is needed
- Gut microbiome-derived [[short-chain-fatty-acids|SCFAs]] and immune dysfunction are linked: relationship between gut microbiome-derived SCFAs and immune dysfunction in early MS was proposed by Trend et al. (2021)
- [[Tryptophan]] metabolism is a promising biomarker axis: RRMS shows specific urinary Trp metabolic signature with lower kynurenine and lower K/T ratio; K/T negatively correlates with EDSS disability
- Gut microbiome composition could be a potential biomarker: *Bacteroides* enrichment correlates with MS disease activity, and *Prevotella histocola* treatment is as efficacious as interferon beta and glatiramer acetate in EAE
- Neurofilament light chain (NfL) in serum/CSF is the most promising molecular biomarker: correlates with disease activity, treatment response, and brain atrophy; high NFL is an independent risk factor for CIS-to-MS conversion
- CHI3L1 (chitinase-3-like-1 or YKL-40) levels > 189 ng/L in CSF predict faster disease conversion from CIS to MS
- EBV infection contributes to B-cell dysfunction leading to aberrant B-cell responses that guide pathogenic T-cell responses in CNS
- Metabolomics and lipidomics approaches on urine reveal altered sphingolipid profiles in MS
- Circulating miRNAs (17-25 nucleotides) are relatively stable biomarker candidates found in CSF, serum, and PBMCs

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review (2023) consolidating MS biomarker landscape
- **Biomarker categories**: Routine (MRI, CSF analysis), molecular (NfL, GFAP, chitinases, cytokines), genetic (HLA alleles), metabolic (tryptophan metabolites, SCFAs), transcriptomic (miRNAs), immunologic (Tregs, B cells)
- **Key validated biomarkers**: Anti-EBNA antibodies (predictive), KFLC (diagnostic), IgG oligoclonal bands (diagnostic), MMP9 (disease activity), anti-AQP4 (diagnostic), anti-JC virus (natalizumab risk)
- **Gut microbiome connections**: Alpha diversity inversely correlated with MS; *Bacteroides* associated with disease activity; probiotic and dietary interventions modify disease progression
- **Emerging approaches**: Transcriptomics for drug repositioning; metabolomics lipid signatures; combined multi-omic panels
- **Limitations**: Most biomarkers are exploratory; lack reproducibility across cohorts; heterogeneity in patient selection criteria
