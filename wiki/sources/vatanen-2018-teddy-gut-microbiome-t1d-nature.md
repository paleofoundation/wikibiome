## Key Findings

- **SCFA producers depleted in T1D progressors**: Children who developed islet autoimmunity and progressed to T1D had significantly fewer short-chain fatty acid-producing bacteria, including **[[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]]**, **[[roseburia]]**, and Eubacterium rectale (Vatanen et al. 2018, prospective cohort, n=783)
- **LPS biosynthesis genes enriched**: Metagenomic analysis revealed increased lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis gene pathways in T1D cases, suggesting a pro-inflammatory Gram-negative shift
- **Bifidobacterium vulnerability**: **[[bifidobacterium]]** abundance was particularly sensitive to antibiotic exposure, and its depletion correlated with autoimmunity progression
- **Geographic variation**: Finnish and Estonian children (higher T1D incidence) had distinct microbiome trajectories compared to Russian children (lower incidence), with Bacteroides dominance in the high-incidence populations
