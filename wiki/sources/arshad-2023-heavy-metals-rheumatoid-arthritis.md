# Key Claims

- Serum [[copper]] concentration is significantly lower in RA patients compared to controls (p = 0.04)
- No significant differences found for [[zinc]], [[lead]], [[cobalt]], or [[nickel]] between RA patients and controls
- No significant relationship between heavy metal concentrations and sex or age groups in RA patients
- RA females had higher concentrations of Cu, Pb, Co, and Ni compared to RA males, while Zn was higher in males (non-significant)

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Case-control study comparing RA-positive patients with healthy controls in Sargodha, Pakistan
- **Sample**: Blood samples collected from diagnostic laboratories; serum analyzed by atomic absorption spectrophotometry (AAS)
- **Metals studied**: Cu, Zn, Pb, Co, Ni
- **Age groups**: Below 35 years and 35+ years
- **Key result**: Only Cu showed statistically significant difference (lower in RA; t=2.09, P=0.04, df=97)
- **Non-significant**: Zn (p=0.22), Pb (p=0.85), Co (p=0.82), Ni (p=0.07)
- Two-sample t-test used; Minitab Version 15; significance threshold p < 0.05
