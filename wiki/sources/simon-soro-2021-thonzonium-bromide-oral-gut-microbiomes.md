# Key Claims

- Repurposed FDA-approved drug thonzonium bromide (TB), used topically for dental caries, causes niche-specific microbial disturbances in oral sites but does not globally affect the fecal/gut microbiome
- TB treatment specifically disrupted oral Rothia and Veillonella without cytotoxic effects on host tissues, while increasing gut taxon Sutterella in the gut microbiota
- The oral-gut microbiome rodent model recapitulates human body site microbiome differences, providing a feasible system for assessing drug repurposing impacts on microbiota
- Machine learning and nestedness analyses identified specific oral-gut microbial interactions that were altered by TB treatment, demonstrating that topical oral treatments can influence distant gut microbiota
- TB-containing nanoparticle carriers (NPC.TB) enhanced drug delivery while maintaining antimicrobial efficacy at the oral site

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original research, rodent model (Sprague Dawley rats)
- **Experimental design**: 48 rats, 4 groups (PBS, NPC, TB, NPC.TB), topical oral treatment 2x/day for 3 weeks; 16S rRNA sequencing of oral swabs, dental plaque, and feces
- **Body site differences**: Composition differed substantially between fecal, oral swab, and dental plaque samples (PERMANOVA P = 0.001); fecal samples dominated by Bacteroides, Bacteroidales S24-7, Sutterella
- **TB effects on oral sites**: Decreased Rothia in oral swabs; reduced Veillonella in dental plaque; caries reduction on teeth
- **Gut microbiome**: Global composition unaffected by TB; however, nestedness analysis showed increased sharing of specific oral bacteria (especially Sutterella ASVs) between oral and gut sites
- **Linear discriminant analysis**: TB-containing groups discriminable from non-TB groups in oral sites (P < 0.001) but not in feces (P > 0.05)
- **NPC characteristics**: 13.2 nm size, 18.8 mV charge, PDI 1.91; corona:core ratio 3.7
- **Host tissue safety**: Histological analysis of soft oral and small/large intestine tissues showed no adverse effects
