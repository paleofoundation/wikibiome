# Assessment of Exposure to Nickel Intake with Selected Cereal Grains and Cereal-Based Products

## Key Claims

- Cereal grains and cereal-based products are significant contributors to dietary nickel exposure, as recognized by EFSA
- Mean nickel content in analyzed cereal grain samples was 1.16 mg/kg; mean in cereal-based products was 0.66 mg/kg (95th percentile 1.93 mg/kg)
- Highest nickel levels found in bran, groats, and flakes compared to other grain-based products (MB concentration 0.61 mg/kg, P95 1.84 mg/kg)
- Roasted buckwheat products had the highest nickel content at 2.53 mg/kg; barley groats and oat flakes at 1.81 mg/kg
- Lower nickel content observed in barley groats, pasta, and flour
- Estimated average exposure of adults and children to nickel from grains/cereal products ranged from 1.1% to 13.4% of TDI
- The nickel content does not pose a health risk at population level, though individual high-consumption samples showed significant contamination

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Analysis of 56 samples of cereal grains and cereal products from the Polish market (2019-2020)
- **Analytical method**: Microwave mineralization followed by graphite furnace atomic absorption spectrometry (GFAAS) using VARIAN SpectrAA 880Z
- **Reference standard**: SRM 1515 (Apple leaves, NIST); validation LOD 0.05 ug/L, LOQ 1.25 ug/L
- **TDI comparison**: Used EFSA TDI of 13 ug/kg body weight/day (updated 2020) and earlier 2.8 ug/kg bw/day value
- **Product categories**: 11 samples of pasta, 13 flour, 10 groats, 5 flakes, 5 bran; grain group included wheat, rye, barley, millet
- **Millet**: Highest nickel content among grains at 4.80 mg/kg
- **Rye**: Lowest among grains at 0.10 mg/kg
- **Left-censored data**: 27% of results below LOQ, managed per EFSA lower-bound/middle-bound/upper-bound methodology
- Exposure for children from cereals estimated at up to 13.4% TDI; adults up to 13.4% TDI -- does not exceed thresholds individually but contributes to cumulative dietary load
