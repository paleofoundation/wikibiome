# Key Claims

- **Elevated levels of serum anti-tubulin, CaM kinase II, anti-dopamine receptor D1, and SpCO** found in majority of ASD subjects
- **ASD severity is correlated with SpCO** at baseline (R=0.38, p=0.029)
- **Anti-lysoganglioside GM1** (R=0.83, p=0.022), anti-tubulin (R=0.69, p=0.042), and anti-D1 (R=0.71, p=0.045) correlated with SpCO in the treatment group
- **Carboxyhemoglobin (SpCO)** is a non-invasive measurement of inflammation not previously studied in ASD
- **Daily Lactobacillus plantarum** probiotic was administered for 16 weeks in the parent RCT

# Methods & Findings

**Post-hoc analysis** of a previously published RCT of L. plantarum in 35 ASD individuals aged 3-20 years.

- Randomly assigned to L. plantarum probiotic (6 x 10^10 CFUs) or placebo for 16 weeks
- Outcomes included SRS, ABC-2, CGI, SpCO by CO-oximetry, fecal microbiome by 16S rRNA, blood serum inflammatory markers, autoantibodies, and oxytocin
- Kendall's correlation used to examine interrelationships
- SpCO correlated with multiple autoimmune markers and ASD severity
- Suggests CO as potential biomarker for inflammation-mediated ASD subtype

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **SpCO as non-invasive inflammation biomarker** for ASD severity assessment
- **Autoimmune markers** (anti-tubulin, anti-ganglioside, anti-dopamine receptor) elevated in ASD and correlated with CO
- **L. plantarum probiotic** effects on inflammation-autoimmunity axis in ASD
