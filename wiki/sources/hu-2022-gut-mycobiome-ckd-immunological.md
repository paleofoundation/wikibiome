## Key Findings

- **CKD gut mycobiome differed significantly** from healthy controls, establishing that fungal dysbiosis accompanies bacterial dysbiosis in kidney disease.
- **[[candida-albicans]] was depleted** in CKD — a surprising finding given Candida's typical enrichment in other dysbiotic states. This may reflect the unique metabolic environment of CKD (uremic toxins, altered pH).
- **[[saccharomyces-cerevisiae]] was enriched** in CKD and may play a protective or compensatory role.
- **CRP was significantly elevated** in CKD (4.92 vs 2.53 mg/L, P=0.001), confirming chronic systemic inflammation.
- **Free light chain (FLC) kappa was dramatically elevated** in CKD, suggesting B-cell hyperactivation potentially driven by fungal antigen exposure.
- **Ganoderma** was among the differentially abundant fungi, though its clinical significance in CKD remains unclear.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

One of few studies characterizing the gut mycobiome in CKD. Supports the [[candida-albicans]] entity page with a counterintuitive depletion finding that contrasts with other conditions. The Saccharomyces enrichment is relevant to probiotic strain selection. The FLC kappa finding connects fungal dysbiosis to immune dysregulation, relevant to interkingdom relationship concepts (Primitive 6).
