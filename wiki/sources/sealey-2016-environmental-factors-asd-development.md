# Sealey et al. 2016 -- Environmental Factors in the Development of ASD

## Key Claims

- Environmental factors play a significant contribution to ASD etiology in addition to genetic factors; comprehensive literature search implicates pesticides, phthalates, PCBs, solvents, air pollutants, fragrances, glyphosate, and heavy metals
- Most of the common synthetic chemicals that humans are exposed to (fragrances, cosmetics, food additives) have not been evolutionarily tested and may contribute to ASD
- [[mercury]] exposure during childhood is associated with neurophysiological abnormalities in language, attention, and memory; thimerosal (ethyl mercury) in vaccines has been investigated but studies found no credible evidence linking vaccines to autism
- Maternal antibodies against fetal brain proteins (LDH, YBX1, cypin, STIP1, CRMP1, CRMP2) can adversely affect fetal neurodevelopment, with over 50% of ASD mothers showing reactivity
- Gender bias toward males may be partially explained by differences in myelination cell responses to environmental toxicants

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Comprehensive review (11 pages)
- **Scope**: Broad survey of environmental factors including neurotoxins, vaccines, gender bias, genetic-environmental interactions, glyphosate, and maternal antibodies
- **Heavy metal/neurotoxin coverage**:
  - Arsenic, manganese, organophosphate insecticides, DDT, ethyl alcohol listed as established neurotoxins
  - Mercury: Methylmercury exposure from seafood/environment linked to neurodevelopmental harm; thimerosal removed from vaccines, no evidence linking vaccines to ASD
  - Aluminum: Used as vaccine adjuvant; concerns raised but evidence for ASD link remains speculative
- **Fragrances and cosmetics**: Popular fragrances contain average of 14 secret chemicals; many with potential endocrine-disrupting properties
- **Glyphosate**: Correlation between glyphosate use on crops and autism prevalence noted (ecological data only); proposed mechanism via disruption of serotonin pathway and gut microbiota
- **Maternal antibodies**: 10% of pregnant women can recognize certain antigens in developing fetal brain cells; anti-brain antibodies may explain a subset of ASD cases
- **Vaccine section**: Thoroughly reviews evidence and concludes no credible link between vaccination (including MMR) and autism
- **MZ twin concordance**: Despite genetic identity, discordance in MZ twins demonstrates environmental contribution
