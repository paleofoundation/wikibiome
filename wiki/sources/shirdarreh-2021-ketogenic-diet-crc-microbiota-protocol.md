# Shirdarreh 2021 -- KD Impact on CRC Progression and Gut Microbiota Co-evolution Protocol

## Key Claims

- KD may alter overall species diversity in CRC through either elimination of harmful bacterial genera or perturbation of beneficial gut microbiota
- The protocol hypothesizes that mice fed KD will show altered microbiota diversity, decreased cytokine production, and fewer/smaller polyps compared to normal diet
- *Fusobacterium* (Fusobacteriaceae) and *Porphyromonas* (Porphyromonadaceae) are highly enriched in CRC intestinal microbiome profiles, and hydrogen sulfide production by *Fusobacterium* is a likely contributing factor to CRC development
- KD (64% carbohydrates replaced with fat, 5% carbohydrates, 8% protein, 87% fat) expected to decrease harmful bacteria abundance in CRC

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Research protocol using ApcMin/+ (Multiple Intestinal Neoplasia) CRC mouse model on C57BL/6J background
- **Methods**: Normal chow vs. KD for 8 weeks; 16S rRNA sequencing of V4-V5 hypervariable regions; qPCR for Th17 cytokine gene expression (IL-17A, IL-17F, IL-21, IL-22); alpha and beta diversity assessment
- **Endpoints**: Polyp size and number in small intestine and colon; cytokine levels; microbiome composition changes at weeks 0, 2, 4, 6, 8
- **Limitations noted**: Mouse microbial community differs from human; 16S rRNA has amplification biases and low resolution
