# Microbiome-Derived Metabolites as Therapeutic Targets in Autoimmune Diseases

## Key Claims

- Key microbiome-derived metabolites with immunomodulatory roles in autoimmune diseases include: [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] (acetate, propionate, butyrate), [[tryptophan]]/[[kynurenine]] catabolites, secondary [[bile-acids]], and [[polyamines]] (spermidine, putrescine)
- Butyrate is a histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitor that enhances [[Foxp3]] expression and promotes [[Treg]] differentiation; propionate promotes intestinal Tregs via FXR and TGR5 receptor pathways
- In MS specifically, patients display decreased levels of SCFAs and tryptophan-derived metabolites; SCFA supplementation and [[aryl-hydrocarbon-receptor]] (AhR) activation have been proposed as strategies to restore immune tolerance
- Five therapeutic strategies are proposed: (1) probiotics/prebiotics for SCFA-producing strain supplementation, (2) direct metabolite supplementation (butyrate, indole-3-aldehyde, bile acid derivatives), (3) enzyme modulation (tryptophanases, bile salt hydrolases), (4) synthetic biology (engineered microbes for in situ metabolite production), (5) systems biology integration of metagenomics and metabolomics
- Polyamines (spermidine) enhance autophagy and have anti-inflammatory properties in colitis and lupus models, representing an underexplored therapeutic avenue

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Brief review from Cheikh Anta Diop University, Dakar, Senegal
- **Metabolite classes covered**: SCFAs (microbial fermentation products), tryptophan catabolites (indoles and kynurenines via AhR), bile acids (primary to secondary conversion by gut bacteria), polyamines (spermidine/putrescine from host and microbial synthesis)
- **MS-specific evidence**: Decreased SCFAs and tryptophan-derived metabolites; AhR activation by Lactobacillus-derived indole derivatives alleviates EAE symptoms (Rothhammer et al., 2016); SCFA supplementation protective in EAE (Trompette et al., 2014)
- **Translational challenges**: Inter-individual microbiome variability; metabolite instability and rapid absorption limit direct supplementation; difficulty determining causation vs correlation
