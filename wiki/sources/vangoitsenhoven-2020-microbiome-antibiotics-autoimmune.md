# Key Claims

- The global rise in autoimmune disease incidence has paralleled widespread antibiotic use, and antibiotic-induced [[dysbiosis]] may facilitate autoimmunity through gut microbiota disruption
- IBD dysbiosis is characterized by decreased Bacteroidetes and Firmicutes, increased Proteobacteria and Bacilli, with reduced [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] as a hallmark finding
- Early-life antibiotic exposure is causally related to IBD development -- each antibiotic course increased IBD hazard by 6%, with decreased effect at older age of exposure
- [[probiotics]] (VSL#3, Lactobacilli) and FMT show promise for IBD and other autoimmune diseases, but clinical evidence remains limited
- [[bacteroides-fragilis]] and Bacteroides species play crucial roles in immune regulation through Th1/Th17 modulation and Treg induction

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Invited review covering IBD and type 1 diabetes as key autoimmune disease examples
- **IBD microbiome changes** (Table 2): Decreased Akkermansia, Bacteroides, Bifidobacterium, Clostridium groups IV/XIVA, F. prausnitzii, Lactobacillus, Prevotella, Ruminococcus, Roseburia; increased Proteobacteria
- **Antibiotics and IBD**: UK cohort (>1 million children, 1994-2009) -- antibiotic prescriptions associated with IBD development; relationship decreased with increasing age; beta-lactam, fluoroquinolone, metronidazole, rifaximin effective in CD but not UC
- **Antibiotic/probiotic/FMT summary** (Table 3): Comprehensive matrix of evidence across IBD, T1D, RA, MS for each intervention type
- **Probiotics mechanisms**: Competitive exclusion, antimicrobial substance production, epithelial barrier improvement, mucosal adhesion enhancement, immunomodulation (Treg induction, IL-10/TGF-beta promotion)
- **FMT in IBD**: More effective in CD than UC; reduced disease activity; clinical trials ongoing; variable outcomes due to donor selection, preparation methods, route of administration
- **Microbiome restoration**: Ciprofloxacin effects persist >2 years post-exposure; clindamycin effects persist up to 2 years; full restoration may take months to years
