# Key Claims

- 40% of hidradenitis suppurativa (HS) patients harbor a gut microbiota configuration similar to CD, characterized by pathogenic genera enrichment and depletion of beneficial genera
- Both HS and CD patients consumed a Western diet similar to controls, but habitual diet in HS and CD was significantly different from controls
- HS patients with CD-like microbiota showed enrichment of Enterococcus, Veillonella, and [[escherichia-coli|Escherichia/Shigella]], with depletion of [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii|Faecalibacterium]]
- Growth-arrest specific 6 (Gas6) inflammatory marker was significantly lower in HS patients with CD-like microbiota
- The fecal microbiota may help identify HS patients at greater risk for CD development

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional comparative study; HS (n=55), CD (n=102), controls (n=95)
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing + food frequency questionnaire; ANCOM-BC differential abundance analysis
- **Microbiota overlap**: HS and CD share a set of commonly enriched or depleted genera vs controls
- **Key depleted genera**: Faecalibacterium, Subdoligranulum, Romboutsia, Clostridium_sensu_stricto_1 in both HS and CD
- **Inflammatory markers**: Gas6, TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL-12 levels measured; Gas6 significantly lower in CD-microbiota HS group
- **Diet clusters**: Four dietary clusters identified; no significant dietary difference between HS patients with CD-like vs normal microbiota

# Connections

- Demonstrates shared [[dysbiosis]] patterns between [[crohns-disease]] and HS via the gut-skin axis
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] depletion is a common signature across both inflammatory conditions
- [[escherichia-coli]] enrichment at the expense of beneficial commensals drives [[inflammation]] in both diseases
- Antibiotics, commonly used in both conditions, co-vary with microbiota composition changes
- Diet-microbiome-[[inflammation]] axis relevant to [[gut-metal-microbiome]] through nutrient processing and metal chelation
