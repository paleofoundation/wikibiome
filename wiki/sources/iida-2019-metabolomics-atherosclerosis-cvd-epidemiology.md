# Application of Metabolomics to Epidemiological Studies of Atherosclerosis and Cardiovascular Disease

## Summary

Review article summarizing epidemiological metabolomics studies applied to atherosclerosis and CVD, including biomarker discovery, cardiovascular risk prediction, early disease diagnosis, and exploration of disease mechanisms. Covers both NMR and LC-MS platforms across major prospective cohort studies.

## Key Findings

### Metabolomic Profiling for CVD Prediction
- FINRISK study: phenylalanine, MUFA, omega-6 fatty acids, and DHA associated with future CVD events
- SABRE study: metabolite score from NMR (lipid fractions, glucose, valine, ornithine, glutamate, creatinine, glycoproteins, citrate, 1,5-anhydrosorbitol) predicted CHD incidence
- Four plasma lipid classes (lysophosphatidylcholine, phosphatidylcholine, sphingomyelin, triacylglycerides) significantly associated with incident CVD

### Risk Factor Profiling
- 24-hour urinary metabolomics (INTERMAP study): alanine, hippurate linked to blood pressure
- Urinary metabolites revealed nine metabolites associated with blood pressure across ethnic groups
- Alcohol consumption distinguishable by PLS-DA in metabolomic profiles (pipecolate, glutamine, ornithine, cysteine-glutathione disulfide)

### TMAO and Microbiome-Derived Metabolites
- TMAO associated with CVD risk in Shanghai Women's Health Study (OR 1.91 for highest vs lowest quartile)
- Effect highlighted importance of dietary choline, gut microbiota, and host interaction
- BCAAs associated with metabolic health and CVD independently

### Metabolic Profiling of Lifestyle Factors
- Tsuruoka Metabolomics Cohort: physical activity and dietary intake significantly associated with metabolomic profiles
- Higher physical activity associated with lower BCAAs and other amino acids
- 14 metabolites identified as discriminators between drinking categories

### Technical Challenges
- Cross-platform comparability remains difficult
- Semi-quantitative measurements in most high-throughput studies
- Consortium of Metabolomics Studies (COMETS) developed in 2014 to address standardization
