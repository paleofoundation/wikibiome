# Advantages and Limitations of Experimental Autoimmune Encephalomyelitis in Breaking Down the Role of the Gut Microbiome in Multiple Sclerosis

## Key Claims

- Multiple EAE models capture different MS aspects: MOG-induced in C57BL/6 (chronic progressive), PLP-induced in SJL (relapsing-remitting), MOG in NOD mice (relapsing progressive), germ-free models, TMEV viral models, cuprizone/lysolecithin demyelination models
- Antibiotic effects in EAE are timing-dependent: oral broad-spectrum antibiotics before EAE induction are protective (Treg accumulation), but intraperitoneal antibiotics during active disease can worsen outcomes; specific antibiotics (minocycline, tetracycline) showed some protective effects in human MS
- Probiotic results in EAE are inconsistent: *Lactobacillus* strains generally protective, but *L. reuteri* showed conflicting results (protective alone but worsened when co-colonized with novel Erysipelotrichaceae strain); PSA from *B. fragilis* induces Treg expansion and prevents EAE in IL-10-dependent manner
- Key translational limitations: rodent immune systems differ from human; EAE primarily involves spinal cord while MS affects brain; fecal sampling misses small intestinal microbiome where most nutrient absorption and immune interaction occurs; human microbiome confounded by diet, medications, and geography
- Comprehensive tables catalog: (1) antibiotic effects in EAE (12 studies: 8 protective, 3 detrimental, 1 mixed), (2) antibiotic effects in MS (5 studies), and human MS antibiotic epidemiology (mixed results)

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Review from University of Texas at Austin Dell Medical School
- **EAE models cataloged**: C57BL/6 (B6), SJL, PL/J, NOD, OSE (spontaneous), TCR transgenics, shiverer, connexin knockouts, TMEV-induced, cuprizone, lysolecithin
- **Key EAE findings**: GF mice do not develop EAE; reconstitution with MS patient fecal content restores disease susceptibility; segmented filamentous bacteria promote EAE via Th17 induction; *Prevotella histicola* treatment as efficacious as interferon beta and glatiramer acetate in EAE
- **Alcohol evidence**: Moderate alcohol may modulate EAE/MS through gut microbiome and immune effects
- **FMT evidence**: MS twin-to-GF mouse transfers increased spontaneous EAE with reduced IL-10 production; blocking IL-10 in recipients of healthy twin fecal transplants also increased EAE
