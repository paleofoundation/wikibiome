# Proinflammatory MAIT Cells React to Gut Flora Yeasts and Infiltrate MS Brain

## Key Claims

- MS patients have higher fungal abundance and richness in fecal samples compared to healthy donors (HD), with enrichment of *S. cerevisiae*, *S. kudriavzevii*, and *S. mikatae*
- MAIT (mucosal-associated invariant T) cells from pwMS are significantly more activated by *C. albicans* and *S. cerevisiae* extracts than those from HD, producing more IL-17, GM-CSF, and TNF-alpha (but equivalent IFN-gamma)
- MAIT cells (CD8+CD161+Va7.2+) infiltrate MS brain lesions: detected in 53% of post-mortem progressive MS brain tissues, localized to active and chronic active white matter lesions, perivascular spaces of postcapillary venules, and inflamed meninges
- MAIT cell activation by fungi is mediated by IL-23: monocytes stimulated with fungal extracts produce significantly more IL-23 in pwMS than HD, and IL-23 directly activates MAIT cells in vitro
- In NEDA (no evidence of disease activity) MS, MAIT cells are expanded in peripheral blood; in active disease (EDA), MAIT frequency drops dramatically (below HD levels), suggesting migration to CNS
- MAIT cells from pwMS produce CCL20, a chemokine that recruits CCR6+ cells across the BBB into the CNS
- Total of 2,000 cultivable fungal isolates obtained from fecal samples: MS group yielded 1,608 isolates (24 species) vs 392 from HD; *C. albicans* most abundant (388 vs 30 isolates)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Case-control with multiple components: mycobiome profiling (27 pwMS, 21 HD, including 5 twin pairs discordant for MS), MAIT cell immunology (freshly isolated PBMCs), brain histology (15 progressive MS post-mortem cases)
- **Mycobiome methods**: Culture-based isolation (selective media with bacterial growth inhibitors); ITS1-ITS2 amplicon sequencing; ITS1 region high-throughput metataxonomic analysis (20 MS, 18 HD)
- **Table 1**: 47 fungal species identified; *C. albicans* (388 MS isolates), *C. parapsilosis* (196), *S. delbrueckii* (185), *S. cerevisiae* (99), *Pichia membranifaciens* (200, MS only)
- **Phenotypic characterization**: 25 isolates tested for growth at supra-optimal temperatures, hyphal formation, sporulation, invasiveness, and oxidative stress resistance (Table 2)
- **MAIT immunology**: CD69 upregulation on CD4+ and CD8+ T cells; MAIT proliferation assays; intracellular cytokine staining (IL-17, GM-CSF, IFN-gamma, TNF-alpha); Luminex multiplex cytokine analysis
- **Brain histology**: Immunofluorescent staining for CD8, CD161, TCR Va7.2, and CCL20 on post-mortem tissue from 15 SPMS cases; MAIT cells found in perivascular spaces of postcapillary venules in active lesions
- **Limitations**: Culture-based approach may miss non-cultivable fungi; twin cohort small (5 pairs); post-mortem tissue from progressive MS only
