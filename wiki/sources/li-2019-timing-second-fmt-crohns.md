# Key Claims

- Sequential FMT courses can maintain clinical benefits in active CD; the median time of maintained clinical response to first FMT was 125 days (IQR 82.5-225.5)
- The second FMT should be administered less than 4 months after the first FMT to maintain benefits
- Fecal microbiota composition post-FMT became closer to that of the donor
- Urine metabolome showed significant changes including increased indoxyl sulfate, 4-hydroxyphenylacetate, creatinine, and TMAO between pre-first and pre-second FMT
- Clinical response was supported by host-microbial metabolic changes

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Prospective serial FMT study (NCT01793831); 69 active CD patients receiving two FMT courses
- **Clinical assessment**: Harvey-Bradshaw Index, CRP, erythrocyte sedimentation rate at multiple timepoints
- **Microbiome**: 16S rRNA gene sequencing from stool of 9 randomly selected recipients pre- and post-transplant
- **Metabolomics**: Urine NMR spectroscopy at baseline, 3 days post-first FMT, and pre-second FMT
- **Key metabolites**: Indoxyl sulfate (tryptophan metabolite), TMAO, glycylproline, hippurate altered

# Connections

- Demonstrates that FMT for [[crohns-disease]] requires repeated administration, not just single treatment
- Metabolomic changes connect to [[gut-metal-microbiome]] through shared tryptophan and amino acid pathways
- Donor microbiota engraftment including [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] is time-limited, requiring reinforcement
- TMAO elevation connects to cardiovascular risk and [[inflammation]] pathways
- Sequential FMT protocol addresses the transient nature of [[dysbiosis]] correction
