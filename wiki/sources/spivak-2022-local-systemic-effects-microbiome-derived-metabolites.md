# Key Claims

- **Microbiome-derived metabolites have multiple local and systemic effects** on host physiology
- **SCFA and bile acids** are mostly linked to beneficial effects on host health
- **Tryptophan metabolites** can have either beneficial or harmful impact (indoles vs TMAO)
- **"Postbiotic" therapy** proposed: inactivated microorganisms or their bioactive metabolites for therapeutic use
- **Disrupted metabolite signaling** may enable therapeutic application through personalized precision interventions

# Methods & Findings

Review highlighting key examples of bioactive microbe-associated metabolites:

- Local, systemic, and immune environment effects discussed
- Metabolite repertoire is dependent on host genetic and ecological diversity
- Diet, age, geographic location, and lifestyle influence metabolite production

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: SCFA production enzymes (butyrate kinase, butyryl-CoA transferase) are zinc-dependent
- **[[iron]]**: Iron-sulfur cluster proteins central to multiple metabolite biosynthesis pathways
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium modulates host response to microbial metabolites through selenoprotein-mediated detoxification
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Metal cofactors are the fundamental determinants of microbial metabolite production capacity
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Metabolite [[dysbiosis]] (disrupted metabolite profile) reflects metal-dependent functional impairment
