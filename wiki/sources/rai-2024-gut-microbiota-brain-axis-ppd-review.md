# Key Claims

- **PPD affects 10-30% of mothers** in India and approximately 22% of mothers globally
- **Gut microbiota influence PPD** through neurotransmitter synthesis, precursor generation, and secretion of vital metabolites via [[gut-brain-axis]]
- **Estrogen and progesterone** interact with gut microbiota composition; hormonal decline postpartum contributes to [[dysbiosis]]
- **Serotonin (5-HT)** production by gut microbiota and tryptophan metabolism play key roles in PPD pathogenesis
- **Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)** and HPA axis modulation are additional mechanistic pathways

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Narrative review with summary table of gut microbiota distribution in PPD patients and animal models
- **Key mechanisms**: Estrogen-microbiome crosstalk, tryptophan/serotonin pathway, SCFA production, HPA axis regulation
- **Key table**: Compares Zhou et al. (2017) case-control findings with Zhao et al. rat model findings on Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio changes
