## Key Claims

- 93 cow milk-based infant formulas from Beijing (27 domestic, 66 imported from 9 countries) contained measurable levels of Cr (27.38 ug/kg), As (3.32 ug/kg), Cd (0.98 ug/kg), and Pb (2.03 ug/kg)
- No significant differences in toxic element content between domestic and imported formulas (p>0.05), though Cd was slightly lower in domestic samples
- All Pb levels were below maximum limits set by China (150 ug/kg), EU (50 ug/kg), and CAC (10 ug/kg); Cd below EU limit of 10 ug/kg
- THQ values for all four elements were <1 and HI values were <1 for all age/gender groups, indicating non-carcinogenic risk does not represent a health risk
- HI values were higher for girls than boys (due to lower body weight) and inversely associated with age
- Imported brands (more expensive) did not have significantly lower heavy metal content than domestic brands

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: 93 infant formulas from Beijing supermarkets (stages 1-4), 27 domestic and 66 imported, collected 2019-2020
- **Analysis**: ICP-MS (Agilent 7700) after microwave-assisted acid digestion; LODs: Pb 0.2, As 0.5, Cr 2.0, Cd 0.1 ug/kg; CRM recovery 92.3-104.3%
- **Concentrations**: Cr 2.51-83.80 ug/kg (mean 27.38); As 0.89-7.87 (mean 3.32); Cd 0.13-3.58 (mean 0.98); Pb 0.36-5.57 (mean 2.03)
- **Stage differences**: Cr relatively stable across stages; As peaked at stage 2 (3.57); Cd increased with stage; Pb highest in stage 4 (2.55)
- **EDI calculations**: For 0.5-year-old boy: Cr 0.256, As 0.028, Cd 0.0083, Pb 0.0152 ug/kg BW/day; all below RfD values
- **THQ**: As 0.024-0.103; Pb 0.0015-0.0046; Cr 0.0025-0.0093; Cd 0.0025-0.0090
- **HI**: Ranged 0.055-0.209; highest for 0.5-year-old girls (0.209)
- **International comparison**: Cr similar to Tanzania (<7-53 ug/kg), higher than Egypt; Pb within range of other countries; Cd comparable to Saudi Arabia and Poland
