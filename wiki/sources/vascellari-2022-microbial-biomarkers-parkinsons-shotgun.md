# Gut Microbiome Biomarkers in Parkinson's Disease via Shotgun Metagenomics

## Summary

This study uses [[shotgun-metagenomics]] to identify microbial biomarker signatures for [[parkinsons-disease]], employing machine learning approaches to build diagnostic models from metagenomic data.

## Key Findings

- Machine learning models based on gut microbiome data could distinguish PD patients from controls with high accuracy
- A panel of microbial species-level biomarkers was identified, including enriched Enterobacteriaceae and depleted Prevotellaceae
- Functional pathway analysis revealed altered metabolism of amino acids, vitamins, and xenobiotics in PD
- Microbial biomarker signatures were partly independent of PD medication effects
- The approach demonstrated feasibility of microbiome-based diagnostic testing for PD
- Combining taxonomic and functional biomarkers improved diagnostic performance

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

Microbial biomarker panels for [[parkinsons-disease]] should incorporate metal-related functional markers for maximum diagnostic power. The enriched Enterobacteriaceae identified here carry characteristic [[iron]]-acquisition genes (enterobactin, aerobactin, yersiniabactin) that could serve as functional biomarkers. The depleted taxa are typically metal-sensitive commensals. Integrating [[calprotectin]], [[lactoferrin]], and fecal metal levels with metagenomic biomarkers could create a "metallomic-microbiome diagnostic panel" with greater specificity than either approach alone.

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional biomarker discovery requires prospective validation
- Medication confounders partially but not fully addressed
- Geographic and dietary factors affect microbiome composition
- Clinical utility of microbiome biomarkers not yet validated in clinical practice
