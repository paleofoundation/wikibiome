# Gut Microbiome and Kidney Disease: A Bidirectional Relationship

## Key Claims

- Host-microbe interactions in kidney disease are bidirectional
- Alterations in microbiome affect [[chronic-kidney-disease]] progression and vice versa
- Potential targeted interventions (probiotics, prebiotics) may re-establish symbiosis
- Novel therapeutic strategies may help deploy traditional treatments in kidney disease patients

## Methods & Findings

Narrative review of host-microbiome symbiotic relationship in kidney disease, covering [[dysbiosis]], end-stage renal disease, hemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis, and acute kidney injury.
