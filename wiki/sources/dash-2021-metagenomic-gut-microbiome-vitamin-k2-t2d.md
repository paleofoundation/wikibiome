# Key Claims

- **T2DM enriched in menaquinone (vitamin K2) superpathway biosynthesis** (PWY-5838) compared to healthy subjects
- **No significant differences in overall taxonomic composition** between T2DM and healthy when controlling for diet
- **Firmicutes, Actinobacteria, and Bacteroidetes** phyla contribute significantly to vitamin K2 biosynthesis
- **T2DM with high HbA1c (>6.5%)** exhibited high abundance of genes involved in lysine biosynthesis and low abundance in creatinine degradation

# Methods & Findings

Shotgun metagenomics (MiSeq) of 12 T2DM and 6 healthy subjects:

- Covariance analysis between bacterial genera and metabolic pathways
- Diet-controlled comparison revealed functional rather than taxonomic differences
- Vitamin K2 pathway enrichment is a novel metabolic biomarker finding

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Menaquinone biosynthesis enzymes are zinc-dependent -- enrichment may reflect compensatory response to zinc-mediated metabolic stress
- **[[iron]]**: Iron-sulfur cluster proteins are central to vitamin K2 biosynthesis pathway
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium and vitamin K2 share roles in anti-calcification and antioxidant pathways
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Metal cofactor availability directly determines capacity for menaquinone production
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Functional enrichment without taxonomic change suggests metal-dependent metabolic reprogramming
