# Toxic Metals and Metalloids in Infant Formulas Marketed in Brazil

## Key Claims

- Aluminum, arsenic, tin, and uranium were the major toxic elements detected in Brazilian infant formulas; [[cadmium]] and [[mercury]] were generally below detection limits
- [[lead]] was detected in only 3 of 30 samples
- Arsenic content in infant formulas showed THQ > 1 in most samples, indicating potential health risk concerns for infants
- Aluminum was present at the highest concentrations (0.432-1.241 mg/kg), followed by [[tin]] (0.007-0.095 mg/kg) and arsenic (0.012-0.034 mg/kg)
- Al contamination likely originates from Al cans, glass containers, or tin pack lining with Al foil during manufacturing/storage
- Target Cancer Risk (TCR) values for Al, As, and Pb were within negligible cancer risk range (10^-4 to 10^-6)
- Infants are more vulnerable to toxic metal effects due to rapid growth, immature organ systems, higher food intake relative to body weight, and alkaline gastric pH (6-8) increasing bioavailability

## Methods/Findings

- **Document type**: Original research article
- **Samples**: 30 infant formula samples (10 brands from 3 manufacturers, phase 1 for 0-6 months and phase 2 for 6-12 months), purchased in Rio de Janeiro
- **Analytical method**: ICP-MS (inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry) per US EPA method 6020B
- **Risk assessment**: Target Hazard Quotient (THQ) and Target Cancer Risk (TCR) calculated using EPA/ATSDR reference doses
- **Key THQ results**: Most THQ values < 1 for Hg, Cd, Pb, Sn, Al, and U; significant potential risks observed for arsenic (iAs THQ often > 1, up to 2.0472)
- **Metal concentrations**: Al ranged 0.432-1.241 mg/kg; As 0.012-0.034 mg/kg; Sn 0.007-0.095 mg/kg; U 0.002-0.016 mg/kg; Cd, Hg, Pb mostly below LOD
- **Inter-batch variability**: Significant for Al and Sn, suggesting contamination during manufacturing rather than inherent to milk
