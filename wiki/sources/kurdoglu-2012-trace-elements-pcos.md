# Key Claims

- **Serum Cu and Zn levels were significantly higher** in PCOS patients compared to controls (p < 0.001 and p = 0.006)
- **Mn and Pb levels were significantly lower** in PCOS patients (p < 0.001)
- No significant differences in Mg, Co, or Cd levels between groups
- Cu level was **negatively correlated with BMI** in PCOS patients (r = -0.710, p < 0.01), a relationship not found in healthy controls
- **Pb was inversely correlated with total testosterone** (r = -0.543, p < 0.01) in PCOS -- a novel finding suggesting Pb may modulate androgen metabolism
- This was the **first study** investigating Cu, Zn, Mn, Pb, Mg, Co, and Cd levels together in PCOS
- Higher Cu in PCOS may relate to its role as a cofactor in redox reactions (cytochrome c oxidase, ascorbate oxidase, SOD) and its capacity to induce [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]] by catalyzing ROS formation and decreasing GSH levels
- Lower Mn may reflect increased consumption in the antioxidant defense system (MnSOD) due to elevated [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]] in PCOS

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Case-control study at Yuzuncu Yil University, Van, Turkey
- **Participants**: 35 PCOS patients and 30 age/BMI-matched healthy controls (originally 50 per group, reduced after exclusions)
- **Diagnosis**: Rotterdam criteria
- **Measurements**: Cu, Zn, Mn, Mg, Co, Cd, Pb in serum via atomic absorption spectrophotometry; total testosterone and DHEAS via chemiluminescent immunoassay
- **Key quantitative results** (mean +/- SD):
  - Cu: PCOS 0.75 +/- 0.13 vs control 0.64 +/- 0.10 mcg/mL (p < 0.001)
  - Zn: PCOS 0.92 +/- 0.20 vs control 0.77 +/- 0.19 mcg/mL (p = 0.006)
  - Mn: PCOS 0.05 +/- 0.01 vs control 0.10 +/- 0.07 mcg/mL (p < 0.001)
  - Pb: PCOS 0.45 +/- 0.32 vs control 0.89 +/- 0.25 mcg/dL (p < 0.001)
  - TT: PCOS 83.24 vs control 45.8 ng/dL (p < 0.001)
  - DHEAS: PCOS 307.81 vs control 180.85 mcg/dL (p < 0.001)
- **Limitations**: Small sample size; single-center; serum only; no measurement of oxidative stress markers directly; limited metabolic profiling
