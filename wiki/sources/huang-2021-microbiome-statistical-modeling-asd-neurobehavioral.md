# Overview

Case-control study using a **microbiome-specific multivariate modeling approach (MaAsLin2)** to simultaneously compare ASD gastrointestinal microbiome with that from age-sex matched controls and first-degree relative controls. Identifies microbial taxa, functional pathways, and biomarkers associated with ASD core symptoms.

# Key Findings

## Differentially Enriched Taxa in ASD

- **Enterobacteriaceae** (including *Escherichia/Shigella*) and ***Phyllobacterium*** significantly enriched in ASD
- Relative abundances followed pattern: ASD > first-degree relative control > healthy control
- ***Akkermansia*** reduced in ASD compared to first-degree relatives and healthy controls

## Metabolic Pathway Alterations

- ASD microbiota showed lower levels of **D-Gln, D-Glu metabolism**, **Tyr**, **Trp**, and **fatty acid metabolism**
- **Tryptophan, phenylalanine, and tyrosine biosynthesis** deficient in ASD GI microbiome
- These pathways are pathophysiologically relevant to ASD neurobiology

## Microbiome Biomarkers Linked to ASD Symptoms

- ***Erysipelatoclostridium*** positively associated with ADOS social affect score
- ***Lachnoclostridium*** significantly associated with ADOS total score, ADOS SA score, and calibrated severity score
- ***Tyzzerella*** positively associated with stereotypical behavior (only taxon linked to RBS-R)

## Clinical Factors Predict Microbiome Diversity

- GIS total score, ADOS social affect score, weight, and "waking up at night" were significant predictors of Shannon diversity
- Both intestinal irritability and GI conditions were strong predictors of beta diversity
