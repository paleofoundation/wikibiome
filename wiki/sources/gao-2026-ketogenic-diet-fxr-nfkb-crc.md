# Gao 2026 -- Ketogenic Diet Suppresses CRC via FXR/NF-kB Signaling

## Key Claims

- The ketogenic diet provides both preventive and therapeutic benefits against colorectal cancer in AOM/DSS mouse models by reducing inflammatory responses and restoring intestinal barrier integrity
- KD significantly reprogrammed bile acid metabolism: serum metabolomic analysis showed increased primary bile acids and decreased secondary bile acids (CA, CDCA, DCA, 12-keto LCA concentrations in colorectal tissues/feces matched KD-treated subjects)
- KD combined with antibiotics and FMT consistently increased *Muribaculaceae*, *Akkermansia*, and *Lachnospiraceae NK4A136*, while *Parasutterella* decreased following KD intervention
- FXR upregulation in colorectal tissues modulated NF-kB metabolic pathways, contributing to reduced inflammatory responses
- Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) concentrations positively correlated with the effectiveness of KD in CRC treatment; B-KD group showed significant tumor number and burden reduction during AOM/DSS modeling

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: AOM/DSS mouse model with six groups (B-control, B-model, B-KD, L-control, L-model, L-KD); FMT experiments in pseudo-germ-free mice
- **Methods**: 16S rDNA sequencing, targeted bile acid metabolomics, transcriptomics, serum metabolomics, LC-MS analysis
- **Key results**: KD reduced DAI scores, tumor number, and tumor volume; H&E confirmed efficacy in prevention and treatment; 16S rDNA showed altered microbiota composition and bile acid metabolism
- **FMT validation**: Fecal transplant from KD-fed mice to PGF recipients confirmed microbiota-dependent effects on CRC suppression
