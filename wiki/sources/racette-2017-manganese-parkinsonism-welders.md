# Key Claims

- Progression of parkinsonism **increases with cumulative [[manganese]] exposure** in welders, with an estimated annual UPDRS3 increase of 0.24 points (95% CI 0.10-0.38) per mg Mn/m3-year of exposure
- The parkinsonian phenotype was predominant (15.2% had UPDRS3 score >=15); parkinsonism was the most common finding among 886 welding-exposed workers
- Exposure was most strongly associated with progression of **upper limb bradykinesia**, upper and lower limb rigidity, and impairment of speech and facial expression
- The association was particularly marked in workers who did **flux core arc welding (FCAW)** in confined spaces (0.67 UPDRS3/year per mg Mn/m3-year) compared to non-confined FCAW (0.10)
- Progression appeared particularly marked in workers whose baseline examination was **within 5 years of first Mn exposure**
- A worker with 20 years of welding exposure would be predicted to have nearly a 7-point increase in UPDRS3 score

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Trade union-based longitudinal cohort study
- **Population**: 886 American welding-exposed workers from 3 US Midwestern worksites; 1,492 examinations by movement disorders specialists; 398 workers with 606 follow-up examinations up to 9.9 years after baseline
- **Assessment**: Unified Parkinson Disease Rating Scale motor subsection part 3 (UPDRS3); examinations by 2 movement disorders-trained neurologists; blinded to exposure history; validated with timed motor task
- **Exposure metric**: Cumulative Mn exposure in mg Mn/m3-year estimated from detailed work histories; converted weighted welding years by multiplying by 0.14 mg Mn/m3
- **Demographics**: 94% male, 97% non-Hispanic Caucasian, mean age 45.8 years, mean welding exposure 15.6 years, mean cumulative Mn 1.0 mg Mn/m3-year
- **Primary analysis**: Linear mixed model regression; annual UPDRS3 change of 0.24 per mg Mn/m3-year (p<0.001); adjusted for age, sex, race, tobacco, alcohol, pesticide exposure
- **Sensitivity analyses**: Results robust across multiple adjustments; point estimates ranged from 0.23 to 0.33
- **Subscore analysis** (Table 3): Lower limb rigidity showed strongest association (0.067 per mg Mn/m3-year); speech (0.024) and expression/facial masking (0.021) also significant
- **Stratified analysis** (Table 2): Workers <5 years of exposure at baseline showed dramatically higher progression (4.45 vs 0.23 for >=5 years; p_interaction=0.05)
