# Overview

Systematic review and meta-analysis of **32 RCTs (594 children from 14 RCTs in meta-analysis)** evaluating comprehensive psychosocial interventions for pre-school ASD children (ages 0-6). Categorises interventions into behavioural, social-communication focused, and multimodal developmental models.

# Key Findings

## Overall Effects

- No statistically significant difference between the three intervention models on autism general symptoms
- Evidence for effect on **reciprocity of social interaction towards others** (SMD = 0.53, 95% CI: 0.29-0.78, high quality)
- **Parental synchrony** showed significant improvement (SMD = 0.99, 95% CI: 0.70-1.29)

## By Intervention Type

- **Behavioural interventions**: tended to focus on IQ, adaptive behaviour, language
- **Social-communication focused**: addressed language, social interaction, autism symptoms
- **Multimodal developmental**: measured developmental quotient, language, social interactions, autism symptoms

## Quality of Evidence

- Moderate quality for autism general symptoms, developmental quotient, expressive/receptive language, adaptive behaviour
- High quality specifically for reciprocity of social interaction
- Small number of studies limited subgroup comparisons
