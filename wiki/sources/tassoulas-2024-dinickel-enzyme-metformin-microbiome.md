# Dinickel Enzyme Evolved to Metabolize Metformin

## Key Findings

- **Nickel-dependent drug metabolism**: A novel metallohydrolase (**MfmAB**) with a **dinickel active site** specifically degrades [[metformin]] — the world's most prescribed diabetes drug. This is the first enzyme known to metabolize metformin [[tassoulas-2024-dinickel-enzyme-metformin-microbiome]], in-vitro.

- **Pharmacomicrobiomics implications**: If gut bacteria possess MfmAB homologs, they could alter metformin bioavailability. Since the enzyme is nickel-dependent, **dietary nickel exposure could modulate metformin efficacy** through its effect on microbial drug metabolism.

- **Metal-drug-microbiome triangle**: Nickel availability → bacterial enzyme activity → drug metabolism → therapeutic outcome. This is a concrete example of how [[microbial-metallomics]] directly affects pharmaceutical efficacy.
