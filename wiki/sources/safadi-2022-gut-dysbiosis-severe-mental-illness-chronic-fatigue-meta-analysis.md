# Gut Dysbiosis in Severe Mental Illness and Chronic Fatigue: A Novel Trans-Diagnostic Construct?

## Key Claims

- Systematic review and meta-analysis of 33 studies (N = 2,758 patients, N = 1,847 controls) investigating proxy biomarkers of gut [[dysbiosis]] across severe mental illness and chronic fatigue
- Zonulin was significantly elevated in patients with depression and bipolar disorder (SMD = 0.97, P = 0.03)
- LPS was significantly elevated across all conditions (SMD = 0.77, P < 0.01)
- Antibodies against endotoxin were elevated in bipolar disorder, [[depression]], [[schizophrenia]], and chronic fatigue (SMD = 0.99, P < 0.01); schizophrenia showed highest effect (SMD = 2.72)
- sCD14 was elevated in patients vs controls (SMD = 0.54, P < 0.01)
- Alpha-1-antitrypsin was elevated in bipolar disorder, depression, and schizophrenia (SMD = 1.23, P < 0.01)
- Elevated levels of gut dysbiosis markers positively correlated with severity of sickness behavior in patients with severe mental illness and chronic fatigue
- Gut dysbiosis may underlie symptoms of sickness behavior across traditional diagnostic boundaries as a trans-diagnostic construct
- Figure 1 illustrates the pathway: gut dysbiosis -> loosening of tight junctions -> bacterial translocation -> intestinal inflammation -> systemic inflammation (LBP, sCD14, immunoglobulins)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Biomarkers analyzed**: Zonulin, LPS, LBP, sCD14, antibodies against endotoxin, A-1-AT, I-FABP
- **Forest plots**: Provided for each biomarker across diagnostic categories
- **Key finding**: Schizophrenia had the highest anti-endotoxin antibody levels of any diagnostic group
