# He 2021 -- Gut Microbiome and Sex Hormone-Related Diseases

## Key Claims

- Sex differences in gut microbiota composition are observed in both clinical and animal models; estradiol and testosterone play roles in maintaining gut microbiota diversity through bidirectional interactions
- The gut microbiota regulates sex hormone levels through bacterial beta-glucuronidase (converting conjugated to deconjugated estrogens), the brain-gut axis, and influence on gonadal function
- Ovariectomized (OVX) mice showed decreased *Akkermansia* abundance, while orchiectomized (ORX) mice exhibited increased *Ruminococcaceae* family; androgen supplementation eliminated microbiome deviation between gonadectomy and sham mice
- Table 1 systematically summarizes animal and human studies on sex hormone-microbiota interactions, showing progesterone increases oral *Bacteroides* species, and hormonal differences between sexes affect *Enterobacteriaceae*, *Lactobacillaceae*, *Peptococcaceae* levels
- The gut microbiome may contribute to ovarian cancer development by affecting estrogen levels; *Enterococcus hirae* translocation from small intestine to secondary lymphoid organs increases intratumoral CD8/Treg ratio

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Review covering ovarian cancer, PMOP, PCOS, and T1D as sex hormone-related diseases with microbiome involvement
- **Figure 1**: Cellular and molecular mechanisms of sex hormone-microbiome interaction, depicting bidirectional communication via beta-glucuronidase, STS, HSD enzymes
- **Key mechanisms**: Bacterial beta-glucuronidase deconjugates estrogens for reabsorption via enterohepatic circulation; *Mycobacterium neoaurum* can degrade testosterone
