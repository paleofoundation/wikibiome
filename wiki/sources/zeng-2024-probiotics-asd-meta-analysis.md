# Key Claims

- **Probiotics significantly improve GI symptoms** in ASD children (6-GSI: MD = -0.59, 95% CI [-1.02, -0.17], P = 0.006)
- **No significant improvement** in core behavioral ASD symptoms (ABC, CBCL, SRS, DQ, CGI-I scales all non-significant)
- **GI symptom improvement** is the primary measurable benefit of probiotic supplementation in ASD

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis of 6 RCTs (n = 302 children with ASD).

**Included studies:** From Italy, USA, Taiwan, China (2019-2022), treatment durations 4 weeks to 6 months, ages 3-14 years, 64-92% boys.

**Behavioral outcomes (all non-significant):**
- ABC: MD = -1.86, 95% CI [-6.21, 2.48], P = 0.4
- CBCL: MD = -0.17, 95% CI [-4.09, 3.76], P = 0.93
- SRS: MD = -5.05, 95% CI [-14.57, 4.46], P = 0.3
- DQ: MD = 4.49, 95% CI [-3.34, 12.32], P = 0.26
- CGI-I: MD = -0.16, 95% CI [-0.60, 0.28], P = 0.48

**GI outcome (significant):**
- Total 6-GSI: MD = -0.59, 95% CI [-1.02, -0.17], P = 0.006

**Probiotic strains used:** Various multi-strain formulations including [[lactobacillus]], [[bifidobacterium]], [[Streptococcus thermophilus]], and single-strain [[Lactobacillus plantarum]] PS128.

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

The dissociation between GI improvement and behavioral non-improvement is critical:

- **GI-brain disconnect**: Probiotics restore local gut ecology (metal-dependent barrier function, SCFA production) but may not sufficiently modulate systemic metal homeostasis to affect CNS
- **Strain specificity**: Different probiotic strains have distinct metal requirements and metal-sequestration capacities, explaining heterogeneous results
- **Duration dependency**: Short treatment durations (4 weeks to 6 months) may be insufficient for systemic metallomic rebalancing
- **Primitive 5 (Two-Sided Ecological Engineering)**: Probiotics represent one-sided intervention; combination with metal-directed therapy may be needed for behavioral outcomes
