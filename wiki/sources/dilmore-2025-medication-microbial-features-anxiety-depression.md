# Key Claims

- **Antidepressant use** is associated with significant differences in gut microbiota beta diversity with larger effect size than NPD diagnosis itself
- **Specific microbes** were associated with ANXD and MDD, highlighting potential for non-pharmacological intervention
- **Random Forest classifiers** can predict diagnoses of NPD and medication use from microbial profiles (AUC 93.2% for schizophrenia)
- **Effect sizes were larger in females** than males, though similar trends for both sexes
- **SSRI antidepressants** are particularly relevant when discussing gut microbiome in psychiatric disorders

# Methods & Findings

**Cross-sectional study** using 16S rRNA and shallow shotgun WGS on 666 fecal samples from Tulsa-1000 and NeuroMAP CoBRE cohorts.

- Examined anxiety disorder (ANXD) and major depressive disorder (MDD)
- Assessed influence of medication use on microbiome composition
- Antidepressant use had larger effect than diagnosis on microbiome beta diversity
- Machine learning models could predict diagnosis and medication use
- External validation attempted using ASD and PD cohorts showed disease specificity
