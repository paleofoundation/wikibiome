# Key Claims

- Obesity and CD in endoscopic remission share overlapping colonic mucosal-luminal interface (MLI) microbiome features, suggesting shared microbiome contributions to CD and obesity
- CD is characterized by expansion of [[escherichia-coli|Escherichia-Shigella]] and Fusobacteriota at the MLI, with depletion of Firmicutes including [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii|Faecalibacterium]]
- CD polygenic risk score (GRS) is associated with select taxonomic shifts overlapping with CD-associated changes, including Fusobacterium enrichment
- MLI metabolome in CD patients shows increased cholate:deoxycholate ratio compared to controls, indicating altered bile acid metabolism
- Random forest classifiers using MLI bacteria achieve AUC 0.91 (sigmoid) and 0.93 (cecum) for distinguishing CD from controls

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional study of mucosal-luminal interface; 88 CD patients in endoscopic remission + 110 non-IBD controls
- **Sampling**: Colonic lavage of sigmoid and cecum during colonoscopy; 16S rRNA + untargeted metabolomics (LC-MS)
- **Microbiome shifts in CD**: Lower bacterial diversity (Chao1, Shannon); increased Proteobacteria and Fusobacteriota; decreased Firmicutes and Bacteroidota
- **Obesity overlap**: BMI>30 associated with Escherichia-Shigella expansion and Mycoplasma ASVs in cecum; similar dysbiosis index patterns to CD
- **CD dysbiosis index**: Log ratio of taxa enriched in CD to depleted taxa; significantly elevated in CD (p<10^-5 at both sites)
- **Biogeographic disruption**: CD reverses normal cecum-sigmoid alpha diversity gradient and increases compositional differences between sites
- **GRS associations**: High CD genetic risk associated with Fusobacterium enrichment independent of disease status
- **Metabolomics**: 4,441 ions detected; CD patients show altered bile acid profiles with increased cholate:deoxycholate ratio

# Connections

- Demonstrates persistent [[dysbiosis]] even during CD endoscopic remission, suggesting microbiome changes are not purely secondary to [[inflammation]]
- [[escherichia-coli]] (as Escherichia-Shigella) expansion at the mucosal interface -- most biologically relevant sampling site
