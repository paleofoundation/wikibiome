# Key Claims

- Gut fungal [[dysbiosis]] occurs in the state of prehypertension, predating the onset of full hypertension, and fungal dysbiosis can predict the dysregulation of serum immunoglobulin light chains (LCs) in hypertension patients
- **Malassezia** abundance increased in the HTN group compared to normotensive controls, and was positively associated with serum LC kappa and LC lambda concentrations -- identifying it as a potential pathological fungus in hypertension
- **Mortierella** was depleted in pre-HTN and HTN states compared to controls; *Mortierella* may play a protective/probiotic role in hypertension based on its positive association with LC lambda
- Fecal mycobiomes of both pre-HTN and HTN groups differed significantly from normotensive controls (PERMANOVA R2=0.032 and 0.025, FDR<0.05), but pre-HTN and HTN mycobiomes did not differ from each other
- Fungal richness and diversity tended to increase in pre-HTN and HTN groups compared to controls -- opposite to the pattern seen with bacterial diversity in hypertension
- Dominant fungal genera: Pre-HTN dominated by *Candida* (29.97%), *Malassezia* (17.78%), *Aspergillus* (14.46%); HTN by *Malassezia* (28.45%), *Candida* (18.26%), *Cladosporium* (14.30%); NT by *Candida* (21.80%), *Aspergillus* (19.88%), *Cladosporium* (19.77%)
- Immunoglobulin LCs (kappa, lambda, kappa/lambda ratio) were all significantly elevated in pre-HTN and HTN groups, linking fungal dysbiosis to immune dysregulation

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional cohort with ITS sequencing
- **Population**: 38 pre-HTN, 46 HTN, 34 normotensive controls (age/gender/BMI matched)
- **Methods**: ITS1F/ITS2 primer amplification; Novaseq 6000 sequencing; QIIME V1.9.1 analysis; Vsearch OTU classification against UNITE 12_11 database; immunoturbidimetric LC assay
- **Key fungal phyla**: Ascomycota dominated all groups (55-65%); Basidiomycota second (31-45%)
- **Hypertension subtypes**: ISH, IDH, and SDH showed no significant mycobiome differences
