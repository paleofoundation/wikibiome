# Key Claims

- Metal dyshomeostasis is a significant contributing factor to various cancers, and [[metallomics]] provides a platform for early detection and prognosis through multi-element profiling
- Elevated [[copper]] levels are the most common biomarker across nearly all examined cancer types in biofluids (blood, serum, plasma), suggesting Cu accumulation as a general marker for cancer risk, diagnosis, prognosis, and therapy resistance
- Each cancer type has a unique distribution of metals across biofluids, hair/nails, and tumor-affected tissues, creating cancer-specific metallomic fingerprints
- Genetic variations in metalloprotein genes (MMPs, selenoproteins, Cu/Zn-SOD, ceruloplasmin, transferrin) are associated with cancer susceptibility and prognosis
- The Cu/Zn ratio is mainly increased in blood/serum across various cancers, serving as a general circulating marker

# Methods/Findings

- **Review scope**: Comprehensive critical review covering metallomics in lung, prostate, colorectal, gastric, esophageal, breast, thyroid, pancreatic, laryngeal, gallbladder, renal cell, and non-cirrhotic hepatocellular cancers
- **Lung cancer (LC)**: Elevated Cr, Cd, Cu, Zn, Ni, Se in urine; decreased Mn, Al, Fe, Zn in various biofluids; Cu and Ni significantly changed in multiple studies; [[nickel]] elevated in several biofluids of LC patients
- **Prostate cancer (PC)**: Significantly increased levels of Fe, Cu, Ni, and Cd; decreased Zn and Se; Cu/Zn ratio elevated; Ni and Cd associated with prostate carcinogenesis
- **Colorectal cancer (CRC)**: Increased Cu, Fe, Ni; decreased Se, Zn, Mn; Cu/Zn ratio first suggested as a new CRC marker
- **Breast cancer (BC)**: Elevated Cu, Cd levels; decreased Se, Zn; Cu/Zn ratio increased; contradictory results across studies suggest complex tissue-specific patterns
- **Thyroid cancer (TC)**: As, Cd, Cr, Cu, Pb, Se elevated; decreased Zn, Fe, Ni; strong correlations between metal pairs
- **Pancreatic cancer (PaC)**: Increased Cu and Cd; decreased Zn and Se; urine metallomics emerging as non-invasive detection tool
- **Metalloprotein gene associations (Table 2)**: MMPs (Zn-dependent) linked to carcinogenesis across multiple cancers; selenoprotein gene variants (GPX1, GPX4, TXNRD1/2) associated with cancer risk; Cu-transporting ATPase variants (ATP7B) linked to chemotherapy response
- **Hierarchical clustering (Fig. 3)**: Cu is almost universally increased across cancer types in blood/serum/plasma; Zn shows mixed patterns; Se tends to decrease

# Connections

- [[nickel]] elevation in lung cancer, prostate cancer, and other malignancies supports its role in [[metal-carcinogenesis]] through [[oxidative-stress]] induction and DNA damage
- [[cadmium]] accumulation across multiple cancer types connects to its known carcinogenic mechanisms including disruption of DNA repair and epigenetic modification
- [[chromium]] and [[arsenic]] elevation in certain cancers links to their established roles as Group 1 carcinogens
- [[copper]] as a universal cancer biomarker connects to cuproplasia (Cu-dependent cell growth) and its roles in epigenetic dysregulation, receptor tyrosine kinase signaling, PD-L1 immune evasion, and cellular metabolism
- [[selenium]] decreases across cancers relate to impaired selenoprotein-mediated antioxidant defense
- [[zinc]] dysregulation connects to its roles in >300 metalloenzymes, DNA repair, and immune function
- The metalloprotein genetics findings bridge [[metallomics]] with genomics, offering integrated diagnostic approaches for [[metal-carcinogenesis]]
