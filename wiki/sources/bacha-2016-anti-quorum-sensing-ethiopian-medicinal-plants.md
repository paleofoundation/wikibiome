# Bacha 2016 — Antimicrobial and Anti-Quorum Sensing Activities of Ethiopian Medicinal Plants

## Key Findings

- **Nigella sativa seed (petroleum ether extract)** showed the highest antimicrobial activity against both lab isolate B. cereus (IZ 44±0.31mm) and ATCC strain (IZ 40±2.33mm) ([[bacha-2016-anti-quorum-sensing-ethiopian-medicinal-plants]], in vitro).
- **Aframomum corrorima** (fruit extract) showed strong activity against **C. albicans** (IZ 35±1.52mm) and **S. aureus** (IZ 25±1.32mm) ([[bacha-2016-anti-quorum-sensing-ethiopian-medicinal-plants]]).
- **Two extracts demonstrated quorum sensing (QS) quenching** — Albiza schimperiana root methanol extract and Justica schimperiana seed petroleum ether extract interfered with N-(β-ketocaproyl)-L-homoserine lactone (3-oxo-C6-HSL) signaling in E. coli reporter strain ([[bacha-2016-anti-quorum-sensing-ethiopian-medicinal-plants]]).
- QS interference disrupts pathogenicity, swarming, and biofilm formation **without direct bactericidal action** — reducing selection pressure for resistance ([[bacha-2016-anti-quorum-sensing-ethiopian-medicinal-plants]]).

## Methods

18 plant extracts (oil, resins, crude extracts) from Ethiopian medicinal plants. Agar well diffusion for antimicrobial activity against E. coli K12, P. aeruginosa DSM 498, S. aureus DSM 346, B. cereus (lab + ATCC 10987), C. albicans ATCC 90028. QS interference via E. coli AI1-QQ.1 reporter strain with 3-oxo-C6-HSL.
