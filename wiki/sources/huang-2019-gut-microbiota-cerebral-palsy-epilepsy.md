## Key Findings

- **Microbial diversity was paradoxically higher in CPE children** (Shannon index 2.33 vs 1.49 in healthy controls), suggesting compositional disruption rather than simple loss of diversity.
- **Butyrate-producing genera were consistently depleted**: [[bacteroides]], [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]], [[blautia]], Ruminococcus, and [[roseburia]] were all reduced in CPE.
- **Enriched genera** included [[bifidobacterium]], [[streptococcus]], [[akkermansia-muciniphila]], [[enterococcus]], [[prevotella]], and [[veillonella]] — a mixed profile of commensals and opportunists.
- The taxonomic shift indicates loss of SCFA-producing capacity alongside expansion of taxa that may thrive in altered gut environments (motility dysfunction, medication effects).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Higher diversity in the disease group is counterintuitive — most dysbiotic states show reduced diversity. This may reflect the specific ecology of neurological disability (altered diet, medications, motility) rather than typical inflammatory dysbiosis.

## Open Questions

- How much of the taxonomic shift is driven by antiepileptic drugs vs. the neurological condition itself?
- Would longitudinal sampling show progressive loss of butyrate producers over time?
