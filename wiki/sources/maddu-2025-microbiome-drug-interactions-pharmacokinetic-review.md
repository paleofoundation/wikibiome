# Microbiome-Drug Interactions: A Critical Review of Pharmacokinetic and Pharmacodynamic Modulation

## Summary

Review of how the human gut microbiome functions as a metabolic organ affecting drug pharmacokinetics (PK) and pharmacodynamics (PD), covering mechanisms of microbial drug metabolism, drug effects on the microbiome, and implications for precision medicine.

## Key Findings

### Microbiome Effects on Drug PK
- **Drug metabolism by microbes**: beta-glucuronidase, azoreductase, nitroreductase transform prodrugs (e.g., sulfasalazine to 5-ASA) or deactivate drugs (e.g., digoxin by *Eggerthella lenta*)
- **Altered drug absorption**: Microbes affect bioavailability by modifying gut pH, permeability, and bile secretion
- **Modulation of host metabolism**: SCFAs and bile acids affect CYP450 enzymes critical for drug metabolism
- **Drug transporters**: P-glycoprotein (P-gp) expression and activity altered by microbiota
- **Enterohepatic recycling**: Microbial deconjugation of bile-excreted drugs enables reabsorption

### Drug Effects on Microbiome
- **Antibiotics**: Reduce diversity, may cause *C. difficile* overgrowth and resistance
- **PPIs**: Raise stomach pH, promoting *Enterococcus* and *Enterobacteriaceae*; reducing *Lactobacillus*
- **NSAIDs**: Change composition; increase GI inflammation
- **Antidepressants/antipsychotics**: SSRIs change microbiome via Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio
- **Metformin**: Increases *Akkermansia muciniphila*; improves medication efficacy through microbiome

### Pharmacogenomic Implications
- CYP450 enzyme expression influenced by microbial metabolites including SCFAs
- Immune checkpoint inhibitor response affected by specific gut bacteria
- Individual microbiome variation as major contributor to drug response variability

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **CVD polypharmacy** - Cardiovascular patients typically on multiple drugs (statins, antihypertensives, anticoagulants) whose metabolism is influenced by the microbiome
2. **Precision cardiovascular medicine** - Microbiome profiling could predict drug response and optimize therapy in [[cardiovascular-disease]]
3. **Drug-microbiome feedback loops** - Drugs alter the microbiome which in turn alters drug metabolism, creating complex therapeutic dynamics
