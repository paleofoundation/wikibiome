# Key Claims

- **Mediterranean-based diet** limited postpartum changes in behaviour, neuroinflammation, and gut microbiota in mice
- Prenatal stress is a risk factor for mental disorders in offspring via HPA axis dysfunction, immune dysfunction, and gut microbiota perturbations
- **Diet may improve mental health** in both the postpartum mother and prenatally stressed offspring
- Timing of prenatal stressor matters for physiological and behavioural outcomes
- Sex differences exist in physiological and behavioural effects of prenatal stress

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: PhD thesis with multiple experiments in postpartum and prenatally stressed mice; Mediterranean-based vs. control diet
- **Setting**: University of Ottawa, Department of Cellular and Molecular Medicine
- **Key chapters**: Diet development, pilot study, Mediterranean diet effects on postpartum behaviour/neuroinflammation/microbiota
