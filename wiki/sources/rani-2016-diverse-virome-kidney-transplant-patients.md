# Diverse Virome in Kidney Transplant Patients

## Key Findings

- **A complex and diverse urinary virome** was identified in kidney transplant recipients, extending beyond the commonly screened BK polyomavirus to include multiple viral families and subtypes.
- **BK virus was predominant in the BKV+ group** but was also detectable at low levels in clinically BKV-negative patients, suggesting subclinical viral carriage is common post-transplant.
- **JC polyomavirus and Torque teno virus (TTV)** were also detected, indicating that immunosuppression enables multi-viral colonization of the urogenital tract.
- **Distinct viral polymorphisms** were observed within individual patients, suggesting rapid viral evolution under immune selection pressure in the immunosuppressed host.

## Methods

- **Design**: Cross-sectional metagenomic study of urinary viral communities.
- **Subjects**: 23 kidney transplant recipients, including BKV-positive and BKV-negative patients based on clinical screening.
- **Sequencing**: Shotgun metagenomic sequencing of urine samples for unbiased viral detection.
- **Analysis**: Viral taxonomic classification, phylogenetic analysis, variant calling for within-patient polymorphism assessment.

## Detailed Summary

Kidney transplant recipients require chronic immunosuppression that creates vulnerability to opportunistic viral infections. BK polyomavirus nephropathy is a major cause of graft loss. This study used unbiased shotgun metagenomic sequencing to characterize the full urinary virome, revealing a far more complex viral landscape than targeted BK virus screening alone captures. Beyond BK virus, JC polyomavirus and Torque teno virus were frequently co-detected. The observation of distinct viral polymorphisms within individual patients indicates active viral evolution in the immunosuppressed host, with potential implications for antiviral resistance and immune evasion. While focused on the virome, this study complements gut microbiome studies in [[chronic-kidney-disease]] by demonstrating that microbial ecological disruption in CKD/transplant extends across all domains of life.
