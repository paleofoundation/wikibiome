# Overview

Cohort study of **88 participants** (58 ASD, 30 controls) from Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region in Southern China. Combined **16S rRNA sequencing** and **untargeted urine metabolomics** to identify regional microbiota and metabolomic characteristics in ASD children of the Zhuang ethnic group.

# Key Findings

## Microbial Diversity

- Alpha diversity **significantly lower** in ASD (ACE, Chao, Sobs indices all reduced, p < 0.05)
- Beta diversity showed **distinct community structure** in ASD (PERMANOVA: F = 7.318, R-squared = 0.079, p = 0.001)

## Key Bacterial Biomarkers

- Five species enriched in ASD via DESeq2 and random forest classifiers:
 - ***Faecalibacterium prausnitzii***
 - ***Bifidobacterium catenulatum***
 - ***Blautia obeum***
 - ***Lachnoclostridium* sp.**
 - ***Blautia* sp.**
- LEfSe analysis confirmed these as **critical microbiota distinguishing ASD from controls** (all Q < 0.05)

## Functional Analysis

- ASD microbiota enriched in: ATP-binding cassette (ABCB-BAC) and ABC-2 type transport system functions
- Chemoheterotrophic and fermentation microbiota inactivation observed in ASD

## Urine Metabolomics

- 97 metabolites identified from 88 samples; 66 positive-ionic metabolites discriminated ASD from controls
- Positive correlations between microbiota and **androstenedione, stearamide, oleamide, cadaverine, hexadecanamide, orotic acid, linoleic acid, palmitoleic acid, lauric acid**
- Potential association with **arginine and proline metabolism pathway**

## Clinical Characteristics

- 93.1% of ASD children had GI problems (functional constipation 50%, diarrhea 12.07%)
- Significantly higher proportion of caesarean delivery in ASD group (58.62% vs 26.67%, p = 0.004)
- Premature infant rate also higher in ASD (53.45% vs 33.33%)
