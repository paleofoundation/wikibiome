# Key Claims

- RA patients living in townships with high soil [[copper]] levels had higher WBC counts, ESR, DAS28 scores, and platelet counts
- Blood [[copper]] levels positively correlated with WBC counts (p=0.009), ESR (p=0.001), platelet counts (p=0.001), and rheumatoid factor-IgM (p=0.032)
- Blood [[nickel]] levels also correlated with ESR (p=0.040)
- RA patients had higher blood copper levels than gout, ankylosing spondylitis, and steel plant worker groups
- Copper was the only metal with a significant correlation to ESR in multiple regression (p=0.008)
- This is the first study to correlate heavy metal pollution in the environment with RA disease activities

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Cross-sectional study of 122 RA patients from Changhua Christian Hospital, central Taiwan
- **Soil data**: Copper levels in farm soils from national survey; townships graded 1-4 (Grade 1 = highest, >23.83 mg/kg)
- **Blood metals**: 9 metals measured by ICP-MS (Cu, Zn, Se, Cr, Pb, As, Ni, Hg, Cd)
- **Key finding**: RA patients in Grade 1 (highest Cu) townships had significantly higher WBC, ESR, DAS28, platelet counts vs. Grade 4
- RA patients had highest blood Cu among disease groups (RA > gout > AS > steel workers)
- Nickel and mercury were decreased in RA patients compared to other groups
- No differences found for arsenic, cadmium, chromium, lead, selenium, or zinc between groups
- Multiple regression showed Cu as significant predictor of ESR (beta=0.058, p=0.021) alongside Ni (beta=15.850, p=0.081)
