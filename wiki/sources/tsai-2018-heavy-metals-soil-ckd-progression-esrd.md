# Key Claims

- Soil-based heavy metal Factor 1 ([[chromium]], [[copper]], [[nickel]], [[zinc]]) associated with increased ESRD risk (aHR 1.08, 95% CI 1.01-1.14, p = 0.02)
- Factor 1 also associated with hypertension (OR 1.16), diabetes (OR 1.11), and cerebrovascular accident (OR 1.10) in CKD patients
- Only [[zinc]] (aHR 1.08) and [[nickel]] (aHR 1.08) were individually significant risk factors for ESRD progression
- Factor 2 ([[cadmium]] and [[lead]]) was NOT significantly associated with ESRD, death, or composite outcomes
- CKD patients progressing to ESRD had higher residential soil copper, nickel, and zinc concentrations
- Groups of metals from shared contamination sources (electroplating industry) may exert additional combined harm

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Retrospective cohort study; 2,343 CKD patients (eGFR <60) from Changhua Christian Hospital, Taiwan (Jan 2003-June 2015)
- **Exposure**: Soil-based heavy metal concentrations from national EPA survey (8 metals: As, Cd, Cr, Hg, Cu, Pb, Ni, Zn); linked to patient residential addresses
- **Follow-up**: 3.49 +/- 2.27 years; 533 progressed to ESRD, 1,810 did not
- **Factor analysis**: 8 metals grouped into Factor 1 (Cr, Cu, Ni, Zn -- electroplating) and Factor 2 (Cd, Pb -- combustion/fertilizer)
- Multivariate Cox regression adjusted for DM, hypertension, CHF, CAD, CVA, etc.
- ESRD patients: younger (64.5 vs 66.9), more female, lower eGFR (17.8 vs 33.7), lower Hb, higher proteinuria
- Changhua County: one of Taiwan's highest heavy metal contamination areas from electroplating industry
