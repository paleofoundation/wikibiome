# Silva 2022 — Anti-Inflammatory and Low FODMAP Diet in Fibromyalgia (RCT)

## Key Findings

- **Anti-inflammatory + low FODMAP diet** (3 months) significantly improved multiple FM outcomes vs. healthy eating control ([[silva-2022-anti-inflammatory-low-fodmap-fibromyalgia-rct]], RCT, n=46):
  - FIQR (Revised FM Impact Questionnaire): p=0.001
  - VAS pain: p=0.002
  - BPI (Brief Pain Inventory): p=0.011
  - FSS (Fatigue Severity Survey): p=0.042
  - GI symptoms (VAS_GI): p=0.002
  - Sleep (PSQI): p=0.048
  - SF36 quality of life: p=0.045
- **Inflammatory biomarkers (hs-CRP, ESR) did NOT change** in either group — suggesting the dietary benefit operates through gut-brain, SIBO, or microbiome mechanisms rather than systemic inflammation ([[silva-2022-anti-inflammatory-low-fodmap-fibromyalgia-rct]]).
- Benefits were **independent of age, disease duration, BMI variation, and body fat change** ([[silva-2022-anti-inflammatory-low-fodmap-fibromyalgia-rct]]).
- The dietary intervention excluded gluten, dairy, free sugars, ultra-processed foods, and high-FODMAP foods in month 1; months 2–3 continued anti-inflammatory diet only (low FODMAP reintroduced) ([[silva-2022-anti-inflammatory-low-fodmap-fibromyalgia-rct]]).

## Methods

Two-arm parallel-group RCT (NCT04007705), Portuguese Institute of Rheumatology, Lisbon. April 2019–June 2020. 46 female FM patients (ACR 2010 criteria), age 18–75, stable pharmacological therapy ≥4 weeks. Intervention: anti-inflammatory diet (Dietary Inflammatory Index) + low FODMAP (month 1) → anti-inflammatory only (months 2–3). Control: WHO healthy eating recommendations. Certified dietitian supervised both arms. 3-day food diaries. Outcomes: FIQR, VAS, BPI, FSS, PSQI, VAS_GI, SF-36, hs-CRP, ESR.
