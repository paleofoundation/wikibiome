# Key Claims

- **Indole stimulated in vitro GLP-1 secretion** in a concentration-dependent manner and improved acute glucose tolerance
- **Indole drives enteroendocrine L cell differentiation** in murine ileal organoids, resulting in increased L cell density
- **Sub-chronic indole administration** improved glucose tolerance and insulin sensitivity in a diabetic mouse model
- **Indole acts as a glucose-lowering molecule** through the gut, with potential for nutraceutical supplementation
- **Gut microbiota-derived metabolites** are important for metabolic health

# Methods & Findings

In vitro and in vivo studies of indole in glucose homeostasis:

- GLP-1 secretion measured from cultured murine enteroendocrine cells
- Intraperitoneal glucose tolerance tested after indole treatment in mice
- Murine ileal organoid cultures used for L cell differentiation studies
- Chronic indole administration in diabetic mouse model assessed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Tryptophan metabolism to indole by gut bacteria requires zinc-dependent tryptophanase enzymes
- **[[iron]]**: Iron-sulfur clusters are cofactors in tryptophan catabolism pathways producing indole derivatives
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenoproteins protect enteroendocrine L cells from oxidative damage during indole-mediated differentiation
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Indole production capacity depends on the metal environment sustaining tryptophan-metabolizing bacteria
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Loss of indole-producing bacteria in [[dysbiosis]] reduces GLP-1 secretion capacity
