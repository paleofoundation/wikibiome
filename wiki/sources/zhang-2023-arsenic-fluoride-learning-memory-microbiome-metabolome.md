# Key Claims

- **Arsenic and/or fluoride exposure** from intrauterine to adult period reduced neurobehavioral performance and caused hippocampal CA1 lesions
- **16S rRNA sequencing** revealed As and/or F exposure significantly altered gut microbiome composition and diversity
- **Learning and memory impairment** may be related to tryptophan, lipoic acid, glutamate, GABA, and arachidonic acid metabolism
- **Gut microbiota, metabolites, and learning memory indicators** were significantly correlated
- **Learning memory impairment triggered by As and/or F** may be mediated by different gut microbes and their associated metabolites

# Methods & Findings

**Animal model study** using SD rats exposed to arsenic and/or fluoride from intrauterine to adult period.

- Examined neurobehavioral performance, hippocampal histopathology, gut microbiome (16S rRNA), and metabolome
- Lachnospiraceae, Ruminococcus, Prevotellaceae, Eubacterium xylanophilum featured in dysbiosis
- Metabolomic pathways affected: tryptophan, GABA, arachidonic acid metabolism
- Multi-omics integration demonstrated microbiome-metabolome-brain axis
