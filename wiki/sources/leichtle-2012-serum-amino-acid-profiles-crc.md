# Key Claims

- 11 of 26 serum [[tryptophan|amino acid]] concentrations are significantly different between [[colorectal-cancer]] patients and healthy controls
- A model combining CEA, glycine, and tyrosine is the best-discriminating biomarker panel with AUROC of 0.878 (95% CI 0.815-0.941), superior to CEA alone (AUROC 0.794)
- Serum glycine and tyrosine in combination with CEA are superior to CEA alone for discriminating between CRC patients and controls
- The amino acid concentration-based model shows non-inferiority and inferred superiority over CEA alone as a screening marker
- Amino acids decreased in CRC: lysine, leucine, threonine, valine, and tyrosine; increased: alanine, methionine, threonine, leucine, isoleucine, valine, glycine, and lysine show complex patterns

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original research, case-control metabolomics study
- **Cohort**: 59 CRC patients (37 male/22 female, median age 59) and 58 age/gender-matched healthy controls from University Hospital Leipzig, Germany
- **Methods**: Fasting serum analyzed by tandem mass spectrometry (FIA-MS/MS) for 26 amino acids using standardized derivatization protocol; CEA measured by electrochemiluminescence immunoassay
- **Statistical approach**: Bootstrap resampling (B = 999), PCA, binary logistic regression (BMA model selection), AUROC analysis with non-inferiority/superiority testing
- **Key findings**: 19 of 26 amino acids decreased, 7 increased in CRC; glycine (AUROC 0.707), histidine (0.691), and alanine (0.677) were top single discriminators
- **ROC analysis**: CEA alone AUROC 0.794; CEA + glycine + tyrosine AUROC 0.878; CEA + PC1 AUROC 0.844
- **UICC staging**: Stages I-IV represented; CEA median 3.5 ng/mL in CRC vs 1.7 in controls (P < 0.001)
