# Key Claims

- Increased abundance of *RuminococcusUCG010* genus leads to lower risk of CAD (OR 0.88, 95% CI 0.78-1.00) and MI (OR 0.88, 95% CI 0.79-0.97), consistent across CARDIoGRAMplusC4D, FinnGen, and UK Biobank databases
- Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) was identified as a **mediating factor** in the causal effect of *RuminococcusUCG010* on CAD/MI, with an average mediation proportion of 20% on CAD and 17% on MI
- No significant causal relationships were found at phylum, class, order, or species levels -- only genus-level associations emerged
- When the abundance of *RuminococcusUCG010* doubled, the risk of CAD or MI was reduced by 12% in the random-effect IVW model
- *Ruminococcus* is a butyrate-producing bacterial genus with anti-inflammatory properties that inhibit proinflammatory macrophages; it was enriched in stable angina and acute MI patients in fecal gene sequencing studies
- The TwinsUK cohort revealed a negative correlation between arterial stiffness and Ruminococcaceae abundance

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample MR with multivariable MR (MVMR) and mediation analysis
- **Exposure**: MiBioGen GWAS (18,340 participants, 211 taxa at 5 levels)
- **Outcome databases**: CARDIoGRAMplusC4D (184,305 total), FinnGen (260,405 total), UK Biobank (361,194 total)
- **Mediation**: T2DM data from FinnGen and meta-analysis of DIAGRAM/GERA/UKB; LDL-C from GLGC (188,578 participants); hypertension from FinnGen/UKB
- **Key finding**: No causal effect of butyrate proxy (PWY-5022) on CAD/MI, though *RuminococcusUCG010* (a butyrate producer) was protective -- suggesting mechanisms beyond butyrate alone
