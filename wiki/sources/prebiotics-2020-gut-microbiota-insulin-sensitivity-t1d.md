# Key Claims

- **Prebiotics and [[probiotics]] can modulate gut microbiota** to improve insulin sensitivity in T1D
- **Synbiotic approaches** (combining prebiotics and [[probiotics]]) may be more effective than either alone
- **Gut microbiota plays a role in insulin sensitivity** through SCFA production, barrier function, and immune modulation
- **Clinical trial evidence** supports potential for microbiota-targeted interventions in T1D management

# Methods & Findings

Review of evidence for prebiotics, [[probiotics]], and synbiotics in T1D insulin sensitivity:

- Mechanisms include SCFA-mediated insulin sensitization, barrier restoration, immune modulation
- Clinical trials in T1D populations reviewed
- Gap analysis of current evidence and future directions

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Prebiotic fermentation produces SCFAs via zinc-dependent enzymes -- zinc co-supplementation may enhance prebiotic efficacy
- **[[iron]]**: Prebiotics (e.g., inulin) can increase iron absorption through microbiome-mediated pathways
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-enriched prebiotics could provide combined prebiotic and antioxidant benefits
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Prebiotic-probiotic-metal optimization represents an untapped therapeutic strategy for T1D
- **[[probiotics]]**: Metal-fortified synbiotic formulations could maximize insulin-sensitizing effects
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: SCFA-mediated barrier restoration from prebiotic use is zinc-dependent
