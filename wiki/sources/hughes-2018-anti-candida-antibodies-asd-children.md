# Key Claims

- **36.5% (19/52) of children with ASD** were positive for anti-C. albicans IgG antibodies vs 14.3% (4/28) in TD controls
- **ASD children had a higher rate of high-positive values** with unadjusted odds ratio of 3.45 (95% CI 1.0409-11.4650, p=0.041)
- **GI dysfunction found in about half** of ASD children who were positive for anti-Candida IgG
- Study provides evidence of **a new microbial risk factor** for ASD
- Fungal microbiota in ASD has been poorly studied compared to bacterial components

# Methods & Findings

**Case-control study** measuring anti-C. albicans IgG in plasma from 80 children in the UC Davis MIND Institute CHARGE study.

- Commercial ELISA kit used for antibody measurement
- 52 ASD children and 28 TD controls compared
- Higher antibody positivity rate in ASD group
- GI dysfunction associated with Candida antibody positivity
- Suggests current or previous Candida overgrowth in ASD children

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Fungal dysbiosis** as underexplored component of ASD gut ecosystem
- **Candida overgrowth** may contribute to GI symptoms and immune activation in ASD
- **Antibody-based detection** suggests systemic immune response to fungal translocation
