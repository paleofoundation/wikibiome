# Key Findings

- **High-Candida guts showed increased lactate-producing bacteria and decreased SCFA producers** (notably *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*), establishing that the bacterial metabolic environment gates fungal expansion ([[seelbinder-2023-candida-expansion-lung-cancer-ecological]], cross-sectional, n=75).
- **Machine learning models predicted Candida abundance from bacterial composition** with AUC 78.6–81.1%, demonstrating that bacterial ecology is a reliable predictor of fungal overgrowth risk.
- **Bacterial ecology determines whether Candida can expand** — Candida is not the primary driver but an opportunist exploiting a lactate-rich, butyrate-poor niche.

# Methods

Cross-sectional analysis of gut mycobiome and bacteriome in 75 lung cancer patients using ITS and 16S sequencing, with machine learning modeling of bacterial predictors of Candida abundance.
