# Key Claims

- **Low total tryptophan (TRP) levels** in postpartum days 1-5 were significantly associated with PPD (SMD: -5.39, 95% CI [-7.72, -3.05])
- **No significant association** found between free TRP levels and [[postpartum-depression]] (SMD: -3.43, 95% CI [-7.76, 0.89])
- **Tryptophan** is metabolized through kynurenine, hydroxylation, decarboxylation, and transamination pathways
- **Kynurenine pathway** accounts for ~95% of dietary TRP metabolism; TRP 2,3-dioxygenase (TDO) and indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase (IDO) are key enzymes
- IDO and TDO pathways important for establishing and maintaining fetal/neonatal immune tolerance during pregnancy

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis following PROSPERO guidelines; 13 studies met inclusion, 5 with sufficient data for meta-analysis
- **Setting**: Monash University, Melbourne, Australia
- **Key implication**: Total plasma TRP on postpartum days 1-5 may identify women at greater PPD risk

# Connections

- Tryptophan-serotonin pathway is a key [[gut-brain-axis]] mechanism in [[postpartum-depression]]
