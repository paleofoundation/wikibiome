# Key Claims

- **Mind-blindness theory** explains social and communication difficulties in ASD but cannot explain nonsocial features
- **Empathizing-Systemizing (E-S) theory** is a two-factor model explaining both social and nonsocial features of autism
- **E-S theory** is related to weak central coherence theory and executive dysfunction theory
- **Extended to the extreme male brain theory** as a way of understanding the biased sex ratio in autism
- **Etiological predictions** and clinical applications arise from the E-S theory

# Methods & Findings

**Theoretical review** of cognitive theories of autism.

- Critiqued mind-blindness theory as insufficient for explaining the full ASD phenotype
- Presented E-S theory as explaining both empathizing deficits and systemizing strengths
- Connected to extreme male brain hypothesis to explain 4:1 male-to-female ratio
- Discussed clinical implications for diagnosis and intervention
- Related to other cognitive frameworks (weak central coherence, executive dysfunction)

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Cognitive framework** for understanding ASD beyond biological mechanisms
- **Sex differences** in ASD explained through empathizing-systemizing dimensions
- **Nonsocial features** (restricted interests, repetitive behavior) explained as hyper-systemizing
