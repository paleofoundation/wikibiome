# Gut Mycobiome Dysbiosis Contributes to the Development of Hypertension and Its Response to Immunoglobulin Light Chains

## Summary

Original research examining the gut mycobiome in 38 pre-hypertension (pre-HTN), 46 hypertension (HTN), and 34 normotensive (NT) subjects via ITS sequencing. Found that fungal [[dysbiosis]] occurs early in the pre-hypertension stage and correlates with immunological light chain dysregulation.

## Key Findings

### Fungal Community Differences
- Both pre-HTN and HTN had different fungal communities vs NT (PERMANOVA R2=0.032 and 0.025, FDR<0.05)
- Pre-HTN dominated by: *Candida* (29.97%), *Malassezia* (17.78%), *Aspergillus* (14.46%), *Cladosporium* (12.79%)
- HTN dominated by: *Malassezia* (28.45%), *Candida* (18.26%), *Cladosporium* (14.30%), *Aspergillus* (12.13%), *Saccharomyces* (8.46%)
- *Malassezia* increased from NT to pre-HTN to HTN (significant between HTN and NT)
- *Mortierella* depleted in pre-HTN and HTN compared to NT

### Immunoglobulin Light Chain Associations
- LC kappa, LC lambda, and kappa/lambda ratio all significantly higher in pre-HTN and HTN vs NT
- *Malassezia* positively associated with LC kappa in pre-HTN (r=0.510, P=0.044) and with both LC kappa and LC lambda in HTN
- *Mortierella* positively associated with LC kappa in HTN (r=0.420, P=0.026)
- *Debaryomycetaceae* positively associated with kappa/lambda ratio (r=0.483, P=0.009)

### Early Dysbiosis
- Fungal dysbiosis detected already at the pre-hypertension stage
- No significant difference between pre-HTN and HTN fungal communities
- Suggests mycobiome changes precede clinical hypertension

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Early [[dysbiosis]] detection** - Mycobiome shifts occurring at pre-HTN stage could serve as early biomarkers for metal-induced cardiovascular risk
2. **[[Inflammation]] via immunoglobulin** - Light chain dysregulation connecting to fungal dysbiosis provides a novel immune mechanism linking [[dysbiosis]] to hypertension
3. ***Malassezia* as pathobiont** - Known to produce inflammatory lipases and activate immune cells; its enrichment in hypertension may compound [[lead]] and [[cadmium]]-driven [[inflammation]]
4. ***Mortierella* depletion** - Loss of this potentially protective fungus parallels loss of protective bacterial taxa with metal exposure
