# Sun 2022 — Probio-M8 RCT in Parkinson's Disease

## Key Findings

- 3-month double-blind RCT in 82 PD patients (48 probiotic, 34 placebo) of **Bifidobacterium animalis subsp. lactis Probio-M8** co-administered with benserazide + dopamine agonists. RCT evidence level.
- Probio-M8 conferred **added benefits over placebo** for sleep quality (PDSS), anxiety (HAMA), depression (HAMD-17), UPDRS-III motor scores, and GI symptoms (Bristol score, defecation frequency, feces hardness, PAC-QOL). All p-values in patient-level reduction were significant.
- Shotgun metagenomics: **Bifidobacterium animalis, Ruminococcaceae, Lachnospira** SGBs significantly increased; **Lactobacillus fermentum, Klebsiella oxytoca** SGBs significantly decreased in the probiotic arm.
- **Lactobacillus fermentum** abundance correlated positively with UPDRS-III, HAMA, and HAMD-17 severity and negatively with MMSE (cognition) — identifying it as a potential pathogenic marker in PD, a notable finding since Lactobacillus has been variably classified.
- Probio-M8 increased gut metabolic modules for **tryptophan degradation, GABA biosynthesis, SCFA production (acetate, propionate, butyrate), and secondary bile acid biosynthesis**.
- Serum metabolomics: **acetate and dopamine significantly elevated** in the Probio-M8 group.

## Methods

- Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial; 3-month intervention; conventional regimen kept constant in both arms.
- Participants: 82 PD patients; 45 (Probio-M8) and 29 (placebo) completed stool + serum sampling for metagenomic and metabolomic analysis.
- Strain: Bifidobacterium animalis subsp. lactis Probio-M8, originally isolated from breast milk.
- Assessments: UPDRS-III, MMSE, HAMA, HAMD-17, PDSS, Bristol stool scale, PAC-QOL; shallow shotgun metagenomic sequencing of stool; LC-MS/GC-MS serum metabolomics.

## Relevance to Cureva

**Triangle evidence for Probio-M8 in PD:**
- I→f (intervention affects feature): Probio-M8 reduces L. fermentum and K. oxytoca; raises SCFA-producers. Evidence: strong (shotgun metagenomics, n=74).
- I→D (clinical improvement): Significant UPDRS-III, HAMA, HAMD-17, PDSS improvements. Evidence: RCT.
- f→D (mechanism): L. fermentum correlates with disease severity; SCFAs and dopamine are mechanistically linked to PD pathology.

Classification: **validated** for adjunctive use in mild-moderate PD with GI symptoms.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Lactobacillus fermentum as pathogenic contradicts older probiotic studies that included L. fermentum as a "beneficial" strain for PD. The species-level resolution here suggests strain specificity matters. Note conflict on [[lactobacillus]] page.
- L. fermentum enrichment in PD was observed in prior studies; this confirms it as PD-associated rather than neutral.

## Open Questions

- Durability beyond 3 months unknown.
- Whether effect generalizes to advanced PD or other PD medications.
- Whether reduction in L. fermentum is causal or correlative with clinical improvement.
