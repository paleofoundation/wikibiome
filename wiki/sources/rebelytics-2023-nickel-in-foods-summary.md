# Nickel in Foods: Summary Tables, Unweighted Averages (July 2023)

## Key Claims

- Comprehensive database of nickel content per serving across hundreds of food items, organized by 20+ food categories
- **Highest nickel foods per serving**: soy-based products dominate -- soy beverage (53.54 ug/250g), soy yogurt substitute (107.95 ug/175g), TVP (251.33 ug/100g), tofu (101.99 ug/85g)
- **Nuts and seeds** are consistently high: cashews (165.93 ug/30g), sunflower seeds (101.59 ug/30g), walnuts (100.23 ug/30g), pecans (170.40 ug/30g)
- **Chocolate and cocoa** are major sources: cocoa powder (50.25 ug/5g), chocolate (67.55 ug/40g), chocolate confections (36.49 ug/40g)
- **Shellfish** extremely high: mussels (103.17 ug/125g), clams (136.05 ug/8g), scallops (176.46 ug/100g), oysters (58.00 ug/125g)
- **Lowest nickel foods**: dairy products (cream 0.29, whipped cream 0.05 ug/serving), most meats (venison 1.90, rabbit 1.47 ug/125g), eggs (1.67 ug/100g), white sugar (0.08 ug/4g)
- **Tea and coffee**: Tea leaves extremely high (59.50 ug/3g); brewed tea moderate (9.01 ug/250g bag); coffee moderate (3.84 ug/250g)
- **Plant milks** vary enormously: oat beverage (33.40 ug/250g), soy beverage (53.54 ug/250g), almond beverage (8.98 ug/250g)

## Methods/Findings

- **Data compilation**: Aggregated from multiple published sources; reports number of sources, mean, standard deviation, min, and max nickel content per standard serving size
- **Categories covered**: Dairy, grains/bakery, whole grains/flours/starches, fruits (berries, citrus, other), vegetables (root, leafy, other, prepared), meat/poultry/eggs, fish/seafood, beans/legumes, nuts/seeds, beverages (alcoholic, juices, hot, other), herbs/spices, fats/oils, snacks/desserts, sugars/sweets, sauces/condiments, miscellaneous
- **Key high-Ni grains**: Buckwheat (raw 99.26 ug/45g), millet (raw 47.90 ug/40g), oats (raw 53.62 ug/40g), quinoa (raw 35.26 ug/45g)
- **Key high-Ni vegetables**: Peas (34.54 ug/85g raw, 28.40 ug/130g cooked), asparagus (25.25 ug/85g), bean sprouts (29.40 ug/65g), spinach (8.94 ug/85g raw, 20.28 ug/130g cooked)
- **Key high-Ni beans**: Beans various dried (78.47 ug/35g), lima beans (46.97 ug/80g), lentils (68.08 ug/80g)
- **Prepared vegetables**: Cooking generally concentrates nickel per serving due to water loss; cooked cassava (65.05 ug/167g), cooked peas (28.40 ug/130g)
