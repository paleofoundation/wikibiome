# Recent Advances in Gut Microbiota and Thyroid Disease: Pathogenesis and Therapeutics in Autoimmune, Neoplastic, and Nodular Conditions

## Key Findings

- **Consistent dysbiosis across thyroid diseases**: A pronounced shift in [[gut-microbiome]] composition is observed across HT, GD, thyroid nodules, and thyroid cancer, with significant reduction in [[bifidobacterium]], Bacillaceae, Megamonas, and [[clostridium]], and notable increase in [[bacteroides-fragilis]], [[proteobacteria]], [[actinobacteria]], Desulfobacterota, and [[klebsiella-pneumoniae]] ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).
- **SCFA depletion drives immune imbalance**: The potential decrease in [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] may influence T regulatory cells (Tregs), leading to Th1/Th2 imbalance and increased pro-inflammatory cytokines including IFN-gamma and TNF-alpha ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).
- **FMT evidence in animal models**: FMT from HT patients intensified thyroiditis and immune dysregulation in CBA/J mice, with elevated serum TgAb levels and disrupted T-cell balance. FMT from healthy donors, along with hydrogen sulfide (H2S), mitigated these effects ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).
- **Probiotic adjunct therapy shows promise**: Specific strains including *Lactiplantibacillus plantarum* 299v and *Bifidobacterium longum* have demonstrated potential in improving thyroid function when combined with conventional treatments ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).
- **Thyroid cancer microbiota**: In thyroid cancer patients, Neisseria and Streptococcus are significantly enriched while Butyricimonas and Lactobacillus are decreased; microbial dysbiosis affects metabolic pathways including butyrate production and amino acid biosynthesis ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).

## Methods

- **Study type**: Narrative review synthesizing experimental studies from the past 5 years (published December 24, 2024)
- **Search strategy**: NCBI PubMed for articles related to gut microbiota and thyroid diseases, specifically targeting experimental studies published within the past 5 years, omitting review articles; analogous search for probiotic and thyroid disease research
- **Scope**: Gut microbiota in four thyroid diseases: Hashimoto's thyroiditis (HT), Graves' disease (GD), thyroid nodules (TN), and thyroid cancer (TC)
- **Data presentation**: Summary tables of gut microbiota changes for each condition (Tables 1-4) covering studies from 2019-2024 across multiple countries

## Detailed Summary

**Hashimoto's Thyroiditis** (Table 1): Six studies from 2020-2024 (Egypt, Spain, Brazil, China) consistently show decreased Bifidobacterium and increased Bacteroides. The "ABC transporter" metabolic pathway is highly associated with HT. Free thyroxine (T4) may promote Romboutsia and Haemophilus while being regulated by free triiodothyronine (T3), and Faecalibacterium and Lachnospiraceae levels are also influenced. A Mendelian randomization study confirmed causal links between gut microbiota and autoimmune thyroiditis, with 6 gut microbiota species significantly associated with AIT ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).

**Graves' Disease** (Table 2): Fourteen studies from 2020-2024 across multiple countries show shifts in Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes composition. Significant decrease in SCFA-producing bacteria (Faecalibacterium, Roseburia) and relative increase in Bacteroides, Proteobacteria, and Actinobacteria. Eukaryotic microbes (fungi and protists) may play a more significant role than previously thought. The role of specific microbiota in GD immune tolerance loss is linked to the loss of tolerance to self-antigens ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).

**Thyroid Nodules** (Table 3): Neisseria and Streptococcus increased significantly; Butyricimonas and Lactobacillus decreased. Metabolic pathway alterations include reduced butyrate production and increased amino acid biosynthesis. Over 60% of the general population has thyroid nodules, and gut microbiome shifts suggest a complex interplay between gut microbiota imbalance and thyroid nodule development ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).

**Thyroid Cancer** (Table 4): Fifteen studies from 2019-2024 including multiple Mendelian randomization analyses (n up to 6,699 TC vs. 1,620,354 controls) identify risk factors (Ruminiclostridiaceae, Mollicutes, Tenericutes) and protective factors (Betaproteobacteria, Sutterella, Odoribacter). Intratumor mycobiome and archaeome show distinct dysregulation in PTC, with Volvariella volvacea overabundant in BRAF V600E-positive tumors ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).

**Therapeutic evidence**: Probiotic *Bifidobacterium longum* supplied with methimazole improved thyroid function in GD patients, reducing TRAb and influencing neurotransmitters via the gut-thyroid axis. The IMITHOT trial is evaluating FMT for subclinical autoimmune hypothyroidism in the Netherlands. Berberine combined with methimazole regulated the intestinal microbiota and significantly reduced TRAb levels while restoring TSH ([[fang-2024-gut-microbiota-thyroid-disease-advances]], expert-opinion).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Contrasting findings on Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio direction in GD across different geographic populations (some show decreased F/B ratio, others show increased)
- The role of Lactobacillus is ambiguous: a probiotic consortium including Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium increased orbital CD25+ Treg cells and promoted Graves' orbitopathy phenotypes in one mouse model, while Lactobacillus is generally considered beneficial
- Probiotic utilization fails to counteract detrimental effects of radioactive iodine-131 therapy on thyroid carcinoma microbiota

## Open Questions

- Whether specific probiotic strains can prevent thyroid autoimmunity onset vs. only ameliorate existing disease
- Long-term effects of FMT on thyroid function in humans (IMITHOT trial results pending)
- Whether the mycobiome (fungal) and archaeome components are causally involved in thyroid cancer or merely associated
- The precise mechanism by which bile acid pathway disruption connects to thyroid hormone recycling
