# Ferreira 2019 -- CRC Targeting Peptide Through Phage Display Targeting MCT1

## Key Claims

- A peptide named RKOpep (CPKSNNGVC) was identified by phage display that specifically binds to CRC cell line RKO, Caco-2, HCT116, and HCT-15, as well as to human colorectal cancer tissues, but not normal colon cells (CCD-841-CoN)
- Bioinformatics analysis identified monocarboxylate transporter 1 (MCT1) as the target of RKOpep; MCT1 is implicated in colorectal cancer progression and prognosis via gene downregulation and immunocytochemistry co-localization
- MCT1 expression was 6-10x higher in CRC cell lines compared to normal CCD-841-CoN cells; RKOpep binding correlated with MCT1 expression levels across cell lines
- After five rounds of biopanning using BRASIL method (Biopanning and Rapid Analysis of Selective Interactive Ligands), phage enrichment rate increased 45-fold in the final selection round
- Computational docking (CABS-dock) predicted favorable binding of RKOpep to MCT1 with hydrogen bonds and hydrophobic contacts confirmed by LigPlot analysis

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Phage display using disulfide-constrained peptide library (Ph.D.-C7C) on RKO cells with CCD-841-CoN negative selection
- **Methods**: BRASIL biopanning, cell-ELISA, FAM-labeled peptide fluorescence microscopy and flow cytometry, immunofluorescence on fresh human CRC tissues, western blot for MCT1, gene knockdown (siRNA) validation
- **Key validation**: MCT1 silencing reduced RKOpep binding by ~75%; MCT4 silencing showed ~13% decrease, confirming MCT1 specificity
