# Key Claims

- **Parental high salt diet (HSD)** altered maternal gut microbiome and this was shared with offspring
- **Offspring of HSD-fed parents** showed hyperactivity, increased repetitive behaviors, and impaired sociability in adult male mice
- **ASD-like behavioral abnormalities** are strongly associated with parental HSD consumption via gut microbiome changes
- **Maternal gut microbiome** can profoundly influence neurodevelopment and shape offspring behavior
- **High salt intake** depletes Lactobacillus spp., disrupts T helper cell-mediated immune dysregulation, and causes cognitive dysfunction

# Methods & Findings

**Animal model study** using 16S genome sequencing of fecal samples and behavioral analysis.

- HSD changed maternal gut microbiome composition
- Altered microbiome was transmitted to offspring
- Behavioral analysis of offspring showed ASD-like phenotypes: hyperactivity, repetitive behaviors, impaired sociability
- Effects were sex-specific, primarily affecting male offspring
- Demonstrates dietary-microbiome-behavior axis in autism-like phenotypes

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Maternal diet** as a modifiable environmental risk factor for ASD through microbiome-mediated mechanisms
- **Sex differences** in susceptibility align with the male preponderance of ASD
- **Lactobacillus depletion** by high salt parallels findings in ASD gut microbiome studies
