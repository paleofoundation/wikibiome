# The Impact of DNA Extraction Methods on Stool Bacterial and Fungal Microbiota Community Recovery

## Summary

Methodological study comparing five DNA extraction methods (QIAamp, PureLink, ZR Fecal DNA, NucleoSpin, IHMS protocol Q) for their efficiency in recovering both bacterial and fungal gut microbiota from germ-free mouse feces spiked with defined organisms and human stool samples.

## Key Findings

### DNA Yield and Quality
- IHMS and ZR methods produced highest DNA yields (p<0.05)
- NS, QIA, and PL resulted in five times lower yields
- All methods generated sufficient quality for NGS analysis
- ZR extraction showed highest fungal DNA yield

### Bacterial Community Recovery
- Bacterial alpha diversity similar between IHMS, NS, PL, and ZR methods
- QIA showed lowest rarefaction curve
- 49 core bacterial taxa detected across all methods; 11 rare taxa were method-associated
- Significant abundance differences in 19/52 taxa across methods

### Fungal Community Recovery
- Fungal core microbiome consisted of only 5 taxa (vs 49 bacterial)
- Most fungal taxa (27/47) uniquely detected by one method only
- PCoA showed distinct clustering by extraction method for fungi
- Fewer significant differences in fungal abundance than bacterial
- Fungal contamination from kit reagents detected in blank controls

### Recommendations
- IHMS protocol Q performed best overall for simultaneous bacterial and fungal analysis
- Blank isolation controls are essential for mycobiome research
- Method selection significantly impacts fungal community recovery more than bacterial

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Methodological rigor for mycobiome-CVD studies** - When studying fungal [[dysbiosis]] in [[cardiovascular-disease]], extraction method choice critically affects results; IHMS protocol recommended
2. **Contamination awareness** - Kit contamination is a significant concern for fungal analysis, relevant to all [[gut-metal-microbiome]] mycobiome studies
3. **Dual-kingdom analysis** - Validates that simultaneous bacterial and fungal profiling is feasible with appropriate methods, important for comprehensive [[dysbiosis]] assessment
