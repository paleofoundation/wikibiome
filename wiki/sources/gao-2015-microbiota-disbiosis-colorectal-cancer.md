# Gao 2015 -- Microbiota Dysbiosis Is Associated with Colorectal Cancer

## Key Claims

- The mucosa-associated microbiota is dynamically associated with CRC and may provide evidence for microbiota-associated diagnostic, prognostic, preventive, and therapeutic strategies
- Firmicutes and Fusobacteria were over-represented in CRC patients while Proteobacteria was under-represented; *Lactococcus* and *Fusobacterium* exhibited relatively higher abundance
- Shannon diversity was significantly lower in CRC vs healthy individuals (3.43 vs 4.01; p < 0.001; Simpson 0.24 vs 0.15, p = 0.0002)
- The microbial composition of tumor tissue was significantly different from adjacent non-cancerous tissue, with Firmicutes contributing 63.46% in cancerous vs 39.54% in adjacent tissues

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Cross-sectional study (31 CRC patients, 30 healthy controls)
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA gene V3 pyrosequencing; cancerous tissue, adjacent non-cancerous tissue, and mucosal samples
- **Results**:
  - 468 bacterial phylotypes identified; *Lactococcus*, *Fusobacterium*, *Escherichia-Shigella*, *Peptostreptococcus* enriched in CRC
  - *Pseudomonas* statistically significantly enriched in adjacent non-cancerous tissues (p < 0.001)
  - In proximal tumors: *Prevotella*, *Pyramido-bacterium*, *Selenomonas*, *Peptostreptococcus* more abundant
  - In distal tumors: *Fusobacterium*, *Escherichia-Shigella*, *Leptotrichia* more abundant
  - PCA analysis: significant separation between proximal and distal tumors (PC1 39.07%, PC2 11.2%)
  - Driver-passenger model: CRC initiated by "driver" bacteria then replaced by "passenger" bacteria
