# Metal Homeostasis in Pathogenic Streptococci

## Key Claims

- Pathogenic *[[streptococcus-pneumoniae]]* species have evolved complex, often redundant metal transport systems for [[zinc]], [[manganese]], [[iron]], [[copper]], and [[nickel]] to survive the dynamic metal environments encountered during host colonization and infection
- Metal transport systems are critical virulence factors; mutants lacking key importers show attenuated disease in animal models
- The host exploits [[nutritional-immunity]] -- both metal starvation and metal intoxication -- to combat streptococcal infections
- [[nickel]] transport in Streptococci is largely uncharacterized, with only the UreMQO system in *S. salivarius* known

## Methods/Findings

This is a **review article** summarizing characterized metal transport systems across 12 pathogenic Streptococcus species.

### Zinc (Zn)
- **Import**: AdcABC/AdcAII/Lmb (ABC transporter), Pht proteins (histidine triad, Zn binding/storage)
- **Export**: CzcD (CDF pump), ZccE (P-type ATPase, unique to *S. mutans*)
- **Virulence**: Adc mutants show attenuated colonization in rat tooth, nasopharynx, meningitis, and skin infection models
- Host uses calprotectin (CP) to sequester Zn at infection sites; Zn intoxication in neutrophil phagosomes also attacks bacteria

### Manganese (Mn) and Iron (Fe)
- **Mn Import**: MtsABC/SloABC (ABC), MntH (NRAMP), TmpA (ZIP, in *S. sanguinis*)
- **Mn Export**: MntE (CDF pump), MgtA (P-type ATPase)
- **Fe Import**: PitABCD (ABC), FeoABC (ferrous iron), heme ABC transporters (Shp/Shr system), siderophore ABC transporters
- **Fe Export**: PmtA (P-type ATPase, in *S. pyogenes* and *S. suis*)
- Mn is the primary cofactor for SOD (superoxide dismutase), critical for [[oxidative-stress]] defense
- Cadmium disrupts Mn uptake/efflux in *S. pneumoniae*, indirectly increasing oxidative stress susceptibility

### Copper (Cu)
- **Export**: CopA (P-type ATPase), CopZ/CupA (chaperones)
- Regulated by CopY repressor; Cu intoxication inhibits biofilm formation and decreases virulence
- *S. agalactiae*, *S. pneumoniae*, *S. pyogenes* all have characterized Cu efflux systems

### Nickel (Ni)
- Ni bioavailability in host is limited; mammals do not synthesize Ni-dependent enzymes
- Nine known bacterial Ni-requiring enzymes including urease, Ni-superoxide dismutase, and [NiFe] hydrogenases
- Only known Ni transporter in Streptococci: UreMQO in *S. salivarius* (ABC-type, part of Ni-dependent urease operon)
- LarA (Ni-lactate racemase) homolog found in *S. pneumoniae*
- Role of Ni in Streptococcal pathogenesis remains largely unelucidated
