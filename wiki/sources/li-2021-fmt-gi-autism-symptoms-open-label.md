# Key Claims

- **FMT improved GI symptoms** (GSRS decreased 35% after 4 weeks) and **ASD symptoms** in 40 children
- **FMT shifted bacterial community** of ASD children toward that of typically developing controls
- **Serum neurotransmitter levels** (5-HT, dopamine, GABA) significantly changed after FMT
- **Eubacterium coprostanoligenes** pre-FMT positively correlated with high GSRS scores
- **Beneficial effects gradually lost** within weeks after FMT cessation

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Open-label clinical trial with 40 ASD children (ages 3-17) and 16 TD controls. 4-week FMT treatment + 8-week follow-up. Trial: ChiCTR1800014745.

**FMT protocol:**
- 27 children: oral freeze-dried capsules; 13 children: colonoscopic FMT
- Dose: ~2 x 10^14 CFU once weekly for 4 weeks
- No vancomycin or PPI pretreatment
- 2L GOLYTELY bowel prep before transplantation

**Clinical outcomes:**
- GSRS: decreased from 55.75 to 25.00 (P < 0.001) at week 4, reversed by week 12
- ABC: decreased from 73.2 to 62.9 (P < 0.0001) at week 4
- CARS: decreased 10% at end of follow-up, 6% remained decreased at week 12
- SRS: decreased at week 4 but reversed by week 12
- SAS (parental anxiety): decreased then returned to baseline by 8 weeks post-treatment

**Microbiota findings:**
- ASD children had higher protein consumption (P < 0.05) and more antibiotic use in first 3 years (P < 0.001)
- Higher food allergy incidence in ASD (60% vs. 6.25%, P < 0.001)
- FMT promoted colonization of donor microbes
- [[bile-acid-metabolism|Eubacterium coprostanoligenes]] decreased by FMT, associated with FMT response

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Neurotransmitter-metal axis**: 5-HT, dopamine, and GABA production requires iron, copper, zinc, and manganese-dependent enzymes; FMT-induced changes reflect microbial metal-metabolite restoration
- **Food allergy burden**: 60% food allergy rate in ASD suggests immune dysregulation involving metal-dependent mast cell degranulation and IgE-mediated pathways
- **Early antibiotic exposure**: Higher antibiotic use in ASD children disrupts metal-sequestering commensal colonization during critical developmental windows
- **Transient efficacy**: Loss of benefits after FMT cessation implies ongoing metal-ecological perturbation requiring sustained intervention
