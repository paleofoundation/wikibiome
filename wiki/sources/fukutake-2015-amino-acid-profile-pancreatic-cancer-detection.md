# Key Claims

- A multivariate index based on plasma free [[tryptophan|amino acids]] (PFAAs) can discriminate [[pancreatic-cancer]] from healthy controls with AUC of 0.89 (95% CI: 0.86-0.93) in training set and 0.86 (95% CI: 0.84-0.89) in validation set
- Plasma tryptophan and histidine were particularly low in PC patients, while serine was higher than healthy controls
- The PFAA index composed of serine, asparagine, isoleucine, alanine, histidine, and tryptophan was effective even for early-stage (stage 0-IIB) disease with AUC of 0.89 (95% CI: 0.83-0.95)
- The index could also distinguish PC from chronic pancreatitis (AUC 0.87, 95% CI: 0.80-0.93)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional multi-institution study; 360 PC patients, 28 chronic pancreatitis, 8,372 healthy controls; HPLC-ESI-MS measurement of 19 PFAAs
- **Training set**: 120 PC + 600 HC; validation set: 240 PC + 7,772 HC + 28 CP
- **Amino acid alterations**: 14 of 19 amino acids significantly different in PC vs HC; Ser significantly increased; Thr, Asn, Pro, Ala, Cit, Val, Met, Leu, Tyr, Phe, His, Trp, Lys, Arg significantly decreased
- **PFAA index model**: Logistic regression with variable selection; representative model used 6 amino acids (Ser, Asn, Ile, Ala, His, Trp)
- **Stage-specific AUCs**: Stage IIA 0.79, Stage IIB 0.85, Stage III 0.88, Stage IV 0.91; sensitivity at 95% specificity: 60.0% for all PC, 53.5% for stage 0-IIB
- **PC characteristics**: Mean age 66.6 years, BMI 21.0 (significantly lower than HC 22.8), 52% stage IV in validation set
