# Cek1 Regulates ß(1,3)-Glucan Exposure Through Calcineurin Effectors in Candida albicans

## Key Findings

- **Calcineurin as switchboard**: The calcineurin phosphatase pathway acts as a **bidirectional switch** for β-glucan exposure in *C. albicans*. The Cek1 MAPK pathway drives **unmasking** (glucan exposure → immune recognition); calcineurin responding to extracellular calcium drives **masking** (glucan concealment → immune evasion). The balance between these pathways determines the masking phenotype [[wagner-2022-cek1-betaglucan-calcineurin-candida]], animal-model.

- **Novel virulence factors**: Two new cell wall proteins identified — **Fgr41** and **Cwp419** — that are downstream effectors of the calcineurin regulon required for maintaining β-glucan masking. These are genuine virulence factors: Fgr41 loss reduces fungal burden in mouse systemic infection in a host immune system-dependent manner [[wagner-2022-cek1-betaglucan-calcineurin-candida]].

- **Caspofungin-calcineurin connection**: Calcineurin is necessary for caspofungin-induced β-glucan unmasking — explaining why echinocandin antifungals expose glucan to immune recognition. This connects the pharmacological mechanism of caspofungin to a specific signaling pathway [[wagner-2022-cek1-betaglucan-calcineurin-candida]].

- **Environmental signals converge**: Lactate, acidic pH, iron-replete conditions, hypoxia, and caspofungin all feed into the Cek1/calcineurin switchboard to regulate masking/unmasking. This means the **metabolic environment created by the microbiome** (lactate from Lactobacillus, pH from SCFA, iron from siderophores, oxygen from colonocyte butyrate oxidation) directly regulates Candida's immune visibility.
