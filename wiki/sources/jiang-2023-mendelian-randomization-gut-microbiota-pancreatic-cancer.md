# Key Claims

- Senegalimassilia (OR 0.635, p=0.049) was identified as protective against pancreatic cancer via Mendelian randomization
- Odoribacter (OR 1.899, p=0.011), Ruminiclostridium9 (OR 1.976, p=0.017), Ruminococcaceae UCG011 (OR 1.433, p=0.015), and Streptococcus (OR 1.712, p=0.025) were identified as causative risk factors for PC
- Sensitivity analyses (Cochran's Q, MR-Egger, MR-PRESSO) indicated no significant heterogeneity, horizontal pleiotropy, or reverse causality
- This is among the first MR-based studies to investigate the causal relationship between gut microbiota and pancreatic cancer

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Two-sample MR using 119 genus-level GM from MiBioGen consortium as exposure; FinnGen PC GWAS (1416 cases, 287,137 controls) as outcome
- **IV selection**: 1198 SNPs identified as IVs (P < 1x10^-5); exclusion of PC risk factor-associated SNPs via PhenoScanner
- **Five significant genera identified**: Senegalimassilia (protective), Odoribacter, Ruminiclostridium9, Ruminococcaceae UCG011, Streptococcus (risk-increasing)
- **About Senegalimassilia**: Known as protective factor against hypertension; anti-steatosis and cirrhotic properties may explain its protective role
- **Odoribacter**: Known for producing essential SCFAs; its association with increased PC risk suggests complex context-dependent effects
- **Streptococcus**: Identified as risk factor; similar discoveries apply to its role in different organs
