# Key Claims

- **92% of included reviews focused on ASD** (36/39 reviews); depression, anxiety, bipolar, OCD reviews are lacking
- **Clostridium clusters** consistently increased in ASD youth across observational reviews
- **Bifidobacterium** consistently decreased in ASD youth
- **Biotic supplementation** associated with ASD symptom improvement but evidence quality is low
- **Over half of reviews** scored low or critically low for methodological quality

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Umbrella review of 39 systematic reviews (23 observational, 16 interventional) from 7 databases through February 2023.

**Taxonomic evidence synthesis - ASD (observational):**
- Increased OTUs: [[clostridium]] clusters, [[candida-albicans]], [[dorea]], [[roseburia]], [[bacteroides-fragilis]], [[Oscillospira]], [[ruminococcus]], [[Barnesiella]], [[desulfovibrio]], [[lactobacillus]]
- Decreased OTUs: [[bifidobacterium]], [[prevotella]], [[dialister]], [[veillonella]], [[Escherichia]], [[fusobacterium]], [[streptococcus-pneumoniae]], [[coprococcus]]

**Taxonomic evidence synthesis - ADHD (observational):**
- Increased: [[bacteroides-fragilis]]
- Decreased: [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]]

**Interventional evidence:**
- 16 interventional reviews examined probiotics/prebiotics/synbiotics
- Biotic supplementation showed some ASD symptom improvement
- No interventional reviews for depression, anxiety, bipolar, or OCD

**Methodological concerns:**
- Significant heterogeneity in stool collection, sequencing platform, and analytical methods
- Only 3% used comprehensive search strategies
- High overlap of primary studies across reviews (CCA analysis performed)

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Clostridium-metal link**: Clostridial enrichment in ASD produces metal-dependent neurotoxic metabolites (propionic acid via cobalt-dependent pathways, p-cresol)
- **Bifidobacterium depletion**: Loss of Bifidobacterium reduces iron-sequestering, zinc-dependent beneficial metabolic pathways
- **Candida elevation**: Fungal overgrowth in ASD may reflect disrupted metal ecology (Candida has extensive heavy metal biosorption capacity)
- **Review-level evidence gap**: No umbrella reviews address metal-microbiome interactions in pediatric mental health, representing a critical research gap
