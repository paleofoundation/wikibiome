# Key Claims

- **Microbial metabolites are recognized by host** or further processed endogenously in T2D context
- **Main classes**: SCFAs, bile acids, branched-chain amino acids, TMAO, indoles, imidazole propionate
- **SCFAs (acetate, propionate, butyrate)** are mostly linked to beneficial effects on host health
- **Bile acids are metabolized by gut bacteria** into secondary forms that signal through FXR and TGR5
- **Gut-derived microbial metabolites may fuel** development of metabolic syndrome and T2D

# Methods & Findings

Review discussing mechanisms by which microbial metabolites influence T2D:

- Structural components (LPS, peptidoglycan) and metabolites both discussed
- Intestinal barrier as key interface between microbial metabolites and host metabolism
- Translational studies combining animal models with human interventions reviewed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Multiple microbial metabolite-producing enzymes are zinc-dependent (tryptophanase, butyrate synthase, bile salt hydrolase)
- **[[iron]]**: Iron-sulfur cluster enzymes are central to microbial metabolite biosynthesis pathways
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenoprotein-dependent detoxification protects host from harmful microbial metabolites (TMAO, LPS)
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Metal availability is the fundamental determinant of microbial metabolite production capacity
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: SCFA-mediated barrier maintenance and LPS-mediated barrier disruption are both metal-dependent processes
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Metabolite [[dysbiosis]] (not just taxonomic) reflects metal-dependent functional impairment
