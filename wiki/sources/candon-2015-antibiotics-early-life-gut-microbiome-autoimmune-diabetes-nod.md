# Key Claims

- **Wide spectrum antibiotics** (streptomycin, colistin, ampicillin) increased diabetes incidence in male NOD mice
- **Vancomycin alone** resulted in near-complete ablation of gut microbiota and increased T1D incidence
- **Antibiotic treatment increased Escherichia, Lactobacillus, and Sutterella** and decreased Clostridiales order and Lachnospiraceae/Prevotellaceae/Rikenellaceae families
- **Massive elimination of IL-17-producing CD4+ T cells** observed in vancomycin-treated mice lamina propria
- **Results prompt caution** in use of antibiotics in pregnant women and newborns

# Methods & Findings

NOD mouse model treated with antibiotics from conception through adult life:

- Oral administration of broad-spectrum antibiotics or vancomycin alone
- 16S V3 region sequencing of gut microbiota composition
- CD4+TCRab+ and TCRgd+ T cells measured in ileum and colon lamina propria
