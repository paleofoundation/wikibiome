# Fecal Microbiota Transplantation: A New Therapeutic Attempt from the Gut to the Brain

## Summary

This review examines [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]] (FMT) as a therapeutic strategy for [[parkinsons-disease]], synthesizing evidence from preclinical models and early clinical studies. FMT aims to restore gut microbial homeostasis by transferring a complete microbial community from a healthy donor, addressing the [[dysbiosis]] documented in PD.

## Key Findings

- FMT from healthy donors to PD animal models reduces motor deficits and neuroinflammation
- FMT restores SCFA-producing bacteria and normalizes fecal [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] levels
- Gut barrier integrity improves after FMT, with reduced intestinal inflammation markers
- Alpha-synuclein pathology in the enteric nervous system decreases after FMT in animal models
- Microglial activation in the substantia nigra is reduced post-FMT
- Early clinical case reports and small trials show improvements in constipation and motor symptoms
- Donor selection, delivery method (colonoscopy vs. capsule), and dosing frequency remain to be optimized

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

[[Fecal-microbiota-transplant]] is the most comprehensive microbiome intervention for the metal-microbiome axis in [[parkinsons-disease]]. By restoring an entire microbial ecosystem, FMT can simultaneously rebuild [[butyrate]]-producing communities (restoring HIF-2alpha-mediated [[iron]] regulation), reestablish colonization resistance against metal-scavenging pathogens (Enterobacteriaceae), and normalize bile acid metabolism that influences [[iron]] absorption. The restoration of the mucin layer by beneficial *Bacteroides* and *Akkermansia* at appropriate levels could restore the physical barrier against dietary [[nickel]], [[lead]], and [[manganese]] absorption. FMT represents a "reset" of the entire gut-metal-microbiome axis that targeted [[probiotics]] cannot achieve.

## Limitations

- Most evidence from animal models; human PD trials very limited
- Safety concerns including transmission of infections and antibiotic resistance genes
- Long-term engraftment of donor microbiota is not guaranteed
- Regulatory and standardization challenges for FMT in PD
