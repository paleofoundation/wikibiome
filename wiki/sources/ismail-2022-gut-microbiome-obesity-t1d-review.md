# Key Claims

- **T1D risk and progression associated with gut bacterial imbalances** -- lower gram-positive to gram-negative ratios in children with T1D or islet antibody positivity
- **Obesity prevalence in T1D rising** (up to 46.1% in some populations), associated with worse outcomes
- **Specific gut microbiome changes** include increased virulence factors, elevated phage/prophage, higher amplitude stress responses
- **Elevated Firmicutes:Bacteroidetes ratio** associated with increased body fat and obesity in both T1D and general population

# Methods & Findings

Review examining gut microbiome changes in T1D with and without obesity:

- Obesity in T1D associated with reduced insulin sensitivity and higher complication risk
- Gut microbiome differences between obese and lean T1D patients parallel general population differences
- Potential for [[probiotics]], prebiotics, and dietary interventions to modify disease and improve outcomes

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc deficiency is common in both T1D and obesity -- dual deficiency may synergistically worsen [[dysbiosis]] and [[intestinal-permeability]]
- **[[iron]]**: Iron overload in obese T1D patients may drive pathogenic microbiome shifts
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Obesity-associated inflammation alters metal absorption and distribution, creating a metal-dysbiotic feedback loop
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Dual immune dysregulation in obese T1D reflects metal-dependent immune pathway dysfunction
- **[[probiotics]]**: Probiotic interventions targeting both obesity and T1D-associated [[dysbiosis]] should consider metal cofactor supplementation
