## Key Findings

- **Deferiprone reduced intracellular [[iron]] by 33.1%** and corresponding bacterial load by 78% in vitro, demonstrating direct linkage between iron availability and pathogen survival
- **In vivo fish mortality reduced by 34.9%** with deferiprone treatment, confirming therapeutic efficacy of pharmacological iron chelation
- **"Assisted nutritional immunity"** — the concept that chelation amplifies the host's own iron-withholding defense rather than directly killing bacteria — validated as a host-directed therapeutic strategy

## Relevance to WikiBiome

Key proof-of-concept for Primitive 2 (nutritional immunity as interpretive constraint) applied therapeutically. Demonstrates that pharmacological chelation mimics and enhances the host's innate metal-sequestration response. Informs [[nutritional-immunity]] and [[iron]] entity pages.

## Relevance to Cureva

Deferiprone as a candidate intervention for conditions where intracellular iron-dependent pathogens drive disease. The "assisted nutritional immunity" framework is directly applicable to human conditions with elevated hepcidin and calprotectin — chelation supports, rather than replaces, host defense.
