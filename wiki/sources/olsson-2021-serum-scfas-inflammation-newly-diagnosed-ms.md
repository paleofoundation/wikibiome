# Serum Short-Chain Fatty Acids and Associations With Inflammation in Newly Diagnosed Patients With Multiple Sclerosis and Healthy Controls

## Summary

This study analyzed serum levels of [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] and performed targeted metabolomics in 58 newly diagnosed RRMS patients (before first DMT) and 50 healthy controls from Denmark. It uniquely examines SCFAs in treatment-naive patients with emphasis on kynurenine pathway metabolites.

## Key Findings

### SCFA Levels
- Total SCFAs were lower in MS patients (453 micromol/L) vs HCs (510 micromol/L, p=0.022)
- Acetate was the only individual SCFA significantly lower in MS (402 micromol/L vs 510 micromol/L, p=0.021)
- Ratios of acetate/butyrate and acetate/(propionate+butyrate) were significantly lower in MS
- Higher proportion of butyrate found in subjects taking [[probiotics]]

### SCFA-Inflammation Correlations
- In HCs: acetate negatively correlated with pro-inflammatory *IFNG* (rho=-0.524, q<0.01)
- In MS: butyrate and valerate positively correlated with *IFNG* (rho=0.374, p=0.006) and *TNF* (rho=0.370, p=0.006)
- This suggests complex bidirectional regulatory properties of SCFAs
- FFAR2 and HCAR2 (SCFA receptors) strongly correlated with *IL1B* in both groups

### Kynurenine Pathway Metabolites
- Kynurenic acid (KYNA) levels were lower in MS (0.017 vs 0.019 micromol/L, p=0.009)
- 3-hydroxyanthranilic acid (3HAA) levels were higher in MS (0.18 vs 0.16 micromol/L, p=0.040)
- 3HAA correlated positively with butyrate in both groups (rho=0.556 and 0.517, q<0.001)
- Valine and L-methionine were lower in MS
- Creatine, pantothenic acid, and D-glucuronic acid were higher in MS

### 12-Month Follow-Up
- 38% of patients developed new/enlarging T2 lesions at 12 months
- Patients with active disease had nominally higher propionate (13.5%, CI 2-26%) and valerate (18%, CI 11-35%) levels at baseline

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This study is important for the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] framework because:

1. **Treatment-naive patients** eliminate confounding by DMTs, showing that SCFA depletion exists from disease onset, consistent with pre-existing [[dysbiosis]] (potentially metal-driven)
2. **Kynurenine pathway alteration** connects to [[indoles]] and AHR signaling; decreased KYNA (neuroprotective) and increased 3HAA (neurotoxic) align with metal disruption of tryptophan-metabolizing gut bacteria
3. **Bidirectional SCFA-inflammation relationship** suggests that once [[inflammation]] is established, it may alter SCFA metabolism, creating the vicious cycle central to the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] hypothesis
4. **Methionine depletion** in MS is notable because methionine is crucial for methylation and metal detoxification pathways

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional design at baseline
- Serum SCFAs may not directly reflect gut production
- Relatively small cohort
- After FDR correction, most individual SCFA differences became non-significant
