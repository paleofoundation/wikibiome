## Key Claims

- All 10 commercial baby food products from Houston, TX tested positive for heavy metals (Al, Cr, Ni, Zn, As, Cd, Pb) at varying concentrations
- [[aluminum]] and [[zinc]] had the highest concentrations across products; Al was highest in samples S2 (4.09 ug/g) and S3 (2.50 ug/g), which were stages 2 and 3 baby food purees
- [[aluminum]] (4.09 ug/g and 2.50 ug/g) and [[zinc]] (33.5 ug/g and 69.5 ug/g and 30.2 ug/g) exceeded recommended safe levels per ATSDR minimal risk levels
- Heavy metal presence was independent of brand (detected in all 7 brands tested)
- No significant correlation between packaging materials and metal concentrations, indicating contamination originates from food type and soil, not packaging
- Mixed model analysis found significant differences between food types (F=2.75, p=0.035) with mean concentration 0.96 ug/g
- The study supports the 2021 FDA "Closer to Zero" action plan for reducing metals in baby food

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: 10 baby food products from 7 brands purchased in Houston, TX; included purees, cereals, syrup, beef/gravy, fruit punch, and 2 infant formulas; stages spanning 0-12 months to 2-6+ years
- **Analysis**: QQQ-ICP-MS (UltraWAVE method) after SRC microwave digestion at 1500W; analyzed for Al, Cr, As, Cd, Zn, Ni, Pb; triplicate measurements
- **Key concentrations (Table 3)**: Al ranged 0.085-4.089 ug/g; Zn 0.014-69.535 ug/g; Ni 0.019-0.414 ug/g; As 0.001-0.012 ug/g; Cr 0.005-0.148 ug/g; Cd 0.0-0.004 ug/g; Pb 0.0-0.008 ug/g
- **Highest contamination**: Sweet potato/turkey puree (S1) and banana/raspberry fruities (S2) had among the highest overall metal loads
- **ANOVA (Table 4)**: Significant main effects for all individual metals (p<0.05): Pb (p=0.0378), Cd (p=0.0379), As (p=0.0411), Cr (p=0.0420), Ni (p=0.0492), Zn (p=0.0516)
- **Packaging analysis (Table 5)**: Regression analysis showed no significant relationship between metal concentrations and packaging type (p>0.05 for all)
- **Risk comparison**: Used ATSDR Minimal Risk Levels (MRLs) as benchmarks
