# Metallomic and Untargeted Metabolomic Signatures of Human Milk from SARS-CoV-2 Positive Mothers

Arias-Borrego et al. (2022). *Molecular Nutrition and Food Research* 66:2200071.

## Overview

This study investigates the impact of maternal SARS-CoV-2 infection on the elemental and metabolomic profile of human milk (HM). An observational study compared HM from 35 COVID-19 positive mothers to 20 prepandemic controls. The study found significant alterations in 9 of 14 elements and 18 metabolites, with SARS-CoV-2 infection reducing concentrations of most essential elements while altering amino acid, fatty acid, and tryptophan metabolism pathways.

## Key Findings

### Metallomic Profile Alterations
- **Most elements decreased** in COVID-19 mothers compared to prepandemic controls
- Significantly lower concentrations of: Se, Ni, Fe, Cu, V, and Al (p < 0.05 FDR-corrected)
- Zinc was the notable exception: **Zn increased** 1.7-fold (p = 0.0001) in COVID-19 HM
- Some minor metals increased: Tl (1.4-fold, p = 0.008) and As (1.3-fold, p = 0.031)
- The **Cu/Zn ratio** was significantly reduced in COVID-19 HM (p = 0.001), contrasting with serum data where Cu/Zn ratio increases with COVID-19 severity
- Se/Zn ratio also significantly reduced (p = 0.000)

### Metabolomic Alterations
- 18 individual metabolites significantly different between COVID-19 and controls
- **Down-regulated** in COVID-19 HM: glycine, phenylalanine, tyrosine, proline, valine, isoleucine, uric acid, tocopherol, cholesterol
- **Up-regulated**: inositol, phosphoric acid, pantothenic acid, glycerol, myristic acid, and others
- Most altered pathways: aminoacyl-tRNA biosynthesis, phenylalanine/tyrosine/tryptophan biosynthesis, linoleic acid metabolism

### Metal-Metabolite-Antibody Associations
- Two groups of metals showed opposite behavior relative to SARS-CoV-2 antibodies:
 - **Group I** (Cd, V, Ni, Se, Cu, Al, Fe): positively correlated with tyrosine, uric acid, tocopherol, cholesterol, proline, valine, phenylalanine, glycine, isoleucine
 - **Group II** (As, Zn, Tl, Cr, Co, Mn): negatively correlated with the same metabolites
- Higher anti-SARS-CoV-2 IgA levels associated with lower Se and Co, and higher IgG with lower Ni
- Cu levels not significantly associated with immunoglobulin levels (p > 0.05)

### Toxic Element Identification
- Tl (thallium), Ni, V, Al, and As identified as toxic elements present in COVID-19 HM for the first time
- Tl detected at very low concentrations in all samples; found naturally in environment and can contaminate water/food

### Lactational Stage Effects
- COVID-19 influenced elemental profile at different lactational stages
- No differences between symptomatic vs asymptomatic COVID-19 mothers for most elements

## Methods

- **Study design**: Observational, comparing COVID-19 HM (n = 35) to prepandemic controls (n = 20)
- **COVID-19 diagnosis**: 28 PCR-confirmed, 6 seropositive (IgG+)
- **Metallomic analysis**: ICP-MS for 14 elements
- **Metabolomic analysis**: GC-MS untargeted metabolomics
- **Antibody analysis**: IgA, IgG, IgM against SARS-CoV-2 spike protein
- **Statistics**: PCA, OPLS-DA, pathway topology analysis

## Relevance to Wiki

This is the first study to comprehensively characterize both the metallomic and metabolomic profile of human milk from SARS-CoV-2 infected mothers. The finding that most essential elements are depleted in COVID-19 HM -- with the notable exception of zinc -- has significant implications for infant nutrition and development. The inverse Cu/Zn ratio behavior in milk vs serum suggests tissue-specific metal regulation during infection. The identification of toxic elements (Tl, As) in COVID-19 HM raises concerns about infant exposure.
