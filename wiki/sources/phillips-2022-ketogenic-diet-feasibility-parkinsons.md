# A Mixed Methods Feasibility Study of a Ketogenic Diet as Treatment for Parkinson's Disease

## Summary

This mixed-methods feasibility study evaluates a ketogenic diet as a therapeutic intervention for [[parkinsons-disease]]. It assesses both the practicality of implementing a ketogenic diet in PD patients and its effects on motor and non-motor symptoms.

## Key Findings

- The ketogenic diet is feasible in PD patients, though adherence requires significant support
- Non-motor symptoms showed significant improvement, particularly in cognitive and urinary domains
- Motor symptoms (UPDRS Part III) improved in the ketogenic diet group
- Blood beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) levels confirmed ketosis in adherent participants
- BHB serves as an alternative fuel for neurons with impaired glucose metabolism
- Quality of life measures improved during the ketogenic period
- Some participants experienced weight loss as a side effect

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

The ketogenic diet's effects on PD may partly operate through the metal-microbiome axis. Beta-hydroxybutyrate, the primary ketone body, inhibits HDACs similarly to [[butyrate]], potentially mimicking the epigenetic effects of microbially-produced [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] that are depleted in PD [[dysbiosis]]. BHB also has anti-inflammatory effects that could reduce [[hepcidin]]-driven [[iron]] trapping in microglia. However, the ketogenic diet dramatically reduces fiber intake, which could worsen SCFA production and constipation -- a potential concern for the metal-microbiome axis. The diet's effects on gut microbiome composition in PD need to be studied, as ketogenic diets typically shift microbiome composition in ways that may either benefit or harm the PD gut. The interplay between BHB-mediated neuroprotection and fiber depletion-mediated [[iron]] dysregulation deserves careful investigation.

## Limitations

- Small feasibility study without long-term follow-up
- Adherence to ketogenic diet is challenging, especially in PD patients
- Weight loss may be concerning in already underweight PD patients
- Effects on gut microbiome not directly assessed
- Fiber depletion on ketogenic diet could worsen constipation and dysbiosis
