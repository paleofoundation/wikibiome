# Key Claims

- Multi-omics approaches (metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, metaproteomics, metabolomics) are essential for understanding heavy metal-tolerant microbial communities and their bioremediation potential
- 16S rRNA gene sequencing of nickel-contaminated sites in Slovakia revealed phyla Firmicutes and Proteobacteria dominated by Cyanobacteria, with Dyella, Methylobacterium, Burkholderia, and Staphylinidae enriched
- Metatranscriptomics reveals that 83% of microbes showed changes in relative abundance at RNA level in response to arsenic stress, with genes involved in oxidative stress, transport, and resistance upregulated
- Metaproteomics enables identification of actual protein function and abundance in HM-contaminated environments beyond what DNA/RNA abundance alone can predict
- [[metabolomics]] of HM-contaminated soils and HM-tolerant plants have been instrumental in identification of metal-resistant metabolites

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Review covering omics technologies for HM-tolerant microbiome characterization
- **Table 1**: 15 studies cataloging plant species, heavy metal type, omics technique, associated microbes, and major findings (e.g., Piformospora indica aiding Cd tolerance in rice via proteomics; Pseudomonas fluorescens promoting Cd phytoremediation in Sedum alfredii via transcriptomics)
- **Metagenomics approaches**: Targeted amplicon (16S/18S/ITS) and whole-genome shotgun; culture-independent identification of metal tolerance genes and operons
- **Metatranscriptomics**: Reveals active gene expression under HM stress vs. mere gene presence; identified upregulation of siderophore production, ACC deaminase, and catalase genes
- **Metaproteomics**: Bacillus halotolerant and Arthrobacter strains showed differential protein expression under Cd and Zn stress
- **Metabolomics applications**: Non-targeted metabolomics of Bacillus subtilis inoculation in alfalfa showed regulated amino acids, fatty acids, carbohydrates, and flavonoids under Cd stress
- **Plant-microbe phytoremediation**: Dual co-cultivation of Agrobacterium tumefaciens and Sinorhizobium meliloti with alfa alfa increased nitrogen fixation and metal uptake
