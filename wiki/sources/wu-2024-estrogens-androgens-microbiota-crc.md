# Wu 2024 -- Direct and Indirect Effects of Sex Hormones and Microbiota on CRC

## Key Claims

- Estrogen at genetic and epigenetic levels directly inhibits CRC cell proliferation by enhancing DNA mismatch repair, regulating miRNAs, blocking the cell cycle, and modulating ion channels; however, estradiol's activation of GPER promotes oncogene expression
- Androgen contributes to epigenetic dysregulation and CRC progression via nuclear receptors while inducing apoptosis through membrane receptors
- Estrogen in the tumor microenvironment reverses immunosuppression, increases gut microbiome diversity and commensal bacteria abundance, and decreases pathogen enrichment; androgen disrupts intestinal microecology and diminishes immunotherapy efficacy
- The gut microbiota regulates estrogen metabolism through the 'estrobolome' -- bacterial genes encoding beta-glucuronidase that deconjugate estrogens; ERbeta knockout leads to decreased gut microbiota diversity and increased pathogen enrichment
- Bidirectional relationship: sex hormones shape microbiota composition, while microbiota influences hormone levels through beta-glucuronidase, STS, HSD, and steroid-17,20-desmolase enzymes

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Comprehensive review
- **Box 1**: Summary of bidirectional sex hormone-microbiota interactions
- **Table 1 (in companion paper)**: Detailed sex hormone-based differences in CRC including E2, ERbeta, progesterone, testosterone effects across models
- **Key insight**: The gradual loss of ERbeta during CRC development exacerbates microbiota diversity decrease and pathogen enrichment
