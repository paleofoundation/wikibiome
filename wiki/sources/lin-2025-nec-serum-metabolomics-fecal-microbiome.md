## Key Findings

- Prospective study at Third Affiliated Hospital of Guangzhou Medical University (Oct 2022–Oct 2023). Bell stage II-III NEC infants (n=7) with gestational-age-matched non-NEC controls (n=7). Serum (for LC-MS/MS untargeted metabolomics) and stool (for shotgun metagenomics) collected in acute (NEC-D) and recovery (NEC-R, 5–6 days post-resumed feeding) phases.
- Alpha diversity (Chao1) was **significantly reduced in NEC-D vs both non-NEC (p=4.88e-4) and NEC-R (p=2.44e-4)** — diversity recovers during recovery phase.
- LEfSe (LDA >2): NEC-D enriched for **Enterobacterales, Enterobacteriaceae, Escherichia coli, Enterobacter, Citrobacter**. Non-NEC enriched for **Bacillales, Staphylococcaceae, Staphylococcus, S. paracasei (L. paracasei), S. epidermidis, S. aureus, S. haemolyticus, Lactobacillus rhamnosus**.
- KEGG functional analysis: NEC-D had reduced pathways for folding/sorting/degradation, pyruvate metabolism, fatty acid metabolism, valine/leucine/isoleucine degradation, thiamine/tryptophan/biotin metabolism; increased carbohydrate digestion, phosphonate/phosphinate metabolism, pancreatic secretion, bacterial chemotaxis, cell motility, pentose & glucuronate interconversions.
- Serum metabolomics (OPLS-DA, VIP ranking): 15 metabolites discriminated NEC-D from non-NEC. **Decreased in NEC-D: ornithine, DL-arginine, diethanolamine, nicotinamide, L-threonine, leucine, D-proline, taurodeoxycholic acid, glycocholic acid, chenodeoxycholic acid.** Increased: atorvastatin, cortisol 21-sulfate, 2,5-dimethoxycinnamic acid.
- Multi-omics correlation: **D-proline** and **ornithine** showed strong positive correlations with *Lactobacillus rhamnosus, L. paracasei, S. epidermidis*, and *S. aureus* — mapping a lost metabolite-producer relationship. N,N-dimethylaniline positively correlated with *E. coli*.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Strengthens [[necrotizing-enterocolitis]] disease entity with multi-omics evidence of reduced amino acid (ornithine, arginine, proline) and bile acid metabolism in NEC acute phase.
- Supports [[bile-acid-metabolism]] concept page: reduced secondary and primary bile acids in serum indicates lost microbial deconjugation/dihydroxylation capacity with NEC dysbiosis.
- Reinforces [[escherichia-coli]] entity as the NEC-expanded pathogen and [[lactobacillus]] / [[staphylococcus-aureus|staphylococcus]] as contextual commensals lost in NEC.
- Adds ornithine/arginine/urea-cycle data to the nutritional immunity / host metabolic crosstalk layer of the NEC narrative.

## Relevance to Cureva

- Signature: reinforces NEC 5-layer signature with a metabolomic dimension — ornithine, arginine, proline, and bile acid depletion as biomarker candidates.
- Intervention hypothesis: arginine/ornithine/proline supplementation or bile-acid-restorative strategies merit evaluation (with mechanism-via-f links).
- Diagnostic application: serum metabolite panel (ornithine + bile acids) as early NEC biomarker.

## Tensions / Open Questions

- Very small sample (n=7 per group). Serum metabolites may reflect systemic NEC pathology rather than the gut-specific source.
- Why are Staphylococcus species higher in non-NEC — in preterm NICU context, S. haemolyticus/epidermidis are often nosocomial; context-dependent.
