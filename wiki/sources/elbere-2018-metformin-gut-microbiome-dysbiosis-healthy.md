# Association of Metformin Administration with Gut Microbiome Dysbiosis in Healthy Volunteers

## Summary

Exploratory longitudinal study investigating the short-term effect of metformin (850 mg twice daily for 7 days) on the gut microbiome of 18 healthy volunteers. Stool samples collected at three time points: baseline (M0), 24 hours (M24h), and 7 days (M7d) after first intake.

## Key Findings

### Metformin Reduces Gut Diversity
- Metformin significantly reduced inner diversity of gut microbiota immediately (after 2-3 doses)
- After 7 days, diversity slightly increased but remained significantly lower than baseline
- Gut microbiome communities highly individual-specific (PERMANOVA R2 = 0.74, p = 0.001)
- Merging M24h and M7d samples showed significant difference from baseline (PERMANOVA R2 = 0.019, p = 0.036)

### Opportunistic Pathogen Increase
- Increase in relative abundance of *Escherichia-Shigella* spp. (common gut opportunistic pathogen) within 24 hours
- Significant decrease in *Peptostreptococcaceae* and *Clostridiaceae_1* families after 1 week
- Severity of GI side effects correlated with microbial changes

### GI Side Effects
- 9/18 participants experienced severe side effects (loose stools, diarrhea, vomiting)
- 6/18 had mild side effects (meteorism, stomach ache, nausea)
- 3/18 had no side effects
- Average time of onset for severe effects: day 3 of treatment

### Study Design Limitations
- Small sample size (n=18), no placebo control arm
- Short duration (7 days)
- Individual variation dominated overall microbiome structure

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Metformin-microbiome interaction** - [[metformin]] causes immediate dysbiosis even in healthy individuals, relevant to understanding its cardiovascular protective effects
2. **Opportunistic pathogen expansion** - Rapid increase in *Escherichia-Shigella* could increase endotoxemia risk in [[cardiovascular-disease]] patients
3. **Drug-induced dysbiosis** - Metformin's microbiome effects may partially explain both its therapeutic benefits and GI side effects
