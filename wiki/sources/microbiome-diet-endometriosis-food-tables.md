# Microbiome Diet for Endometriosis - Food Tables

## Key Claims

- **Document type**: Supplementary data tables (7 pages), not a research paper. Contains two comprehensive food categorization tables for a dietary intervention related to endometriosis.
- **Table 1 - "Categorized Inclusion Food Table"**: Lists foods to INCLUDE in the endometriosis diet, with columns indicating whether each food is Low Nickel, Low Zinc, Low Iron, Low Cadmium, Low Lead, and whether to "Include in Diet" (all marked "Yes")
- **Table 2 - "Exclude in Endometriosis Diet"**: Lists foods to EXCLUDE, with the same metal columns but "Include in Diet" marked "No"
- Foods are categorized by: Beverages, Breads and Cereals, Condiments, Dairy, Desserts, Fruits, Grains, Herbs and Spices, Juices, Legumes, Meats, Nuts and Seeds, Oils and Fats, Prebiotics, Protein Powders, Seafood, Snacks, Spices, Vegetables
- **Included foods** (low in all metals): citrus fruits, filtered coffee/tea, white rice, refined pasta, sourdough bread, plain dairy, lean meats (chicken, turkey), olive oil, fresh vegetables (lettuce, carrots, bell peppers, tomatoes, zucchini)
- **Excluded foods** (higher in one or more metals): whole grains, oats, buckwheat, legumes (lentils, peas), most nuts and seeds, dark chocolate, seafood (shellfish, mussels), red/processed meats (beef, lamb, pork), soy products, fortified cereals, dried fruits, certain spices, canned beverages

## Methods/Findings

- **Format**: Data tables only; no methodology, abstract, or discussion text provided
- The tables appear to cross-reference multiple metal content databases to identify foods simultaneously low in [[nickel]], [[zinc]], [[iron]], [[cadmium]], and [[lead]]
- Some foods marked "No" for Low Nickel but "Yes" for other metals (e.g., beef is No for Low Nickel, but Yes for Low Zinc, Iron, Cadmium, Lead)
- Canned/processed items consistently excluded
- The diet appears to target reducing metal intake broadly as part of an anti-inflammatory or microbiome-supportive approach to endometriosis management
