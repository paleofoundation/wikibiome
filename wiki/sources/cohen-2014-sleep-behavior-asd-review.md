# Overview

Review examining the nature of **sleep difficulties in ASD** and their relationship to challenging daytime behaviors, with focus on children with **low-functioning autism** who are often underrepresented in sleep research. Sleep problems affect **50-80% of ASD children** vs 25-40% in typically developing children.

# Key Findings

## Sleep Disturbances in ASD

- **Insomnia**: most commonly reported (increased sleep latency, night awakenings, poor sleep efficiency)
- **Parasomnias**: nightmares, sleep walking, complex movements -- higher rates in ASD
- **Circadian rhythm disorders**: elevated daytime and lower nocturnal melatonin, misalignment with light/dark cycles
- Sleep problems correlate with **ASD severity, sensory sensitivity, and GI issues**

## Sleep-Behavior Relationships

- Sleep disturbance is highest predictor of challenging behaviors in ASD
- Poor sleep exacerbates: **stereotypy, hyperactivity, aggression, ADHD symptoms, mood variability**
- Children who slept fewer hours per night: lower IQ, adaptive functioning, socialization, and communication skills
- Studies show effect sizes ranging from 0.09 to 0.75 (small to large)

## Melatonin Abnormalities

- Subgroup of ASD individuals shows dysregulated circadian rhythms
- Elevated daytime melatonin, lower nocturnal melatonin compared to controls
- Some evidence for "free-running" sleep not entrained to 24-hour cycle

## Treatment

- Behavioral interventions: bedtime routines, minimizing screen time, reducing emotional stimulation
- Melatonin supplementation: influenced by type of sleep disturbance and environmental factors
- Light therapy for circadian disruption
- Pharmacotherapy used cautiously for comorbid psychiatric conditions
