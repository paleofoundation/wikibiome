# Key Claims

- **Serum copper was significantly elevated** in PCOS patients (17.27 mcmol/L) compared to non-PCOS tubal infertility controls (15.4 mcmol/L, p < 0.001) in a large retrospective cohort
- Serum **iron was also significantly elevated** in PCOS (16.4 vs 15 mcmol/L, p = 0.023); no significant differences in Zn, Mg, or P
- Cu positively correlated with **BMI** (r = 0.198, p = 0.004) and **triglycerides (TG)** (r = 0.214, p = 0.002) in PCOS
- Cu negatively correlated with **HDL** in non-PCOS (r = -0.184, p < 0.001)
- In the non-PCOS group, Cu correlated with FG (r = 0.131, p = 0.002), TG (r = 0.277, p < 0.001), TC (r = 0.163, p < 0.001), and LDL (r = 0.240, p < 0.001)
- **No significant association** between serum copper levels and IVF outcomes (oocyte recovery rate, MII oocyte rate, fertilization rate, blastocyst formation, embryo quality) after adjusting for confounders
- Cu is a valuable indicator reflecting the **metabolic status** of infertility patients, particularly [[lipid metabolism]], but not a direct predictor of IVF success

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Retrospective 5-year study, January 2018 -- December 2022, Affiliated Suzhou Hospital of Nanjing Medical University, China
- **Participants**: 766 women undergoing first IVF cycle -- 266 with PCOS and 560 with tubal infertility (no-PCOS controls), aged up to 38
- **Diagnosis**: Modified Rotterdam Criteria
- **Measurements**: Cu, Zn, Fe, P, Mg via colorimetric/chelation assays; hormonal profiles; lipid panels; IVF outcomes
- **Statistical methods**: Mann-Whitney U test; Spearman correlations; linear trend analysis across Cu quartiles; odds ratios for IVF outcomes by Cu quartile

 - PCOS group: Higher total Gn dose, more retrieved oocytes, higher MII oocytes, but lower oocyte recovery rate (71.43% vs 79.17%, p < 0.001) and lower normal fertilization rate (77.78% vs 80%, p = 0.005)
 - Cu correlated with total Gn dose in both groups but not with other IVF parameters after adjustment
- **Limitations**: Single-center; retrospective; serum only (no follicular fluid or urine Cu); Chinese population
