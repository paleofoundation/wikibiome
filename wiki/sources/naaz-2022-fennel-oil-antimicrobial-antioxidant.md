## Key Findings

- **Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) oil composition**: trans-anethole dominant (78.4-86.1%); fenchone 6.65-8.95%; methyl chavicol 2.32-2.54%.
- **Broad-spectrum antibacterial activity** comparable to ampicillin:
  - *E. coli* — 14 mm zone (ampicillin 19 mm)
  - *P. aeruginosa* — 12 mm (ampicillin 11 mm — fennel superior)
  - *S. aureus* — 14 mm (ampicillin 12 mm — fennel superior)
  - *B. subtilis* — 17 mm (ampicillin 8 mm — fennel substantially superior)
- **Strong antioxidant activity**: DPPH IC50 0.37-0.43 mg/mL; FRAP and metal-chelating capacity present. TBARS lipid peroxidation IC50 0.07-0.09 mg/mL.
- **Elicitation enhances oil yield and bioactivity**: irradiated sodium alginate (ISA 80 mg/L) foliar spray increased fennel EO yield from 0.9 to 1.52 mL per plant; improved antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.

## Methods

Fennel plants grown in 1:4 earthen:soil ratio; ISA applied as foliar spray at 0, 40, 80 mg/L. Hydrodistilled EO analyzed by GC-FID. Antibacterial testing via disc diffusion against S. aureus, P. aeruginosa, B. subtilis, E. coli (HiMedia). Antioxidant assays: DPPH (517 nm), FRAP/ferrous ion chelating (562 nm), TBARS lipid peroxidation.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Zone-diameter data only; no MIC values reported here.
- P. aeruginosa and S. aureus outperformance of ampicillin may reflect test-strain resistance profiles rather than universal superiority.

## Open Questions

- Trans-anethole vs methyl chavicol individual contribution to the antimicrobial activity.
- In vivo efficacy and dose-response.
