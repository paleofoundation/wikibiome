# Key Claims

- A personalized whole-food ketogenic diet (KMT ratio 1:5:1) achieved complete remission of OCD (Y-BOCS 0) within 12 weeks and clinical remission of UC (PMS 0, UC-PRO 0) within 3 weeks in a single patient
- Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) directly inhibits TLR4 signaling, modulates SCFA production, suppresses NLRP3 inflammasome activation, and reduces microglial neuroinflammation
- The bidirectional inflammatory cascade linking gut [[dysbiosis]], UC, and neuroinflammation-driven OCD may share common inflammatory pathways targetable by ketogenic intervention
- Metabolic improvements included 9.4% body weight loss, decreased body fat percentage, clearing of psoriatic plaques, and normalization of thyroid function

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Single case report with 12-week follow-up
- **Patient**: 37-year-old woman with 25-year OCD, 5-year moderately severe UC, psoriasis, Hashimoto's thyroiditis, obesity
- **Diet**: Personalized KMT -- 75% fat, 20% protein, 5% carbohydrate; whole foods, no seed oils/sweeteners; animal-based proteins
- **UC remission**: Within 3 weeks; all cramping and bloody diarrhea ceased; stool normalized
- **OCD remission**: FOCI 0 at 9 weeks; Y-BOCS 0 at 12 weeks; major depression remitted at 9 weeks
- **Metabolic**: Weight decreased 12.2%; body fat 45.3% to 42.1%; visceral fat decreased; psoriasis cleared in 8 weeks
- **Monitoring**: Capillary BHB oscillated ~0.8 mmol/L; GKI mostly <=6
