# Key Claims

- Heavy metal tolerance in plants requires complex mechanisms at molecular, biochemical, physiological, cellular, tissue, and whole plant levels that can be characterized through multi-omics approaches
- [[metabolomics]] reveals perturbations in lipid metabolism, amino acid metabolism, and TCA cycle under heavy metal stress, with specific metabolite signatures for different metals
- Heavy metals cause [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]] through ROS generation (superoxide, H2O2, hydroxyl radicals), and antioxidant defense enzymes (SOD, CAT, APX) are key tolerance markers
- Hyperaccumulator plants (metallophytes) employ constitutive overexpression of metal transporters (ZIP, Nramp, CDF, MATE families) and enhanced xylem loading
- Cd exposure causes inhibition of photosynthesis, decreased RuBISCO activity, and disruption of DNA repair via altered p53 and p21 expression
- Heavy metals can induce epigenetic modifications including DNA methylation changes, histone modifications, and altered microRNA expression

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of omics approaches to plant heavy metal tolerance
- **Ionomics**: ZIP (zinc-regulated transporter), Nramp, CDF, and MATE family transporters mediate metal uptake and sequestration; Thlaspi caerulescens as model hyperaccumulator
- **Transcriptomics**: As exposure induces GSH synthetase, arsenate reductase, and CDC25 phosphatase genes; Cd upregulates phytochelatin synthase and metallothionein genes
- **Proteomics**: Cd exposure in rice showed 72 differentially expressed proteins; stress-responsive proteins include HSPs, chaperonins, and antioxidant enzymes
- **Metabolomics**: Cd exposure increases proline, glutathione, organic acids; Pb alters amino acid profiles; As disrupts sulfur amino acid metabolism
- **ROS defense**: SOD, CAT, APX, GR, MDHAR, DHAR constitute the enzymatic antioxidant system against HM-induced oxidative damage (Figure 3)
- **Epigenetics**: HM exposure alters DNA methylation patterns, potentially heritable across generations
