# Streptococcus thermophilus ST285 Alters Pro-Inflammatory to Anti-Inflammatory Cytokine Secretion against Multiple Sclerosis Peptide in Mice

## Key Claims

- *[[Streptococcus thermophilus]]* strain ST285 shifts the immune response from pro-inflammatory Th1 to anti-inflammatory Th2 phenotype in MBP83-99-immunized SJL/J mice
- ST285 significantly increased anti-inflammatory cytokines [[IL-4]], [[IL-5]], and [[IL-10]] while decreasing pro-inflammatory [[IFN-gamma]] and [[IL-1beta]]
- The Th1-to-Th2 shift suggests ST285 could be beneficial for managing autoimmune conditions like [[multiple-sclerosis]]

## Methods/Findings

- **Model**: SJL/J mice immunized with MBP83-99 peptide conjugated to mannan (subcutaneous, 3 injections every 2 weeks)
- **In Vitro Assay**: Spleen cells isolated 10-14 days post-immunization, co-cultured with ST285 bacteria (1 x 10^6 CFU/mL) and MBP83-99 recall peptide for 24 hours
- **Cytokine Measurement**: Bioplex multiplex bead assay (9-plex panel)
- **Pro-inflammatory Cytokine Results**:
  - IFN-gamma: Reduced by ST285 co-culture vs. MBP83-99 alone (p < 0.05)
  - TNF-alpha: Not secreted by spleen cells under any condition
  - IL-1beta: Slightly but significantly reduced (p < 0.05)
  - IL-2: Significantly decreased with ST285 (p < 0.05)
- **Anti-inflammatory Cytokine Results**:
  - IL-4: Significantly increased (p < 0.001) - approximately 3-fold increase
  - IL-5: Significantly increased (p < 0.01)
  - IL-10: Significantly increased (p < 0.001) - approximately 4-fold increase
  - IL-6: Profoundly increased (p < 0.001)
- **GM-CSF**: No change with ST285 co-culture
- **Key Interpretation**: The significant increase of IL-10 could drive differentiation of naive CD4+ T cells into Tregs, further supporting anti-inflammatory phenotype. IL-6 increase was interpreted as beneficial because it promotes Th2 differentiation.
