# Parkinson's Disease: A Systematic Review

## Summary

This systematic review provides a comprehensive clinical overview of [[parkinsons-disease]], covering epidemiology, pathogenesis, clinical features, diagnosis, and treatment. It serves as a foundational reference for understanding PD in the context of microbiome and metallomic research.

## Key Findings

- PD is the second most common neurodegenerative disease, affecting 1-2% of people over 60
- [[Alpha-synuclein]] aggregation (Lewy bodies) is the pathological hallmark, though not universally present
- Dopaminergic neuron loss in the substantia nigra pars compacta drives motor symptoms
- Non-motor symptoms (constipation, anosmia, REM sleep behavior disorder) precede motor onset by years
- Genetic factors (LRRK2, GBA, SNCA, PARK2) account for 5-10% of cases
- Environmental risk factors include pesticide exposure, heavy metal exposure, and rural living
- Current treatments are symptomatic; no disease-modifying therapy exists

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This review confirms several features that anchor the metallomic-microbiome framework: (1) non-motor gut symptoms precede motor PD by years, consistent with a gut-origin model; (2) environmental exposures including [[heavy-metals]] are established risk factors; (3) [[iron]] accumulation in the substantia nigra is a consistent pathological finding; (4) the substantia nigra's vulnerability relates to its high metabolic demand and neuromelanin content (which binds metals); (5) disease-modifying therapies targeting the microbiome-metal axis represent an unmet need.

## Limitations

- Broad clinical review -- not focused on microbiome or metals specifically
- Some findings may have been updated since publication
- Treatment landscape is evolving
