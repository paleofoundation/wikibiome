## Key Findings

- **CVB4 restructures microbiome before disease onset**: Coxsackievirus B4 infection in NOD mice induced gut microbiome dysbiosis PRIOR to islet autoimmunity, establishing the microbiome as a mediator (not consequence) of virus-triggered T1D (Morse et al. 2023, animal model, n=30)
- **FMT transfers disease susceptibility**: Fecal microbiota transplant from CVB4-infected mice into germ-free NOD mice enhanced T1D development, demonstrating the microbiome's causal role in virus-mediated diabetes
- **Bacterial translocation confirmed**: Bacteria were detected in pancreatic lymph nodes of CVB4-infected mice, providing a direct pathway for microbiome-to-islet immune activation
- **Akkermansia paradox**: **[[akkermansia-muciniphila]]** was paradoxically enriched in the diabetogenic microbiome configuration, despite its generally protective reputation
