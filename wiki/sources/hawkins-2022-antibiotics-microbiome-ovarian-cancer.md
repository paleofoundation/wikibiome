# Unintended Consequences of Antibiotic Therapy on the Microbiome in Ovarian Cancer

## Key Claims

- Broad-spectrum antibiotic treatment altered the gut microbiome and increased tumor growth and cisplatin resistance in ovarian cancer models
- ABX treatment enhanced cisplatin-induced stemness and enriched ovarian cancer stem cells
- Two gut-derived metabolites -- indole-3-propionic acid and indoxyl sulfate -- were suppressed by ABX and restored by FMT
- The gut microbiome is important for immune surveillance of ovarian cancer stem cells

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Commentary on Chambers et al. Cancer Research study
- **Key mechanism**: ABX-induced microbiome disruption led to enrichment of cancer stem cells
- **Metabolites**: Indole-3-propionic acid and indoxyl sulfate as effectors connecting gut microbiome to OC growth
