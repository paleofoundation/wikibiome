# DuPont 2023 — FMT in Parkinson's Disease (Randomized Pilot)

## Key Findings

- Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot of **multi-dose oral lyophilized FMT** in 12 patients with mild-to-moderate PD and constipation.
- **Dosing**: 60 g donor feces (lyophilized, capsule) twice weekly × 12 weeks (24 doses total). Randomization 2:1 FMT:placebo (8 FMT, 4 placebo).
- **Safety**: Tolerated well; only non-severe transient upper GI symptoms. One FMT subject developed unrelated metastatic cancer and was removed.
- **Microbiome**: Beta diversity increased at 6 wk (p=0.008) and 13 wk (p=0.0008). Proportions of Firmicutes families increased; **Proteobacteria proportion significantly decreased** — consistent with the dysbiosis-reversal direction hypothesized for PD.
- **Constipation**: Significantly improved; gut motility index improved (p=0.0374).
- **Motor outcomes**: Subjective improvements reported; objective motor findings showed only temporary improvement.
- 12-month follow-up documented durability of some microbiome changes.

## Methods

- Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot clinical trial (NCT03671785); 12-month total follow-up.
- Population: 55–80 years old, UK Brain Bank PD criteria, H&Y ≤3, ≤10 years disease duration, MoCA >23, constipation.
- Assessment: UPDRS motor in "OFF" state by blinded movement disorders specialist; SmartPill for gut transit/pH/motility; stool 16S microbiome at baseline, week 6, week 13, months 4/6/9.
- FMT product: Lyophilized stool from 4 screened donors, combined capsules, produced under FDA-compliant compounding.

## Relevance to Cureva

**Triangle evidence for FMT in PD:**
- I→f (intervention affects feature): FMT reduces Proteobacteria, increases Firmicutes alpha and beta diversity. Evidence: moderate (small n, single center).
- I→D (clinical improvement): Constipation, gut motility, subjective motor/non-motor improvement. Evidence: RCT-level for GI outcomes; temporary/subjective for motor outcomes.
- f→D (mechanism): Proteobacteria (including LPS-producing Gammaproteobacteria) are mechanistically linked to gut permeability, neuroinflammation, and alpha-synuclein aggregation.

Classification: **promising** for PD with constipation; efficacy on motor symptoms requires larger trials.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Supersedes single-dose FMT reports. This is the first multi-dose PD FMT RCT, showing that repeat dosing is needed to sustain diversity changes.
- Temporary motor improvement echoes Scheperjans 2024 single-dose FMT findings — mechanism may be more GI than CNS.

## Open Questions

- Optimal dosing frequency and duration for sustained benefit.
- Whether motor benefit requires longer treatment or higher dose.
- Donor selection criteria for PD-specific outcomes.
