# Intestinal microbiota regulates the gut-thyroid axis: the new dawn of improving Hashimoto thyroiditis

## Key Claims

- [[hashimotos-thyroiditis]] (HT) prevalence has reached 10-12% globally, with a 7-10:1 female-to-male ratio, and intestinal microbiota composition differs significantly between HT patients and healthy controls
- Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes are the dominant phyla in HT patients; Bacteroides, Roseburia, Ruminococcus, Dorea, Fusicatenibacter, and Eubacterium are increased while Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus are decreased
- [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] (SCFAs) produced by gut microbiota inhibit [[nf-kappa-b]] signaling and [[inflammation]], induce Treg cell function, and enhance intestinal barrier integrity relevant to thyroid autoimmunity
- [[selenium]] supplementation increases Treg proportion, suppresses pro-inflammatory cytokine secretion, and delays thyroid follicular cell apoptosis in autoimmune thyroid disease
- [[iodine]] excess can trigger thyroiditis and hypothyroidism through increased ROS production in thyroid follicular cells
- Gut [[dysbiosis]] damages intestinal barrier integrity, increases intestinal permeability, and promotes bacterial translocation that activates systemic immune responses against thyroid antigens
- Microbial metabolites including SCFAs, [[bile-acid-metabolism]], and tryptophan derivatives (indoles) play important roles in immune regulation relevant to HT pathogenesis

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive narrative review (18 pages)
- **Microbiota changes in HT** (Table 1 summarizing multiple cohorts):
 - Brazil cohort: Bacteroides increased, Bifidobacterium decreased (real-time PCR)
 - China cohorts (multiple): Blautia, Roseburia, Ruminococcus, Dorea increased; Faecalibacterium, Bacteroidetes, Prevotella_9 decreased (16S rRNA)
 - Proteobacteria, Cyanobacteria, Actinobacteria increased; Firmicutes, Bacteroidetes decreased in some cohorts

 - Microbiota regulates Th17/Treg balance; imbalance in HT drives autoimmunity
 - NLRP3 inflammasome activation by microbial-derived ATP promotes IL-1beta and IL-18 secretion
 - LPS from dysbiotic bacteria activates TLR4/NF-kB pathway promoting [[inflammation]]
 - Molecular mimicry between bacterial proteins and thyroid antigens (TPO, Tg) may trigger autoantibody production

 - [[selenium]]: selenoproteins (GPx, TrxR) protect thyroid from [[oxidative-stress]]; deficiency promotes autoimmunity
 - [[iron]]: deficiency associated with higher TSH and lower free T4; impairs thyroid hormone synthesis
 - [[zinc]]: cofactor for >300 metalloenzymes; mediates T lymphocyte differentiation and cytokine production
 - [[copper]]: both excess and deficiency associated with thyroid dysfunction; copper and selenium are positively associated with FT4 levels
 - [[iodine]]: U-shaped relationship with thyroid disease; excess increases AITD incidence via ROS and TPO inhibition
- **Therapeutic implications**: [[probiotics]], prebiotics, and synbiotics show potential for modulating gut microbiota to improve HT, though evidence remains preliminary
