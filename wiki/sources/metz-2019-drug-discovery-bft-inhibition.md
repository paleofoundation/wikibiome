# Key Claims

- Chenodeoxycholic acid (CDCA), a naturally-occurring primary [[bile-acid-metabolism]], effectively inhibits the oncogenic [[bacteroides-fragilis]] toxin (BFT), a metalloprotease that drives E-cadherin cleavage and CRC development
- CDCA directly interacts with recombinant BFT-1 protein, confirmed by thermal shift assay (delta-Tm = 1.10 at 85 uM, 2.10 at 175 uM; P < 0.0001)
- BFT inhibition by CDCA reduces E-cadherin cleavage (18% for BFT-1, 15% for BFT-3) and IL-8 secretion (67% and 69% reduction at 175 uM) in HT29/c1 colonic epithelial cells
- The in silico drug discovery pipeline combining KRIPO structure-based pharmacophore fingerprinting and MOE molecular docking identified 12 candidate inhibitors from 476 screened compounds
- ETBF carrying BFT is identified in the mucosa of >80% of CRC patients; it is a major CRC-driving bacterium
- Natural CDCA concentrations in the gut (0.015-0.12 mM) are within the range for BFT inhibition, suggesting that inter-individual bile acid variation could explain differential CRC susceptibility to ETBF

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original research (computational + in vitro)
- **In silico screening**: KRIPO pharmacophore fingerprint comparison against Protein Data Bank; MOE molecular docking guided by pharmacophore model of BFT-3 active site (zinc atom in catalytic domain with HIS348, HIS352, HIS358 and ASP194)
- **12 candidate compounds tested**: Including CDCA, EBSHIPA, PPAA, and others; morphological assay on HT29/c1 cells exposed to BFT-3
- **CDCA results**: 100% morphology score reduction for BFT-1 and BFT-3; 68-100% and 64-84% inhibition respectively at 175 uM over 4 hours
- **E-cadherin ELISA**: CDCA at 8.5 uM reduced E-cadherin cleavage by 18% (BFT-1) and 15% (BFT-3)
- **IL-8 ELISA**: CDCA dose-dependently reduced IL-8 secretion; maximum 67% reduction for BFT-1 and 69% for BFT-3 at 175 uM
- **BFT isoforms**: Three isoforms (BFT-1, BFT-2, BFT-3) with >93% sequence identity; CDCA most effectively inhibits BFT-3, then BFT-1, least BFT-2
- **FXR connection**: CDCA is the strongest natural FXR agonist; FXR activation may provide additional anti-inflammatory protection in the gut
