# Overview

Integrative review of medical conditions most commonly co-occurring with [[autism-spectrum-disorder]] -- **epilepsy, sleep problems, and gastrointestinal/immune dysfunction**. Proposes models integrating these comorbidities in relation to shared aetiological mechanisms and argues for studying physiological systems holistically rather than in isolation.

# Key Claims

## Epilepsy

- Prevalence estimates: 21.5% in ASD with intellectual disability vs 8% without
- 2-fold higher mortality rate in comorbid ASD + epilepsy vs general population
- Complex partial seizures most frequent type; infantile spasms (19.9%) strongly linked to ASD
- Shared genetic mechanisms (NRXN1, CNTNAP2, GABAergic neurotransmission genes)

## Sleep Problems

- Prevalence: **50-80%** of children with ASD vs 9-50% of typically developing children
- Insomnia, bedtime resistance, sleep onset delay most commonly reported
- Circadian rhythm disruption linked to mutations in Per1, NPAS2, and clock genes
- Melatonin production abnormalities common in ASD

## Gastrointestinal Dysfunction

- GI problem prevalence: **9-91%** across studies (constipation most common at 22% median)
- Chronic constipation strongly associated with challenging behaviours
- Food selectivity in 27% of ASD children
- Altered melatonin, serotonin, circadian rhythm may underlie multi-system dysfunction

## Integrative Model

- Three proposed relationship models between ASD and comorbidities: (A) common mechanism, (B) cumulative impact, (C) effect of medical abnormalities on underlying mechanisms
- Future research should use **robust and sensitive biomarkers across physiological systems**
