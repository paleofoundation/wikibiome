# Gut Microbiome and Plasma Microbiome-Related Metabolites in Patients With Decompensated and Compensated Heart Failure

## Summary

Prospective study comparing gut microbiome composition and circulating microbiome-related metabolites between 22 heart failure patients (in decompensated [Decomp] and compensated [Comp] phases during hospitalization) and 11 comorbidity-matched hospitalized controls. Combined 16S rRNA sequencing with CE-TOFMS plasma metabolomics.

## Key Findings

### Study Design
- 22 HF patients enrolled at Kobe University Hospital (October 2016-April 2017)
- Controls: 11 age-, sex-, and comorbidity-matched hospitalized patients without HF
- HF patients evaluated at two time points: decompensated and compensated phases
- Primary causes of HF: dilated cardiomyopathy (32%), hypertension (18%), valvular disease (23%), ischemic heart disease (9%)
- Mean LVEF: 42 +/- 17% in Decomp HF

### Microbiome Composition
- Shannon diversity index comparable between groups (control 2.27, Decomp HF 2.36, Comp HF 2.40)
- No significant differences in Bacteroidetes/Firmicutes ratio among groups
- *Actinobacteria* enriched in HF patients (Decomp HF: 2.99%, Comp HF: 3.80% vs. control: 0.62%; P = 0.035 and P = 0.026)
- At genus level: *Bifidobacterium* enriched in Comp HF vs. controls (P < 0.05)
- *Megamonas* depleted in both Decomp and Comp HF vs. controls (P < 0.001)

### Plasma Metabolites
- **Choline**: Significantly increased in Decomp HF vs. Comp HF (P < 0.05)
- **Carnitine**: Significantly increased in Decomp HF vs. both Comp HF and controls (P < 0.001)
- **TMA**: Not detected in any group
- **TMAO**: Significantly increased in HF patients (Decomp HF P = 0.003; Comp HF P = 0.004 vs. control)
- TMAO elevated in both HFrEF and HFpEF compared to controls

### Correlation Analysis
- Positive correlations between *Escherichia/Shigella* abundance and TMAO levels (Comp HF: r = 0.63, P = 0.002) and indoxyl sulfate levels (r = 0.63, P = 0.002)
- *Escherichia/Shigella* more abundant in Decomp than Comp HF (P = 0.030)

## Relevance to the Wiki

First study to characterize both gut microbiome and circulating metabolites across the decompensated and compensated phases of [[heart-failure]]. The *Escherichia/Shigella*-TMAO-indoxyl sulfate axis in HF provides mechanistic links between gut [[dysbiosis]], uremic toxin production, and cardiac decompensation. Complements [[fromentin-2022-microbiome-metabolome-cardiometabolic-spectrum]] on the HF stage of the cardiometabolic disease spectrum.
