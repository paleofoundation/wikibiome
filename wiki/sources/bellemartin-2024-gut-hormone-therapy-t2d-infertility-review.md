# Belle Martin & Duggan 2024 — An exploration of gut hormone therapy to treat infertility caused by Type 2 Diabetes

**Citation:** Belle Martin A, Duggan E. *An Exploration of Gut Hormone Therapy to Treat Infertility Caused by Type 2 Diabetes.* UCC Student Medical Journal. 2024;4:9–23. DOI: [10.33178/SMJ.2024.1.1](https://doi.org/10.33178/SMJ.2024.1.1)

## Key Findings

- **Infertile T2D women have a distinct microbial signature.** Across 5 studies (Komiya 2020, Khan 2022, Christ 2018, Baker 2017, Jensterle 2019), infertile T2D women showed:
  - Increased *Verrucomicrobia* (includes [[akkermansia-muciniphila]]) and *Phascolarctobacterium*.
  - Decreased *Stenotrophomonas*, *Streptococcus*, *Roseburia*.
  - These shifts associated with depleted circulating estrogen, irregular menstrual cycles, and hyperandrogenism.
- **Probiotic and prebiotic interventions show efficacy.**
  - **Komiya 2020** (n=18 infertile + 18 fertile, Japan): PHGG (partially hydrolyzed guar gum) supplementation homogenized infertile microbiome toward fertile profile. **58.3% of supplemented infertile patients became pregnant and carried to term.**
  - **Khalili 2019** (n=40 T2D, Iran, double-blind RCT): Lactobacillus casei 10⁸ CFU/mL × 8 weeks significantly reduced caloric intake, improved fetuin-A, SIRT1, and glycemic response vs. maltodextrin placebo.
  - **Rittipairoj 2021 meta-analysis** (26 trials, n=1,947): probiotics reduced fasting blood glucose by -12.99 mg/dL short-term and -2.99 mg/dL long-term vs. placebo; HbA1c also improved.
  - **Salles 2020 systematic review** (34 probiotic intervention trials): 79% of studies showed probiotic supplementation correlates with significant beneficial alterations in insulin resistance markers.
- **Gut hormone receptor axis in reproduction.**
  - **Khan 2022** (Ulster, mouse study): 14-week-old female mice deficient in GIPR or GLP-1R showed significantly disrupted oestrous cycling, fewer breeding successes across 3 cycles, and smaller litters. Oral metformin supplementation significantly improved litter size — demonstrating the gut-gonadal axis depends on GIP and GLP-1 signaling.
  - **Jensterle 2019 systematic review** (983 potentially relevant, filtered to 6 observational + 24 interventional): GLP-1 agonists show anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic effects in the endometrium. Ovulation rate and menstrual frequency improved in T2D/obese patients treated with GLP-1 agonists (exenatide, liraglutide).
  - **Shyangdan 2010 meta-analysis** (28 RCTs): All GLP-1 agonists reduced HbA1c by ~1% vs. placebo; exenatide and liraglutide showed greatest HbA1c and weight-loss effects.
  - **Rosenstock 2021 (SURPASS-1) phase 3 RCT** (n=478): Tirzepatide (dual GIP+GLP-1 agonist) significantly improved glycemic control and body weight vs. placebo, without hypoglycemia risk.
- **Bariatric surgery as reference intervention.** Christ 2018 (n=930 bariatric surgery patients, 44 with PCOS): bariatric surgery led to significant reduction in androgen levels, hyperandrogenism, and irregular menstrual cycling in PCOS. Confirms that extreme gut-hormone-axis modulation reverses reproductive dysfunction — providing upper bound for what pharmacologic gut-hormone therapy can achieve.

## Methods

PRISMA-compliant systematic review. PubMed and Diabetes and Metabolic Syndrome Journal searched Jan–Mar 2023. Search strategy: (Gut Microbiota OR Gut Microbiome OR GIP OR GLP-1 OR Probiotics) AND (Infertility OR Reproductive Function OR Menstrual Irregularities OR Reproduction OR Oestrogen). Filters: clinical trial, meta-analysis, RCT, systematic review, female. 234 PubMed + 8 D&MS + 3 reference-traced = 241 deduplicated records → 241 screened → 34 full-text → 10 included. CASP checklist appraisal for each record.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Verrucomicrobia *enrichment* in infertile T2D women contradicts the metabolic literature where *Akkermansia* (the dominant Verrucomicrobia genus) is typically protective. May reflect species- or strain-level variation, or compensatory expansion in response to mucin-layer damage.
- Student-journal peer-review quality raises evidence concerns; several included studies have small sample sizes or conflicts of interest (e.g., Komiya 2020 funded by EA Pharma, the PHGG manufacturer).
- Khan 2022 mouse model (GIPR−/−, GLP-1R−/−) may not translate directly to human T2D patients, who have partial GLP-1 resistance rather than complete receptor loss.

## Open Questions

- Whether probiotic + GLP-1 agonist combination outperforms either alone for T2D infertility (analog of the COM arm of Long 2025 in PCOS).
- Whether Verrucomicrobia enrichment is species-specific (e.g., Akkermansia strain-dependent effects).
- Whether the gut-gonadal axis mediated by GIP/GLP-1 is also active in non-T2D fertility disorders.
