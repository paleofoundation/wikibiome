# Key Claims

- **Gut microbiota dysbiosis** induces neuroinflammation through intermediator cytokines and metabolites in ASD
- **Loss of intestinal barrier integrity**, activation of microglia, and dysregulation of neurotransmitters are caused by neural inflammatory factors
- **Neuroinflammatory biomarkers** including BDNF, calprotectin, eotaxin, metabolites, and microRNAs have been considered etiological markers for ASD
- **Probiotic trials** may reestablish healthy gut microbiota balance to reduce neuroinflammation in ASD
- The gut-brain interaction with microbiota alterations serves as a **foundational framework** for ASD etiology, diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment

# Methods & Findings

**Review** examining the neuroinflammatory mechanisms linking gut microbiota to ASD pathogenesis.

- Critically examined evidence for gut microbiota symbiosis inducing neuroinflammation
- Identified specific neuroinflammatory intermediates: cytokines, calprotectin, eotaxin, BDNF, microRNAs
- Emphasized that dysregulated gut microbiota leads to circulating immune profile changes
- Proposed that correcting microbiota load would lower GI tract inflammatory chaos and modulate neurotransmitters

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Neuroinflammatory cascade** from gut dysbiosis to brain via specific molecular mediators
- **Biomarker panel** approach combining multiple inflammatory markers for ASD diagnosis
- **Microglia activation** as downstream effect of gut-derived inflammatory signals
