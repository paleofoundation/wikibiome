# Key Claims

- SCFAs (acetate, propionate, butyrate) attenuate inflammation by inhibiting NF-kB activation, decreasing pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha), and stimulating anti-inflammatory cytokines (IL-10, TGF-beta)
- SCFAs function epigenetically by inhibiting histone deacetylases (HDACs) and modulating Wnt, Hedgehog, Hippo, and Notch signaling pathways that contribute to cancer pathogenesis
- SCFAs block cancer stem cell proliferation by targeting epidermal growth factor receptor, hepatocyte growth factor receptor (MET), and promoting expression of tumor suppressors (PTEN, p53)
- Dysbiosis is characterized by diminished levels of SCFAs in stool, and the presence of a "leaky gut" permitting microbial penetration through the gut wall, triggering chronic inflammation

# Methods/Findings

- **Review scope**: Comprehensive review of SCFA roles in cancer biology covering dysbiosis, epigenetics, Wnt/Hedgehog/Hippo signaling, FGFR2 pathways, and immunomodulation
- **Butyrate as HDAC inhibitor**: Most potent natural HDACs among all known compounds; blocks active site and activates GPCRs reducing HDAC-encoding gene expression
- **Wnt signaling**: SCFAs overcome constitutive Wnt activation by mutational inactivation of APC; butyrate-mediated demethylation restores SFRP expression suppressing beta-catenin
- **Cancer prevention by delay**: SCFA-based therapeutics that maintain chronic inflammatory homeostasis may delay tumor appearance through "prevention by delay" concept
- **SCFA-targeted immunomodulation**: SCFAs promote CRAMP expression, restore colonocyte metabolism, re-establish normal gut barrier function
- **Chronic liver disease**: HBV/HCV-related HCC connects through SCFAs restoring immunosurveillance; butyrate reduces liver fibrosis and HCC incidence in transgenic mice
