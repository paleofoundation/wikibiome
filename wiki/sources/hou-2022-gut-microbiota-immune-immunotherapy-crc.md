# Effects of Gut Microbiota on Immune Responses and Immunotherapy in Colorectal Cancer

## Key Claims

- Gut microbiota modulates both innate and adaptive immune responses in CRC through multiple mechanisms: recruiting MDSCs and TAMs, activating neutrophil extracellular traps (NETs), modulating T cell differentiation (Th1/Th2/Th17/Treg balance), and influencing B cell maturation
- *F. nucleatum* colonization leads to increased intestinal [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] levels and shapes Th17 response via FFAR2 receptor; in Ffar2-knockout mice, *F. nucleatum* failed to increase Th17 cell frequency
- Gut microbiota contributes to formation of pre-metastatic niches: *E. coli* induces gut vascular barrier (GVB) disruption allowing bacteria to reach the liver and initiate metastatic niche formation with immunosuppressive cell recruitment
- In MSS-type CRC tumor-bearing mice, gut microbiome changes affected expression of IFN-gamma and IL-2 in the tumor microenvironment, producing different therapeutic effects of PD-1 and *Prevotella_sp_CAG:485* and *Akkermansia* maintained normal PD-1 antibody efficacy
- Table 1 comprehensively summarizes microbial changes across all CRC stages (I-IV) from 13 studies, showing stage-dependent shifts with *F. nucleatum*, *Peptostreptococcus stomatis*, *Gemella morbillorum*, and *Parvimonas micra* consistently enriched in advanced stages

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of microbiota-immune interactions in CRC covering clinical findings, innate/adaptive immunity mechanisms, metastatic niche formation, and immunotherapy modulation
- **Key immune mechanisms reviewed**: NLRP3 inflammasome activation by PAMPs/DAMPs; MDSC/TAN/TAM recruitment; CD8+ T cell exhaustion via PD-1/CTLA-4; FAS/FASL-mediated immune cell apoptosis; B cell regulation via IgA and TGF-beta
- **Immunotherapy interactions**: *Bifidobacterium pseudolongum*, *Lactobacillus johnsonii*, and *Olsenella* species enhanced anti-PD-L1 and anti-CTLA-4 efficacy; FMT combined with anti-PD-1 therapy trial (NCT04729322) is underway for metastatic CRC
- **Metastasis mechanisms**: CRC-secreted CTSK stimulates CRC progression by M2 polarization of TAMs via TLR4-mTOR pathway
