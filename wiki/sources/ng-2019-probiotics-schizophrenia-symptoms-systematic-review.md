# A Systematic Review of the Effect of Probiotic Supplementation on Schizophrenia Symptoms

## Key Claims

- First systematic review and meta-analysis investigating [[probiotics]] effect on [[schizophrenia]] symptoms
- Only 3 RCTs met inclusion criteria (all from the same clinical trial NCT01242371, same patient population), using combined L. rhamnosus strain GG and B. animalis subsp. lactis strain Bb12 for 14 weeks
- Pooled SMD was -0.0884 (95% CI -0.380 to 0.204, p = 0.551), indicating no statistically significant effect of probiotic supplementation on PANSS scores
- No significant difference was observed for positive symptoms or negative symptoms alone
- Despite non-significant symptom effects, probiotic supplementation was found to significantly increase brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) levels in one study
- Probiotics may have other benefits: regulating bowel movement, ameliorating metabolic effects of antipsychotics (constipation, weight gain)
- The review highlights that strain specificity is crucial; future trials should report specific probiotic strains used
- All three studies were RCTs with generally low risk of bias

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **Studies included**: 3 RCTs (329 records screened, 27 full-text reviewed)
- **Intervention**: L. rhamnosus GG + B. animalis lactis Bb12, 14 weeks
- **Meta-analytic model**: Fixed-effects (I^2 = 0%, heterogeneity = 0%)
- **Limitation**: All studies from same trial/population, very small evidence base
