# Blakeley-Ruiz 2019 — Functional stability vs taxa volatility in Crohn's

## Key Findings

- **Metaproteomes personalize more than they vary temporally.** 25 fecal metaproteomes from 4 post-resection Crohn's remission patients sampled across ~1 year. Temporally related metaproteomes clustered most closely within individual, indicating intra-individual functional identity persists despite taxa turnover.
- **Function is conserved across phyla.** When KEGG orthologous groups were mapped to metabolic modules, similar and redundant metabolic functions appeared across multiple phyla within the same individual — carbohydrate → lipid → amino-acid degradation pathways converge on fermentation products regardless of which taxa perform them.
- **Implication for disease characterization.** The authors argue that for Crohn's, *functional composition* (what the community does) may be a more robust phenotyping axis than *membership composition* (who is there). This reframes the microbiome signature question for IBD.

## Methods

4 Crohn's patients followed post-resection surgery with 5–8 stool samples each over ~1 year. Metaproteomics via LC-MS/MS on fecal material. Proteins annotated to KEGG orthology, clustered by metabolic modules. Compared taxonomic identity of proteins (membership) vs metabolic module identity (function) across intra- and inter-individual variation.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Calibrates [[crohns-disease-signature]] interpretation.** When two CD patients have different taxonomic signatures, they may share convergent metabolic output — intervention should target function (SCFA production, bile-acid metabolism, mucus degradation) not just taxa.
- Supports intervention strategies that target metabolic function (prebiotics, SCFA-producing probiotics) over strict taxon replacement.
- Primitive 5 (two-sided ecological engineering — restore function, not just remove taxa) and primitive 9 (oxygen state as ecological determinant — fermentation-dominated function indicates anaerobe dominance) are active.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Contrasts with papers that treat specific taxa ([[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]], [[bifidobacterium]]) as diagnostic. This paper argues taxa presence is insufficient; function must be measured.

## Open Questions

- Does the functional-stability principle hold in active CD vs remission?
- Can fecal metaproteomics predict flares better than 16S or metagenomics?
- Small n=4; replication needed.
