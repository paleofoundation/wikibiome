# Efficacy of Probiotics in Patients of Cardiovascular Disease Risk: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## Summary

Systematic review and meta-analysis of 34 RCTs (2,177 adults) evaluating [[probiotics]] effects on blood pressure, blood glucose, BMI, and lipid profiles in patients with CVD risk factors (hypertension, metabolic syndrome, T2D, obesity, hypercholesterolaemia).

## Key Findings

### Blood Pressure
- SBP reduced by -1.31 mmHg (95% CI: -2.23 to -0.39, P = 0.005)
- DBP reduced by -1.87 mmHg (95% CI: -2.41 to -1.33, P < 0.001)
- Greater effect in diabetic patients: SBP -5.82 mmHg (P = 0.02), DBP -3.81 mmHg (P < 0.001)
- Yoghurt form more effective: SBP -3.56 mmHg, DBP -3.54 mmHg
- Treatment >1.5 months showed SBP reductions of -2.38 mmHg (P = 0.04)

### Cholesterol and Lipids
- Total cholesterol: -6.05 mg/dL (95% CI: -8.49 to -3.61, P < 0.001)
- LDL-C: -8.77 mg/dL (95% CI: -11.86 to -5.69, P < 0.001)
- No significant changes in triglycerides or HDL-C
- Heterogeneity not statistically significant for most outcomes

### Blood Glucose and Metabolic Markers
- Serum glucose: -4.92 mg/dL (95% CI: -6.15 to -3.69, P < 0.001)
- HbA1C: -0.18% (95% CI: -0.31 to -0.06, P = 0.005)
- BMI: -0.31 kg/m2 (95% CI: -0.41 to -0.21, P < 0.001)

### Subgroup Analyses
- Females benefited more than males for blood pressure outcomes
- Higher dosage (>1.0x10^9 CFU) did not clearly outperform lower dosage
- Capsule, milk, yogurt, and other formulations showed variable efficacy
- Studies conducted across 11 countries, demonstrating global applicability

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Clinical evidence for probiotics in CVD** - Meta-analytic evidence supports [[probiotics]] as adjunctive therapy for [[cardiovascular-disease]] risk factor management
2. **Multiple risk factor reduction** - Single intervention affects blood pressure, lipids, glucose, and BMI simultaneously
3. **Formulation matters** - Yoghurt delivery and longer treatment duration enhance probiotic cardiovascular benefits
