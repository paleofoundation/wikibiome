# Overview

Review examining how **TORCH infections** (Toxoplasma, Others, Rubella, Cytomegalovirus, Herpes simplex), imbalanced microbiome, and persistent inflammation contribute to ASD pathogenesis. Argues that ASD-related changes begin prenatally via **maternal-to-fetal immune activation** triggered by infection.

# Key Findings

## Clinical Presentation of ASD

- Comorbidities include: GI disturbances, allergies, autoimmune disorders, endocrine/metabolic problems, neurological issues, persistent infections
- Thyroid diseases among the most frequently found in autistic individuals
- Type 2 diabetes found to exacerbate ASD symptoms via tissue damage from high blood glucose
- Prevalence: 1 in 44 US children (CDC 2021); was only 1 in 2500 fifty years ago

## Autistic Brain Changes

- Brain enlargement during first year of life, followed by shrinkage rather than return to normal
- Significant decrease in Purkinje cells (formation initiated only during prenatal period)
- Hyper-expansion of cortical surface between 6-12 months precedes brain volume overgrowth at 12-24 months

## Neuroinflammation Pathway

- Chronic inflammation: overactive microglia produce IL-8 and TNF-alpha continuously
- IL-8 recruits neutrophils causing persistent acute inflammation and oxidative stress
- Abnormal myelination: oligodendrocytes susceptible to oxidative damage from insufficient glutathione
- Multiple brain regions show abnormal myelination in ASD (orbitofrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, arcuate fasciculus, amygdala)

## Molecular Abnormalities

- Microdeletions in 15q11.2: increased white matter volume but reduced corpus callosum size
- Disruption of IGF-1/PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling pathways implicated
- GLT1 receptor knockout: elevated excitation-inhibition ratio in astrocytes correlating with repetitive behavior

## Oxidative Damage

- Persistent inflammation + oxidative stress --> neuronal and glial damage
- Brain's high lipid content makes it particularly susceptible to reactive oxygen species damage
- Even when acute inflammation resolves, chronic inflammation may persist producing more pro-inflammatory mediators
