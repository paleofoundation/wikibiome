# Alterations and Mechanism of Gut Microbiota in Graves' Disease and Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

## Key Claims

- The overall structure of [[gut-microbiome]] in [[graves-disease]] (GD) and [[hashimotos-thyroiditis]] (HT) patients was significantly different from healthy controls, though bacterial abundance and diversity were similar
- [[proteobacteria]] and [[actinobacteria]] contents were highest in the HT group; GD and HT groups had higher abundance of Erysipelotrichia and Cyanobacteria and lower levels of Bacillaceae and Megamonas compared to controls
- KEGG analysis showed that the "ABC transporter" metabolic pathway was highly correlated with the occurrence of GD and HT, with enrichment in carbohydrate transport and metabolism
- Bacillus, Blautia, and Ornithinimicrobium could be used as potential markers to distinguish GD and HT from healthy populations
- At genus level, Prevotella_9, Ruminococcus_2, and Lachnospiraceae_NK4A136_group were higher in GD patients, while Coprococcus_3, Ruminococcus_gnavus_group, Acetanaerobacterium, and Ruminiclostridium_5 were enriched in HT

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional original study
- **Population**: 70 fecal samples from 27 GD patients, 27 HT patients, and 16 healthy volunteers in northeastern China
- **Inclusion criteria**: GD patients aged 18-70 with clinical hyperthyroidism; HT patients with FT4 <22.1 IU/l, TSH >0.27 mIU/l, and ultrasound consistent with Hashimoto's; controls with normal thyroid function
- **Exclusion criteria**: Pregnancy, lactation, smoking, alcohol, use of probiotics/prebiotics/synbiotics/hormones/drugs/laxatives in recent 3 months; multiple sclerosis, RA, and other autoimmune diseases; Chinese herbal medicine
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA gene sequencing (V3-V4 region) on Illumina MiSeq platform; UPARSE pipeline at 97% similarity; RDP classifier with Silva database (SSU123); PLS-DA, LEfSe, KEGG analysis
- **Alpha diversity**: 686 total OTUs; 389 shared among groups; 63, 61, and 21 unique OTUs in GD, HT, and control respectively; Shannon and Simpson indices not significantly different
- **Beta diversity**: PLS-DA and ANOSIM showed significantly different overall community composition (R=0.2519) between groups; binary Jaccard similarity showed modest differences
- **LEfSe analysis**: 24 discriminant features for GD vs. controls (LDA>3); 13 for HT vs. controls; at genus level, Blautia, Eubacterium_ventriosum_group, Dorea, Lachnospira enriched in HT
- **Family level in HT**: Enterococcaceae, Erysipelotrichaceae, Bacillobacteriaceae enriched; Peptostreptococcaceae, Bacillaceae, Matophyaceae enriched in controls
- **KEGG functional**: ABC transporter pathway enriched in both disease groups vs. controls
