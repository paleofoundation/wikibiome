## Key Claims

- Infant formula contributed up to 64% (50th percentile) of total dietary exposure for infants (<1 year) for some contaminants, decreasing to 43% for toddlers (1 to <3 years)
- [[cadmium]] levels exceeded the HBGV (health-based guidance value) for about 30% of infants and toddlers, with dietary Cd reaching 80%+ of TWI and highly exposed infants at 178% TWI
- The MoE for [[dietary-arsenic-exposure|inorganic arsenic]] was below 1 for all children, indicating a potential health concern for cancer risk (skin cancer benchmark)
- [[nickel]] dietary exposure was 2.96-3.54 ug/kg bw/day in infants, approximately a quarter of the TDI (13 ug/kg bw/day), with high-consuming toddlers reaching 17.9 ug/kg bw/day
- Cr, Ni, and Se exposures were well below corresponding HBGVs
- Mn and Zn exposures in the 95th percentile were close to their safe level of intake and upper level respectively
- Pb MoE was approximately 2 for infants (close to 1 at 95th percentile), indicating risk of neurotoxic effects cannot be ruled out
- Infant formula was a more significant Cd source in the first 4 months (96% of exposure) declining to 18% by 13 months

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Combined data from the BfR MEAL Study (first German total diet study, 356 foods sampled 2017-2019) and KiESEL nutrition survey (1,008 children aged 0.5 to <6 years); assessed iAs, Cd, Cr, Pb, Mn, Hg, Ni, Se, Zn
- **Analysis**: ICP-MS for most elements; HPLC-ICP-MS/MS for iAs; cold vapor AAS for Hg; LODs ranged 0.001-0.03 mg/kg depending on element and matrix
- **Infant formula occurrence (Table 2)**: iAs 4.55 ug/kg; Cd 4.00 ug/kg; Cr 32.8 ug/kg; Pb 4.00 ug/kg; Mn 840 ug/kg; Hg 3.00 ug/kg; Ni 78.8 ug/kg; Se 130 ug/kg; Zn 40.0 mg/kg
- **Exposure (Table 5)**: Median total exposure for infants: iAs 0.22, Cd 0.30, Cr 1.37, Pb 0.25, Mn 828, Ni 3.11, Se 15.7, Zn 4310 ug/kg bw/day (or ug/day for Mn, Se, Zn)
- **Formula contribution**: Up to 64% for infants at P50 (Cr); Cd from formula was 10% at P50; Pb from formula 12% at P50
- **HBGV comparison (Table 6)**: Cd 84-89% of HBGV at mean, 133-178% at P95; Ni 24-29% at mean; Mn 41-42% at mean; Zn 65-67% at P50
- **MoE for iAs (Table 7)**: 0.17-0.28 at mean (all below 1); for Pb: 1.30-2.27
