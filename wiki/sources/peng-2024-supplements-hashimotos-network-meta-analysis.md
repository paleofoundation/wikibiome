# Effects of Different Supplements on Hashimoto's Thyroiditis: A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis

## Key Findings

- **Selenium** (typical dose 200 µg/day selenomethionine, 6-month treatment) produced the most consistent reduction in both **TPOAb** (SMD -2.44, 95% CI -4.19 to -0.69) and **TgAb** (SMD -2.76, 95% CI -4.50 to -1.02) vs. placebo across 7 selenium RCTs.
- **Myo-inositol alone** (1 RCT) did not significantly reduce antibody titres; **vitamin D alone** (4 RCTs, 2,000–4,000 IU/day) showed non-significant antibody reduction with high heterogeneity attributable to small sample sizes.
- **Selenium + myo-inositol combination** (1 RCT, Nordio 2017, n=168) numerically reduced TPOAb (SMD -3.01, 95% CI -8.96 to 2.93) and TgAb (SMD -3.64, 95% CI -9.20 to 1.92) but did not reach statistical significance due to small sample.
- **6-month treatment duration** was required for detectable selenium effects; 3-month studies were insufficient.
- The association between AITD and low vitamin D (<20 ng/mL) was confirmed but supplementation evidence remains weak.

## Methods

Network meta-analysis pooling 10 RCTs (4 Chinese CNKI + 6 international) evaluating selenium, vitamin D, myo-inositol, and combinations vs. placebo in euthyroid Hashimoto's patients (not on levothyroxine, not pregnant/lactating). Quality assessed by Jadad scale (range 3–7). STATA v17.0, random-effects model, SMD as primary outcome.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Validated intervention**: Selenium (200 µg/day selenomethionine, 6 months) for Hashimoto's patients with elevated TPOAb/TgAb and euthyroid status. I→D evidence level: meta-analysis of RCTs.
- **Conditional intervention**: Myo-inositol + selenium combination where vitamin D deficiency coexists — pilot-level evidence only.
- **STOP candidate**: Vitamin D monotherapy for antibody reduction — evidence insufficient at current doses/durations despite biological plausibility.

## Open Questions

- Optimal selenium form (selenomethionine vs. sodium selenite) not resolved.
- Whether selenium effects extend to patients already on levothyroxine.
- Whether combining selenium with vitamin D at ≥4,000 IU/day exceeds selenium alone.
