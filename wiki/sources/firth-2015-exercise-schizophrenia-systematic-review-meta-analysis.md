# A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Exercise Interventions in Schizophrenia Patients

## Key Claims

- Across 17 trials with 659 participants, exercise had no significant effect on BMI but improved physical fitness and other cardiometabolic risk factors
- Psychiatric symptoms were significantly reduced by ~90 min/week of moderate-to-vigorous exercise (SMD 0.72, 95% CI -1.14 to -0.29)
- Exercise also significantly improved functioning, co-morbid disorders, and neurocognition
- Yoga showed significantly greater effects than exercise in some measures (facial emotion recognition)
- Metabolic syndrome incidence increases fivefold within first few years of antipsychotic treatment, and body weight increases by up to 15 kg
- Life expectancy of people with [[schizophrenia]] is reduced by 15-20 years compared to general population
- Exercise is a feasible and effective intervention when implemented with sufficient dose in supervised or group settings

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of 20 eligible studies (17 trials)
- **Median illness duration**: 10 years; median age 33 years
- **Exercise dose**: Median 75 min/week (mean 72 min, range 25-160 min)
- **RCTs**: 11 of 17 trials were randomized controlled trials
