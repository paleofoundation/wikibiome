# Targeting the Gut to Treat Multiple Sclerosis

## Key Claims

- The [[gut-brain-axis]] (GBA) involves bidirectional communication via gut microbiota, intestinal barrier, immune system, autonomic nervous system (vagus nerve), and endocrine system (HPA axis) -- all disrupted in MS
- Four comprehensive tables catalog preclinical and clinical evidence: (1) antibiotic therapy in EAE (7 studies), (2) probiotic supplementation in EAE (9 studies), (3) SCFA/FMT/diet modifications in EAE (12 studies), and (4) clinical trials in MS patients (14 entries)
- Intestinal barrier permeability is increased in MS: plasma HP2 (haptoglobin-2/zonulin precursor) is elevated and correlates with BBB disruption on MRI; HP2 was shown to increase BBB permeability in vitro
- Propionic acid clinical trial: 91 pwMS + 24 HCs received propionate for 2 weeks as add-on therapy; results showed lowered Th17, increased Tregs, and effects more pronounced in pwMS; longitudinal 1-year data showed decreased annualized relapse rate, disability stabilization, and decreased brain atrophy
- [[akkermansia-muciniphila]] has context-dependent effects: associated with lower disability in one study but also linked to pro-inflammatory mucinolytic activity; miR-30d transferred *Akkermansia* abundance and suppressed EAE via Treg promotion

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review in JCI's "Gut-Brain Axis" series, from Washington University / University of Sydney / University of Milan
- **Antibiotic evidence**: Oral broad-spectrum antibiotics before EAE induction = disease amelioration via Treg accumulation; intraperitoneal ampicillin during EAE = disease worsening; oral ampicillin reduced EAE severity but amplified MOG responses when combined with novel Erysipelotrichaceae strain
- **Probiotic evidence**: *L. plantarum* + *B. animalis* attenuated EAE clinically and increased Tregs; *P. histicola* suppressed EAE development and increased IL-10/TGF-beta; conflicting results with *L. reuteri* (beneficial alone but worsened with novel strain)
- **SCFA evidence**: Treatment with LCFAs exacerbated EAE (mainly Th1/Th17) while SCFAs ameliorated; propionic acid supplementation before immunization prevented EAE; butyrate pre-treatment enhanced remyelination in cuprizone model
- **FMT evidence**: MS twin-to-GF mouse transfer increased spontaneous EAE; single SPMS patient FMT case report showed EDSS stabilization at 10-year follow-up

- Antibiotic timing paradox (beneficial pre-EAE, harmful during) is critical for [[microbiome-modulating-drugs]] research design
