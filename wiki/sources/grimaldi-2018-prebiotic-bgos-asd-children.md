# Key Claims

- **First study** evaluating prebiotic (B-GOS) intervention combined with exclusion diets in ASD children
- **B-GOS supplementation improved anti-sociability scores** on the ATEC questionnaire (P = 0.05, diet x treatment interaction)
- **Significant metabolic changes** in fecal and urinary profiles after B-GOS intervention
- **Exclusion diets** alone reduced GI symptoms (abdominal pain P < 0.05, bowel movement P < 0.001)

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** RCT with 30 ASD children (exclusion and unrestricted diets) given B-GOS prebiotic (Bimuno galactooligosaccharide) or placebo for 6 weeks. Trial: NCT02720900.

**Dietary findings:**
- Vitamin D significantly lower in unrestricted diet ASD children vs. exclusion diet (P < 0.01)
- Exclusion diet children had lower abdominal pain and improved bowel movement

**Behavioral outcomes:**
- Anti-sociability (ATEC): significant reduction over time with B-GOS on exclusion diet (P = 0.05)
- Social skills (AQ): improved with B-GOS after intervention and follow-up (P < 0.05)

**Microbiota changes (16S rRNA):**
- B-GOS increased [[bifidobacterium]], [[ruminococcus]], Lachnospiraceae family
- [[coprococcus]], [[dorea|Dorea formicigenerans]], [[dysbiosis|Oribacterium]], [[dysbiosis|Eubacterium dolchum]], TM7-3 family, Mogibacteriaceae also associated with B-GOS
- Increased diversity (rarefaction curves) though not statistically significant

**Metabolomic findings (1H-NMR):**
- Fecal: B-GOS increased ethanol, DMG, SCFAs (butyrate, valerate)
- Urinary: increased creatinine, dimethylglycine, DMA, carnitine, citrate, TMAO; decreased phenylacetylglycine, phenylalanine, beta-hydroxybutyrate
- Significant metabolic perturbations between exclusion and unrestricted diets at baseline

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Vitamin D deficiency**: Low vitamin D in ASD children on unrestricted diets links to impaired calcium/metal homeostasis and immune dysregulation
- **B-GOS as metal-dependent prebiotic**: Galactooligosaccharides selectively feed Bifidobacterium, which has strong zinc and iron-dependent metabolic pathways
- **Butyrate production**: B-GOS-enhanced butyrate and valerate production restores HDAC inhibition and metal-dependent epigenetic regulation
- **TMAO increase**: Elevated trimethylamine N-oxide after B-GOS may reflect shifts in choline metabolism with implications for metal-dependent methylation pathways
