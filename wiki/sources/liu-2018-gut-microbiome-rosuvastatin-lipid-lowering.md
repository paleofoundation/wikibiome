# Gut Microbiome Associates With Lipid-Lowering Effect of Rosuvastatin in Vivo

## Summary

Clinical trial examining the lipid-lowering effect of rosuvastatin (10 mg/day, 4-8 weeks) and its association with gut microbiome composition, diversity, and taxa in 64 patients with primary hyperlipidemia, stratified by treatment response (Group I: high efficacy; Group II: low efficacy).

## Key Findings

### Statin Efficacy and Microbiome
- Group I (high efficacy): 41.9% reduction in TC, 38.5% in LDL-C
- Group II (low efficacy): 26.6% reduction in TC, 26.6% in LDL-C
- Significant difference in alpha-diversity by Shannon index (p < 0.05) between groups
- Shannon indices: 6,240.54 (Group I) vs. 5,810.49 (Group II)

### Microbiome Composition Differences
- Both groups dominated by *Firmicutes* and *Bacteroidetes* (>80% combined)
- Group I: higher *Ruminococcaceae* (20.61%), *Lachnospiraceae* (17.27%), *Bacteroidaceae* (16.89%)
- Group II: higher *Bacteroidaceae* (21.33%), *Prevotellaceae* (18.19%), *Ruminococcaceae* (17.04%)
- At genus level, Group I dominated by *Bacteroides*, *Prevotella_9*, *Dialister*
- Group II: higher *Bacteroides*, *Prevotella_9*, *Faecalibacterium*

### Taxa-Lipid Correlations
- *Firmicutes*, *Fusobacteria* negatively correlated with LDL-C
- *Cyanobacteria* and *Lentisphaerae* positively correlated with LDL-C
- *Bacteroidaceae* and *Acidaminococcaceae* positively correlated with TG and LDL-C
- 42 differentially abundant OTUs between groups (LDA score > 3.0)

### Age and Gender Effects
- Patients aged 65-75 had significantly more *Fusobacteria* and notable lower OTUs of *Bacteroidaceae*
- Gender-based differences observed at family level

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Statin-microbiome interaction** - Demonstrates that [[pharmacomicrobiomics|statins]] efficacy correlates with gut microbiome composition, suggesting microbiome-based prediction of treatment response
2. **Personalized medicine** - Gut microbiome profiling could guide [[pharmacomicrobiomics|statin]] therapy optimization in [[cardiovascular-disease]] patients
3. **Lipid-microbiome axis** - Specific taxa correlate with lipid parameters, opening avenues for microbiome-targeted lipid management
