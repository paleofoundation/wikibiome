# Saito 2019 -- Synbiotics Suppress Colitis-Induced Tumorigenesis in Colon-Specific Cancer Mouse Model

## Key Claims

- Synbiotics treatment (combination of *L. casei* Shirota + *B. breve* Yakult + galactosyl-sucrose prebiotic) suppressed DSS-induced dextran sulfate sodium colitis and tumorigenesis in CPC;Apc mice, reducing mortality and inhibiting tumorigenesis accelerated by DSS administration
- Neither probiotics nor prebiotics alone had any effect on inflammation and tumorigenesis, demonstrating synergistic benefit of the combined synbiotic approach
- *Lactobacillus casei* and *Bifidobacterium breve* were detected in fecal microbiota of synbiotics-treated mice, confirming colonization
- Synbiotics treatment suppressed DSS-induced expression of inflammatory genes *IL-6*, *STAT3*, *COX-2*, and *TNF-alpha* in normal colon epithelium, indicating suppression of inflammation-driven tumor development
- DSS administration promoted colonic tumor development in CPC;Apc mice with loss of *Apc* heterozygosity and beta-catenin accumulation in tumor cell cytoplasm; tumors were well-differentiated adenocarcinomas

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: CPC;Apc colon-specific cancer mouse model (CDX2P 9.5-NLS Cre; Apc+/flox); DSS-induced chronic colitis (1% DSS weeks 8, 11, 14, 17); synbiotics orally administered from week 7 to 20
- **Methods**: Histopathological scoring, immunohistochemistry (beta-catenin, CDX2, p53, Ki-67), RT-qPCR for inflammatory genes, bacterial enumeration by RT-qPCR
- **Key finding**: Synbiotics specifically suppressed the inflammatory milieu that drives colitis-to-cancer progression, targeting IL-6/STAT3 and COX-2 pathways
