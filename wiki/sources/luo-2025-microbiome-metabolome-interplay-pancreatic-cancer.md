# Key Claims

- The predominant intratumoral microbiome species in [[pancreatic-cancer]] is *Pseudomonas*, with significant differences in alpha and beta diversity between tumor and adjacent normal tissues
- Untargeted [[metabolomics]] identified 298 metabolites significantly altered in PC (fold change >1.5, P < 0.05), with amino acid metabolites (Lys-Leu, Pro-Leu, Arg-Leu, etc.) prominently dysregulated
- KEGG pathway analysis highlighted glycine, serine, and threonine metabolism; alanine, aspartate, and glutamate metabolism; and amino acid biosynthesis as key altered pathways
- Correlation analysis revealed significant associations between *Pseudomonas* abundance and several metabolites including alpha-ketoisovaleric acid, 16-hydroxyhexadecanoic acid, and myristic acid

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: 47 paired pancreatic cancer and adjacent normal tissues analyzed by 16S rRNA sequencing + untargeted metabolomics (LC-MS/MS)
- **Diversity analysis**: Significant differences in alpha diversity (Chao 1, P=0.84; Simpson, P=0.021; Shannon, P=0.026) and beta diversity (ANOSIM index P=0.001) between tumor and adjacent tissues
- **Dominant intratumoral species**: *Pseudomonas* (LDA score >5.74), followed by Pseudomonadales, Pseudomonadaceae; adjacent tissues enriched in Alphaproteobacteria, Moraxellaceae, *Acinetobacter*
- **Metabolite alterations**: Upregulated in tumors -- Zaleplon, Derythromimidazoyl Glycol Phosphate, Dobutamine, Pro-hyp, 1-methyl-3-isobutylxanthine, N-methylnicotinamide; Downregulated -- multiple amino acid dipeptides
- **Pathway enrichment**: Met PA analysis highlighted alanine/aspartate/glutamate metabolism, taurine and hypo taurine metabolism, cysteine and methionine metabolism
- **Microbe-metabolite correlation**: *Pseudomonas* significantly correlated with altered amino acid metabolism (Spearman |r| >0.5, P < 0.05)
