# Key Claims

- Pre- and post-natal exposures to environmental factors predispose to the onset of neurodegenerative diseases in later life
- Neurotoxic metals (Pb, Hg, Al, Cd, As) and some pesticides/nanoparticles are involved in AD via their ability to increase amyloid-beta and phosphorylated tau (P-Tau)
- Exposure to lead, manganese, solvents, and some pesticides is related to PD hallmarks including mitochondrial dysfunction, metal homeostasis alterations, and alpha-synuclein aggregation
- Common mechanisms include oxidative stress increasing amyloid-beta and alpha-synuclein, reduced activity of degrading enzymes (neprilysin, IDE), and epigenetic changes
- Epigenetic mechanisms by maternal nutrient supplementation and heavy metal/pesticide exposure have been proposed to lead phenotypic diversity and susceptibility to NDDs

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of epidemiological and experimental studies on environmental factors in AD and PD

 - Pb exposure in early life increases amyloid-beta and APP levels in aged rats; effect is age-dependent
 - Hg exposure increases amyloid-beta levels and reduces neprilysin expression in SH-SY5Y cells
 - Al exposure shown to increase APP mRNA expression and amyloid-beta levels in animal models
 - Cd and As in combination with Pb induce amyloid-beta and APP processing changes
 - Circulating miRNAs differentially associated with AD by modulating genes involved in amyloid-beta production (BACE1) and inflammation

 - Mn treatment (50-300 uM) induces cytochrome C release, caspase activation, and protein aggregation in dopaminergic neurons
 - Iron and its deregulated homeostasis proposed to have role via Fenton reactions generating ROS
 - Pesticides (rotenone, paraquat, dieldrin) shown to cause alpha-synuclein aggregation and dopaminergic neuron loss
 - Nanoparticles can cross BBB and affect brain areas

 - Early developmental arsenic exposure impacts DNA methylation at promoter regions of genes involved in neuronal plasticity
 - Lead exposure affects APP gene methylation status
 - Gestational Pb exposure produces AD-like changes in aged animals through epigenetic reprogramming
