## Key Findings

- Prospective cohort at Children's Hospital of Chongqing Medical University (April 2020–October 2021). 113 preterm infants (GA ≤34 weeks) with early GI symptoms; 17 ultimately developed NEC (Group N at diagnosis, Group P at enrollment) matched 1:1 to 17 non-NEC (Group C).
- **Alpha diversity** (Ace, Chao1) was HIGHER in Group P (pre-NEC) than controls (p<0.05) — counterintuitive, suggesting blooms of opportunistic taxa before NEC onset rather than diversity collapse. Simpson/Shannon unchanged.
- **Genus-level comparison (P vs C)**: *Clostridioides*, *Blautia*, *Clostridium_sensu_stricto_1* increased; *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium* (unclassified Bacilli) decreased. At diagnosis (Group N vs C): *Stenotrophomonas*, *Streptococcus*, *Prevotella* increased.
- **Species-level pre-NEC (P vs C)**: **Streptococcus salivarius** and **Rothia mucilaginosa** significantly increased; **Bifidobacterium animalis subsp. lactis** and unclassified *Lactobacillus* significantly decreased.
- **SCFAs (GC-MS)** — pre-NEC (Group P) vs control (Group C): **acetic acid** (AUC 0.73), **propionic acid** (AUC 0.70), **butyric acid** (AUC 0.68) all significantly lower in pre-NEC. Isobutyric, isovaleric, valeric, hexanoic: not significantly different.
- Clinical outcomes: NEC group had higher septicemia (88.2% vs 23.5%, p=0.000), longer hospital stay (83.4 vs 48.4 days, p=0.007), and 23.5% required surgery.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Supports [[necrotizing-enterocolitis]] disease entity with evidence that SCFA depletion and commensal loss PRECEDE disease onset — the dysbiosis is causal/predictive, not merely reactive.
- Reinforces [[bifidobacterium]] entity as a protective commensal whose depletion increases NEC risk — specifically B. animalis subsp. lactis.
- Adds *Streptococcus salivarius* and *Rothia mucilaginosa* as early NEC-risk oral-origin organisms (both oral-associated), suggesting oral-gut translocation as a risk factor.
- Strengthens [[butyrate]] / [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] concept pages: butyrate depletion is not just a marker of NEC but an antecedent.

## Relevance to Cureva

- Predictive biomarker panel (acetate + propionate + butyrate, combined AUC not reported but individual 0.68–0.73) — supports clinical use of fecal SCFA measurement for early NEC risk stratification.
- Reinforces the probiotic intervention rationale (Bifidobacterium animalis subsp. lactis) as specifically relevant to NEC prevention — ties to the Zhou 2023 probiotics meta-analysis.
- STOP signal: early broad-spectrum antibiotic use that further depletes Bifidobacterium may accelerate NEC risk trajectory.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Increased alpha diversity (Ace/Chao1) pre-NEC contrasts with the usual "low diversity = dysbiosis" narrative. Suggests NEC precursor state = unusual diversity from opportunist bloom, not simple depauperation. This is consistent with Lin 2025 where diversity drops only at the acute phase.

## Open Questions

- Temporal resolution: how many days before NEC onset does the SCFA depletion become detectable? Could guide screening frequency.
- Mechanism of S. salivarius / R. mucilaginosa enrichment pre-NEC: oral colonization, PICC/ET tube reflux, or inherent feature of at-risk infants?
