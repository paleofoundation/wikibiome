# Wagner 2008 — Pseudomonas 16S rDNA in pediatric CD ileal biopsies

## Key Findings

- **Pseudomonas sequences more prevalent in CD.** Pseudomonas-specific 16S PCR on ileal biopsies of 50 CD and 36 non-IBD children. 18/32 (56%) of CD children PCR-positive vs 12/36 (33%) non-IBD (p<0.05). CD group enriched in Pseudomonas-specific signal despite normal flora being Pseudomonas-positive at baseline.
- **CD Pseudomonas is more diverse.** 581 sequences clustered into 6 OTUs. OTU1 (closest to `P. xanthomarina`) dominated CD library (67.2%) but only 43.3% of non-IBD. OTU4, OTU5, OTU6 were preferentially present in CD. Some OTUs grouped with `P. protegens`, `P. nitroreducens`, `P. psychrophila`.
- **P. aeruginosa was uniquely in non-IBD patients.** The canonical opportunistic `P. aeruginosa` (which many would expect in inflammatory disease) was detected only in non-IBD subjects. CD-associated Pseudomonas appears to be environmental/soil-origin species, raising the question of whether dietary/environmental exposure drives colonization.

## Methods

DNA extracted from 64 ileal biopsies of Australian children (2005–2007). Pseudomonas-specific 16S rDNA PCR performed with genus-specific primers. Positive PCR products cloned and sequenced. Jukes-Cantor distance matrix, LIBSHUFF and DOTUR analyses for comparing CD vs non-IBD libraries, Fisher's exact test for per-OTU enrichment.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **[[crohns-disease-signature]] layer 2 (taxonomic) — minority signal.** Pseudomonas carriage is a secondary signal (~56% carriage) but biologically plausible: Pseudomonas species are iron-scavenging siderophore producers (pyoverdine, pyochelin).
- Primitive 8 (siderophore competition) is active — Pseudomonas pyoverdine is a classical iron-piracy system that could contribute to iron sequestration in inflamed mucosa.

## Open Questions

- Does paediatric CD Pseudomonas carriage predict different disease trajectories vs Pseudomonas-negative CD?
- Do metagenomic shotgun studies confirm or refute the paediatric CD Pseudomonas enrichment seen here? (Several later studies show mixed results.)
- n=62 is small; replication in larger cohorts needed.
