# Key Claims

- Genetically predicted IBD is causally associated with 8 extraintestinal manifestations: PBC (OR: 1.453), sarcoidosis, iridocyclitis (OR: 1.216), interstitial lung disease (OR: 1.226), atopic dermatitis, ankylosing spondylitis (OR: 1.357), psoriatic arthropathies (OR: 1.266), and PSC (OR: 1.273)
- IBD causally alters gut microbiota composition: increased Bacteroidaceae, Porphyromonadaceae, Bacteroides, Erysipelatoclostridium; decreased Holdemanella, Ruminococcus 2
- Gut microbiota does NOT mediate the causal pathway from IBD to extraintestinal manifestations -- the microbiome is a consequence rather than a mediator
- The gut-skin, gut-liver, and gut-lung axes are confirmed as IBD-related pathways, but through non-microbiome mechanisms

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample MR with mediation analysis; IBD as exposure, 20 EIMs as outcomes, gut microbiota as potential mediator
- **Data sources**: IIBDGC for IBD (31,665 cases/33,977 controls); MiBioGen for microbiota (18,340 individuals); multiple EIM GWAS
- **IBD-to-EIM results** (Figure 2): 8 of 20 EIMs causally associated; strongest for PBC (P=1.29e-6) and interstitial lung disease (P=6.10e-23)
- **IBD-to-microbiota** (Figure 3): 11 causal associations identified; 5 stable (Erysipelatoclostridium, Holdemanella, Odoribacter, Ruminococcus 2, Ruminococcus gauvreauii)
- **Microbiota-to-EIM** (Figure 4): Multiple associations found for sarcoidosis, iridocyclitis, ILD, atopic dermatitis, etc. but these do NOT overlap with IBD-altered taxa, ruling out mediation
