# Key Claims

- Copper (Cu) concentration of 1.9 umol/L reduced growth of the methanogen Methanococcus maripaludis, while zinc (Zn) at 1.0 mmol/L enhanced growth; the two metals interact competitively at the ACS/CODH active site
- VFAs (acetate and propionate) do not directly interact with the C-fixation machinery of M. maripaludis; propionate uptake by this methanogen is a novel finding important for VFA cycling in anaerobic environments
- Combination of Cu and Zn at highest tested concentrations counteracted Cu toxicity, likely because both are divalent cations competing for the same transporter
- [[short-chain fatty acids|SCFAs]] (acetate) at 10.3 mmol/L supported growth without reduction of specific growth rate; propionate stimulated growth and CH4 production
- These findings have implications for understanding VFA cycling and metal-microbe interactions in anaerobic microenvironments including the gut

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original experimental research; closed batch cultivation of M. maripaludis S2 on H2/CO2
- **Zn effects**: Complete growth inhibition at 2.4 and 3.5 mmol/L; 1.0 mmol/L showed only longer lag phase; maximum OD values 1.07-0.63 depending on concentration
- **Cu effects**: Growth inhibited at concentrations >1.9 umol/L (two orders of magnitude lower than Zn threshold); 4.4 and 6.3 umol/L further retarded biomass
- **Zn rescues Cu toxicity**: Addition of 1.0 mmol/L Zn with increasing Cu showed homogeneous, Cu-independent growth (Figure 2c)
- **Acetate effects**: Growth at 12.2 mmol/L acetate showed OD of ~1.25; without acetate, acetate generation compromised due to disruption of ADP-forming acetyl-CoA synthetase
- **Propionate effects**: Putative growth inhibition at high propionate via H+ gradient disruption; propionate stimulated CH4 production at 52.0 and 104.1 mmol/L (Figure 3c)
- **Key metrics**: mu_max, MER_max, and qCH4_max analyzed across 35 experimental conditions (Figure 5)
