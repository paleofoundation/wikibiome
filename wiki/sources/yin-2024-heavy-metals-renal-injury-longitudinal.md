# Key Claims

- [[chromium]] positively associated with NAG (beta=2.12) and negatively with eGFR-EPI (beta=-0.97) and eGFR-MDRD (beta=-0.91)
- [[cadmium]] positively associated with NAG (beta=3.81), beta2-MG, umAlb, and UACR; negatively with eGFR
- [[manganese]] positively associated with NAG (beta=-2.14 negatively with NAG at high levels), UACR (beta=2.60)
- Synergistic effects found for Cd-Cr on NAG, UACR; Cd-Pb on NAG, UACR; Pb-Cr on umAlb, UACR, eGFR-MDRD, eGFR-EPI
- Triple synergistic effect of Pb-Cd-Cr on UACR
- Metal mixtures positively associated with NAG and negatively with umAlb in BKMR model
- Females and smokers showed higher kidney damage at same metal exposure levels

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Longitudinal study with 4 repeated measurements, Northeast China, 2016-2021; n = 384 participants (1,536 observations)
- **Metals**: Urinary Cr, Cd, Pb, Mn measured by ICP-MS
- **Biomarkers**: NAG, beta2-MG, umAlb, UACR, eGFR-MDRD, eGFR-EPI
- **Methods**: Linear Mixed Effects (LME) model, Quantile g-computation, BKMR, GAMM, Marginal Effect models
- Population: 60% female, mean age 60.7, mostly Han ethnicity, 60% farmers
- Cd had highest PIP for NAG and Mn for beta2-MG in BKMR
- Dose-response curves showed near-linear negative trend of Cr with eGFR-MDRD and eGFR-EPI

# Connections

- Rare longitudinal design strengthens causal inference for [[chronic kidney disease]] and metal exposure
- Synergistic metal interactions support the concept of cumulative [[nephrotoxicity]] from [[sabath-2012-renal-health-heavy-metal-nephrotoxicity]]
- [[chromium]] nephrotoxicity via ROS production, cell apoptosis, mitochondrial dynamics disorder
- [[cadmium]] damages proximal tubules via [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]] and mitochondrial dysfunction
- [[lead]] activates NF-kappaB, renin-angiotensin system -- inflammatory kidney damage
- Sex and smoking as effect modifiers relevant to [[environmental health disparities]]
