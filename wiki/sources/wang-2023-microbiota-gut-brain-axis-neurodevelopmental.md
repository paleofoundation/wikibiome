# Overview

Comprehensive review of the **microbiota–gut–brain axis in neurodevelopmental disorders (NDDs)**, including **autism, ADHD, and Rett Syndrome**. Identifies three major pathways through which microbiota influences neurodevelopment and mechanisms disrupted in NDDs.

# Three Major Pathways

## Pathway 1: Neuronal Pathway

- **Vagal signaling**: gut microbiota → vagus nerve → CNS
- **Neurotransmitter-producing bacteria**: direct production of serotonin, GABA, dopamine precursors
- **Microbial-derived signals** (short-chain fatty acids, metabolites) → neuronal function

## Pathway 2: Immune Pathway

- Microbiota-derived antigens activate **intestinal immune system**
- **T-cell education** in gut (Th17, Treg, ILCs)
- Systemic immune tolerance development
- **Dysbiosis** → dysregulated immune response affecting CNS
- Mechanisms: cytokine signaling, blood-brain barrier (BBB) integrity, microglial activation

## Pathway 3: Endocrine/Systemic Pathway

- Microbiota regulates **short-chain fatty acid production** → metabolic signaling
- Influences **metabolic endotoxemia** (LPS-driven systemic inflammation)
- Affects **serum metabolite profiles** (tryptophan, phenolic compounds, etc.)
- Impacts **hormonal signaling** (growth hormone, thyroid, etc.)

# Overlaps and Cross-Talk

- **Three pathways are not isolated** — significant cross-talk
- Immune activation affects neuronal function (cytokines, BBB permeability)
- Metabolite changes affect both immunity and neurotransmission
- Single dysbiotic event can cascade through all three pathways

# Neurodevelopmental Disorders Reviewed

## Autism Spectrum Disorder

- **Dysbiosis documented** across multiple studies
- **Microbiota modification** shows promise for symptom reduction
- Mechanisms: immune dysregulation + reduced tryptophan/SCFA production
- Developmental window sensitivity: dysbiosis during critical neurodevelopment → lasting effects

## Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

- Overlapping GI symptoms with autism
- **Dysbiosis associated with ADHD severity**
- Mechanisms overlap with ASD (immune, metabolite, neurotransmitter pathways)

## Rett Syndrome

- **Genetic mutation (MECP2)** causes CNS dysfunction
- Evidence for **secondary dysbiosis** in Rett Syndrome
- Microbiota dysbiosis may **amplify or perpetuate symptoms**
- Potential microbiota intervention target

# Physiological Functions Affected by Dysbiosis in NDDs

- **Intestinal permeability** ↑ → BBB dysfunction
- **SCFA production** ↓ → reduced epigenetic regulation, intestinal barrier support
- **Tryptophan metabolism** ↓ → reduced serotonin/AhR signaling
- **Immune tolerance** ↓ → microglial activation, neuroinflammation
- **Neurotransmitter precursor production** ↓ → altered CNS monoamine signaling

# Microbiota Interventions for NDDs

- **Probiotics**: modest effects; strain-specific
- **Prebiotics**: support beneficial taxa growth
- **FMT/MTT**: comprehensive dysbiosis reversal; limited NDD data
- **Dietary modification**: influence dysbiosis pattern; emerging evidence

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**High relevance** — explicitly connects dysbiosis to neurodevelopmental dysfunction via three mechanistic pathways, all of which are **heavily metal-dependent**:

### Metal Cofactors in NDD-Relevant Pathways

- **Intestinal barrier (tight junctions, mucus)**: [[zinc]]-dependent proteins (claudins, MUC2, ZO-1)
- **Immune tolerance (Treg, IL-10, TGF-β)**: [[zinc]], [[iron]]-dependent signaling
- **Tryptophan metabolism (IDO, kynurenine pathway)**: [[iron]]-dependent (IDO), [[zinc]]-dependent (KAT)
- **SCFA production**: [[iron]]-dependent pyruvate dehydrogenase, acetyl-CoA production
- **BBB integrity**: [[zinc]]-dependent tight junctions (occludin, claudins), [[iron]]-dependent hypoxia response
- **Microglial function**: [[iron]]-dependent enzymes, [[zinc]]-dependent signaling
- **Vagal signaling**: unclear but likely metal-dependent (neurotransmitter synthesis, neuropeptide release)

### ASD-Specific Metal Hypothesis

**Dysbiotic microbiota** may:
1. **Sequester metals** (siderophore producers, biofilm-forming taxa)
2. **Impair metal absorption** (dysbiotic mucus layer, altered pH, bile acid metabolism)
3. **Dysregulate systemic metal distribution** (hepcidin, inflammatory response → iron sequestration; IL-6 → zinc redistribution)
4. → **Local and systemic metal deficiency phenotypes** that perpetuate:
   - Impaired barrier function (zinc)
   - Reduced SCFA/tryptophan production (iron)
   - Dysregulated immunity (zinc, iron)
   - Perpetual dysbiosis (nutrient-limited competition favors dysbiotic taxa)
