# Key Claims

- The [[gut-metal-microbiome|gut microbiome]] is required for full protection against acute [[arsenic]] toxicity in mouse models
- Antibiotic-treated and germ-free mice excrete less arsenic in stool and accumulate more arsenic in organs compared to controls
- Mice lacking the arsenic detoxification enzyme As3mt are hypersensitive to arsenic after antibiotic treatment or when germ-free
- Human microbiome (stool) transplants protect germ-free As3mt-KO mice from arsenic-induced mortality, but protection depends on microbiome stability and presence of specific bacteria
- [[Faecalibacterium prausnitizii]] provides protection against arsenic toxicity and is sufficient for at least partial protection

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Experimental animal study (mouse models)
- **Cefoperazone (Cef) treatment**: Cef-treated mice excreted significantly less arsenic in feces and accumulated more in liver, spleen, heart, lung (p<0.0001 at 25 ppm; p=0.025 at 100 ppm iAsV)
- **GF As3mt-KO mice**: Significantly lower survival than Sham-treated As3mt-KO mice at both 25 ppm (p<0.0001) and 100 ppm (p<0.0001) iAsV
- **Human stool transplants**: Humanized GF As3mt-KO mice survived significantly longer than conventional GF As3mt-KO mice (p<0.0014); inter-individual differences in protection observed across 5 human donors
- **Microbiome diversity and survival**: Increased alpha diversity (Shannon and inverse Simpson) significantly correlated with longer survival (p=0.0447 and p=0.0155); microbiome stability also correlated with survival
- **Specific OTU associations**: 48 OTUs significantly associated with survival; Faecalibacterium was identified as consistently associated with protection
- **F. prausnitizii gnotobiotic experiments**: E. coli + F. prausnitizii bi-colonization significantly increased survival compared to E. coli alone (p=0.0035) and E. coli mono-associated mice (p=0.0024)
- **16S rRNA gene sequencing**: Arsenic exposure significantly perturbed microbiome composition (p<0.0001, Kruskal-Wallis test); perturbation magnitude depended on starting microbiome composition
