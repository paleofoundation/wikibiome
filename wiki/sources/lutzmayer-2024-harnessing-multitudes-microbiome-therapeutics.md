# Harnessing the Multitudes: Charting the Future of Microbiome Therapeutics

## Summary

This IQVIA white paper consolidates industry insights on the microbiome therapeutics field following the FDA approval of the first FMT-based microbiome drugs (Ferring Pharmaceuticals, Nov 2022; Seres Therapeutics, Apr 2023). It covers microbiome innovation, clinical progress, regulatory alignment, investor trends, and key success factors.

## Key Findings

### Mapping Microbiome Therapeutics (Continuum)
- **FMT**: minimally processed stool-derived material; variable composition; limited characterization
- **Microbiome ecosystem therapies**: industrial-scale standardized products
- **Live biotherapeutic products (LBPs)**: engineered consortia regulated as biologics/drugs
- **Probiotics/prebiotics/postbiotics**: food/supplements, not disease-targeted
- **Non-living biotherapeutics and phage therapies**: inactivated microbes or bacteriophages with targeted action

### Industry Trends
- Field transitioning from exploratory to focused, evidence-based product development
- Disease-related microbial signatures increasingly used for patient stratification
- Companies like GMT Science and BioCorteX developing AI-driven metagenomic profiling
- ZOE's PREDICT studies using large-scale multi-omics for personalized nutrition
- Focus shifting to reproducibility, scalability, and regulatory readiness

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This industry overview contextualizes how [[gut-microbiome]]-targeted therapies could reach [[multiple-sclerosis]] patients. The regulatory pathway for live biotherapeutic products (LBPs) is now established, enabling development of MS-specific microbiome interventions. The phage therapy category is particularly relevant to the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] axis, as bacteriophages could be designed to selectively eliminate metal-tolerant pathobionts that thrive in [[heavy-metals]]-contaminated gut environments, potentially restoring beneficial [[short-chain-fatty-acids]]-producing communities.

## Limitations

- Industry white paper, not peer-reviewed research
- Focused on commercial landscape rather than scientific mechanisms
- Does not specifically address MS or neurological applications
