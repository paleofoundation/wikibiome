## Key Findings

- **Bidirectional CKD-microbiome axis**: CKD drives dysbiosis through uremic milieu changes, and the resulting dysbiosis accelerates renal decline via uremic toxin production (Wehedy et al. 2022, expert-opinion, narrative review).
- **Uremic toxin producers enriched**: [[eggerthella-lenta]] and [[fusobacterium-nucleatum]] are consistently enriched in CKD patients, contributing to indoxyl sulfate (IS) and p-cresyl sulfate (PCS) generation.
- **SCFA producers depleted**: [[roseburia]], [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]], [[bifidobacterium]], and [[prevotella]] are reduced, diminishing butyrate-mediated renal protection.
- **TMAO as progression marker**: Trimethylamine N-oxide inversely correlates with eGFR and independently predicts cardiovascular events in CKD.
- **Dietary modulation**: Fiber supplementation and low-protein diets shift fermentation from proteolytic (toxin-generating) to saccharolytic (SCFA-generating) pathways.

## Methods

Narrative review synthesizing 57 studies across clinical cohorts, animal models, and mechanistic investigations of the gut-kidney axis in CKD.

## Detailed Summary

The review frames CKD-associated dysbiosis as a self-reinforcing cycle. Uremic retention solutes alter intestinal pH and oxygen gradients, selecting for proteolytic organisms ([[eggerthella-lenta]], [[fusobacterium-nucleatum]]) at the expense of saccharolytic commensals ([[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]], [[roseburia]], [[bifidobacterium]]). The proteolytic shift increases production of IS (from tryptophan), PCS (from tyrosine), and TMAO (from choline/carnitine). These toxins damage renal tubular cells, promote fibrosis, and impair barrier integrity — further worsening dysbiosis.

[[akkermansia-muciniphila]] depletion contributes to mucin layer thinning and increased translocation. [[alistipes]] is noted as variably reported — enriched in some CKD cohorts, depleted in others.

Dietary interventions (high fiber, low protein, resistant starch) consistently reduce IS and PCS levels, supporting the ecological engineering principle of shifting the fermentation balance.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- [[alistipes]] role is inconsistent across studies — sometimes enriched, sometimes depleted in CKD.
- The review acknowledges that dietary interventions show variable effect sizes across CKD stages.
