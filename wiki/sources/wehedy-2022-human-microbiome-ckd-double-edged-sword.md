# The Human Microbiome in CKD: A Double-Edged Sword

## Key Claims

- Relationship between [[chronic-kidney-disease]] and [[dysbiosis]] is bidirectional
- Gut-derived metabolites and toxins affect CKD progression, and uremic milieu affects microbiota
- Low-protein and high-fiber diets increase abundance of SCFA-producing and anti-inflammatory bacteria

## Methods & Findings

Review of gut and urinary microbiome roles in CKD pathophysiology and potential dietary interventions.
