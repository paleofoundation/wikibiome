# Key Claims

- **Women with endocrine disease history** had a 42% higher risk of [[postpartum-depression]] (risk ratio 1.42, 95% CI: 1.24-1.62) compared to women with no endocrine disease
- **Reverse association** also found: women with PPD history had 50% higher risk of endocrine disease (HR 1.5, 95% CI: 1.4-1.6)
- **Bi-directionality** suggests a common underlying factor linking endocrine disorders and PPD
- Endocrine diseases included thyroid disease, pre-pregnancy diabetes, PCOS, and prior gestational diabetes

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Nationwide register-based cohort study; 888,989 Danish deliveries (1996-2018); Cox proportional hazards model
- **Key implication**: More attention should be given to pregnant women with endocrine disease history for early PPD screening
