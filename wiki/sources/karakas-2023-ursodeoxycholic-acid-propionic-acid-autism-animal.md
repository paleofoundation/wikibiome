# Key Claims

- **UDCA administration ameliorated** PPA-induced autism-like behavioral, biochemical, and histopathological changes in rats
- **Sociability, open field, and passive avoidance** learning tests were impaired in PPA group but closer to normal in PPA+UDCA group
- **MDA, TNF-alpha, IL-2, IL-17, NF-KB, lactate, NGF, and NRF2** levels in brain tissues showed significant differences between controls and PPA+UDCA groups
- **PPA injection caused** increased glial activity, neural body degeneration, decreased neuron count and dysmorphic changes in hippocampal and cerebellar tissues
- **UDCA treatment significantly ameliorated** these histopathological changes

# Methods & Findings

**Animal study** using 30 male Wistar albino rats in three groups.

- Controls, PPA-induced autism model (250 mg/kg/day intraperitoneal, 5 days), PPA + oral UDCA (100 mg/kg/day)
- Oral treatments applied for 15 days
- Behavioral tests and MR spectroscopy at day 15
- Brain tissue and blood samples collected for histopathology and biochemistry
- UDCA reduced neuroinflammation and oxidative stress markers

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Bile acid therapy** as novel approach to modulate gut microbiota and reduce neurotoxic metabolites in ASD
- **PPA-autism model** demonstrates microbiota-derived metabolite effects on brain
- **UDCA** may represent a repurposable drug for ASD with established safety profile
