# The Effects of Probiotic Supplementation on Gene Expression Related to Inflammation, Insulin, and Lipids in Patients With Multiple Sclerosis

**NOTE**: This paper has an associated **Expression of Concern** (2022) from the journal. The publisher raised serious concerns about the integrity of the reported methods, results, and analysis. The authors were unable to provide original data. Interpret with due caution.

## Key Claims

- 12-week probiotic supplementation significantly downregulated gene expression of [[IL-8]] (p < 0.001) and [[TNF-alpha]] (p < 0.001) in peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) of MS patients compared to placebo
- No significant effect was observed on gene expression of [[IL-1]], [[PPAR-gamma]], or oxidized low-density lipoprotein receptor (LDLR)
- This was claimed as the first study evaluating probiotic effects on gene expression related to inflammation, insulin, and lipids in [[multiple-sclerosis]] patients

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial
- **Participants**: 40 patients with relapsing-remitting MS (EDSS score <=4.5), aged 18-55, at Shahid Beheshti Hospital in Kashan, Iran (July 2016 - October 2016)
- **Intervention**: Probiotic capsule containing *Lactobacillus acidophilus*, *Lactobacillus casei*, *Bifidobacterium bifidum*, and *Lactobacillus fermentum* (2 x 10^9 CFU/g each) for 12 weeks (n=20 probiotic, n=20 placebo)
- **Primary Outcome**: Gene expression in PBMCs measured by RT-PCR
- **Results**: Probiotic group showed significant downregulation of IL-8 and TNF-alpha mRNA vs placebo; no effect on IL-1, PPAR-gamma, or LDLR
- **Proposed Mechanism**: Probiotics may reduce pro-inflammatory cytokine gene expression through modulation of TLRs, NF-kappaB, and MAPK pathways; increased butyrate production may also contribute

## Connections

- Companion study to [[kouchaki-2017-clinical-metabolic-probiotic-ms]] which used the same probiotic formulation and reported clinical/metabolic outcomes
- The probiotic strains used overlap with those studied in [[tankou-2018-probiotic-microbiome-ms]] (human trial) and [[dargahi-2020-streptococcus-thermophilus-ms]] (mouse model)
- The anti-inflammatory gene expression effects are consistent with findings from [[libbey-2018-diet-microbiota-eae]] showing that *Lactobacillus* inversely correlates with disease severity in the EAE model
- Relevant to broader [[gut-brain-axis]] and [[microbiome-immune-modulation]] themes in MS
- The focus on [[TNF-alpha]] and [[IL-8]] connects to the cytokine networks also studied in the context of [[metal-homeostasis]] where inflammatory signaling is disrupted
