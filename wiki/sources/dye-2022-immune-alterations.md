## Key Findings

- **Immune dysregulation**: Postpartum mental illness involves significant aberrations in immune system function, including elevated inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-8) and altered immune cell populations (systematic review level).
- **Postpartum-specific mechanisms**: The postpartum period involves unique immunological transitions (shift from Th1 to Th2, restoration of immune tolerance) that create vulnerability windows distinct from general depression (systematic review level).
- **Microglia and neuroinflammation**: Evidence from animal models and clinical studies suggests microglia activation and central nervous system inflammation contribute to postpartum mood symptoms (systematic review level).

## Methods

Comprehensive systematic review synthesizing evidence from both basic science (animal models) and clinical research examining immune alterations in postpartum mental illness, including postpartum depression and anxiety.

## Detailed Summary

The review establishes that postpartum mental illness is not merely an extension of antepartum depression, but involves distinct immune-related mechanisms:

- **Inflammatory markers elevated**: TNF-α, IL-6, IL-8, and other pro-inflammatory cytokines elevated in postpartum depression and anxiety
- **Immune cell changes**: Altered T-cell populations, reduced regulatory T cells (Treg), shifted Th1/Th2 balance
- **Microglia activation**: Maternal immune activation during pregnancy and postpartum can activate microglia, altering brain neuroinflammatory state
- **HPA axis**: Dysregulation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis contributing to cortisol abnormalities
- **Neuroplasticity**: Inflammatory signals impair hippocampal neurogenesis and synaptic plasticity underlying mood regulation
- **Hormonal factors**: Rapid hormonal transitions (estrogen, progesterone, prolactin) interact with immune system changes

The review highlights that both pregnancy and the postpartum period involve dramatic immunological remodeling, and dysregulation of this process contributes to mental health vulnerability.
