# The Effects of Ketogenic Metabolic Therapy on Mental Health and Metabolic Outcomes in Schizophrenia and Bipolar Disorder

## Key Claims

- RCT protocol for 100 stable community patients with [[schizophrenia]], schizoaffective disorder, or bipolar disorder, comparing modified ketogenic diet (MKD) vs Australian Guide to Healthy Eating over 14 weeks
- Brain constitutes 2-3% of body weight but consumes ~20% of basal metabolic energy; glucose is the main energy substrate
- Systemic glucose metabolism abnormalities (hyperglycemia, insulin resistance) predate antipsychotic agents and are found in treatment-naive first-episode psychosis patients
- Reduced expression of glycolytic enzymes and diminished activity in glutamatergic neurons of dorsolateral prefrontal cortex observed in schizophrenia
- Multiple mitochondrial impairments including altered Complex-I and Complex-V expression demonstrated in schizophrenia brains
- Brain imaging shows decreased glucose utilization (hypofrontality) and elevated brain lactate in schizophrenia
- Primary outcomes: PANSS, Young Mania Rating Scale, Beck Depression Inventory, WHO Disability Assessment, cognitive battery
- Secondary outcomes: body weight, blood pressure, lipid profiles, insulin resistance, ketone and glucose levels
- Optional measures include hair cortisol, fecal microbiome composition, autonomic nervous system activity via wearables

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Randomized controlled clinical trial protocol (registered)
- **Design**: 14-week intervention with 2-week adaptation, stratified by diagnosis, sex, and duration of illness
- **Theoretical basis**: Ketones as alternative brain fuel bypassing impaired glucose metabolism
