# Multiple Sclerosis Patients Have an Altered Gut Mycobiome and Increased Fungal to Bacterial Richness

## Key Claims

- RRMS patients have a distinct gut mycobiome with phylum-level shift from Ascomycota toward Basidiomycota; Basidiomycota/Ascomycota ratio significantly increased in MS (p = 0.0053)
- *Candida* (p_adj = 0.021) and *Epicoccum* (p_adj = 0.021) are significantly increased in MS, while *Saccharomyces* (p_adj = 0.021) is depleted
- *Malassezia* increased in MS but did not reach statistical significance after multiple comparison correction
- Random forest analysis identifies *Candida*, *Saccharomyces*, *Epicoccum*, *Malassezia*, and *Penicillium* as the most important discriminating fungal genera (Boruta algorithm, p < 0.01)
- Fungal functional profiles differ: amino acid permease, cellobiohydrolase, endoglucanase, and invertase decreased in MS; amylase increased
- Increased fungal-to-bacterial richness ratio in MS; more diverse fungal-bacterial interactions in MS
- Bacterial microbiome of same patients: decreased *Barnesiellaceae* and *Barnesiella*; decreased *Odoribacter*; increased *Eggerthellaceae* and *Eggerthella*; increased *Blautia* and *Hungatella*

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Case-control; 20 RRMS patients (McDonald criteria) and 33 healthy controls; University of Iowa
- **Methods**: ITS2 rRNA sequencing for fungi; 16S rRNA V3-V4 for bacteria; same fecal samples; FungalTraits database for functional profiling
- **Demographics**: Mean age 43 (MS) vs 42 (HC); BMI significantly higher in MS (30 vs 24, p = 0.015); 75% female
- **Fungal results**: 136 genera identified; 73 unique to MS, 31 to HC; beta diversity significantly different (Bray-Curtis PERMANOVA p = 0.011); alpha diversity (Chao1) not significantly different (p > 0.08)
- **Phylum level**: Basidiomycota increased (p_adj = 0.011); Ascomycota decreased (p_adj = 0.011) in MS
- **Species level**: *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* (88% of Saccharomyces) and *Candida albicans* (81% of Candida) dominate
- **Bacterial findings**: Lower bacterial richness in MS (Chao1 p = 0.020); distinct beta diversity (p = 0.004); *Prevotella* and *Akkermansia* trends consistent with prior MS studies
- **Dimethyl fumarate effect**: Significant impact on mycobiome composition (p = 0.872 for DMF vs not significant for other DMTs)
- **Limitations**: Small sample; BMI significantly different between groups; cross-sectional; ITS2 sequencing has fungal-specific biases
