# The Potential Links Between Human Gut Microbiota and Cardiovascular Health and Disease - Is There a Gut-Cardiovascular Axis?

## Summary

Review exploring the gut-heart axis concept, covering bacterial, viral, and fungal microbiota contributions to CVD. Discusses SCFAs, TMAO, bile acids, and polyamines as key metabolite classes linking gut microbiota to cardiovascular health.

## Key Findings

### Bacterial Microbiota and CVD
- F/B ratio altered in CVD; higher capacity for LPS production in CAD microbiomes
- Butyrate producers (*Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*, *Eubacterium rectale*) depleted in CVD
- LPS from gut bacteria binds TLR4, triggering NF-kB [[inflammation]] cascade
- *Salmonella* spp. can breach intestinal epithelium, causing diarrhea and water/electrolyte loss

### Fungal Microbiota and CVD
- *Candida* more prevalent in CVD patients; can damage blood vessels and promote [[inflammation]]
- *Aspergillus* also linked to increased CVD risk
- *Mucor racemosus* associated with decreased CVD risk (Framingham Risk Score and cIMT)
- Mycobiome research in CVD still in early stages

### SCFAs
- Acetate, propionate, and butyrate have anti-inflammatory effects and improve endothelial function
- Butyrate is the primary energy source for colonocytes
- Propionate may help reduce food intake, promote weight loss, and improve insulin sensitivity

### Bile Acids
- Primary BAs (CA, CDCA) produced in liver; secondary BAs produced by gut bacteria via 7-alpha dehydroxylation
- BAs act through FXR and TGR5 receptors; affect fatty acid oxidation, triglyceride accumulation, NF-kB
- Elevated secondary BAs and increased ratio of secondary:primary BAs associated with CVD

### Polyamines (Cadaverine, Putrescine, Spermidine)
- Bacterially synthesized from amino acid decarboxylation
- Spermidine is cardioprotective: reduces cardiac hypertrophy, improves echocardiographic parameters
- Spermidine modifies intestinal microbiota toward more anti-inflammatory composition (*Desulfovibrionaceae* increase)

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Multi-kingdom perspective** - Integrating bacterial, viral, and fungal microbiota provides a comprehensive framework for understanding [[gut-metal-microbiome]] contributions to CVD
2. **Bile acid-metal interactions** - [[Iron]] and [[copper]] affect bile acid metabolism; metal-induced bile acid dysregulation may accelerate atherosclerosis
3. **Polyamine metabolism** - [[Cadmium]] disrupts amino acid metabolism, potentially reducing cardioprotective polyamine production
4. **Gut-heart axis model** - Provides conceptual framework for metal-microbiome-CVD pathways
