# Key Claims

- **Specific micronutrient deficiencies** (vitamins A, D, E, C, iron, [[zinc]], selenium, omega-3 fatty acids) are associated with [[postpartum-depression]] and anxiety outcomes
- **Vitamin D deficiency** is commonly associated with PPD; folate exposure at beginning of pregnancy is especially important
- **Iron deficiency** creates physiological stress that could lead practitioners to misunderstand or underestimate PPD risk
- **Gut-brain connection** influences feelings toward the newborn via brain activity and dopamine pathways
- **Vitamin C** is important for immune support during lactation; B12 is necessary for offspring DNA creation

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Literature review and research poster; database search using "postpartum depression," "maternal mental health," and related terms
- **Key conclusion**: Micronutrient deficiencies during pregnancy and postpartum are modifiable risk factors for PPD
