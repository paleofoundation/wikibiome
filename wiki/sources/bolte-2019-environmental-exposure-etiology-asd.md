# Overview

Comprehensive review from Karolinska Institute mapping **environmental risk factors for ASD** including parental age, teratogenic compounds, perinatal risks, medication, smoking, alcohol, nutrition, vaccination, toxic exposures, and psychosocial factors. Emphasizes that ASD heritability (~50%) leaves substantial room for environmental contributions. Concludes that multiple-hit and threshold models integrating gene-environment interactions are most promising.

# Key Claims

## Parental Age
- Advanced maternal age: OR 1.84 for age >40 vs <25 (meta-analysis of 27 observational studies)
- Advanced paternal age: OR 0.93 for 10-year increase but only when mother is <30
- Mechanisms: de novo mutations, epigenetic drift, altered hormonal milieu

## Fetal Environment
- **Teratogens**: valproate (4-5% ASD risk), thalidomide, misoprostol
- **Maternal hormones**: sex steroids (testosterone), altered zinc-copper cycles
- **Infections during pregnancy**: rubella, CMV, influenza — OR 2.8-fold increase for hospitalization
- **Maternal immune activation (MIA)**: LPS, poly(I:C) → offspring neuroinflammation

## Prenatal Nutrition
- **Folate**: protective factor — prenatal supplementation reduces ASD risk
- **Vitamin D**: deficiency associated with increased ASD risk
- **Fatty acid intake**: emerging evidence for protective role
- **Iron**: not specifically addressed but implied through methylation/folate pathway

## Toxic Exposures
- **Air pollution**: PM2.5 and PM10 associated with increased ASD risk
- **Pesticides**: organophosphates — proximity during pregnancy linked to ASD
- **Heavy metals**: not extensively discussed but referenced via air pollution studies
- Specificity of environmental risks remains unknown; multiple confounders uncontrolled

## Medication
- **Valproate**: strongest medication-associated risk
- **SSRIs**: conflicting evidence; some studies show 1.5-4.5-fold increase
- **Acetaminophen**: emerging concern

## Psychosocial Factors
- Limited evidence for direct psychosocial causation
- Extreme psychosocial deprivation (e.g., Romanian orphanages) → quasi-autistic features
- Stress during pregnancy may interact with genetic vulnerability

## Key Methodological Recommendations
- Twin studies using MZ and DZ discordance designs
- Longitudinal and experimental designs needed
- Gene-environment interaction studies
- Multiple-hit and cumulative risk models
- Critical windows of neurodevelopmental vulnerability

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**Moderate relevance** — environmental exposures interact with metal homeostasis:

- **Altered [[zinc]]-[[copper]] cycles** explicitly mentioned as potential mechanism
- **Air pollution** delivers [[heavy-metals]] to developing fetus
- **Folate/methylation pathway** requires [[zinc]], [[iron]], B-vitamins
- **Valproate** affects histone deacetylases (HDACi) — [[zinc]]-dependent enzymes
- **Vitamin D** metabolism involves [[iron]]-dependent cytochrome P450s
- Multiple-hit model could incorporate metallomic disruption as one "hit"
