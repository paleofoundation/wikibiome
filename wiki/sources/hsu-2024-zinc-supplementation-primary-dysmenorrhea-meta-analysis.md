# Key Findings

- **Zinc supplementation significantly reduced dysmenorrhea pain severity** compared to placebo (Hedges's g = −1.541; 95% CI: −2.268 to −0.814; P < 0.001), representing a clinically meaningful effect ([[hsu-2024-zinc-supplementation-primary-dysmenorrhea-meta-analysis]], systematic review/meta-analysis, 6 RCTs, n=739).
- **Longer treatment durations (≥8 weeks)** enhanced efficacy (P=0.003), while the incremental benefit per additional milligram was modest (regression coefficient = −0.02 per mg, P=0.005).
- **Doses as low as 7 mg/day** of elemental zinc produced significant pain relief — much lower than typical supplementation doses.
- **No significant difference in adverse events** between zinc and placebo groups (OR 2.54; 95% CI: 0.78 to 8.26; P=0.122), supporting good tolerability.
- Zinc's proposed mechanisms: inhibiting cyclooxygenase activity, inhibiting prostaglandin production, enhancing microcirculation, preventing ischemia in uterine tissues, and increasing superoxide dismutase (SOD1) activity.

# Methods

- Systematic review and meta-analysis following PRISMA 2020 guidelines.
- Registered in PROSPERO (CRD42024579213).
- Searched PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library, Web of Science, Google Scholar through Aug 2024.
- Included 6 RCTs of zinc supplementation for primary dysmenorrhea.
- Quality assessed via Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) tool.
- Random-effects model; meta-regressions for dose and duration effects.

# Detailed Summary

This is the first systematic review and meta-analysis specifically evaluating zinc for primary dysmenorrhea. The large effect size (g = −1.541) is notable and clinically meaningful. The dose-response analysis suggests that even low doses are effective, and the duration analysis indicates that zinc needs ≥8 weeks to achieve optimal benefit — consistent with zinc's role in enzyme regulation rather than acute pharmacological action. The mechanism involves zinc's anti-inflammatory properties (COX inhibition, prostaglandin reduction) and antioxidant effects (SOD1 upregulation).
