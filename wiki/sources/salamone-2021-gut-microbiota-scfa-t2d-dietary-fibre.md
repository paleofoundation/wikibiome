# Key Claims

- **SCFAs are modulators of metabolic pathways** influencing glucose metabolism and insulin sensitivity
- **Dietary fibre may increase SCFA production** via prebiotic effect on fibre-fermenting bacteria
- **Main mechanisms of beneficial gut microbiota effect**: (1) maintenance of intestinal barrier, (2) reduction in bacterial translocation/endotoxemia, (3) SCFA production
- **Dietary fibre plays pivotal role** by virtue of prebiotic effect on fibre-fermenting bacteria that may increase SCFA production
- **No specific microbial communities** related to onset of T2D have been identified yet

# Methods & Findings

Review of observational studies and RCTs investigating dietary fibre-microbiota-SCFA-glucose metabolism:

- Gut microbiota composition influenced by internal and external factors
- Genetics plays marginal role; external factors (diet, antibiotics) are dominant
- Possible mechanisms behind fibre-SCFA-glucose association discussed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Fibre-fermenting bacteria producing SCFA require zinc-dependent fermentation enzymes
- **[[iron]]**: Dietary fibre chelates iron, reducing iron availability for pathogenic bacteria while feeding beneficial fermenters
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-enriched dietary fibre could provide combined prebiotic and antioxidant benefits
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Dietary fibre shapes the metal-microbiome by modulating metal bioavailability alongside prebiotics
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: SCFA-mediated barrier restoration is the primary mechanism of dietary fibre benefit
- **[[probiotics]]**: Synbiotic approach (fibre + [[probiotics]]) may maximize SCFA production for diabetes prevention
