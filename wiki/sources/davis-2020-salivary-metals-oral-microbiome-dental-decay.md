# Low Levels of Salivary Metals, Oral Microbiome Composition and Dental Decay

## Key Findings

- **21 metals quantified in saliva**: All 5 essential metals (cobalt, copper, manganese, molybdenum, zinc) detected in nearly all participants. Of 12 heavy metals tested, 4 (antimony, arsenic, mercury, cadmium) detected above LOD in 7-100% of participants. Zinc had the highest concentrations (median 303 ppb) [[davis-2020-salivary-metals-oral-microbiome-dental-decay]], cross-sectional, n=61.

- **Metal-specific microbiome shifts**: After Benjamini-Hochberg correction:
  - **Antimony**: Significantly increased relative abundance of 4 unclassified *Lactobacillus* species and *E. coli* (effect sizes 0.60-0.69).
  - **Arsenic**: Significantly decreased *Prevotella* species (effect size -0.46).
  - **Mercury**: Significantly decreased *Granulicatella* (effect size -0.46) and increased *Prevotella* species (effect size 0.49-0.54).
  - Metals were NOT associated with alpha diversity (Shannon, Chao1) but WERE associated with specific taxon composition [[davis-2020-salivary-metals-oral-microbiome-dental-decay]].

- **Metals associated with dental decay**: Antimony levels in the upper tertile associated with untreated dental lesion (OR = 3.91, 95% CI 0.99-15.40). Several metals associated with presence of untreated carious lesions [[davis-2020-salivary-metals-oral-microbiome-dental-decay]].

## Methods

- 61 participants (37 families) from Center for Oral Health Research in Appalachia Study I. Stimulated saliva samples. ICP-MS for 21 metals. 16S rRNA V6 region sequencing. ALDEx2 for differential abundance. Mixed linear models within families.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- **[[dental-caries]]** — metal-microbiome interface in caries pathogenesis.
- **[[antimicrobial-metals]]** — even low-level salivary metals shift oral microbiome composition at the species level.
- **[[arsenic]]** — arsenic depletes oral Prevotella, potentially affecting oral-systemic translocation.
- **[[mercury]]** — mercury shifts oral community structure.
- **[[lactobacillus]]** — antimony-driven Lactobacillus enrichment (context-dependent: cariogenic in oral cavity).
- Key insight: **metal levels below clinical toxicity thresholds still reshape microbial communities** — subclinical metal exposure matters for microbiome ecology.

## Open Questions

1. Do salivary metal-microbiome associations predict periodontal disease progression?
2. Does antimony-driven Lactobacillus enrichment in the oral cavity increase caries risk (Lactobacillus is cariogenic orally but probiotic in the gut)?
3. Can salivary metallomics serve as a non-invasive biomarker for systemic metal exposure effects on the gut microbiome?
