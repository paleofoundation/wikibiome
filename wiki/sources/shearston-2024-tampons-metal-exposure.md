# Tampons as a Source of Exposure to Metal(loid)s

## Key Claims

- All 16 metals assessed were found at measurable concentrations in tampon samples, including toxic metals Pb (GM 120 ng/g), Cd (GM 6.74 ng/g), and As (GM 2.56 ng/g)
- **Nickel** detected in 100% of samples: geometric mean 80.1 ng/g (range 0.76-2.66 ug/g), median 75.6 ng/g
- Calcium and zinc had elevated concentrations (Ca GM 39,000 ng/g; Zn GM 52,000 ng/g)
- Metal concentrations differed by tampon characteristics: organic vs non-organic, US vs EU/UK origin, store vs name-brand
- Most metals were higher in non-organic tampons; arsenic was higher in organic tampons
- No single category had consistently lower concentrations across all metals
- The vagina is a highly absorptive tissue, and metals leaching from tampons could cross the vaginal epithelium into systemic circulation

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Laboratory analysis of 30 tampons from 14 brands and 18 product lines purchased in US and EU/UK
- **Method**: ICP-MS after microwave-acid digestion; 60 samples total (about 0.2-0.3g per sample)
- **Metals measured**: As, Ba, Ca, Cd, Co, Cr, Cu, Fe, Hg, Mn, Ni, Pb, Se, Sr, V, Zn
- **Detection rates**: As (95%), Ba/Ca/Cd/Co/Cu/Fe/Mn/Ni/Pb/Sr/Zn (100%), Cr (10%), Hg (8.3%), Se (98.3%), V (100%)
- **Pb finding**: Detected in all samples -- no safe exposure level exists for lead
- **Regional differences**: EU/UK tampons had higher concentrations for most metals; US tampons higher for Pb in 4 metals
- **Low variability within products**: Metal concentrations similar across absorbency levels and lot numbers within product lines
- **Limitations**: Did not measure metal leaching rates or actual systemic absorption; focused on total metal content
