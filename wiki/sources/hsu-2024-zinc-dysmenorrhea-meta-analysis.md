# Key Claims

- [[zinc]] supplementation significantly reduces pain severity in women with primary [[dysmenorrhea]] compared to placebo: Hedges's g = -1.541 (95% CI: -2.268 to -0.814, p < 0.001), representing a large effect size
- Longer treatment durations (>=8 weeks) enhance efficacy: meta-regression coefficient = -0.452 per month (p < 0.001)
- Higher daily zinc doses are associated with greater pain reductions: regression coefficient = -0.02 per mg (p = 0.005)
- Doses as low as 7 mg/day of elemental zinc are sufficient for significant pain relief
- Zinc supplementation is well-tolerated with no significant difference in adverse events vs placebo: OR = 2.536 (95% CI: 0.78-8.26, p = 0.122)

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis following PRISMA 2020 guidelines; registered in PROSPERO (CRD42024579213)
- **Search**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Web of Science, Google Scholar through August 2024
- **Included studies**: 6 RCTs involving 739 participants aged 13-27 years
- **Countries**: Iran (4 studies), Pakistan (1), Nigeria (1)
- **Zinc forms**: Zinc sulfate (4 studies), zinc gluconate (1), zinc sulfate + mefenamic acid (1)
- **Daily doses**: Ranged from 7 mg/day (zinc gluconate) to 150 mg/day (zinc sulfate); treatment durations 1-6 months
- **Pain assessment**: All used Visual Analog Scale (VAS, 0-10)
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2); 33% low risk, 67% some concerns

 - 1 month: Hedges's g = -0.529 (95% CI: -1.163 to 0.105, p = 0.102) -- not significant
 - 2 months: g = -1.409 (95% CI: -2.146 to -0.672, p < 0.001) -- significant
 - 3 months: g = -1.08 (95% CI: -1.697 to -0.439, p = 0.001) -- significant
- **Heterogeneity**: High (I2 = 94.31%), partly explained by dose and duration differences
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Consistent results after removing any single study
- **Publication bias**: Funnel plot showed some asymmetry suggesting potential bias
- **Mechanism**: Zinc inhibits prostaglandin production via cyclooxygenase modulation, enhances microcirculation, exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties through SOD1 upregulation
