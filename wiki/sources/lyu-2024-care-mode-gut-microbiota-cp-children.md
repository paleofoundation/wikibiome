## Key Findings

- **Family-centered care enriched butyrate-producing genera** ([[bacteroides]], [[lachnospiraceae]]) in the gut microbiome of CP children, compared to welfare-centered (institutional) care.
- **Welfare-centered care was associated with higher abundance of [[prevotella]], [[fusobacterium]], and [[oscillibacter]]** — genera that include opportunistic pathogens and pro-inflammatory species.
- **Care modality functions as an ecological intervention**: diet, feeding practices, environmental exposure, and caregiver consistency shape the gut microbiome independently of the neurological condition itself.
- This reframes the care environment as a modifiable ecological variable rather than just a social welfare consideration.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Informs entity pages for [[cerebral-palsy]], [[bacteroides]], [[lachnospiraceae]], [[fusobacterium]], and [[prevotella]].
- **Primitive 5** (two-sided ecological engineering): the care environment simultaneously promotes beneficial organisms (via diet and feeding practices in family care) and selects against opportunists — or fails to do so in institutional settings.
- Highlights that microbiome composition in CP is not fixed by the neurological condition — environmental and social factors are powerful modifiers.

## Relevance to Cureva

- Provides ecological context for CP signature interpretation: microbiome data from CP studies must be interpreted in light of care setting as a confounding variable.
- Supports intervention design: improving care practices (feeding consistency, diet quality, environmental diversity) may be a low-cost ecological intervention for CP gut health.
- The Fusobacterium enrichment in welfare care is concerning given Fusobacterium's role in inflammatory conditions and biofilm formation.

## Open Questions

- Which specific aspects of family-centered care drive butyrate-producer enrichment — diet composition, feeding frequency, or environmental microbial exposure?
- Does transitioning from welfare to family care shift the microbiome toward a healthier profile?
- How does care mode interact with medication effects on the gut microbiome?
