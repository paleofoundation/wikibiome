# Research on Metabolic Characteristics of Multiple Sclerosis

## Key Claims

- 56 differential fecal metabolites identified between MS patients and healthy controls (P < 0.05, VIP > 1) from 552 total metabolites detected by untargeted UHPLC-Q-TOF MS
- 8 metabolites upregulated in MS: L-pyroglutamic acid, 4-pyridoxic acid, 2-oxoadipic acid, L-phenylalanine, and 3-methylphenylacetic acid among others
- 48 metabolites downregulated in MS including propionic acid, isobutyric acid, oleic acid, alpha-linolenic acid, adenosine, palmitic acid, succinate, lithocholic acid, nicotinic acid, L-arginine, choline, uracil, creatinine, capsaicin
- Enriched KEGG pathways in differential metabolites: protein digestion and absorption, nicotinate and nicotinamide metabolism, central carbon metabolism in cancer, ABC transporters, mTOR signaling pathway, amino acid biosynthesis, aminoacyl-tRNA biosynthesis
- Amino acids and fatty acids are the most enriched differential metabolite categories, suggesting potential biomarker value
- This is the first untargeted fecal metabolomic analysis in a northern Chinese MS cohort

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Case-control; 37 MS patients (35 RRMS, 2 PPMS) and 30 age/gender-matched healthy controls; northern China (Inner Mongolia)
- **Demographics**: MS patients aged 21-62 years (13 males, 24 females); controls aged 26-59 (7 males, 23 females)
- **Methods**: UHPLC-Q-TOF MS (AB Triple TOF 6600); multivariate analysis including PCA, PLS-DA, OPLS-DA; KEGG pathway enrichment
- **Dietary matching**: Rough stratification based on food type, fruit/vegetable intake, meat consumption, dairy; no FFQ used
- **Key downregulated metabolites**: Propionic acid and isobutyric acid (SCFAs), consistent with SCFA depletion in MS; lithocholic acid (secondary bile acid); alpha-linolenic acid (anti-inflammatory omega-3)
- **Key upregulated metabolites**: L-phenylalanine (aromatic amino acid associated with neuroinflammation); 3-methylphenylacetic acid
- **Pathway implications**: mTOR signaling dysregulation may affect immune cell metabolism; nicotinamide pathway disruption affects NAD+ homeostasis
- **Limitations**: Article in press (preprint stage); small sample size; no FFQ for precise dietary assessment; cross-sectional design; single-center study
