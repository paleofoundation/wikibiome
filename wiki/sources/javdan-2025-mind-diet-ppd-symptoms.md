# Key Claims

- **PPD prevalence** was 15.4% in this Iranian cohort (n=214 women, 2-8 weeks postpartum)
- **Higher MIND diet adherence** was significantly inversely associated with [[postpartum-depression]] symptoms
- Women in the highest tertile of MIND diet scores had significantly lower chance of PPD (OR: 0.04, 95% CI: 0.006-0.37; P<0.001)
- MIND diet combines Mediterranean and DASH dietary patterns, emphasizing brain-healthy foods
- PPD prevalence in Iran reaches 28.7%

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Cross-sectional study; 214 women 2-8 weeks postpartum in Kermanshah, Iran; EPDS, Beck scale, and semi-quantitative FFQ (148 food items)
- **Analysis**: ANCOVA and regression logistic analyses adjusting for confounders
- **Key implication**: MIND diet as accessible dietary intervention for PPD prevention
