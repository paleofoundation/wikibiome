# Sundström-Poromaa 2023 — New Pharmacological Approaches to PMDD Management

## Key Findings

- **Ulipristal acetate** (a selective progesterone receptor modulator, SPRM) showed **clinically significant reduction** in PMDD mental symptom severity in a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover RCT — with maintained low-medium oestradiol levels and negligible side effects ([[sundstrom-poromaa-2023-pmdd-pharmacology-sprm]], review).
- **SSRIs** are valid first-line treatment but **30–50% of MDD/PMDD patients** are insensitive to antidepressants, and SSRIs have a high relapse rate upon discontinuation ([[sundstrom-poromaa-2023-pmdd-pharmacology-sprm]]).
- **Sepranolone** (isoallopregnanolone, a GABA_A modulating steroid antagonist) showed promise in Phase 2 trials but **did not significantly differ from placebo** on primary endpoint — further study needed ([[sundstrom-poromaa-2023-pmdd-pharmacology-sprm]]).
- The putative PMDD mechanism is **maladaptive neural reactivity** to gonadal hormone fluctuations — drugs suppressing or stabilizing these variations have been tested ([[sundstrom-poromaa-2023-pmdd-pharmacology-sprm]]).
- SSRIs in PMDD work via a **different mechanism** than in depression — efficacy within days (vs. weeks for MDD), suggesting direct neurosteroid interaction rather than serotonergic reuptake inhibition ([[sundstrom-poromaa-2023-pmdd-pharmacology-sprm]]).
- The GABA_A receptor system, particularly **alpha-4 and delta subunits**, is altered in PMDD — target for sepranolone ([[sundstrom-poromaa-2023-pmdd-pharmacology-sprm]]).
