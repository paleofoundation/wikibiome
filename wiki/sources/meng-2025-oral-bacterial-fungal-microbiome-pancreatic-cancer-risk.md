# Key Claims

- Three oral bacterial periodontal pathogens (P. gingivalis, E. nodatum, P. micra) and four oral fungi (including Candida) were associated with increased risk of pancreatic cancer in a large prospective cohort of 122,000 individuals
- A microbial risk score (MRS) combining 27 risk-associated bacterial and fungal species conferred more than 3-fold increase in pancreatic cancer risk per 1-SD increase (OR 3.44, 95% CI 2.63-4.51)
- Candida tropicalis (fold change 1.43, p=0.08) was associated with increased risk; Candida albicans was associated with decreased risk
- Oral fungal and bacterial microbiota hold promise as accessible, non-invasive biomarkers for pancreatic cancer risk identification

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Nested case-control within 2 prospective US cohorts (CPS-II and PLCO); 445 incident PC cases matched 1:1 with 445 controls; median follow-up 8.8 years
- **Sequencing**: Whole-genome shotgun sequencing for oral bacteria + ITS1 for fungi
- **Bacterial findings**: Red complex (P. gingivalis, T. forsythia) and orange complex (E. nodatum, P. micra) periodontal pathogens associated with increased PC risk
- **Bacteriome-wide scan**: 21 oral bacterial species associated with PC risk after FDR correction; 13 Proteobacteria and 3 Bacteroidetes species decreased risk; 6 Actinobacteria and certain Firmicutes increased risk
- **Fungal findings**: Candida genus overall 47.6% abundance; Candida tropicalis increased risk; Malassezia globosa not associated; C. albicans slightly decreased risk
- **MRS performance**: Consistent across CPS-II and PLCO cohorts; robust to Monte Carlo cross-validation; stable over follow-up period, BMI, diabetes, alcohol
- **Community clustering**: 3 distinct co-abundance clusters identified among 27 risk-associated taxa
