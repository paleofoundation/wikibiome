# Childhood Trauma, Psychosis and Schizophrenia: A Literature Review with Theoretical and Clinical Implications

## Key Claims

- Child abuse is a causal factor for psychosis and [[schizophrenia]], particularly for hallucinations including voices commenting and command hallucinations
- Among female psychiatric in-patients, the weighted average for child sexual abuse (CSA) was 47.7% and child physical abuse (CPA) was 29.0%
- Among male psychiatric in-patients, the weighted average CSA rate was 28.3% and CPA was 50.1%
- The relationship between childhood trauma and psychosis is causal with a dose-effect pattern
- Studies using the MMPI found that abuse survivors scored higher on schizophrenia and paranoia scales
- Combat veterans with PTSD show symptoms remarkably similar to schizophrenia in both positive and negative symptom domains
- Only 0.05% of articles on schizophrenia in PsycINFO concerned child abuse, compared to 4.1% for genetics and 21.5% for drug therapy

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Literature review
- **Data sources**: PsycINFO (1872 to 2004), supplemented by bibliographic searches
- **Key evidence**: Tables of 39+ studies reporting abuse prevalence in female and male psychiatric populations
- **Theoretical framework**: Bio-psycho-social integration needed; adverse events alter brain functioning
