# Key Claims

- **Gestational diabetes (GDM)** is strongly associated with declines in intellectual function for mothers and higher risk of developmental, behavioural, and ASD in children
- **Mothers with GDM history** scored 2.47 points lower on cognitive assessments than peers without GDM
- **Children exposed to GDM** showed: nearly 4-point IQ reduction, 36% increased risk of ADHD, 56% increased risk of ASD, and 45% higher risk of developmental delays
- **Data from 9 million pregnancies** across 20 countries analyzed
- **GDM affects about 1 in 5 pregnancies** globally

# Methods & Findings

**Global synthesis / meta-analysis** presented at EASD Annual Meeting, Vienna 2025.

- Analyzed data from more than 9 million pregnancies across 20 countries including Singapore
- Mothers with GDM history scored significantly lower on cognitive assessments
- Children of GDM mothers showed significantly elevated risks for ADHD, ASD, and developmental delays
- Called for sustained neurocognitive monitoring after GDM diagnosis
- Suggested non-fasting blood test in first trimester for earlier GDM detection

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Metabolic dysregulation during pregnancy** as major modifiable risk factor for ASD
- **56% increased ASD risk** from GDM exposure represents substantial population-level impact
- **Maternal metabolic health** links to offspring neurodevelopment through multiple pathways including inflammation and glycemic exposure
