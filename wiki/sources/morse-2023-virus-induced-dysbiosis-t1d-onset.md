# Key Claims

- **Coxsackievirus B4 (CVB4) induces restructuring** of the intestinal microbiome in NOD mice prior to T1D onset
- **Microbiome restructuring associated with** eroded mucosal barrier, bacterial translocation to pancreatic lymph node, and increased commensal-reactive antibodies
- **CVB4-induced community composition strikingly similar** to that of uninfected NOD mice that spontaneously developed diabetes, creating a mutual "diabetogenic" microbiome
- **Bifidobacteria and Akkermansia** emerged as conspicuous members of this diabetogenic microbiome
- **FMT of diabetogenic microbiota** from CVB4-infected mice enhanced T1D susceptibility via diminished SCFA receptor GPR43 and fewer IL-10+ Tregs

# Methods & Findings

Original research in NOD mouse model:

- CVB4 infection induced intestinal [[dysbiosis]] before T1D onset
- Diabetogenic microbiome transferable via FMT
- Overlap between viral and microbiome risk factors for T1D demonstrated
- [[intestinal-permeability]] impairment and immune dysregulation central to mechanism

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Viral infection depletes zinc stores, simultaneously impairing gut barrier integrity and selecting for zinc-resistant pathobionts
- **[[iron]]**: Viral-induced inflammation alters iron sequestration, changing the competitive landscape for gut microbes
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium deficiency enhances viral virulence (well-established for Coxsackievirus) and worsens [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Virus-metal-microbiome triangle: viral infection disrupts metal homeostasis, driving [[dysbiosis]] that promotes autoimmunity
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Loss of IL-10+ Tregs reflects metal-dependent immune pathway disruption
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Mucosal barrier erosion is a zinc-dependent process worsened by viral infection
