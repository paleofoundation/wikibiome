# Cortisol Promotes Surface Translocation of Porphyromonas gingivalis

## Key Findings

- **Cortisol stimulates P. gingivalis migration** in a concentration-dependent manner (0.13–13 µM range). Surface translocation on soft agar was activated from the point of inoculation in the presence of 10 µM cortisol [[kim-2022-cortisol-surface-translocation-pgingivalis]], in-vitro.

- **T9SS and fimbrial genes upregulated by cortisol**: qPCR showed significant upregulation of *mfa1*, *mfa5*, *sigP*, *porP*, *sprA*, *PG1881*, *fimC*, *rhs*, *ppad*, *porV*, and *porX* (all p ≤ 0.01). Gingipain genes (*kgp*, *rgpA*, *rgpB*) and heme uptake (*hmuY*) were unchanged [[kim-2022-cortisol-surface-translocation-pgingivalis]], in-vitro.

- **Metabolic coupling requirement**: Surface translocation occurred only with sheep blood, lactate (45 mM), or pyruvate (45 mM) — not with NAD alone. P. gingivalis is asaccharolytic and relies on protein/amino acid substrates; erythrocytes provide both **heme/iron** (via hemolysis) and **lactate/pyruvate** (glycolytic metabolites). Oral streptococci produce abundant lactate, creating a metabolic cross-feeding link [[kim-2022-cortisol-surface-translocation-pgingivalis]], in-vitro.

- **Mfa5 is essential**: The *mfa5* deletion mutant showed significantly less surface translocation; complementation restored the phenotype. Mfa5 is a minor fimbrial tip protein containing a **von Willebrand factor type A (vWF) domain** — a eukaryotic adhesion domain rarely found in bacteria — secreted through the T9SS [[kim-2022-cortisol-surface-translocation-pgingivalis]], in-vitro.

- **Stress-periodontitis link**: Salivary cortisol ranges from 0.02 µM (healthy resting) to >0.15 µM (periodontitis) to >1.24 µM (acute stress). The cortisol concentrations that activated P. gingivalis migration (1.3–13 µM) are within the range of levels found in periodontal pockets during stress, providing a direct mechanism for stress-induced periodontitis progression [[kim-2022-cortisol-surface-translocation-pgingivalis]], in-vitro.

## Methods

- P. gingivalis strains W50 and 381 grown anaerobically (85% N₂, 10% CO₂, 5% H₂) in THBHK broth supplemented with hemin (5 µg/mL) and menadione (1 µg/mL).
- Surface translocation assay on 0.35% soft agar plates ± cortisol ± lactate/pyruvate.
- qRT-PCR for T9SS, fimbrial, and gingipain gene expression.
- Mutant construction: Δmfa5, ΔsprA, ΔfimC, ΔPPAD deletion mutants.
- Migration microscopy in chamber slides under anaerobic conditions.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- **[[porphyromonas-gingivalis]]** — Adds cortisol-responsive virulence mechanism; stress hormones directly activate bacterial migration and tissue invasion.
- **Iron/heme connection**: P. gingivalis surface translocation requires metabolic substrates derived from erythrocyte lysis — confirming that gingival bleeding is not just a symptom but a **nutrient source** that feeds the pathogen's expansion.
- **Metabolic cross-feeding**: Oral streptococci provide lactate that enables P. gingivalis migration — another example of interkingdom/interspecies cooperation driving disease.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Stress management as periodontitis intervention**: If cortisol drives P. gingivalis migration, then stress reduction (cortisol lowering) could slow periodontal disease progression — a non-antibiotic ecological intervention.
- **STOP consideration**: Iron supplementation in periodontal patients may provide additional heme substrate for P. gingivalis migration.

## Open Questions

1. Does the PorXY two-component system directly sense cortisol, or is the effect indirect?
2. Do other stress hormones (adrenaline, noradrenaline) similarly activate P. gingivalis migration?
3. Can cortisol-blocking strategies (local application of cortisol receptor antagonists) reduce P. gingivalis virulence in periodontal pockets?
