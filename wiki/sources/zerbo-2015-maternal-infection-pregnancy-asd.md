# Key Claims

- **No overall association** between any maternal infection during pregnancy and ASD (adjusted OR=1.15, 95% CI 0.92-1.43)
- **Women with infections requiring hospital admission** had increased ASD risk (OR=1.48, 95% CI 1.07-2.04)
- **Bacterial infections** particularly associated with ASD risk (OR=1.58, 95% CI 1.06-2.37)
- **Multiple infections during pregnancy** associated with ASD (OR=1.36, 95% CI 1.05-1.78)
- Severity and type of infection matter more than infection per se

# Methods & Findings

**Nested case-control study** from Kaiser Permanente Northern California clinical databases.

- 407 ASD cases and 2075 frequency-matched controls
- Maternal infections ascertained from clinical databases
- Adjusted for potential confounders
- Infection severity (requiring hospitalization) was the key risk factor
- Bacterial infections showed stronger association than viral
