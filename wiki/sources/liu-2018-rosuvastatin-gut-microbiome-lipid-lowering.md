# Liu 2018 -- Gut Microbiome Associates With Rosuvastatin Lipid-Lowering Effect

## Key Claims

- Rosuvastatin therapy had different blood lipid-lowering effects on hyperlipidemia patients, and the gut microbiota exhibited variation in community composition, diversity, and taxa in association with the hypolipidemic effect
- Group I (high efficacy, n=33) had significantly different Shannon index (P<0.05) from Group II (low efficacy, n=31), suggesting gut microbiome complexity correlates with statin effectiveness
- Phyla *Firmicutes* and *Fusobacteria* were negatively correlated with LDL-C level, while *Cyanobacteria* and *Lentisphaerae* had positive correlation; at family level, *Bacteroidaceae* and *Acidaminococcaceae* positively correlated with TG and LDL-C
- LDA analysis identified 42 differentially abundant taxa between groups; 29 taxa enriched in Group I (high efficacy) vs. 13 in Group II (low efficacy)
- Age and gender influenced gut microbiome distribution in rosuvastatin-treated patients; females of Group I showed higher *Firmicutes*, *Tenericutes*, and *Ruminococcaceae*

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Clinical trial (ChCTR-ORC-17013212) of 64 hyperlipidemia patients treated with rosuvastatin 10 mg/day for 4-8 weeks
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA gene sequencing (V3-V4 regions), QIIME analysis, LEfSe for differential taxa, Spearman correlations between microbiota and lipid parameters
- **Results**: TC reduced ~41.9% in Group I and ~26.6% in Group II; LDL-C reduced ~38.5% and ~26.6% respectively; *Bacteroides* (16.93%), *Prevotella_9* (15.07%), *Dialister* (6.70%) were dominant genera in Group I
