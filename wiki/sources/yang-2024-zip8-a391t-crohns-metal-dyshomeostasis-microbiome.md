# Key Claims

- The Crohn's disease-associated SNP rs13107325 in [[metal-homeostasis|SLC39A8]] (ZIP8 A391T) alters metal ion homeostasis at the colonic mucosal-luminal interface
- Homozygous A393T mice (MUT) exhibit increased cobalt uptake in mucosal epithelium and higher cadmium and manganese in bulk colon tissue, with reduced luminal availability of iron, cobalt, copper, zinc, cadmium, and manganese
- The variant induces age-dependent microbiome compositional shifts, with genotype-microbiome association R^2 increasing from 3% (2-month) to 9% (12-month) cohorts
- MUT mice develop spontaneous intestinal inflammation at 10 months but not at 5 months, suggesting the variant requires aging as an additional insult
- This is the first demonstration that a Crohn's disease-linked missense variant modulates colonic microbiome composition, positioning SLC39A8 A391T as a possible microbiome quantitative trait locus (mb-QTL)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Experimental animal study using CRISPR-generated SLC39A8 A393T knock-in mice (matching human A391T)
- **Trace element analysis (ICP-MS)**: MUT mice had significantly lower Fe, Co, Cu, Zn, Cd, Mn in colonic luminal content; higher Co in mucosal epithelium; higher Co, Cd, Mn in bulk colon tissue (all p<0.05)
- **16S rRNA gene sequencing**: Three cohorts analyzed (2-month luminal/mucosal, 12-month luminal/mucosal, 3-4-month fecal)
 - PERMANOVA: Genotype significantly associated with microbiome in all cohorts; R^2 increased with age (3% at 2 months to 9% at 12 months)
 - Differentially abundant genera: 5-7 in younger mice, 18-29 in 12-month-old mice
 - Lactobacillus reduced in 2-month MUT colonic lumen; Ligilactobacillus decreased in 12-month MUT
 - Staphylococcus, Rikenella, Akkermansia, Candidatus_Arthromitus enriched in 12-month MUT
- **Predicted functional pathways (PICRUSt2)**: "Cofactor biosynthesis" pathways consistently differentially enriched across cohorts; depleted in 2-month MUT mucosa, both depleted and enriched in 3-4-month fecal, exclusively enriched in 12-month MUT
- **Histological scoring**: No inflammation differences at 5 months; significantly increased inflammation at 10 months in MUT vs WT (p<0.05)
- **Chemically induced colitis (DSS, TNBS)**: No differences in body weight, colon length, or histology between genotypes, suggesting the variant does not modulate susceptibility to acute chemical colitis
