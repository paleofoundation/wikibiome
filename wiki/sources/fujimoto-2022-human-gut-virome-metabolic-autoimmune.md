# Characterization of the Human Gut Virome in Metabolic and Autoimmune Diseases

## Summary

Review exploring the role of the intestinal viral microbiome [[virome]] in metabolic and autoimmune diseases. Covers human studies of the gut virome in obesity, NAFLD, T2DM, CVD, hypertension, and autoimmune conditions (T1DM, RA, SLE, MS).

## Key Findings

### Virome in Cardiovascular Disease
- Limited case-control studies characterize the CVD gut virome
- *Virgaviridae* (rod-shaped plant viruses) higher in coronary heart disease (CHD) patients; *Microviridae* lower
- In ACVD cohort: several bacteriophages more or less abundant than in healthy controls; known hosts from Enterobacteriaceae and *Streptococcus*
- In hypertension: two viral types predominant; primary bacteriophages were *Erwinia* phage phiEaH2 and *Lactococcus* phage 1706
- *Cnaphalocrocis medinalis* granulovirus, *Cronobacter* phage CR3, and *Streptococcus* virus phiAbc2 could distinguish healthy from hypertensive/prehypertensive individuals

### Virome in Metabolic Diseases
- Obese subjects: lower diversity and proportionally fewer phages than lean controls
- T2DM: *Siphoviridae* and *Podoviridae* more diverse/rich; four viral species higher and 13 lower in T2DM vs. obesity alone
- NAFLD: lower phage diversity correlated with histologic fibrosis severity
- T2DM: combination of eight selected phages differentiated T2DM from nondiabetic controls (diagnostic potential)

### Virome in Autoimmune Diseases
- T1DM: *E. coli* phage to *E. coli* ratio changes observed prior to seroconversion
- RA: CCP-positive individuals had higher *Streptococcaceae*, *Bacteroidaceae*, *Lachnospiraceae* phages
- SLE: *Podoviridae* with symbiotic relationship to *Faecalibacterium* significantly decreased

### Technical Challenges
- No universally conserved gene in viruses (unlike 16S rRNA for bacteria)
- Whole metagenome shotgun sequencing required; most reads are "viral dark matter"
- Multiple displacement amplification introduces biases

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Viral dysbiosis in CVD** - Gut virome alterations in [[cardiovascular-disease]] represent an underexplored dimension of the microbiome-CVD relationship
2. **Trans-kingdom interactions** - Phage-bacteria dynamics shape the bacterial communities driving CVD-associated metabolite production
3. **Diagnostic potential** - Virome profiling may complement bacterial profiling for CVD risk stratification
