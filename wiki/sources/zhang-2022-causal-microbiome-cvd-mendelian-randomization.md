# Key Claims

- Genus *Oxalobacter* was positively associated with CAD risk (OR=1.06, 95% CI 1.03-1.10, p=1.67x10-4) using two-sample [[Mendelian randomization]]
- Family *Clostridiaceae_1* was associated with decreased risk of stroke (OR=0.83, 95% CI 0.75-0.93, p=7.76x10-4) and ischemic stroke (OR=0.82, 95% CI 0.74-0.92, p=4.15x10-4)
- At the genome-wide statistical significance threshold, no causal relationship was found between gut microbiome and CVDs, suggesting effects may be modest
- The causal association between *Oxalobacter* and CAD attenuated to null after adjusting for blood pressure (SBP or DBP), suggesting blood pressure may mediate the effect
- *Clostridiaceae_1* and stroke causality also weakened after adjusting for blood pressure, further suggesting gut microbiome may affect CVDs risk through blood pressure regulation
- No reverse causality was detected (MR Steiger test), supporting the direction of microbiome to CVD

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample Mendelian randomization study
- **Exposure data**: MiBioGen consortium GWAS (18,473 individuals, 122,110 loci of variation)
- **Outcome data**: CAD from CARDIoGRAMplusC4D/UK Biobank (122,733 cases, 424,528 controls); MI from 48 studies (43,676 cases, 128,199 controls); AF from 55,114 cases; HF from HERMES (47,309 cases, 930,014 controls); stroke from MEGASTROKE (40,585 cases)
- **Methods**: IVW, Wald ratio, MR-Egger, weighted median, MR-PRESSO; sensitivity analyses with leave-one-out, Cochran's Q, MR Steiger
- **Key SNPs**: 2,789 SNPs at locus-wide significance and 17 at genome-wide significance used as IVs
- **Nine CVD outcomes tested**: CAD, MI, AF, HF, stroke (total, ischemic, cardioembolic, large-artery atherosclerotic, small-vessel)
