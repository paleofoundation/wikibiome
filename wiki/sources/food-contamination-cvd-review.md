# Food Contamination and Cardiovascular Disease: A Narrative Review

## Key Findings
- Multiple dietary heavy metals contribute to cardiovascular disease through oxidative stress and vascular damage
- Lead, mercury, cadmium, and arsenic each act through distinct but converging pathways of endothelial dysfunction
- Chronic low-level dietary exposure to multiple metals may have synergistic cardiovascular toxicity
