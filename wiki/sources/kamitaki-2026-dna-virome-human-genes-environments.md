# The DNA Virome Varies with Human Genes and Environments

## Summary

This large-scale study analyzed the DNA viral load of 31 common viruses in human blood and saliva using whole-genome sequencing data from UK Biobank (n=490,401), All of Us (n=414,817), and SPARK (n=12,519). It identified human genetic variation at dozens of loci associated with DNA load of seven viruses, with the MHC/HLA region generating the strongest associations.

## Key Findings

### Viral Prevalence and Abundance
- EBV, HHV-7, and HHV-6B are the most commonly detected viruses in blood and saliva
- Viral DNA load varied by age, time of day, and season; most viruses more abundant in men
- EBV saliva prevalence increased sharply from ~60% at ages 2-4 to >95% by age 10
- HHV-7 viral load in blood and saliva declined with age
- HHV-6B prevalence declined sharply from childhood (~15%) to adults (~8.5%)

### Human Genetic Determinants of Viral Load
- The HLA-B*08:01 allele exhibited a host-virus genetic interaction with EBV subtype (P=7.4x10^-79)
- ERAP1 gene (encoding endoplasmic reticulum aminopeptidase) associated with both EBV and HHV-7 DNA load
- ERAP2 variants also associated with viral load control
- Mendelian randomization supported a strong causal effect of EBV DNA load on increased risk of Hodgkin lymphoma (P=1.8x10^-3), but not [[multiple-sclerosis]] (P=0.52)

### Sex and Environmental Differences
- Most viruses present at greater abundance in men than women
- EBV viral load was significantly higher in men in blood and saliva
- HHV-6B showed sex differences with males having higher abundance in saliva
- Seasonal variation detected: EBV blood prevalence peaked in summer months

## Relevance to Multiple Sclerosis and Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

While this study found no direct Mendelian randomization evidence for EBV DNA load causally increasing MS risk, it is relevant to the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] framework because:

1. **EBV is a major MS risk factor** - the genetic control of viral load involves immune genes (HLA, ERAP1/2) that also influence autoimmune susceptibility
2. **Metal-induced immunosuppression** could impair viral load control, allowing EBV reactivation that triggers MS in genetically susceptible individuals
3. **The virome interacts with the bacterial microbiome** - bacteriophages and eukaryotic viruses in the gut can modulate bacterial community structure, and metal-induced virome changes could compound bacterial [[dysbiosis]]
4. **HLA associations** with viral load control overlap with HLA associations in MS, suggesting shared genetic architecture between viral immunity and autoimmune susceptibility

## Limitations

- WGS-based viral detection measures DNA load, not active infection
- Focused on blood and saliva virome, not gut virome specifically
- Mendelian randomization for MS was negative (P=0.52)
- UK Biobank cohort is predominantly European ancestry
