# Vagus Nerve and the Gut Microbiota-Brain Axis in Behavior and Neurodegenerative Diseases

## Key Findings

- **Vagus nerve anatomy**: The vagus nerve (CN X) is the longest cranial nerve, with **80% afferent (sensory) fibers** carrying gut-to-brain information and 20% efferent (motor) fibers carrying brain-to-gut instructions. Three types of vagal afferent endings: intraganglionic laminar endings (IGLEs — mechanoreceptors), intramuscular arrays (IMAs — stretch receptors), and mucosal endings (chemoreceptors near enterochromaffin cells) [[han-2022-vagus-nerve-gut-microbiota-brain-axis-neurodegeneration]], expert-opinion.

- **Microbiota-vagus-brain signaling**:
  - Enterochromaffin cells release [[serotonin]] when stimulated by microbial SCFAs → vagal afferent activation.
  - Gut bacteria produce neurotransmitters directly: [[gaba]] (Bifidobacterium, Lactobacillus), [[serotonin]] (Enterococcus, Streptococcus), [[dopamine]] (Bacillus, Serratia), norepinephrine (E. coli, Bacillus), [[indole]], and [[tryptamine]].
  - Cytokines ([[interleukin-6|IL-6]], [[il-1beta|IL-1β]], [[tnf-alpha|TNF-α]]) cross into CNS through blood-brain barrier AND activate vagal afferents — dual pathway.
  - [[cortisol]]/HPA axis: Germ-free mice show exaggerated stress responses (elevated ACTH/corticosterone) [[han-2022-vagus-nerve-gut-microbiota-brain-axis-neurodegeneration]].

- **Disease-specific microbiome signatures** (Table 1):

| Disorder | Enriched | Depleted |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Depression | Bacteroidetes, Gammaproteobacteria, Bacteroidales | Firmicutes, Clostridia, Clostridiales, Ruminococcaceae |
| Anxiety | Bacteroides, E. coli, Lactobacillus, Fusobacterium, R. gnavus | Faecalibacterium, Roseburia, Subdoligranulum, Lachnospira |
| ASD | Collinsella, Lactobacillus, Corynebacterium, Dorea | Alistipes, Bilophila, Dialister, Veillonella |
| PD | Bifidobacteriaceae, Lactobacillaceae, Akkermansia | Lachnospiraceae, Roseburia, Faecalibacterium |
| AD | Actinobacteria | Bacteroidetes, Lachnospiraceae, Bacteroidaceae |
| ALS | Bacteroidetes | Firmicutes, Megamonas |

## Open Questions

1. Can targeted vagus nerve stimulation (VNS) modulate microbiome composition via efferent pathways?
2. Do specific microbial metabolites activate distinct vagal afferent subtypes (IGLEs vs. mucosal endings)?
3. Can vagotomy studies in humans confirm the causal role of the vagus in microbiome-brain communication?
