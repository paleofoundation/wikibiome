# Key Claims

- **Anti-inflammatory drug residues** detected in 30.0% of breast milk samples from 90 volunteer mothers in Ankara
- **94.4% had quinolone**, 93.3% beta-lactam, 31.1% aminoglycoside, and 13.3% polymycin residues
- Drugs used during pregnancy or lactation did not affect presence of residues, suggesting **environmental contamination** (food chain)
- Mothers with anti-inflammatory drug residues in breast milk had **significantly lower BMI** changes (p=0.017)
- EPDS scores and infant crying/sleeping problems were similar in cases with and without residues

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Descriptive study; 90 mother-infant pairs; breastfed infants under 3 months; EPDS applied; breast milk analyzed by RANDOX Infiniplex kit
- **Setting**: Hacettepe University, Ankara, Turkey
- **Key implication**: Environmental pharmaceutical contamination in breast milk may affect maternal weight and potentially infant [[dysbiosis]]
