## Key Findings

- **Butyrate pathway depleted**: Butyryl-CoA dehydrogenase — the rate-limiting enzyme for butyrate production — was significantly more abundant in control metagenomes than T1D cases (P=0.0003), establishing a functional mechanism for barrier dysfunction (Brown et al. 2011, case-control, n=8)
- **Iron acquisition genes enriched in T1D**: Metagenomic analysis revealed enrichment of iron acquisition systems including FecB (ferric citrate transport) and IsdDEF analogs (iron-regulated surface determinants) in T1D-associated microbiomes
- **Lactate diversion model**: The functional model proposes that T1D-associated microbiomes divert lactate toward propionate, acetate, and succinate instead of butyrate, weakening the intestinal barrier and enabling bacterial translocation
- **Virulence factor enrichment**: T1D-associated metagenomes were enriched in LPS biosynthesis genes, type VI secretion systems, and pathogenicity island components

## Relevance to Cureva

The lactate diversion model is the mechanistic fulcrum of the T1D signature's ecological layer. Iron acquisition enrichment directly supports Karen's Brain Primitive 8 (siderophore competition). The butyryl-CoA dehydrogenase finding provides a specific enzymatic target for the virulence enzyme layer.

## Contradictions / Tensions

Small sample size (n=8) limits generalizability. Findings are directionally consistent with the larger TEDDY cohort (Vatanen et al. 2018) but the specific functional model has not been independently replicated at scale.

## Open Questions

- Does the lactate diversion model hold in larger cohorts with metagenomic depth?
- Are the iron acquisition genes from specific pathobionts or broadly distributed?
