# Efficacy of the Autoimmune Protocol Diet as Part of a Multi-disciplinary, Supported Lifestyle Intervention for Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

## Key Claims

- A 10-week online Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) diet and lifestyle intervention significantly improved health-related quality of life (HRQL) in middle-aged women with [[hashimotos-thyroiditis]], as measured by all 8 subscales of the SF-36
- Clinical symptom burden (MSQ) decreased significantly from a mean of 92 (SD 25) pre-intervention to 29 (SD 20) post-intervention (p=0.0003, g=2.81)
- [[Inflammation]] as measured by hs-CRP decreased by 29% (p=0.0219) from 1.63 mg/L pre-intervention to 1.15 mg/L post-intervention
- No statistically significant changes were noted in thyroid function (TSH, T3, T4) or thyroid antibodies (TPO, TGA), suggesting AIP improves symptoms without directly altering thyroid hormones
- AIP is a modification of the Paleolithic diet that eliminates foods implicated in disrupting the [[gut microbiome]] flora and the [[intestinal barrier]], including grains, legumes, nightshades, dairy, eggs, coffee, alcohol, nuts, seeds, refined sugars, oils, and food additives

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Pilot study (uncontrolled, pre-post design)
- **Population**: 17 women aged 20-45 with prior HT diagnosis, normal/overweight BMI (18.4-29.9), recruited via social media; 16 completed the study (1 became pregnant)
- **Intervention**: 10-week program consisting of 6-week phased food elimination followed by 4-week nutrient-dense maintenance phase; additions included foods rich in mono/polyunsaturated [[fatty acids]], bone broth, seafood, [[fermented foods]], and organ meats
- **Lifestyle modifications**: Sleep hygiene, stress management, movement, increased outdoor time
- **SF-36 results**: Physical role functioning improved from median 25 to 100 (p<0.001); vitality from 19 to 58 (p<0.001); general health from 40 to 70 (p<0.0001)
- **Thyroid markers**: No significant changes in TSH, total T3, free T3, total T4, free T4, TPO antibodies, or TGA (all p>0.1)
- **hs-CRP**: Post-hoc analysis removing one outlier showed decrease from 1.22 mg/L to 0.85 mg/L (p=0.037, g=0.475)
- **Stool testing**: Week 5 stool and organic acid tests guided individualized dietary recommendations addressing B-vitamin deficiencies, mineral insufficiency, bacterial overgrowth, and pancreatic insufficiency
