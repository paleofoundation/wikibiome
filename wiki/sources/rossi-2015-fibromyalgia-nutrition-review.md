# Rossi 2015 — Fibromyalgia and Nutrition: What News? (Review)

## Key Findings

- **Trace element deficiencies** in FM: magnesium, selenium, iron, zinc — supplementation (particularly Mg and Se) may reduce musculoskeletal pain and protect against oxidative damage ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]], review).
- **Mg deficiency** linked to low-grade chronic inflammation, elevated substance P (pain neurotransmitter), and contributes to FM multisystem features ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- **Se deficiency** linked to musculoskeletal pain; adequate Se intake protects against ischemia-reperfusion tissue damage ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- **Non-coeliac gluten sensitivity (NCGS)** frequently overlaps with FM; gluten elimination shows clinical improvement in some patients ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- **FM patients eat less** dairy (OR=0.32), whole grains (OR=0.59), fresh fruits (OR=0.66), fish (OR=0.64) compared to healthy controls ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- **Oxidative stress** markers elevated in FM: lower total antioxidant capacity, higher ROS. Antioxidant enzyme activity (SOD, GPx, catalase) altered ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- **CoQ10** supplementation showed reduced pain and improved quality of life in FM. **Vitamin D** supplementation widely recommended but definitive evidence lacking ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- **Tryptophan/serotonin pathway:** possible tryptophan depletion in FM via altered TRP degradation (activated by cytokines), linking inflammation to pain sensitization ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
- Cellufood (nutritional supplement with 78 ionic/colloidal minerals and amino acids) found to improve oxidative balance markers in FM pilot study ([[rossi-2015-fibromyalgia-nutrition-review]]).
