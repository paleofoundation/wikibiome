# Key Claims

- Nine bacterial genera were found to be causally associated with CHD via [[Mendelian randomization]]: Class Actinobacteria, Class Lentisphaeria, Family Clostridiales vadinBB60 group, genus *Clostridium innocuum*, genus *Bifidobacterium*, genus *Butyricoccus*, genus *Oxalobacter*, genus *Turicibacter*, and Order Victivallales
- **Protective taxa**: Class Lentisphaeria (OR 0.92), Family Clostridiales vadinBB60 (OR 0.92), genus *Butyricoccus* (OR 0.87), and Order Victivallales (OR 0.92) inhibit CHD occurrence
- **Risk-increasing taxa**: Class Actinobacteria (OR 1.08), genus *Clostridium innocuum* (OR 1.07), genus *Bifidobacterium* (OR 1.07), genus *Oxalobacter* (OR 1.08), and genus *Turicibacter* (OR 1.12) were positively correlated with CHD
- *Bifidobacterium* as a CHD risk factor contradicts its typical probiotic reputation; this may relate to specific species-level effects or strain-dependent metabolite production
- The gut microbiome may influence CHD through TMAO production, SCFA modulation, bile acid metabolism, and immune pathway activation

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample MR using MiBioGen consortium and IEU Open GWAS data
- **Exposure**: 18,340 individuals from 24 cohorts, 131 genera with >1% mean abundance (119 genus-level taxa analyzed)
- **Outcome**: CHD from IEU Open GWAS (184,305 adults, 123,504 controls)
- **Methods**: IVW, weighted median, MR-Egger, simple mode, weighted mode; 2,458 SNPs as IVs for 211 bacterial genera
- **Key validation**: Leave-one-out analysis showed no single SNP dominated the results; MR-PRESSO found no significant outliers
