# Key Claims

- **21 urinary amino acid indicators** significantly different between ASD and TD children
- **7 UAA biomarker panel** discriminates ASD from TD with AUC = 0.92 (lysine, proline, 5-HT, hydroxyproline, arginine/ornithine, aspartate, 2Aib)
- **Ornithine/urea cycle abnormalities** in ASD: elevated arginine, arginine/ornithine ratio, hydroxyproline
- **Tryptophan metabolism disruption**: increased 5-hydroxytryptamine, decreased carnosine and anserine
- **Methionine cycle disruption**: increased methionine sulfoxide, decreased homocysteine

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Two-phase discovery-validation study. Discovery: 28 ASD + 41 TD children; Validation: 29 ASD + 41 TD children. Ages 2-14, predominantly male.

**LC-MS/MS analysis of urinary amino acids:**
- 63 UAA indicators identified; 27 significantly different between ASD and TD after gender adjustment
- Discovery and validation phases confirmed 21 consistent indicators

**Ornithine/urea cycle abnormalities:**
- 8 of 21 different UAAs associated with urea cycle
- Arginine, Arg/Orn ratio, Hyp at higher levels; Pro, Cit, Asp at lower levels in ASD
- Dysfunction of arginase (Arg to Orn) and ornithine transcarbamylase (Orn to Cit) suggested
- May result in accumulation of ammonia in blood

**Methionine cycle abnormalities:**
- Homocysteine (Hcy) and methionine sulfoxide (MetS) abnormal
- Lower Hcy and higher MetS suggest abnormality in methionine cycle intermediates
- Free methionine to S-adenosylmethionine conversion disrupted

**Tryptophan/serotonin pathway:**
- 5-HT elevated in ASD urine (consistent with ~25% of ASD children having elevated blood serotonin)
- Carnosine (Car) and anserine (Ans) decreased - both are antioxidant dipeptides

**SVM biomarker model:**
- 7 UAA indicators: Lys, Pro, 5HT, Hyp, Arg/Orn, Asp, 2Aib
- AUC = 0.92 for discriminating ASD from TD

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Urea cycle-metal link**: Arginase is a manganese-dependent enzyme; its dysfunction in ASD may reflect manganese dyshomeostasis
- **Methionine-zinc axis**: SAM cycle requires zinc-dependent enzymes; methionine cycle disruption indicates systemic zinc insufficiency
- **5-HT-iron link**: Tryptophan hydroxylase (rate-limiting for serotonin synthesis) is an iron-dependent enzyme; elevated urinary 5-HT may paradoxically reflect impaired central serotonin due to iron maldistribution
- **Antioxidant depletion**: Decreased carnosine and anserine (zinc-chelating dipeptides) reflects reduced zinc-dependent antioxidant capacity
