# Microbiome and Metabolome Features of the Cardiometabolic Disease Spectrum

## Summary

Major multi-omic study from the European MetaCardis cohort of 1,241 individuals spanning healthy controls, metabolically matched controls, and IHD patients (including ACS, CIHD, and HF). Integrates gut metagenomics with serum and urine metabolomics to characterize the natural trajectory of ischemic heart disease.

## Key Findings

### Study Design
- 275 healthy controls, 222 untreated metabolically matched controls (UMMCs), 372 metabolically matched controls (MMCs), 372 IHD patients (112 ACS, 158 CIHD, 102 HF)
- Profiled 735 metagenomic species, 770 functional modules, 1,513 serum metabolites, 45 urine metabolites

### Disease Trajectory Features
- 767 dysmetabolism features (DMFs), 283 IHD-specific features (IHDFs), 98 escalation features (ESCFs), 98 de-escalation features (DSCFs)
- Most DSCFs (89% metabolites, 100% microbiome functions) exhibited initial depletion at dysmetabolism stage with reversal at treated IHD
- TMA production (MC0022) and butyrate production (MF0089) showed depletion at dysmetabolism but apparent reversal at IHD

### Key Microbial Findings
- *Bacteroides* 1, *Bacteroides* 2, *Prevotella*, *Ruminococcus* showed progressive changes from HC to IHD
- Gene richness significantly reduced in ACS, CIHD, and HF compared to controls
- Microbiome classifier performance was high for HC vs IHD (AUC >0.8)

### Key Metabolite Findings
- 4-Cresol and phenylacetylglutamine (uremic toxins) were IHD-specific features
- Indoxyl sulfate showed escalation pattern from dysmetabolism to IHD
- O-acetylsalicylate appeared as archetypal DSCF (treatment marker)
- Bilirubin and catecholamine intermediates identified as DSCFs

### IHD Sub-Phenotypes
- CIHD exhibited most differential changes in gut microbiome functional potentials
- ACS exhibited predominantly metabolome alterations unrelated to dysmetabolism
- HF showed unique metabolome markers related to fluid homeostasis

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Uremic toxin trajectory** - Indoxyl sulfate and 4-cresol escalation from metabolic pre-morbidity to IHD suggests a pathway where [[cadmium]] and [[lead]]-driven [[dysbiosis]] accelerates this progression
2. **Butyrate production loss** - Reduced butyrate synthesis capacity aligns with metal-sensitive Clostridia depletion
3. **Gene richness decline** - Microbial gene richness loss in CVD mirrors the diversity reduction seen with heavy metal exposure
4. **[[metabolic-syndrome]] continuum** - The dysmetabolism-to-IHD trajectory provides a framework for understanding how metal-induced metabolic disruption progresses to [[cardiovascular-disease]]
5. **Multi-omic integration** - Demonstrates the power of combining metagenomics and metabolomics for understanding the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] axis
