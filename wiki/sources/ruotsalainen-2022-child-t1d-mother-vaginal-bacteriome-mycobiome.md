# Key Claims

- **Alpha-diversity of bacteriome increased (p<0.002)** in mothers with diabetic child, while alpha-diversity of mycobiome was decreased (p<0.001)
- **Beta-diversity analysis suggested significant differences** in mycobiomes between groups (p=0.001)
- **Random forest models** could effectively predict diabetes and control status (bacteria: 0.86 AUC; fungi: 0.96 AUC)
- **Mother vaginal microbiome** (both bacteriome and mycobiome) associated with child T1D risk
- **Several fungal genera and bacterial metabolic pathways** of mother vaginal microbiome associated with child T1D

# Methods & Findings

25 mothers with T1D child + 24 control mothers:

- Vaginal DNA extracted, 16S (bacteria) and ITS2 (fungi) amplified and sequenced
- Machine learning classification achieved high AUC values
- Suggests early onset of T1D in child has relationship with altered mother vaginal microbiome

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Maternal zinc status influences vaginal microbiome composition and vertical transmission to neonate
- **[[iron]]**: Vaginal iron availability selects for specific bacterial and fungal communities transmitted to infant
- **[[selenium]]**: Maternal selenium deficiency may shift vaginal mycobiome toward T1D-risk profile
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Vertical microbiome transmission establishes the infant's foundational [[gut-metal-microbiome]] from birth
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Maternal vaginal [[dysbiosis]] may be the earliest modifiable risk factor for child T1D
