# Caboni 2014 — Lysophosphocholines-PAF Receptor Interaction in Fibromyalgia (Metabolomics)

## Key Findings

- **Lysophosphocholines (lysoPCs)** — mainly PC(14:0/0:0) and PC(16:0/0:0) — were the most discriminating metabolites between FMS and controls (PLS-DA: R²X=0.345, R²Y=0.901) ([[caboni-2014-lysophosphocholines-paf-fibromyalgia-metabolomics]], case-control, n=43).
- **21 lipid compounds** identified as differentially abundant: lysoPCs, phosphocholines, and ceramides ([[caboni-2014-lysophosphocholines-paf-fibromyalgia-metabolomics]]).
- **Molecular docking and MD simulation** showed that discriminating lysoPCs have PAF receptor (PAFr) binding affinity similar to PAF itself — suggesting these lipid oxidation products act as **PAF-like bioactivators** ([[caboni-2014-lysophosphocholines-paf-fibromyalgia-metabolomics]]).
- **Oxidative fragmentation hypothesis:** lipid peroxidation in FMS generates lysoPCs in abundance, which activate PAF receptor signaling → inflammation → pain ([[caboni-2014-lysophosphocholines-paf-fibromyalgia-metabolomics]]).
- **High IBS comorbidity:** 16/22 (73%) FMS patients had IBS; 16/22 had tension headache; 14/22 had sleep disturbance ([[caboni-2014-lysophosphocholines-paf-fibromyalgia-metabolomics]]).
- FMS patients: age 52±3 (27–72), 68% menopausal, BMI 26±1. Medications: tricyclics (36%), SSNRIs (23%), SSRIs (9%).

## Methods

LC-Q-TOF/MS metabolomics of plasma from 22 FMS patients (2010 ACR criteria) and 21 controls at University of Cagliari, Italy. Multivariate analysis (PCA, PLS-DA). Molecular docking and 10ns MD simulations of discriminating lysoPCs with PAFr (homology model from PAF crystal structure). AMBER protocol.

- **Intervention target:** antioxidants to reduce lipid peroxidation → fewer PAF-like lysoPCs → reduced PAFr activation.
- IBS comorbidity (73%) supports shared microbiome-gut-brain pathophysiology between FM and IBS.
