# Key Claims

- **L. plantarum ST-III-fermented milk** was effective in ameliorating impaired social interaction in male VPA-induced ASD mouse models
- **Gender specificity** shown in VPA-induced behavioral abnormalities; fermented milk effective in males but not for anxiety in females
- **Dietary changes** were the main cause of altered gut microbiota in mice
- **Additional intake of L. plantarum ST-III-fermented milk** improved autistic-like behaviors by modulating specific gut microbes
- **Probiotic-fermented foods** represent a potential dietary intervention for ASD

# Methods & Findings

**Animal model study** using VPA-induced ASD mice.

- L. plantarum ST-III-fermented milk used as oral intervention
- Behavioral tests and 16S rRNA gene sequencing performed
- Sex-specific effects observed on behavioral outcomes
- Male mice showed improvement in social interaction
- Female mice did not show improvement in anxiety behavior
- Gut microbiota composition changes correlated with behavioral improvements

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Fermented milk** as practical, food-based probiotic delivery for ASD
- **Sex-specific treatment responses** align with known sex differences in ASD presentation
- **VPA model validation** of probiotic intervention approach
