# Key Claims

- FMT for CD is safe in the short and long term, with only 13.6% of mild adverse events occurring within 1 month post-FMT
- No adverse events occurred beyond 1 month, suggesting a 1-month cutoff for defining short-term vs long-term AE risk
- Improved fecal microbiota preparation (automated purification) reduced AE rates from 21.7% (manual) to 8.7% (automatic)
- 76.7% clinical remission rate at 1 month after FMT, consistent with prior studies
- Preparation method did not correlate with clinical efficacy, only with safety profile

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Single-center cohort study (NCT01793831); 139 CD patients receiving 184 FMTs over 5 years (2012-2016)
- **FMT delivery**: Mid-gut via naso-jejunal tube or gastroscope under anesthesia
- **AE assessment**: CTCAE criteria; longest follow-up 5 years
- **AE types**: Increased defecation frequency, fever, abdominal pain, flatulence, hematochezia, vomiting, bloating, herpes zoster (all mild, Grade 1-2)
- **Purification evolution**: Manual methods (pre-2014) vs GenFMTer automated system (post-2014)

# Connections

- Safety data support FMT as a viable intervention for [[crohns-disease]] [[dysbiosis]] correction
- Preparation quality affects safety, suggesting that donor microbiome composition matters
- Repeated FMTs (33 patients received multiple) support serial treatment for maintaining remission
- FMT may restore [[gut-metal-microbiome]] balance alongside bacterial composition
- Long-term safety is critical for chronic disease management in IBD where repeated treatments are needed
