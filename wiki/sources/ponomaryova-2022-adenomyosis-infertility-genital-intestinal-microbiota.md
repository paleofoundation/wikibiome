# Ponomaryova 2022 — Changes in the microbiota of genital tract and intestines in patients with adenomyosis and infertility

**Citation:** Ponomaryova IG, Lisyana TO, Trokhimovych OV, Matyashova OI. *Changes in the microbiota of the genital tract and intestines in patients with adenomyosis and infertility.* Medical Research Journal. 2022;7(3):197–202. DOI: [10.5603/MRJ.a2022.0034](https://doi.org/10.5603/MRJ.a2022.0034)

## Key Findings

- **Vaginal dysbiosis is the rule in adenomyosis + infertility.** 39% of patients showed bacterial vaginosis (defined by Gram-positive cocci, Gram-negative rods, decreased lactobacilli); 37.5% showed vaginitis (leukocyte 50–100 per field, Gram-positive cocci and Gram-negative rods, Candida). Healthy controls showed no vaginosis or vaginitis.
- **Lactobacillus prevalence preserved, abundance crashed.** 100% of patients and 100% of healthy controls had detectable Lactobacillus, but median abundance fell from 6.2 log CFU/mL (healthy) to 3.4 log CFU/mL (patients) — a ~1000-fold decrease (p<0.05).
- **Enrichment of pathobionts** (all p<0.05 vs. healthy, lg CFU/mL values): *S. epidermidis* 23.4% vs 20%, *S. aureus* 12.5% vs 3.3%, *E. faecalis* 15.6% vs 13.3%, *S. agalactiae* 14.1% vs 6.7%, *Corynebacterium* 18.8% vs 10.0%, **E. coli 25% vs 13.3%**, *E. coli heme+* 14.1% vs 6.7%, *Klebsiella* 15.6% vs 3.3%, *Enterobacter* 12.2% vs 6.7%, **Candida 25% vs 10%**, *Peptostreptococcus* 29.7% vs 20%, **Fusobacterium 26.6% vs 13.3%**, *Prevotella* 18.8% vs 40.0%, *Atopobium vaginae* 28.1% vs 6.7%, *Clostridium* 15.6% vs 13.3%.
- **Bifidobacterium and Bacteroides decline.** *Bifidobacterium* dropped from 13.3% prevalence in healthy to 10.9% in patients; *Bacteroides* from 43.3% to 17.2% — marked reduction in the protective commensal pool.
- **Dual-compartment dysbiosis.** Intestinal microbiota simultaneously perturbed — multicomponent pathogenic associations form in the gut comprising Enterobacteria (E. coli, E. coli heme+, Klebsiella, Enterobacter) + Candida + conditionally pathogenic Gram-positive cocci, mirroring the vaginal pattern.

## Methods

Case-control cultural microbiology study. 64 women with adenomyosis and infertility confirmed by anaerobic/aerobic culture, Bergi's identifier for bacteria, Anaerotest 23 kits for anaerobes, Mikrola-Test 'Candidatest 21' for yeast. 30 age-matched healthy control women. Vaginal and intestinal samples cultured on blood agar, yolk-salt agar, Endo's medium, Sabouraud's agar, Candida selective medium; anaerobic incubation in AnaeroStat-25. MRSA and BD BBL Crystal systems for confirmation. Statistical analysis via Student's t-test (p<0.05 threshold).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Methodology relies on cultural microbiology rather than 16S or shotgun sequencing — limits taxonomic resolution for fastidious anaerobes. Findings for *Atopobium vaginae*, Fusobacterium, and Peptostreptococcus warrant replication with molecular methods.
- Small sample per subgroup; effect sizes may be overestimated due to publication bias for Ukrainian medical journals.
- Prevotella was higher in healthy (40%) than patients (18.8%) — contradicts some reports of Prevotella as a dysbiosis marker; may reflect species-level resolution loss in genus-level culture.

## Open Questions

- Whether adenomyosis dysbiosis drives the lesion or is a consequence.
- Whether treating the vaginal dysbiosis changes fertility outcomes or menstrual symptoms of adenomyosis.
- Whether intestinal Enterobacteriaceae translocation drives the vaginal E. coli enrichment (gut → vagina translocation axis).
