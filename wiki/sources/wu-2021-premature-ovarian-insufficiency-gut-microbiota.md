# Association Between Premature Ovarian Insufficiency and Gut Microbiota

## Key Findings

- **POI women had distinct gut microbial profiles**: reduced **Bulleidia** and **[[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii|Faecalibacterium]]**; enriched **[[butyricimonas]]**, **[[dorea]]**, **Lachnobacterium**, and **[[sutterella]]** ([[wu-2021-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-gut-microbiota]], cross-sectional).
- **Microbial alterations closely correlated with sex hormone levels**: FSH, LH, estradiol (E2), anti-Mullerian hormone (AMH), and FSH/LH ratio all showed significant associations with specific genera.
- **Firmicutes-to-Bacteroidetes ratio was altered** in POI, consistent with a pro-inflammatory gut environment.
- **Reduced SCFA-producing taxa** (Faecalibacterium, Bulleidia) suggest impaired gut barrier function in POI patients.

## Methods

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional case-control study
- **Population**: Women diagnosed with premature ovarian insufficiency compared with age-matched healthy controls
- **Key measurements**: 16S rRNA gene sequencing (V3-V4 region) of fecal samples; serum hormone panels (FSH, LH, E2, AMH); correlation analysis between microbial abundance and hormonal markers

## Detailed Summary

This study characterized the gut microbiota of women with premature ovarian insufficiency using 16S rRNA sequencing and correlated microbial community shifts with circulating sex hormone concentrations. POI patients displayed a signature depletion of butyrate-producing genera (**Faecalibacterium**, **Bulleidia**) and enrichment of genera associated with inflammatory or metabolically active profiles (**[[butyricimonas]]**, **[[dorea]]**, **Lachnobacterium**, **[[sutterella]]**). The depletion of Faecalibacterium is notable given its well-established role in maintaining gut barrier integrity through butyrate production. The enrichment of **[[sutterella]]**, a genus associated with mild inflammatory activity, and **[[dorea]]**, linked to altered bile acid metabolism, suggests multiple microbial pathways may contribute to the hormonal dysregulation observed in POI. Statistical correlation analysis revealed that the abundance of specific genera tracked closely with FSH, LH, E2, AMH, and the FSH/LH ratio, providing evidence for bidirectional gut-ovarian axis communication.
