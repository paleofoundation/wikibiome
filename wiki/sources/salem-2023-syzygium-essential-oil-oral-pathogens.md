## Key Findings

- **Hydrodistillation (HD) vs Supercritical Fluid (SF) vs Headspace (HS) extraction** yields different compositions and activities:
  - *S. malaccense*: HD oil dominated by oxygenated sesquiterpenes (caryophyllene oxide 18.48%, tau-cadinol 11.99%); SF oil dominated by squalene (60.60%); HS aroma dominated by γ-terpinene (33.06%).
  - *S. samarangense*: HD oil dominated by oxygenated monoterpenes; HS by α-pinene (30.18%).
- **Antimicrobial spectrum** (MIC data):
  - HD EOs both species vs *E. coli* ATCC 8739 — 3.75 µg/mL (most potent)
  - Vs *S. aureus*, *E. faecalis*, *C. albicans* — variable 3.75-13.7 mg/mL range
- **Anti-biofilm activity** (crystal violet assay at 630 nm):
  - *C. albicans* biofilm: S. malaccense 83.43%, S. samarangense 87.27% suppression
  - Gram-positive biofilms (S. aureus, E. faecalis): SF-EOs suppressed 76.45-82.95%
- Both species are traditionally used for tooth infections and toothache — study validates phytochemical basis.

## Methods

Leaves harvested from Mazhar Botanical Garden, Cairo. HD (Clevenger), SF (SFE-2/4, 40°C/150 bar CO2), HS (static 70°C 1h + dynamic 70°C 3h). GC-MS (Shimadzu GCMS-QP2010). Agar well diffusion vs MHA medium with amikacin, amoxicillin, ampicillin/sulbactam, norfloxacin, ofloxacin, nystatin controls. Broth microdilution MIC. Antibiofilm: crystal violet quantitative assay on 96-well polystyrene plate.
