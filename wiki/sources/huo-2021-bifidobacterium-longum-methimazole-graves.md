# Probiotic Bifidobacterium longum Supplied with Methimazole Improved the Thyroid Function of Graves' Disease Patients Through the Gut-Thyroid Axis

## Key Findings

- **TRAb recovered to healthy threshold (1.75 IU/mL) only in the probiotic (B. longum) group** after 6 months; methimazole alone and MI+black bean groups showed TRAb reduction but did not reach the healthy threshold ([[huo-2021-bifidobacterium-longum-methimazole-graves]], quasi-experimental, n=25)
- **Methimazole alone caused a dramatic shock to the gut microbiota**: Shannon and Simpson diversity indices sharply declined, and Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, Ligilactobacillus salivarius, Lactococcus lactis, and Prevotella significantly decreased ([[huo-2021-bifidobacterium-longum-methimazole-graves]], quasi-experimental, n=25)
- **Black bean adjuvant maintained intestinal microbiome homeostasis for 3 months**, buffering the drastic shock of methimazole on gut flora, but this effect waned by 6 months ([[huo-2021-bifidobacterium-longum-methimazole-graves]], quasi-experimental, n=25)
- **The B. longum probiotic group showed significant increases in Bifidobacterium adolescentis, B. angulatum, B. breve, B. longum, and Faecalibacterium prausnitzii**, while Blautia hansenii, Clostridium estertheticum, Haemophilus parainfluenzae, and Klebsiella pneumoniae decreased ([[huo-2021-bifidobacterium-longum-methimazole-graves]], quasi-experimental, n=25)
- **FT3, FT4, TSH, dopamine, bile acid, and Fe all significantly changed by 6 months in the probiotic group**, indicating the probiotic modulated not only gut microbiota but also systemic metabolites and trace elements through gut-thyroid and gut-brain axes ([[huo-2021-bifidobacterium-longum-methimazole-graves]], quasi-experimental, n=25)

## Methods

- **Study type**: 6-month longitudinal intervention study with 3 treatment arms and healthy controls
- **Population**: 25 GD patients from Hainan Provincial People's Hospital: Group A (MI alone, n=8), Group B (MI + black bean adjuvant, n=9), Group C (MI + probiotic B. longum, n=9); 9 healthy controls for reference
- **Dosing**: MI: methimazole standard clinical dose. Berberine tablets as in [[han-2022-berberine-methimazole-graves-microbiome]] for context. B. longum: specific strain administered alongside methimazole.
- **Sequencing**: Shotgun metagenome sequencing monthly for 6 months; SCFAs and trace elements measured at baseline, 3 months, and 6 months
- **SNP analysis**: 12 SNPs annotated in 3 genomes responding to probiotic treatment; mutations in Bacteroides xylanisolvens scaffold 19 related to DNA-binding and conjugative transposon proteins
- **Metabolites**: Bile acids, dopamine, SCFAs, trace elements (Fe, Zn) measured longitudinally

## Detailed Summary

### Thyroid Function Recovery

All three treatment groups showed FT3 and FT4 normalization by 6 months. However, TRAb — the key autoantibody driving GD — recovered to the healthy threshold of 1.75 IU/mL only in the probiotic group (Group C). This is clinically significant because TRAb normalization is the strongest predictor of sustained remission in GD. TSH recovery also differed among groups but exact values were tracked monthly.

### Methimazole Gut Microbiota Shock

Methimazole alone caused a sharp decline in both Shannon and Simpson diversity indices, representing a dramatic ecological disruption. Key taxa that significantly decreased with MI alone included F. prausnitzii (a major butyrate producer), Ligilactobacillus salivarius, Lactococcus lactis, and Prevotella species. This suggests methimazole has direct or indirect antimicrobial effects that may counteract its thyroid benefits by destabilizing the gut ecosystem.

### Black Bean Buffering Effect

The black bean adjuvant (Group B) maintained microbiome homeostasis for the first 3 months, preventing the sharp diversity drop seen with MI alone. However, by 6 months this buffering effect waned. This suggests dietary fiber/prebiotic adjuncts can partially protect against methimazole-induced dysbiosis but may not be sufficient long-term.

### Probiotic Group Microbiome Changes

The B. longum probiotic group showed sustained beneficial changes: B. adolescentis, B. angulatum, B. breve, B. longum, and F. prausnitzii all significantly increased. Pathogenic or unfavorable taxa decreased: B. hansenii, C. estertheticum, H. parainfluenzae, and K. pneumoniae. The probiotic reshaped the gut ecosystem toward a more anti-inflammatory composition.

### SNP Evolution and Adaptation

Intriguingly, 12 SNPs were identified in 3 bacterial genomes that appeared to evolve in response to probiotic treatment. Mutations in Bacteroides xylanisolvens scaffold 19 were related to DNA-binding and conjugative transposon proteins, suggesting the probiotic intervention drove adaptive evolution in resident gut bacteria.

### Systemic Effects via Gut-Thyroid and Gut-Brain Axes

The probiotic group showed significant changes in dopamine levels (suggesting gut-brain axis modulation), bile acid profiles, iron (Fe) levels, and zinc (Zn) levels by 6 months. These systemic changes indicate that probiotic-mediated gut microbiome modulation can influence distant organ function through metabolite and trace element pathways.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Very small sample size per group (n=8-9) severely limits statistical power
- Non-randomized, non-blinded design introduces potential bias
- The research group (Huo, Cen, Ou, Pan, Zhang) overlaps with [[han-2022-berberine-methimazole-graves-microbiome]], from the same hospital and period (2018-2019); unclear if patient populations overlap
- Methimazole dosing and B. longum strain/dose details are incompletely reported in the existing page; PDF access for full extraction was limited

## Open Questions

- Would a larger, randomized, double-blind trial confirm B. longum's superiority?
- What specific B. longum strain was used, and does strain identity matter for efficacy?
- How long do the microbiome benefits persist after probiotic discontinuation?
- Can B. longum prevent methimazole-induced dysbiosis if started simultaneously vs after?
- How do the trace element changes (Fe, Zn) mechanistically connect to probiotic supplementation?
