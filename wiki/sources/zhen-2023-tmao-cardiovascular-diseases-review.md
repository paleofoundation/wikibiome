# The Gut Microbial Metabolite Trimethylamine N-Oxide and Cardiovascular Diseases

## Summary

Comprehensive review of the biological and chemical characteristics of [[tmao]], its production by gut microbiota, and its mechanisms of action in promoting [[cardiovascular-disease]] including [[atherosclerosis]], heart failure, hypertension, arrhythmia, and coronary artery disease.

## Key Findings

### TMAO Biosynthesis
- Dietary precursors: choline (eggs, fish, seafood, liver, dairy), phosphatidylcholine (eggs, dairy, meat), betaine (shellfish, beets, spinach), L-carnitine (red meat, fish)
- Gut microbiota metabolize precursors to TMA using TMA lyases (CutC/D and CntA/B systems)
- TMA transported via portal circulation to liver; oxidized to TMAO by hepatic flavin monooxygenases (FMOs), particularly FMO3
- FMO3 has the highest activity among all FMOs for TMA oxidation
- Males have lower FMO3 expression than females; gender specificity in TMAO levels demonstrated in mice
- TMAO has a high turnover rate; >90% excreted in urine

### TMAO and Atherosclerosis
- TMAO inhibits reverse cholesterol transport (RCT) by downregulating ABCG5/G8 heterodimer
- Upregulates CD36 and scavenger receptor A1 (SR-A1) in macrophages, increasing cholesterol uptake and foam cell formation
- Activates NF-kB pathway, increasing pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6) and inhibiting anti-inflammatory IL-10
- Generates ROS via SIRT3-SOD2-mitochondrial ROS signaling pathway
- Activates NLRP3 inflammasome through thioredoxin-interacting protein (TXNIP)

### TMAO and Heart Failure
- Plasma TMAO positively associated with HF risk
- TMAO accelerates myocardial hypertrophy via TGF-beta1/Smad3 signaling
- Exacerbates mitochondrial dysfunction in cardiomyocytes
- Induces transverse-tubule (T-tubule) remodeling

### TMAO and Endothelial Dysfunction/Thrombosis
- TMAO activates HMGB1/TLR4 signaling, destroying tight junction proteins (ZO-2, occludin, VE-cadherin)
- Increases endothelial cell permeability allowing LDL oxidation in intima
- Activates PKC/NF-kB pathway upregulating VCAM-1 and ICAM-1
- Enhances platelet hyperreactivity through Ca2+ release from intracellular stores

### TMAO and Hypertension
- Prolonged elevated TMAO activates pro-inflammatory pathways contributing to vascular remodeling
- Associated with increased Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio
