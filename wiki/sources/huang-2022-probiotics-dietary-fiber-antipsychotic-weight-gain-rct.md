# The Effects of Probiotics Plus Dietary Fiber on Antipsychotic-Induced Weight Gain: A Randomized Clinical Trial

## Key Claims

- Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT comparing 4 groups (probiotics + dietary fiber, probiotics alone, dietary fiber alone, placebo) over 12 weeks in patients with severe mental disorder who gained >10% of predrug weight on atypical antipsychotics
- Probiotics + dietary fiber was significantly superior to probiotics alone and placebo for weight, BMI, and total cholesterol reduction; insulin resistance was worse in placebo group
- Probiotics + dietary fiber significantly reduced weight and prevented further deterioration of metabolic disturbances
- Probiotics or dietary fiber alone could prevent further weight gain but were less effective than combination
- 16S ribosomal RNA sequencing revealed increased abundance of microbiota after probiotics + dietary fiber treatment
- Logistic regression showed higher richness of microbiota was associated with favorable weight loss
- Probiotic formulation: Lactobacillus, [[Bifidobacterium]], and Enterococcus bacteria (1.7x10^10 CFU Lactobacillus, 3.8x10^7 Bifidobacterium, 7.8x10^7 Lactobacillus, 7.8x10^7 Enterococcus)
- Dietary fiber: 10g Hi-FIBER DRINK plus 20g Extra Herb Powder per packet (maltodextrin-based)
- Most frequently reported adverse events: constipation (19.6%), hypokalemia (11.3%), hypocalcemia (5.2%)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled, 2x2 factorial RCT
- **Sample**: 118 patients randomized; 100 completed 12-week treatment
- **Duration**: 12 weeks with assessments at baseline, week 4, and week 12
- **Key finding**: Combined probiotics + fiber superior to either alone for antipsychotic weight gain
