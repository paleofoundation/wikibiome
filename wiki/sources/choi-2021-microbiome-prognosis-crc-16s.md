# Analysis of Changes in Microbiome Compositions Related to the Prognosis of Colorectal Cancer Patients Based on Tissue-Derived 16S rRNA Sequences

## Key Claims

- Alpha diversity was significantly lower in CRC tumor tissues than in matched normal tissues (Shannon index P = 0.00014, observed OTUs P = 0.0011), with distinct beta diversity clustering (Bray-Curtis R-squared = 0.039, P = 0.001)
- *Fusobacterium*, *Treponema*, *Selenomonas*, and *Campylobacter* were enriched in tumor tissues, while *Bacteroidetes*, *Actinobacteria*, and *Firmicutes* (including Clostridia) were more abundant in normal tissues
- *F. nucleatum* and *B. fragilis* were significantly more abundant in tumor tissue (P = 0.0022 and P = 0.011 respectively), and their abundance positively correlated with expression of host genes in cell cycle, P53 signaling, Wnt signaling, and focal adhesion pathways
- Tumor-enriched bacteria including *B. fragilis* and *F. nucleatum* were more abundant in patients with recurrence vs. non-recurrence, suggesting potential as prognostic [[microbial-biomarkers]]
- Bacterial motility proteins (flagella) and lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis pathways were significantly enriched in tumor-associated microbiomes (PICRUSt analysis, P < 0.05)

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Retrospective analysis of 102 microbiome datasets from 51 Korean CRC patients with matched tumor and normal tissue
- **Participants**: 51 CRC patients from Kangwon National University, South Korea; prognosis retrospectively determined
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing on MiSeq; QIIME for OTU analysis; PICRUSt for metabolic pathway prediction; xCell for immune cell deconvolution; ssGSEA for gene set enrichment
- **Host-microbe correlations**: *Fusobacterium* abundance correlated with expression levels of multiple immune cell types (mast cells, plasma cells, B cells, memory B cells, CD4+ naive T cells) and cancer-related pathways
- **Prognosis analysis**: Patients divided into recurrent vs. non-recurrent subgroups for both normal-tissue and tumor-derived microbiomes
