# Immunomodulatory and Anti-Inflammatory Effects of Probiotics in Multiple Sclerosis: A Systematic Review

## Key Findings

- PRISMA-registered systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42018086594) across PubMed, Cochrane, EMBASE, Web of Science, Scopus, Google Scholar. 4,650 initial records → 7 eligible studies (2 RCTs in MS patients, 5 animal EAE studies).
- **Consistent finding**: probiotics downregulate Th1/Th17 effector pathway (IFN-γ, TNF-α, IL-17, iNOS) and upregulate Treg/anti-inflammatory pathway (IL-10, TGF-β) across human and animal studies.
- **Strain heterogeneity**: most common were *Lactobacillus plantarum*, *L. acidophilus*, *L. casei*, *Bifidobacterium lactis* — often as multi-strain cocktails.
- **Mechanism**: SCFA production (acetate, butyrate, propionate) induces FOXP3+ Treg differentiation; reduces intestinal permeability; attenuates CNS microglial activation via gut-brain axis signaling.
- **Quality**: heterogeneity in intervention, dose, duration, and outcome measurement. Authors rate methodological quality as fair-to-good. Meta-analysis not possible due to outcome heterogeneity.

## Methods

PICOTS framework, AHRQ standards for RCTs, SYRCLE for animal studies. Two independent reviewers; discrepancies resolved by third-party consensus. Interventions restricted to defined probiotic species; outcomes included CRP, cytokine panel, disease score (EDSS or EAE score), relapse rate.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Supportive adjunctive intervention**: Multi-strain probiotic (Lactobacilli + Bifidobacteria, ≥10⁹–10¹⁰ CFU/day) as add-on to MS disease-modifying therapy. I→f (Treg shift, cytokine modulation) and f→D (reduced relapse frequency, EDSS stabilization) both supported.
- **Triangle status**: I→D evidence is RCT-graded but only 2 human trials — classification *promising* not fully validated.
- **Cureva-internal cross-reference**: Pair with **Zangeneh 2025** meta-analysis for complete probiotics-MS evidence bundle.

## Open Questions

- Optimal strain, dose, and duration — this review could not recommend specifics.
- Whether effects persist after discontinuation.
- Synergy with specific disease-modifying therapies (interferon-β, glatiramer, ocrelizumab).
