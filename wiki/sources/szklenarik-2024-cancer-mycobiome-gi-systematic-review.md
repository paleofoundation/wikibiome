# Szklenarik 2024 -- Cancer-Related Mycobiome in GI Cancers: Systematic Review

## Key Claims

- From 14 eligible studies (700 initially screened), the Ascomycota/Basidiomycota (A/B) ratio from healthy/control state decreased in cancer in n=2 studies, increased in n=1, and did not change significantly in n=2 studies
- *Malassezia* relative abundance increased in n=4 studies, *Candida* in n=5, *Saccharomyces* in n=2, and *Aspergillus* in n=2 of cancer cases compared to healthy controls
- Intraindividual differences in the A/B ratio were identified in cancer vs. adjacent tissue (n=4) and cancer vs. stool (n=1) studies, suggesting tissue-specific fungal colonization patterns
- Stool (n=8) was the most common specimen type; ITS sequencing used in n=10 studies, metagenomic approaches in n=3; UNITE was the most commonly used taxonomic assignment tool (7/14 studies)
- *Saccharomyces* may be protective against CRC disease progression; *Candida* and *Aspergillus* show increasing abundance

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: PRISMA 2020 systematic review of PubMed database through March 2023
- **Inclusion**: Patients with GI cancers (early or advanced), clinical participation; MG or ITS sequencing on tumor/fecal/blood/plasma
- **Table 1**: Characteristics of 14 included studies across CRC, gastric cancer (GC), pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDA), HCC, and GEP-NEN
- **Key gap**: Unclassified taxa present in 7/14 studies at phylum level; sequencing databases need improvement
