# Gut Microbiota and Vascular Biomarkers in Patients Without Clinical Cardiovascular Diseases

## Summary

Cross-sectional study of 92 participants (aged 25-76) without clinical manifestation of [[cardiovascular-disease]] but with possible CVD risk factors. Assessed associations between gut microbiota composition (16S rRNA V3-V4 sequencing) and vascular biomarkers: intima-media thickness (IMT), carotid-femoral pulse wave velocity (PWV), and CRP levels.

## Key Findings

### Study Population
- 92 subjects from Moscow region (Caucasian)
- Average age 52 +/- 13 years; 29% men, 71% women
- Most common risk factor: dyslipidemia (85%); abdominal obesity (58%); hypertension (37%)
- Exclusions: clinically evident atherosclerosis, coronary artery disease, stroke, any cardiovascular disease

### Microbiome Composition
- Dominant phyla: Bacteroidetes (12.7 +/- 9.86%) and Firmicutes (57.09 +/- 13.6%)
- Approximately 50% of total microbial abundance represented by five genera: *Blautia*, *Bacteroides*, *Prevotella*, *Faecalibacterium*, and *Clostridium*
- *Blautia* was the most dominant genus
- Alpha diversity index higher than average in Western populations (Shannon 3.71 +/- 0.56)

### Gut Microbiota and Low-Grade Inflammation
- Average CRP: 3.50 +/- 4.39 mg/l
- High CRP levels positively associated with *Serratia* abundance (P = 0.0001)
- CRP level >/= 10 pg/ml in 24 participants

### Gut Microbiota and Arterial Wall Properties
- Higher *Blautia* (P = 0.004) and *Serratia* (P = 0.009) representation associated with increased IMT
- Higher *Bacteroides* representation associated with higher PWV in non-diabetic subjects (PWV >/= 10 m/s, P = 0.0001)
- Carotid artery stenosis (20 +/- 20.12%) directly associated with high *Serratia* abundance

## Relevance to the Wiki

Provides evidence that gut microbiome composition is associated with subclinical [[atherosclerosis]] markers even in patients without manifest [[cardiovascular-disease]]. The *Serratia*-CRP-IMT axis suggests a link between specific microbes, low-grade [[inflammation]], and early vascular damage. Complements [[li-2021-network-gut-microbiome-biomarkers-carotid-atherosclerosis]] on trans-omic approaches to subclinical disease.
