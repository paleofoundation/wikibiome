# Gao 2023 — Allopregnanolone-GABA_A Receptor Sensitivity in PMDD Pathogenesis

## Key Findings

- **Allopregnanolone (ALLO) decrease during late luteal phase** is a key trigger for PMDD symptoms — operates through altered GABA_A receptor sensitivity, not absolute hormone levels ([[gao-2023-allopregnanolone-gaba-receptor-pmdd]], review).
- **GABA_A receptor subunits α4, δ, and α6** are the primary targets — these subunits mediate persistent tonic suppression in low extrasynaptic GABA concentrations ([[gao-2023-allopregnanolone-gaba-receptor-pmdd]]).
- ALLO is synthesized from progesterone via **3β-HSD → 5α-reductase → 3α-HSD** pathway in brain, adrenal glands, and gonads ([[gao-2023-allopregnanolone-gaba-receptor-pmdd]]).
- **Rapid withdrawal of ALLO** (not low absolute levels) produces PMDD-like behavior in rodent models — anxiety, depression, social withdrawal ([[gao-2023-allopregnanolone-gaba-receptor-pmdd]]).
- **Sepranolone** (isoallopregnanolone) — a GABA_A modulating steroid antagonist — is the most viable targeted drug for PMDD, currently in Phase II clinical trials ([[gao-2023-allopregnanolone-gaba-receptor-pmdd]]).
- Low-dose fluoxetine can prevent ALLO oxidation to 5α-DHP, increasing ALLO concentrations in brain and potentially preventing PMDD onset ([[gao-2023-allopregnanolone-gaba-receptor-pmdd]]).

## Methods

Narrative review of ALLO-GABA_A receptor interactions in PMDD pathogenesis, covering rodent models, human clinical studies, neurosteroid pharmacology, and drug development.

## Detailed Summary

The review synthesizes the "ALLO sensitivity" model of PMDD: PMDD is not caused by abnormal hormone levels (ALLO and progesterone are normal in PMDD patients) but by **abnormal receptor sensitivity** to normal hormonal fluctuations. During the luteal phase, progesterone is metabolized to ALLO, which normally enhances GABA_A receptor function. In PMDD, the rapid ALLO fluctuation triggers paradoxical responses in GABA_A receptors — particularly those containing α4 and δ subunits, which are upregulated during periods of sustained ALLO exposure and then dysregulated during withdrawal.

The saccadic eye velocity (SEV) test provides neurophysiological evidence: ALLO dose-dependently decreases SEV via GABA_A receptors, confirming the receptor sensitivity hypothesis in humans.
