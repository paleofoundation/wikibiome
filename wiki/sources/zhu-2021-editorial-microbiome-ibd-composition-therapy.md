# Key Claims

- The gut microbiome composition in IBD is significantly altered and contributes to disease pathogenesis
- Metformin, commonly used for type 2 diabetes, may have a beneficial effect on IBD through increased Akkermansia muciniphila abundance
- Adherent-invasive [[escherichia-coli]] can be studied using human colonoid-derived epithelial monolayers, advancing mechanistic understanding
- Nano-targeted drug delivery of berberine effectively regulated [[inflammation]] and reconstructed intestinal microbial community
- FMT provides an overview of therapeutic potential but requires further investigation for optimal IBD application

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Editorial overview of research topic collection on microbiome-IBD therapy
- **Key articles highlighted**: Metformin-microbiome interactions (drug repurposing); AIEC colonoid studies; berberine nano-delivery; mesalamine microbiome effects; natural polysaccharide therapies; FMT review
- **Metformin finding**: Significantly increased anti-inflammatory A. muciniphila in IBD treatment group
- **Drug-microbiome focus**: Multiple drugs affect gut microbiota composition, creating opportunities for drug repurposing

# Connections

- Drug repurposing through microbiome modulation is an emerging strategy for [[crohns-disease]]
