# Identification of Host Gene-Microbiome Associations in Colorectal Cancer Patients Using Mendelian Randomization

## Key Claims

- Six bacterial taxa showed causal relationships with CRC at locus-wide significance: family *Porphyromonadaceae* (OR 1.26), genera *Anaerotruncs* (OR 1.17), *Intestinibacter* (OR 1.31), *Slackia* (OR 1.24), *Ruminococcaceae UCG004* (OR 1.27), and species *Eubacterium coprostanoligenes group* (OR 1.25) were all positively associated
- Meta-analysis combining AGWAS and FinnGen datasets replicated the causal associations for *Porphyromonadaceae* and genera *Slackia*, *Anaerotruncs*, and *Intestinibacter*
- SNP annotation at locus-wide significance identified 24 host genes that may be related to pathogenic intestinal microflora in CRC patients, providing the first gene-microbiome association map for CRC
- *Porphyromonadaceae* contains genera such as *Parabacteroides*, *Odoribacter*, and *Porphyromonas* that are rarely seen in healthy populations but associated with CRC progression from chronic inflammation to adenocarcinoma
- *Intestinibacter* had the strongest individual effect (OR 1.31, P = 0.0038) and was replicated in the FinnGen validation cohort

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Two-sample MR with AGWAS discovery and FinnGen replication, plus SNP annotation for host gene identification
- **Data sources**: MiBioGen consortium (18,340 subjects, 25 cohorts) for microbiome; AGWAS (East Asian, 6,692 CRC cases, 27,278 controls); FinnGen (7,427 CRC cases, 25,600 controls) for validation
- **Methods**: IVW, weighted median, MR-Egger, simple median, MR-PRESSO; PhenoScanner for confounding; gSNPense for SNP annotation
- **166 bacterial features** analyzed at four taxonomic levels (order, family, genus, species)
- **Key genes identified**: Including COL13A1, DGE, P1P900, PRX3, CEP112, RGS7, SERINC5, CD200 among 24 host genes linked to CRC-associated microbiota
