# Causal Relationship Between the Gut Microbiota, Immune Cells, and Coronary Heart Disease: A Mediated Mendelian Randomization Analysis

## Summary

First mediated MR study investigating the causal pathway from gut microbiota through immune cells to coronary heart disease (CHD). Identified *Desulfovibrio piger* as a protective factor against CHD, operating through modulation of effector memory double-negative (EM DN) T cells.

## Key Findings

### Disease-Associated Gut Microbiota
- Six GM taxa causally associated with CHD: families Clostridiaceae and Sutterellaceae, genus *Clostridium*, *Escherichia* unclassified species, and others
- 25 immune cells causally associated with CHD

### Key Mediation Finding
- *g__Desulfovibrio.s__Desulfovibrio_piger* increases EM DN (CD4-CD8-) %T cells (OR=1.771, P=0.0922 by IVW, P=0.0476 by weighted median)
- EM DN (CD4-CD8-) %T cells are protective against CHD (OR=0.871, P=0.0177 by IVW)
- The mediated effect: increased *Desulfovibrio piger* -> increased EM DN T cells -> reduced CHD risk
- Proportion mediated was -0.0176 (range -0.0452 to 0.0099)

### Mechanistic Discussion
- *Desulfovibrio piger* is a sulfate-reducing bacterium that produces H2S
- Helps digest indigestible substances like cellulose, produces beneficial metabolites
- Can enter plasma and produce alkylglycerol phosphate (A-GPC), inhibiting CXCR3+ T-cell activity
- DN T cells (CD4-CD8-) constitute 1-3% of T cells; present in heart, liver, GI tract
- DN T cells can endure chronic stimuli, positioning them as key players in chronic inflammation immunity

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Immune mediation pathway** - Demonstrates that gut microbiome effects on CHD are partially mediated through immune cell modulation, adding a layer to the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] model
2. **Sulfate-reducing bacteria** - *Desulfovibrio* species are sensitive to heavy metals; [[cadmium]] and [[lead]] exposure could deplete this protective taxon, removing CHD protection
3. **H2S signaling** - Metal-induced depletion of H2S-producing bacteria may reduce cardioprotective H2S signaling
4. **DN T cell immunology** - Provides a specific immune mechanism by which metal-induced [[dysbiosis]] could increase CHD risk through altered T cell populations
