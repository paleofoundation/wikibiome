# Lin 2023 — Gut Microbiota in Perimenopausal Panic Disorder

## Key Findings

- **Reduced alpha diversity** in perimenopausal panic disorder (PPD): OTU index (p=0.003), Shannon (p=0.012), Simpson (p=0.008) all lower in PPD vs. controls ([[lin-2023-gut-microbiota-perimenopausal-panic-disorder]], case-control, n=80).
- **Phylum level:** Bacteroidetes and Verrucomicrobia more abundant in PPD; Firmicutes and Actinobacteria scarcer ([[lin-2023-gut-microbiota-perimenopausal-panic-disorder]]).
- **Enriched in PPD:** Bacteroidetes, Phascolarctobacterium, Alistipes, Paraprevotella, Akkermansia, Megasphaera, Veillonella, Oscillibacter, Odoribacter, Butyrivibrio, Desulfovibrionaceae ([[lin-2023-gut-microbiota-perimenopausal-panic-disorder]]).
- **Depleted in PPD:** Fecalibacterium, Blautia, Pseudobutyrivibrio, Subdoligranulum, Roseburia, Coprococcus, Bifidobacterium, Clostridium, Streptococcus, Dorea, Anaerospipes, Anaerotruncus, Collinsella, Turicibacter ([[lin-2023-gut-microbiota-perimenopausal-panic-disorder]]).
- **Symptom correlations:** PASS positively correlated with Phascolarctobacterium (r=0.358), Alistipes (r=0.366), Parabacteroides (r=0.394), Akkermansia (r=0.313). HAMA positively correlated with Alistipes (r=0.466), negatively with Fecalibacterium (r=−0.322, p=0.043) ([[lin-2023-gut-microbiota-perimenopausal-panic-disorder]]).
- **Bacteroidetes and Alistipes dysbiosis** may be a potential pathogenesis and physio-pathological feature of PPD ([[lin-2023-gut-microbiota-perimenopausal-panic-disorder]]).

## Methods

Case-control study at Menopausal Syndrome Specialist Clinic in Shenzhen, China. 40 PPD patients (age 41–60, DSM-5 criteria, HAMA ≥14) and 40 age-matched controls. 16S rRNA sequencing (V4 region), RDP Classifier, OTU-based analysis (97% similarity). Alpha diversity (OTU, ACE, Chao, Shannon, Simpson), beta diversity (PCA, OPLS-DA), LEfSe analysis (LDA >2). HAMA, PASS (Panic Associated Symptoms Scale), PDSS (Panic Disorder Severity Score).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Akkermansia enrichment in PPD contradicts its typically protective role in metabolic health — context-dependent pathogenicity vs. commensalism.
