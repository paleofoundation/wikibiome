# Overview

Preclinical study demonstrating that **Faecalibacterium hominis 4P-15**, a novel species isolated from typically developing children's feces, ameliorates ASD-like behaviors in **BTBR T+ Itpr3tf/J mice** by reducing intestinal [[indoles|indole]] and its derivatives and correcting downstream **AhR signaling dysregulation**. 4P-15 administration restored **glutamate transporter and GABA receptor expression**, correcting excitatory/inhibitory (E/I) imbalance — a hallmark ASD pathophysiology.

# Key Findings

## Faecalibacterium Abundance in ASD

- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] significantly reduced in ASD vs TD individuals (p = 0.0061, FDR-adjusted)
- 183 bacterial strains from 65 species isolated from fecal samples
- 4 *F. hominis* strains obtained — all from TD samples (none from ASD)
- *F. hominis* identified as new species (96.56% similarity to *F. prausnitzii* A2-165)

## Behavioral Restoration in BTBR Mice

- **Self-grooming**: reduced from ~30s to ~10s (p = 0.003)
- **Social interaction** (three-chamber): social preference duration increased (p = 0.003)
- **No effect** on anxiety (open field) or depression (sucrose preference)
- Negative correlation between *F. hominis* fecal abundance and stereotypic behavior

## Indole and Derivative Reduction

In BTBR mouse feces (comparing C57BL/6J control, BTBR+PBS, BTBR+4P-15):
- **Indole**: BTBR elevated → 4P-15 reduced (p = 1e-04)
- **Indole-3-acetic acid (IAA)**: BTBR elevated → 4P-15 reduced (p = 7e-07)
- **Indole-3-propionic acid (IPA)**: BTBR elevated → 4P-15 reduced (p = 0.0039)
- **Indole-3-aldehyde (IAld)**: BTBR elevated → 4P-15 reduced (p = 9e-06)
- **Indole-3-ethanol (IEt)**: BTBR elevated → not significantly reduced
- **Tryptamine**: no significant difference

## Mechanism: Reduced Indole-Producing Bacteria

- 4P-15 did not directly reduce indole/IPA in vitro (YCFA medium)
- 16S rRNA sequencing: 4P-15 reduced abundance of **[[dysbiosis|Peptococcus]]** and **Tuzzerella** (p = 0.036 and 0.034)
- [[dysbiosis|Peptococcus]] is identified as a potential indole-producing bacterium
- 4P-15 reshapes microbiota composition to reduce indole production

## AhR/Glutamate/GABA Pathway Correction

- 4P-15 significantly decreased brain **IPA levels** and modestly reduced **indole**
- Reduced IPA/indole → attenuated AhR signaling
- AhR hyperactivation suppresses glutamate transporters (Slc1a1, Slc1a2, Slc1a3) and GABA receptors (Gabra1, Gabrb2, Gabrg2, Gabbr1)
- 4P-15 upregulated glutamate transporters and GABA receptors
- IPA concentrations negatively correlated with *Slc1a2/Slc1a3* expression
- Corrects **E/I imbalance** — the fundamental neurophysiological deficit in ASD

## No Effect on Inflammatory Cytokines or SCFAs

- IL-1beta, IL-17A, TNF-alpha, IFN-gamma, IL-6, IL-10: no significant changes with 4P-15
- SCFAs: no significant changes in feces or brain

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**High relevance** — indole/AhR pathway intersects metal metabolism:

- **AhR**: a ligand-activated transcription factor; its DNA-binding domain is a [[zinc]] finger motif
- **Indole overproduction** in ASD: bacterial tryptophanase is often an [[iron]]-dependent enzyme
- **Glutamate transporters** (SLC1A family): their expression regulated by [[zinc]] signaling
- **GABA receptors**: allosterically modulated by [[zinc]]
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] as next-generation probiotic may affect metal bioavailability through microbiome remodeling
- E/I imbalance correction may depend on adequate [[zinc]] and [[iron]] cofactor availability

# Methodological Notes

- BTBR mouse model — well-validated idiopathic ASD model
- *F. hominis* 4P-15 is a novel species (new to science)
- Oral gavage administration (daily for ~2 weeks)
- Chinese Academy of Sciences and Peking Union Medical College
- Limitation: mouse model — translation to humans requires clinical trials
- Does not address whether *F. hominis* supplementation affects metal status
- Published August 2025 — very recent
