## Key Findings

- **Neuroinflammation is immune-mediated, not primarily neuroinvasive** — Although SARS-CoV-2 has some neurovirulence potential, most neurological complications in COVID-19 arise from dysregulated immune responses and vascular dysfunction rather than direct viral invasion of the CNS.
- **Multiple neurological syndromes share immune-vascular pathophysiology** — COVID-19 causes encephalopathy, encephalitis, acute demyelinating encephalomyelitis (ADEM), stroke, and Guillain-Barré syndrome through convergent immune and vascular mechanisms.
- **Long COVID includes significant neuropsychiatric burden** — A substantial proportion of COVID-19 patients develop long COVID with neuropsychiatric manifestations (cognitive dysfunction, mood disturbance, fatigue), though mechanisms remain poorly understood.
- **Immune dysregulation and vascular injury are central mechanisms** — Excessive pro-inflammatory cytokine production, endothelial dysfunction, and thrombosis drive neurological complications in both acute and long COVID.

## Detailed Summary

This comprehensive review examines the pathophysiological mechanisms underlying COVID-19 neurological complications and their relationship to long COVID neuropsychiatric symptoms.

**Key mechanistic insights:**

1. **Limited neurotropism but significant immune dysregulation** — While SARS-CoV-2 can infect neural tissues, direct viral invasion of the CNS is not the primary driver of neurological complications. Instead, systemic immune dysregulation and vascular dysfunction create a permissive environment for CNS damage.

2. **Immune-mediated encephalopathy** — COVID-19 encephalopathy arises from:
   - Excessive pro-inflammatory cytokine production (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, IFN-γ)
   - Microglia activation and neuroinflammation
   - Endothelial disruption enabling BBB breakdown
   - Microthrombi and vascular occlusion

3. **Vascular complications as primary mechanism** — Thrombosis and vascular dysfunction are central to COVID-19 neurological injury:
   - Systemic hypercoagulability and vascular thrombosis
   - Endothelial activation by pro-inflammatory cytokines
   - Platelet hyperactivity and microthrombi formation
   - Stroke (both arterial and venous) as major complication

4. **Autoimmune and molecular mimicry mechanisms** — Some neurological syndromes may involve:
   - Cross-reactive antibodies (molecular mimicry between viral and neuronal epitopes)
   - Autoimmune reactions (anti-neuronal antibodies in some patients)
   - Antibody and complement-mediated demyelination

5. **Long COVID neurological mechanisms** — Persistent neuropsychiatric symptoms in long COVID may result from:
   - Prolonged microglial activation and chronic neuroinflammation
   - Persistent vascular endothelial dysfunction
   - Microthrombi in cerebral vasculature
   - Autonomic nervous system dysregulation
   - Mitochondrial dysfunction in neurons
   - Persistent viral or viral-derived antigen reservoirs (unclear)
