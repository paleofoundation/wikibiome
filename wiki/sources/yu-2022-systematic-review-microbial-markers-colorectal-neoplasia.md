# A Systematic Review of Microbial Markers for Risk Prediction of Colorectal Neoplasia

## Key Claims

- Nine fecal microbiota (*Fusobacterium*, *Enterococcus*, *Porphyromonas*, *Salmonella*, *Pseudomonas*, *Peptostreptococcus*, *Actinomyces*, *Bifidobacterium*, and *Roseburia*) were found to be consistently associated with colorectal neoplasia across 45 observational studies
- Two oral pathogens, *Treponema denticola* and *Prevotella intermedia*, and serum antibody levels to *Streptococcus gallolyticus* were also consistently associated with CRC risk
- Of 30 prediction studies using microbial markers, 83.3% achieved acceptable-to-good discrimination (AUROC > 0.75), but most were limited to small sample sizes (range 9-485 cases) and lacked independent validation (76.7%)
- Combining microbial markers with qFIT/gFOBT improved screening accuracy over either alone; the best-performing combined model achieved AUROC of 0.99 (qFIT + microbiota + age + race + BMI)
- *Fusobacterium nucleatum* was the most commonly used single predictor, with AUROC ranging from 0.67-0.94 depending on study design and population

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: PRISMA-compliant systematic review of MEDLINE and EMBASE databases
- **Scope**: 45 observational studies and 30 prediction studies identified from 5,741 screened articles
- **Table 2**: Comprehensive taxonomy of bacteria differentially abundant in CRC, adenomas, and controls across phyla (Actinobacteria, Bacteroidetes, Firmicutes, Fusobacteria, Tenericutes)
- **Table 3**: Details of 30+ multi-bacteria prediction models with AUROC, sample sizes, and validation status
- **Key limitation**: Heterogeneity in study protocols, databases, and geographic populations limits cross-study comparability
