# Key Claims

- **Fungal dysbiosis (high abundance of Saccharomyces and Candida)** found in children who progressed to clinical T1D
- **Both fungal and bacterial dysbiosis** co-occur in children with beta-cell autoimmunity who progress to T1D
- **Increased Bacteroidales and Clostridiales ratio** is a common denominator in progressors vs non-progressors
- **Markers of intestinal inflammation** (fecal HBD2, ASCA IgG to fungal antigens) increased in progressors
- **Anti-Saccharomyces cerevisiae antibodies (ASCA)** elevated, indicating immune response to fungal antigens

# Methods & Findings

Case-control study of 26 autoantibody-positive children with HLA-conferred T1D susceptibility:

- Bacterial 16S and fungal ITS2 sequencing performed
- Fecal human beta-defensin-2 (HBD2), calprotectin, secretory total IgA measured
- ASCA and circulating cytokines (IFNG, IL-17, IL-22) analyzed
- Children followed for median 8 years; 9 autoantibody-positive developed T1D

- **[[iron]]**: Candida and Saccharomyces are iron-requiring fungi -- iron availability in the gut promotes their expansion

- **[[immune-balance]]**: ASCA antibodies represent immune response to fungal antigens -- metal-dependent immune tolerance breakdown
