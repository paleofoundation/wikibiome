# Exposure to Cadmium and Its Impacts on Human Health: A Short Review

## Key Claims

- [[cadmium]] has a half-life of 25-30 years in plants and mammals; accumulates primarily in kidneys and liver
- **Renal tubular dysfunction**: Prolonged Cd exposure decreases serum glucose, calcium, phosphorus, amino acids, and low-molecular-weight proteins; urinary Cd levels can reach 200 mg/kg in exposed individuals
- **Lung dysfunction**: 40-60% of inhaled Cd is absorbed; cigarette smokers show 4-5x higher blood Cd levels; linked to emphysema, COPD, and lung cancer
- **Neurotoxicity**: Cd enters neurons via voltage-gated calcium channels; diminishes glutathione peroxidase, catalase, and SOD activity; connected to Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, and multiple sclerosis
- **Cardiovascular effects**: Cd damages endothelial cells, raises LDL profiles, increases atherosclerosis risk via lipid aggregation in blood vessel walls; depletes IL-1, IL-6, and TNF-alpha
- **Carcinogenesis**: Cd does not form stable DNA adducts; carcinogenicity likely through epigenetic pathways (abnormal gene expression, suppressed DNA repair, disrupted apoptosis); Cd-resistant cells show 2.5x higher MT content
- **Blood-brain barrier**: Cd disrupts BBB in early life more severely; properties of BBB in adults are more protective
- **Pregnancy effects**: Even minimal Cd exposure disrupts gene expression in placenta and embryo via methyl transferase activity disruption

## Methods/Findings

- **Document type**: Comprehensive narrative review (2025, published in Journal of Hazardous Materials Advances)
- **Scope**: Reviews 2019-2024 published literature on Cd health impacts across organ systems
- **Kidney pathology detail**: Cd-metallothionein (Cd-MT) complex filtered in glomeruli, reabsorbed in proximal tubules; Cd released from MT in lysosomes at pH 4.5-5.5; biologically permissible Cd in urine is 0.0445 mol/L; elevated urine-cadmium prevalence is 2.3% in US population
- **Cadmium excretion**: Three stages - (1) renal cortex accumulation proportional to exposure, (2) large exposure with MT depletion, (3) renal tubular damage with significant urinary excretion
- **Cancer mechanism**: Cd may hinder DNA repair, upregulate mitogenic signaling, and suppress apoptosis; approximately 65% of Cd-exposed cells died via apoptosis while 35% survived with resistance
- **Safety protocols**: "TOP model" - Technical, Organizational, and Personal measures for handling Cd-containing materials
