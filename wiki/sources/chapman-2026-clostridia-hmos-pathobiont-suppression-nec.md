# Chapman 2026 — Non-toxigenic Clostridia Metabolize HMOs and Suppress Pathobionts

## Key Findings

- **Non-toxigenic *Clostridium perfringens* (pfoA-negative) and *Clostridium tertium*** metabolize human milk oligosaccharides (HMOs) — overturning the assumption that only Bifidobacteria benefit from HMOs.
- **Cell-free supernatants** from these strains inhibit NEC-associated Enterobacteriaceae (*E. coli*, *Enterobacter*, *Klebsiella*) by 40–90% in vitro.
- Genomic and proteomic analyses identify distinct HMO catabolic pathways in non-toxigenic strains, absent in toxigenic *C. perfringens*.
- **Clinical translation:** proposes non-toxigenic Clostridia as a novel probiotic class for preterm infants.

## Methods

Comparative genomics + proteomics of toxigenic vs. non-toxigenic Clostridium isolates. HMO metabolism assays. Cell-free supernatant inhibition assays against NEC pathobionts. Organoid models of preterm intestinal epithelium.
