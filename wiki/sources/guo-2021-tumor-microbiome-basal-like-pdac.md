# Key Claims

- The tumor [[microbiome]] differs significantly across PDAC molecular subtypes, with basal-like tumors harboring distinctive microbial communities enriched in *Acinetobacter*, *Pseudomonas*, and *Sphingopyxis*
- Higher abundance of these three bacterial genera predicted significantly worse outcomes for PDAC patients
- Functional characterization of microbial genes suggested potential to induce pathogen-related [[inflammation]]
- Host genetics influence the tumor microbiome composition, demonstrating [[microbiome-host interactions]] in shaping tumor biology

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Metagenomic sequencing of resectable PDAC tumors classified into basal-like (n=17), hybrid (n=23), and classical (n=22) subtypes
- **Diversity measures**: Richness significantly higher in basal-like tumors (p=0.0017); Shannon index significantly decreased (p=0.017); Bray-Curtis dissimilarity much higher within basal-like
- **Enriched genera**: 83 microbial genera significantly differed across subtypes; *Acinetobacter*, *Pseudomonas*, and *Sphingopyxis* specifically enriched in basal-like tumors
- **Survival analysis**: PDAC patients with higher abundance of *Acinetobacter* (p=0.037) and *Pseudomonas* (p=0.021) had significantly worse overall survival
- **FISH validation**: 16S rRNA FISH confirmed presence of bacterial DNA within PDAC tumors; LPS immunohistochemistry detected bacterial presence
- **Functional analysis**: Basal-like enriched genes involved in transport of amino acids, nucleotides, carbohydrates, lipids; defense mechanisms and cell motility/envelope biogenesis
- **Host-microbiome correlation**: KRAS pathway genes, EMT and inflammatory response gene sets correlated with microbial abundance; correlation network between functional variation and infection-related pathways
