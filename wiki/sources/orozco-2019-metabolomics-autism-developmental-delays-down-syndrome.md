# Key Claims

- **Significant associations between 11 plasma metabolites** and neurodevelopmental outcome found across ASD, idiopathic-developmental delay (i-DD), and Down syndrome (DS)
- **Similar perturbation in one-carbon metabolism pathways** observed across DS and ASD cases
- **Energy-related tricarboxylic acid cycle** perturbations seen in DS and i-DD cases
- **Some metabolites and pathways uniquely associated with ASD or DS**, allowing differentiation between conditions
- Comparing metabolic signatures across developmental disabilities reveals both **overlapping and specific biological perturbations**

# Methods & Findings

**Untargeted NMR metabolomics study** from the CHARGE (Childhood Autism Risk from Genetics and the Environment) study.

- ASD (n=167), i-DD (n=51), DS (n=31), and typically developing (TD, n=193) children compared
- 49 metabolites identified and quantified including amino acids, organic acids, sugars
- Multiple linear regression used to identify metabolite-outcome associations
- One-carbon metabolism perturbation shared across ASD and DS
- Condition-specific metabolic signatures also identified

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **One-carbon metabolism** disruption in ASD links to folate, methionine, and methylation pathways
- **Cross-condition comparison** identifies ASD-specific vs shared developmental disability metabolites
- **CHARGE cohort** provides well-characterized environmental exposure data alongside metabolomics
