# Key Claims

- *Candida*, *Shigella*, and *Campylobacter* were not directly associated with heart failure incidence, but *Shigella* increased risk of myocarditis by 38.1% and hypertrophic cardiomyopathy by 13.3% per unit increase
- *Candida* concentration increase raised chronic kidney disease risk by 7.1% -- a key HF risk factor
- Among gut metabolites, **betaine** increased risk of HF (OR 1.014, p=0.030) and MI (OR 1.029, p=0.008), representing a suggestive causal association; the PREDIMED study confirmed betaine-HF association (OR 1.65)
- **Tryptophan** was causally associated with elevated risk of systolic blood pressure (OR 1.148, p=0.009), hypertensive cardiomyopathy, and dilated cardiomyopathy (OR 0.798, p=0.010)
- **Phenylalanine** elevated risk of heart failure (OR 1.017, p=0.037) and hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (OR 1.080, p=0.046); it was an independent predictor of HF death in PROSPER/FINRISK cohorts
- **TMAO** was associated with increased systolic blood pressure (OR 1.071, p=0.029) and myocarditis risk (OR 1.225, p=0.009), with beta-hydroxybutyric acid also linked to myocarditis
- After Bonferroni correction, no causal relationship remained significant between gut flora/metabolites and HF risk factors, suggesting modest effect sizes

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample MR examining both gut microbiota and metabolites on HF and 15 risk factors
- **Outcomes**: HF, T2DM, AF, CAD, MI, hypertension, CKD, myocarditis, valvular heart disease, hypertrophic/dilated cardiomyopathy
- **Metabolites tested**: TMAO, betaine, carnitine, choline, glutamate, kynurenine, serotonin, beta-hydroxybutyric acid, propionic acid, tryptophan, phenylalanine, tyrosine
- **Methods**: IVW (primary), MR-Egger, MR-PRESSO, maximum likelihood, weighted median
- **Data sources**: Gut microbiota GWAS (2,076 samples), gut metabolites GWAS (18,340 samples), multiple outcome GWAS consortia
