# Key Claims

- **Microbiota-gut-brain axis (MGBA)** involves autonomic and enteric nervous systems, hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis, immune system, enteroendocrine cells, and microbial metabolites
- **Vagus nerve plays essential role** in modulating appetite and nutritional preferences via enteroendocrine cell interaction
- **"Diabesity"** defines the pathophysiological connection between overweight and T2DM
- **Gut microbiota affects CNS functions** including mood, stress, memory, and appetite regulation

# Methods & Findings

Review exploring interconnected pathways of MGBA:

- Vagus nerve mediates bidirectional communication between gut microbiota and brain
- GLP-1 is a key mediator of gut-brain metabolic signaling
- Gut microbiota shift during diabetes participates in autonomic nervous system and gut-brain axis alterations

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc modulates vagal nerve function and GLP-1 secretion from enteroendocrine cells
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent enzymes protect vagal neurons from oxidative damage in diabetic neuropathy
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Metal availability in the gut influences microbial metabolite production that signals through the vagus nerve
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Metal-driven [[dysbiosis]] may disrupt gut-brain axis signaling, contributing to metabolic dysregulation
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Neuroinflammation mediated through MGBA is modulated by metal-dependent immune pathways
