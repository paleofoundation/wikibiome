# Probiotics Ingestion Does Not Directly Affect Thyroid Hormonal Parameters in Hypothyroid Patients on Levothyroxine Treatment

## Key Findings

- Prospective single-blind RCT, 80 primary hypothyroid patients on stable levothyroxine, randomized to **VSL#3** (B. breve, B. longum, B. infantis, L. acidophilus, L. plantarum, L. paracasei, L. bulgaricus, S. thermophilus; 450×10⁹ CFU/sachet) + LT4 vs LT4 alone. 2-month intervention + 2-month follow-up.
- **No direct effect** on TSH, fT3, fT4, peripheral tissue markers (creatine phosphokinase, myoglobin, SHBG, ferritin) between groups.
- **Stability effect**: LT4 dose adjustments occurred 6 times in control vs 4 decreases in probiotic group (p=0.007) — interpreted as probiotics **preventing fluctuation** rather than inducing a mean shift.
- fT3/fT4 ratio directly correlated with TSH at each visit in both groups.
- Plasma TSH control distributions broader in placebo group; probiotic group more hormonally stable.

## Methods

Investigator-started, single-blind RCT (NCT03095963). Enrolled from Endocrinology Unit, Modena, Italy. Inclusion: primary hypothyroidism on stable LT4, euthyroid at baseline. 2-month probiotic phase, monthly TSH/fT3/fT4. Sample size calculated a priori for 70% TSH change detection, 90% power, α=0.05.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Tempers optimism about probiotic monotherapy for **[[hashimotos-thyroiditis]]** hypothyroidism — does NOT correct thyroid function on its own.
- Informs **[[gut-thyroid-axis]]** concept: probiotic effect is homeostatic (buffering LT4 absorption) rather than gland-targeted.
- Contrasts with **[[lin-2022-probiotics-thyroidectomy-oral-gut-dysbiosis-rct]]**: acute perturbation (THW) is more probiotic-responsive than chronic stable hypothyroidism.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Conditional intervention**: VSL#3 or equivalent multi-strain probiotic in LT4-treated patients with **unstable TSH** or frequent dose adjustments — I→D preliminary, mechanism likely gut absorption stabilization.
- **Not validated** as thyroid-hormone-lowering or antibody-reducing intervention on its own.
- **Triangle weakness**: I→f (probiotics alter microbiome) is plausible but not characterized in this study (no sequencing performed); I→D (dose stability) is RCT-graded at n=80.

## Open Questions

- Whether probiotics affect LT4 absorption kinetics directly (deconjugation, intestinal transit, mucin).
- Whether antibody-positive HT patients respond differently than non-autoimmune hypothyroid patients.
- Whether specific strains drive the stability effect (VSL#3 is an 8-strain cocktail).
