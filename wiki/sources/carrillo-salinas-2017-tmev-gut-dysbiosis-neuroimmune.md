# Gut Dysbiosis and Neuroimmune Responses to Brain Infection with TMEV

## Key Claims

- Intracerebral TMEV (Theiler's murine encephalomyelitis virus) infection causes moderate gut dysbiosis in SJL/J mice, with temporal changes in bacterial composition across acute (14 dpi), subacute (28 dpi), and chronic (85 dpi) phases
- Antibiotic (ABX) treatment during the acute TMEV phase dampens brain CD4+ and CD8+ T cell infiltration but paradoxically enhances microglial activation with clusters of activated microglia disseminated throughout the brain
- ABX treatment significantly increases mortality at 21-22 dpi in TMEV-infected mice (p = 0.002), demonstrating that commensal microbiota is protective during the acute viral phase
- TMEV infection increases intestinal permeability (elevated Na-F plasma concentration, p < 0.05) and increases CD4+ T cells in the lamina propria of the ileum, with retraction of ZO-1 tight junction expression
- Key dysbiosis changes: *Akkermansia* decreased at 14 dpi then recovered by 85 dpi; *Clostridium XIVa* decreased; *Eubacterium* increased at 85 dpi; *Streptococcus* reduced chronically; *Alloprevotella* reduced acutely
- ABX-induced microbiota depletion recovers incompletely at 28 dpi, with more Verrucomicrobia and fewer Bacteroidetes; by 85 dpi diversity is recovered but composition differs from TMEV-only mice
- VP2 capsid protein expression not affected by ABX, suggesting microbiota does not influence viral replication

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Experimental; SJL/J mice intracerebrally inoculated with TMEV (2x10^6 pfu); oral ABX cocktail (ampicillin, metronidazole, neomycin sulfate, vancomycin) for 4 weeks pre-infection plus 2 weeks post-infection
- **Timepoints**: 14, 28, and 85 dpi (acute, subacute, and chronic autoimmune phases)
- **Microbiota analysis**: 16S rRNA sequencing; alpha and beta diversity; temporal abundance changes
- **Key acute (14 dpi) changes**: *Alloprevotella* decreased; *Clostridium XIVa* decreased in sham but further in TMEV
- **Key chronic (85 dpi) changes**: *Streptococcus* reduced; *Eubacterium* and *Clostridium XIVa* increased in TMEV
- **ABX effects on brain**: Reduced CD4+ and CD8+ T cell proportion in brain; enhanced microglial Iba1 staining with clusters; increased MHC-II expression; ameboid morphology suggesting activation
- **ABX effects on gut**: Depleted intestinal microbiota; incomplete recolonization; more Verrucomicrobia
- **Cytokine/gene expression**: ABX enhanced expression of cytokines and chemokines (IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, IL-10, IFN-gamma) in cerebral cortex but increased FoxP3 mRNA
- **Limitations**: Animal model (SJL/J mice); ABX cocktail is broad-spectrum; cannot distinguish direct viral effects from microbiota-mediated effects
