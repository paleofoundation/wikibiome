# Key Claims

- **First serum metabolomic analysis** in PPD using targeted metabolomics (SRM) measuring up to 300 metabolites
- **Increased levels** of glutathione-disulfide, adenylosuccinate, and ATP in PPD group, associated with oxidative stress, nucleotide biosynthesis, and energy production pathways
- **Molecular changes related to PPD** are detectable in peripheral serum material
- **Early life stressful conditions** in periconceptual, fetal, and early infant periods may be linked to adverse childhood outcomes and maternal psychiatric disorders

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Case-control within Rhea pregnancy cohort (Crete, Greece); PPD mothers vs. controls; serum analyzed by mass spectrometry (SRM)
- **Setting**: University of Ioannina, Greece; University of Crete, Greece
- **Key implication**: Serum metabolomics can detect PPD-related molecular changes, enabling potential biomarker development
