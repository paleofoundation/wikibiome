# Overview

Comprehensive review of the relationship between the **gut mycobiome** and neuropsychiatric disorders including schizophrenia, Alzheimer's disease, ASD, ADHD, bipolar disorder, and depression. Summarizes metagenomic findings, key fungal taxa, and therapeutic potential of targeting fungal communities.

# Key Findings

## ASD and Mycobiome

- Fungal families **Saccharomycetaceae** and **Debaryomycetaceae** observed at altered levels in ASD
- *Saccharomycetaceae* (including common yeast species) may influence gut motility and permeability
- *Debaryomycetaceae* may contribute to neurobehavioral metabolites impacting neurodevelopmental pathways
- Imbalances between bacterial and fungal populations may affect host microRNA expression and ASD development

## Key Findings Across Disorders (Table 2)

| Disorder | Key Fungal Species | Dysbiosis Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Schizophrenia | *Candida* | Overrepresentation; depletion of beneficial fungi |
| ASD | *Saccharomycetaceae*, *Debaryomycetaceae* | Altered levels; emerging biomarker potential |
| ADHD | *Candida albicans* | Higher abundance; affects gut permeability |
| Alzheimer's | *Dipodascaceae*, *Botrytis* | Altered profiles; inflammation and metabolic alterations |
| Depression | *Candida* | Increased levels; reduced diversity of beneficial fungi |

## Therapeutic Approaches

- Antifungal treatments, probiotics, and dietary modifications show promise
- Diet-based strategies (e.g., Mediterranean ketogenic diet) can reduce harmful fungi
- Multi-omics approaches needed to understand complex interkingdom interactions
- Mycobiome-based biomarkers could aid diagnosis and monitoring of disease progression

## Methodological Considerations

- ITS1 and ITS2 sequencing both used; some studies find ITS2 more suitable for revealing richness
- Specialized pipelines (LotuS, FindFungi) improving fungal sequence classification
- Data on fungal communities still limited partly because fungi are more challenging to manipulate
