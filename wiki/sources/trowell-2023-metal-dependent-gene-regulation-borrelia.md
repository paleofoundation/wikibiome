# Trowell & Yang 2023 — Metal-Dependent Gene Regulation in Borrelia burgdorferi

## Key Findings
- **Metal-dependent transcriptional regulators** (BosR, Fur-like) coordinate Borrelia virulence factor expression by sensing Fe, Mn, Zn, and Cu availability during tick-to-mammal transition [[trowell-2023-metal-dependent-gene-regulation-borrelia]]
- Borrelia lacks siderophores and canonical iron uptake systems, relying instead on manganese-centric metabolism — a unique metal dependency profile among spirochetes
- Multi-metal sensing allows Borrelia to distinguish host compartments (tick gut vs. mammalian blood) by their distinct metal landscapes

## Relevance to WikiBiome
- Demonstrates how a pathogen's metal-sensing apparatus doubles as a host-environment compass — directly supports Primitive 1 (metals as selective pressures)
- Borrelia's unusual manganese preference over iron illustrates how metal dependencies create exploitable vulnerabilities (Primitive 4)
