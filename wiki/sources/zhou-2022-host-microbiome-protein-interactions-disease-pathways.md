# Key Claims

- **Microbiome broadly targets human proteins** involved in immune, oncogenic, apoptotic, and endocrine signaling pathways
- **15,252 unique inter-species PPIs** identified between human proteins and gut microbiome-derived proteins
- **Differential targeting** of putative human-interacting bacterial genes observed across nine independent metagenomic studies
- **Akkermansia muciniphila P9** binds ICAM2 to increase thermogenesis and GLP-1 secretion -- therapeutic target for T2D
- **Fusobacterium nucleatum** protein Fap2 binds TIGIT, inhibiting natural killer cytotoxicity

# Methods & Findings

Computational analysis leveraging interspecies PPI data to identify disease-relevant microbiome-host interactions:

- Clusters of microbiome-derived proteins with high sequence identity to known human protein interactors identified
- Disease relevance demonstrated for IBD, CRC, obesity, and T2D
- Platform provides mechanistic hypothesis-generating framework

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Many identified protein-protein interactions involve zinc-dependent enzymes and zinc-finger transcription factors
- **[[iron]]**: Iron-binding proteins mediate key host-microbiome interactions at the intestinal epithelium
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Metal-dependent microbial proteins may preferentially interact with host metal-binding partners
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Identified immune pathway interactions (TIGIT, ICAM2) are metal-cofactor dependent
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Microbiome-host PPIs at tight junctions may be modulated by [[zinc]] availability
