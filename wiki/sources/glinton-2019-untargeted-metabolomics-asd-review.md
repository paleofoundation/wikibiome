# Overview

Review of **untargeted metabolomics principles and applications in ASD**, covering the neurometabolic framework, metabolomic profiling methodology, associations between ASD and inborn errors of metabolism (IEMs), and nonspecific metabolic disturbances. Describes the current state of technology and proposes future applications for ASD diagnosis and treatment management.

# Key Claims

## ASD and Inborn Errors of Metabolism

- Only ~5% of ASD children found to have an identifiable IEM
- Key IEMs associated with ASD:
 - **Phenylketonuria (PKU)**: OMIM 261600 — abnormal accumulation of urinary metabolites
 - **Creatine deficiency syndromes**: GAMT (OMIM 612736), AGAT (OMIM 612718), SLC6A8 (OMIM 300352)
 - **Adenylosuccinate lyase deficiency**
 - **5-nucleotidase and purine pyrimidine disorders**
 - **Cerebral creatine deficiency syndromes**
- 30-40% of children with ASD have IEM-related features prompting metabolic workup

## Metabolomic Profiling Primer

- NMR: minimal sample preparation, quantitative, but lower sensitivity
- Mass spectrometry (LC-MS, GC-MS): higher sensitivity, broader coverage
- **Targeted** vs **untargeted** approaches: complementary strengths
- Untargeted captures previously unrecognized metabolic changes

## ASD and Nonspecific Metabolic Disturbances

### Mitochondrial Dysfunction
- ~5% of ASD patients have definable mitochondrial disorder
- Elevated lactate in plasma common finding
- Complex I, IV, and V deficiency most frequently reported in brain tissue
- Oxidative phosphorylation deficits may emerge secondary to environmental exposures

### Cholesterol Metabolism
- Smith-Lemli-Opitz syndrome (SLOS): OMIM 270400 — defective cholesterol synthesis
- Low cholesterol levels reported in some ASD subpopulations
- Cholesterol essential for brain myelination, synaptic function, and steroid hormone synthesis

### Creatine Metabolism
- Creatine deficiency may be underdiagnosed in ASD
- Creatine phosphocreatine system essential for energy metabolism in neurons
- Creatine transporter (SLC6A8) X-linked — may explain sex ratio

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**High relevance** — untargeted metabolomics captures metal-dependent pathway disruptions:

- **Mitochondrial dysfunction**: [[iron]]-sulfur cluster proteins essential for Complex I-IV
- **Creatine synthesis**: requires [[zinc]]-dependent guanidinoacetate N-methyltransferase (GAMT)
- **Cholesterol metabolism**: multiple [[iron]]-dependent cytochrome P450 enzymes
- **PKU/phenylalanine metabolism**: phenylalanine hydroxylase requires [[iron]] cofactor
- Untargeted metabolomics ideal for discovering metal-metabolite interactions without a priori hypotheses
- Review explicitly mentions heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic) as environmental exposures in ASD etiology framework (Figure 1)

# Methodological Notes

- Comprehensive review from Baylor College of Medicine
- Published 2019 — captures state of field before major multi-omics studies
- Emphasizes need for larger cohorts, standardized protocols
- Notes that routine metabolic testing not recommended for isolated ASD (AAP)
- Identifies gap: untargeted metabolomics not yet standard in ASD clinical care
