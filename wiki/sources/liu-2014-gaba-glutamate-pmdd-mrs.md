# Liu 2014 — GABA and Glutamate-Glutamine Alterations in PMDD (3T MRS)

## Key Findings

- **GABA+ concentrations and GABA+/tCr ratios significantly lower** in both ACC/mPFC and left basal ganglia of PMDD women vs. controls during late luteal phase ([[liu-2014-gaba-glutamate-pmdd-mrs]], case-control, n=44).
- **Glx/tCr ratios significantly higher** in ACC/mPFC of PMDD women ([[liu-2014-gaba-glutamate-pmdd-mrs]]).
- This is the **first direct neuroimaging evidence** of GABA and glutamate-glutamine imbalance in mood-related brain regions in PMDD.
- Results suggest dysregulation of the amino acid neurotransmitter system is an important neurobiological mechanism in PMDD pathogenesis.

## Methods

Case-control study with 22 PMDD women (DSM-IV-TR criteria, 2 months prospective daily symptom ratings) and 22 age-matched controls. 3T proton MRS (1H-MRS) during late luteal phase. GABA+ and Glx quantified in anterior cingulate cortex/medial prefrontal cortex (ACC/mPFC) and left basal ganglia (ltBG). Water-scaled concentrations and metabolite/tCr ratios reported.

## Detailed Summary

The ACC/mPFC is a critical hub for emotional regulation, and the basal ganglia are involved in mood and reward processing. The simultaneous finding of **low GABA (inhibitory)** and **high Glx (excitatory)** in ACC/mPFC suggests a shift toward neuronal excitability in PMDD — consistent with the anxiety, irritability, and emotional dysregulation that characterize the disorder. This excitatory/inhibitory (E/I) imbalance provides a mechanistic target for the allopregnanolone (ALLO) hypothesis: ALLO normally enhances GABA-A receptor function, so reduced GABA availability plus impaired ALLO modulation creates a double hit.
