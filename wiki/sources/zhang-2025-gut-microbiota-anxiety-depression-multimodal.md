# Key Claims

- **Depression and anxiety** affect approximately 3.1% and 4.8% of the world's population respectively
- **Gut microbiota disruptions** may trigger or exacerbate symptoms via vagus nerve, HPA axis, immune responses (pro-[[inflammation]]atory cytokines), and SCFA-related metabolic processes
- **Multimodal interventions** reviewed: probiotics, prebiotics, postbiotics, synbiotics, dietary modifications, FMT, fecal virome transplantation (FVT), and traditional Chinese medicine (TCM)
- Inflammasome activation in depression patients can exacerbate neuroinflammation leading to reduced diversity and imbalance in gut microbiota

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Review synthesizing mechanistic evidence and translational potential of microbiota-targeted therapies
- **Key pathways**: Neural (vagus nerve), immune (cytokines, inflammasome), endocrine (HPA axis), metabolic (SCFAs)
