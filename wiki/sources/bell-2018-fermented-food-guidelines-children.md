# Key Claims

- **Fermented foods and mushroom biomass** may modulate the probiotic profile of gut flora in children
- **Children with neurobehavioral disorders** (ADHD, ASD) may benefit from fermented foods that improve brain function through dietary changes
- **Lactobacillus** is the most abundant genus in fermented products, followed by Enterococcus, Vibrio, Weissella, Pediococcus, Enterobacter, Salmonella, Acinetobacter, Macrococcus, Kluyvera, and Clostridium
- **Food regulatory frameworks** for fermented foods for children are confusing and limiting

# Methods & Findings

**Mini review** of fermented food guidelines for children with emphasis on microbiome modulation.

**Key points:**
- Fermentation processes developed 9000 years ago; preserve food, improve flavor, reduce poisonous effects
- Children get their gut microbiota from birth and milking; intestinal flora remains relatively stable throughout life
- Some studies suggest children with autism have abnormal communities of digestive bacteria
- Specific types of gut bacteria associated with more severe autism symptoms (including Clostridia producing propionic acid)
- Propionic acid from microbiome modulation by fermentation can affect mitochondrial function in ASD
- Two types of probiotic compounds: microbial peptides (produced by dairy fungi) and biogenic amines (HA) from metabolic activity of lactic acid bacteria
- Biogenic amines in dairy products include histamine, tyramine, putrescine

**Recommendations:**
- Include small amounts of fermented foods from early age
- Breast milk contains >500 different fatty acids and beneficial bacteria
- Fermented foods and mushroom biomass supplements as adjunctive therapy
- Need for RCTs measuring quantitative repeatable effects

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Fermentation-metal axis**: Lacto-fermentation produces organic acids that enhance metal bioavailability (iron, zinc, calcium absorption improved by lactic acid)
- **Biogenic amines**: Histamine and tyramine production requires metal-dependent decarboxylase enzymes; dysregulation may contribute to ASD symptoms
- **Propionic acid**: Clostridial fermentation product requiring cobalt-dependent enzymes; modulates mitochondrial function in ASD
- **Early introduction**: Fermented food introduction during critical metal-dependent neurodevelopmental windows may support beneficial microbiome establishment
