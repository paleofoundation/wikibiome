# Key Claims

- **PPD affects 13-19% of females** who newly attained motherhood; symptoms appear 4-6 weeks postpartum
- **Pathophysiology** explained by biological, psychological, integrated, and evolutionary models
- **Methyldopa** used during pregnancy for hypertension may contribute to PPD development through different mechanisms
- **SSRIs** (sertraline) are effective in acute management; venlafaxine and desvenlafaxine also used
- **Brexanolone** (allopregnanolone) made into sterile solution leads to rapid decline of PPD symptoms
- **30% of women** may continue depression for 2 years postpartum; 55% have major depression throughout

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive literature review covering PPD pathophysiology, risk factors, diagnosis, and treatment
- **Key comparison table**: Postpartum blues vs. PPD vs. postpartum psychosis symptom differences
