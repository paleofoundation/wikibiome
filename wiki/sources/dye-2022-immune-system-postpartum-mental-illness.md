# Key Claims

- **Immune system aberrations** contribute to [[postpartum-depression]], postpartum anxiety (PPA), and postpartum psychosis (PP)
- **Peripheral cytokine signaling** changes are documented in clinical data; animal models reveal central [[inflammation]] may also contribute
- **Pro-inflammatory Th1 response** predominates in first trimester and late third trimester; shift to anti-inflammatory Th2 during mid-pregnancy
- **PPD prevalence**: 10-20%; onset within weeks to months; PPA: 10-20% with generalized anxiety; PP: rare (1:1,000 births)
- Neural and endocrine interactions with immune function are critical for postpartum mental health

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Review of clinical and preclinical evidence for immune involvement in postpartum psychiatric disorders
- **Key findings**: Pro-inflammatory cytokine elevations (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-alpha) associated with PPD risk; Th1/Th2 balance disruption during peripartum transition
