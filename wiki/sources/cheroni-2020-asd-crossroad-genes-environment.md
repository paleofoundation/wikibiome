# Overview

Review from European Institute of Oncology (Milan) addressing the **convergence of genetic and environmental factors** in ASD pathophysiology. Presents the genetic architecture of ASD (SFARI database: 913 genes), reviews environmental risk factors (heavy metals, air pollution, endocrine disruptors, phthalates, pesticides), and proposes **stem cell-based experimental approaches** (iPSC, brain organoids, CRISPR) to dissect gene-environment interactions at single-cell resolution.

# Key Claims

## Genetic Architecture of ASD
- SFARI: 913 genes cataloged and scored
- Key convergent pathways: epigenetic regulation of gene expression, alternative splicing, regulation of translation, synaptic activity
- Chromatin regulators prominent: CHD8, CHD2, MECP2, HDAC4, FOXP1, TBR1
- Polygenic risk scores overlap with schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, educational attainment

## Environmental Risk Factors (Table 2)

### Heavy Metals
- Systematic reviews support positive association between child exposure to heavy metals and ASD risk
- **[[lead]]**: higher levels and lower [[manganese]] in ASD (tooth-matrix biomarker study)
- Blood lead concentration inversely and significantly associated with IQ
- Pooled analysis: 6.9 IQ point decrease per 2.4-30 ug/dL blood lead increase

### Methylmercury
- Prenatal exposure → language, attention, memory deficits
- "Lowest observable adverse effect hair concentration" defined from 48 studies
- Some studies show no relevant association at age 9

### Air Pollution
- PM2.5: OR 1.57 (95% CI 1.22-2.03) per IQR increase
- PM10: OR 1.07 (CI 1.06-1.08) per 10 ug/m3 increase
- **Diesel, lead, manganese, mercury, methylene chloride**: OR 1.5-2.0 for ASD
- Stronger association in boys for PM2.5 (hazard ratio 1.18 for first trimester)

### Endocrine Disruptors
- BPA, phthalates: prenatal exposure → adverse neurobehavior
- Di-n-butyl phthalate (DnBP), diisobutyl phthalate (DiBP), benzyl butyl phthalate (BBzP): adversely affect child mental, motor, behavioral development

### Pesticides
- Organophosphates, organochlorines
- Proximity to agricultural areas during pregnancy increases risk

## Gene x Environment Convergence
- Environmental factors perturb same molecular pathways as ASD risk genes
- Chromatin remodeling, epigenetic regulation, synaptic function
- Convergence suggests shared vulnerability targets

## Experimental Approaches Proposed
- **iPSC-derived brain organoids**: model gene-environment interactions in human tissue
- **CRISPR**: introduce ASD-relevant mutations and assess environmental vulnerability
- **Single-cell multi-omics**: dissect cell-type-specific responses
- **Biomonitoring + epidemiology + experimental models**: integrative framework needed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**Very high relevance** — provides direct evidence tables for metal exposures and ASD:

- Table 2 comprehensively catalogs heavy metal-ASD epidemiological evidence
- Chromatin regulators in ASD (CHD8, HDAC4, MECP2) are [[zinc]]-dependent
- Air pollution delivers complex metal mixtures to developing brain
- Organoid models could test metal exposure effects on ASD-relevant neurodevelopment
- Gene-environment convergence framework ideal for testing whether metal dyshomeostasis amplifies genetic ASD risk
