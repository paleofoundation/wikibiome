## Key Findings

- **Peak MeHg intake occurs at baby food stages 3 and 4** (9-12 and 12-17 months), reaching 346.6 and 297.5 ng/kg bw/day respectively — substantially higher than in formula milk (2.2 ng/kg bw/day).
- **MeHg represents ~90% of total Hg in the diet** when THg ≥ 1 ng/g, consistent with fish as the dominant dietary Hg source in Japan.
- **MeHg intake exceeded reference dose or PTWI in several children**, despite MHLW recommendations for pregnant women to limit large-fish consumption.
- **Hair THg weakly correlated with dietary MeHg** (r=0.170), suggesting considerable interindividual variation in uptake/elimination or contributions from prenatal exposure and non-dietary sources.

## Methods

3 days of 24-hour duplicate-diet sampling in 260 children aged 0-5 years from the MHLW Low-risk Infants Observational Study (2020-2022). Formula (0-4 months), baby foods (stages 1-4 per age), toddler meals (stages 1-3). Total Hg by CVAAS (Hg-201); MeHg by alkaline decomposition/dithizone/GC-ECD. Hair THg by direct thermal decomposition gold amalgamation CVAAS.
