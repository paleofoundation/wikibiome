# Key Claims

- Clostridium innocuum group (OR: 2.17, P=0.00038) and Lachnospira (OR: 2.45, P=0.017) causally increase hidradenitis suppurativa (HS) risk
- Family XI (Clostridiales, OR: 0.67) and Porphyromonadaceae (OR: 0.29) are protective against HS
- No reverse causation was found -- HS does not alter gut microbiota composition
- The gut-skin axis in HS involves loss of SCFA-producing bacteria and expansion of pathogenic Clostridium species, paralleling IBD-associated dysbiosis patterns

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Bidirectional two-sample MR using MiBioGen (18,340 individuals) and FinnGen (409 HS cases, 211,139 controls)
- **Results**: 4 significant genera identified; all passed sensitivity analyses (MR-Egger intercept P>0.05, Cochrane Q P>0.05)
- **Clostridium innocuum**: Gram-positive spore-forming anaerobe linked to antibiotic-associated diarrhea and IBD; excessive colonization reported in IBD
- **Protective taxa**: Porphyromonadaceae (Bacteroidetes/Clostridiales) produce SCFAs that maintain barrier function; Family XI produces SCFAs preventing systemic endotoxemia
- **Mechanism**: Loss of butyrate-producing bacteria weakens gut epithelial barrier, allowing translocation of pro-inflammatory antigens to skin
