# Antidepressants Fluoxetine and Amitriptyline Induce Alterations in Intestinal Microbiota and Gut Microbiome Function

## Summary

Study examining the effect of SSRI fluoxetine (Flu) and tricyclic antidepressant amitriptyline (Ami) on gut microbiota composition, diversity, and microbial function in a chronic unpredictable mild stress (CUMS) depression rat model using both 16S rRNA and shotgun metagenomic sequencing.

## Key Findings

### Compositional Changes
- 6-week antidepressant treatment led to decreased F/B ratio due to enhanced *Bacteroidetes* and reduced *Firmicutes*
- Flu was more potent than Ami at altering F/B ratio
- Both antidepressants significantly increased *Porphyromonadaceae*
- Increased *Bacteroidaceae* associated with Ami but not Flu treatment
- At genus level, *Parabacteroides*, *Butyricimonas*, and *Alistipes* increased following both treatments

### Alpha and Beta Diversity
- Ami treatment enhanced Shannon Index (richness and evenness); p < 0.05 vs. CUMS
- Flu treatment increased observed OTU number; p < 0.05 vs. CUMS
- Both treatments shifted beta-diversity back toward healthy control profiles

### Functional Changes (Shotgun Metagenomics)
- Both antidepressants modulated metabolic pathways: carbohydrate metabolism, membrane transport, signal transduction
- Both affected antibiotic resistome: aminoglycoside (*aph3iiiA*), multidrug (*mdtK, mdtP, mdtH, mdtG, acrA*), tetracycline (*tetM*) resistance genes altered
- Direct antimicrobial-like effects of antidepressants on gut microbiota demonstrated

### Depression-Microbiome Link
- CUMS rats showed distinct microbiota clustering from healthy controls (PERMANOVA p < 0.05)
- Strong association between gut microbiota composition and depression-like behaviors
- Antidepressant treatment partially reversed CUMS-induced dysbiosis

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Antidepressant-microbiome interactions** - [[Antidepressants]] directly reshape gut microbiota, relevant to CVD patients on SSRIs or tricyclics
2. **Antibiotic resistance** - Antidepressants alter the gut antibiotic resistome, with implications for infection management in [[cardiovascular-disease]] patients
3. **Gut-brain-heart axis** - Depression-associated dysbiosis overlaps with CVD-associated microbiome changes, suggesting shared pathophysiology
