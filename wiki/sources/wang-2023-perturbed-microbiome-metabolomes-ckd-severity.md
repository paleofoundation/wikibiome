## Key Findings

- **26 species significantly changed** across CKD severity stages, with progressive depletion of SCFA producers and enrichment of uremic toxin producers.
- **[[ruminococcus-bromii]]** was the main contributor to the microbial co-occurrence network, acting as a keystone species whose loss cascades through the ecosystem.
- Combined microbiome + metabolite model achieved **AUC 0.972 for detecting mild CKD**, outperforming serum creatinine alone.
- **Gut disruption is detectable earlier than conventional biomarkers**, supporting the use of microbiome signatures for early CKD screening.
- **[[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]]** and **[[eubacterium-rectale]]** were depleted (SCFA loss), while **[[citrobacter]]** and **[[flavonifractor]]** were enriched (uremic toxin production).
