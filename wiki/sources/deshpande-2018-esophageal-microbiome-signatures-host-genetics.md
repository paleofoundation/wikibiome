# Signatures within the esophageal microbiome are associated with host genetics, age, and disease

## Key Claims

- The [[esophageal microbiome]] clusters into three functionally distinct community types (esotypes) defined by the relative abundances of [[Streptococcus]] and [[Prevotella]] in 106 prospectively recruited patients
- Age was a significant factor driving microbiome composition; age positively correlated with *Streptococcus parasanguinis* but inversely correlated with *P. melaninogenica*
- Gram-negative oral-associated bacteria and microbial lactic acid production were associated with early stages of the [[esophageal adenocarcinoma]] cascade, linking [[dysbiosis]] to [[Barretts esophagus]] progression
- Non-bacterial microbes including archaea, [[Candida]] spp., and bacteriophages were identified in low abundance in the esophageal microbiome
- Specific host SNPs in *NOTCH2*, *STEAP2-AS1*, and *NREP* were associated with the composition of the esophageal microbiome, establishing a host-genetics link to [[microbiome composition]]
- The ratio of *Streptococcus* to *Prevotella* is a defining characteristic across esophageal community types, with co-exclusion interaction consistently observed

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional prospective cohort study
- **Population**: 106 patients assessed using both 16S rRNA and 18S rRNA amplicon sequencing and shotgun sequencing (99 samples)
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA amplicon and shotgun metagenomics via MetaPhlAn2 and HUMAnN2
- **Community types**: Cluster 1 enriched for glycolysis and [[SCFA]] metabolism; Cluster 2 enriched for pentose phosphate pathway and fructose/mannose metabolism; Cluster 3 enriched for lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis
- **Cluster 2**: Dominated by *Streptococcus* (*S. mitis/oralis/pneumoniae*), had lower alpha diversity (species richness, evenness, Shannon diversity)
- **Cluster 3**: Enriched for *Prevotella* (*P. melaninogenica*, *P. pallens*) and *Veillonella*
- **PPI use**: Associated with clustering of esophageal microbiome via both amplicon and shotgun data
- **Disease association**: Bacterial signatures and functions associated with age, disease status, and [[proton pump inhibitor]] use
- **SparCC analysis**: Co-exclusion interaction between *Streptococcus mitis/oralis/pneumoniae* and *Prevotella* spp. consistently found across all three community types
