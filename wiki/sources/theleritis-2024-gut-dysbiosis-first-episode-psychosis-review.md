# Association of gut dysbiosis with first-episode psychosis

## Summary

This narrative review summarizes the significant differences in altered microbiome composition in first-episode psychosis (FEP) patients versus healthy controls, and discusses the effects of gut [[dysbiosis]] on symptom occurrence and intensity in FEP. It includes a comprehensive table of 10 studies evaluating gut microbiome alterations in FEP.

## Key Findings

- FEP patients show increased *Lactobacillus*, decreased *Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*, and *Coprococcus*
- Increased *Haemophilus* positively associated with negative psychiatric symptoms; *Coprococcus* negatively associated
- FMT from schizophrenia patients to germ-free mice induces psychomotor hyperactivity and altered neurotransmitter levels
- *Streptococcus vestibularis*, a schizophrenia-enriched bacterium, induced social behavior deficits in recipient mice
- Gut microbiota responsible for GABA, serotonin, [[tryptophan-metabolism]], and dopamine production
- [[Short-chain-fatty-acids]] from butyrate-producing bacteria (e.g., *Faecalibacterium*) are depleted in FEP
- Increased intestinal permeability, chronic inflammation, and oxidative stress proposed as mediating mechanisms
- Treatment with risperidone alters gut microbiome composition and increases butyric acid levels

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This review highlights that [[dysbiosis]] in first-episode psychosis is characterized by depletion of butyrate-producing bacteria and enrichment of opportunistic species. The loss of [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] production removes a key anti-inflammatory and gut barrier-maintaining signal, potentially allowing increased translocation of metals and endotoxins. The disruption of [[tryptophan-metabolism]] and serotonin synthesis links directly to the kynurenine pathway abnormalities observed in schizophrenia. The finding that FMT from schizophrenia patients transfers behavioral phenotypes supports a causal role for [[dysbiosis]] via the [[gut-brain-axis]]. The role of [[probiotics]] in modifying this trajectory is discussed but evidence remains limited.

## Limitations

- Narrative review with inherent selection bias
- Studies reviewed had small sample sizes
- Heterogeneity in FEP definitions and medication status
