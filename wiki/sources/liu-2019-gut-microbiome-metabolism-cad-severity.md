# Alterations in the Gut Microbiome and Metabolism with Coronary Artery Disease Severity

## Summary

Multi-omic study of 161 CAD patients (44 SCAD, 80 UA, 37 MI) and 40 controls combining 16S rRNA V3-V4 sequencing and untargeted LC-MS metabolomics. Identified 29 metabolite modules correlated with CAD phenotypes and bacterial co-abundance groups (CAGs) with characteristic changes at different stages of CAD.

## Key Findings

### Metabolomic Signatures
- CAD patients had significantly different metabolic profiles compared to controls
- CAD-positive metabotypes: phospholipid metabolism, phosphocholines, neutral glycosphingolipids, steroids/sterol derivatives
- CAD-negative metabotypes: amino acids, carboxylic acids, fatty acyl carnitines, potassium chloride, taurine/hypotaurine
- Benzene and substituted derivatives were the strongest discriminatory features (AUC up to 0.955)

### Gut Microbiome-Metabolite Integration
- 9 gut microbiota CAGs were significantly correlated with 14 metabolic modules
- CAG4 (*Faecalibacterium* and *Roseburia*) closely related to 10 serum modules, important for maintaining normal coronary artery physiology
- CAG13 (*Butyricomonas*) positively associated with carboxylic acids and glycerolipid metabolites
- CAG9 (*Clostridium*) negatively correlated with glycerophospholipids

### Disease Classifier
- Random forest classifier using CAG + metabolite modules achieved AUC of 0.955 for Control vs. CAD
- Key features: benzene derivatives, phenolic glycoside, heteroaromatic compounds, taurine, tyrosine, amino acids
- Could distinguish SCAD from ACS (AUC 0.897) and classify MI from UA (AUC 0.855)

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Butyrate producer depletion** - Loss of CAG4 (*Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*) in severe CAD mirrors [[cadmium]] and [[lead]]-induced depletion of these same taxa
2. **Taurine metabolism disruption** - Taurine/hypotaurine pathway alterations link to [[selenium]]-dependent antioxidant pathways and [[oxidative-stress]]
3. **Sphingolipid changes** - Altered sphingolipid metabolism connects [[dysbiosis]] to [[metabolic-syndrome]] progression
4. **Multi-omic diagnostic potential** - Integration of microbiome and metabolomics could detect metal-induced CVD risk earlier
