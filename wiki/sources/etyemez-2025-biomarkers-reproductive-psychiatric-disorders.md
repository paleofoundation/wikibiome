# Etyemez 2025 — Biomarkers of Reproductive Psychiatric Disorders (Review)

## Key Findings

- **No clinically useful biomarker** currently exists for any reproductive psychiatric disorder — PMDD, perinatal depression, postpartum psychosis, or perimenopausal depression ([[etyemez-2025-biomarkers-reproductive-psychiatric-disorders]], review).
- **PMDD-specific cytokine signature:** IL-8 (CXCL-8) and TNF-α elevated during luteal phase — a measurable inflammatory signal tied to symptom cycling. **IL-8 positively correlates with symptom severity** ([[etyemez-2025-biomarkers-reproductive-psychiatric-disorders]]).
- **Allopregnanolone (ALLO)** levels and **ALLO/progesterone ratios** distinguish PMDD from PMS and controls — neurosteroid axis biomarker ([[etyemez-2025-biomarkers-reproductive-psychiatric-disorders]]).
- **PMDD epigenetic candidates:** DNA methylation changes at GABA_A receptor subunits and ESR2 (estrogen receptor beta) / OXTR (oxytocin receptor) genes identified in small studies ([[etyemez-2025-biomarkers-reproductive-psychiatric-disorders]]).
- **HP1BP3 and TTC9B** epigenetic markers for antenatal depression prediction showed replication potential; OXTR methylation associated with postpartum depression ([[etyemez-2025-biomarkers-reproductive-psychiatric-disorders]]).
- Key biomarker domains across reproductive psychiatric disorders: **epigenetics, neuroactive steroids, immune function, and neuroimaging** ([[etyemez-2025-biomarkers-reproductive-psychiatric-disorders]]).

## Methods

Narrative review of PubMed articles (2010+) on biomarkers for six conditions: perinatal depression/anxiety, OCD, postpartum psychosis, PMDD, and perimenopausal depression. Keywords: biomarker, epigenetics, neuroactive steroid, immune, inflammatory, neuroimaging.

- Cross-condition biomarker patterns: shared epigenetic vulnerability across PMDD, PPD, perimenopausal depression.
