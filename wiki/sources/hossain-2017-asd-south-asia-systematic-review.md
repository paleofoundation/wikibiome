# Overview

Systematic review of ASD prevalence studies in **eight South Asian countries** (Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, Maldives, Afghanistan). Only **6 studies** from 3 countries met inclusion criteria, revealing major gaps in epidemiological data.

# Key Findings

## ASD Prevalence in South Asia

- **Bangladesh**: 0.2-0.84% (Dhaka city reported highest at 3%)
- **India**: 0.09-0.23%
- **Sri Lanka**: 1.07% (one in 93 children)
- No studies found from **Pakistan, Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives, or Afghanistan**

## Study Characteristics

- Sample sizes ranged from 374 (Sri Lanka) to 18,480 (India)
- Age range: 1 to 30 years across studies
- Eight different screening/diagnostic instruments used (SDQ, DAWBA, RQC, DSQ, ADOS, ISAA, DSM-IV-TR, M-CHAT)

## Barriers to Accurate Prevalence

- Methodological differences in case definition, screening instruments, and diagnostic criteria
- Poor knowledge about autism: frequently missed in South Asian health systems
- Limited specialist services; pluralistic health systems with faith in traditional healers
- Low and middle income countries (LMICs) have limited research output

## Implications

- 1.7-2 million children with neurodevelopmental disorders estimated in India alone
- Urgent need for standardized screening tools applicable across cultures
- WHO recognized importance through 67th World Health Assembly resolution
