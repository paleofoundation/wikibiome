# Overview

Public health review of **autism spectrum disorder (ASD) etiology, epidemiology, and clinical presentation**. Emphasizes ASD as growing public health concern affecting ~1% of children globally, with substantial variation in prevalence estimates.

# Key Claims

- **ASD affects 1 in 100 children** (WHO estimate); prevalence varies with assessment methodology
- Core features: **impaired social skills, specific repetitive behaviors, abnormal sensory responses**
- Symptom severity and manifestation **highly heterogeneous** across individuals
- **Genetic and environmental factors** both implicated; etiology remains unclear
- Early detection and intervention **essential for outcomes** and family functioning
- Clinical symptoms span social/communication domains and **cognitive functioning**

# Etiology Notes

- Strong **genetic heritability** but environmental contributions recognized
- **Leo Kanner** first described autism systematically (founding clinical framework)
- Suggests multifactorial model combining genetic predisposition + environmental triggers

# Epidemiology

- Prevalence varies widely depending on:
 - Assessment criteria
 - Age group studied
 - Geographic region
 - Healthcare access
- Growing recognition as major public health issue

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework
