# Key Findings

- **Missing mandibular molars** (a marker of severe periodontal disease) associated with **2.36-fold increased risk of breast cancer** (OR 2.36) in a prospective cohort followed over 16 years [[soder-2010-periodontal-disease-breast-cancer-association]], prospective-cohort, n=3,273.
- Breast cancer incidence was **1.75% in subjects with periodontal disease vs. 0.95% without** [[soder-2010-periodontal-disease-breast-cancer-association]].
- Chronic periodontal disease appeared as an **independent predictor** of breast cancer after adjusting for age, smoking, and socioeconomic status.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Strongest epidemiological evidence connecting [[periodontitis]] to breast cancer risk.
- The prospective design (16-year follow-up) strengthens the causal inference beyond cross-sectional studies.
- Connects to the oral-breast translocation hypothesis and to [[porphyromonas-gingivalis]] as the keystone periodontal pathogen.
