# Key Claims

- [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]] from healthy donors can restore the dysbiotic gut, modulating or reconstructing intestinal microbiota to improve [[immunotherapy]] efficacy in cancer treatment
- Gut microbiota dysbiosis contributes to CRC development through inflammation (NF-kB, STAT3 pathways), DNA damage (colibactin from E. coli), oxidative stress, and altered metabolite production
- [[Fusobacterium nucleatum]] activates Wnt/beta-catenin and NF-kB signaling via FadA adhesin, directly promoting [[colon cancer]] cell proliferation
- FMT combined with anti-PD-1 immunotherapy can convert non-responders to responders; sterile mice treated with antibiotics then given FMT showed enhanced response to anti-PD-1
- Multiple ongoing clinical trials (Table 1) combine FMT with immune checkpoint blockers (ICBs) across melanoma, NSCLC, gastrointestinal cancer, and other solid tumors
- Short-chain fatty acids (butyrate, propionate) inhibit inflammation and tumor development, while secondary bile acids can promote cancerous growth

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review
- **Microbial mechanisms of tumorigenesis**: (1) Inflammation via NF-kB/STAT3, (2) DNA damage by colibactin and reactive oxygen species, (3) altered metabolites (SCFAs vs secondary bile acids), (4) biofilm formation, (5) distant site effects via blood circulation
- **Pathogenic bacteria in CRC**: F. nucleatum (FadA/Wnt signaling), B. fragilis (BFT toxin/beta-catenin), E. coli (colibactin/DNA damage), Enterococcus faecalis (superoxide/ROS), segmented filamentous bacteria (Th17/IL-17)
- **FMT delivery methods**: Upper GI (duodenal tube, oral capsules) or lower GI (colonoscopy, enema); each has advantages
- **Clinical trials**: 20+ trials listed combining FMT + ICBs; most are Phase 1-2, primarily in melanoma; gastrointestinal cancer trials include FMT via stool capsules + ICBs (NCT04130763, Peking University, recruiting)
- **FMT + chemotherapy**: Microbiome can metabolize drugs (5-FU, cyclophosphamide); FMT may improve chemotherapy recovery rate and reduce toxicity
- **FMT + radiotherapy**: Gut microbiota affected by radiation; FMT may help reduce radiation-induced GI toxicity
