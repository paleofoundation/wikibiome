# Overview

Comprehensive systematic review of **44 studies** assessing **gut microbiota composition in ASD children** and microbiota-modulation interventions. Identifies consistent trends in dysbiosis patterns and intervention efficacy.

# Key Microbiota Findings

## Taxonomic Signature (Dysbiosis Pattern)

- **Increased Firmicutes**: 36–81% of ASD children (consistent elevation)
- **Increased Pseudomonadota**: 78% of ASD children (significant enrichment)
- **Decreased Bacteroidetes**: 56% of ASD children (consistent depletion)
- **Bacteroidetes:Firmicutes ratio** tends to be **lower (56%) vs controls**
 - **Correlates with behavioral and gastrointestinal abnormalities**
 - Key indicator of dysbiosis severity

## Gastrointestinal Comorbidity

- Many ASD children experience **functional gastrointestinal disorders**
- Dysmotility, constipation, diarrhea impact quality of life
- **Microbiota imbalance may exacerbate core and GI symptoms**

# Intervention Evidence

## Probiotics

- **Most promising intervention** for behavioral and GI symptoms (66% efficacy reported)
- **Effective strains**: [[lactobacillus]], [[bifidobacterium]], [[streptococcus-pneumoniae]]
- Show promise in alleviating both behavioral and gastrointestinal symptoms
- Mechanism: metabolite production, intestinal barrier support, immune modulation

## Microbiota Transfer Therapy (MTT)

- Limited data but **lasting benefits** observed in one longitudinal study
- May restore microbial diversity and function more comprehensively than probiotics

## Prebiotics

- Potentially helpful for **GI and behavioral issues**
- **Heterogeneous evidence** — different prebiotic formulations, doses, durations
- Requires further standardized research for conclusive efficacy

# Mechanistic Insights

- **Gut microbiota–ASD interplay** likely involves:
 - Microbial metabolite production (short-chain fatty acids, tryptophan metabolites)
 - Intestinal barrier integrity
 - Immune system activation
 - Microbial-derived signals to CNS via vagal and systemic pathways

# Methodological Limitations

- **Study heterogeneity** high
- **Small sample sizes** across most studies
- Methodological variations complicate meta-analysis
- Need for **standardized protocols** for microbiota assessment, interventions, outcome measures

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**High relevance** — directly addresses microbiota signatures in ASD. Identifies [[Firmicutes]] enrichment and [[Bacteroidetes]] depletion as core dysbiosis pattern. However, **paper does not assess metal cofactors, metal-dependent enzymes, or microbial metal dependencies**. Could be enhanced by integrating metal-metallome data:

- Are [[Firmicutes]] enriched because they tolerate or depend on specific metals?
- Do metal restrictions correlate with [[Bacteroidetes]] recovery?
- What metal cofactors support the metabolite-producing capacity of depleted taxa?
