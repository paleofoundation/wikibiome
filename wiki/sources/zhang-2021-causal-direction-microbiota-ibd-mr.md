# Key Claims

- Three bacterial features are causally associated with IBD risk: family Verrucomicrobiaceae (beta=0.04, p=0.05), genus Akkermansia (beta=0.04, p=0.05), and genus Dorea (beta=-0.07, p=0.04)
- All three were replicated in an independent British IBD study with consistent effect direction
- Akkermansia, which has known anti-inflammatory properties and is depleted in IBD patients, may have a causal anti-protective role when its regulatory mechanisms are overwhelmed
- Dorea, part of the Lachnospiraceae family, is negatively associated with IBD, and its depletion along with decreased butyric acid has been documented in IBD patients

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample Mendelian randomization using TwinsUK microbiota GWAS as exposure, UKIBD and USP study as discovery, British IBD as replication
- **Exposure**: TwinsUK microbiota GWAS (N=1,126 twin pairs); 30 features (15 families, 15 genera) with genome-wide significant SNPs
- **Outcome (discovery)**: UKIBD (N=48,328) and USP (N=35,289)
- **Discovery results**: Verrucomicrobiaceae (2 IVs, beta=0.04), Akkermansia (2 IVs, beta=0.04), Dorea (1 IV, beta=-0.07)
- **Replication**: All three replicated (Verrucomicrobiaceae p=0.02, Akkermansia p=0.02, Dorea p=0.01)
- **Instrumental variables**: SNPs at loci 6q24.1 (near PNLDC1/SLC39A8), 18q12.3 (near SLC14A1), and 18q12.3 (near KCO6)
