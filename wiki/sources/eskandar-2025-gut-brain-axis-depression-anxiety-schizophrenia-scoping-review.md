# The Gut-Brain Axis in Depression, Anxiety, and Schizophrenia: A Scoping Review

## Key Claims

- Scoping review of 145 studies (2015-2025) on [[gut-brain-axis]] mechanisms, biomarkers, and therapeutic implications in [[depression]], anxiety, and [[schizophrenia]]
- Consistent patterns of microbial [[dysbiosis]], short-chain fatty acid disturbances, kynurenine pathway alterations, and immune activation found across all three disorders
- In MDD, depletion of butyrate- and propionate-producing bacteria with lower plasma SCFAs correlated with greater symptom severity and poorer remission rates
- In anxiety, depletion of SCFA-producing taxa and enrichment of Proteobacteria observed, though confounded by diet, medication, and lifestyle
- In schizophrenia, altered metabolite profiles including low SCFAs, increased markers of immune activation, and compromised BBB integrity documented
- Table 1 summarizes four mechanistic pathways: Neural (vagus/ENS), Immune (IL-6, TNF-alpha, LPS translocation), Endocrine (HPA axis, cortisol-microbiome loop), Metabolic (SCFAs, [[tryptophan-metabolism]], KYN/TRP ratios)
- SCFA reductions of 20-50% lower in MDD; KYN/TRP ratios 15-50% elevated in MDD/psychosis
- Interventions (probiotics, prebiotics, synbiotics, diet, FMT) showed modest benefits for depression/anxiety but mixed/preliminary findings for schizophrenia
- Cross-disorder synthesis suggests shared inflammatory-metabolic pathways and disorder-specific signatures

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR, registered in PROSPERO)
- **Studies included**: 145 empirical studies (human, animal, multi-omics)
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Embase, PsycINFO, Web of Science, Scopus (Jan 2015 - June 2025)
