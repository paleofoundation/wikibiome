# Key Claims

- **Microbial biomarkers for T1D may be present as early as 1 year of age**, up to 20 years before diagnosis
- **Parasutterella and Eubacterium** more abundant in healthy control infants
- **Porphyromonas differentially more abundant** in infants with future T1D diagnosis
- **Ruminococcus was strong determinant** in differentiating both control and future T1D infants
- **Predicted gene content** regarding butyrate production and pyruvate fermentation differentially higher in controls

# Methods & Findings

ABIS population cohort study: 16 future T1D infants + 268 matched controls at mean age 12.5 months:

- 16S rRNA sequencing and qPCR used for microbial analysis
- PICRUSt used to predict functional content from 16S amplicons
- Random forest analysis with 32 matched iterations of 32 controls

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Infant zinc status at 1 year may determine butyrate-producing community establishment -- zinc-dependent developmental window
- **[[iron]]**: Early iron supplementation patterns may shape the microbial community trajectory toward or away from T1D risk
- **[[selenium]]**: Infant selenium status influences antioxidant capacity that protects beta-cells during critical developmental period
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Early-life metal exposure shapes the founding microbial community that persists for decades
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: 1-year microbiome reflects cumulative metal-nutrient-microbial interactions from birth
- **[[probiotics]]**: Early probiotic intervention in at-risk infants could be combined with metal optimization
