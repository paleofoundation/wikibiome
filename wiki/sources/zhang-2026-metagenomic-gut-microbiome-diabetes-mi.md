# A Metagenomic Study of the Gut Microbiome in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus and Myocardial Infarction

## Summary

Shotgun metagenomic study comparing gut microbiota of DM patients complicated by MI (DM+MI, n=29) vs. DM alone (n=33). Investigated taxonomic composition, metabolic functions, and gene functions using KEGG, COG, and CAZy annotations.

## Key Findings

### Taxonomic Differences
- Overall species and pathway diversity comparable between groups
- However, significant differences in specific taxa and functional genes
- DM+MI enriched: *Bacteroidales*, *Bacteroidota*, *Prevotellaceae*, *P. copri*, *Negativicutes*, *Lachnospiraceae*
- DM enriched: *Firmicutes/Clostridia/Eubacteriales*, *Lachnospiraceae*, *Negativicutes*
- Key discriminative species: *Burkholderia*, *Bacteroides stercoris*, *Ralstonia*, *Subdoligranulum variabile*, *Phocaeicola*, *Eubacterium callanderi*, *Enterococcus faecium*, *Cellulomonas carbonis*, *Clostridium porci*

### Functional Alterations
- 510 KEGG KOs and 21 level-3 pathways significantly different
- ABC transporters, metabolic pathways, quorum sensing among most enriched
- CAZy annotation: glycoside hydrolases (GHs) and glycosyl transferases (GTs) dominant
- 7,742 KOs detected; metabolism most enriched functional category

### Diagnostic Models
- Random forest model using top 10 microbial species: AUC = 0.868
- Random forest using top 49 KO units: AUC = 0.885
- Random forest using top 7 KEGG pathways: AUC = 0.820
- *Streptococcus sanguinis* showed largest between-group difference by LEfSe

### Ecological Dynamics
- PERMANOVA R2 = 0.024 (p = 0.076); ANOSIM R = 0.038 (p = 0.052) - borderline significance
- Reduced ecosystem stability and insufficient suppression of potentially harmful taxa in DM+MI
- Compositional separation evident despite borderline statistical significance

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Diabetes-CVD comorbidity** - Gut microbiome distinguishes DM patients with MI from those without, supporting microbiome-based risk stratification for [[cardiovascular-disease]]
2. **Shotgun metagenomics** - Provides functional resolution beyond 16S, identifying pathways and genes relevant to MI pathogenesis
3. **Diagnostic potential** - Random forest models with AUC > 0.85 suggest clinical applicability of microbiome-based MI risk prediction
