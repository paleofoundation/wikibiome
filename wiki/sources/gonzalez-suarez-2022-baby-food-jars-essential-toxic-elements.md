## Key Claims

- Baby food jars from Tenerife, Spain contain both essential and toxic elements at levels that pose health risks when consumed at typical intake scenarios (130-250 g/day)
- [[manganese]] intake from baby food jars is 40 times higher than recommended values, and [[molybdenum]] is 4 times higher than maximum, posing neurotoxic risk
- Toxic element intakes from baby food jars (130 and 250 g/day) far exceed maximum values set by international institutions for [[lead]] (MOE of 112.5-450), [[nickel]] (79.4% of TDI), [[cadmium]] (31.1-124% of TWI), and [[aluminum]] (55.2-160% of TWI)
- The two proposed consumption scenarios are not safe for babies aged 6-11 months
- Vegetable jars contained the highest [[aluminum]] levels (8.22 +/- 8.97 mg/kg), likely due to plant uptake from soil
- [[environmental-metal-exposure|Lithium]] levels were high across all jar types (mean 319 +/- 48 mg/kg in meat jars)
- Mixed jars had highest concentrations of Na, Ca, Zn, and Fe; meat and vegetable jars had highest K and Mg

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: 45 samples of baby food in jars (fruits, vegetables, meat, mixed) from supermarkets in Tenerife, Spain
- **Analysis**: ICP-OES (ICAP 6300 Duo, Thermo Scientific) for 28 elements; recovery >92% using NIST SRMs
- **Essential elements**: Mixed jars highest in Na (2464 mg/kg) and Ca (921 mg/kg); vegetable jars highest in K (4213 mg/kg) and Mg (319 mg/kg); Zn highest in mixed (18.6 mg/kg); Fe highest in meat (10.5 mg/kg)
- **Toxic elements**: Al highest in vegetables (8.22 mg/kg); Cd highest in meat (0.03 mg/kg) and Pb highest in meat (0.17 mg/kg); Li high across all types (~319 mg/kg in meat); Ni ranged from 0.13-0.17 mg/kg
- **Risk assessment**: At 130 g/day for a 9-kg infant, Pb MOE ranged 114-450 across jar types; Ni at 79.4% of TDI (2.8 ug/kg bw/day); Cd at 31.1-93.5% of TWI; Al at 55.2-83.2% of TWI; B at 30-93% of UL
- **Consumer guidelines**: Fruit jars rated lowest risk; meat and vegetable jars rated high risk; mixed jars rated extreme risk for some elements
- **Statistical differences**: Significant (p<0.05) between jar types for Ca, Na, Fe, Cd, Pb, Ba, Cu, Mo, Ni, B, Zn, and Sr
