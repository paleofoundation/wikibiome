# Overview

Original research comparing gut mycobiome composition in **38 children with ASD** and **36 neurotypical (NT) children** aged 3-17 years. Used ITS sequencing for fungi and 16S rRNA V4 for bacteria. Found **significant decrease in fungal OTUs** and altered fungal/bacterial composition in ASD, with *Candida* showing a bimodal distribution linked to ASD behavioral severity.

# Key Findings

## Fungal Diversity

- Significantly **fewer observed fungal OTUs** in ASD vs NT (median 250.6 vs 514.3, p < 0.05)
- Significant difference in beta diversity between ASD and NT fungal communities (PCoA, p < 0.05)
- ASD subjects showed **lack of potentially beneficial fungi**

## Key Fungal Taxa

- Higher relative abundances of *Candida*, *Pichia*, *Penicillium*, and *Exophiala* in ASD -- though not statistically significant due to large data dispersion
- **Bimodal distribution** of *Candida* in ASD: children with high *Candida* had significantly higher **ATEC scores** (more severe ASD behavioral symptoms)
- *Candida*'s relative abundance not significantly correlated with GI scores

## Bacterial Findings

- Marginally lower observed bacterial OTUs in ASD
- Significantly **lower relative abundance of *Prevotella*** in ASD (consistent with prior studies)
- Bacterial diversity and SCFA concentrations also associated with stool form

## Fungal-Bacterial Interactions

- Some bacterial families showed differential abundance according to stool firmness
- Autism closely linked with a **distinct gut mycobiome** characterized by loss of fungal and bacterial diversity and altered composition
