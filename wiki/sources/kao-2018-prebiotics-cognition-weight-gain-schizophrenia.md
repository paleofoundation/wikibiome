# Can Prebiotics Assist in the Management of Cognition and Weight Gain in Schizophrenia?

## Key Claims

- [[schizophrenia]] is among the top 25 leading causes of disability worldwide with 10-20 year decrease in life expectancy
- Ineffective pharmacotherapy for cognitive deficits and weight gain are significant contributors to disability; interventions targeting both would be highly beneficial
- Preclinical studies show 2-4 week prebiotic supplementation (galacto-oligosaccharides, GOS) has beneficial effects on learning, memory, and prevents pro-inflammatory signals detrimental to cognitive processes
- Prebiotics influence metabolism and in obesity increase anorexigenic gut hormones (peptide tyrosine tyrosine, GLP-1, leptin) while decreasing orexigenic hormones (ghrelin)
- SCFAs produced by prebiotic fermentation act on G protein-coupled receptors (GPR41, GPR43) to modulate immune function and energy homeostasis
- Prebiotics may enhance BDNF levels and modulate NMDA receptor function relevant to the glutamatergic hypothesis of schizophrenia
- Despite compelling preclinical evidence, human clinical trials evaluating prebiotics for cognition or weight in schizophrenia are needed

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Perspective/review article (University of Oxford)
- **Key argument**: Prebiotics could simultaneously address cognitive impairment and weight gain in schizophrenia through gut microbiome modulation
- **Proposed mechanisms**: SCFA signaling, BDNF enhancement, anti-inflammatory effects, gut hormone regulation
