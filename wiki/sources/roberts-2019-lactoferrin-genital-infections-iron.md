# Key Claims

- Vaginal [[lactoferrin]] (Lf) concentrations increase significantly with genital infections: 6.6-fold higher with [[bacterial vaginosis]] (BV), 11.5-fold higher with [[Trichomonas vaginalis]], and 1.8-fold higher with vaginal discharge
- Lf concentration is positively associated with serum [[hepcidin]] (P = 0.047), serum [[ferritin]] (P = 0.018), and total body [[iron]] stores (P = 0.042), suggesting nutritional and iron status influence mucosal Lf responses
- Lf concentrations increased with higher BMI, MUAC (mid-upper-arm circumference), and sexual activity
- Women who maintained persistently high or low Lf concentrations from before through pregnancy, suggesting stable individual variation
- Iron homeostatic mechanisms controlling vaginal Lf may be influenced by nutritional status and infection

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Analysis of samples from a randomized, controlled periconceptional iron supplementation trial in Burkina Faso
- **Participants**: 712 non-pregnant women at end assessment and 303 pregnant women (at ANC1 or ANC2 visits); young nulliparous and primigravid women aged 15-24 from 30 rural villages
- **Lf measurement**: Competitive double-sandwich ELISA on vaginal eluates from weighed swabs
- **Infection assessment**: BV by Nugent scoring (7-10 positive); T. vaginalis by PCR; microbiota profiling by 16S rRNA gene (community state types CST I, III, IV)
- **Iron biomarkers**: Hemoglobin, plasma ferritin (adjusted for inflammation via CRP), sTfR, hepcidin, body iron stores, ZPP

 - Mean Lf: pregnant 806 ug/ml (n=271); non-pregnant 201 ug/ml (n=712)
 - Pregnant women had higher log Lf (difference = 0.45, 95% CI: 0.33-0.57, P < 0.0001)
 - BV prevalence: 11% at non-pregnant end assessment; 4% at ANC1
 - T. vaginalis prevalence: 7% and 13% at ANC1
 - Lf by infection: BV difference in log10 Lf = 0.82 (0.63-1.00, P < 0.001); T. vaginalis = 1.06 (0.85-1.26, P < 0.001)
- **Microbiota community state types**: CST I (Lactobacillus crispatus dominated, 40.4%), CST III (L. iners, 25.8%), CST IV (mixed/reduced Lactobacillus, 33.8%); Lf was higher in CST III and IV
- **Longitudinal findings**: Within-participant ICC for Lf = 0.57, suggesting stable individual variation; women maintained high or low Lf through pregnancy
- **Iron deficiency**: 11.3% at baseline (non-pregnant); 20.3% at end assessment; iron-deficient women had lower Lf at baseline, and women with normal vaginal flora were more likely to be iron-replete
- **Antifungal prescriptions**: Associated with higher Lf values (P < 0.001)
- **Limitations**: Vaginal Lf measurement not standardized; swab weight normalization used rather than volume; rural West African population may not generalize
