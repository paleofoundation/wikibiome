# Overview

Review of **autoimmune involvement in ASD pathogenesis**, covering the microbiome as a trigger of innate immune system inflammation in the CNS, bidirectional gut-brain axis communication, cytokine dysregulation, and autoimmune disease prevalence in ASD families. Discusses anti-inflammatory therapeutic approaches.

# Key Claims

## Microglia in ASD Pathogenesis

- Microglia are resident macrophages of CNS, critical for neuroinflammation
- Microglial activation with chronic inflammation observed in multiple ASD brain regions
- M1 (pro-inflammatory) and M2 (anti-inflammatory) phenotype switching relevant to ASD
- Synaptic pruning by microglia during development affected in ASD

## Astrocyte Dysfunction

- Astrocytes significantly increased in frontal cortex of ASD patients
- ASD-derived astrocytes had fewer branches and enhanced synapses
- Astrocyte-neuron interactions altered, affecting glutamate transport and synaptic function

## Cytokine Dysregulation

- Elevated plasma levels of **IL-1beta, IL-6, TNF-alpha, IFN-gamma** in ASD children
- Pro-inflammatory Th1/Th17 profile predominant
- TNF-alpha and IL-6 exclusively elevated in autistic children vs siblings and controls
- Lower levels of anti-inflammatory IL-10 in some studies

## Autoimmune Connections

- Higher prevalence of autoimmune disorders in first-degree family members
- Maternal hypothyroidism associated with 4x higher probability of ASD offspring
- Maternal immune activation (MIA) during pregnancy linked to ASD risk
- Anti-brain antibodies in mothers may cross placenta

## Therapeutic Approaches

- **Resveratrol** and **palmitoylethanolamide (PEA)** show promise as anti-inflammatory agents
- Both may relieve ASD manifestations by reducing neuroinflammation
