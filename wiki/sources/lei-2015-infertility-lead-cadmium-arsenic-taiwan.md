# Key Claims

- Blood [[lead]] and [[arsenic]] were significantly higher in infertile women than pregnant women (Pb: 15.7 vs 11.6 ug/L; As: 13.9 vs 11.6 ug/L; both p < 0.01)
- Blood [[cadmium]] was NOT significantly different between groups (1.48 vs 1.37 ug/L)
- Chinese herbal medicine use 1-6 times/week associated with elevated blood Pb (aOR = 2.82, 95% CI 0.98-8.09)
- Physical activity 1-2 times/week may reduce Pb accumulation (aOR = 0.37, p = 0.05)
- Blood Cd levels negatively correlated with As (rho = -0.12, p < 0.05); Pb and Cd positively correlated (rho = 0.22, p < 0.01)
- LH levels positively correlated with FSH (rho = 0.14, p < 0.05) in infertile women

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Hospital-based cross-sectional study; Taiwan Adventist Hospital, Aug 2008-March 2010
- **Participants**: 310 infertile women + 57 pregnant women (PCOS excluded)
- **Measurements**: Blood Pb, Cd, As by ICP-MS; FSH and LH by radioimmunoassay
- Infertile women: more alcohol use (27.7% vs 11.5%), more Chinese herbal medicine, less physical activity
- Chinese herbal medicines (Si-Wu-Tang, Angelicae sinensis radix, etc.) contain detectable Pb, Cd, As
- Si-Wu-Tang had highest Pb concentration (8.8-16.6x higher than other herbs)
- Multiple regression: Chinese herbal medicine use significantly predicted blood Pb (beta = 0.04, p = 0.02)
