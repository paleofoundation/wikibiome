# Key Claims

- **Dysbiosis as developmental origin** of ASD/ADHD via microbiota-gut-brain axis dysfunction
- **Psychobiotics** (selected probiotic strains) target neuropsychiatric symptoms through microbial metabolite production
- **FMT proven effective** in restoring eubiotic microbiota and improving neurobehavioral outcomes in some ASD/ADHD cohorts
- **Therapeutic mechanisms**: immune modulation, barrier restoration, metabolite production (SCFA, neurotransmitters), pathogen suppression

# Methods & Findings

Review of psychobiotic and FMT mechanisms in neurodevelopmental disorders:

**Psychobiotic mechanisms:**
- Selected strains produce neuroactive compounds (GABA, serotonin, dopamine precursors)
- Reduce lipopolysaccharide (LPS) translocation → lower systemic inflammation
- Enhance [[butyrate]] production → histone deacetylase inhibition → epigenetic remodeling
- Restore intestinal barrier integrity → reduced intestinal permeability

**Dysbiosis in ASD/ADHD:**
- Reduced microbial diversity
- Enriched pro-inflammatory taxa
- Depleted protective/metabolite-producing taxa
- Associated with core behavioral symptoms

**FMT outcomes:**
- Microbiota engraftment toward neurotypical composition
- Improvement in GI symptoms (primary outcome)
- Improvement in behavioral symptoms (secondary outcomes)
- Long-term persistence of benefits (6-12 months follow-up)

**Putative active strains:**
- [[lactobacillus]] species
- [[bifidobacterium]] species
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] (major SCFA producer)
- Other [[roseburia]] and [[eubacterium-rectale]] (SCFA producers)

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

This review focuses on psychobiotic mechanism but lacks metal perspective—critical integration points:

- **Primitive 2 (Nutritional Immunity)**: Dysbiosis reflects **failed antimicrobial defense against metal-dependent pathogens**. Psychobiotics work by restoring metal-sequestering capacity (lactoferrin production, calprotectin elevation)
- **Primitive 4 (Microbial Metal Dependencies)**: Dysbiotic taxa enrichment in ASD/ADHD may select for metal-dependent pathogens. Psychobiotics preferentially survive in **metal-restricted environments** (low [[nickel]], [[cadmium]], [[lead]])
- **Butyrate production tie to metals**: [[Faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] requires specific metal cofactors for butyrate synthase—dysbiosis = loss of this taxa = loss of epigenetic remodeling capacity. FMT restores it
- **GABA production requires B6 (pyridoxal)**: GABA-producing psychobiotics depend on B6 bioavailability—dysbiotic microbiota may fail to produce/absorb B6, creating vicious cycle
- **Oral dysbiosis to GI dysbiosis progression**: Dysbiotic oral flora (metal-selected) seed dysbiotic gut flora → loss of psychobiotic potential

**Cureva opportunity**: Psychobiotics + metal restriction (diet or chelation) could work synergistically—create metal-restricted niche where protective strains thrive.
