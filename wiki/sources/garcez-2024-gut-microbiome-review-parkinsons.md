# Gut Microbiome and Its Role in Parkinson's Disease

## Summary

This comprehensive review synthesizes the current understanding of the gut [[gut-microbiome|microbiome]] in [[parkinsons-disease]], covering microbial signatures, mechanistic pathways, and therapeutic implications. It provides an updated overview of the field as of 2024.

## Key Findings

- The PD gut microbiome signature is now well-established across multiple cohorts worldwide
- Core PD-associated changes: increased Enterobacteriaceae, *Akkermansia*, *Bifidobacterium*; decreased *Prevotella*, *Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*
- Alpha-synuclein pathology in the enteric nervous system precedes motor symptoms by years
- Gut [[dysbiosis]] promotes [[neuroinflammation]] through LPS/TLR4 signaling and reduced SCFA anti-inflammatory effects
- The vagus nerve is the primary conduit for gut-to-brain pathological spread
- Microbiome-based biomarkers show promise for early PD detection
- Therapeutic strategies ([[probiotics]], [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]], dietary interventions) are advancing to clinical trials

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This comprehensive review provides the microbiome foundation upon which the metal-microbiome axis is built. Every core PD microbial alteration has metal-handling implications: Enterobacteriaceae enrichment means more [[iron]] siderophore activity; *Akkermansia* expansion thins the mucin barrier protecting against metal absorption; *Faecalibacterium* depletion reduces [[butyrate]]-mediated [[iron]] regulation; *Prevotella* loss removes a genus that produces propionate and maintains anaerobic conditions. The review's coverage of therapeutic approaches provides the roadmap for interventions targeting the gut-metal-microbiome axis. The progression from microbiome characterization to mechanistic understanding to therapeutic intervention parallels the trajectory of the metal-microbiome framework: from identifying metal-microbial interactions, to understanding their pathological consequences, to developing metal-aware microbiome therapies.

## Limitations

- Review article synthesizing heterogeneous literature
- Some PD microbiome findings remain inconsistent across studies
- Causal versus consequential relationship between dysbiosis and PD not definitively established
- Geographic and methodological variation complicates synthesis
