# Key Claims

- **Machine learning classification** using gut microbiome time series data can predict [[postpartum-depression]] with balanced accuracy of 0.62
- **Bacterial abundance ratios** extracted from microbiome time series are statistically significant predictors of depression
- Method handles **incomplete compositional data** through imputation, dimensionality reduction, and logarithmic transformation
- Demonstrates gut microbiome collected during pregnancy as a prospective biomarker for PPD

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Binary classification of compositional microbiome time series; imputation for missing values; two case studies (infant food allergies, PPD prediction)
- **Data**: Gut microbiome data collected during pregnancy at 6-week intervals
- **Key implication**: Pregnancy microbiome data could enable early PPD risk screening before symptom onset
