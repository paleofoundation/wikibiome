# Key Claims

- The gut [[mycobiome]] plays a significant role in cardiometabolic disease (CMD) progression through fungal metabolites (enzymatic derivatives, alcohol), enhanced intestinal lipid absorption, hepatic steatosis, and systemic insulin resistance
- Dysregulated cross-kingdom interactions between fungi and bacteria disrupt intestinal barrier function, leading to endotoxemia and vascular inflammation that promotes [[atherosclerosis]]
- In obesity, elevated abundance of *Candida*, *Saccharomyces cerevisiae*, and *Malassezia restricta* is consistently reported, while *Mucor* abundance is decreased
- In type 2 diabetes, fungal biodiversity is consistently reduced with *Candida* and *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* increased across multiple studies
- In atherosclerosis/CAD, *C. albicans* is consistently elevated; *Candida*, *Thermomyces*, and *Saccharomyces* may constitute key drivers of AS progression, while *Mucor racemosus* and *Fusarium* may be protective
- In hypertension, *Malassezia* abundance is significantly associated with blood pressure regulation and positively correlated with serum immunoglobulin light chain concentrations; *Mortierella* may be protective
- In heart failure, *Candida* spp. proliferation is associated with elevated intestinal permeability, systemic inflammation, and disease severity; *Saccharomyces boulardii* supplementation failed to improve cardiac function in the GutHeart trial
