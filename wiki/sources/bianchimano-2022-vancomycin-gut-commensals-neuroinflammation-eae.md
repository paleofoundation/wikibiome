# Mining the Microbiota to Identify Gut Commensals Modulating Neuroinflammation in a Mouse Model of Multiple Sclerosis

## Summary

This study systematically characterized the gut microbiome of untreated and vancomycin-treated EAE mice over time to identify bacteria with neuroimmunomodulatory potential. Using 16S rRNA sequencing at 11 timepoints spanning pre- and post-MOG immunization, they identified 50 bacterial taxa correlating with EAE severity and demonstrated that *Anaerotruncus colihominis* ameliorates EAE.

## Key Findings

### Vancomycin Treatment and EAE
- Vancomycin treatment ameliorates EAE, and this protective effect is mediated via the microbiota
- Cohousing experiments confirmed microbiota-mediated protection (vancomycin-treated mice cohoused with control mice still showed benefit)
- Fecal transplant from vancomycin-treated mice to conventionally raised or germ-free mice reduced EAE severity

### Microbiome Changes During EAE
- Most taxa changes occurred within first 2 weeks post-EAE induction
- Increased: *Clostridium cocleatum*, *Ruminococcus flavefaciens*, *C. ruminantium*, *C. chauvoel* at 3 DPI
- Increased at 15 DPI: *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *C. scindens* (Clostridium cluster XIVa)
- Decreased at 3 DPI: *Clostridium viride* (cluster IV), *C. indolis* (cluster XIVa)
- Decreased at 15 DPI: multiple *Clostridia* cluster XIVa species, *Dorea formicigenerans*, *Lactobacillus gasseri/hominis/johnsonii/taiwanensis*

### Vancomycin Increases Treg-Inducing Bacteria
- Vancomycin-treated mice showed increased abundance of bacteria known to be strong inducers of regulatory T cells
- Members of *Clostridium* clusters XIVa and XVIII were increased in vancomycin-treated mice during presymptomatic and peak disease phases

### Anaerotruncus colihominis - A Protective Commensal
- *A. colihominis*, a butyrate producer, had a positive correlation with disease severity when reduced
- Human-derived *A. colihominis* ameliorates EAE when administered to mice
- *A. colihominis* is associated with induction of ROR-gamma-t+ regulatory T cells in mesenteric lymph nodes
- *Enterococcus faecalis* (control bacterium) did not ameliorate EAE

### 50 Bacteria Correlating with EAE Severity
- The study identified 50 bacterial taxa that correlate with EAE severity
- Several of these exist in the human gut and have been implicated in [[multiple-sclerosis]]
- This serves as a starting point to define pathogenic and beneficial host-microbiota interactions

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This study is highly relevant to the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] model:

1. **Clostridia clusters XIVa and IV** - the same taxa depleted by [[heavy-metals]] are shown here to be critical for EAE protection through Treg induction
2. **Anaerotruncus colihominis** - as a butyrate-producing Clostridiales member, it is likely metal-sensitive; its depletion by [[cadmium]] or [[lead]] could remove a protective brake on [[neuroinflammation]]
3. **Fecal transplant proof** - demonstrates that the microbiome composition causally determines disease severity, supporting the idea that metal-induced [[dysbiosis]] could drive MS pathogenesis
4. **Akkermansia muciniphila increase** during EAE mirrors findings in [[cadmium]]-exposed mice (Zhang et al. 2021) and MS patients

## Limitations

- Mouse EAE model, not human MS
- Vancomycin selectively targets Gram-positive bacteria; effects may not generalize to other causes of dysbiosis
- Single MOG peptide-induced EAE; may not capture full MS complexity
