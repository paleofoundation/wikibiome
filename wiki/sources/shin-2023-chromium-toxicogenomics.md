# Adverse Human Health Effects of Chromium by Exposure Route

## Key Claims

- [[chromium]] toxicity depends heavily on exposure route (dermal, inhalation, ingestion), with distinct molecular mechanisms and hub genes for each
- DNA damage and metastasis are common toxic mechanisms across all three Cr exposure routes
- Trivalent Cr (Cr(III)) is largely non-toxic; hexavalent Cr (Cr(VI)) is toxic and carcinogenic (IARC Group 1)
- **Dermal exposure**: causes DNA damage, immune system disorders, allergic reactions; hub genes include CXCL8, PTGS2, FOS, HMOX1, ATF3, IRS1, E2F1
- **Inhalation exposure**: causes DNA damage, cell cycle alteration, immune system disorder; hub genes include TLR4, TGM2, TNFRSF11B, BDKRB1, KIT, ZEB1; associated with lung cancer
- **Ingestion exposure**: causes DNA damage, metastasis, liver dysfunction; hub genes include VEGFA, EGFR, APP, SGK1, JUN, TLR2; associated with colorectal cancer and GI tract diseases
- Allergic reaction is a toxicity mechanism specific to dermal Cr exposure

## Methods/Findings

- **Document type**: Bioinformatics-based review using public gene expression data
- **Approach**: Molecular pathway analysis using Pathway Studio software (v12.5.0.2); Gene Ontology (GO) term analysis; gene-gene interaction networks via Cytoscape (v3.7.2)
- **Datasets used**: GEO database microarray datasets - GSE16394 (dermal fibroblasts), GSE24025/GSE36684 (bronchial epithelial BEAS-2B cells), GSE120146 (mouse duodenum)
- **Dermal findings**: Enriched GO terms for RNA modification, sterol biosynthesis, DNA replication; Cr disrupts CTCF transcription factor binding
- **Inhalation findings**: Enriched ribosome biogenesis, rRNA metabolic processes; suppressed cell-substrate adhesion and macrophage cytokine production
- **Ingestion findings**: Enriched nucleotide biosynthesis; suppressed alcohol/steroid/lipid metabolic processes indicating liver dysfunction
- **Common mechanism**: Cr-DNA adducts and metastasis identified across all routes
