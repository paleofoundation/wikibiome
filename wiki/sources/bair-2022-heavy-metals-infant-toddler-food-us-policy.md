## Key Findings

- **Inadequate U.S. oversight**: FDA has established limits only for apple juice (Pb), rice cereal (As), and infant formula (EU). No action level exists for most baby foods or toxic elements.
- **Findings from Congressional investigation** of 7 major U.S. infant-food brands (Feb 2021, Sept 2021):
  - Happy BABY: >25% of products had >100 ppb As; ~8% had >20 ppb Pb; 56% had >2 ppb Hg; 65% had >5 ppb Cd
  - Earth's Best Organic: 24 ingredients with >100 ppb As; 88 ingredients with >20 ppb Pb; 102 ingredients with >20 ppb Cd
  - Beech-Nut: 45 ingredients with >100 ppb As; 483 ingredients with >5 ppb Pb (voluntarily recalled rice cereal Feb 2021)
  - Plum Organics: 100% rice-based products >200 ppb As
- **Toxic element health impacts on children**:
  - Arsenic — #1 on ATSDR hazardous substances list; links to carcinogenic, neurological damage, lower IQ
  - Lead — #2 on list; decreased cognitive performance, behavioral problems, delayed puberty, stunted growth
  - Mercury — #3; neurodevelopmental impact, lowered IQ
  - Cadmium — #7 overall, #4 of metals; negatively affects Full Scale IQ, linked to ADHD incidence
- **WIC program rice cereal** in Oregon, Alaska, and Hawaii had inorganic As levels >100 µg/kg cf. U.K. and European Defense Fund 2018 limit for rice aimed at children (60 µg/kg) — WIC cereal exceeds EU's "stricter" limit of 100 µg/kg at 27.5% exceedance, and adults 200 µg/kg at 6.1%.

## Methods

Narrative review (PubMed 1999-April 2022). Focused on U.S. policy context for heavy metals in infant/toddler complementary foods. Evaluated Congressional Subcommittee reports and manufacturer-submitted internal standards.
