# Key Claims

- **Maternal diabetes is significantly associated with a greater risk of ASD** in the offspring
- **Pooled relative risk of 1.48** (95% CI 1.25-1.75, p<0.001) among cohort studies
- **Pooled odds ratio of 1.72** (95% CI 1.24-2.41, p=0.001) for case-control studies
- **No indication of significant heterogeneity** across studies or publication bias
- Both meta-analyses of cohort and case-control studies showed **significant associations**

# Methods & Findings

**Systematic review and meta-analysis** of 12 articles (3 cohort studies and 9 case-control studies).

- Searched 178 potentially relevant articles
- Included 12 meeting inclusion criteria
- Cohort studies: RR 1.48 (1.25-1.75)
- Case-control studies: OR 1.72 (1.24-2.41)
- No significant heterogeneity or publication bias detected
- Registered at PROSPERO as CRD42012003373

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Maternal metabolic health** as established prenatal risk factor for ASD
- **Gestational diabetes** represents a modifiable risk factor through prenatal care
- **48-72% increased ASD risk** from maternal diabetes has significant population-level implications
