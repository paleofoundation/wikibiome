# Key Claims

- Lung cancer (LC) and COPD have significantly different metallomic signatures in human serum compared to healthy controls, and these signatures can discriminate between the disease groups
- COPD patients who later developed LC (COPD-LC) show the most significantly altered metallomic profiles, suggesting progressive metal dyshomeostasis as COPD transitions to cancer
- [[nickel]] is significantly elevated in LC patients (1.60-fold, p = 0.00) and in COPD-LC patients (1.37-fold, p = 0.00), consistent with Ni as a pulmonary carcinogen that induces [[oxidative-stress]] and DNA damage
- Element ratios (Al, Mn, Ni relative to other elements) have higher AUC values in the COPD-LC group, suggesting their potential as harbingers of LC development in susceptible COPD patients

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Observational study of 191 serum samples from the CHAIN cohort (COPD History Assessment in SpaiN): 47 LC, 90 COPD (subdivided by severity), 54 healthy controls (HC), and 16 COPD-LC patients
- **Analytical method**: ICP-MS analysis of 18 elements (V, Al, As, Mn, Co, Cu, Zn, Cd, Se, W, Mo, Sb, Pb, Tl, Cr, Mg, Ni, U)

 - **LC vs HC**: Al increased 2.35-fold, As 1.35-fold, Mn 1.26-fold, Pb 1.87-fold (all p = 0.00)
 - **COPD vs HC**: Mg increased 1.38-fold, Sb 1.33-fold; As decreased 0.68-fold, Cd 0.59-fold, Tl 0.67-fold
 - **COPD-LC vs HC**: Most altered group -- Mg 1.60-fold, Ni 1.37-fold, Se 1.23-fold increased; As 0.23-fold, Cd 0.08-fold, Mn 0.41-fold, Pb 0.04-fold decreased
- **ROC analysis**: Al and Mn had AUC values close to 1 for LC vs HC comparison; As, Al, Mn, Pb, Mg, and Cr showed AUC > 0.75 as potential biomarkers for LC vs COPD-LC
- **PCA/PLS-DA**: Clear separation between HC, LC, COPD, and COPD-LC groups; Al, As, Pb, and Zn showed VIP > 1 across multiple comparisons
- **Metal correlations**: Distinct correlation patterns between disease groups -- HC group showed more positive metal-metal correlations (e.g., Zn-Se rho=0.69, Cu-Fe rho=0.65) that were disrupted in LC and COPD-LC groups, indicating dysregulated metal homeostasis
- **COPD severity**: Metallomic profiles differed only between COPD-vs (very severe) and COPD-mo (moderate), with no differences between adjacent severity grades

# Connections

- [[nickel]] elevation in LC and COPD-LC directly supports its role as a pulmonary carcinogen causing [[oxidative-stress]], oncogenic signaling pathway activation, and DNA damage in [[metal-carcinogenesis]]
- [[arsenic]] and Pb exposure associations with decreased lung function connect to environmental/occupational [[metal-exposure]] risks
- [[cadmium]] in cigarette smoke destroying lung tissue links to smoking-related COPD and subsequent cancer risk
- [[aluminum]] elevation in LC patients may relate to energy metabolism imbalances via citrate interactions and potential endocrine disruption
- [[selenium]] as a biomarker of tobacco-related diseases connects to its antioxidant role via selenoproteins
- The disrupted metal-metal correlation patterns in disease groups illustrate how [[metallomics]] reveals systemic dyshomeostasis beyond individual element changes
- COPD-to-LC transition tracked through metallomic changes provides a model for understanding progressive [[metal-carcinogenesis]] in the lung
