# Exploring the Oral-Gut Microbiota During Thyroid Cancer: Factors Affecting Thyroid Functions and Cancer Development

## Key Findings

- **Heavy metals and thyroid cancer**: [[cadmium]], [[lead]], and [[arsenic]] decrease thyroid follicle size and alter TSH levels. A meta-analysis showed increased [[copper]] and decreased [[selenium]]/[[magnesium]] in TC patients. Exposure to a combination of heavy metals rather than a single metal likely accounts for toxicity ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).
- **Oral microbiota shifts in TC**: Oral microbial changes include increased TSH and taxa diversity; dominance of [[alloprevotella]], Anaerogtlobus, and [[acinetobacter]] in salivary samples of TC patients. C. Saccharibacteria, Mobiluncus, and [[treponema]] are prevalent in thyroid nodule patients ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).
- **Gut microbiota in TC**: Analysis of 74 TC patients showed alteration related to both TC and thyroid nodules; [[neisseria|Neisseria]] and [[streptococcus|Streptococcus]] significantly higher in TC compared to healthy controls; notable decrease in Butyricimonas and [[lactobacillus]] ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).
- **Probiotics as trace element vehicles**: [[selenium]], [[zinc]], and [[copper]] are accumulated by probiotics and integrated into essential organic compounds beneficial for thyroid function. L. reuteri increased T4 count and enhanced T-regulatory cells in mice ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).
- **Environmental factors**: PCBs, BPA, PFAS, pesticides, perchlorates all disrupt thyroid function; soy isoflavones increase TSH levels; cigarette smoking reduces TSH via thiocyanate from tobacco cyanide ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

## Methods

- **Study type**: Comprehensive narrative review (published June 19, 2023; 18 pages with extensive tables and figures)
- **Scope**: Lifestyle factors (nourishment, smoking, alcohol, BMI), pollutants (chemicals, heavy metals), TC types and risk factors, oral and gut microbiota, TC treatments and microbiota interactions, probiotic potential
- **Coverage**: Includes Tables 1-2 (pollutants and their thyroid effects; gut microbiota studies in TC) and Figures 1-7 (thyroid gland anatomy, lifestyle effects, bacterial communities, TC microbiota relationships, metabolic pathways, probiotic mechanisms)

## Detailed Summary

**Lifestyle factors**: Soy isoflavones discreetly elevate TSH levels; soy negatively affects patients with subclinical hypothyroidism. Cigarette smoking reduces TSH via thiocyanate but FT4 rise and fT3 reduction are variable. Alcohol increases T3 and reduces TSH through decreased pituitary TRH receptor sensitivity. High BMI positively correlates with TSH and T3 levels ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

**Pollutants** (Table 1): PCBs interfere with thyroxine production and transport. BPA disturbs the human metabolic system and decreases neonatal TSH levels. PFAS reduce thyroid cell viability and increase TSH. Pesticides increase hypothyroidism risk and destroy thyroid axis. Perchlorates competitively inhibit normal iodine uptake ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

**TC types and epidemiology**: Papillary (70-80%), follicular (~15%), medullary (~3%), anaplastic (<2%). Papillary TC is 3x more common in women. Risk factors include age, gender, iodine deficiency, radiation exposure, genetic aspects (MEN2, familial adenomatous polyposis) ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

**Gut microbiota and TC** (Table 2): 12 studies summarized showing diverse microbiota patterns. Common findings: reduced Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes phyla, increased Proteobacteria and Actinobacteria. Loss of SCFA-producing bacteria leads to disrupted host metabolic pathways including lipid metabolism and amino acid degradation ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

**TC treatments and microbiota**: Radioactive I-131 therapy significantly reduced Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio. External radiation disrupts microbiome. TSH suppression therapy affects gut flora. Chemotherapy efficacy is modulated by gut microbial composition ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

**Probiotics**: Supplementation with B. infantis, L. acidophilus, E. faecalis, and B. cereus reduced TC complications post-thyroidectomy. Probiotics decreased oral Prevotella_9, Fusobacterium, and Haemophilus. Mannooligosaccharide prebiotic enhanced Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium growth ([[kun-2023-microbiota-thyroid-cancer]], expert-opinion).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The evidence level is classified as expert-opinion despite being called a "systematic review" in tags — this is a narrative review without meta-analytic pooling
- Some heavy metal data (Cu increased, Se/Mg decreased in TC) comes from meta-analyses cited within the review rather than original data
- The causal direction between TC and gut microbiota changes remains unclear — TC treatment effects confound observational microbiome studies

## Open Questions

- Whether oral microbiota screening could serve as a non-invasive TC screening tool
- Whether prebiotics protecting the microbiome during I-131 therapy could improve outcomes
- The specific mechanisms by which probiotics accumulate and deliver trace elements to the host
- Whether the oral-to-gut microbial transition plays a direct role in TC development or metastasis
