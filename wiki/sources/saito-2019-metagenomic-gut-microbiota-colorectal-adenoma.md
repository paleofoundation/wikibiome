# Metagenomic Analyses of the Gut Microbiota Associated with Colorectal Adenoma

## Key Claims

- *Fusobacterium varium*, rather than *F. nucleatum*, was the key species distinguishing both colorectal adenoma (CRA) and intramucosal CRC patients from healthy subjects, appearing in approximately 80% of CRA patients
- *F. varium* was statistically more abundant in patients with CRA and intramucosal CRC than in healthy subjects, while *F. nucleatum* and *F. periodonticum* were more prevalent in healthy controls
- The Shannon diversity index was significantly different between CRA and healthy subjects (P = 0.019), but not between intramucosal CRC and healthy subjects (P = 0.068)
- Intramucosal CRC microbiome was characterized by preponderance of *Fusobacterium*, *Actinobacillus*, *Peptostreptococcus*, *Parvimonas*, and *Actinomyces*, while healthy microbiome showed preponderance of *Megamonas* and *Sphingobium*
- This study demonstrates *F. varium* is at least partially involved in the pathogenesis of both CRA and intramucosal CRC, shifting attention from *F. nucleatum* to broader Fusobacterium species

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Cross-sectional metagenomic profiling of colonoscopy aspirates from CRA, intramucosal CRC, and healthy controls
- **Participants**: 81 Japanese patients (47 CRA, 24 intramucosal CRC, 10 healthy) from Jikei University Kashiwa Hospital
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA V3-V4 amplicon sequencing on Illumina MiSeq; QIIME analysis; LEfSe for differential abundance; Fusobacterium-targeted ITS region sequencing
- **Sample type**: Colonoscopy aspirates (intestinal content from rectum) rather than stool or biopsy
