# Overview

16S rRNA sequencing study of **9 ASD children and 6 healthy controls** (ages 3-12) in China, showing significant differences in gut microbiota composition at family, genus, and species levels. Functional pathway analysis via KEGG revealed alterations in nervous system, environmental information processing, and cellular processing pathways.

# Key Findings

## Compositional Differences

- **Bacteroidales** and **Selenomonadales** abundance significantly lower in ASD group
- **Ruminococcaceae** abundance higher in ASD (25.4 vs 13.6, p = 0.0285)
- **Prevotellaceae** significantly lower in ASD (5.5 vs 21.9, p = 0.0111)
- [[firmicutes]] was the most abundant phylum in all samples

## Functional Pathway Analysis

- **53 KEGG orthology** pathways differentially expressed between ASD and HC groups
- Pathways related to **Organismal Systems/Nervous System** were differentially expressed
- **Environmental Information Processing** pathway significantly lower in ASD group
- Cellular Processes pathways also altered

## Alpha and Beta Diversity

- PCA analysis clearly distinguished ASD from HC children
- Chao1 index and Shannon index reflected different microbiota diversity trends between groups
