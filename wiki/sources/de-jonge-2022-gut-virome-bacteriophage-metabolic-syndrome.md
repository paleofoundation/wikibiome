# Gut Virome Profiling Identifies a Widespread Bacteriophage Family Associated with Metabolic Syndrome

## Summary

Study characterizing gut virome changes in 196 participants with or without metabolic syndrome (MetS) using combined whole genome and virus-like particle sequencing. Identified decreased phage richness and diversity in MetS and discovered *Candidatus Heliusviridae*, a widespread gut phage lineage.

## Key Findings

### MetS Virome Alterations
- MetS gut viromes exhibited decreased richness and diversity compared to controls
- Enriched in phages infecting *Streptococcaceae* and *Bacteroidaceae*
- Depleted in phages infecting *Bifidobacteriaceae*
- 18 viral clusters (VCs) significantly associated with MetS or healthy viromes
- MetS-associated *Roseburia* VC identified; healthy control-associated *Faecalibacterium* and *Oscillibacter* VCs

### Phage-Bacteria Correlations
- Strong positive correlations between phage richness and bacterial richness (Spearman rho = 0.3, p = 5.9e-06)
- Phage richness negatively correlated with obesity, blood glucose, and triglycerides
- Bacterial evenness negatively correlated with MetS clinical parameters
- *Ruminococcaceae* and *Clostridiaceae* bacteria significantly more abundant in controls; their phages also more abundant

### Candidatus Heliusviridae
- Found in 90+% of participants; a highly widespread temperate gut phage lineage
- Nine shared genes identified across all *Ca. Heliusviridae* contigs
- Represents starting point for studying phage effects on gut bacteria and role in MetS

### *Crassvirales* Depletion
- Apparent depletion of *Crassvirales* phage in MetS bulk viromes (p = 0.005)
- May indicate decrease in *Crassvirales* infectiousness
- First link observed between this prominent human gut phage order and a disease state

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Virome-metabolic axis** - Gut phage populations directly correlate with metabolic parameters relevant to [[cardiovascular-disease]] risk
2. **Trans-kingdom dynamics** - Phage predation of beneficial bacteria (e.g., *Bifidobacteriaceae*) could exacerbate MetS-associated dysbiosis
3. **Novel phage families** - Discovery of *Ca. Heliusviridae* opens new avenues for understanding phage-mediated regulation of CVD-associated bacteria
