## Key Findings

- **Both EFA doses reduced PMS severity**: by month 3, statistically significant reduction in median PRISM score in both 1g FA group and 2g FA group, in both follicular and luteal phases, compared to baseline ([[rocha-filho-2011-essential-fatty-acids-pms-rct]], RCT, n=116)
- **2g dose superior sustained effect**: at month 6, continued improvement in 2g group; 1g group showed meaningful but smaller reduction; placebo showed significant reduction at month 3 but not month 6 ([[rocha-filho-2011-essential-fatty-acids-pms-rct]])
- **Prolactin hypothesis not supported**: no statistically significant differences in serum prolactin between active treatment and placebo groups at month 3 or month 6 — ruling out prolactin as the primary mechanism ([[rocha-filho-2011-essential-fatty-acids-pms-rct]])
- **Cholesterol unaffected**: no significant changes in total cholesterol levels — confirming safety of EFA supplementation in this population ([[rocha-filho-2011-essential-fatty-acids-pms-rct]])
- **Mechanism inference**: EFAs (gamma-linolenic acid → DGLA → PGE1) reduce prostaglandin E2–driven inflammation and modulate GABA-A receptor sensitivity; authors propose this as the mechanism for symptom reduction ([[rocha-filho-2011-essential-fatty-acids-pms-rct]])

## Methods

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. 214 women with PMS screened; 120 enrolled and randomized (1:1:1) to three groups: Group A (1g FA/day), Group B (2g FA/day), Group C (placebo mineral oil). 116 completed analysis (4 excluded: 1 hyperprolactinemia, 1 thyroid dysfunction, 2 use of antidepressants in placebo group). Inclusion: women 18–49 with regular menstrual cycles, no current hormone therapy, no psychiatric or endocrine disease. EFA formulation: each 1-gram capsule contained gamma-linolenic acid 210mg + oleic acid 175mg + linoleic acid 345mg + other PUFAs 250mg + vitamin E 20mg. Treatment: 15 capsules/blister-pack, 2 capsules/day, orally, 8–10pm, days 15–30 of cycle for 6 months. PRISM calendar for symptom tracking (25 physical and psychological items). Blood samples for prolactin and total cholesterol at baseline, month 3, and month 6. ITT analysis. ANOVA for quantitative variables; Wilcoxon signed-rank for paired samples; Mann-Whitney for independent samples. Institution: Federal University of Pernambuco, Brazil.

## Detailed Summary

**EFA composition rationale:**
Gamma-linolenic acid (GLA) is an omega-6 PUFA and precursor to dihomo-gamma-linolenic acid (DGLA) and prostaglandin E1 (PGE1). PGE1 is anti-inflammatory and reduces the effect of prolactin at target tissues — the theoretical basis for EFA supplementation in PMS. In the PMS literature, deficient GLA conversion from linoleic acid was proposed as a pathophysiological mechanism, making GLA supplementation a logical intervention.

**Clinical results:**
By month 3, both FA groups showed statistically significant reductions in PRISM scores (both follicular and luteal phases). The 2g group showed larger score reductions than the 1g group. Placebo effect was present at month 3 (typical for PMS trials) but did not persist to month 6. In contrast, both FA groups maintained significant improvement at month 6 relative to baseline. The median overall PRISM score in the luteal phase was significantly higher than the follicular phase in all groups at all timepoints, confirming that symptoms were indeed luteal-phase concentrated — validating the trial design.

**Prolactin finding:**
Elevated prolactin has been proposed as a mechanism in PMS (hyperprolactinemia increases breast tenderness, fluid retention, irritability). This trial found no significant change in prolactin levels in any group — not supporting prolactin as the primary mechanism of EFA benefit, and not raising safety concerns.

**Implications for mechanism:**
The benefit is likely through direct modulation of arachidonic acid cascade (GLA → DGLA → reduced PGE2 and leukotrienes) rather than through prolactin normalization. This anti-inflammatory mechanism has relevance to the neuroinflammation layer of PMDD pathophysiology, where luteal-phase cytokine elevation (TNF-alpha, IL-8) drives tryptophan shunting and GABA-A receptor modulation.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Single-site Brazil study — population may differ from other ethnic groups
- Placebo was mineral oil capsules (not inert, as mineral oil has mild laxative/barrier effects) — potential for unblinding
- No gut microbiome or inflammatory biomarker data (no IL-6, TNF-alpha, prostaglandin measurements) — mechanism is inferred, not demonstrated
- PRISM calendar is self-report — susceptible to expectation bias despite blinding

## Open Questions

- Does GLA supplementation directly reduce kynurenine/IDO pathway activity (the neuroinflammation mechanism)?
- Is the benefit of EFAs additive with SSRIs or other first-line PMDD treatments?
- What is the ideal treatment duration — 3 months appears threshold, 6 months superior for 2g dose?
