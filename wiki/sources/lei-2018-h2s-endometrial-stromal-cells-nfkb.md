# Key Claims

- Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) promotes the proliferation of endometrial stromal cells (ESCs) via activation of the NF-kB signaling pathway in [[endometriosis]]
- Cystathionine beta-synthase (CBS) and cystathionine gamma-lyase (CSE), the two main H2S-producing enzymes, are overexpressed in ectopic endometrium from endometriosis patients compared to normal endometrium
- Both exogenous (NaHS) and endogenous H2S (via CBS/CSE overexpression) promote human ESC proliferation in vitro
- Inhibiting endogenous H2S production in vivo significantly reduces the number and weight of endometriotic lesions in a mouse model
- H2S-induced proliferation is dependent on NF-kB signaling, as specific NF-kB inhibitors (PDTC, CAPE) reverse the pro-proliferation effect

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Combined in vitro (human ESC cultures) and in vivo (mouse allotransplantation model) experimental study
- **Tissue samples**: Ectopic endometrial tissues from 21 endometriosis patients and normal endometrial tissues from 35 control women (Shanghai First Maternity and Infant Hospital, Jan 2016 -- Jan 2017)

 - CBS and CSE expression higher in ectopic endometrium vs. normal (immunohistochemistry and immunocytochemistry)
 - NaHS (H2S donor) dose-dependently increased HESC viability, peaking at 300 umol/l (CCK-8 assay)
 - CBS and CSE overexpression plasmids promoted HESC proliferation
 - CBS inhibitor AOAA (1 mmol/l) and CSE inhibitor PAG (3, 10 mmol/l) reduced NaHS-induced proliferation

 - NaHS (300-500 umol/l) increased NF-kB p65 phosphorylation and IKKalpha/beta phosphorylation
 - PDTC (NF-kB inhibitor, pyrrolidine dithiocarbamate) dose-dependently diminished NaHS-induced proliferation
 - CAPE (caffeic acid phenethyl ester, NF-kB inhibitor) similarly reversed the pro-proliferation effect

 - Intraperitoneal allotransplantation of endometrial fragments in BALB/c mice
 - PAG (50 mg/kg) and AOAA (30 mg/kg) administered intraperitoneally on day 5
 - Both PAG and AOAA groups showed significantly reduced number and weight of ectopic lesions compared to control (p<0.05)
- **Mechanistic model**: ESCs from ectopic tissue express high CBS and CSE --> catalyze cysteine to H2S --> H2S activates NF-kB pathway --> promotes ESC survival, proliferation, and endometriosis progression; this can be reversed by CBS/CSE inhibitors (PAG, AOAA) or NF-kB pathway inhibitors (PDTC, CAPE)

# Connections

- [[nickel]] -- NF-kB is a central inflammatory pathway that is also activated by [[nickel]] exposure; Ni-induced inflammation in allergic contact mucositis may share this NF-kB-mediated mechanism with endometriosis progression
- [[oxidative-stress]] -- H2S has dual roles: anti-inflammatory at physiological concentrations but pro-inflammatory at higher concentrations; parallels the dose-dependent toxicity seen with [[nickel]] and other metals
- [[inflammation]] -- NF-kB pathway is a master regulator of inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-alpha) that are elevated in peritoneal fluid of endometriosis patients; proinflammatory cytokines in turn activate NF-kB, creating a positive feedback loop
- [[endocrine-disruption]] -- endometriosis is an estrogen-dependent disease; H2S signaling interacts with hormonal regulation of endometrial tissue
- [[metabolic-syndrome]] -- H2S homeostasis affects cardiovascular, neuronal, gastrointestinal, respiratory, immune, and endocrine systems; disrupted H2S may link metabolic and reproductive pathology
