# Islam 2022 -- Opposing Microbiome Signatures Distinguish Autoimmune Diseases and Cancers

## Key Claims

- From 82 eligible studies (37 autoimmune, 45 cancer including 27 CRC studies with 1630 cases/2131 controls), cancer and autoimmune diseases show consistent, opposite-directionality microbiome signatures
- *Fusobacterium*, *Peptostreptococcus*, and *Porphyromonas* were the most consistently increased genera in cancer cases (found in 13, 11, and 8 cancer studies respectively); *Bacteroides* showed the most prominent opposite directionality -- increased in 12 autoimmune studies and decreased in 6 cancer studies
- Six genera (*Enterococcus*, *Parabacteroides*, *[[odoribacter|Odoribacter]]*, *Paraprevotella*, *Desulfovibrio*, *[[oxalobacter|Oxalobacter]]*) showed true opposite directionality: increased in cancer but decreased in autoimmune diseases
- Sulfur-metabolism pathways were the most frequent pathway among cancer-increased genera and species, suggesting a shared metabolic mechanism
- Alpha diversity showed an increasing trend in cancer studies (25%) and opposite decreasing trend in autoimmune studies (30%), though most studies (62% cancer, 67% autoimmune) showed no significant difference

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis of studies from PubMed, Web of Science, EMBASE (2008-2020)
- **Methods**: Random-effects meta-analysis model on log fold change data; 40 genera identified with consistent trends; Table 1 summarizes 82 included studies across 27 countries
- **CRC data**: Largest cancer category with 27 studies (1630 cases, 2131 controls) from 12 countries using both 16S and shotgun approaches
- **Key implication**: Opposing immunological profiles between cancer and autoimmunity yield analogously inverted gut microbiome signatures
