## Key Findings

- **[[klebsiella-pneumoniae]] overgrowth is highly predictive of brain injury** in preterm infants, establishing a direct microbial-neurological link relevant to cerebral palsy etiology.
- **Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) cross the blood-brain barrier** and promote myelination through histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibition — a mechanism by which gut microbiota directly influence white matter development.
- **Probiotic interventions** (particularly [[lactobacillus]] and [[bifidobacterium]]) reduce white matter injury severity in animal models of prematurity.
- **[[iron]]** ecology is relevant: Klebsiella is a potent siderophore producer, and iron availability in the neonatal gut may drive its competitive dominance.
