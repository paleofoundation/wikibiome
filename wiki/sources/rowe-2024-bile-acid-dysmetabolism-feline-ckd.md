# Fecal Bile Acid Dysmetabolism and Reduced Ursodeoxycholic Acid in Feline CKD

## Key Claims

- CKD cats have significantly reduced fecal concentrations of UDCA
- Bile acid dysmetabolism characterized by less than 50% secondary bile acids in 8/28 CKD cats
- P. hiranonis combined relative abundance significantly reduced in CKD cats with bile acid dysmetabolism
- Gut-kidney axis mediated through microbial-derived secondary bile acids is relevant to [[chronic-kidney-disease]] pathophysiology

## Methods & Findings

Prospective cross-sectional study; healthy cats (n=6), CKD cats (n=28). 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing and targeted bile acid metabolomics.
