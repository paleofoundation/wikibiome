# Key Claims

- **Poor dietary quality** in ASD correlates with core autistic symptoms, GI complications, and atypical eating behaviors
- **ASD children exhibit more pronounced diet-microbiome interaction networks** compared to neurotypical peers
- **Synthetic emulsifiers** (polysorbate-80 and carrageenan) linked to fragmented microbial connectivity in neurotypical but not ASD children
- **Dietary quality is a key contributor to microbial taxonomic variation** (1.57% of variance, second only to stool consistency)
- **Firmicutes species** (F.SGB4348, Megamonas funiformis, Lacrimispora amygdalina) emerged as indicators of healthier dietary patterns

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Systematic analysis of fecal metagenomic data paired with granular dietary assessments in 818 children (462 ASD, 356 non-ASD; mean age 8.4 years; 27.3% female).

**Dietary profiling:**
- Chinese Children Healthy Dietary Index (CCDI) used for dietary quality assessment
- CCDI negatively correlated with microbial dysbiosis in ASD (rho = -0.121, P = 0.009)
- This association NOT observed in non-ASD peers (ASD-specific)
- Poor dietary patterns correlated with more severe autistic symptoms, increased medication use, GI complications, and multiple atypical eating behaviors

**Diet-microbiome interactions (MaAsLin2 analysis):**
- ASD showed stronger diet-microbe connections than non-ASD peers
- Protein intake: 28 significant microbial associations in ASD
- CCDI scores: 27 significant associations
- Bean consumption: 18 significant associations
- [[Lacrimispora amygdalina]]: specifically correlated with insufficient vitamin/mineral intake in ASD (coefficient > 0.5, qval < 0.1)
- [[Massilioclostridium coli]] and [[escherichia-coli]]: exhibited inverse correlations with dietary metrics

**Emulsifier findings:**
- Polysorbate-80 and carrageenan demonstrated stronger associations with microbial features in ASD children
- These synthetic food additives fragmented microbial connectivity in neurotypical children -- a phenomenon attenuated in ASD children
- Random forest classification: higher AUC performance in ASD vs. non-ASD for diet-microbiome predictions

**Functional pathways:**
- Peptide/nickel transport system was the main metabolic pathway involved in differential species in ASD
- Microbial metabolism in diverse environments negatively correlated with phascolarctobacterium succinatutens

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Peptide/nickel transport system**: The finding that nickel transport is the main metabolic pathway in ASD-associated differential species is a direct metallomic connection -- nickel-dependent urease and hydrogenase pathways may be selectively enriched in ASD microbiome
- **Diet-metal-microbiome triad**: Poor dietary quality limits metal cofactor supply, which in turn shapes microbial community composition and function in an ASD-specific manner
- **Emulsifier-barrier-metal axis**: Polysorbate-80 and carrageenan disrupt intestinal mucin barrier, potentially increasing metal permeability; their differential effects in ASD vs. NT suggests altered metal-barrier dynamics
- **Valine-ornithine-nickel link**: Peptide/nickel transport positively correlated with valine levels and negatively with ornithine -- connecting amino acid metabolism directly to microbial metal transport in ASD
- **Personalized nutrition**: ASD-specific diet-microbiome interactions argue for metal-aware dietary guidance tailored to individual microbiome profiles
