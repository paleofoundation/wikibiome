# Key Claims

- Neither UC nor CD is causally associated with the risk of Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, or ALS based on two-sample Mendelian randomization
- Reverse MR analysis also showed no causal effect of neurodegenerative diseases on UC or CD risk
- Previous observational associations between IBD and neurodegeneration (e.g., IBD-AD OR 2.40, IBD-PD adjusted HR 1.25-1.45) may reflect confounding, shared genetic architecture (e.g., LRRK2), or gut microbiota as a common risk factor rather than direct causation

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample bidirectional Mendelian randomization
- **Sample sizes**: UC (n=27,432 cases/20,464 controls), CD (n=20,883 cases/14,927 controls); AD (71,880 cases/383,378 controls); PD (33,674 cases/449,056 controls); ALS (20,806 cases/59,804 controls)
- **Primary analysis (IVW)**: CD on PD: OR 1.01 (0.97-1.05, p=0.54); CD on AD: OR 1.00 (0.99-1.00, p=0.26); CD on ALS: OR 0.99 (0.96-1.02, p=0.41); similar null results for UC
- **Validation analysis**: Consistent null results across all disease pairs
- **Sensitivity**: MR-Egger, weighted median all consistent; no directional pleiotropy detected
