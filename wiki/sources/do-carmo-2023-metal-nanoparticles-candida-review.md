## Summary

A comprehensive review of metal nanoparticles (silver, gold, iron, and other metals) as therapeutic strategies against Candida albicans infections. The review covers mechanisms of action, synthesis methods (including green synthesis), drug delivery applications, and bimetallic nanoparticle combinations.

## Key Findings

- Metal nanoparticles exhibit antifungal activity through: ion release, cell wall/membrane damage, ROS production, enzyme inactivation, DNA damage, mitochondrial dysfunction, and decreased ATP levels
- Silver nanoparticles (AgNPs): most extensively studied; synergistic with fluconazole against resistant C. albicans strains; restore susceptibility phenotype in FCZ-resistant strains by reducing efflux pump activity
- Gold nanoparticles (AuNPs): FDA-approved for biomedical use; chitosan-functionalized Chi-AuNPs showed 25 mm inhibition zones at 2000 ug/mL; tyrosol-functionalized Chi-TY-AuNPs inhibited biofilm formation
- Iron oxide nanoparticles (IONPs): ferumoxytol (FDA-approved for anemia) disrupts oral biofilms; AMB-IONPs showed 16-25 fold improved efficacy vs. amphotericin B alone
- Bimetallic nanoparticles: Ag-NiNPs showed potent activity at 0.19-1.56 ug/mL; Ni-Cu-Zn-IONPs caused complete yeast cell lysis; Ag/Sn-SnO2 NPs inhibited C. albicans by targeting ergosterol biosynthesis
- Nickel-containing bimetallic nanoparticles (Ni-Zn-IONPs, Ni-Cu-Zn-IONPs, Ag-NiNPs) showed notable antifungal activity through multiple mechanisms including ROS production and membrane disruption

## Relevance to Wiki

Demonstrates the antimicrobial properties of metal ions and nanoparticles against C. albicans, including nickel-containing formulations, relevant to understanding how metal availability in the host environment may influence Candida pathogenicity and how metals could be harnessed therapeutically.
