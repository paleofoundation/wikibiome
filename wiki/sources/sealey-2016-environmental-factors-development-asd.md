# Overview

Comprehensive review of **environmental factors associated with ASD development**, including pesticides, phthalates, polychlorinated biphenyls, solvents, air pollutants, fragrances, glyphosate, and heavy metals (especially aluminum). Discusses genetic overestimation, exome sequencing evidence, monozygotic twin discordance, gender bias, and the role of synthetic chemicals in neurodevelopment. Argues that environmental exposures deserve greater attention given that >1000 genetic loci explain only a minority of ASD risk.

# Key Claims

## Genetic Overestimation
- Over 1000 genetic and genomic disorders associated with ASD
- But environmental factors underappreciated
- MZ twin concordance is ~50-90%, not 100% — environment contributes significantly
- De novo mutations more common in older fathers

## Environmental Risk Factors (Table 2)
- **Heavy metals**: lead (inverse IQ relationship), methylmercury (prenatal neurotoxicity), manganese (positive association with ASD severity)
- **Air pollution**: PM2.5, PM10, NO2, diesel, ozone — OR 1.07-2.32 for ASD
- **Endocrine disruptors**: BPA, phthalates — adverse neurobehavior
- **Pesticides**: organophosphates proximity linked to ASD
- **Fragrances**: contain benzene derivatives, may be neurotoxic
- **Glyphosate**: disrupts gut microbiome, chelates minerals

## Gender Bias
- 4:1 male:female ratio in ASD
- Fetal testosterone exposure hypothesis
- Male brains more vulnerable to environmental insults

## Maternal Factors
- Maternal age, obesity, diabetes, infections during pregnancy
- SSRI use during pregnancy — emerging risk factor
- Maternal antibiotics may alter fetal microbiome

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**High relevance** — directly addresses metal and chemical exposures:

- **[[lead]]**: blood levels inversely associated with IQ; IQ decrease of 6.9 points per 2.4-30 ug/dL increase
- **[[mercury]]** (methylmercury): prenatal exposure → language, attention, memory dysfunction
- **[[aluminum]]**: discussed in context of vaccines and environmental exposure
- **Glyphosate**: chelates [[manganese]], [[zinc]], [[iron]] — disrupts mineral bioavailability and gut microbiome
- Air pollutants contain [[heavy-metals]] (diesel, lead, manganese, mercury, methylene chloride)
