# Key Findings

- **Bacteroides 2 enterotype (dysbiotic) was significantly enriched** in T2DM Emirati subjects, while the Prevotella enterotype dominated non-T2DM controls (Fisher exact p=0.004). The Bacteroides 2 enterotype has been previously re-assigned from Bacteroides 1 in Crohn's and IBD cohorts, linking T2DM to IBD-like dysbiosis in this population.
- **Fungal alpha diversity was significantly decreased** in the Bacteroides 2 enterotype compared to Bacteroides 1 and Prevotella enterotypes (Kruskal-Wallis p=0.012). Three fungal genera differed between T2DM and controls: **Malassezia furfur** and unclassified **Davidiella** were increased in T2DM, while unclassified **Basidiomycota** was decreased.
- **Functional profiling of 16S data revealed T2DM enrichment in amino acid degradation modules** (isoleucine, proline, valine, cysteine, glutamine, aminobutyrate) and **LPS/lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis** modules (M00064 ADP-L-glycero-D-manno-heptose biosynthesis, M00307 pyruvate dehydrogenase complex), while carbohydrate degradation (arabinoxylan, pectin, melibiose) was depleted — a signature consistent with metabolic endotoxemia.

# Methods

16S rRNA and ITS2 gene-based microbial and fungal profiling of 50 stool samples from 25 T2DM and 25 non-T2DM age/BMI/diet-matched Emirati adults. All T2DM subjects were on DPP4 inhibitors and metformin. Enterotype clustering via Dirichlet Multinomial Mixture (DMM). Functional prediction via PICRUSt and KEGG modules. Linear regression controlled for age, BMI, diet, gender.
