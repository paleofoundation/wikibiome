# Key Claims

- **Gut fungal diversity and composition differ significantly** between T2DM, T2DM with diabetic foot (DF), and healthy controls
- **Ascomycota and Basidiomycota significantly altered** in both T2DM and T2DM-DF
- **Candida abundance specifically seen only** in T2DM-DF patients, not in T2DM alone or healthy controls
- **11 fungi identified as potential biomarkers** for distinguishing T2DM from healthy controls
- **KEGg enzyme expression analysis** demonstrated more pronounced fungal function alteration in T2DM-DF

# Methods & Findings

Cross-sectional study: 33 T2DM, 32 T2DM-DF, 32 healthy controls:

- ITS2 sequencing and Illumina 18s rRNA for fungal analysis
- Random forest analysis ranked top 15 significant fungi per group
- ROC analysis demonstrated enhanced accuracy with fungal biomarkers

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc deficiency is a known risk factor for diabetic foot wounds -- may simultaneously drive gut mycobiome [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[iron]]**: Candida species are iron-dependent pathogens; iron overload in diabetic foot may promote systemic Candida expansion
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent antifungal immune defense may be compromised in diabetic foot patients
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Fungal-bacterial interactions in the gut are mediated by metal competition
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Mycobiome [[dysbiosis]] represents a distinct layer of metal-dependent microbial imbalance in diabetes
