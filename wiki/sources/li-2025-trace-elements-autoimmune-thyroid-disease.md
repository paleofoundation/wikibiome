# Recent advances of trace elements in autoimmune thyroid disease

## Key Claims

- Imbalances in trace elements are pivotal environmental triggers for autoimmune thyroid disease (AITD), affecting 1-5% of the global population with a 5-10-fold higher incidence in females
- [[selenium]] plays a multifaceted role through selenoproteins (GPx, TrxR, DIO) that protect thyroid from [[oxidative-stress]], regulate T4-to-T3 conversion, and modulate Th1/Th2/Th17/Treg balance
- [[iodine]] has a complex U-shaped dose-response relationship with AITD; both deficiency and excess increase risk through distinct mechanisms including ROS generation and NIS expression inhibition
- [[zinc]] serves as cofactor for >300 metalloenzymes, mediates T lymphocyte differentiation, and modulates thyroid hormone receptor activity; low zinc prevalence is 49.1% in hypothyroid patients (OR 5.926)
- [[iron]] deficiency is associated with higher TSH (2.31 vs 1.75 mIU/L) and lower free T4 (10.7 vs 13.3 pmol/L) in a meta-analysis of 47 studies with 53,152 pregnant women
- [[copper]] and selenium are both positively associated with FT4 levels; copper serves as key cofactor for superoxide dismutase (SOD)
- Vitamin D deficiency prevalence is significantly higher in AITD patients (72% vs 30.6% in healthy controls)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review (published November 2025)
- **Selenium and AITD** (Table 1 summarizing clinical studies):
 - Se levels significantly lower in HT (104.36 ug/l) and GD (97.68 ug/l) vs controls (122.63 ug/l) (p<0.001)
 - Combined myo-inositol + Se supplementation reduced TSH, TPOAb, and TgAb after 6 months
 - Se monotherapy (83 ug/day) normalized TSH in 17.2% of subjects vs 31.3% controls
 - Selenium modulates Th1/Th2 cytokine balance, increases regulatory T cells (Tregs), and reduces Foxp3 deficiency
- **Iodine mechanisms**: Excess iodine directly inhibits TPO activity and NIS expression, induces ROS in thyroid cells, promotes Th17 cell proliferation and alters Th1/Th2 balance
- **Zinc and AITD**: Zinc mediates IL-1, IL-6, TNF-alpha synthesis; thyroid autoantibodies and zinc were significantly positively correlated in the AITD group
- **Iron-thyroid connection**: Iron is essential for TPO activity; dual-fortified salt (iodine + iron) improved both iron and iodine status without inhibiting thyroid function
- **Vitamin D**: 1,25-(OH)2D3 blocks [[nf-kappa-b]] activation, downregulates IL-8 and IL-12, reduces T cell numbers and activity in thyroid tissue
