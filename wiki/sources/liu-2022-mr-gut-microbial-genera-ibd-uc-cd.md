# Key Claims

- Six bacterial genera are causally associated with IBD risk, with specificity between UC and CD subtypes
- Eubacterium ventriosum is protective against both IBD (OR: 0.68, P=1.00e-4) and UC (OR: 0.68, P=0.008), while Coprococcus 2 increases IBD risk (OR: 1.21, P=0.022) and UC risk (OR: 1.32, P=0.007)
- CD-specific causal genera: Lachnospiraceae UCG001 (protective, OR: 0.81, P=0.023) and Oxalobacter (risk, OR: 1.23, P=0.002) with Ruminococcaceae UCG014 (risk, OR: 1.23, P=0.005)
- No reverse causal effects of IBD, UC, or CD on these six bacterial genera, confirming forward causality

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample MR using largest available GWAS databases; 196 bacterial traits tested against IBD, UC, and CD
- **Sample sizes**: IBD (12,882 cases, 21,770 controls); UC (6,968 cases, 20,464 controls); CD (5,956 cases, 14,927 controls)
- **24 initial associations** narrowed to 6 stable genera after sensitivity analyses (IVW, weighted median, MR-Egger, MR-PRESSO)
- **Disease-specific taxa**: UC-specific (Eubacterium ventriosum protective, Coprococcus 2 risk); CD-specific (Lachnospiraceae UCG001 protective, Oxalobacter risk); shared IBD (Enterorhabdus protective, Ruminococcaceae UCG014 risk)
- **F-statistics**: All >10 (range 21.63-144.84), indicating strong instruments

# Connections

- Reveals disease-specificity in the causal microbiome-IBD relationship -- different genera drive UC vs CD
- Lachnospiraceae UCG001 protection in CD connects to SCFA-producing capacity and [[dysbiosis]] patterns
- Oxalobacter association with CD risk relevant to hyperoxaluria frequently observed in IBD
- Coprococcus as a butyrate producer paradoxically increasing UC risk -- may relate to strain-level variation
- Complements [[Knights et al. 2014|knights-2014-host-genetics-microbiome-ibd]] on genotype-microbiome specificity
