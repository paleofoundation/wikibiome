# Key Claims

- This first RCT of FMT in CD found that the primary endpoint (donor microbiota engraftment at week 6, Sorensen index >0.6) was not reached in any patient
- Steroid-free clinical remission rates were similar: 44.4% (FMT) vs 33.3% (sham) at week 10, and 87.5% vs 50.0% at week 24
- Crohn's Disease Endoscopic Index of Severity (CDEIS) decreased 6 weeks after FMT (p=0.03) but not after sham
- CRP increased after sham transplantation (p=0.008) but not after FMT (p=0.5)
- Higher colonization by donor microbiota was associated with maintenance of remission, despite not meeting the primary engraftment threshold

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Single-blind, sham-controlled, randomized pilot trial (NCT02097797)
- **Participants**: 17 adults with colonic or ileocolonic CD; 8 FMT, 9 sham
- **Protocol**: Patients enrolled during flare received corticosteroids; once in remission, randomized to FMT or sham via colonoscopy; steroids tapered; follow-up 24 weeks
- **FMT preparation**: Fresh stool from healthy donors, 50-100g resuspended in 250-350ml sterile saline
- **Safety**: No safety signal identified; no serious adverse events attributed to FMT

# Connections

- FMT addresses [[dysbiosis]] directly by transplanting healthy donor microbiota in [[crohns-disease]]

- Endoscopic improvement (CDEIS) suggests FMT reduces mucosal [[inflammation]]
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] restoration through FMT may be a key mechanism for remission maintenance
