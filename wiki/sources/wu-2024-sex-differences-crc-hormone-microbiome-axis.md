# Wu 2024 -- Sex Differences in CRC: Sex Hormone-Gut Microbiome Axis

## Key Claims

- Men exhibit 33% higher average annual CRC incidence rate and 43% higher overall mortality rate compared to women (2015-2019 data), with women having slightly higher five-year relative survival
- The 'microgenderome' concept underscores the interactions between sex hormones and gut microbiota; women exhibit higher diversity and richness of gut microbiota than men, potentially underlying sex-based CRC risk differences
- Table 1 comprehensively summarizes sex hormone-based differences in CRC: estradiol (E2) inversely associated with CRC risk in postmenopausal women; E2 inhibits CRC via DNA mismatch repair enhancement, miRNA regulation, and KCNQ1 inhibition of Wnt/beta-catenin pathway
- Free testosterone levels negatively associated with CRC incidence and mortality in both men and women; testosterone enhances AOM/DSS-induced CRC development; androgen deprivation therapy for prostate cancer increases CRC risk
- ERbeta expression is reduced in colorectal precancerous stages and plays a key role in inhibiting CRC development; ERbeta knockout significantly induces TNF-alpha expression and NF-kB inflammatory signals
