# Key Claims

- **Bidirectional relationship** between T2D and periodontitis mediated through microbiome and viral interactions
- **Viruses including HBV, HCV, HSV-1, Coronavirus, HCMV, EBV, HIV** and phageome play important roles
- **Oral microbiome modulates innate and adaptive immune systems** during periodontitis-T2D interaction
- **Microecological-epithelial interaction**, soft tissue degradation, bone coupling disorder, and immune regulation are closely related

# Methods & Findings

Review analyzing changes in microbiome and bacterial metabolites in periodontitis with/without diabetes:

- Inflammatory factors/receptors, oxidative stress, microRNA involved in bidirectional relationship
- AGE/RAGE and RANK/RANKL pathways discussed for diabetes effects on periodontitis
- In-depth analysis of mechanism needed for therapeutic remission approaches

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc-dependent matrix metalloproteinases drive periodontal tissue destruction, linking oral [[dysbiosis]] to systemic effects
- **[[iron]]**: Oral pathogens (Porphyromonas gingivalis) require iron -- oral-gut [[dysbiosis]] axis is iron-dependent
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent glutathione peroxidase protects oral epithelium from viral-induced oxidative damage
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Oral-gut microbiome axis transmits metal-dependent [[dysbiosis]] from mouth to gut
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Bidirectional immune dysregulation reflects shared metal-dependent immune pathways
