# Innovative therapeutic strategies in the treatment of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD): A review of progress and perspectives

## Key Claims

- Potassium-competitive acid blockers (P-CABs) offer rapid and sustained acid suppression regardless of food intake, with advantages over PPIs including immediate effect without requiring activation by acid
- Electrical stimulation of the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) showed promising results: median GERD-HRQL score decreased from initial 23.5 to 0 points, with 76% of patients able to stop PPIs altogether
- The "acid pocket" concept describes a postprandial phenomenon where hydrochloric acid, proteolytic enzymes, lipases, and pepsin accumulate in the lower esophagus, exacerbating GERD
- Vonoprazan (tegoprazan) has been successfully used in GERD therapies in Japan; P-CABs show effect only after repeated use but provide rapid sustained efficacy
- Bile acid binders and NF-kB activation agents represent emerging therapeutic categories beyond acid suppression
- Studies indicate that weakness of the LES accounts for 80% of affected individuals and significantly contributes to GERD severity
