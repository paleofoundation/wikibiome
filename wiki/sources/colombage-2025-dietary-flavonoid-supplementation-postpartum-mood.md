# Key Claims

- **Dietary flavonoid supplementation** (2-week intervention) reduced [[postpartum-depression]] symptoms in mothers 0-6 months postpartum, though fathers did not benefit
- **Earlier postpartum intervention** (within 4 days of delivery for 30 days) improved executive function and reduced subjective cognitive complaints but did not significantly change mood
- **Acute anthocyanin-rich wild blueberry** supplementation improved verbal and working memory in both parents without additional mood benefits
- Timing of supplementation influences effectiveness; postpartum period represents a particularly vulnerable window

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: PhD thesis comprising multiple intervention studies in postpartum parents
- **Setting**: University of Reading, UK
- **Key implication**: Flavonoids may serve as accessible, cost-effective adjunctive interventions for postpartum cognitive and mood symptoms
