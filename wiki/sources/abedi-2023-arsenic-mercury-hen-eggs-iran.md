## Key Findings

- **Mean toxic-metal levels in Iranian hen eggs** (n=84): As 0.79 µg/kg (winter 0.52, summer 1.07); Hg 0.18 µg/kg (winter 0.26, summer 0.10) — Hg higher in winter (p<0.001), As no seasonal difference.
- **Estimated daily intake (EDI)** from mean adult consumption (25.4 g eggs/day, body weight 70 kg): 0.29 µg As/day, 0.06 µg Hg/day.
- **ILCR (Incremental Lifetime Cancer Risk) for As = 4.35×10^-4**, exceeding USEPA's 10^-4 concern threshold. Exceeds the stricter 10^-6 safe-exposure threshold by 2 orders of magnitude.
- **THQ values <1** for both As and Hg — no non-carcinogenic risk under current consumption patterns.
- **Source traceable to poultry feed** (contaminated with heavy metals from water, crops, industrial/agricultural waste) — chicken eggs act as sentinel indicators of environmental contamination in feed supply chains.
- Iranian eggs had lower As than Bangladesh (30 µg/kg), Turkey (2.96), Belgium (2), France (8), UK (1) reports, but As still carried cancer risk.

## Methods

84 eggs from 21 top Iranian brands (Tehran local supermarkets). ICP-MS (ULTIMA2, 6100 DRC-e Perkin Elmer). Heat-block acid digestion (HNO3 + H2O2) at 150°C for 4h per FDA Elemental Analysis Manual. LOD 0.00033 µg/kg. Monte Carlo Simulation (10,000 iterations, Crystal Ball) for probabilistic risk assessment at 95th percentile.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Results are total As and total Hg — not speciated (inorganic vs organic).
- Monte Carlo simulations may over/underestimate depending on consumption assumptions.

## Open Questions

- Whether egg-associated As is dominantly inorganic or organic (major carcinogenicity difference).
- Feed-source contribution breakdown (water, crops, antibiotics, marine feed).
