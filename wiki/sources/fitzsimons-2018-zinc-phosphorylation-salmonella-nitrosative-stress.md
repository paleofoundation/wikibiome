# Zinc-Dependent Phosphorylation Powers Salmonella Under Nitrosative Stress

## Key Findings

- **Zinc as survival cofactor inside macrophages**: When host macrophages produce nitric oxide (NO) via iNOS to kill engulfed *Salmonella*, the pathogen's electron transport chain is inhibited. *Salmonella* survives by switching to **zinc-dependent substrate-level phosphorylation** for ATP generation [[fitzsimons-2018-zinc-phosphorylation-salmonella-nitrosative-stress]], in-vitro.
- **Zinc as Achilles' heel**: This zinc dependency creates a therapeutic vulnerability — restricting zinc access inside the phagosome (via [[calprotectin]] or zinc chelation) would prevent the ATP-generating backup pathway, making the host's NO attack lethal.
- **Metal-dependent metabolic flexibility**: Demonstrates that pathogens use metals not just for enzymes but for **emergency energy metabolism** — zinc enables survival under immune assault.
