# Overview

Cross-sectional study comparing gut microbiota of **36 ASD children with GI symptoms** and **40 typically developing (TD) children** aged 3-6 years using 16S rRNA gene sequencing. Constructed a **Support Vector Machine (SVM) classification model** achieving AUC = 0.88 to discriminate ASD+GI from TD individuals.

# Key Findings

## Microbial Diversity

- ASD group showed **decreased alpha diversity** (both observed richness and Shannon index, p = 0.018 and p = 0.0058)
- Beta diversity clearly separated ASD and TD on PCoA plot (Bray-Curtis R-squared = 0.025, p = 0.01; UniFrac R-squared = 0.028, p = 0.005)

## Key Taxonomic Changes

- **Higher in ASD**: Proteobacteria (phylum level); *Enterococcus*, *Sellimonas*, and *Eggerthella lenta* (genus/species level)
- **Higher in TD**: *Faecalibacterium* and *Coprococcus* at genus level; *F. prausnitzii*, *B. bifidum*, *Coprococcus comes*, and *Coprococcus catus* at species level
- ASD co-abundance network showed harmful bacteria (*Klebsiella*, *Enterococcus*, *Clostridium*) clustered together; TD network had more complex connections among beneficial bacteria

## Enterotyping

- Two genus-level enterotypes identified: Cluster 1 (higher *Coprococcus*, *Ruminococcus*, *Faecalibacterium*) and Cluster 2
- Enterotypes highly correlated with sample group (Chi-square p = 0.0002): most TD in Cluster 1, most ASD in Cluster 2

## Functional Analysis

- Microbial functional analysis revealed abnormalities in: **SCFA synthesis/degradation**, **neurotoxin-related p-cresol degradation**
- Both pathways closely associated with ASD-related behaviors in animal models

## Clinical Correlations

- Cesarean section and problems during pregnancy associated with gut microbiome composition (p = 0.05 and 0.03)
- Fathers' age at birth of ASD children was significantly higher
- Picky eating significantly more common in ASD (p = 4.28e-07)
- High salt intake and high protein diet differed significantly between groups

## SVM Classifier

- Classification model robustly discriminated ASD+GI from TD (validation AUC = 0.88)
- Supports gut microbiota as potential biomarker for early ASD identification
