# Key Claims

- **Altered gut microbiota** observed frequently in autistic patients may trigger leaky gut
- **Bacterial metabolites** (SCFAs, LPS, beta-cresol) and proteins (calprotectin, zonulin, lysozyme) serve as biomarkers for leaky gut detection in ASD
- **Disrupted gut-blood-brain barriers** can explain leakage of bacterial metabolites
- **Probiotics** may represent a non-invasive tool to reestablish healthy gut microbiota
- **Diagnostic and therapeutic value** of intestinal proteins and bacterial-derived compounds as new ASD biomarkers
