# Yang 2025 — PMDD as Predisposing Factor for Alzheimer's Disease (Review)

## Key Findings

- **Five shared mechanisms** between PMDD and AD: (1) dynamic E2 fluctuations, (2) ALLO dysregulation of GABA_A receptors, (3) GABAergic system dysfunction, (4) serotonergic (5-HT) disruption, (5) APOE ε4 allele sex-specific vulnerability ([[yang-2025-pmdd-alzheimers-predisposing-review]], review, 168 studies).
- **PMDD may be a biological precursor to AD** — the repeated neuroinflammatory and neurotransmitter insults during menstrual cycling could accumulate toward neurodegeneration ([[yang-2025-pmdd-alzheimers-predisposing-review]]).
- **ALLO bimodal effect**: both high and low ALLO concentrations trigger PMDD-like symptoms via GABA_A receptor sensitivity paradox — inverted U-curve ([[yang-2025-pmdd-alzheimers-predisposing-review]]).
- APOE ε4 carriers show sex-specific AD vulnerability that may interact with reproductive hormone cycling ([[yang-2025-pmdd-alzheimers-predisposing-review]]).
- Serotonergic dysfunction in PMDD (atypical neurotransmission, reduced transporter/receptor density, elevated 5-HT in luteal phase) parallels early AD serotonergic degeneration ([[yang-2025-pmdd-alzheimers-predisposing-review]]).

## Methods

Narrative review synthesizing 168 studies from Google Scholar, PubMed, Web of Science. PRISMA flow diagram: 753 records screened, 245 assessed, 168 included. Focuses on integrating five mechanistic pathways across PMDD and AD.
