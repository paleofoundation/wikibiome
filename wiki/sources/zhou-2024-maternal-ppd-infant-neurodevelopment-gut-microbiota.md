# Key Claims

- **Infants born to mothers with PPD** had lower neurodevelopmental scores at 6 months and altered gut microbiota at 42 days postpartum
- **Increased alpha diversity** in PPD-exposed infants with abundant *Veillonella* and *Finegoldia*, depleted *Bifidobacterium*, *Dialister*, *Cronobacter*, and *Megasphaera*
- **Metabolite alterations** in alanine, aspartate, and glutamate pathways mediated the relationship between maternal [[postpartum-depression]] and infant problem-solving skills
- **N-Acetyl-L-aspartic acid and L-Aspartic acid** levels served as mediators between PPD symptoms and neurodevelopment

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Analysis of 101 mother-infant dyads; infant stool at 42 days analyzed by 16S rRNA sequencing and LC-MS metabolomics; neurodevelopment assessed at 6 months using ASQ-3
- **Key implication**: Maternal PPD has transgenerational effects on infant gut microbiome and neurodevelopment via metabolite pathways
