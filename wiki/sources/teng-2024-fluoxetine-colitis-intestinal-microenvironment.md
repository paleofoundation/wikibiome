# Teng 2024 -- Fluoxetine Alleviates Colitis by Reshaping Intestinal Microenvironment

## Key Claims

- Fluoxetine was the most effective antidepressant for alleviating DSS-induced colitis among three tested (fluoxetine, fluvoxamine, venlafaxine), significantly reducing body weight loss, DAI scores, and colon shortening
- Single-cell RNA sequencing revealed fluoxetine affected distribution of different cell clusters and cell-cell communication; enterocytes, goblet cells, and stem cells became dominating cells under fluoxetine treatment
- Pseudotime analysis showed fluoxetine promoted M1 macrophage differentiation into M2 macrophages, shifting from pro-inflammatory to anti-inflammatory phenotype
- Fluoxetine restored intestinal barrier function by upregulating ZO-1 and Occludin expression and increasing MUC2-positive goblet cells, while reducing TNF-alpha and IL-1beta inflammatory markers
- Anti-inflammatory effects confirmed in vitro: fluoxetine exhibited anti-inflammatory effects on intestinal mucosa via remodeling of intestinal cells and macrophages

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: C57BL/6 mice DSS-induced colitis model with antidepressant treatment (10 mg/kg/day IP injection for 3 weeks); 10x Genomics single-cell RNA sequencing
- **Methods**: Seurat/UMAP visualization, Cell Chat for cell-cell communication, Monocle for pseudotime analysis, RT-qPCR, western blot, immunofluorescence
- **Key finding**: Fluoxetine is a promising therapeutic drug for IBD and psychiatric comorbidities through direct intestinal microenvironment remodeling
