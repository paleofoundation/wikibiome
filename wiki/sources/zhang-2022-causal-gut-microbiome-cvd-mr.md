# Causal Associations Between Gut Microbiome and Cardiovascular Disease: A Mendelian Randomization Study

## Summary

Two-sample Mendelian randomization (MR) study investigating causal relationships between genetically predicted gut microbiome composition and 9 CVDs (CAD, MI, AF, heart failure, stroke subtypes). Used SNPs from 18,473 individuals across 122,110 loci as instrumental variables.

## Key Findings

### Causal Effects at Locus-Wide Significance
- Genus *Oxalobacter* positively associated with CAD risk (OR=1.06, 95% CI: 1.03-1.10, P=1.67x10^-4)
- Family *Clostridiaceae_1* associated with decreased stroke risk (OR=0.83, 95% CI: 0.75-0.93, P=7.76x10^-4) and ischemic stroke risk (OR=0.82, 95% CI: 0.74-0.92, P=4.15x10^-4)
- No causal relationship found for other gut microbiome components at genome-wide significance

### Blood Pressure Mediation
- *Oxalobacter*-CAD association attenuated to null after adjusting for SBP, DBP, or both
- *Clostridiaceae_1*-stroke association also weakened after BP adjustment but remained significant when adjusting for both SBP and DBP (OR=1.07, P=0.045)
- Suggests gut microbiome may affect CVD risk partly through blood pressure

### Sensitivity Analyses
- No horizontal pleiotropy detected (MR-Egger intercept P>0.05 for all)
- No significant heterogeneity
- MR-PRESSO showed no outliers
- MR Steiger test confirmed no reverse causality

### Mechanistic Discussion
- *Oxalobacter formigenes* degrades oxalate; may affect calcium nephrolithiasis and gut mucosa
- *Clostridiaceae* includes *Clostridium butyricum* which has neuroprotective effects via butyric acid
- SCFA-producing *Clostridiaceae* may protect against stroke through butyrate-mediated mechanisms

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Causal evidence for microbiome-CVD link** - MR provides stronger evidence than observational studies that gut microbiome changes causally affect CVD risk
2. **Blood pressure mediation** - The BP-mediated effect suggests that [[lead]] and [[cadmium]]-induced [[dysbiosis]] may drive hypertension, which then increases CVD risk
3. **SCFA-producing Clostridiaceae protection** - Metal exposure that depletes *Clostridiaceae* could remove this protective causal effect against stroke
4. **[[gut-metal-microbiome]] implications** - Metals that alter the abundance of causally implicated taxa would have downstream effects on CVD that go beyond direct toxicity
