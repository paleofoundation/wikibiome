## Key Findings

- **Copper and zinc used as livestock growth promoters** co-select for antibiotic resistance in foodborne pathogens including *Salmonella*, *E. coli*, and *Enterococcus* ([[wales-2015-co-selection-resistance-foodborne-pathogens]], expert-opinion).
- **Cross-resistance via shared efflux pumps**: Biocide and metal resistance genes share export mechanisms with antibiotic resistance determinants.
- **Fitness cost caveat**: Some co-selected resistance adaptations carry metabolic costs and may revert in the absence of selection pressure — relevant to intervention timing.
