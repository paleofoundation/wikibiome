# Key Claims

- **Firmicutes bacteria prevailed (>77%)** in all groups at phylum level
- **Significant differences at L2 (phylum) and L6 (genus)** demonstrated in bacterial profiles, particularly in T2DM group
- **Negative correlation observed** between several genera of bacteria and HbA1c percentage in T2DM
- **Positive correlation** between Bifidobacterium genus and HDL cholesterol levels in both T1DM and T2DM
- **Next-generation sequencing** allows rapid identification of all bacterial DNA present

# Methods & Findings

Cross-sectional study with 3 groups: healthy (n=23), T1DM (n=22), T2DM (n=23):

- DNA isolated from fecal samples examined on MiSeq platform
- Bacterial differences more pronounced in T2DM than T1DM
- Personalized therapy based on microbiota modification proposed as new glycemia control approach

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Bifidobacterium-HDL correlation may reflect zinc-dependent lipid metabolism pathways
- **[[iron]]**: Firmicutes dominance may reflect iron-mediated competitive advantage over Bacteroidetes
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Genus-level differences between T1DM and T2DM may reflect distinct metal environments
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Microbiota modification for glycemia control must account for metal cofactor availability
- **[[probiotics]]**: Bifidobacterium supplementation may improve both glycemic and lipid parameters
