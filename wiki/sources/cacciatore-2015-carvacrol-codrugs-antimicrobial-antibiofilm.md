## Key Findings

- **Carvacrol mechanism**: 2-methyl-5-[1-methylethyl]phenol destabilizes bacterial membranes, dissipates pH gradients, and perturbs lipid fractions of bacterial cytoplasmic membranes. The hydroxyl group and delocalized electron system are structurally required.
- **Codrug strategy**: direct-coupled codrugs link carvacrol to N-acetyl-cysteine (NAC, codrug 1), alkyl cysteine analogs (2-4, 9), methionine (5), selenomethionine (6), R-α-lipoic acid (7), cyclic cysteine derivatives (8, 10) via an ester bond hydrolyzable in vivo.
- **Codrug 4 (N-Ac-Cys(Allyl)-CAR)** was most active — MIC/MBC = 2.5 mg/mL for most bacterial strains; 5 mg/mL and 10 mg/mL against P. aeruginosa ATCC 9027. Demonstrated antibiofilm activity against S. epidermidis mature biofilm.
- **Multi-target action**: NAC breaks disulfide bonds in biofilm EPS; allyl-cysteine (garlic) has antibacterial/antifungal/antiviral/antiprotozoal activity; methionine neutralizes C. albicans gliotoxin.
- All 10 codrugs were active against C. albicans ATCC 10231 (antifungal).

## Methods

Synthesis: acetic anhydride acetylation of cysteine derivatives, then DCC/DMAP coupling to carvacrol. Structures confirmed by 1H and 13C NMR, LC-MS. Broth microdilution MIC/MBC against S. aureus, S. epidermidis (Gram+), E. coli, P. aeruginosa (Gram-), C. albicans. Hemolysis assay. TEM of morphological changes in S. aureus, E. coli, C. albicans post-treatment.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Only in vitro evidence; no animal or clinical data.
- Codrug pharmacokinetics (ester cleavage rate) not characterized in vivo.

## Open Questions

- In vivo activity of codrug 4 in biofilm-dependent infections.
- Whether codrug carvacrol retains activity in host GI environment.
