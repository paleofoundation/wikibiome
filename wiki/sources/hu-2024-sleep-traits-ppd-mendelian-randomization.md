# Key Claims

- **Sleep apnea syndrome** (OR: 1.122), **sleeplessness/insomnia** (OR: 1.465), and **frequency of tiredness/lethargy** in last 2 weeks (OR: 1.725) genetically predicted increased risk of [[postpartum-depression]]
- **Reverse MR** showed PPD caused sleeplessness/insomnia and frequency of tiredness/lethargy (bidirectional relationship)
- Six other sleep traits showed no significant association with PPD
- No heterogeneity or horizontal pleiotropy detected

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Bidirectional two-sample MR using IVW, weighted median, weighted mode, MR-Egger methods; IEU Open GWAS and FinnGen data
- **Nine sleep traits examined**: Sleep apnea, insomnia, sleep duration, chronotype, tiredness frequency, daytime dozing, napping, snoring, ease of getting up

# Connections

- Sleep disruption in postpartum period exacerbates [[postpartum-depression]] risk
- Sleep-microbiome interactions relevant to [[dysbiosis]] (sleep fragmentation alters gut microbiota)
- [[gut-brain-axis]] regulation of sleep via GABA and serotonin production
- Sleep deprivation increases [[inflammation]] and oxidative stress
- [[magnesium]] and [[zinc]] deficiency associated with sleep disturbances
- [[developmental-metal-vulnerability]] through maternal sleep deprivation affecting infant care
