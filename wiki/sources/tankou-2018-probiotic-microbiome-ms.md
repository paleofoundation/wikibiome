## Key Claims

- Probiotic supplementation (VSL#3) modulates both the gut microbiome composition and peripheral immune responses in MS patients
- Probiotic treatment was associated with changes in the abundance of specific gut bacterial taxa
- Immune modulation included effects on regulatory T cell (Treg) populations and anti-inflammatory pathways
- The study provides human clinical evidence supporting the [[gut-brain-axis]] hypothesis in [[multiple-sclerosis]]

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Clinical trial in MS patients using the multi-strain probiotic VSL#3
- **Approach**: Combined microbiome sequencing, metabolomics, and immune profiling
- **Key Outcomes**: Changes in gut microbiome composition, immune cell populations, and metabolic profiles following probiotic supplementation
- This was one of the first studies to comprehensively investigate probiotic effects on both the microbiome and immunity specifically in MS patients
