# Key Findings

- **Magnesium deprivation caused a 2-fold increase in beta-glucan exposure** on the *C. albicans* cell surface, effectively unmasking the fungus for immune recognition ([[hans-2022-magnesium-candida-immune-evasion]], in-vitro).
- **Mg deprivation simultaneously reduced hyphal-mediated damage** inside macrophages, creating a complex trade-off: unmasked but less virulent.
- **Metal availability directly controls the masking phenotype** — this is the clearest demonstration that Primitive 4 (microbial metal dependencies) directly modulates Primitive 6 (functional shielding).

# Methods

In-vitro experiments measuring beta-glucan exposure via Dectin-1 staining and macrophage damage assays under controlled metal-limitation conditions (Mg, Fe, Zn, Mn depletion).

# Relevance to WikiBiome

- Key evidence for [[magnesium]], [[candida-albicans]], and the metal-masking nexus. Demonstrates that metals beyond iron play critical roles in fungal immune evasion.
- Informs [[nutritional-immunity]] — metal sequestration has both intended (virulence suppression) and unintended (masking modulation) consequences.

# Relevance to Cureva

- **Primitive 4 → Primitive 6 bridge**: Metal restriction as an intervention tool must account for masking effects. Mg deprivation unmasks but also reduces phagocytic killing efficiency.
- Suggests that multi-metal manipulation could be used therapeutically — restrict iron (starve siderophores) while maintaining magnesium (keep masking down).
