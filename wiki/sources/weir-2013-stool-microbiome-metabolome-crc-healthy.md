# Weir 2013 -- Stool Microbiome and Metabolome Differences in CRC vs. Healthy Adults

## Key Claims

- No significant differences in overall microbial community structure were found between CRC (n=10) and healthy (n=11) stool samples, but several butyrate-producing species were under-represented in CRC, while *Akkermansia muciniphila* was about 4-fold higher in CRC (p<0.01)
- Proportionally higher amounts of butyrate were detected in stool of healthy individuals, while relative concentrations of acetate were higher in CRC patient stools
- GC-MS metabolite profiling revealed higher concentrations of amino acids, higher poly- and monounsaturated fatty acids, and ursodeoxycholic acid (a conjugated bile acid) in CRC patient stool samples (p<0.01)
- Table 2 lists 14 bacterial species significantly more abundant in healthy individuals: *Prevotella copri* (40x fold change), *Dialister invisus* (48.7x), *Dialister pneumosintes* (52.6x), *Ruminococcus albus* (10.3x)
- Correlative analysis between combined metabolome and microbiome datasets revealed potential relationships between certain bacterial species and associated metabolites

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Case-control study; stool from 10 CRC patients (pre-surgery) and 11 healthy adults at University of Colorado
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA pyrosequencing (V4 region, 454 GS FLX), GC-MS nontargeted metabolite profiling, targeted SCFA analysis
- **Key limitation**: Small sample size (n=21); no significant community-level clustering by disease status despite species-level differences
