# Microbiome and Metabolome Modifying Effects of Several CVD Interventions in Apo-E-/- Mice

## Summary

Preclinical study characterizing the compositional and functional effects of three nutritional/pharmaceutical [[cardiovascular-disease]] interventions on the gut microbiome in apolipoprotein-E-deficient mice using metagenomic and metabolomic approaches. Interventions: plant sterol ester (PSE), oat beta-glucan (OBG), and bile salt hydrolase-active *Lactobacillus reuteri* APC 2587 (BSH), compared to atorvastatin (STAT) and high-fat/cholesterol diet control (HFC).

## Key Findings

### Atherosclerotic Plaque Outcomes
- PSE: Reduced total aortic plaque from 15.2% (HFC) to 2.4% (P < 0.001)
- OBG: Reduced plaque to 7.4% (P < 0.05)
- BSH and STAT: No significant plaque reduction
- Normal chow (NC) control: No plaque whatsoever

### Metabolic Effects
- PSE: Significantly reduced total cholesterol, reduced body fat; greatest degree of adiposity
- OBG: Increased HDL-C by approximately 100% (P < 0.01)
- BSH and STAT: Reduced liver triglycerides
- PSE significantly dampened microbial production of proatherogenic precursor compound trimethylamine (P < 0.05)
- PSE nearly abolished atherogenesis in the model (P < 0.05)

### Microbiome Composition
- Significant dissimilarity between groups (PERMANOVA, P < 0.05)
- PSE clustered away from HFC significantly; BSH approaching significance
- OBG: Significant increase in *Verrucomicrobia*; STAT: significant decrease
- *Proteobacteria* elevated in PSE animals
- PSE mice: Significant reduction in *Streptococcaceae*; *Desulfovibrionaceae* reduced in OBG and NC
- *Porphyromonadaceae* elevated in BSH animals

### SCFA and Metabolome
- OBG: Significantly increased n-butyrate levels compared to HFC
- PSE: Capable of enhancing isovalerate production
- BSH intervention associated with reduced TMA/TMAO
- All interventions significantly impacted host serum metabolome in acylcarnitines associated with metabolic dysfunction

## Relevance to the Wiki

Demonstrates that dietary and probiotic interventions can simultaneously modify gut microbiome composition, [[short-chain-fatty-acids|metabolites]] including [[tmao]], and [[atherosclerosis]] outcomes. PSE emerges as the most effective intervention for plaque reduction and TMA suppression. Supports the concept that microbiome-targeted therapies have potential for CVD prevention.
