# Key Claims

- **PPD develops in about 15% of women** during pregnancy or first postpartum year
- **Multiple metabolic pathways** altered in PPD: energy metabolism, tryptophan and amino acid metabolism, steroid metabolism, purine cycle, and neurotransmitter metabolism
- **Metabolomics** can help define biochemical pathways directly involved in PPD etiology, diagnosis, and course
- Metabolome reflects different PPD therapies and exposure of breastfed infants to pharmacotherapy
- Endocrine disrupting metabolites during pregnancy also linked to PPD

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Review of published metabolomic studies examining metabolome-PPD associations
- **Key metabolic alterations**: Tryptophan pathway disruption, amino acid imbalances, steroid hormone changes, energy metabolism shifts
