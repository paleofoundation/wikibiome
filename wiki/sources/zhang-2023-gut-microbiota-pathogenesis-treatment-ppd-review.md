# Key Claims

- **PPD pathogenesis** involves disturbances of neuroendocrine regulation, immune system, neurotransmitters, hormone secretion, and the gut microbiome
- **Gut microbes** play essential physiological and pathological roles in gut-brain axis pathways involved in CNS and psychiatric disorders including PPD
- **Disruptions in gut microbes** are associated with multiple signaling pathways leading to PPD development
- **PPD network** connects gut microbiota disturbances with other pathogenic mechanisms including [[inflammation]]
- Only 24% and 13% of women diagnosed with PPD remain depressed at 1 and 2 years after delivery, but 25% remain symptomatic long-term

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of gut microbiota function in PPD pathogenesis and treatment
- **Key pathways**: Neuroendocrine regulation, immune modulation, neurotransmitter production, hormone secretion via [[gut-brain-axis]]
- **Therapeutic targets**: Probiotics, FMT, dietary interventions modulating gut microbiota for PPD management
