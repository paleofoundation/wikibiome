# ATSDR Toxicological Profile for Tin and Tin Compounds

## Key Claims

- **Document type**: Comprehensive federal toxicological profile prepared per CERCLA/Superfund mandates; supersedes September 2003 draft
- Covers both inorganic tin and organotin compounds (e.g., tributyltin, trimethyltin)
- Prepared in accordance with ATSDR and EPA guidelines originally published in the Federal Register (April 17, 1987)
- Tin is on the CERCLA National Priorities List of hazardous substances posing significant potential threat to human health
- Profile structure includes: Public Health Statement (Ch. 1), Relevance to Public Health (Ch. 2), Health Effects (Ch. 3), Children's Susceptibility (Sec. 3.7), Biomarkers (Sec. 3.8), Methods for Reducing Toxic Effects (Sec. 3.11)
- Health effects covered by route of exposure (inhalation, oral, dermal) and duration (acute, intermediate, chronic)
- Addresses both human and animal studies; includes pediatric-specific sections on children's susceptibility and exposure

## Methods/Findings

- **Scope**: Exhaustive review of toxicologic and epidemiologic literature on tin and tin compounds
- **Note**: First 10 pages are largely preliminary/administrative (title page, disclaimer, update statement, foreword, legislative background, quick reference, contributors); substantive toxicological data begins in later chapters
- **Peer review**: Reviewed by CDC, other federal scientists, and an external nongovernmental panel
- **Key distinction**: Inorganic tin (low oral absorption, mainly GI effects) vs. organotin compounds (much more toxic, neurotoxic, immunotoxic) - mirrors the EU SCF distinction
- **Legislative basis**: SARA of 1986 (Public Law 99-499) amending CERCLA
