## Key Findings

- **Oregano (1% w/v) reshapes microbial community composition**: significantly reduces the genus *Streptococcus* (p=1.6e-3) and significantly increases *Lactobacillus* in mixed caecum cultures.
- **SCFA production increased**: acetic acid and butyric acid concentrations rose significantly in oregano-supplemented cultures vs controls — suggesting support for beneficial fermentation pathways.
- **Active compounds**: carvacrol (CAR), thymol, p-cymene, γ-terpinene comprise ~80% of oregano essential oil. CAR and thymol act at low doses to reduce cell membrane potential, cause membrane disruption, and kill susceptible cells.
- **Enterococcus faecium was increased** — a mixed effect. Broad community-level rearrangement rather than single-target effect.
- Oregano demonstrated superior in vitro antimicrobial activity vs rosemary and comparable to antibiotic growth promoter avilamycin in prior studies.

## Methods

Enhanced LYHBHI anaerobic medium inoculated with mixed caecum community from 3 heritage-breed roosters. Oregano powder added at 1% w/v; anaerobic incubation at 37°C, 24 hr. 16S rRNA (V3-V4) sequencing on Illumina MiSeq. SCFA analyzed by GC-MS (Shimadzu GC-MS-QP2010 Ultra). Differential abundance analysis via DESeq2.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Caecum chicken model; translation to human colon/gut is approximate.
- 1% w/v is a high dose; lower doses may have different selectivity.
