# Key Claims

- **Multiple amino acid abnormalities** in ASD: elevated phosphoethanolamine, histidine, cystathionine, 3-methyl histidine, phenylalanine/tyrosine ratio
- **Homocysteine, carnosine, methionine, cystine, and threonine** lower in ASD vs. controls
- **Vitamin B12 and D** both significantly lower in ASD group (P < 0.001 each)
- **Vitamin D deficiency** correlated with amino acid changes; after controlling for vitamin D, significant differences persisted for phosphoethanolamine, cystathionine, cystine, and threonine

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Case-control study of 21 ASD patients (ages 2-18) and 21 age/sex-matched healthy controls in Turkey.

**Amino acid findings (Table II):**
- Significantly higher in ASD: ammonium, phosphoethanolamine, histidine, cystathionine, 3-methyl histidine, phenylalanine/tyrosine ratio
- Significantly lower in ASD: homocysteine, carnosine, methionine, cystine, threonine, phosphoserine

**Vitamin status (Table III):**
- Vitamin B12: ASD 233.62 vs. control 428.50 (P < 0.001)
- 25-OH Vitamin D: ASD 14.94 vs. control 29.42 (P < 0.001)

**Vitamin-amino acid correlations (Table IV):**
- Vitamin D positively correlated with aminoadipic acid; negatively correlated with alanine, arginine, aspartic acid, phosphoserine, hydroxylysine, homocysteine, ornithine, cystathionine, citrulline, taurine
- Vitamin B12 positively correlated with ethanolamine only

**Key discussion points:**
- Phosphoethanolamine (PE) is a phosphomonoester relevant to membrane biosynthesis; high PE in ASD similar to Alzheimer's disease
- Threonine deficiency causes neurological dysfunction and lameness in experimental animals
- Cystathionine elevation with low methionine and cystine suggests transsulfuration pathway disruption

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **B12-metal axis**: Severe B12 deficiency in ASD impairs cobalt-dependent methylation pathways, directly affecting homocysteine metabolism and metal-dependent one-carbon cycling
- **Vitamin D-metal homeostasis**: Profound vitamin D deficiency disrupts calcium, zinc, and iron absorption; compounds metal dyshomeostasis
- **Transsulfuration disruption**: High cystathionine with low cystine reflects impaired cystathionine gamma-lyase (a zinc-dependent enzyme), suggesting systemic zinc insufficiency
- **Methionine cycle**: Low methionine and high homocysteine indicate disrupted SAM cycle, which requires zinc, B12, and folate for proper metal-dependent methylation
