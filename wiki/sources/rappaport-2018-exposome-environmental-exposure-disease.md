# Redefining Environmental Exposure for Disease Etiology

## Summary

Perspectives article outlining how environmental exposure can be defined by the blood exposome -- the totality of chemicals circulating in blood from both endogenous and exogenous sources. Argues for exposure-wide association studies using archived blood to identify causal exposures for chronic diseases including CVD.

## Key Findings

### The Blood Exposome Concept
- Blood contains chemicals from: genome, epigenome, transcriptome, proteome, metabolome, and foreign DNA/RNA
- Exogenous chemicals from diet, vitamins, drugs, pathogens, microbiota, pollution, and lifestyle factors
- Endogenous chemicals represented by proteome and metabolome
- The exposome integrates all sources, unlike traditional studies focusing on one chemical at a time

### CVD-Relevant Metabolomics Findings
- 18 small-molecule features including choline, betaine, and TMAO associated with incident CVD in Gene Bank cohort
- Branched-chain and aromatic amino acids associated with T1D risk in DIPP study
- 4859 polar and nonpolar metabolites in SCHS cohort associated with T2D risk
- EPIC cohort: 140 lipids, amino acids, and biogenic amines associated with cancer/pre-diabetes

### Key Argument
- GWAS have detected small effects (8% cancer, 22% CVD from monozygotic twin studies)
- Roughly half of 50 million global deaths in 2010 were attributed to 18 environmental exposures
- The blood exposome can capture gene-environment interactions that single-exposure studies miss
- Prospective blood samples from archived collections enable exposure-wide association studies

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Integrated exposure assessment** - The exposome framework naturally incorporates [[lead]], [[cadmium]], and other metals alongside microbiota-derived metabolites like TMAO
2. **Blood as integration site** - Blood captures both exogenous metal exposures and endogenous microbiome metabolites, making it ideal for studying the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] axis
3. **Multi-pollutant approach** - Figures show how heavy metals (Pb, As, Cd, Hg) lead to [[oxidative-stress]] and [[inflammation]] as markers of effect, with downstream CVD outcomes
4. **Microbiome as endogenous exposure** - Microbial metabolites are part of the exposome, linking [[dysbiosis]] to disease through the same framework as chemical exposures
