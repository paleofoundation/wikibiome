# Agakidou 2020 — Emerging Biomarkers for NEC Prediction and Early Diagnosis

## Key Findings

- **Most promising protein biomarkers**: fecal calprotectin (fC-FABP, intestinal fatty acid binding protein) and **trefoil factor 3 (TFF3)** — both reflecting intestinal epithelial injury at the mucosal level, detectable before systemic signs ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]], narrative review)
- **7-protein urine panel** (Sylvester et al. 2014): AUC **98% for NEC vs. sepsis** discrimination; AUC **98.4% for medical vs. surgical NEC** — suggesting urine proteomics can stratify NEC severity pre-operatively ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])
- **ApoSAA score** (Pro-apoC2 + serum amyloid A): most promising serum biomarker pair for distinguishing NEC from late-onset sepsis ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])
- **Ceramides + sphingomyelins** elevated in Bell's stage II–III NEC (Rusconi et al. 2018) — sphingolipid pathway disruption as a hallmark of established NEC (consistent with the Lin 2023 finding of reduced sphingolipid metabolism in NEC vs controls) ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])
- **Linoleate metabolism metabolites** associated with NEC (Stewart et al. 2016) — fatty acid oxidation pathway disruption ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])
- **Early amino acid and acylcarnitine deviations** increase progressively as NEC approaches clinical diagnosis (Sinclair et al. 2020) — suggesting metabolomic profiling can build a predictive temporal signature ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])
- **Urinary gluconic acid** elevated in NEC — a pentose phosphate pathway metabolite that may reflect oxidative stress in the inflamed intestine ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])
- **Buccal swab proteomics** (Torrazza et al. 2013): IL-1 receptor antagonist (IL-1RA), peroxiredoxin-1, and alpha1-antitrypsin showed high fold-differences between NEC and control infants — non-invasive sampling avenue for NEC prediction ([[agakidou-2020-nec-biomarkers-metabolomics-proteomics]])

## Methods

**Design:** Narrative review of proteomics and metabolomics NEC biomarker studies published through 2020. No systematic search or meta-analytic methods.

**Scope:** Covers serum, urine, stool, and buccal swab samples; proteomics (2D-DIGE, mass spectrometry, ELISA) and metabolomics (LC-MS/MS, GC-MS, ¹H NMR spectroscopy) approaches.

**Key limitation:** Narrative review with heterogeneous study populations; most primary studies are small (n<50) and used different NEC diagnostic criteria (Bell's stages), sample types, and analytical platforms. Biomarker validation in independent cohorts is limited. No meta-analysis possible across studies.

## Detailed Summary

NEC diagnosis remains clinical and radiological — by the time radiographic pneumatosis appears (Bell's stage II), significant gut injury has already occurred. The biomarker field aims to detect NEC before the clinical diagnostic threshold, creating a window for pre-emptive intervention.

### Protein Biomarkers

**I-FABP (fecal calprotectin / intestinal FABP)** reflects enterocyte death and tight junction disruption — it leaks into stool from damaged villous enterocytes before systemic signs appear. TFF3 (trefoil factor 3) is a mucosal healing peptide elevated in early mucosal injury.

The **7-protein urine panel** (including fibrinogen, clusterin, and vitamin D binding protein) is the most diagnostically precise single finding in this review — 98% AUC for NEC vs. sepsis discrimination means it could resolve one of the most common and dangerous diagnostic dilemmas in NICU medicine. The panel's discriminatory power for medical vs. surgical NEC (98.4% AUC) would change clinical management.

**Buccal swab proteomics** represents a particularly significant advance: non-invasive, could be performed serially without blood drawing in fragile preterm infants. IL-1RA, peroxiredoxin-1 (an antioxidant enzyme elevated under oxidative stress), and alpha1-antitrypsin (acute-phase protease inhibitor) were identified as high-fold-difference markers.

### Metabolomic Signatures

The **sphingolipid elevation in Bell's II–III NEC** (ceramides and sphingomyelins) cross-validates with the Lin 2023 finding that sphingolipid metabolism is higher in healthy controls — converging evidence that NEC disrupts normal mucosal sphingolipid biology and that sphingolipid biomarkers track severity.

**Acylcarnitine and amino acid deviations** preceding NEC diagnosis (Sinclair 2020) suggest that mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation is impaired in the pre-NEC period — either from the emerging dysbiosis, hypoxia in the gut wall, or both. This is mechanistically consistent with the ketone body pathway activation found by Lin 2023 (PICRUSt2 prediction of ketone synthesis/degradation in NEC).

**Gluconic acid** elevation connects to the pentose phosphate pathway, which is upregulated under oxidative stress and required for NADPH production. Elevated urinary gluconic acid in NEC likely reflects the oxidative stress of the inflamed intestine.

### Analytical Methods

The review covers three metabolomics platforms:
- **LC-MS/MS**: High sensitivity/specificity for targeted metabolite panels
- **GC-MS**: Broad coverage of small volatile metabolites
- **¹H NMR spectroscopy**: Non-destructive, semi-quantitative, useful for urine metabolome surveys

- **NEC vs. sepsis discrimination**: ApoSAA score and 7-protein urine panel as decision-support tools for the NEC-vs-sepsis diagnostic dilemma, which currently drives empirical broad-spectrum antibiotic use
- **STOP**: The ceramide/sphingomyelin elevation in severe NEC suggests sphingolipid pathway disruption is a severity marker — interventions that further disrupt sphingolipid metabolism in preterm infants should be flagged

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Ceramide elevation in Bell's II–III NEC (this review) + reduced sphingolipid metabolism in NEC vs controls (Lin 2023, PICRUSt2) appear to contradict — but these reflect different compartments: Lin 2023 measured microbial pathway activity (sphingolipid synthesis by gut bacteria), while Rusconi 2018 measured host plasma ceramides (host membrane breakdown products). These are complementary, not contradictory.
- Most biomarker studies are small and single-center; none of the promising panels have been validated in multi-center RCTs.

## Open Questions

- Has the 7-protein urine panel been validated in an independent multi-center cohort?
- Can serial acylcarnitine + amino acid monitoring replace or supplement microbiome surveillance for NEC prediction?
- What is the mechanistic link between ceramide elevation and NEC severity — is it cause (ceramide-induced apoptosis) or consequence (membrane breakdown)?
- Does urinary gluconic acid elevation precede Bell's stage II diagnosis, and by how many hours/days?
