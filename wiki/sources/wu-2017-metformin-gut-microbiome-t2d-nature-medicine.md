# Metformin Alters the Gut Microbiome of Individuals with Treatment-Naive Type 2 Diabetes

## Summary

Randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind study in 40 treatment-naive T2D individuals showing that metformin (850-1700 mg/day for 4 months) had strong effects on the gut microbiome. Transfer of metformin-treated fecal samples to germ-free mice improved glucose tolerance, supporting a causal microbiome-mediated mechanism.

## Key Findings

### Metformin-Induced Microbiome Changes
- Only one bacterial strain altered over 4-month placebo period vs. significant alterations with metformin
- 2 months of metformin: 81 bacterial strains significantly altered; 4 months: 86 strains
- Increased *Escherichia coli* and decreased *[[intestinibacter|Intestinibacter]]* at genus level
- Metformin-induced increase in *Bifidobacterium adolescentis* (peak-to-trough ratio significantly increased)
- *A. muciniphila* increased in both DNA and RNA abundance in response to metformin

### Functional Shifts
- Metformin enriched genes for bacterial secretion system, chemotaxis, PTS, CAMP resistance, LPS biosynthesis
- Butyrate metabolism, propionate metabolism, and amino acid metabolism enriched
- 626 KOs increased and 130 KOs decreased after 2 months; 473 increased and 69 decreased after 4 months
- Pathway enrichment linked to enrichment of genes for bacterial environmental responses (ABC transporters, two-component systems)

### SCFA and Bile Acid Changes
- Significantly larger increases in fecal propionate and butyrate in metformin group
- Trend toward larger increase in fecal lactate and succinate
- Significantly larger increases in plasma bile acids (total, primary, secondary, unconjugated)
- Increased abundance of *bsh* (bile salt hydrolase) genes after 2 months on metformin

### Causal Evidence from FMT
- Transfer of M4 (4-month metformin) microbiota to germ-free mice improved glucose tolerance vs. M0 (baseline) microbiota
- All three independent transfer experiments showed similar results
- Metformin directly promoted growth of *B. adolescentis* and *A. muciniphila* in pure cultures

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Metformin's microbiome-mediated effects** - [[Metformin]] therapeutic effects partly mediated through gut microbiome alterations, relevant to its cardioprotective properties
2. **SCFA production** - Increased [[SCFAs]] production may contribute to metformin's cardiovascular benefits via anti-inflammatory and barrier-protective effects
3. **Drug repurposing framework** - Demonstrates how understanding drug-microbiome interactions can reveal novel therapeutic mechanisms for [[cardiovascular-disease]]
