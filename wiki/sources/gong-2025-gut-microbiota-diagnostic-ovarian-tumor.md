# Characteristics and Potential Diagnostic Value of Gut Microbiota in Ovarian Tumor Patients

## Key Claims

- Gut microbiota composition in ovarian tumor patients differs significantly from healthy controls in bacterial abundance and diversity
- Escherichia_Shigella was markedly higher in ovarian tumor group while Coprococcus, Fusicatenibacter, Butyricicoccus, and Oscillibacter were lower
- Random forest models using gut microbiota achieved AUC of 0.77 for benign ovarian tumors and 0.86 for ovarian cancer vs. healthy controls
- Ovarian cancer and benign ovarian tumor patients showed unique gut microbiota compositions

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Fecal samples from 382 individuals (239 OT, 90 OBT, 53 HC) at Zhejiang Cancer Hospital, May 2018-Jan 2022
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA V4 region sequencing; QIIME2 analysis; random forest classification
- **Key taxa**: 14 gut microbiota showed significant differences at genus level between OT and HC groups
