# Key Claims

- **Caregiver distress** can lead to dysregulation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis
- **Pro-inflammatory state** of the immune and central nervous system develops from chronic caregiver stress
- **Gut microbiome imbalance** associated with caregiver psychological distress
- **Increased mortality risk** in caregivers of children with ASD, though some individual factors diminish these risks
- Findings may be relevant to **other chronic diseases including cancer**

# Methods & Findings

**Review** of biological consequences of chronic psychological distress in ASD caregivers.

- Examined epidemiological evidence for increased mortality risk in caregivers
- Reviewed HPA axis dysregulation from chronic stress
- Described pro-inflammatory immune state and CNS effects
- Identified gut microbiome changes associated with caregiver stress
- Called for structural psychological and physical screening of caregivers

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Caregiver health** as an often-neglected dimension of ASD impact
- **Stress-microbiome-immune axis** in caregivers parallels biological mechanisms studied in ASD individuals
- **Family-level intervention** needed beyond patient-focused approaches
