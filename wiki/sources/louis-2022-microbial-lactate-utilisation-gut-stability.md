# Key Findings

- **Lactate-utilizing bacteria (primarily Firmicutes) convert lactate to butyrate and propionate**, preventing lactate accumulation in the gut and maintaining ecological stability ([[louis-2022-microbial-lactate-utilisation-gut-stability]], expert-opinion).
- **Depletion of lactate utilizers causes lactate accumulation**, which enables expansion of acid-tolerant pathogens (*Campylobacter*, *Salmonella*, *E. coli*) and — via the masking connection — promotes Candida immune evasion.
- **Key lactate-utilizing species**: *Anaerobutyricum hallii* (77% prevalence in healthy adults) and *Anaerostipes caccae* (66%) are the dominant cross-feeders converting lactate to butyrate.

Narrative review synthesizing published data on lactate metabolism in the human gut microbiome, including prevalence surveys and metabolic pathway analyses.
