# Key Claims

- **Intestinal microbiota** is significantly different between ASD and neurotypical children in China
- **Probiotics combined with ABA training** reduce both ASD-related and GI symptoms more effectively than ABA alone
- **ASD children without GI symptoms** are more sensitive to probiotics treatment than those with GI symptoms
- **Bacteroides, Bifidobacterium, Ruminococcus, Roseburia, and Blautia** significantly lower in ASD vs. neurotypical children

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Multi-center clinical study of 114 ASD children and 40 neurotypical children from 4 clinical centers in China.

**Key microbiota findings:**
- Shannon index: ASD group significantly higher than NT (greater abundance but lower evenness)
- Simpson index: ASD with GI events significantly lower than NT
- At phylum level: [[bacteroidetes]] significantly lower in ASD; Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio increased
- At genus level: [[bacteroides-fragilis]], [[bifidobacterium]], [[ruminococcus]], [[roseburia]], [[blautia]] significantly lower in ASD
- [[lachnospira]] significantly more abundant in ASD

**Probiotic intervention (n = 37 ASD children, 4 weeks):**
- 30-40 billion CFU/day combined with ABA training
- 83.8% showed decreased ATEC scores
- ATEC total scores decreased by 8.1 points on average
- Speech/Language Communication, Sociability, Sensory/Cognitive Awareness, Health/Physical/Behavior all significantly decreased
- ASD children without GI events: average ATEC dropped from 59.3 to 50.2
- GI score improvement in 86.4% (19/22) of children with GI symptoms

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Bacteroides depletion**: Reduced Bacteroides in ASD may impair bile acid deconjugation and metal-chelating secondary bile acid production
- **Bifidobacterium reduction**: Loss of Bifidobacterium decreases iron-sequestering siderophore production and zinc-dependent metabolic pathways
- **ABA-probiotic synergy**: Behavioral training combined with microbial ecosystem restoration suggests multi-modal intervention targeting both neural and gut ecological axes
- **Constipation predominance**: 80% of ASD participants had constipation, consistent with reduced butyrate-producing taxa and impaired metal-dependent barrier function
