# Key Claims

- **Microbiota-gut-brain axis (MGBA)** is a critical determinant in depression pathogenesis, integrating gut microbiota with CNS function
- **Three key pathways**: Immune regulation (cytokine release), endocrine modulation (HPA axis), and neural signaling (vagus nerve, neurotransmitter regulation)
- **Advanced therapeutic strategies** include probiotics, FMT, and AI-enabled microbiome interventions
- **CUMS mouse model** demonstrates FMT from depressed individuals induces depression-like behaviors in healthy recipients
- Altered *Lactobacillus* and *Bifidobacterium* abundance consistently found in depression

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of MGBA mechanisms in MDD with implications for [[postpartum-depression]]
- **Key preclinical evidence**: Gut microbiota modulates neurotransmitter production, [[inflammation]], and stress response systems
- **Therapeutic review**: Probiotics, prebiotics, FMT, and personalized microbiome treatments
