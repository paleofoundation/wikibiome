# Key Claims

- Heavy metal exposure causes significant damage to the intestinal epithelial barrier, leading to loss of microbial and immune homeostasis
- The intestinal barrier consists of three key components: mucus layer (goblet cells), intestinal epithelial cell layer (enterocytes, Paneth cells, M cells, enteroendocrine cells, tuft cells), and immunological barrier
- Intercellular junctions are critical targets: **tight junctions** (claudins, ZO-1, occludin, JAM-A), **adherens junctions** (E-cadherin, catenins), **desmosomes** (desmoglein, desmocollins), and **gap junctions** (connexins)
- Gut microbiota are essential for gut barrier function: germ-free mice have impaired barriers with downregulated claudin 4, occludin, TFF3, and MUC3
- Microbial metabolites regulate host immunity via receptors: AhR, FXR, PXR, M-BAR/TGR5, P2X7, GPR41/43/109A
- Heavy metals accumulated significantly more in GF mice organs vs. conventional mice, demonstrating microbiota's role in metal clearance
- [[Urolithin A]] (UroA), a gut microbial metabolite, protected colon epithelial cells against arsenic-induced oxidative stress and barrier dysfunction

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of mechanisms through which metals disrupt gut barrier
- **Arsenic**: 225 million chronically exposed worldwide; disrupts colonic epithelial structure; increases paracellular transport; induces IL-6, IL-8, TNF-alpha; Faecalibacterium essential for As metabolism; biotransformation via arsB, arsP, acr3 transporters and arsM methylation
- **Lead**: Reduces colonic MUC2, tight junction proteins (ZO-1, claudin-1, occludin); Pb-intolerant gut microbes (A. muciniphila, F. prausnitzii, O. ruminantium) reduce Pb burden when supplemented; enhanced SCFA production and gut barrier by upregulating TJPs
- **Mercury**: Downregulates claudin 1, occludin, ZO-1, JAM1 in colon epithelial cells; increases intestinal cell volume and membrane permeability; L. brevis 23017 protects via MAPK and NF-kappaB pathway regulation; dental fillings increase Hg- and antibiotic-resistant bacteria
- **Cadmium**: Reduces ZO-1, ZO-2, JAM-A, occludin, claudin-1 expression; low Cd doses decrease Akkermansia muciniphila; modifies gut-liver axis; L. plantarum CCFM8610 reduces Cd-induced toxicity
- **Chromium**: Hexavalent Cr exposure causes gut barrier damage via ZO-1, occludin, claudin-1, MUC2 downregulation; NLRP3 inflammasome activation; L. plantarum TW1-1 reduces Cr accumulation
