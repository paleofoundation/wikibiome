# Key Claims

- **Gut microbiome is a 'super-organism'** with metabolic, immune, and endocrine functions critical for host health
- **Diseases influenced by gut microbial metabolism** include obesity, IBD, autism, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and colon cancer
- **Drug metabolism affected by microbiome** -- p-cresol production by gut bacteria alters acetaminophen metabolism
- **Quantitative analysis of gut microbiome activity** essential for future personalized healthcare strategies

# Methods & Findings

Review of recent metagenomic and metabonomic advances:

- Gut microbiome influences immune system maturation, epithelial cell injury, xenobiotic metabolism, and energy metabolism
- Four dominant phyla: Firmicutes, Bacteroidetes, Actinobacteria, and Proteobacteria
- Phylotype composition is specific, stable, and conserved over ~60% across 2-year intervals
- Core microbiome concept: each host has a unique biological relationship with its microbiota

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc is a critical cofactor for microbial enzymes involved in drug metabolism and xenobiotic processing
- **[[iron]]**: Iron availability shapes the Firmicutes:Bacteroidetes ratio central to metabolic health
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: The metabolic interactions between host and microbiome are fundamentally dependent on metal cofactor availability
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenoprotein-dependent antioxidant defense protects the microbiome-host interface from oxidative damage
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Metal imbalances may be upstream drivers of the [[dysbiosis]] patterns associated with multiple diseases
