# Overview

Original research comparing **gut viral communities** in 11 ASD children and 11 healthy controls using **viral metagenomic techniques**. Found reduced alpha diversity and significantly different viral compositions in ASD, with *Podoviridae* dominant in ASD and *Alphaflexiviridae* dominant in controls.

# Key Findings

## Viral Diversity

- Alpha diversity of ASD virome **significantly lower** than healthy controls at phylum, class, and order levels (Wilcoxon p = 0.0083, 0.013, 0.023 respectively)
- Beta diversity (PCoA): **statistically significant compositional differences** across phylum, class, order, family, and genus levels

## Viral Composition

- 51 viral families identified (29 dsDNA, 8 ssDNA, 2 dsRNA, 12 ssRNA)
- 18 viral families showed different compositions (>=2-fold change) between groups
- In ASD: ***Podoviridae*** accounted for **58.81%** of viral proportion (dominant)
- In healthy controls: ***Alphaflexiviridae*** accounted for **50.82%** (dominant; only 0.08% in ASD -- a drastic difference)
- Other prominent families in ASD: *Caliciviridae* (31.06%), *Virgaviridae* (5.33%), *Siphoviridae* (2.51%)

## Specific Viral Differences

- **STAMP analysis**: *Phixviricota*, *Malgrandaviricetes*, *Petitvirales*, and *Microviridae* contributed most to group differences
- **Human astrovirus** detected in ASD samples (99.73% identity to Human astrovirus 1)
- **Picobirnaviridae**: two strains identified via phylogenetic analysis
- **Norovirus**: detected by phylogenetic analysis in ASD samples

## More Than 800 Viruses

- Accumulation curves revealed potentially >800 different viruses across 22 libraries
- Species richness leveled off at end suggesting sufficient sequencing depth
