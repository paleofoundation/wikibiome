# Key Claims

- The colon tumor-promoting effect of [[dietary fat]] depends on its fatty acid composition: high dietary n-3 [[PUFAs]] lack tumor-promoting effects compared to diets high in n-6 PUFAs or saturated fats
- Diets rich in n-3 PUFAs inhibit colon carcinogenesis through modulation of colonic ras-p21, cyclooxygenase-2 ([[COX-2]]), and inducible nitric oxide synthase activities, and induction of apoptosis
- Gene expression analysis via DNA microarrays shows n-3 fatty acid (DHA) activates cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitors (p21, p27, p57, p19) and inactivates anti-apoptotic Bcl-2 family genes
- Decreasing n-6 PUFAs and saturated fats while increasing n-3 PUFAs, particularly EPA and DHA, has the potential to be a major component of [[colon cancer]] control
- The upward trend in colon cancer in Japan since the 1960s correlates with dietary Westernization (increased animal fat consumption)
- n-3 PUFAs also show cardioprotective effects, suggesting dual benefit of dietary modification

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of epidemiological and laboratory animal studies
- **Epidemiological evidence**: Case-control and correlational studies show positive association between saturated fat/animal fat intake and colon cancer; inverse relationship with fish and fish oil (n-3 PUFAs)
- **Japan time-trend data**: Male colon cancer death rates rose from 3.6/100,000 (1960) to 14.7 (1999), paralleling increased animal fat intake from 24.7 g to 57.9 g/day
- **Animal models**: F344 rats fed AOM (azoxymethane) showed that high-fat corn oil (n-6) diets promoted more tumors than low-fat or fish oil diets; mixed lipids (20% HFML) at 23-38 weeks showed significantly more adenocarcinomas
- **Mechanistic studies**: n-3 PUFAs suppress COX-2 overexpression in colon tumors; COX-2 is elevated in neoplastic human colon tissue. n-3 PUFAs modulate ras-p21 function during initiation and postinitiation stages
- **Phospholipid effects**: Dietary fat influences intestinal bacterial 7-alpha-dehydroxylase and phospholipase C/PKC activities during different stages of colon tumor promotion
- **Secondary bile acids**: High-fat diets increase secondary bile acid production, which may promote colon carcinogenesis
