# Key Claims

- [[Synbiotics]] (probiotics + inulin prebiotics) significantly elevated CD8+ T cell proportion (61.50 vs 54.83 in probiotics-only, p=0.049) and IFN-gamma expression (60.50 vs 52.50 in probiotics-only, p=0.013) in PDAC tumor tissue
- Both [[probiotics]] and [[synbiotics]] significantly decreased postoperative complications including anastomotic leakage (p=0.032), diarrhea (p=0.044), and abdominal distension (p=0.042) with remarkable reduction in bacteremia in synbiotics group
- Inflammatory cytokines IL-1beta showed significant gradual decline in synbiotics group; IL-10 concentrations declined from OP-0d to PO-30d (p=0.000) in synbiotics group
- Clinical trial registered as NCT06199752

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Single-blind RCT; 90 PDAC patients randomized to placebo (n=30), probiotics (n=30), or synbiotics (n=30); 14 days preoperative + 1 month postoperative administration
- **Probiotic formulation**: 25 billion CFU of 10 strains -- *Lactobacillus acidophilus*, *Bifidobacterium lactis*, *L. plantarum*, *L. paracasei*, *B. breve*, *Streptococcus thermophilus*, *L. salivarius*, *B. longum*; synbiotics group added 1000mg inulin
- **IHC results**: CD8+ T cells in placebo 15.83%, probiotics 54.83%, synbiotics 61.50% (both p<0.05 vs placebo); IFN-gamma placebo 15.83%, probiotics 52.50%, synbiotics 60.50%
- **Cytokine changes**: IL-1beta significantly declined in synbiotics from baseline through PO-30d (p=0.000); IL-6 showed gradual reduction in both probiotic and synbiotics groups
- **Clinicopathological associations**: Higher pN-stage and TNM stage associated with greater CD8 and IFN-gamma expression in synbiotics group; female patients and those <50 years showed stronger IFN-gamma response
