# Key Claims

- **Male excess in T1D** among European-origin populations aged 15-40 with ~3:2 male:female ratio, maintained over 2-3 generations
- **T1D is the only major organ-specific autoimmune disease** not to show a strong female bias
- **Sex ratio is roughly equal** in children diagnosed under age 15, but populations with highest incidence show male excess
- **Fathers with T1D more likely** to transmit the condition than mothers of childbearing age

# Methods & Findings

Review of sex effects in both Type I (insulin-dependent) and Type II diabetes:

- Low-risk populations of non-European origin characteristically show a female bias
- Male excess is a consistent finding in European-origin populations
- The 3:2 male:female ratio has remained constant in young adults over two or three generations
- Women are more likely to transmit T1D to their offspring
