# Dietary Pro- and Prebiotics for the Management of Cardiovascular Risk

## Summary

Conference review summarizing how human gut microbiome activities connect to CVD and how diet can modulate these activities. Covers probiotics, prebiotics, fermentable fibers, plant polyphenols, and their effects on cholesterol, TMAO, bile acid metabolism, and gut barrier function.

## Key Findings

### Gut Microbiota and CVD Risk
- Aberrant microbiota profiles associated with obesity, T1D, T2D, NAFLD, cancers, and autoimmune diseases
- Transfer of fecal microbiota from obese animals induces metabolic disease; lean donors can increase insulin sensitivity
- Choline, phosphatidylcholine, and L-carnitine metabolized to [[tmao]] by gut bacteria, contributing to CVD

### Prebiotics and CVD
- Fermentable fibers and plant [[polyphenols]] regulate microbial activities in the gut
- Inulin and fructo-oligosaccharides increase *Bifidobacteria* and *Lactobacilli*
- Mediterranean diet dominated by *Firmicutes* enriched with butyrate and propionate producers
- High SCFA concentrations linked to reduced F/B ratio and reduced inflammation
- African children (plant-rich diet) had higher *Bacteroidetes*-*Prevotella* dominance, enriched butyrate/propionate producers

### Probiotics and Cholesterol
- Table 1: Multiple strains tested for cholesterol-lowering in hypercholesterolaemic individuals
- *L. acidophilus* ATCC 43121: significant TC reduction in 14 subjects
- *L. reuteri* NCIMB 30242: cholesterol reduction via bile salt hydrolase activity (BSH)
- *E. faecium* M-74 with selenium: 12% serum cholesterol reduction in 43 volunteers
- Mechanisms: cholesterol binding in gut lumen, incorporation into bacterial cell walls, SCFA-mediated cholesterol synthesis inhibition, bile acid deconjugation

### Polyphenols
- 95% of dietary polyphenols reach colon where they are fermented by gut microbiota
- Polyphenol metabolites absorbed and appear in plasma as mammalian conjugates
- Both polyphenols and their microbial metabolites have cardioprotective properties

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Dietary intervention framework** - Establishes [[prebiotics]] and [[probiotics]] as microbiome-based tools for CVD risk reduction
2. **Bile acid-cholesterol axis** - BSH-active probiotics directly modulate bile acid metabolism relevant to [[cardiovascular-disease]]
3. **Polyphenol-microbiome synergy** - Gut microbiota required for polyphenol bioactivation, linking [[polyphenols|dietary-polyphenols]] to cardiovascular protection
