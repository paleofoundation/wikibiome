# Key Claims

- Eight specific gut microbiota taxa are causally associated with pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor (pNET) risk, including risk-enhancing Sutterellaceae, Paraprevotella, and Ruminococcus torques
- Gammaproteobacteria, Acidaminococcaceae, Paraprevotella xylaniphila, and Bacteroides finegoldii were protective against pNETs
- Ruminococcus torques mediated its effect on pNETs through CD25 on CD45RA+CD4 non-regulatory T cells, accounting for 3.6% of the total effect
- Seven immune cell traits showed causal effects on pNET risk, with CD25 on CD45RA+CD4 non-regulatory T cells demonstrating protective effects

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Two-sample MR using 412 GM taxa from MiBioGen and 731 immune cell traits from GWAS catalog; pNETs data from FinnGen (122 cases, 287,137 controls)
- **Risk-enhancing taxa**: Sutterellaceae (OR 3.332), Paraprevotella (OR 1.34), Paraprevotella unclassified (OR 1.40), Ruminococcus torques (OR 1.45)
- **Protective taxa**: Gammaproteobacteria (OR 0.75), Acidaminococcaceae (OR 0.432), Paraprevotella xylaniphila (OR 0.394), Bacteroides finegoldii (OR 0.646)
- **BWMR validation**: Confirmed four taxa and five pathways as robust predictors
- **Mediation analysis**: Immune cells mediate the microbiome-pNET relationship; Ruminococcus torques effect partly through T cell modulation
