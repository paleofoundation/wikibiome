# Gut Dysbiosis Contributes to the Imbalance of Treg and Th17 Cells in Graves' Disease

## Key Claims

- Gut [[dysbiosis]] in [[graves-disease]] patients is mechanistically linked to Treg/Th17 immune imbalance, a hallmark of autoimmune thyroid pathology
- GD patients show significantly reduced [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] (SCFA)-producing bacteria, particularly butyrate producers, which are essential for maintaining Treg differentiation and immune tolerance
- The Th17/Treg ratio is elevated in GD, driven by gut dysbiosis that promotes pro-inflammatory Th17 expansion via IL-6, IL-23, and TGF-beta signaling while suppressing anti-inflammatory Treg development
- Specific bacterial taxa alterations include decreased Bacteroides, Faecalibacterium, and Roseburia (SCFA producers) and increased Prevotella and Lactobacillus in GD patients
- Restoration of gut microbiota balance through [[probiotics]], prebiotics, or metabolite supplementation (butyrate) could represent a therapeutic strategy for rebalancing Treg/Th17 in GD

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Observational case-control with 16S rRNA gene sequencing and immune cell phenotyping
- **Population**: GD patients vs healthy controls; peripheral blood Treg and Th17 cell quantification by flow cytometry
- **Alpha diversity**: Reduced Shannon and Chao1 indices in GD patients, indicating lower microbial richness and evenness
- **Beta diversity**: Significant separation between GD and control groups on PCoA, confirming distinct microbiota composition
- **Key taxa changes**: Decreased Bacteroidetes at phylum level; reduced Faecalibacterium prausnitzii and Roseburia intestinalis (major butyrate producers); increased Prevotella and certain Lactobacillus species
- **Treg/Th17 correlation**: Butyrate-producing bacterial abundance positively correlated with peripheral Treg frequency (r > 0.5, P < 0.01); inversely correlated with Th17 frequency
- **SCFA mechanism**: Butyrate promotes Treg differentiation via histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibition and enhances Foxp3 expression; butyrate also suppresses [[nf-kappa-b]] in dendritic cells, reducing Th17-polarizing cytokine production
- **Metabolic pathway analysis**: SCFA biosynthesis pathways (particularly butyrate and propionate) significantly depleted in GD microbiome; amino acid fermentation pathways enriched
- **Serum cytokines**: IL-17A and IL-6 elevated in GD; IL-10 and TGF-beta reduced; cytokine profile consistent with Th17 dominance

## Connections

- Confirms and extends microbiota findings from [[chen-2021-gut-microbiota-thyroid-graves]] with mechanistic Treg/Th17 link
- Butyrate deficiency connects to SCFA-immune axis relevant to [[probiotics]] interventions for autoimmune disease
- Reduced Faecalibacterium and Roseburia pattern parallels dysbiosis signatures in [[yang-2022-intestinal-flora-graves-methimazole]]
- SCFA-mediated HDAC inhibition and Foxp3 induction connects to [[inflammation]] resolution pathways
- [[nf-kappa-b]] suppression by butyrate provides mechanistic rationale for microbiome-targeted therapy
- Th17/Treg imbalance framework relevant to understanding berberine's immunomodulatory effects in [[han-2022-berberine-methimazole-graves-microbiome]]
- [[selenium]] and [[zinc]] are cofactors for antioxidant enzymes that protect gut barrier integrity, indirectly affecting SCFA production
- Gut barrier dysfunction from [[dysbiosis]] may increase translocation of bacterial antigens, amplifying [[inflammation]] through [[gut-metal-microbiome]] axis
- [[iron]] availability influences bacterial community composition, potentially affecting butyrate producer populations
- [[oxidative-stress]] from GD hyperthyroidism may further damage gut epithelium, perpetuating dysbiosis cycle
