# Biodiversity Composition of Microbiome in Ovarian Carcinoma Patients

## Key Claims

- Microbial diversity and richness were significantly decreased in ovarian cancer tissues compared to normal distal fallopian tubes
- The ratio of Proteobacteria/Firmicutes was notably increased in ovarian cancer
- Transcriptome profiling identified 84 different inflammation-associated or immune-associated genes with modulated expression between cancer and normal tissue
- Microbial composition change may be associated with ovarian cancer development by influencing the local immune microenvironment

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Discovery (25 OC + 25 control) and validation (46 OC + 41 control) phases using 16S rRNA sequencing
- **Tissue source**: Ovarian cancer tissues and normal distal fallopian tube tissues
- **Diversity**: Shannon index significantly different (P=0.0215) between cancer and normal groups
- **RNA-seq**: Identified inflammation and immune-response genes with altered expression
