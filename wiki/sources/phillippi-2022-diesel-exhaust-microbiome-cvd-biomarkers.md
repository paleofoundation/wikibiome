# Inhaled Diesel Exhaust Particles Result in Microbiome-Related Systemic Inflammation and Altered Cardiovascular Disease Biomarkers in C57Bl/6 Male Mice

## Summary

Animal study investigating how inhaled diesel exhaust particles (DEP) alter the gut microbiome and promote microbiome-related systemic [[inflammation]] and CVD biomarkers in male C57Bl/6 mice on low-fat or high-fat diets, and whether probiotics can mitigate these effects.

## Key Findings

### Microbial Profile Changes
- DEP exposure on LF diet: 20.96% increase in Firmicutes, decrease in Actinobacteria
- DEP exposure on HF diet: 10.70% decrease in Firmicutes, 41.9% increase in Proteobacteria
- *Pseudomonas* increased with DEP regardless of diet
- *Escherichia* increased 59.41% in HF+DEP vs decreased 23.43% in LF+DEP
- *Bifidobacterium* decreased with DEP in both diet groups
- Beta diversity significantly different between diet groups (AMOVA p<0.001)

### Systemic Inflammation
- LPS significantly increased with DEP exposure (p=0.023 in LF+DEP, p<0.001 in HF+CON and HF+DEP)
- HF diet alone elevated LPS significantly vs LF
- IL-1alpha significantly decreased with DEP exposure
- TNF-alpha increased in HF+CON vs controls
- MIP-2 increased in HF diet groups
- G-CSF increased in LF+DEP

### CVD Biomarkers
- sICAM decreased in HF+CON, increased in HF+DEP
- sP-Selectin increased with DEP and HF diet (p=0.010)
- Thrombomodulin decreased with DEP exposure
- PAI-1 decreased in LF+DEP but increased in HF+DEP

### Probiotic Mitigation
- Probiotics attenuated Actinobacteria reduction and Proteobacteria expansion from DEP
- Probiotics mitigated circulating cytokines (IL-3, IL-13, G-CSF, RANTES, TNF-alpha)
- Probiotics mitigated CVD biomarker alterations (sICAM, PAI-1, sP-Selectin, thrombomodulin, PECAM)

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Environmental exposure-microbiome-CVD pathway** - Demonstrates a complete pathway from inhaled pollutant exposure through gut [[dysbiosis]] to systemic [[inflammation]] and CVD biomarker alteration, paralleling [[lead]] and [[cadmium]] exposure pathways
2. **Diet-exposure synergy** - High-fat diet dramatically worsens DEP effects on microbiome, relevant to understanding how metal exposure interacts with dietary factors in [[metabolic-syndrome]]
3. **LPS translocation** - DEP-induced LPS elevation mirrors the endotoxemia seen with metal-induced gut barrier disruption
4. **Probiotic therapeutic potential** - Demonstrates that microbiome-targeted interventions can mitigate environmental exposure-driven CVD risk
