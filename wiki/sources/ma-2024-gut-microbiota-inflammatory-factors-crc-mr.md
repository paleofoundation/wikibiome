# Association between the Gut Microbiota, Inflammatory Factors, and Colorectal Cancer: Evidence from Mendelian Randomization Analysis

## Key Claims

- MR analysis revealed 8 gut bacterial taxa with causal relationships to CRC: *Lachnospiraceae* species showed a negative (protective) correlation, while *Porphyromonadaceae* and *Sellimonas* genus showed positive correlations with CRC risk
- IL-10 was causally associated with increased CRC risk (OR = 1.49, 95% CI 1.20-1.87, P = 4.31E-04), representing the first MR-based evidence linking this anti-inflammatory cytokine to CRC
- A causal relationship was identified between genus *Lachnospiraceae UCG010* id.11330 and IL-10, but the increase in CRC caused by this taxon is hypothesized not to be mediated by IL-10, suggesting two unrelated pathogenic processes
- Family *Clostridiales vadin BB60 group* (OR = 0.75, P = 2.96E-02) and genus *Lachnospiraceae UCG008* (OR = 0.74, P = 1.37E-02) showed protective effects against CRC
- *Lachnospira* id.2004 showed a strong risk association (OR = 4.43, P = 3.03E-02), representing one of the largest effect sizes among bacterial taxa

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Two-sample MR analysis integrating gut microbiota, 41 inflammatory factors, and CRC GWAS data
- **Data sources**: MiBioGen consortium (n = 24,000) for microbiota; comprehensive cytokine meta-analysis from three independent Finnish cohorts (n = 8,293) for inflammatory factors; Finngen database for CRC outcomes
- **Methods**: Five MR methods (IVW, weighted generalized linear, MR-Egger, weighted median, weighted mode); Cochrane Q for heterogeneity; MR-Egger intercept for pleiotropy
- **Sensitivity**: All heterogeneity tests yielded P > 0.05; all pleiotropy tests were non-significant
