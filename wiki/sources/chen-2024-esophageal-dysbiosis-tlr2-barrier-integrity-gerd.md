# Esophageal microbial dysbiosis impairs mucosal barrier integrity via toll-like receptor 2 pathway in patients with gastroesophageal reflux symptoms

## Key Claims

- Patients with GER symptoms (both [[gerd]] and functional esophageal disorders) showed significantly increased proportion of Gram-negative bacteria (35% increase) compared to healthy controls, regardless of objective evidence of reflux
- Enriched Gram-negative bacteria induced esophageal barrier dysfunction via the LPS-[[TLR2]]-[[IL-6]]-[[claudin-1]]-DIS pathway, providing a mechanistic link between [[dysbiosis]] and reflux symptoms
- In vitro, exposure of human esophageal epithelial cells (HEECs) to [[lipopolysaccharide]] resulted in TLR2 upregulation, claudin-1 downregulation, and dilated intercellular spaces (DIS)
- These barrier-disrupting effects were mitigated by blocking TLR2 or IL-6, identifying therapeutic targets
- Microbial cluster typing revealed two distinct community types: Type I (Firmicutes/Gram-positive, *Streptococcus*-enriched) associated with healthy controls; Type II (Proteobacteria/Gram-negative, *Pseudomonas*-enriched) associated with GERD/FED patients
- All healthy volunteers and duodenal ulcer patients were categorized into Type I microbiome, while GERD and FED patients clustered into Type II

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Prospective cross-sectional study with in vitro mechanistic validation
- **Population**: 12 GERD, 16 FED, 10 duodenal ulcer, 10 healthy volunteers; esophageal mucosal biopsies
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA gene V3-V4 region on MiSeq; 2691 OTUs classified into 780 genera
- **Transcriptomics**: Illumina HiSeq for transcriptome analysis of esophageal mucosa
- **IHC markers**: TLR2, TLR4, claudin-1, zonula occludens (ZO-1), occludin, E-cadherin
- **qRT-PCR**: TLR2, TLR4, claudin-1, occludin, E-cadherin, ZO-1, TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-4, IL-8, IL-6, IL-10
- **In vitro**: HEEC line (BNCC, BNCC359279) exposed to LPS at various concentrations; TLR2 blocked with anti-hTLR2-IgA; IL-6 neutralized with antibody; claudin-1 knocked down with siRNA
- **Key mechanism**: LPS -> TLR2 upregulation -> IL-6 production -> claudin-1 downregulation -> dilated intercellular spaces -> submucosal sensory neuron exposure -> GER symptoms
