## Key Claims

- A [[low-nickel-diet]] reduces GERD symptom severity in 95% (19/20) of participants after 8 weeks, with significant decreases in total GERD-HRQL, heartburn, and regurgitation scores (all p<0.001)
- Patch test positivity to nickel does NOT predict responsiveness to a low-nickel diet for GERD; both nickel-positive and nickel-negative patients responded equivalently
- This is the first study to examine the effect of a low-nickel diet specifically on GERD symptoms
- Mean total GERD-HRQL decreased by 27.05 points; heartburn by 11.45; regurgitation by 10.85

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Prospective, single-site pilot study at West Virginia University (Feb 2019 - Dec 2019); registered at ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT03720756)
- **Population**: 20 refractory GERD patients (16 females, 4 males; mean age 49.95 +/- 12.74; all Caucasian; mean BMI 35.24 +/- 9.04; 40% smokers); actively taking PPI for 3+ months without resolution; GERD-HRQL score 30+
- **Protocol**: All patients had epicutaneous patch testing (nickel sulfate hexahydrate, cobalt, chromium, balsam of Peru, cinnamic aldehyde) then followed 8-week low-nickel diet blinded to patch test results; GERD-HRQL repeated after 8 weeks
- **Patch test results**: 3/20 (15%) positive to nickel; 6/20 (30%) to balsam of Peru; 1/20 (5%) to cinnamic aldehyde; 0 to cobalt
- **Results**: Baseline GERD-HRQL 48.60 +/- 11.44; post-diet 21.55 (decrease of 27.05); heartburn 21.10 to 9.65 (decrease 11.45); regurgitation 19.65 to 8.80 (decrease 10.85)
- **Nickel-positive vs negative**: Both improved; positive had more modest improvement (14.67 vs 29.24 total decrease) but difference not statistically significant
- **Satisfaction**: 45% changed from dissatisfied to satisfied; 25% from dissatisfied to neutral; 25% remained dissatisfied; participants lost ~3 kg on average
- **Limitations**: No placebo/control group; small sample; all Caucasian; 100% completion rate (no dropouts); dietary restriction alone may account for some improvement; possible confounders include eosinophilic esophagitis
