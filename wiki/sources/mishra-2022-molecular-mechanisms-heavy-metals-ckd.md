# Molecular Mechanisms of Cellular Injury and Role of Toxic Heavy Metals in Chronic Kidney Disease

## Key Claims

- CKD pathogenesis involves multiple interconnected molecular mechanisms: mitochondrial dysfunction, oxidative stress, carbamylation, impaired DNA damage repair, epigenetic modifications, ferroptosis, endoplasmic reticulum stress, and inflammation
- As CKD progresses and GFR decreases, the ability to eliminate environmental toxicants declines, creating a vicious cycle of metal accumulation and further kidney damage
- **Arsenic**: Increases ROS production, activates MAPK/NF-kB pathways, enhances myeloperoxidase activity, induces apoptosis through pathways involving decreased Bcl-2/Bcl-xl expression
- **Cadmium**: Increases CKD risk from 10% to 25% in exposed individuals; impairs electron transport chain complexes II/III; induces ER stress and disrupts autophagy; blood Cd >1 mcg/L associated with CKD and albuminuria
- **Mercury**: Disrupts mitochondrial membrane potential, triggers oxidative stress, causes cytoskeletal alterations, inhibits Na+/H+ exchangers and aquaporin-1; kidneys with reduced renal mass are more susceptible

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive narrative review (24 pages, 193 references)
- **Structural alterations covered**: Podocyte injury (effacement, detachment), capillary rarefaction, tubular epithelial cell damage
- **Intracellular mechanisms**: Mitochondrial biogenesis (PGC-1alpha pathway), oxidative phosphorylation, autophagy (mTOR, AMPK, sirtuin 1), ER stress (UPR/GRP78 pathway)
- **Carbamylation**: Urea buildup leads to isocyanic acid formation, causing irreversible protein modifications on lysine residues and N-terminal amino groups
- **Ferroptosis**: Iron-dependent phospholipid peroxidation in renal tubular cells; GPX4 loss of function as key trigger; iron-restricted diet protective in animal models
- **DNA damage**: Dysfunctional cell cycle proteins (p53, p21), senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP), activation of NF-kB
- **Epigenetics**: DNA hypomethylation of klotho promoter by TGF-beta; miR-192 upregulation leading to TGF-beta/Smad3 activation and fibrosis
- **Fibrosis**: Epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition via ILEI/Akt/ERK pathways; PDGFR-beta as therapeutic target
