# Draper 2018 — Menstrual Cycle Rhythmicity: Metabolic Patterns in Healthy Women

## Key Findings

- **208 of 397 metabolites** (263 plasma, 114 urine, 19 clinical/vitamin) significantly changed across 5 menstrual cycle phases (p<0.05), with 71 reaching FDR<0.20 ([[draper-2018-menstrual-cycle-metabolomics]], cross-sectional, n=34).
- **39 amino acids and derivatives** and **18 lipid species** decreased in the luteal phase — indicative of an anabolic state during the progesterone peak ([[draper-2018-menstrual-cycle-metabolomics]]).
- **Ornithine, arginine, alanine, glycine, methionine, and proline** were significantly reduced in all 5 luteal-phase contrasts; after FDR correction, 19 amino acids met q<0.20 for luteal-follicular comparisons ([[draper-2018-menstrual-cycle-metabolomics]]).
- **Glutathione metabolism** showed significant cycle rhythmicity — total glutathione was statistically significant for luteal-follicular contrasts ([[draper-2018-menstrual-cycle-metabolomics]]).
- **4-pyridoxic acid** (vitamin B6 metabolite) and **25-OH vitamin D** showed significant cyclic variation ([[draper-2018-menstrual-cycle-metabolomics]]).
- The reduced metabolite levels in luteal phase represent a **window of vulnerability** to hormone-related health issues such as PMS and PMDD ([[draper-2018-menstrual-cycle-metabolomics]]).

## Methods

34 healthy premenopausal women (BMI 22.9±3.5, age 26.6±5.9 years). 4 blood and urine samples per individual timed to 5 cycle phases (menstrual, follicular, periovulatory, luteal, pre-menstrual) using serum hormone measurements (LH, FSH, estradiol, progesterone). LC-MS/GC-MS metabolomics (263 plasma + 114 urine); HPLC-FLD for B vitamins; serum clinical chemistries. Phase comparisons with FDR correction (q<0.20). Pathway connectivity analysis linking urea cycle, 1-carbon metabolism, glutathione metabolism, and citric acid cycle.
