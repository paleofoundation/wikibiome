# Graves' Disease: Is It Time for Targeted Therapy? A Narrative Review

## Key Claims

- Current antithyroid drug therapy for [[Graves' disease]] does not address the underlying autoimmune pathology, leading to high relapse rates (40-60%); targeted immunotherapies may offer disease-modifying potential
- Anti-CD20 therapy (rituximab) for B-cell depletion has shown promise in reducing TRAb levels and achieving sustained remission in GD, though results are variable and evidence limited to small trials
- TSH receptor (TSHR)-targeted therapies including small-molecule TSHR antagonists and TSHR-blocking antibodies represent the most disease-specific approach, directly addressing the pathogenic autoantibody mechanism
- Anti-neonatal Fc receptor (FcRn) inhibitors that accelerate IgG clearance offer a novel mechanism to reduce circulating TRAb without broad immunosuppression
- [[JAK/STAT pathway]] inhibitors (baricitinib, tofacitinib) show potential for GD and Graves' orbitopathy by blocking cytokine signaling pathways central to autoimmune [[inflammation]]

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review of emerging targeted therapies for GD
- **Rituximab (anti-CD20)**: Several case series and small trials; reduced TRAb in some patients; more effective early in disease course; risk of infusion reactions and hypogammaglobulinemia; not consistently effective for established GD
- **TSHR antagonists**: Small molecules (e.g., K1-70, ANTAG3) in preclinical and early clinical development; directly block TRAb stimulation of TSHR; highly specific but early-stage
- **Anti-FcRn inhibitors**: Efgartigimod and rozanolixizumab reduce total IgG including TRAb by blocking IgG recycling; potential for rapid TRAb reduction; clinical trials in Graves' orbitopathy underway
- **JAK inhibitors**: Baricitinib (JAK1/2) and tofacitinib (JAK1/3) modulate Th1/Th17 inflammatory responses; case reports of GD improvement; formal trials needed
- **Anti-BAFF/APRIL**: Belimumab targeting B-cell activating factor may reduce autoantibody production; rationale from SLE and other autoimmune diseases
- **Anti-CD38**: Daratumumab targeting plasma cells could reduce long-lived autoantibody-producing cells; preclinical rationale but no GD clinical data
- **Immune checkpoint considerations**: Checkpoint inhibitor-induced thyroiditis and GD (ipilimumab, nivolumab) demonstrates the delicate balance of immune regulation at the thyroid
- **Combination potential**: Targeted therapies may be combined with ATDs for synergistic immunomodulatory and thyroid-blocking effects
