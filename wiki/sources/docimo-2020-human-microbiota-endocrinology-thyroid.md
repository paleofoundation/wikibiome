# The Human Microbiota in Endocrinology: Implications for Pathophysiology, Treatment, and Prognosis in Thyroid Diseases

## Key Findings

- **Autoimmune thyroid diseases** (ATD) are the most common organ-specific autoimmune disorders with prevalence higher than 5% worldwide; the [[gut-microbiome]] plays a role in thyroid hormone metabolism through bacterial deconjugation of iodothyronine and competition with human albumin for thyroid hormones ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).
- **Mineral absorption mediated by microbiota**: The gut microbiota affects absorption of microelements including [[iodine]], [[copper]], [[iron]], [[selenium]], and [[zinc]] that are vital to thyroid health. Patients with ATD often lack micronutrients including selenium, iodine, and zinc ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).
- **HT microbiota pattern**: A specific subset of gut bacteria with a [[lactobacillus]]-predominant pattern identified in HT patients; [[prevotellaceae|Prevotellaceae]] and Veillonellaceae decreased. [[bifidobacterium]] and [[lactobacillus]] were significantly decreased by real-time PCR ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).
- **GD microbiota**: Decreased Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio; [[prevotella]], [[lactobacillus]], [[bifidobacterium]] reduced in GD patients. Methimazole treatment dramatically increased microbiome diversity after 3-5 months ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).
- **Thyroid cancer microbiota**: Large quantitative and qualitative changes in intestinal microbial flora in thyroid cancer patients with increased [[clostridiaceae|Clostridiaceae]], [[neisseria|Neisseria]], and [[streptococcus|Streptococcus]] ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).

## Methods

- **Study type**: Mini review (published December 4, 2020; 7 pages, 80 references)
- **Scope**: Characterizes microbiota in AITD, evaluates dysbiosis impact on treatment/prognosis, examines microbiome in thyroid cancer
- **Coverage**: Gut microbiome composition, intestinal permeability, molecular mimicry, microbiota-drug interactions, thyroid cancer-microbiome links

## Detailed Summary

**Gut microbiome and thyroid hormone metabolism**: The intestinal microbiota can influence endogenous and exogenous thyroid hormone metabolism. The presence of iodothyronine deiodinase, an enzyme that converts T4 into its active T3 form or into reverse T3, has been found in the intestinal wall, influencing total body T3 levels. Gut bacteria can also deconjugate sulfated iodothyronines, affecting the enterohepatic circulation of thyroid hormones ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).

**HT findings**: HT patients show increased Bacteroides in some studies; Proteobacteria, Actinobacteria, Enterobacteriaceae, and Alcaligenaceae increased compared to controls. A Lactobacillus-predominant pattern was identified, and Prevotellaceae and Veillonellaceae were decreased. Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus were significantly decreased by real-time PCR. The GALT (gut-associated lymphoid tissue) serves as host for 70% of the body's immune tissue ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).

**GD findings**: A predisposing pattern of gut microbiota may be associated with Graves' orbitopathy. The gut microbiome is closely linked to Graves' disease through two main mechanisms: molecular mimicry between bacterial antigens and thyroid self-antigens, and modification of immune cell function. Methimazole treatment dramatically increased microbiome diversity after 3-5 months of therapy ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).

**Mineral-microbiota-thyroid axis**: Patients with intestinal and thyroid health problems are often depleted of mineral [[selenium]]. Hypothyroidism can hinder the gallbladder's bile flow, further affecting T4/T3 conversion. Bile also has natural antibacterial properties, linking thyroid diseases to specific bacterial gut infections such as SIBO. The microbiota processes and breaks down food for better nutrient absorption, including absorption of trace minerals crucial for thyroid function ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).

**Probiotic potential**: *Lactobacillus reuteri* shown in mice to modulate Tregs and suppress autoimmune thyroiditis. Probiotic supplementation in thyroid patients remains an area of active investigation. The interplay between the thyroid gland and the gut has emerged as a pivotal area of interest in endocrinology ([[docimo-2020-human-microbiota-endocrinology-thyroid]], expert-opinion).

## Relevance to Cureva

- Lactobacillus reuteri as Treg modulator provides triangle evidence for probiotic interventions in HT
- Methimazole increasing microbiome diversity suggests conventional treatment has beneficial microbiome effects — relevant to clinical reasoning
- Selenium depletion in ATD + microbiota-mediated absorption impairment suggests combined selenium + probiotic intervention hypothesis
- Bile flow impairment in hypothyroidism creating SIBO susceptibility is a STOP signal for approaches that don't address biliary function

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The evidence level is limited to animal models and observational studies; no RCT data on probiotic interventions for thyroid disease are presented in this review
- The claim of Lactobacillus-predominant pattern in HT appears to conflict with other studies showing Lactobacillus depletion

## Open Questions

- Whether microbiota-directed interventions can reduce the need for levothyroxine dose adjustments in HT patients
- The specific bacterial species responsible for thyroid hormone deconjugation and whether they could be targeted therapeutically
- Whether restoring bile flow in hypothyroid patients could independently improve gut microbiome composition
