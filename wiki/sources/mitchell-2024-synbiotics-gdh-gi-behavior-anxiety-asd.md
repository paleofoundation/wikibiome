# Key Claims

- **Both synbiotics (SYN) and combined synbiotics + gut-directed hypnotherapy (COM)** significantly reduced total GI scores post-intervention and at follow-up
- **COM group** showed beneficial reductions in anxiety scores (p=0.002) and irritability behaviours (p<0.001) not present in SYN group
- **Only COM group** maintained significant reductions in GI pain scores at follow-up (p<0.001)
- **Significant changes in gut microbiota** including increases in Bifidobacterium animalis and Dialister in both groups over time
- Synbiotics with or without GDH may help support standard care for autistic children with comorbid DGBI

# Methods & Findings

**Open-label, randomised pilot study** (n=40) of children with ASD aged 5-10.99 years.

- 12-week intervention of synbiotics (prebiotic + probiotic) +/- GDH with follow-up at 24 weeks
- Randomized 1:1 to synbiotics only (SYN) vs synbiotics + GDH (COM)
- Both groups experienced GI symptom reductions
- COM group showed additional benefits for anxiety and irritability
- Microbiome changes observed in both groups

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Combined gut-brain interventions** (synbiotics + hypnotherapy) more effective than gut-only approaches for behavioral outcomes
- **Bifidobacterium animalis** increase suggests restoration of beneficial commensals
- **GI-behavior-anxiety triad** in ASD addressed through multimodal intervention
