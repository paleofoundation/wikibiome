## Key Findings

- **Major bioactive compounds** (GC-MS): thymoquinone (40.23%), thymol (17.23%), o-xylene (6.90%), ethylbenzene (6.15%), oleic acid (6.60%), ethylenimine (5.50%), 1,2-diphenylethylamine (7.51%), hexadecanoic acid, tetradecanoic acid, linoleic acid.
- **Antibacterial inhibition zones** (agar well diffusion):
  - *P. aeruginosa* — 15.1±1.0 mm
  - *E. coli* — 14.3±1.3 mm
  - *S. typhi* — 14.0±0.6 mm
  - *S. aureus* — 13.7±0.3 mm
  - *Enterobacter* — 12.6±0.9 mm
  - *B. subtilis* — 11.5±1.0 mm
- **Antifungal** activity vs *Aspergillus niger*, *Penicillium*, *Candida albicans* (concentration 2.0 mg/mL).
- **Antiprotozoal**: dose-dependent cytotoxicity vs both promastigote and amastigote forms of *Leishmania tropica* KWH23.
- **Synergy with β-lactams and fluoroquinolones** (ampicillin, ceftriaxone, ciprofloxacin, meropenem) — clinical relevance for MDR infections.
- **Anti-virulence mechanism**: thymoquinone and thymol predicted by docking to bind RhlG (NADP active-site), LasR (PDB 2UV0 2.30 Å), PqsR (PDB 4JVD) — key P. aeruginosa quorum-sensing regulators.

## Methods

N. sativa seeds from Peshawar local market. 160 g ground seed extracted in 1000 mL ethanol 48 h, filtered, rotary-evaporated at 45°C. FTIR (400-4000 cm-1), GC (Agilent 5977B) with NIST library match. Well diffusion in nutrient/TSB/skim agar vs bacterial panel. Antibiotic synergy via disc diffusion overlay. In silico docking with MOE software. DPPH at 517 nm for antioxidant (IC50 at 400 µg/mL).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- GC vs GC-MS ambiguity: some reported "compounds" (ethylenimine, ethylbenzene) may be artifacts or contaminants of extraction solvents.
- Only in vitro and in silico data; no in vivo validation.

## Open Questions

- Relative contribution of thymoquinone vs thymol to observed biofilm disruption.
- Whether antibiotic synergy translates to clinical MDR treatment regimens.
- Mechanism of Leishmania killing — possibly oxidative stress via thymoquinone.
