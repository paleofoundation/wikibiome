# Key Claims

- **Rising incidence of autoimmune diseases parallels widespread antibiotic use**, suggesting causal link
- **Gut microbiome is key for immune system development and maturation** and normal immune function
- **Antibiotics may facilitate autoimmunity** through gut [[dysbiosis]] and disrupted immune tolerance
- **FMT being studied** in clinical trials for several autoimmune diseases
- **[[Probiotics]] and prebiotics** showing promising results in preclinical studies for autoimmune disease

# Methods & Findings

Invited review of mechanistic crosstalk between microbiome, immune system, and antibiotics:

- IBD and T1D used as example autoimmune diseases
- Antibiotic-dysbiosis-autoimmunity cascade described
- Probiotic and prebiotic therapies reviewed as potential countermeasures

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Antibiotic disruption of zinc-absorbing bacteria creates secondary zinc deficiency that impairs immune tolerance
- **[[iron]]**: Antibiotics alter iron-dependent microbial communities, changing the competitive landscape toward autoimmunity-promoting organisms
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent immune regulation is disrupted by antibiotic-induced [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Antibiotics represent the most dramatic perturbation of the [[gut-metal-microbiome]]
- **[[probiotics]]**: Post-antibiotic probiotic restoration should include metal cofactor supplementation
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Antibiotic disruption of [[immune-balance]] operates through metal-microbiome mediated pathways
