# Key Claims

- **Age is a critical factor**: children older than 2.9 years had higher fraction of butyrate-producing Clostridium clusters IV and XIVa
- **Bacilli (notably streptococci) and Bacteroidetes higher** in diabetic children vs controls
- **Clostridium clusters IV and XIVa higher in healthy controls** -- butyrate-producing species appear pivotal
- **Diabetic children older than 2.9 years** could be differentiated by increased microbial diversity (paradoxically)
- **Non-diabetic children have more balanced microbiota** in which butyrate-producing species hold pivotal position

# Methods & Findings

Human Intestinal Tract Chip (HITChip) microarray analysis of children aged 1-5 years with new-onset T1D vs age-matched controls:

- Deep global analysis by phylogenetic microarray
- Principal component analyses highlighted importance of age when comparing age-matched pairs
- Butyrate production capacity is the key differentiating factor
