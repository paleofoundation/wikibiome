# Combined Phage and Antibiotic Therapy for PD Peritonitis

## Key Findings

- **Combined bacteriophage and antibiotic therapy successfully resolved** refractory peritoneal dialysis-related peritonitis caused by multidrug-resistant [[klebsiella-pneumoniae]] in a CKD patient.
- **Conventional antibiotic regimens had failed** to clear the infection, placing the patient at risk of catheter removal and transition to hemodialysis — a significant clinical setback for PD-dependent patients.
- **The patient retained the PD catheter** after phage-antibiotic combination therapy, preserving peritoneal dialysis access and avoiding the complications and quality-of-life reduction associated with hemodialysis.
- This represents one of the first published cases of phage therapy applied to PD peritonitis, expanding the clinical settings in which phage-antibiotic synergy has been demonstrated.

## Methods

- **Design**: Single case report published in Nature Communications.
- **Patient**: CKD patient on peritoneal dialysis who developed refractory peritonitis caused by multidrug-resistant Klebsiella pneumoniae.
- **Intervention**: Lytic bacteriophage(s) targeting K. pneumoniae administered in combination with antibiotics after failure of conventional antibiotic regimens.
- **Outcome measures**: Peritoneal fluid culture clearance, clinical symptom resolution, catheter retention.

## Detailed Summary

PD-related peritonitis is a leading cause of peritoneal dialysis technique failure and patient morbidity in [[chronic-kidney-disease]]. When caused by multidrug-resistant organisms such as [[klebsiella-pneumoniae]], conventional antibiotic therapy may fail, necessitating catheter removal and transition to hemodialysis. This case report describes the successful deployment of combined bacteriophage and antibiotic therapy in a patient with refractory PD peritonitis. The phages were selected for lytic activity against the patient's specific K. pneumoniae isolate, and synergy with antibiotics was exploited to overcome the resistance mechanisms that had rendered conventional therapy ineffective. The successful outcome — infection clearance with catheter preservation — demonstrates the potential of phage therapy as a rescue strategy in nephrology settings where antimicrobial resistance limits treatment options.

## Open Questions

- Whether phage therapy for PD peritonitis can be standardized or whether each case requires individualized phage selection.
- Long-term outcomes including reinfection rates and peritoneal membrane integrity after phage therapy.
- Whether prophylactic phage cocktails could prevent PD peritonitis in high-risk CKD patients.
