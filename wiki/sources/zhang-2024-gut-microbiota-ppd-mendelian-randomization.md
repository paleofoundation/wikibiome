# Key Claims

- **Phylum Actinobacteria** (P=0.014, OR=0.971) has a protective effect on [[postpartum-depression]]
- **Genus Holdemanella** (P=0.023, OR=0.979) also protects against PPD
- Two unknown genera also showed protective causal associations with PPD
- Sensitivity analysis confirmed **no heterogeneity or horizontal pleiotropy**

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Two-sample Mendelian randomization using GWAS data from MiBioGen database and UK Biobank PPD data
- **Statistical methods**: Inverse variance weighted (IVW), MR-Egger, weighted median, weighted model, MR-PRESSO
- **Data**: 211 taxa (9 phylum, 16 orders, 20 families, 35 families, 131 genera) from 18,473 individuals; PPD data from 4834 cases and 33,173 controls
- **Key implication**: Causal link supports dietary and microbiome interventions targeting Actinobacteria enrichment for PPD prevention

# Connections

- Causal evidence strengthens the [[gut-brain-axis]] hypothesis in PPD pathogenesis
- Actinobacteria (including *Bifidobacterium*) depletion also observed in [[dysbiosis]] patterns of other neuropsychiatric conditions
- Supports microbiome-targeted interventions relevant to [[inflammation]] modulation in perinatal period
- Complements observational studies like [[zhou-2020-fecal-microbiota-ppd]]
