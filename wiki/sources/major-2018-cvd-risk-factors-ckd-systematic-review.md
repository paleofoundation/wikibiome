# Key Claims

- CKD affects up to 15% of adult populations and is independently associated with increased CVD risk comparable to diabetes or coronary heart disease
- Traditional CV risk factors (age, male gender, smoking, established CVD, diabetes, increasing total cholesterol) were all statistically significantly associated with increased CV event risk in CKD populations
- Systolic and diastolic blood pressures were **not** associated with increased CV event risk in CKD, contrary to general population findings
- Non-traditional risk factors identified for CKD-specific CV prognostic models: **albumin** (pooled HR 0.62 per g/dL increase, p<0.001), **haemoglobin** (HR 0.90 per g/dL, p<0.001), **phosphate** (HR 1.20 per mg/dL, p=0.005), **urate** (HR 1.07 per mg/dL, p=0.004)
- Left ventricular hypertrophy on echocardiogram was strongly associated with CV events (pooled HR 1.78, 95% CI 1.35-2.35, p<0.001)
- The relative role of atherosclerosis in CV outcomes diminishes in CKD, replaced by uremia-related risk factors (arteriosclerosis, reduced vascular compliance)
- 21 cohorts with 27,465 individuals and 100,838 person-years of follow-up were included

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO registered)
- **Search**: MEDLINE and Embase for observational cohorts and RCTs; CKD stage 3a or worse (eGFR <60 ml/min/1.73m2)
- **Primary outcome**: Composite CV events (acute coronary syndrome, CHF, ischemic stroke)
- **66 potential risk factors** identified; 29 routinely collected included in quantitative synthesis; 20 meta-analyzed
- **Heterogeneity**: Substantial for albumin (I2=66.4%), urate (I2=78.3%), and LVH (I2=72.1%)
- **Key limitation**: Most models had medium-to-high risk of bias; only 14.3% adjusted for all established core CV risk factors
