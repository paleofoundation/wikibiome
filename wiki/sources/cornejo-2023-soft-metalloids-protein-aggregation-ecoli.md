# Soft-Metalloids Induce Protein Aggregation in E. coli

## Key Findings

- **ROS-independent metal toxicity**: Soft metalloids (arsenic, cadmium, mercury, gold) cause protein aggregation in *E. coli* through a mechanism **independent of reactive oxygen species** — a distinct proteotoxicity pathway [[cornejo-2023-soft-metalloids-protein-aggregation-ecoli]], in-vitro.
- **Metal → misfolding → aggregation**: These metals bind to protein thiol groups ([[cysteine]] residues), displacing correct metal cofactors and causing misfolding → aggregation. This is [[mis-metallation]] at the proteome level.
- **Parallels neurodegeneration**: Metal-induced protein aggregation in bacteria parallels amyloid/α-synuclein aggregation in neurodegeneration — suggesting a conserved toxicity mechanism from bacteria to neurons.
