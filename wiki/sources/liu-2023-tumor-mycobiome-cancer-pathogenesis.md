# Liu 2023 -- Tumor Mycobiome Influence on Cancer Pathogenesis

## Key Claims

- The tumor mycobiome promotes tumor progression and metastasis by affecting the immune system, maintaining a pro-inflammatory environment, producing aflatoxins, attenuating cell adhesion mechanisms, and through fungal-bacterial interactions
- *Aspergillus rambellii* was the most abundant fungal species identified in CRC across eight cohorts; it promotes CRC cell growth in vitro and in vivo through aflatoxin production (aflatoxin B, aflatoxin G) activating Toll-like receptors on macrophages and dendritic cells
- *Candida* increases inflammation (IL-1 pathway, neutrophil infiltration) and promotes pro-inflammatory environment, leading to vicious cycle of colonization and chronic inflammation; *Candida*-dominant tumors show enhanced IL-1beta and CARD9 signaling
- *Malassezia* can activate NLR family pyrin domain 9 (CARD9) signaling and accelerate IL-1beta production via Dectin2/CARD9 to exacerbate inflammation; may also induce DNA lesion accumulation
- Tumor fungal-bacterial biome interactions promote CRC carcinogenesis through upregulation of D-arginine and D-ornithine metabolic pathways and butanoate metabolic pathway (*bdhA* and *bdhB* gene expression)
