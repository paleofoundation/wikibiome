## Key Claims

- Total [[aluminum]] concentration in 35 Brazilian infant cereal samples ranged from 197 to 1852 ug/kg, with multicereals and fruit-based cereals presenting the highest values (8.82 and 7.49 mg/kg respectively)
- [[metal-homeostasis|Bioaccessibility]] of Al ranged from 1.5% to 10.4%, generally lower than in non-cereal infant foods
- The intake of Al through cereals contributes up to 10.48% of the PTWI (2 mg/kg bw/week), when considering total Al concentrations
- Corn and rice flour cereals had the lowest Al concentrations (0.92 mg/kg and 1.09 mg/kg respectively)
- Fruit-based cereals (banana and apple) from brand A had lowest bioaccessibility (3.4%), possibly due to lower polyphenol content reducing Al interaction
- Plum-containing cereals (brand B) had highest total Al (1852 ug/kg) but relatively low bioaccessibility (4.7%)
- Bioaccessibility differences were observed by brand, type of cereal, and flour composition

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: 35 cereal-based infant food samples from Campinas, SP, Brazil; 3 brands (A, B, C); compositions including multicereals, fruit-based, rice flour, corn flour, and rice flour + oat
- **Analysis**: Total Al by ICP-OES after closed microwave-assisted digestion; LOD 53 ug/kg, LOQ 89 ug/kg; CRM recovery 87-97%
- **Total Al (Table 3)**: Brand A multicereals 938 ug/kg, fruit (banana/apple) 326 ug/kg, rice flour 197 ug/kg; Brand B multicereals 1034 ug/kg, fruit (plum) 1852 ug/kg, rice flour 1185 ug/kg; Brand C multicereals 1028 ug/kg, rice flour 757 ug/kg
- **Bioaccessibility**: Rice flour from brand A: 14.0 +/- 1.0 ug/kg (1.5%); corn flour from brand A: 36.1 +/- 11 ug/kg (10.4%); multicereals ranged 2.2-6.2%
- **PTWI contribution (Table 4)**: Multicereals 3.46-10.48% of PTWI; rice flour 1.91-5.59%; fruit-based 3.60-6.67%; corn flour 1.30-6.84%
- **Comparison with literature**: Total Al content lower than reported for Turkish infant cereals (0.92-8.82 mg/kg) and Ghanaian infant cereals (2.89-11.07 mg/kg)
