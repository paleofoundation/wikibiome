# Key Claims

- The intestinal microbiota is a critical environmental factor influencing nutrient metabolism, immune responses, and host health in IBD including [[crohns-disease]]
- [[dysbiosis]] in IBD is characterized by decreased [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]], reduced Clostridium groups IV and XIVa, and increased adherent-invasive [[escherichia-coli]]
- Environmental factors including diet, stress, and antibiotics alter the microbiota-host immune interaction and contribute to IBD pathogenesis
- Therapeutic strategies targeting the intestinal micro-ecosystem (FMT, [[probiotics]], prebiotics) show promise for IBD management
- The intestinal barrier formed by epithelial cells provides the first line of defense, with Paneth cells, goblet cells, and immune cells all contributing to homeostasis

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of microbiota-immune crosstalk in IBD
- **Key immune mechanisms**: TLR signaling, NOD2 pathways, Th1/Th17 imbalance, reduced Treg function, impaired IgA secretion
- **Barrier dysfunction**: Increased intestinal permeability, reduced mucin production, decreased defensin expression
- **Dietary factors**: High-fat diet promotes Proteobacteria; fiber increases SCFA-producing commensals
- **Stress effects**: CRF signaling activates mast cells, increases intestinal permeability, alters microbiota composition
- **Therapeutic approaches**: FMT, [[probiotics]] (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Saccharomyces), prebiotics

# Connections

- [[bacteroides-fragilis]] polysaccharide A suppresses IL-17 and enhances IL-10 production, preventing colitis
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] restoration after disease recurrence associated with maintained remission
- Adherent-invasive [[escherichia-coli]] colonizes ileal mucosa in CD, linking to [[inflammation]] through barrier invasion
- Diet-microbiota-immune axis relevant to [[gut-metal-microbiome]] interactions through mineral bioavailability effects
- [[ferroptosis]] pathway may be modulated by oxidative stress from dysbiotic microbiota
