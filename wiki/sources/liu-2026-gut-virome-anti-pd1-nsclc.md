# Key Claims

- Gut virome undergoes marked remodeling during anti-PD-1 therapy in advanced NSCLC, with viral Shannon diversity decreasing progressively with poorer clinical response
- A virus-only random forest model outperformed a bacterium-only model for predicting immunotherapy response (AUC 0.768 vs. 0.664), maintaining superior performance in external validation (AUC 0.742)
- Non-responder enriched vOTUs were predominantly from Peduoviridae and Inoviridae families, while responder-enriched vOTUs were mainly from Herelleviridae and Microviridae
- Akkermansia muciniphila positivity was associated with a higher-diversity, responder-favorable virome configuration

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Metagenomic virome profiling of 338 advanced NSCLC patients treated with PD-1 inhibitors; external validation cohort n=30
- **Virome diversity**: Shannon index significantly lower in PD (progressive disease) vs R (responders); PERMANOVA confirmed significant compositional differences (p<0.05)
- **Differential vOTUs**: 194 NR-enriched and 594 R-enriched vOTUs identified; Crevaviridae was the only viral family significantly different across both R vs SD and R vs PD comparisons
- **Host prediction**: NR-enriched vOTUs targeted Clostridium_M, Bacteroides, Escherichia; R-enriched targeted Faecalibacterium and Roseburia
- **Functional profiling**: NR-enriched vOTUs associated with metabolic functions including enolase (K01689); R-enriched function was cell-wall hydrolase (K01449) suggesting phage-encoded lytic activity
- **Virus-bacteria networks**: Response-specific interaction modules identified; R network denser with higher connectivity
