# Key Claims

- **T1D microbiota characterized by decreased butyrate production** and bile acid metabolism, and increased lipopolysaccharide biosynthesis
- **Combination of 18 bacterial species and fecal metabolites** provided excellent discriminatory power for T1D
- **Gut microbiota from T1D children** induced elevated fasting glucose and declined insulin sensitivity in antibiotic-treated mice
- **Butyrate exerted protective effects** on islet structure and function; lipopolysaccharide aggravated pancreatic inflammatory response
- **Butyrate activated Insulin1 and Insulin2 gene expression** in streptozotocin-induced T1D mice

# Methods & Findings

Integrative profiling in independent cohorts of children with new-onset T1D:

- Metagenomic and metaproteomic analyses
- FMT from T1D children to antibiotic-treated mice reproduced metabolic phenotype
- Butyrate supplementation protected islet function in STZ-induced T1D mice

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Butyrate activation of insulin genes requires zinc-dependent transcription factor machinery in beta-cells
- **[[iron]]**: LPS biosynthesis increase reflects iron-dependent pathogenic pathway upregulation in T1D microbiome
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium protects beta-cells from LPS-induced inflammatory damage described in this study
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: The 18-species discriminatory panel may reflect distinct metal-dependent metabolic niches
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Functional [[dysbiosis]] (butyrate down, LPS up) is directly connected to metal-dependent metabolic pathways
- **[[immune-balance]]**: LPS-driven inflammation disrupts metal-dependent immune tolerance to beta-cells
