# Key Claims

- **Mental health** is determined by complex interplay between the Neurological Exposome and the Human Genome
- **Diet and gut microbiota** play a crucial role in brain development and functioning; modulating the gut-brain axis may protect against neurodevelopmental disorders (NDDs)
- **Nutritional deficiencies and gut microbiota alterations** have been linked to NDDs including ASD
- **Precision Nutrition Models** in Psychiatry and Neuropediatrics classify subjects according to diet response prediction based on microbiome signatures
- **Certain dietary interventions** have emerged as promising alternatives for managing particular NDDs in specific patient subsets

# Methods & Findings

**Review** of early life environmental factors including diet and gut microbiota on neuro-development.

- Summarized mechanisms underlying diet and gut microbiota influence on the brain-gut axis
- Described evidence supporting the key role of gut microbiota, diet, and nutrition in neurodevelopment
- Evaluated effectiveness of dietary and microbiome-based interventions
- Proposed precision nutrition approaches based on individual microbiome profiles
- Emphasized the importance of early life critical windows for intervention

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Precision nutrition** based on microbiome signatures as personalized ASD intervention strategy
- **Exposome concept** integrates dietary, microbial, and environmental factors in ASD etiology
- **Tailored interventions** rather than one-size-fits-all approach for ASD dietary treatment
