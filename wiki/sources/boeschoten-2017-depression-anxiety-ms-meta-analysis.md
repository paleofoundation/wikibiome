# Prevalence of Depression and Anxiety in Multiple Sclerosis: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis of 58 articles (87,756 MS patients) provides pooled prevalence estimates for depression and anxiety in [[multiple-sclerosis]]. It explores sources of heterogeneity including assessment method, prevalence period, study quality, recruitment resource, and region.

## Key Findings

- **Depression prevalence**: 30.5% (95% CI 26.3-35.1%)
- **Anxiety prevalence**: 22.1% (95% CI 15.2-31.0%)
- Prevalence of clinically significant depressive or anxiety *symptoms* was higher (35% and 34%) compared with *disorders* (21% and 10%)
- Depression prevalence was relatively lower in European studies
- Anxiety disorder was more prevalent in community-based samples

### Depression Subgroup Analysis
- Current depressive disorder (0-12 months): 16.8% (95% CI 9.5-27.9%)
- Lifetime depressive disorder: 22.9% (95% CI 15.3-33.0%)
- Current depressive disorder assessed by structured interviews: 20.4% (95% CI 12.5-31.6%)
- BDI-based assessment: highest rates at 47.8% (95% CI 29.6-66.7%)

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

The high prevalence of depression and anxiety in MS is directly relevant to the [[gut-brain-axis]] hypothesis. The [[gut-microbiome]] modulates neurotransmitter production (serotonin, GABA, dopamine) and the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, both central to mood regulation. [[dysbiosis]] in MS may contribute to both neurological disability and psychiatric [[comorbidities]] through shared pathways including [[neuroinflammation]], altered [[tryptophan-metabolism]] metabolism via the kynurenine pathway, and reduced [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] production. [[heavy-metals]] exposure is independently associated with depression risk and could compound these effects through [[gut-microbiome]] disruption.

## Limitations

- High heterogeneity across studies (I-squared >= 98)
- Does not address biological mechanisms underlying psychiatric comorbidity
- Assessment methods varied considerably
