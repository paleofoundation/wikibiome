# Key Claims

- Ferrichrome, a siderophore derived from *Lactobacillus casei* [[probiotics]], inhibits growth of [[pancreatic-cancer]] cells including 5-FU-resistant lines via p53 activation and cell cycle arrest
- Ferrichrome induced apoptosis in pancreatic cancer cells through DNA fragmentation, PARP cleavage, and dysregulation of the cell cycle by activating p53
- The tumor-suppressive effects of ferrichrome were effective in 5-FU-resistant SUIT-2 cells at the same concentration as in original cells, suggesting ability to overcome drug resistance
- In vivo, ferrichrome (10 mg/kg) significantly reduced tumor volume and weight in mouse xenograft models

# Methods/Findings

- **Cell lines tested**: SUIT-2, PANC-1, MIA PaCa-II, PK-1, PCI-43; growth suppression demonstrated across all lines
- **Cell cycle effects**: Ferrichrome accumulated cells in G1 phase (36.16% vs 25.97% in control) and eliminated G2-M phase entry (0% vs 11.80%)
- **p53 pathway**: Western blotting showed increased phospho-p53, decreased securin, decreased cyclin B1, increased CDKN1B (p27), decreased cyclin D1
- **5-FU resistance**: FUR SUIT-2 cells resistant to 3 microg/mL 5-FU but inhibited by 1 microg/mL ferrichrome to the same extent as original cells
- **In vivo xenograft**: Tumor volume significantly reduced from day 4 onwards; tumor weight significantly decreased compared to PBS controls (n=5)
- **Transcriptome**: p53-associated mRNAs significantly altered by ferrichrome; 30 mRNAs directly regulated by activated p53; DDIT3 (DNA damage inducible transcript 3) identified
- **Combination therapy**: Ferrichrome (0.2 microg/mL) combined with 5-FU (0.2 microg/mL) showed synergistic growth suppression
