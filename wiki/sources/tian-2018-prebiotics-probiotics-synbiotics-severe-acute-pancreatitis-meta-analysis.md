# Key Claims

- Supplemented use of [[prebiotics]], [[probiotics]], and [[synbiotics]] effectively shortened hospital stay in severe acute [[pancreatitis]] patients (6 RCTs, MD = -5.57 days, 95% CI: -8.21 to -2.93, P < 0.001)
- A promising trend toward reduced infected pancreatic necrosis (RR = 0.83, 95% CI: 0.59-1.18) and mortality (RR = 0.79, 95% CI: 0.46-1.37) was observed but did not reach statistical significance
- 13 RCTs comprising 950 patients were included, all conducted in Chinese SAP cohorts
- Subgroup analysis by intervention category (pre-, pro-, or synbiotics alone) also confirmed reduced hospital stay

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of 13 RCTs (PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane) comparing pre-/pro-/synbiotics + enteral nutrition vs standard enteral nutrition in predicted SAP
- **Included studies**: 950 total patients; published 2007-2017; sample sizes from 22 to 296; treatment durations from 7 to 28 days
- **Probiotic strains used**: 1-6 strains per study; most common: *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Enterococcus*, *Bacillus*; doses ranged from 10^7 to 10^10 CFU
- **Primary outcomes**: Infected pancreatic necrosis (10 trials), mortality (11 trials), total infection (4 trials), length of hospital stay (6 trials)
- **Hospital stay**: Significant reduction with bionutrition supplementation (MD = -5.57 days); consistent across subgroup analyses
- **Safety**: One major study (Besselink et al., 2008 PROPATRIA trial) initially raised safety concerns but subsequent analysis found no negative association in experimental AP models
