# Zou 2024 -- Multi-omic Profiling of Microbiome, Genome, and Transcriptome in CRC

## Key Claims

- Identified 22 microbial gut species associated with CRC from matched tumor/normal mucosa and stool of 41 CRC patients using integrated metagenomics, exome sequencing, and transcriptomics
- Four significantly mutated genes (TP53, APC, KRAS, SMAD4) were associated with cancer-related microbes; *Fusobacterium nucleatum* positively correlated with different host metabolic pathways
- *F. nucleatum* modified the tumor immune environment by TNFSF9 gene expression, establishing a mechanistic link between a specific bacterium and host gene regulation
- CRC-enriched species included *Bacteroides fragilis*, *Parabacteroides unclassified*, *Prevotella intermedia*, *Clostridium bolteae/symbiosum*; control-enriched species included *Eubacterium hallii*, *Roseburia intestinalis*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*
- Bacteria-related metabolic pathways enriched in CRC: one carbon pool by folate, oxidative phosphorylation, carbon metabolism; control-enriched: tetracycline biosynthesis, photosynthesis, ABC transporters

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Multi-omic study of 41 CRC patients from Sun Yat-sen University; matched stool and tissue samples
- **Methods**: Shotgun metagenomics (MetaPhlAn2 for taxonomy, HUManN2 for function), exome sequencing (MutSigCV for mutations), RNA-seq transcriptomics
- **Key finding**: Unsupervised clustering revealed four community types (C1-C4) with distinct clinical associations; tumor location (colon vs rectum) linked to C4 community type dominated by beneficial species
