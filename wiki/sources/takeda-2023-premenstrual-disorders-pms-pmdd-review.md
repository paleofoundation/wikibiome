# Takeda 2023 — Premenstrual Disorders: PMS and PMDD (Review)

## Key Findings

- **PMS prevalence 20–30%**, **PMDD 1.2–6.4%** worldwide; only 11.5% of physicians perform the recommended prospective two-cycle symptom diary ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]], review).
- **68% of diagnosed PMDD cases** used a diagnostic method other than prospective daily diary — significant diagnostic misclassification ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]]).
- **Etiology:** ALLO sensitivity (not abnormal hormone levels), serotonergic dysfunction (low serotonin in luteal phase, tryptophan removal causes premenstrual symptoms), GABA alterations, BDNF changes ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]]).
- **Sepranolone** Phase 2 trial: promising but did not significantly differ from placebo on primary endpoint — further study needed ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]]).
- **Treatments reviewed:** SSRIs (first-line), OCs (drospirenone-containing preferred), GnRH agonists, CBT, vitamin B6 (100mg), calcium carbonate (1200mg), Vitex agnus castus (17-trial meta-analysis), Kampo (Japanese herbal medicine — Kamisyoyosan) ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]]).
- **Gut microbiota link:** References own Takeda 2022 study showing Parabacteroides, Butyricicoccus, and Megasphaera associated with PMDs; Parabacteroides is a GABA-producing bacterium contributing to BDNF production via butyric acid ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]]).
- Complex dietary carbohydrates elevating serum tryptophan in luteal phase improved PMS mood and appetite symptoms in placebo-controlled RCT ([[takeda-2023-premenstrual-disorders-pms-pmdd-review]]).
