# Zhang et al. 2021 -- Ketogenic Diets and Cardio-Metabolic Diseases

## Key Claims

- [[ketogenic-diet]] (KD) therapy is generally promising in [[obesity]], [[cardiovascular-disease|heart failure]], and [[hypertension]], though different voices still exist regarding diabetes and dyslipidemia
- KD is generally protective to obese-related [[cardiovascular-disease]] while remaining contradictory to diabetes and other metabolic disorder-related CVDs
- Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) regulates the PI3K/Akt pathway in cardiomyocytes to prevent apoptosis, and inhibits HDAC2 to influence mitochondrial biogenesis
- In endothelial cells, ketone bodies inhibit mTOR pathway to regulate eNOS, subsequently dilating blood vessels and enhancing vascular function; also activate Nrf2 to enhance antioxidant gene expression
- Very low-calorie KD (VLCKD) in 377 Italian patients significantly lowered SBP in 3 months; a 4-week KD with micronutrient supplementation improved hypertension control

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Comprehensive narrative review
- **KD formulations** (Table 1): Classic 4:1 KD (90% fat, 2% carb), 3:1, 2:1, 1:1, modified Atkins (MAD; 60-65% fat), medium chain triglyceride KD (MCTKD; 70-75% fat), low glycemic index treatment (LGIT; 60% fat)
- **Obesity**: KD efficient at reducing food intake and elevating energy consumption; evidence on body composition changes is mixed (some studies show fat mass reduction, others show no difference)
- **Heart failure**: MCT-KD supplementation to late-adult rats partly restored age-related decrease of SDH activity; KD of 3 weeks was sufficient to rescue cMPC1-/- hearts from decompensation
- **Hypertension**: Most studies show positive effects, though one study in SHRs showed KD for 4 weeks aggravated hypertension and impaired CD31 and eNOS expression
- **Dyslipidemia**: KD shifted lipoproteins from small dense LDL to large buoyant LDL, thus decreasing CVD risks; effects on total cholesterol and triglycerides are inconsistent
- **NAFLD**: LCKB benefits weight loss and metabolic parameters; long-term KDs may lead to NAFLD and systemic glucose intolerance in mice
- **Safety concerns**: Potential for liver inflammation, elevated lipid levels, and cardiac hypertrophy in rodent models
