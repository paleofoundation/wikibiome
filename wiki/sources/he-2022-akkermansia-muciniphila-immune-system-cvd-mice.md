# Akkermansia muciniphila Alters Gut Microbiota and Immune System to Improve Cardiovascular Diseases in Murine Model

## Summary

Original research investigating the effects of *Akkermansia muciniphila* supplementation on gut microbiota and disease-related biomarkers in a CaCl2-induced abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) mouse model. Demonstrates that *A. muciniphila* inhibits AAA formation by restoring gut microbial diversity and modulating peripheral immune factors.

## Key Findings

### Study Design
- C57BL/6J mice divided into 5 groups (n = 10/group): Control, Sham, AAA, AAA + *A. muciniphila* (Am), AAA + Inactivated Am
- AAA model: CaCl2 application on infrarenal aorta for 28 days
- *A. muciniphila* administered by oral gavage (2 x 10^8 CFU/180 microL) daily

### AAA Inhibition
- *A. muciniphila* inhibited aneurysm formation: reduced abdominal aortic diameter in AAA + Am group vs. AAA group (P < 0.05)
- HE staining showed reduced inflammatory cell infiltration in AAA + Am group
- Picrosirius red and Van Gieson staining showed protection of elastic fibers and collagen in the vascular wall

### Microbiome Changes
- AAA group: reduced gut microbial diversity and alpha diversity
- *A. muciniphila* restored microbial diversity and alpha diversity in AAA mice
- Increased abundance of *uncultured bacterium* and *Lactobacillus*; decreased *Lachnospiraceae NK4A136* group in AAA + Am
- At phylum level: *Bacteroidetes* and *Patescibacteria* decreased while *Firmicutes* and *Verrucomicrobia* increased with *A. muciniphila*
- *Bacteroidetes-unclassified bacterium* and *Firmicutes-Lactobacillus* abundances increased; *Firmicutes-Lachnospiraceae NK4A136* decreased

### Immune and Inflammatory Changes
- AAA group: significantly increased MMP-1, MMP-9, IL-33, CTSB, CTSL in tissue and IL-6, IFN-gamma, CRP in blood; decreased IL-4, IL-10, IL-17A
- *A. muciniphila* reversed these changes
- Gut microbiota function prediction: changes in *E. coli*, *Clostridium*, and *Lactobacillus* metabolism-related functional pathways
- *Akkermansia* negatively correlated with *Helicobacter* and *Lactobacillus* and positively correlated with *Clostridium_sensu_stricto_1* and *Escherichia-Shigella*

## Relevance to the Wiki

Provides direct experimental evidence that a single probiotic species (*A. muciniphila*) can modulate gut microbiome composition and peripheral immune responses to inhibit [[cardiovascular-disease]] (AAA formation). Supports the therapeutic potential of microbiome-targeted interventions for CVD prevention, complementing the review-level evidence in [[wang-2022-gut-microbiota-health-cvd-review]] and [[zhu-2023-gut-microbiota-metabolic-pathways-cvd]].
