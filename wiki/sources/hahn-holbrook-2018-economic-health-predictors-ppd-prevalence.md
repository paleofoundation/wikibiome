# Key Claims

- **Global pooled prevalence of PPD**: 17.7% (95% CI: 16.6-18.8%) across 291 studies from 56 countries
- **Significant variation** across nations: 3% (Singapore) to 38% (Chile)
- **Key national predictors**: Income inequality (R2=41%), maternal mortality (R2=19%), infant mortality (R2=16%), and women working >= 40 hours/week (R2=31%)
- Together these factors explain **73% of national variation** in [[postpartum-depression]] prevalence
- Disparities in wealth inequality and maternal-child health factors drive cross-national PPD differences

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis using Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale; random effects meta-analysis and meta-regression with UNICEF, WHO, and World Bank data
- **Key finding**: Economic and social determinants are stronger predictors of PPD prevalence than previously recognized
