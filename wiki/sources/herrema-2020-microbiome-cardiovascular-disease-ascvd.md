# Microbiome and Cardiovascular Disease

## Summary

Book chapter providing a critical overview of the role of the microbiome (gut and oral) in atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD). Covers gut and oral microbiome communities, viruses and bacteriophages, and microbiome-derived metabolites including [[TMAO]], imidazole-propionate, [[SCFAs]], and [[bile-acids]].

## Key Findings

### Gut and Oral Microbiome in ASCVD
- Predominant bacterial phyla: *Bacteroidetes* and *Firmicutes*, with *Actinobacteria*, *Proteobacteria*, and *Verrucomicrobia* less abundant
- ASCVD patients show decreased abundance of gut commensals such as *Bacteroidetes* (incl. *Bacteroides* and *Prevotella*) compared to healthy controls
- ASCVD microbiome enriched in *Enterobacteriaceae* family species, associated with gut dysbiosis and metabolic disease
- Oral *Streptococcus* spp. relatively higher in gut microbiome of ACVD patients vs. controls
- CANTOS trial confirmed role of inflammation (IL-1-beta inhibition) in ASCVD events

### Causal Evidence from Animal Models
- Germ-free ApoE-/- mice: increased atherosclerotic plaques on chow diet vs. conventionally raised counterparts
- On high-fat/high-cholesterol diet: absence of microbiota increased atherogenesis, but less than conventional mice
- FMT from atherosclerotic mice induced atherosclerosis in recipient mice
- Transplantation of pro-inflammatory microbiome into LDLR-/- mice accelerated atherosclerosis

### Microbiome-Derived Metabolites
- **TMAO**: Produced from dietary choline, phosphatidylcholine, L-carnitine by gut microbiota; promotes cholesterol deposition, platelet activation, inflammation
- **Imidazole-propionate**: Produced from histidine by gut bacteria; impairs insulin signaling; elevated in type 2 diabetes
- **SCFAs**: Fermentation products of dietary fiber; mainly acetate, propionate, butyrate; generally cardioprotective
- **Other metabolites**: Phenylacetylglutamine, 4-cresol, indoxyl sulfate associated with ASCVD

### Oral Microbiome
- Over 700 bacterial species in oral cavity
- Periodontitis bacteria associated with "off-site" systemic effects including ASCVD
- Oral bacteria found in atherosclerotic plaques

### Viruses and Bacteriophages
- Upcoming awareness of viral/phage contribution to microbiome community composition and host metabolism
