# Dysregulated tryptophan metabolism: driving T cell subsets and PI3K-Akt pathway alterations in Hashimoto's thyroiditis

## Key Claims

- HT patients have significantly lower serum tryptophan (Trp) levels (p<0.0001), along with decreased lactate (p<0.01) and alanine (p<0.001), compared to healthy controls
- Tryptophan supplementation alleviated thyroid damage and reduced [[inflammation]] factors (TgAb, TPOAb, IL-17) in autoimmune thyroiditis mouse model, while kynurenine pathway inhibition worsened them
- Trp regulates T cell subsets: supplementation decreased Th1 (p<0.00001), Th2 (p<0.00001), and Th17 (p<0.0001) proportions while increasing CD4+CD25+ regulatory T cells (p<0.00001)
- The PI3K-Akt signaling pathway is significantly enriched in HT transcriptomic analysis (NES=-1.939, p=0.0012), with Trp supplementation decreasing p-PI3K and p-Akt protein expression
- The kynurenine (Kyn) pathway, accounting for 95% of Trp metabolism, is initiated by IDO1/TDO enzymes; the IDO1-Kyn-AhR positive feedback loop maintains self-tolerance and suppresses effector T cells

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Clinical case-control + animal model (C57BL/6 mice)
- **Human participants**: 10 HT patients (25-65 years) and 10 healthy controls from Bengbu Medical University
- **Animal groups**: Control (Con), HT model, HT+Trp supplementation (20 mg/kg), HT+Kyn-pathway inhibitor (IDO1/TDO-IN-4, 20 mg/kg)
- **Key human findings** (Table 1):
  - TT4: 66.41 vs 98.37 nmol/L (p=0.009); FT3: 4.20 vs 5.09 pmol/L (p=0.008); FT4: 12.66 vs 15.70 pmol/L (p=0.023)
  - TPOAb: >1300 vs 47.90 U/mL (p<0.001); TgAb: 235.30 vs 15.90 U/mL (p=0.002)
- **Mouse model results**:
  - HT group showed extensive follicular destruction, marked atrophy/loss of follicles, dense perifollicular lymphocytic infiltrates
  - Trp supplementation (HT+T) partially preserved follicular integrity and reduced inflammatory infiltrates
  - Kyn pathway blockade (HT+I) exacerbated follicular atrophy and lymphocytic infiltration
  - IL-17 levels: HT group significantly elevated; HT+T reduced (p<0.01); HT+I further increased (p<0.01)
- **Transcriptomic analysis**: RNA-seq revealed 880 DEGs in HT+T vs HT group (322 up, 558 down); KEGG analysis showed PI3K-Akt pathway, hematopoietic cell lineage, Yersinia infection, and Ras signaling enrichment
- **Western blot**: Total mTOR, PI3K, and Akt unchanged; p-PI3K decreased in HT+T (p<0.05); p-Akt decreased in HT+T (p<0.001)
