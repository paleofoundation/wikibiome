# Nguyen 2017 — Steroid Metabolome in PMDD During GnRH-Induced Ovarian Suppression

## Key Findings

- **Only sulfated steroid metabolites** showed significant diagnosis-related differences between PMDD and controls — suggesting **sulfotransferase activity** may contribute to differential steroid sensitivity in PMDD ([[nguyen-2017-steroid-metabolome-pmdd-gnrh-suppression]], quasi-experimental, n=30).
- **Estrone-SO4** (q=0.039, q=0.002) and **estradiol-3-SO4** (q=0.166, q=0.001) were significantly elevated in PMDD women during Lupron alone and after E2 treatment ([[nguyen-2017-steroid-metabolome-pmdd-gnrh-suppression]]).
- **Allopregnanolone** (q=0.039) and **pregnanediol** (q=0.077) showed diagnosis-related changes after P4 addback; **cortexone** (q=0.118) and **pregnanediol** (q=0.157) also trended ([[nguyen-2017-steroid-metabolome-pmdd-gnrh-suppression]]).
- PMDD had significantly **attenuated increase in E2-3-sulfate** (q=0.035) compared to controls during Lupron+E2 treatment, and decreased **DHEA-sulfate** (q=0.07) during Lupron+P4 ([[nguyen-2017-steroid-metabolome-pmdd-gnrh-suppression]]).
- No differences in formation of P4-derived neurosteroids under controlled hormone exposures — supporting a receptor sensitivity rather than biosynthesis deficiency model ([[nguyen-2017-steroid-metabolome-pmdd-gnrh-suppression]]).
- This is the **first pharmacometabolomics study** of the steroid metabolome in PMDD ([[nguyen-2017-steroid-metabolome-pmdd-gnrh-suppression]]).

## Methods

15 PMDD women (prospectively confirmed, 2 menstrual cycles) and 15 controls, all medication-free. 6-month GnRH agonist-induced ovarian suppression (Lupron), then 2-week washout, then randomized crossover to 4 weeks E2 or P4 addback. LC-MS/MS of 49 steroid metabolites in serum. Wilcoxon rank-sum tests with FDR correction (q<0.2).

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Intervention target:** sulfotransferase pathway modulation — could theoretically modify the active/inactive steroid ratio.
- Confirms receptor sensitivity model: PMDD is not about abnormal hormone production but abnormal downstream processing.
- Primitive 7: estrobolome relevance — gut bacteria produce sulfatases that can deconjugate sulfated steroids, potentially modifying the PMDD-relevant sulfated steroid pool.
