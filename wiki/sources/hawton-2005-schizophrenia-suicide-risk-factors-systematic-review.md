# Schizophrenia and Suicide: Systematic Review of Risk Factors

## Key Claims

- Twenty-nine eligible studies were identified from the international literature on risk factors for suicide in [[schizophrenia]]
- Previous depressive disorders were the strongest risk factor (OR = 3.03, 95% CI 2.06-4.46)
- Previous suicide attempts strongly predicted completed suicide (OR = 4.09, 95% CI 2.79-6.01)
- Drug misuse was associated with increased risk (OR = 3.21, 95% CI 1.99-5.17)
- Agitation or motor restlessness increased risk (OR = 2.61, 95% CI 1.54-4.41)
- Fear of mental disintegration was a significant predictor (OR = 12.1, 95% CI 1.89-81.3)
- Poor adherence to treatment was associated with suicide (OR = 3.75, 95% CI 2.20-6.37)
- Recent loss also conferred risk (OR = 4.03, 95% CI 1.37-11.8)
- Hallucinations were associated with reduced risk (OR = 0.50, 95% CI 0.35-0.71)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review with meta-analyses using DerSimonian and Laird random effects
- **Included**: 29 studies reporting risk factors (cohort and case-control)
- **Key message**: Prevention of suicide in schizophrenia likely requires treating affective symptoms, improving adherence, and maintaining vigilance after recent losses
