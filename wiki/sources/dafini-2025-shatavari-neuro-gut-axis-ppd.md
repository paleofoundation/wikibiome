# Key Claims

- **Asparagus racemosus (Shatavari)** is an Ayurvedic herb gaining recognition as a botanical psychobiotic for [[postpartum-depression]]
- **Dry Shatavari root** (HPTLC analysis) contains richer bioactive compounds (steroidal saponins, flavonoids) compared to wet counterpart
- These compounds exhibit **prebiotic activity**, influence microbial composition, and support neurotransmitter modulation
- **90% of body's serotonin** is synthesized in the gut from tryptophan, heavily influenced by gut microbiota composition
- Beneficial microbes (*Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium*) enhance serotonin production; pathogenic species (*Clostridium perfringens*, *E. coli*, *Klebsiella*) divert tryptophan toward kynurenine

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Mini review with HPTLC-based phytochemical justification; 74 articles included from approximately 430 records screened
- **Key mechanism**: Prebiotic-microbiome-serotonin axis modulation
