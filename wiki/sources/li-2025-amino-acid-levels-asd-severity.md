# Key Claims

- **Aspartic acid, glutamic acid, phenylalanine, and leucine/isoleucine** positively correlated with ASD severity
- **Tryptophan and valine** negatively correlated with ASD severity
- **Nonlinear relationships** between amino acid concentrations and ASD risk identified via restricted cubic spline analysis
- **Combined amino acid model** achieved AUC of 0.806 for predicting severe ASD

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Cross-sectional study of 344 ASD children (83.4% male) in China, aged 4 months to 12 years. LC-MS/MS measurement of 19 amino acids.

**Univariate logistic regression (Figure 1):**
- Aspartic acid: OR = 1.035 (95% CI: 1.014-1.059, P < 0.01) - positively correlated with severity
- Glutamic acid: OR = 1.005 (95% CI: 1.000-1.010, P = 0.05) - positively correlated
- Phenylalanine: OR = 1.018, P = 0.03 - positively correlated
- Leucine/isoleucine: OR = 1.008, P = 0.03 - positively correlated
- Tryptophan: OR = 0.982 (95% CI: 0.966-0.997, P = 0.02) - negatively correlated (protective)
- Valine: OR = 0.987, P = 0.01 - negatively correlated (protective)

**Multivariate model (Table 2, adjusted for age, gender, BMI):**
- Aspartic acid: OR = 1.037, P = 0.01
- Glutamic acid: OR = 1.009, P = 0.03
- Phenylalanine: OR = 1.036, P = 0.04
- Leucine/isoleucine: OR = 1.021, P = 0.01
- Tryptophan: OR = 0.935, P < 0.01
- Valine: OR = 0.987, P = 0.01

**RCS analysis (nonlinear):**
- Phenylalanine (P < 0.01), aspartic acid (P < 0.0001), proline (P < 0.04): nonlinear U-shaped or inverted-U relationships with ASD risk
- Tryptophan: nonlinear decrease in risk with increasing levels

**ROC/predictive model:**
- Aspartic acid cutoff: 44.36 umol/L (AUC 0.64)
- Combined nomogram model for severe ASD prediction

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Excitatory amino acid excess**: Aspartic acid and glutamic acid elevation drives excitotoxicity via NMDA/AMPA receptors, which are zinc-modulated ion channels
- **Tryptophan protective**: Tryptophan's negative correlation with severity confirms iron-dependent serotonin synthesis pathway disruption in severe ASD
- **Phenylalanine-metal link**: Phenylalanine hydroxylase converting Phe to Tyr requires iron and BH4 cofactors; elevation may reflect impaired iron-dependent hydroxylation
- **BCAA-metal axis**: Leucine/isoleucine elevation and valine protection suggest branched-chain amino acid metabolism (requiring zinc and manganese-dependent enzymes) is differentially disrupted in severe ASD
