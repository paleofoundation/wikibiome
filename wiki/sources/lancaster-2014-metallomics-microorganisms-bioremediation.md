# Key Claims

- **Metal utilization varies dramatically** between Desulfovibrio vulgaris and Enterobacter cloacae despite similar environments
- **DvH assimilated 9 metals and EcH assimilated 19 metals** into their cytoplasmic fractions
- **DvH did not assimilate significant amounts of zinc or copper** whereas EcH assimilated both
- **813 metalloproteins predicted in DvH and 953 in EcH** by bioinformatic genome analysis
- Differential metal assimilation could be rationalized for some metals (Fe, Cu, Mo, W, V) but not others (Zn, Nd, Ce, Pr, Dy, Hf, Th)

# Methods & Findings

**Metalloproteomics study** of two microorganisms from similar heavy metal-contaminated environments.

- Compared Desulfovibrio vulgaris strain Hildenborough and Enterobacter cloacae strain Hanford
- Analyzed metal content of cytoplasmic fractions
- Performed bioinformatic metalloprotein prediction from genomes
- Revealed fundamental differences in metal ecology between species

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Desulfovibrio** is consistently elevated in ASD gut microbiome; its metal handling properties are relevant
- **Microbial metal ecology** determines how gut bacteria interact with dietary and environmental metals
- **Metal-microbiome interactions** shape the metallomic environment of the ASD gut
