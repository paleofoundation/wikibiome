# Key Findings

- **All 20 celiac disease patients** on a gluten-free diet (GFD) with relapsing symptoms tested **positive for nickel allergic contact mucositis** (Ni ACM) via oral mucosa patch test ([[borghini-2020-low-nickel-diet-celiac-ibs-symptoms]], quasi-experimental, n=20).
- A **low-nickel diet (LNiD) significantly improved symptoms**: 19/24 (79.2%) gastrointestinal symptoms and 14/24 (58.3%) extraintestinal symptoms improved (P<0.0083, Bonferroni-corrected).
- **Correct GFD alone worsened symptoms** (T1 vs T2: 20/24 GI symptoms worsened at 83.3%), likely because GFD staples (corn, legumes, buckwheat) are high in nickel.
- The recrudescence of symptoms on GFD may be due to **increased alimentary Ni intake** when gluten-containing grains are replaced with Ni-rich alternatives.

# Methods

- Pilot study: 102 consecutive celiac patients on GFD ≥12 months with relapsing symptoms.
- 20 patients (all female, mean age 39.1) who tested positive for Ni ACM completed the study.
- Protocol: T0 (free diet) → T1 (correct GFD, 3 months) → T2 (GFD recurrence) → T3 (GFD + LNiD, 3 months).
- Symptom assessment via modified GSRS questionnaire (24 GI + extraintestinal symptoms).

# Detailed Summary

This study reveals an important clinical overlap between celiac disease and nickel allergy. Many GFD staple foods are high in nickel (corn, chickpeas, lentils, buckwheat, oats), creating a paradox where the treatment for celiac disease (GFD) inadvertently increases nickel exposure. This is especially relevant for the ~30% of the European population with cutaneous nickel sensitivity. The Venn diagram of high-Ni foods, high-FODMAP foods, and high-lactose foods shows significant overlap, complicating dietary management.

# Contradictions / Tensions

- Small sample size (n=20) and all-female cohort limit generalizability.
- No control group for LNiD intervention (before-after design only).

# Open Questions

- What is the prevalence of Ni ACM in the broader celiac population?
- Does Ni exposure in celiac patients alter gut microbiome composition?
- Would a combined low-Ni, low-FODMAP GFD be more effective than either alone?
