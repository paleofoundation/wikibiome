# Salivary microbiome profiling reveals a dysbiotic schizophrenia-associated microbiota

## Summary

This study used 16S rRNA gene sequencing to characterize the salivary microbiome in 85 first-episode schizophrenia (FES) patients, 43 clinical high-risk (CHR) subjects, and 80 healthy controls. The salivary microbiome exhibited disease-stage-specific alterations, with H2S-producing bacteria enriched in schizophrenia and correlated with [[neuroinflammation]] markers.

## Key Findings

- FES patients showed highest alpha diversity (Shannon) and beta diversity heterogeneity
- Firmicutes were progressively enriched from HC to CHR to FES, while Proteobacteria were depleted
- H2S-producing bacteria (Leptotrichia, Actinomyces, Veillonella, Fusobacterium, Atopobium, Selenomonas, Prevotella) were enriched in FES
- Salivary taxa correlated with blood markers of [[neuroinflammation]]: CRP, IFN-gamma, TNF-alpha, IL-8, IL-1beta, thioredoxin, S100B
- AUC of 0.824 for distinguishing FES from HC using microbial classifiers
- The Firmicutes/Proteobacteria, Actinobacteria/Proteobacteria, and Bacteroidetes/Proteobacteria ratios differed significantly across disease stages
- Salivary microbial alterations might indicate development of schizophrenia

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

The oral microbiome represents an understudied niche in schizophrenia that connects to [[dysbiosis]] through the oral-gut axis. The enrichment of H2S-producing bacteria is particularly relevant, as H2S can bind [[iron]], [[copper]], and [[zinc]] in metalloenzymes, potentially contributing to [[mis-metallation]]. The correlation with inflammatory blood markers supports [[neuroinflammation]] as a mediator between microbial [[dysbiosis]] and brain dysfunction via the [[gut-brain-axis]]. The stepwise progression from HC to CHR to FES suggests microbiome alterations precede full psychosis onset.

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional observational design
- Cannot distinguish direct effects of schizophrenia from medication or lifestyle factors
- Salivary microbiome may not reflect gut microbiome
