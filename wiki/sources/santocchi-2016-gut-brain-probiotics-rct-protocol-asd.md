# Key Claims

- **GI disturbances in ASD** may be linked to gut dysbiosis representing an observable phenotype of gut-brain axis disruption
- **Probiotic supplementation** (Vivomixx) may affect not only GI symptoms but also core ASD deficits in cognition, language, and brain connectivity
- **Phthalate concentrations** in urine may be linked to ASD and will be evaluated as secondary outcome
- **100 preschoolers with ASD** to be classified into GI and Non-GI groups and randomized 1:1 to probiotic vs placebo for 6 months
- Primary outcomes include GI severity, autism symptoms, behavioral and developmental measures, and neurophysiological patterns (QEEG)

# Methods & Findings

**Study protocol** for a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial.

- Vivomixx probiotic mixture administered for 6 months
- Assessments at baseline, 3 months, and 6 months plus 3-month follow-up after discontinuation
- Measures: GI symptoms, ASD symptoms severity, affective/behavioral symptoms, plasmatic/urinary/fecal biomarkers, neurophysiological patterns
- Novel inclusion of phthalate assessment as environmental pollutant exposure marker
- QEEG to evaluate brain function and connectivity changes

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Multimodal assessment** of probiotic effects beyond GI symptoms to brain connectivity
- **Phthalate-microbiome-ASD axis** as novel environmental exposure pathway
- **Endophenotype stratification** (GI vs non-GI) for personalized probiotic intervention
