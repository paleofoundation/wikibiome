# Metabolomics and 16S rRNA Sequencing of Human Colorectal Cancers and Adjacent Mucosa

## Key Claims

- Metabolomic differences between paired tumor and normal tissue were associated with steroid biosynthesis, terpenoid biosynthesis, and bile metabolism pathways in Malaysian CRC patients
- Serotonin, 5-HIAA (5-hydroxy-3-indoleacetic acid), and other [[tryptophan-metabolism]]-metabolism metabolites were detected only in normal tissue samples (Fisher's exact test, P = 0.044), indicating disrupted serotonergic signaling in tumors
- S-adenosyl-L-homocysteine (SAH), involved in methionine metabolism and methylation, was significantly more frequently increased in tumor relative to normal tissues (P = 0.026)
- The relative abundances of *Anaerotruncs*, *Intestinimonas*, and *Oscillibacter* in tumor tissue displayed significant relationships with steroid biosynthesis and terpenoid pathways
- Firmicutes, Bacteroidetes, and Proteobacteria constituted the three predominant phyla in both tumor (~36%, 30.7%, 19.5%) and normal (~33.3%, 31.5%, 19.7%) tissues
- Biofilm presence was observed in 100% of right-sided tumors versus only 36.4% of left-sided tumors

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Paired tumor vs. adjacent normal tissue comparison using LC-MS metabolomics and 16S rRNA sequencing
- **Participants**: 17 Malaysian patients with paired surgical biopsy tissues from the MAL2 cohort
- **Methods**: LC-Quadrupole Time-of-Flight (Q-TOF) MS for untargeted metabolomics; 16S rRNA V3-V4 amplicon sequencing via Illumina; QIIME analysis with KEGG pathway profiling
- **Key metabolomic results**: 708 compounds detected; 158 met minimum abundance/frequency thresholds; 14 compounds significantly higher in normal mucosa; serotonergic metabolites absent from tumor tissue
- **Microbial correlation networks**: Kendall's tau correlations between bacterial genera and KEGG pathways/metabolites were constructed to identify functional relationships
