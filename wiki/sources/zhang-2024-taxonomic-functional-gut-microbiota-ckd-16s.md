# Uncovering Specific Taxonomic and Functional Alteration of Gut Microbiota in CKD through 16S rRNA Data

## Key Claims

- Substantial decrease in microbial diversity in [[chronic-kidney-disease]] group
- Significantly reduced abundance of Faecalibacterium prausnitzii in CKD
- F. prausnitzii negatively correlated with three pathways regulating acid-base disturbance and pro-oxidant metabolism
- F. prausnitzii may serve as preventive and therapeutic tool for CKD

## Methods & Findings

Comprehensive meta-analysis of 16S rRNA data from 980 samples across six references and three nations. DADA2, QIIME2, PICRUSt2 analyses.
