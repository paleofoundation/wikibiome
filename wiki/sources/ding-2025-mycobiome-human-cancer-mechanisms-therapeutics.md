# Ding 2025 -- Mycobiome in Human Cancer: Challenges, Mechanisms, Therapeutics

## Key Claims

- The polymorphic microbiome is now a new hallmark of cancer; fungal microbes (mycobiome) represent ~0.1-1% of the microbiome but are a critical immunologically active component of the tumor microbiome, defined as the immuno-oncology microbiome (IOM) axis
- Pan-cancer analysis using Weizmann (WIS) and TCGA data profiled fungal DNA across 35 cancer types from 17,401 samples; fungi highly enriched in cancers may serve as prognostic markers
- In colon cancer: *Candida* and *Saccharomycetes* are the dominant fungi; *Candida*-dominant (Ca-type) GI cancers showed reduced survival through IL-22, TP53, CDKN2A, CD44, and CARD10 pathways; *Saccharomycetes*-dominant (Sa-type) showed distinct patterns
- Intratumoral mycobiome exists and can migrate from GI tumor site to bloodstream; fungal DNA present in most human cancer tissues; oral mycobiome can enter colon through sphincter of Oddi within 30 minutes
- Key analytical challenges: ITS amplicon sequencing vs. metagenomic sequencing; cross-contamination risks from low fungal biomass; standardized methods for generating and analyzing microbiome sequencing data from low biomass samples needed

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Comprehensive review (26 pages) covering tumor-specific fungal ecologies, gut mycobiome dysbiosis, oral/intratumoral mycobiome, analytical methods, molecular mechanisms, and therapeutic implications
- **Figure 1**: Pan-cancer analysis of intratumoral mycobiome using WIS and TCGA data, showing cancer-specific fungi and immune signatures
- **Key therapeutic directions**: Antifungal therapy (targeting *Malassezia* in PDAC); synergy with immunotherapy; fungi as biomarkers for diagnosis and prognosis
