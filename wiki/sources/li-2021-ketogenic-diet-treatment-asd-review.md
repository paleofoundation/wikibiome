# Key Claims

- **Ketogenic diet is a safe and effective treatment** for ASD through multiple neuroprotective mechanisms
- **KD mechanisms include**: improved energy metabolism, reduced oxidative stress, GABA regulation, mTOR pathway inhibition, and gut microbiota modulation
- **Clinical evidence** from 6 studies (Table 1) shows CARS improvement in majority of treated ASD patients
- **KD increased Akkermansia, Parabacteroides, Bacteroides, and Desulfovibrio** in ASD animal models

# Methods & Findings

**Narrative review** of KD mechanisms and clinical evidence in ASD.

**Clinical studies (Table 1):**
- Gluten-free/casein-free then KD in 1 child: improved cognitive/social skills, seizure-free at 14 months
- KD in 6-year-old: improved behavior/intellect, decreased 18FDG uptake at 16 months
- Modified KD (GF) in 15 children aged 2-17: improved core autism features at 3 months
- KD vs. MAD vs. GFCF in 45 children aged 3-8: KD and MAD improved ATEC and CARS at 6 months
- KD in 6 patients with pathological beta-hydroxybutyrate: 1/6 remarkable CARS improvement
- KD in 30 children aged 4-10: 18/30 improved CARS at 6 months
- Modified Atkins diet in 1 developmentally disabled man: seizure-free, autistic features disappeared at 1 year

**Neuroprotective mechanisms (Figure 1):**
1. **Energy metabolism**: KD generates ketone bodies (beta-hydroxybutyrate, acetoacetate, acetone) that cross BBB and improve ATP production
2. **Anti-inflammatory/antioxidative**: Reduced IL-6, TNF-alpha; decreased ROS
3. **Neurotransmitter regulation**: Increased GABA levels in gut and brain
4. **mTOR inhibition**: KD inhibits mTOR signaling, which is hyperactivated in ASD
5. **Microbiota modulation**: KD restores gut microbial composition; increased Akkermansia muciniphila

**Side effects:**
- Constipation, vomiting, lack of energy, hunger (short-term)
- Hyperuricemia, hyperlipidemia, kidney stones (long-term)
- Growth suppression possible with prolonged use
- ASD children may reject KD due to selective eating habits

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Ketone bodies-metal transport**: Beta-hydroxybutyrate may alter metal transport across BBB by modifying monocarboxylate transporter activity
- **Akkermansia-metal barrier**: KD-restored Akkermansia muciniphila maintains mucin layer integrity, critical for controlling metal permeability in the gut
- **mTOR-zinc axis**: mTOR signaling is zinc-sensitive; KD inhibition of hyperactive mTOR may partially compensate for zinc-driven mTOR dysregulation in ASD
- **GABA-metal synthesis**: KD-enhanced GABA production depends on glutamic acid decarboxylase, which requires zinc and pyridoxal phosphate cofactors
- **Micronutrient risk**: Long-term KD may exacerbate existing vitamin D and folic acid deficiencies in ASD, compounding metal dyshomeostasis
