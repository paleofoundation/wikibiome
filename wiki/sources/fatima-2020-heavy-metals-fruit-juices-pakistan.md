# Evaluation of Heavy Metals Content in the Canned/Packed Fruit Juices from Local and Imported Origin in Lahore, Pakistan

## Key Claims

- 7 out of 9 tested heavy metals (Pb, Mg, Ni, Fe, Cr, Se, Mn) exceeded WHO permissible limits in all four juice categories
- **Nickel levels were dramatically above WHO limits** (0.07 ppm permissible): local packed mean 11.78 ppm, imported packed 13.3 ppm, local canned 6.66 ppm, imported canned 8.51 ppm -- up to 168x the WHO limit
- Individual brands showed Ni as high as 48 ppm (imported packed Brand 16) and 30.75 ppm (local packed Brand 4)
- Lead exceeded limits in all groups (WHO limit 0.01 ppm): means ranged 8.03-11.71 ppm
- Iron exceeded limits in all groups (WHO limit 1 ppm): means ranged 59.3-73.4 ppm
- Commercially available fruit juices are not all safe for consumption despite their nutritive values

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional analysis of 40 juice brands from Lahore, Pakistan over 3 months
- **Categories**: Local packed (10 brands), imported packed (10 brands), local canned (10 brands), imported canned (10 brands)
- **Method**: Di-acid digestion followed by Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometer (AAS, Polarized Zeeman Z-8230)
- **Metals tested**: Cu, Pb, Mg, Ni, Fe, Cr, Zn, Se, Mn (9 metals)
- **Copper**: Only metal consistently below WHO limits (2 ppm) -- not detected in local/imported packed groups
- **Zinc**: Most values below WHO limit (5 ppm), with some exceptions
- **Statistical analysis**: One-way ANOVA via SPSS
