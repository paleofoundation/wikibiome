# Overview

Review discussing the interplay of **genetic, epigenetic, immunologic, and environmental factors** in ASD determinism. Covers immune dysregulation, oxidative stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, environmental causes (toxicants, medications, infections, electromagnetic fields), and epigenetic modifications. Frames ASD as arising from environmental insults acting on genetically predisposed individuals.

# Key Claims

## Genetic Factors
- ASD heritability estimated but environmental factors increasingly recognized
- Copy number variations (CNVs): 2,193 genes, 2,806 SNPs, 4,544 copy number variants, 158 linkage regions identified
- Genetic mutations explain ~10-15% of cases

## Immune Dysregulation
- Aberrant cytokine profiles (TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL-1beta) in ASD
- Pro-inflammatory cytokines elevated → neuroinflammation
- Immune activation during pregnancy linked to ASD risk

## Oxidative Stress and Mitochondrial Dysfunction
- Increased ROS production, decreased detoxification capacity
- Mitochondrial dysfunction in ~5% of ASD cases
- Reduced folate levels, impaired methylation, altered DNA methylation patterns

## Environmental Causes
- **Heavy metals**: [[lead]], [[mercury]], [[cadmium]] — neurotoxic; impaired detoxification in ASD
- **Pesticides**: organophosphates, organochlorines — disrupt Ca2+ signaling
- **Medications**: valproate, thalidomide — teratogenic
- **Infections**: rubella, CMV — prenatal immune activation
- **Vitamin D** deficiency during pregnancy — increased ASD risk

## Epigenetic Mechanisms
- DNA methylation, histone modification, chromatin remodeling, microRNA
- Environmental toxins act by reducing DNA methylation
- Nutritional factors (folate, vitamin B) modulate epigenetic state
- Histone modifications linked to gene silencing in ASD-relevant pathways
