# Ross et al. 2024 -- Diet-Gut Microbiome Interplay: Implications for Health and Disease

## Key Claims

- Each whole dietary regime has distinct effects on [[gut-microbiome]] composition: [[mediterranean-diet]] increases *Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*, *Callimiella*, *Ruminococcus*; [[high-fiber-diet]] increases *Prevotella*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Lactobacillus*; [[ketogenic-diet]] decreases *Bifidobacterium*, *Actinobacteria*, *Firmicutes*
- The [[ketogenic-diet]] is associated with decreased intestinal Th17 cells and [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] production, increased beta-hydroxybutyrate and secondary bile acid levels, and increased endotoxins and TMA/TMAO
- [[polyphenols|Polyphenols]] can increase beneficial bacteria (*Bifidobacterium*, *Akkermansia*, *Lactobacillus*) and produce bioactive microbial metabolites with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, enhancing endothelial function
- Western diet is characterized by increased *Bacteroides spp.*, *Bilophila*, *Alistipes*, *Blautia*, *Ruminococcus*, chronic inflammation, increased oxidative stress, immune dysregulation, and risk of metabolic disorders
- Geographic location profoundly shapes gut microbiome: industrialized populations have lower microbial diversity than traditional/rural populations; the Hadza people exhibit distinct microbial capabilities for digesting resistant plant materials

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Comprehensive review article
- **Figure 1**: Macronutrient composition of whole diets and their effects on bacterial taxa and metabolite production for Mediterranean, high-fibre, plant-based, high-protein, ketogenic, and Western diets

 - Fibre: SCFAs produced via GPCR41/43 signaling -> GLP1, PYY secretion -> appetite regulation, immune modulation; butyrate is energy source for colonocytes
 - Protein: broken down by *Bacteroides*, *Bacillus*, *Clostridium*, *Propionibacterium*, *Fusobacterium*; amino acids fermented to SCFAs (butyrate from lysine/glutamate, acetate from alanine)
 - PUFAs: increase *Bifidobacterium spp.* and butyrate-producing bacteria; decrease *Enterobacterium spp.*
 - Polyphenols: 95% reach colon for microbial metabolism -> phenolic acids with cardioprotective properties
- **Global diets**: Prevotella/Bacteroidetes dominance varies with geography; Westernization reduces *Prevotella copri* prevalence
