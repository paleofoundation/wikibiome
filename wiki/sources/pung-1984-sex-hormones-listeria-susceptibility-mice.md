# Key Claims

- **Estrogenic compounds** (17-beta-estradiol, diethylstilbestrol) significantly increased mortality of BaC3F1 female mice after Listeria infection
- **Compounds with estrogenic activity** (5-alpha-dihydrotestosterone, progesterone, norethindrone) did not alter Listeria-related mortality
- **Estrogen-induced alterations in resistance** were inhibited by adult thymectomy and the estrogen antagonist tamoxifen
- **Estrogen exposure** depressed the accumulation of monocytes and lymphocytes at infective foci
- Significantly elevated numbers of bacteria were observed in infective foci of estrogen-treated mice later in infection

# Methods & Findings

**Animal model study** examining sex hormone effects on Listeria monocytogenes infection in mice.

- Subchronic exposure to pharmacological levels of estrogenic compounds tested
- Estrogen increased susceptibility specifically through thymus-dependent mechanisms
- Tamoxifen blocked estrogen-induced immunosuppression
- Results suggest estrogen-induced impairment of Listeria immunity is partially mediated by the thymus
- T cell-dependent defense mechanisms compromised by estrogen exposure

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Listeria monocytogenes** is a TORCH-related pathogen implicated in prenatal infection and ASD risk
- **Estrogen-immune axis** may explain sex-specific vulnerability to prenatal infections affecting neurodevelopment
- **Thymus-dependent immune suppression** by estrogen relevant to maternal immune activation models of ASD
