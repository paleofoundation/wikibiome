# Key Claims

- The [[gut-brain axis]] has an important role in pancreatic cysts, with microbiota imbalance and increased inflammation observed especially in mucinous neoplasms
- Mucinous neoplasm patients showed significantly lower microbial diversity (Shannon index 2.9 vs 3.8, p=0.012) and higher [[Firmicutes]]/[[Bacteroidetes]] ratio (2.89 vs 1.66, p=0.008) compared to serous cystadenomas
- Depression and anxiety scores were significantly higher in mucinous neoplasm patients (Beck Depression Inventory 18.6 vs 14.2, p=0.032; Hamilton Anxiety Scale 16.4 vs 12.6, p=0.042)
- Two-year overall survival was 100% in serous cystadenomas vs 80% in mucinous neoplasms (p=0.015)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study, 47 patients (16 serous cystadenoma, 31 mucinous neoplasm) at Dicle University, Turkey, 2015-2023
- **Microbiota analysis**: [[16S rRNA sequencing]] of stool samples; biochemical and hormonal parameters from blood
- **Alpha diversity**: Shannon index and Chao1 significantly higher in serous cystadenoma group
- **Genus-level findings**: *Lactobacillus* (12.4% vs 8.2%) and *Bifidobacterium* (8.6% vs 5.3%) higher in serous; *Clostridium* (15.2% vs 23.8%) higher in mucinous
- **Inflammatory markers**: CRP (4.2 vs 1.8 mg/dL), IL-6 (7.8 vs 3.2 pg/mL), TNF-alpha (18.6 vs 12.4 pg/mL) all significantly higher in mucinous group
- **Metabolic parameters**: Fasting glucose (112.6 vs 98.2), insulin (12.8 vs 8.4), HbA1c (6.2% vs 5.6%) all higher in mucinous
- **Cognitive function**: MMSE and MoCA scores not significantly different between groups; neurotransmitters (dopamine, noradrenaline, GABA) also not significantly different

# Connections

- [[Dysbiosis]] with elevated Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio parallels findings in other [[pancreatic cancer]] microbiome studies
- Links [[microbiome and gut-brain axis]] to neuropsychological outcomes in pancreatic disease
- Reduced *Lactobacillus* and *Bifidobacterium* in mucinous neoplasms relevant to [[probiotics]] intervention strategies
- Inflammatory marker elevation connects to [[microbiome and inflammation]] pathways in pancreatic cancer
