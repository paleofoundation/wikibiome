# Key Claims

- **High-fiber diet ameliorates gut microbiota**, serum metabolism, and emotional mood in T2D patients
- **Fiber intake impacts gut microbiome and insulin sensitivity** in prediabetes
- **Combined high-fiber diet with acarbose** alleviates metabolic phenotypes by regulating gut microbiome
- **Fiber-microbiome-SCFA axis** is a key therapeutic target for diabetes management

# Methods & Findings

Multiple studies on high-fiber dietary interventions in diabetes and prediabetes:

- Effects on gut microbiome composition and function assessed
- SCFA production changes measured
- Glycemic and metabolic outcomes evaluated

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: High-fiber diets may reduce zinc bioavailability through phytate binding -- zinc supplementation may be needed
- **[[iron]]**: Fiber reduces iron absorption, which may be protective in iron-overload states but problematic in deficiency
- **[[selenium]]**: Whole grain fiber sources provide selenium alongside prebiotic fiber
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Dietary fiber reshapes [[gut-metal-microbiome]] by modulating both metal and substrate availability
- **[[probiotics]]**: Fiber serves as prebiotic substrate for beneficial [[probiotics]]
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Fiber-derived SCFA restore barrier function through zinc-dependent tight junction assembly
