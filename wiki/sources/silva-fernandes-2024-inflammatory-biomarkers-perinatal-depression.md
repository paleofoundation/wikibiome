# Key Claims

- **10-20% of pregnant women** worldwide experience perinatal depression (PND)
- **56 studies** included in systematic review examining inflammatory biomarkers and perinatal depression
- **Several inflammatory markers** correlated with peripartum depressive symptoms, particularly **IL-6, IL-8, CRP, and TNF-alpha**
- **Notable differences** in timing of biological sampling and methodologies for assessing depression across studies
- Pro-[[inflammation]]atory activity intensifies in late pregnancy, coinciding with increased depressive symptoms

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review of observational studies evaluating inflammatory molecules (protein levels) in women with perinatal depression assessed by ICD/DSM criteria or psychometric instruments
- **Databases**: PubMed, Web of Science, PsychInfo; Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for quality assessment
- **Key finding**: Associations were mainly observed looking at pregnancy vs. delivery vs. postpartum timepoints, with elevated IL-6, CRP, and TNF-alpha in depressed groups
