# Key Claims

- **Microbiome-gut-brain axis** (MGBA) is central to understanding neurodevelopmental and neuropsychiatric disorders
- **Microbial metabolites** (short-chain fatty acids, neurotransmitters) influence neural development and brain function
- **Immune-mediated signaling** between gut microbiota and CNS via vagal and humoral pathways
- **Dysbiosis** in ASD and schizophrenia reflects specific taxonomic shifts with functional consequences

# Methods & Findings

Comprehensive review covering:
- Microbiome development across lifespan
- Neuronal pathways for gut-brain communication (vagal afferents, HPA axis, bacterial lipopolysaccharide signaling)
- Chemical signaling pathways (SCFA, neurotransmitters, tryptophan metabolites)
- MGBA dysfunction in psychiatric conditions (autism, schizophrenia)
- Neuroimmune mechanisms in neurodevelopmental disorders

**Key mechanisms in ASD:**
- Altered microbiota composition → impaired SCFA production → reduced histone deacetylase inhibition → altered gene expression in intestinal barrier
- Loss of tryptophan-metabolizing bacteria → reduced aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) signaling → intestinal permeability increase
- Dysbiotic shift toward pro-inflammatory taxa → lipopolysaccharide translocation → systemic immune activation → blood-brain barrier disruption

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

This review emphasizes neuroimmunomodulation but lacks explicit metal analysis. Critical gaps for metallomic integration:

- **Primitive 2 (Nutritional Immunity as Interpretive Constraint)**: Dysbiotic ASD microbiota may reflect failed metal sequestration; chronic immune activation consumes antimicrobial peptides
- **Primitive 4 (Microbial Metal Dependencies)**: Loss of protective taxa → loss of metal-dependent functional capacity (e.g., [[zinc]]-dependent peptidases, [[iron]]-dependent electron transport)
- **SCFA production link to metals**: Dysbiotic taxa (e.g., [[clostridium]] loss) unable to ferment fiber → loss of [[butyrate]] → loss of histone deacetylase inhibition → failure to upregulate metal-binding proteins
- **Aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) signaling requires zinc**: Reduced tryptophan metabolites → impaired AhR → failure to maintain intestinal barrier → uncontrolled bacterial translocation with potential metal-laden LPS

The review provides mechanistic foundation for hypothesis that dysbiosis in ASD reflects **metal-selective pressure on bacterial communities**, not random compositional change.
