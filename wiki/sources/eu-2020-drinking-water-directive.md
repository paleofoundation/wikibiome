# EU Drinking Water Directive 2020/2184

## Key Claims

- **Document type**: EU legislative directive (recast of Directive 98/83/EC), adopted 16 December 2020
- Establishes minimum quality requirements for water intended for human consumption across the EU
- Introduces a comprehensive **risk-based approach** covering the entire water supply chain: catchment areas, abstraction, treatment, storage, distribution, and point of compliance (the tap)
- **Lead**: Parametric value retained at 10 ug/L for a 15-year transitional period, then reduced to 5 ug/L; Member States should replace lead pipes in domestic distribution systems
- **Chromium**: Value adapted per WHO review; a 15-year transitional period applies before a more stringent value takes effect
- **Bisphenol A**: Added with a health-based parametric value of 2.5 ug/L based on EFSA 2015 opinion
- Introduces a **watch list mechanism** for emerging compounds including endocrine-disrupting compounds, pharmaceuticals, and microplastics (Nonylphenol 0.1 ug/L, Beta-estradiol 1 ng/L as benchmarks)
- Enshrines the **right to water**: Member States must improve access to drinking water for all, including vulnerable/marginalized groups (refugees, Roma and Travellers, homeless)
- Materials in contact with drinking water must meet harmonized EU hygiene requirements; European positive lists of approved substances to be established via ECHA

## Methods/Findings

- **Scope**: Replaces and modernizes the 1998 Drinking Water Directive
- **WHO input**: 2017 WHO Regional Office for Europe review recommended adding 6 new chemical parameters and updating others; 4 of 6 parametric values set more stringently than WHO proposals
- **Risk assessment structure**: Three components - (1) hazard assessment of catchment/abstraction areas, (2) supply system risk assessment from abstraction to distribution, (3) domestic distribution system assessment (e.g., Legionella, lead pipes)
- **Monitoring**: Core parameters must be monitored at specified minimum frequency; water suppliers can adjust monitoring based on risk assessment
- **Consumer information**: Water suppliers must provide accessible information on water quality, treatment, and pricing
- **Transposition deadline**: Member States must transpose by January 2023
