# Key Claims

- **919 syrup (919 TJ)** is a Chinese herbal medicine that alleviates [[postpartum-depression]] by modulating gut microbiota structure and function
- **GABA levels in hippocampus** were altered in PPD mice; 919 TJ treatment restored 4-aminobutyric acid (GABA) levels
- **Key bacterial species** affected: *Mucispirillum schaedleri*, *Bifidobacterium pseudolongum*, *Desulfovibrio piger*, *Alloprevotella tannerae*, *Bacteroides sp.2.1.33B*, and *Prevotella sp. CAG:755*
- **Metabolic markers** identified: Met-Arg, urocanic acid, thioethanamide-PC, L-pipecolic acid, and linoleoyl ethanolamine
- Concept of "GutBalance" proposed -- focusing on composition and function of entire intestinal flora rather than individual species

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Mouse model of PPD; BALB/c mice with induced and untreated PPD vs. 919 TJ-treated; hippocampal metabolism, fecal metabolism, and intestinal microflora analyzed
- **Setting**: Jinshan Hospital, Fudan University, Shanghai, China
- **Key implication**: Herbal medicine acts through [[gut-brain-axis]] modulation of microbiome-metabolome-brain axis

# Connections

- GABA/glutamate system disruption connects microbiome to [[gut-brain-axis]] neurotransmitter balance
- [[dysbiosis]] correction by herbal medicine parallels probiotic interventions for [[postpartum-depression]]
- Metabolomic approach relevant to understanding [[zinc]], [[magnesium]], and [[copper]] cofactor roles in neurotransmitter synthesis
- [[inflammation]] modulation via gut microbiome restoration
