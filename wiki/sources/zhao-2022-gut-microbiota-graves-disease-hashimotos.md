# Alterations and Mechanism of Gut Microbiota in Graves' Disease and Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

## Key Claims

- The overall structure of [[gut microbiome]] in GD and HT groups was significantly different from the healthy control group
- [[Proteobacteria]] and [[Actinomycetes]] contents were highest in the HT group; the HT group also contained a small number of [[Verrucomicrobiaceae]]
- The "ABC transporter" metabolic pathway was highly correlated with the occurrence of GD and HT
- GD and HT groups were enriched in carbohydrate transport and metabolism compared to healthy controls
- [[Bacillus]], [[Blautia]], and [[Ornithinimicrobium]] could be used as potential markers to distinguish GD and HT from healthy populations
- At the genus level, [[Prevotella_9]], [[Ruminococcus_2]], and [[Lachnospiraceae_NK4A136_group]] were higher in GD patient samples

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional case-control study
- **Population**: 27 GD patients, 27 HT patients, and 16 healthy controls from Xiamen, China
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA gene sequencing (V3-V4 region) using MiSeq Illumina
- **Analysis**: UPARSE software (97% similarity OTU clustering), RDP classifier, KEGG functional analysis (COG)
- **OTU results**: 686 total OTUs detected; 389 commonly shared; 63 (GD), 61 (HT), and 21 (controls) unique OTUs
- **Family level**: Lachnospiraceae, Alcaligenaceae, Christensenellaceae, and Erysipelotrichaceae prevalent in GD; Enterococcaceae, Erysipelotrichaceae, and Bacillobacteriaceae abundant in HT
- **LEfSe analysis**: 24 discriminant features (GD vs control); 13 discriminant features (HT vs control) with LDA > 3
