# Oral Administration of Probiotics Inhibits Absorption of the Heavy Metal Cadmium by Protecting the Intestinal Barrier

## Relevance

This study focuses on [[cadmium]] rather than [[nickel]], but is included for its detailed mechanistic work on probiotic-mediated protection against heavy metal absorption. The intestinal barrier protection mechanisms described are broadly applicable to other heavy metals, with potential relevance to [[dietary-nickel-exposure]].

## Key Strain

**L. plantarum CCFM8610** was identified as the most protective probiotic strain because it possessed both strong cadmium-binding ability AND antioxidative capacity. Strains with only one of these properties were less effective, demonstrating that dual functionality is critical.

## In Vitro Results (HT-29 Cells)

- CCFM8610 alleviated cadmium-induced cytotoxicity in human intestinal epithelial cells
- Reduced reactive oxygen species (ROS) production
- Inhibited cadmium-induced apoptosis

## In Vivo Results (Mouse Model, 8 Weeks)

- Probiotic supplementation **increased fecal cadmium excretion** (sequestration in gut lumen)
- **Decreased cadmium accumulation** in liver and kidneys
- Maintained intestinal barrier integrity under cadmium challenge

## Four-Part Protective Mechanism

1. **Intestinal cadmium sequestration**: Bacterial cell wall binding of cadmium ions in the gut lumen, reducing bioavailable cadmium for absorption
2. **Alleviation of [[oxidative-stress]]**: Antioxidative properties of CCFM8610 counteracted cadmium-induced ROS, protecting epithelial cells
3. **Tight junction protection**: Preserved expression of key tight junction proteins — ZO-1, ZO-2, occludin, and claudin-1. Cadmium exposure disrupted these junctions, increasing gut permeability and allowing more cadmium absorption in a vicious cycle. Probiotics reversed this.
4. **Gut immune modulation**: Maintained secretory IgA (sIgA) levels and balanced cytokine profiles in the intestinal mucosa

## Critical Insight: Dual Functionality

The superiority of CCFM8610 over other strains demonstrates that effective probiotic intervention against heavy metals requires both:
- **Metal-binding capacity** (to sequester the metal)
- **Antioxidative capacity** (to protect the intestinal barrier from metal-induced oxidative damage)

Strains with only cadmium-binding or only antioxidative properties provided inferior protection.

## Implications for Nickel

The mechanisms described — metal sequestration, barrier protection, oxidative stress reduction — could theoretically apply to other heavy metals including [[nickel]]. Cadmium and nickel share some toxicological pathways (ROS generation, tight junction disruption), suggesting probiotics may have a role in reducing [[dietary-nickel-exposure]]. This remains to be tested directly.

## Connections

Relates to [[cadmium]], [[oxidative-stress]], and potentially [[dietary-nickel-exposure]]. The tight junction disruption mechanism parallels findings in [[ding-2021-nickel-pregnancy-complications|Ding et al. (2021)]] where pregnancy complications weakened the placental barrier to nickel.
