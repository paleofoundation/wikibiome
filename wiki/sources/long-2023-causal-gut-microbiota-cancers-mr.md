# Causal Relationship between Gut Microbiota and Cancers: A Two-Sample Mendelian Randomisation Study

## Key Claims

- 11 causal relationships were identified between genetic liability in the gut microbiome and cancer, including those involving the genus *Bifidobacterium*; 17 strong associations and 24 additional associations were found across multiple datasets
- For colorectal cancer specifically, genus *Tyzzerella3* (OR = 0.991, P = 5.43 x 10^-4) and genus *Ruminococcustorquesgroup* (OR = 1.014, P = 1.63 x 10^-3) were causally associated at the genome-wide significance threshold
- At a less stringent threshold, *Verrucomicrobiales*, *Verrucomicrobiae*, *Verrucomicrobiaceae*, family *Verrucomicrobiaceae*, class *Verrucomicrobiae*, and class *Deltaproteobacteria* were all causally associated with CRC
- Phylum *Actinobacteria* and class *Actinobacteria* were causally associated with both breast cancer and lung cancer, representing a shared microbiome-cancer axis
- Reverse MR analysis showed that cancers also causally influence the gut microbiota, demonstrating bidirectional causality

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive two-sample bidirectional MR analysis of 8 cancer types using gut microbiota GWAS data
- **Data sources**: MiBioGen consortium GWAS (18,340 participants, 24 cohorts) for microbiome exposure; IEU Open GWAS Project for cancer outcomes including UK Biobank, ILCCO, BCAC, FINNGEN, and others
- **Cancer types**: Breast, colorectal, lung, prostate, ovarian, endometrial, head and neck, and gastric cancer
- **Methods**: IVW, weighted mode, MR-Egger, weighted median, and MR-PRESSO for MR analysis; Steiger directionality test; multiple testing correction at each taxonomic level
- **Sensitivity**: Cochrane Q statistics showed no significant heterogeneity (P > 0.05); MR-PRESSO revealed no outliers
