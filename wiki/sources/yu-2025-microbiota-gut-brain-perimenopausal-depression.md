# Yu 2025 — Microbiota-Gut-Brain Axis in Perimenopausal Depression (Review)

## Key Findings

- **Perimenopausal depression (PMD)** characterized by decreased Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio, with **Lachnospira**, **Roseburia**, and **Coprococcus** depleted; **Eggerthella**, **Alistipes**, and **Flavonifractor** enriched as pro-inflammatory taxa ([[yu-2025-microbiota-gut-brain-perimenopausal-depression]], review).
- **Four inflammatory signaling pathways** mediate gut microbiota → brain effects in PMD: (1) TLR4/NF-κB, (2) NLRP3 inflammasome, (3) JAK-STAT, (4) SCFA-mediated immune modulation ([[yu-2025-microbiota-gut-brain-perimenopausal-depression]]).
- **SCFAs** (butyrate, propionate, acetate) are key mediators — decreased in PMD due to SCFA-producer depletion. Butyrate strengthens intestinal barrier, activates GPR109A, inhibits HDAC ([[yu-2025-microbiota-gut-brain-perimenopausal-depression]]).
- **Estrogen decline** directly drives dysbiosis: ovariectomized (OVX) rats show decreased Lactobacillus, Clostridium, Eubacterium and increased Cyanobacteria ([[yu-2025-microbiota-gut-brain-perimenopausal-depression]]).
- **Treatment comparison:** estrogen therapy (effective but cancer/thrombosis risks), SSRIs (side effects, drug resistance in 30-40%), gut microbiota modulation (high safety, potential for combination therapy) ([[yu-2025-microbiota-gut-brain-perimenopausal-depression]]).
- **Butyrate** has anti-inflammatory, anti-depressant effects: inhibits microglia-mediated neuroinflammation via PPARγ/TLR4/NF-κB signaling ([[yu-2025-microbiota-gut-brain-perimenopausal-depression]]).

Invited review in Expert Reviews in Molecular Medicine. Comprehensive synthesis of gut microbiota metabolites, inflammatory pathways, and clinical applications in perimenopausal depression.
