# Key Claims

- **T1D patients have less diverse gut microbiota** with increased prevalence of Bacteroidetes taxa and aberrant metabolomic profile
- **Environmental factors** (birth mode, diet, antibiotic use) modulate gut microbiota and potentially contribute to T1D
- **Higher levels of diversity** along with presence of beneficial microbes and metabolites can act as protectors against T1D onset
- **Gut virome function** as potential protectors or triggers of T1D onset
- **Both animal and human studies** provide comprehensive evidence on environmental-microbiome-T1D axis

# Methods & Findings

Comprehensive review of both animal and human studies:

- Large longitudinal human studies (TEDDY, DIABIMMUNE, BABYDIET) reviewed
- Environmental factors: birth mode, breastfeeding, diet, antibiotics
- Microbiota-produced metabolites, proteins, and gut virome discussed as mediators

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Environmental zinc exposure (dietary, supplemental) is a modifiable factor that shapes T1D-relevant microbiome
- **[[iron]]**: Iron fortification practices in different countries may explain geographic variation in T1D incidence
- **[[selenium]]**: Geographic selenium variation correlates with T1D incidence patterns
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Environmental metal exposure is a key missing variable in longitudinal T1D microbiome studies
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Metal-environment-microbiome interaction drives the [[dysbiosis]] cascade preceding T1D
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Environmental factors shape [[immune-balance]] through metal-microbiome mediated pathways
