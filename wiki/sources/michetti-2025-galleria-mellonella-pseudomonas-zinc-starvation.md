# Why This Paper Matters

This paper validates Galleria mellonella (greater wax moth larvae) as an in vivo model for studying host [[nutritional-immunity]] responses to bacterial infection, specifically zinc withholding. For the wiki's [[mis-metallation]] framework, the study is significant because it demonstrates that an invertebrate host mounts a zinc-restriction response analogous to mammalian nutritional immunity -- upregulating zinc transporters to redistribute metal away from the pathogen. Critically, P. aeruginosa responds by activating its Zur-controlled zinc acquisition arsenal (znuABC, zrmABCD/pseudopaline), and disruption of bacterial zinc uptake significantly reduces virulence. This confirms that the battle for zinc between host and pathogen is a universal feature of infection biology, not limited to mammals.

# Key Claims

- **G. mellonella larvae upregulate several zinc transporters** upon P. aeruginosa infection, suggesting an active redistribution of zinc in response to the pathogen -- analogous to mammalian [[nutritional-immunity]]
- P. aeruginosa colonizing G. mellonella larvae **induces Zur-regulated genes** (znuABC, zrmABCD), consistent with bacterial zinc starvation during infection
- **Disruption of bacterial zinc uptake** (znuA mutant) significantly reduces P. aeruginosa virulence in G. mellonella, confirming that zinc acquisition is essential for pathogenesis
- The **zrmABCD operon** encodes the pseudopaline metallophore system: pseudopaline is synthesized intracellularly, secreted to scavenge extracellular zinc (including from host proteins like calprotectin), and reimported via TonB-dependent transport
- The **czcABCD operon** (resistance-nodulation-division efflux system) exports excess Zn, Cd, and Co from the cytoplasm/periplasm; it is upregulated upon macrophage phagocytosis, suggesting a response to toxic metal burst in the phagolysosome
- P. aeruginosa thrives in **zinc-limited environments** (like CF lungs) by activating multiple Zur-controlled zinc acquisition systems
- G. mellonella can serve as a **preliminary screening tool** for identifying novel players in host-pathogen metal competition, offering ethical and cost advantages over mammalian models
- Bacteria rapidly colonize G. mellonella hemolymph, reaching a plateau at 18 hpi with no larvae surviving beyond 40 hpi

# Methods & Findings

## G. mellonella Infection Model
- Last-instar larvae injected with P. aeruginosa PA14 (25 c.f.u. per larva)
- Bacteria localize to hemolymph, reaching plateau numbers by 18 hpi
- Time-to-death experiments confirm virulence differences between WT and zinc uptake mutants
- Competition assays using strain pairs at 1:1 input ratios measure fitness via competitive index (CI)

## Host Zinc Response
- Luminescence-based promoter-reporter constructs (PzrmA-lux, PczcA-lux, PrpsL-lux) monitored in infected larvae
- Zinc acquisition genes (zrmABCD) upregulated during infection, detectable as luminescence in intact larvae
- Host zinc transporter gene expression increases upon infection
- Pattern resembles mammalian zinc redistribution during infection

## Bacterial Zinc Acquisition Systems
- ZnuABC: ATP-dependent ABC transporter; ZnuA is the periplasmic zinc-binding subunit; Zur-repressed
- ZrmABCD: metallophore (pseudopaline) biosynthesis and import system; pseudopaline scavenges zinc from host calprotectin
- Both systems are significantly overexpressed in P. aeruginosa isolates from infected tissues
- AmiA deletion (amidase involved in cell wall recycling) also affects zinc acquisition
