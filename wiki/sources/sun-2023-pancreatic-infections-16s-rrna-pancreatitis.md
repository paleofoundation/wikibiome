# Key Claims

- [[16S rRNA sequencing]] of pancreatic fluid identified 660 bacterial strains (450 aerobic, 256 anaerobic) in 123 genera from severe/critical acute [[pancreatitis]] patients, far exceeding the 96 species detected by aerobic culture
- Pancreatic infections originate not only from the gut but also from oral cavity and airways, with intestines (43%), anaerobes (43%), and oral cavity/airways (17%, anaerobes 24%) as isolation sources
- Polymicrobial infections were found in 98.21% of samples, with dynamic bacterial profiles changing significantly over time
- Low-abundance bacteria initially ignored by culture can become dominant pathogens in later disease stages

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: 78 pancreatic fluid samples from 56 SAP and CAP patients (mean age 42, 69.64% male); V3-V4 region 16S rRNA sequencing on NovaSeq platform
- **Dominant aerobes**: *Klebsiella pneumoniae* (48.08% of samples), *Acinetobacter baumannii*, *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*, *Escherichia coli*, *Stenotrophomonas maltophilia*
- **Dominant anaerobes**: *Bacteroides fragilis* (16/56), *B. kribbi* (8/56), *B. ovatus* (7/56), *Dialister invisus* (7/56), *Olsenella uli* (7/56)
- **Culture vs NGS**: Aerobic culturing identified 21.33% (96/450) of bacterial species; 16S rRNA detected 3x more diversity including anaerobes
- **Dynamic changes**: When sampling interval >2 weeks, bacterial composition changed significantly; low-abundance species could become dominant over time
- **SAP vs CAP**: No significant difference in alpha diversity (Shannon) between groups; similar abundance of common species (*A. baumannii*, *K. pneumoniae*, *S. maltophilia*) in both

# Connections

- Bacterial translocation from gut to pancreas links to [[microbiome and disease progression]] in pancreatic diseases
- *Klebsiella* and *Acinetobacter* dominance connects to [[antibiotics]] resistance patterns in pancreatic infections
- Dynamic polymicrobial nature relevant to [[microbiome-targeted therapy]] and [[phage therapy]] approaches
- 16S rRNA NGS superiority over culture supports broader adoption of [[metagenomic analysis]] in clinical settings
