# Antidepressants on Multiple Sclerosis: A Review of In Vitro and In Vivo Models

## Key Claims

- Antidepressants show multi-target beneficial effects in MS models beyond depression treatment: neuroprotection, immunomodulation, promotion of oligodendrocyte differentiation, and reduction of demyelination
- Fluvoxamine (SSRI) enhances neural stem cell proliferation via Notch signaling pathway; at low doses (1-5 nM) promotes astrocyte, neuron, and oligodendrocyte differentiation; in EAE rats reduces IFN-gamma, increases IL-4, reduces demyelination areas by 0.81%, and decreases IFN-gamma/IL-4 ratio (Th1 indicator)
- Venlafaxine (SNRI) reduces secretion of TNF-alpha, IFN-gamma, and IL-6 dose-dependently in MOG-specific encephalitogenic T cells; in EAE mice at 60 mg/kg/d reduces IL-12 p40, TNF-alpha, IFN-gamma, and inflammation-related chemokines (Ccl2, Ccl5); increases BDNF expression at high doses
- Tricyclic antidepressants (clomipramine, desipramine, imipramine, trimipramine): neuroprotective via iron chelation, preventing neuronal loss; reduce T-cell and B-cell proliferation; suppress TNF-alpha secretion
- Doxepin reduces microglial activation and axonal damage in EAE mice
- Clomipramine achieves high brain-to-plasma ratio (751 nM brain vs 28 uM spinal cord), suggesting effective CNS penetration
- About 86% of depressive MS patients receive antidepressant therapy; 50% of MS patients have cognitive impairment -- antidepressants like vortioxetine may enhance cognition
- SSRIs carry risk of sexual dysfunction (already prominent in MS, reaching 85% in females)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review; PubMed, Cochrane, Scopus search through April 2021; 4 in vitro and 16 in vivo studies included
- **In vitro findings (Table 1)**: Fluvoxamine on murine eNSCs (Notch upregulation); fluvoxamine on EAE rat blood/spinal cord (IL-4 increase, IFN-gamma decrease); clomipramine on human neurons (iron chelation, neuroprotection); desipramine/trimipramine on splenocytes (T-cell/B-cell suppression); venlafaxine on T cell clones (cytokine suppression)
- **In vivo findings (Table 1 continued)**: Fluvoxamine in Lewis EAE rats; imipramine in C57BL/6 EAE mice (reduced parenchymal inflammation); doxepin in EAE mice (reduced microglia activation); clomipramine in EAE mice; venlafaxine in SJL/J EAE mice (comprehensive immune suppression)
- **Key mechanisms**: Suppression of Th1/Th17 cytokines; promotion of Th2 responses; oligodendrocyte differentiation via Notch pathway; iron chelation preventing iron-mediated neurotoxicity; reduced demyelination
- **Limitations**: Only preclinical data (in vitro and EAE models); no human clinical trials specifically evaluating antidepressant effects on MS progression; potential adverse effects (sexual dysfunction, augmentation of CD8+ T cells); need for individualized approach
