# Key Claims

- [[pancreatic-cancer]] is a major cause of cancer-associated mortality with an overall 5-year survival rate of less than 7%, and is predicted to become the second leading cause of cancer death in developed countries
- Risk factors include tobacco smoking (2-3 fold increased risk), [[type-2-diabetes]], obesity, chronic [[pancreatitis]] (>10-fold risk increase), and familial history (5-10% of cases)
- Over 90% of tumors carry activating mutations in the [[metal-carcinogenesis|KRAS]] oncogene, with additional frequent inactivation of CDKN2A (95%), TP53 (50-75%), and DPC4/SMAD4 (50%)
- The dense tumor [[metal-carcinogenesis|stroma]] and [[metal-carcinogenesis|tumor microenvironment]] driven by pancreatic stellate cells contribute to poor vascularization and treatment resistance

# Methods/Findings

- **Epidemiology**: Estimated 367,000 new cases worldwide in 2015; highest rates in USA (12-15/100,000 in men) and among Black populations (30-50% higher than Whites)
- **Genetic landscape**: Average of 63 genetic abnormalities per tumor organized in 12 core signaling pathways; high-penetrance genes include BRCA2, p16/CDKN2A, ATM, STK11, and PALB2
- **Carcinogenesis**: Progression from pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PanIN) through grades 1-3 to invasive carcinoma, paralleled by successive accumulation of mutations
- **Cancer stem cells**: 1-5% of tumor population with markers CD24+, CD44+, EMSA, CD133+, ALDH+; resistant to chemotherapy and radiotherapy
- **Tumor stroma**: Pancreatic stellate cells produce collagen, fibronectin, and matrix metalloproteinases; responsible for poor vascularization characteristic of pancreatic cancer
- **Signaling**: Aberrant activation of EGFR, HER2/HER3, IGF1R, FGFRs, c-Met and developmental pathways (Wnt, Hedgehog, Notch) reactivated in cancer

# Connections

- [[type-2-diabetes]] both a risk factor for and consequence of early pancreatic cancer -- long-term diabetes doubles risk
- Dietary factors linked to risk include high intake of saturated [[short-chain-fatty-acids|fatty acids]], low vegetable/fruit intake, and red/processed meat consumption
- The tumor [[gut-microbiome|microbiome]] within the dense stroma may influence treatment response and immune surveillance
- [[pancreatitis|Chronic pancreatitis]] increases risk more than 10-fold, linking inflammation to carcinogenesis
