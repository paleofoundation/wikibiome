# Key Claims

- **Class Alphaproteobacteria** and genus *FamilyXIIIAD3011group* showed positive association with [[postpartum-depression]] risk
- **Family Clostridialesvadinbb60group**, family *Veillonellaceae*, genus *Ruminococcaceae UCG011* showed negative (protective) correlation with PPD risk
- **Inflammatory factors CCL5 and CCL3** demonstrated negative correlations with PPD risk
- **No mediating role** of inflammatory factors between gut microbiota and PPD was observed
- Genetic evidence confirms gut microbiota and inflammatory factors independently contribute to PPD pathogenesis

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Two-step and two-sample MR analyses using IVW method; heterogeneity and horizontal pleiotropy tests
- **Data**: GWAS summary data from MiBioGen, FinnGen, and UK Biobank
- **Key implication**: Gut microbiota and [[inflammation]] factors act as independent causal contributors rather than through mediation
