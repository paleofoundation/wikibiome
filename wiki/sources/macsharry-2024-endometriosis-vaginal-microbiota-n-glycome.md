# Key Claims

- [[Endometriosis]] patients have a distinct vaginal microbiota characterized by loss of dominant *Lactobacillus* species, especially *L. iners*, and increased bacterial diversity
- Endometriosis-specific bacteria include *Anaerococcus senegalensis*, *Prevotella jejuni*, *Porphyromonas bennonis*, and *Anaerococcus octavius*
- Trigalactosylated and triantennary serum glycans and urine core fucosylated mono-antennary glycans from IgG correlated with vaginal presence of *A. senegalensis*
- Four previously unreported modified N-glycans on IgG were identified in urine of endometriosis patients
- The study reveals interconnections between vaginal microbiota, the serum and urine glycome, and [[endometriosis]]

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Case-control, shotgun metagenomic sequencing of vaginal swabs with glycome profiling
- **Participants**: 40 women (19 controls, 11 mild/minimal endometriosis, 10 moderate/severe endometriosis)
- **Microbiota findings**: Four distinct vaginal microbiota groups identified; endometriosis patients enriched in microbiome groups 3 and 4 with highest alpha diversity
- **Glycome findings**: 181 N-glycans from all glycoproteins characterized in urine; endometriosis-associated pool had more complex glycans with increased branching, sialylation, and fucosylation
- **Correlations**: *A. senegalensis* abundance correlated with whole serum glycan features (rho=0.50, p=0.023)

# Relevance to Wiki

This study provides novel evidence linking vaginal [[dysbiosis]] to systemic glycosylation changes in [[endometriosis]], suggesting that microbiota alterations at mucosal surfaces have downstream metabolic consequences detectable in serum and urine. The identification of specific endometriosis-associated bacteria and their correlation with glycan [[biomarkers]] supports the concept of microbiome-driven systemic effects relevant to the [[bacterial-contamination-hypothesis]]. The loss of protective *Lactobacillus* species connects to broader themes of [[oxidative-stress]] and immune dysregulation in endometriosis.
