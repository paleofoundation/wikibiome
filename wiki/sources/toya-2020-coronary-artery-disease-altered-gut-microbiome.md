# Coronary Artery Disease Is Associated with an Altered Gut Microbiome Composition

## Summary

Case-control study at Mayo Clinic comparing gut microbiome composition between 53 advanced CAD patients and 53 age-, sex-, race-, and BMI-matched controls using 16S rRNA V3-V5 sequencing. Demonstrated significantly reduced microbial diversity and specific taxonomic shifts in CAD patients.

## Key Findings

### Study Design
- 213 patients enrolled undergoing cardiovascular risk assessment at Mayo Clinic (2013-2018)
- 96 patients with advanced CAD (>50% luminal stenosis) and 117 without advanced CAD
- 53 matched pairs created for microbiome analysis
- Exclusions: prior GI surgery, antibiotics, probiotics, IBD, autoimmune diseases

### Alpha Diversity
- Chao-1 index significantly decreased in CAD vs. controls (P = 0.002)
- Shannon index significantly decreased in CAD (P = 0.014)
- Number of observed OTUs significantly decreased (P = 0.001)
- Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio not significantly different (P = 0.61)

### Taxonomic Changes
- 23 differentially abundant taxa at genus level; 12 belonged to [[lachnospiraceae]] family
- Three taxa significant after multiple comparison correction:
 - *Lachnospiraceae Anaerosporobacter* (decreased in CAD, Q = 0.017)
 - *Lachnospiraceae NK4B4* group (decreased in CAD, Q = 0.028)
 - *Ruminococcus Gauvreauii* (decreased in CAD, Q = 0.017)
- Five taxa with >2 log-fold changes and >0.002 maximum proportion including *Ruminococcus gnavus* (enriched in CAD)

### CAD Risk Association
- Lower *Ruminococcus Gauvreauii* and higher *Ruminococcus Gnavus* significantly associated with CAD after adjustment for diabetes mellitus and dyslipidemia (P = 0.007 and P = 0.004)
- Associations remained significant after medication adjustment
- Lachnospiraceae family members known to produce [[butyrate]]

## Relevance to the Wiki

Provides direct evidence linking reduced gut microbial diversity and depletion of butyrate-producing [[lachnospiraceae]] family members to [[cardiovascular-disease|coronary-artery-disease]]. The enrichment of *Ruminococcus gnavus* (a pro-inflammatory species) in CAD parallels findings from [[jie-2017-gut-microbiome-acvd]] and [[fromentin-2022-microbiome-metabolome-cardiometabolic-spectrum]].
