# Shi 2024 — Dietary Patterns and Premenstrual Disorders in Chinese College Students

## Key Findings

- **Traditional South China Diet (TSCD)** — characterized by high consumption of rice, red meat, poultry, and fresh vegetables — inversely associated with PMDs (fully adjusted OR=0.88, 95%CI 0.78–1.00, p=0.043; dose-response p-trend=0.035) ([[shi-2024-dietary-patterns-premenstrual-disorders-china]], cross-sectional, n=1,382).
- **24.4%** of participants met PMD criteria (307 PMS, 30 probable PMDD) ([[shi-2024-dietary-patterns-premenstrual-disorders-china]]).
- Association held for **both PMS and PMDD** subtypes (PMS OR=0.87, PMDD OR=0.95, p-for-difference=0.678) ([[shi-2024-dietary-patterns-premenstrual-disorders-china]]).
- Traditional North China Diet and Lacto-ovo Vegetarian Diet showed **no association** with PMDs.
- TSCD shares similarities with the **Mediterranean diet** in promoting protein diversity and fresh vegetable intake.
- Inverse association also noted for **rice consumption** specifically (adjusted OR=0.93, p=0.048).
- Participants with PMDs more likely to have **depression or anxiety** (20.5% vs. 9.4%, p<0.001) and **adverse childhood experiences**.

## Methods

Cross-sectional analysis from the Care of Premenstrual Emotion (COPE) cohort at Sichuan University, China (2021–2023). PMDs assessed via Calendar of Premenstrual Experiences (C-PME). Dietary patterns via PCA of food frequency questionnaire (12 food groups). Three dietary patterns identified: TNCD, TSCD, LVD. Logistic regression with full adjustment (age, school, BMI, ACEs, alcohol).
