## Key Findings

- **1,752 articles** addressing the intersection of inflammation and ASD have been published, with a consistent upward trend and notable acceleration since 2018 ([[chen-2025-inflammation-asd-bibliometric-analysis|this study]], expert-opinion).
- **"Children"** was the most frequently occurring keyword, followed by "oxidative stress" and "brain," reflecting the field's focus on pediatric neuroinflammatory mechanisms.
- **Keyword burst analysis** revealed distinct temporal phases of research focus: IBD and cytokine production dominated earlier periods, while microbiota, gut-brain axis, and neuroinflammation emerged as the most recent burst keywords — indicating the field is actively converging on gut-immune-brain connections.
- **Brain Behavior and Immunity** was identified as the most prominent journal, with the University of California System as the most productive institutional contributor.
- **The United States and China** were the two most productive countries, with increasing collaboration networks between European and Asian institutions.

## Methods

- **Study type**: Bibliometric analysis using CiteSpace and VOSviewer software.
- **Data source**: Web of Science Core Collection database.
- **Search strategy**: Articles addressing both inflammation and autism spectrum disorder, with no date restrictions.
- **Analysis parameters**: Publication trends, country/institution/author productivity, journal analysis, keyword co-occurrence networks, keyword burst detection, and co-citation analysis.
- **Timeframe covered**: Publications through 2024.

## Detailed Summary

This bibliometric analysis maps the research landscape of inflammation in ASD, providing a quantitative overview of how the field has evolved. The study is valuable not for novel biological findings but for identifying where the research community's attention is directed and which topics are emerging.

The keyword burst analysis is the most informative component for WikiBiome purposes. Early research focused on established immunological concepts (cytokine production, immune markers), but the most recent keyword bursts center on the gut microbiota and gut-brain axis — confirming that the field is actively investigating the microbial dimension of neuroinflammation in ASD. This validates WikiBiome's focus on the microbiome-inflammation-neurodevelopment connection.

The co-citation analysis reveals that foundational papers on maternal immune activation (MIA), microglial activation, and cytokine profiles in ASD are the intellectual backbone of this field. The prominence of oxidative stress as a keyword theme aligns with the NAC intervention rationale (see [[hardan-2012-nac-pilot-trial-autism-children]]) and with broader metal-mediated oxidative damage concepts.
