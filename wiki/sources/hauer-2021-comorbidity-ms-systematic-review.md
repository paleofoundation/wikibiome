# A Global View of Comorbidity in Multiple Sclerosis: A Systematic Review

## Key Claims

- Multiple comorbidities are robustly increased in PwMS: mood disorders, cardiovascular disease, other autoimmune conditions, migraine, RLS, and epilepsy are all elevated compared to general population
- Depression prevalence in PwMS ranges from 21.1-57.6% across studies; anxiety 35-49.5%; both are associated with rapid disease progression, increased EDSS, and reduced QoL
- Cardiovascular comorbidities are underestimated in MS despite being the second or third most common cause of death; diabetes, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia are typically elevated
- Cancer rates in PwMS are paradoxically lower than the general population, which warrants further investigation into shared immune mechanisms
- Comorbidities can precede MS diagnosis: anemia, epilepsy, and bipolar disorder reported before MS diagnosis in some cases, suggesting shared pathogenic pathways
- DMT use may interact with comorbidities: interferon beta and glatiramer acetate associated with increased cardiovascular risk factors; some DMTs may increase cancer risk
- RLS associated with faster MS progression; migraine with more symptomatic course; hypertension with structural brain changes; asthma and RA associated with MS relapse
- Metabolic disorders (insulin resistance, dyslipidemia, diabetes) tend to be higher in PwMS with elevated markers of inflammation and oxidative stress

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review; PubMed search Nov 2009-Nov 2019; 121 articles from 1917 identified
- **Comorbidity categories**: Cardiovascular (14 studies), psychiatric (42 studies), epilepsy, migraine/pain, RLS, autoimmune, metabolic, cancer
- **Geographic distribution**: Predominantly European and North American studies; no studies from Africa; limited Asia data; latitudinal gradient in MS likely affects comorbidity representation
- **Key finding**: Comorbidities associated with MS progression and disability; early recognition may improve treatment outcomes
- **Limitations**: Heterogeneous study designs; varying definitions and measurement tools; most studies cross-sectional; recall bias; limited regional representation
