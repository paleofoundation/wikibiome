# Baicalin Promotes β-1,3-Glucan Exposure in Candida albicans

## Key Findings

- **Baicalin unmasks beta-glucan**: The natural flavonoid baicalin (from *Scutellaria baicalensis*) promotes β-1,3-glucan exposure on *C. albicans* SC5314 cell surface by disrupting cell wall architecture. This reverses the immune cloaking that allows Candida to evade Dectin-1-mediated recognition [[pan-2024-baicalin-betaglucan-exposure-candida-macrophage]], in-vitro.

- **Enhanced macrophage killing**: Baicalin-pretreated *C. albicans* was phagocytosed more efficiently by RAW264.7 macrophages, with increased [[tnf-alpha|TNF-α]] and [[il-1beta|IL-1β]] production — demonstrating that glucan unmasking restores immune recognition [[pan-2024-baicalin-betaglucan-exposure-candida-macrophage]].

- **Transcriptomic mechanism**: 842 differentially expressed genes identified. Cell wall organization genes (GSC1 — β-1,3-glucan synthase; CHS3 — chitin synthase; ENG1 — endo-1,3-β-glucanase; MKC1 — cell wall integrity MAPK) were significantly altered, indicating baicalin disrupts the masking machinery at the gene expression level [[pan-2024-baicalin-betaglucan-exposure-candida-macrophage]].

- **Environmental signals**: The paper notes that acidic pH, iron-replete conditions, hypoxia, and lactate all regulate β-1,3-glucan exposure — connecting masking to the metabolic environment controlled by the microbiome.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- **[[functional-shielding]]** — baicalin as a pharmacological tool to collapse Candida's immune cloak, potentially exposing both the fungus AND its bacterial partners (P. gingivalis, S. aureus) to immune clearance.
- **[[candida-albicans]]** — beta-glucan masking mechanism; natural product intervention.
- **Drug repurposing**: Baicalin joins caspofungin and other echinocandins as beta-glucan unmasking agents — but from traditional Chinese medicine rather than synthetic antifungals.
