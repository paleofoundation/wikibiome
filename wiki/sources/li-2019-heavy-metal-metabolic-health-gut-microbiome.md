# Key Claims

- Both [[arsenic]] and [[cadmium]] exposure at environmentally relevant levels significantly perturb the gut metabolome and alter metabolic health-associated gut microbiota
- Cd exposure caused a **significant decrease** in microbial diversity (p=0.0028) while As exposure produced non-significant diversity decreases
- Both metals affected bile acid metabolism and amino acid profiles, with shared impacts on butyrate-producing bacteria
- Several metabolically important genera including Blautia, Eisenbergiella, Anaerostipes were affected by both treatments
- The metabolic changes are linked to type 2 diabetes (T2DM) pathways, suggesting environmental metal exposure may contribute to metabolic disease progression
- Integrative bipartite network analysis revealed genera with significantly changed numbers of interactions with metabolites under metal exposure

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original animal experiment; C57BL/6 mice treated with vancomycin then exposed to 50 ppm CdCl2 or 50 ppm NaAsO2 in drinking water for 2 weeks
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA gene amplicon sequencing, untargeted LC-MS/MS metabolomics, mass spectral molecular networking (GNPS), random forest classifier analysis
- Cd treatment: 5 phyla and 42 genera significantly differentially abundant; Bacteroidetes significantly decreased; Proteobacteria and Tenericutes increased
- As treatment: 2 phyla differentially abundant; Bacteroidetes decreased; Tenericutes decreased
- Key genera downregulated by Cd: Clostridium_XIVb, Syntrophococcus, Cellulosyliticum, Blautia, Anaeroplasma, Gemmiger, Intestinimonas
- Metabolome: Cd treatment explained 7% of variance (p<0.001); As treatment explained 5.1% (p=0.0456)
- As exposure uniquely affected bile acid molecular families; many features structurally related to bile acids showed increased abundance
- Cd exposure: amino acids (valine, aspartic acid, methionine, tyrosine, norleucine) and alpha-Cyano-4-hydroxycinnamic acid differentially abundant
- Bipartite network analysis identified Eisenbergiella, Anaerostipes, Syntrophococcus, Oribacterium, Hespellia with significantly changed metabolite interaction numbers
