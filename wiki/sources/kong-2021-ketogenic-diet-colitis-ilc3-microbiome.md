# Key Claims

- Ketogenic diet (KD) alleviates DSS-induced colitis in mice by reducing colonic group 3 innate lymphoid cells (ILC3s) and their effector cytokines (IL-17a, IL-22, IL-18, Ccl4)
- KD uniquely increases Akkermansia abundance while low-carbohydrate diet (LCD) increases [[escherichia-coli|Escherichia/Shigella]], demonstrating diet-specific microbiome effects
- FMT from KD-fed mice to germ-free mice conferred colitis protection, proving the KD effect is microbiome-dependent
- KD protects intestinal barrier function (increased Occludin, ZO-1, MUC-2 expression) and reduces serum LPS levels
- LCD exacerbates colitis despite sharing carbohydrate restriction with KD, indicating that fat quality/ketone production is the critical variable

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Experimental animal study (DSS-induced colitis in C57BL/6J mice) with FMT validation in germ-free mice
- **Diets**: KD (high-fat, low-carb ketogenic), LCD (low-carb, high-fat non-ketogenic), ND (normal diet) for 16 weeks pre-colitis
- **Microbiome changes**: KD enriched Akkermansia and Roseburia; LCD enriched Escherichia/Shigella and Oscillibacter; KD reduced Proteobacteria
- **Colitis outcomes**: KD-C mice had longer colons, lower histological scores, lower DAI vs LCD-C and ND-C; LCD-C mice had worst outcomes
- **ILC3 reduction**: KD reduced RORgamma+CD3- ILC3 cells in colonic tissue; LCD increased them; KD significantly lowered IL-17a, IL-22, IL-18, Ccl4 expression
- **FMT validation**: FKD-C (FMT from KD) mice survived DSS challenge better than FLCD-C; had longer colons, lower inflammation scores, fewer ILC3s
- **Metabolomics**: KD altered amino acid metabolism, carbohydrate metabolism; distinct from LCD metabolic profile
