# Xu 2020 -- Gut Microbiome Influences PD-1 Immunotherapy Efficacy in MSS-Type CRC

## Key Claims

- The gut microbiome plays a key role in PD-1 antibody treatment of MSS-type CRC (CT26 tumor model); antibiotics counteracted PD-1 antibody efficacy in inhibiting tumor growth compared to non-antibiotic controls
- Vancomycin-treated mice showed the best anti-tumor response to PD-1 antibody (medium response), while colistin-treated mice showed poor response; broad-spectrum antibiotics (ampicillin+streptomycin+colistin) completely abolished PD-1 efficacy
- *Bacteroides* sp., *Prevotella* sp., *Akkermansia muciniphila* were enriched in the vancomycin group (good response); *Bacteroides* sp. and *Bacteroidales* S24-7 enriched in control group
- Metabolites enriched in the glycerophospholipid metabolic pathway consistent with metagenomic prediction in the vancomycin group; *Prevotella* sp. and *Akkermansia* may maintain PD-1 antibody efficacy by affecting glycerophospholipid metabolism
- 16S rRNA and whole-genome shotgun (WGS) sequencing confirmed clear separation of microbial communities between antibiotic groups

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: CT26 xenograft model in BALB/c mice with four antibiotic groups (control, vancomycin, colistin, broad-spectrum) + PD-1 antibody treatment
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA sequencing, WGS metagenomics, UPLC-MS/MS non-targeted metabolomics, LEfSe analysis, KEGG pathway prediction
- **Key finding**: Changes in gut microbiome affect glycerophospholipid metabolic pathway, altering IFN-gamma and IL-2 expression in the tumor microenvironment
