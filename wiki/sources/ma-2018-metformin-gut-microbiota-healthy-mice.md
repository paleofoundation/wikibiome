# Key Claims

- **Metformin treatment for 30 days** significantly changed 46 gut microbes in healthy mice
- **Verrucomicrobiaceae and Prevotellaceae** classes were enriched by metformin
- **Lachnospiraceae and Rhodobacteraceae** were depleted by metformin
- **Metformin microbiome profile** in diabetes patients positively correlates with colon cancer and type 1 diabetes, indicating a confounding effect on gut microbiome in diabetic patients
- **In healthy (non-diabetic) mice**, metformin exhibits a negative correlation with multiple inflammatory diseases, suggesting a protective anti-inflammatory role

# Methods & Findings

**Animal model study** using 16S rRNA-based microbiome profiling in healthy mice.

- Treated healthy mice with metformin for 30 days
- Observed significant changes in 46 microbial taxa
- Used comparative microbiome tool MicroPattern to compare profiles across disease conditions
- Found context-dependent effects: pro-inflammatory profile in diabetic context vs anti-inflammatory in healthy context
- Results suggest metformin's effects on microbiome depend on metabolic status

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Metformin as microbiome modulator** relevant to ASD given GDM-ASD connection and maternal metformin use during pregnancy
- **Verrucomicrobiaceae enrichment** (includes Akkermansia) by metformin parallels findings in ASD microbiome studies
- **Context-dependent microbiome effects** of medications relevant to understanding drug-microbiome interactions in ASD
