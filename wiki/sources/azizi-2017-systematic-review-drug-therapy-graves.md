# A Systematic Review of Drug Therapy for Graves' Hyperthyroidism

## Key Findings

- **No significant difference in relapse rates between Block-Replace and Titration regimens**: overall RR=0.93 (95% CI 0.84 to 1.03) across 12 trials; relapse rates were 51% for Block-Replace and 54% for Titration; true relapse rates estimated at 63-68% when accounting for losses to follow-up ([[azizi-2017-systematic-review-drug-therapy-graves]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, 23 RCTs)
- **Block-Replace regimen has significantly more adverse effects**: participants were more likely to withdraw due to adverse events with Block-Replace (RR=1.89, 95% CI 1.25 to 2.85); higher rates of rashes (10% vs 3%) and drug withdrawal; agranulocytosis occurred in 5 cases on methimazole, 4 on carbimazole ([[azizi-2017-systematic-review-drug-therapy-graves]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, 23 RCTs)
- **Longer-duration Titration therapy (>18 months) showed fewer relapses than 6 months** (RR=0.63, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.99); 12-18 months is the evidence-based optimal duration ([[azizi-2017-systematic-review-drug-therapy-graves]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, 2 RCTs)
- **No benefit from continued T4 replacement after ATD completion**: combining results of 4 trials showed no significant difference in relapse rates between T4 continuation and no T4 groups (RR=1.09, 95% CI 0.86 to 1.39) ([[azizi-2017-systematic-review-drug-therapy-graves]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, 4 RCTs)
- **High loss to follow-up (27% overall)** and high heterogeneity in several analyses limit confidence in conclusions; none of the trials evaluated ophthalmopathy, quality of life, or economic outcomes ([[azizi-2017-systematic-review-drug-therapy-graves]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, 23 RCTs)

## Methods

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials
- **Search strategy**: Cochrane Central Register (Issue 1, 2004), MEDLINE (1966-July 2004), EMBASE (1980-July 2004), BIOSIS (1985-July 2004), CINAHL (1982-July 2004), HEALTHSTAR (1975-June 2002), trial registries; contacted trial authors for additional data
- **Inclusion criteria**: Published and unpublished randomized/quasi-randomized RCTs of any age, any anti-thyroid drug (carbimazole, propylthiouracil, methimazole, lithium, perchlorate); minimum 6 months treatment and 1 year follow-up from drug cessation
- **Outcomes**: Recurrence of hyperthyroidism (primary), incidence of hypothyroidism, course of ophthalmopathy, adverse effects, thyroid antibody status, weight change, quality of life, economic outcomes
- **Quality assessment**: Concealment of allocation (A/B/C rating), intention-to-treat analysis, blinding, comparability at baseline
- **Quantitative synthesis**: Fixed and random effects meta-analyses; relative risks (RR) with 95% CIs; heterogeneity assessed by I-squared statistic; random effects used when I-squared >50%
- **Trials included**: 23 RCTs meeting criteria, from 77 reports of 23 trials identified from 3923 initial records

## Detailed Summary

### Duration of Anti-Thyroid Therapy

Two studies comparing longer (18 months) vs shorter (6 months) Titration therapy showed significantly fewer relapses with longer duration (RR=0.63, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.99). No significant difference between 12 and 6 months using Block-Replace (RR=0.86, 95% CI 0.52 to 1.43). Two studies using longer Titration durations (12 vs 24 months, 18 vs 42 months) showed no significant additional benefit beyond 18 months (RR=0.88, 95% CI 0.67 to 1.16).

### Block-Replace vs Titration

Across 12 trials (n=1,270 total): Block-Replace relapse rate 51% (322/636) vs Titration 54% (332/634). Overall RR=0.93 (95% CI 0.84 to 1.03, I-squared=0, P=0.19). When analyzing by follow-up duration: 12-24 months showed RR=0.85 (P=0.02 for heterogeneity); >24 months showed RR=0.95 (P=0.75). The Block-Replace regimen used higher drug doses (30-60mg carbimazole or 100mg methimazole with T4 replacement), leading to higher adverse event rates.

### Drug Choice

Among 264 participants on carbimazole: 7% (19) developed rashes, 12% (82 of 714) on methimazole had rashes. Five cases of agranulocytosis on methimazole, 4 on carbimazole (2 taking high-dose 100mg/day). The highest-dose Block-Replace study (100mg carbimazole) had 7 of 17 patients withdraw including 2 agranulocytosis and 5 with rashes.

### T4 After ATD Completion

Three studies compared T4 continuation vs no treatment after ATD courses: combined RR=0.65 (95% CI 0.14 to 3.00, random effects) with marked heterogeneity (I-squared=88%). The discrepant results were driven by one Japanese study showing benefit, possibly due to higher iodine intake. Four studies comparing T4 continuation vs placebo/no T4 after Block-Replace or Titration showed RR=1.09 (95% CI 0.86 to 1.39), confirming no benefit.

### Limitations Identified

High loss to follow-up (27% overall, up to 44% in some studies). Most trials had methodological quality rating B (unclear allocation concealment). No trials assessed ophthalmopathy progression, quality of life, or economic outcomes.
