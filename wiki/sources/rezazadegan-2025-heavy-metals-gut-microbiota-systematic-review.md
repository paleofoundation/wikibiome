## Key Findings

- **Universal dysbiosis across metals**: All four heavy metals (As, Pb, Hg, Cd) consistently disrupted gut microbiota composition across the studies reviewed. **Collinsella** was enriched as a pathobiont across multiple metal exposures (Rezazadegan et al. 2025, systematic review, n=3000+)
- **Prenatal lead depletes Bifidobacterium**: Prenatal Pb exposure specifically depleted **[[bifidobacterium]]** bifidum and B. longum — the same species depleted in pre-T1D infants, establishing a potential upstream driver of the T1D-associated Bifidobacterium deficit
- **Cadmium-leaky gut axis**: Cd exposure was consistently linked to increased intestinal permeability and oxidative stress via microbiome disruption, paralleling the leaky gut phenotype central to T1D pathogenesis
- **Desulfovibrio enrichment**: **[[desulfovibrio]]** was enriched across multiple metal exposures, potentially contributing to hydrogen sulfide production and further barrier damage

## Contradictions / Tensions

This is a general heavy metal-microbiome review, not T1D-specific. The T1D relevance is inferred from pattern matching between metal-induced dysbiosis and T1D-associated dysbiosis.

## Open Questions
