# Key Claims

- Exposure to heavy metals (As, Pb, Hg, Cd) disturbs the composition of [[gut-metal-microbiome|gut microbiota]] and can lead to [[dysbiosis]]
- High levels of As, Pb, and Hg increase the abundance of [[Collinsella]] as a pathobiont
- Heavy metal-induced dysbiosis is related to [[leaky gut]], [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]], and diseases including [[inflammatory bowel disease]] and cancers
- Probiotic treatment and nutritional strategies (high fiber intake, antioxidant-rich diets) should be considered for individuals exposed to heavy metals
- No eligible human studies were found for aluminum exposure and gut microbiota

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review of human observational studies (PECO/PICO approach)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Scopus, ISI Web of Science, Google Scholar; searched up to October 2023
- **Included studies**: 12 studies total -- As (n=5), Pb (n=5), Hg (n=4), Cd (n=1)
- **Quality**: NOS scores ranged 4-9; 7 articles scored >=6 (high quality); study designs included cohort (n=4), cross-sectional (n=6), longitudinal (n=1), nested case-control (n=1)
- **Arsenic findings**:
  - High As increased Bacillaceae, decreased Erysipelotrichales
  - Increased Lactobacillus and Collinsella at moderate levels
  - Catenibacterium negatively associated with As in adults
  - Clostridia negatively associated in both adults and infants
- **Lead findings**:
  - Prenatal Pb exposure negatively associated with Bacteroides caccae, Bifidobacterium, Alistipes indistinctus in childhood
  - Lead associated with increased Proteobacteria in children
  - Collinsella increased with Pb exposure
- **Mercury findings**:
  - Decreased Bacteroidetes, increased Firmicutes with higher Hg
  - Increased Desulfovibrio and Methanogens; decreased Proteobacteria
  - Fish consumption (MeHg source) associated with different patterns than inorganic Hg
- **Cadmium**: Only 1 study; Cd exposure associated with Bifidobacterium changes in infants
- **Nutritional implications**: Probiotics, low-fat diets, and high-fiber diets recommended as countermeasures
