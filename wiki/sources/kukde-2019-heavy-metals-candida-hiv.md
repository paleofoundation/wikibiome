## Summary

This study examined the effect of heavy metal ions (lead, zinc, silver, mercury, and cadmium) on 26 Candida strains isolated from oral swabs of 25 HIV-positive patients. The goal was to determine metal susceptibility patterns and explore heavy metal salts as potential therapeutic alternatives.

## Key Findings

- All 26 (100%) Candida strains were resistant to zinc ions with 100 mM concentration; all were sensitive to cadmium ions at 1 mM
- Maximum 8 (29%) Candida species (PLHA) from which Candida species was isolated had CD4 count 300-399 cells/mm3
- 77% and 100% of Candida strains were susceptible to just 10 mM concentrations of silver and mercury ions, respectively; 100% susceptible to 1 mM cadmium
- Candida strains lost membrane potential gradually in the presence of mercury ions
- Silver and mercury ions inhibit plasma membrane ATPase by binding interactions
- Metal ions including cadmium, mercury, cobalt, and nickel also inhibit plasma membrane ATPase of eukaryotic cells

## Relevance to Wiki

Demonstrates differential heavy metal susceptibility of Candida species in immunocompromised patients, suggesting that certain metals (silver, mercury) could have antifungal applications while others (zinc) permit Candida resistance, relevant to understanding metal-pathogen interactions.
