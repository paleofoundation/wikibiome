# Zhao 2025 — Gut Microbiota-Mediated Pain Sensitization: Mechanisms and Therapeutic Implications

## Key Findings

- **Three mechanistic dimensions** of gut microbiota → pain sensitization ([[zhao-2025-gut-microbiota-pain-sensitization-review]], review):
  1. **GPR43 pathway:** SCFAs (acetate, propionate, butyrate) engage GPR43 on immune cells → Gq/11 → PLC → DAG/IP3 → Ca²⁺ surge → NF-κB + MAPK activation → NLRP3 inflammasome. Paradoxically context-dependent: anti-inflammatory in some contexts, pro-inflammatory in others.
  2. **TLR2 pathway:** bacterial metabolites (lipoproteins, peptidoglycans, LPS) → TLR2 → TIRAP/MyD88 → IRAKs → TRAF6 → TAK1 → NF-κB + MAPK → TNF-α, IL-12, IFN-γ.
  3. **GPR109A/HCAR2 pathway:** butyrate and β-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) → GPR109A → suppresses NF-κB → inhibits NLRP3 inflammasome → reduces IL-1β, TNF-α in CNS. Anti-nociceptive.
- **Central sensitization** in chronic pain: sustained glial activation, dysregulated cytokine networks, maladaptive synaptic potentiation via nociceptive pathways ([[zhao-2025-gut-microbiota-pain-sensitization-review]]).
- **Gut dysbiosis → BBB compromise:** pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β) increase BBB permeability → microglial activation → central sensitization ([[zhao-2025-gut-microbiota-pain-sensitization-review]]).
- **Therapeutic potential:** probiotics, FMT, dietary modifications all show promise for pain management via microbiota modulation ([[zhao-2025-gut-microbiota-pain-sensitization-review]]).
