# Key Claims

- The [[Mediterranean diet]] (MD) offers protection against [[colorectal cancer]] (CRC) through multiple bioactive components: n-3 fatty acids, fiber, olive oil, and polyphenolic compounds
- MD components reduce inflammation via distinct pathways: n-3 fatty acids suppress COX-2 and TNF-alpha; fiber/butyrate inhibit NF-kB signaling; olive oil (EVOO) reduces COX-2, iNOS, STAT3; resveratrol decreases IL-1beta, IL-6, TNF-alpha
- CRC develops via the adenoma-carcinoma sequence over ~20 years, driven by three molecular pathways: CIN (chromosomal instability, 80-85%), MSI (microsatellite instability, ~24%), and CIMP (CpG island methylator phenotype, ~27%)
- High-fiber diets promote [[butyrate]] production, which improves NF-kB signaling suppression, increases TGF-beta, and reduces dendritic cells and memory T lymphocytes in the colon
- EVOO supplementation reduces COX-2, iNOS, TNF-alpha, IL-10, and MCP1 expression; a 7% olive oil diet reduces chronic colitis-related neoplasia in IL-10(-/-) mice
- Red meat and processed meat consumption increases CRC risk via N-nitroso compounds (NOCs) causing KRAS mutations (G > A transitions at codons 12 or 13)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of preclinical and clinical studies (last 10 years)
- **MD scoring systems reviewed**: MDS (0-9), aMED, IMDI, MMDS -- varying in component inclusion and scoring methodology (Table 1)
- **Preclinical anti-inflammatory evidence** (Table 2): Systematic compilation of n-3 fatty acid, fiber, olive oil, and phenolic compound studies in HT29 cells, DSS/AOM rats and mice, IL-10(-/-) models
- **n-3 fatty acids clinical**: EPA (58 mg/day) + DHA (32.5 mg) for 4 weeks decreased IL-1beta, IL-6, TNF-alpha in overweight males; EPA + DHA improved HIS in UC patients
- **Fiber clinical**: 20-30g germinated barley fiber improved endoscopic index in UC patients
- **MD and IBD**: Meta-analysis of 17 RCTs showed MD adoption reduces IBD development; MD pattern associated with reduced CRP, fibrinogen, IL-6, homocysteine over 10-year follow-up
- **MD and CRC epidemiology**: Searched "Mediterranean diet AND colorectal cancer"; 40 studies identified; preclinical and clinical evidence reviewed for cereals, grains, fruits, vegetables, fish, olive oil, red meat, potatoes, butter, sugar/sweets
- **Fish oil mechanisms**: n-3 enriched diet (30%) improved HIS, decreased inflammation; at 3.7% by weight, was protective against bacterial-induced inflammation
