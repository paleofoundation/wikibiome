# Key Claims

- **ASD gut phageome** showed wider diversity and higher abundance compared to typically developing (TD) individuals
- **Significant expansion of Caudoviricetes bacteriophages** in ASD individuals
- **Phages infecting Bacteroidaceae** and prophages encoded within Faecalibacterium were more frequent in ASD than TD
- **Phageome differences** between ASD and TD can be used as potential diagnostic biomarkers
- ASD phageome expansion and diversification can **influence bacterial homeostasis** by imposing pressure on bacterial communities

# Methods & Findings

**Metagenomic analysis** of public microbiota databases of ASD and TD Chinese individuals.

- Analyzed phage protein-coding units (pPCU) to find links between phageome and ASD
- ASD individuals had wider phage diversity and higher abundance
- Caudoviricetes bacteriophages significantly expanded in ASD
- Bacteroidaceae-infecting phages and Faecalibacterium prophages enriched in ASD
- Phage-mediated bacterial community disruption proposed as ASD mechanism

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Virome/phageome** as an underexplored dimension of ASD gut dysbiosis beyond bacteria alone
- **Phage-driven dysbiosis** could explain inconsistent bacterial findings across ASD microbiome studies
- **Faecalibacterium** prophage enrichment may reduce this protective commensal in ASD
