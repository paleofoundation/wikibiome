# Key Claims

- [[cadmium]] exposure induces dose-dependent organ damage (liver, spleen, kidney) and significant gut microbiota perturbations in wild long-tailed dwarf hamsters (Cricetulus longicaudatus)
- This is one of the first studies examining Cd effects on gut microbiota of a **wild rodent species** rather than laboratory models
- Cd exposure significantly increased potentially pathogenic bacteria including Treponema and Prevotella while decreasing Oscillibacter
- At the phylum level, Firmicutes, Proteobacteria, Campylobacter, and Verrucomicrobia decreased with increasing Cd dose; Bacteroidetes increased
- Over half of metabolic functions decreased in Cd-treated groups compared to controls, including biosynthesis and degradation of cysteine, methionine, and lysine
- Pathways related to disease and environmental information processing showed significant increases, particularly repair and phosphotransferase systems

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original experimental study on wild-captured animals
- **Design**: Adult male C. longicaudatus captured from Shanxi Province, China; exposed to CdCl2 at 6, 12, 24, and 48 mg/L in drinking water for 35 days; controls received pure water
- **Methods**: HE staining, 16S rDNA sequencing, metagenome sequencing
- **Histopathology**: Kidney tubular degeneration, ileum inflammation with villi shedding, liver and spleen blood vessel stasis; dose-dependent severity
- **Diversity**: Chao1 index initially increased (day 7) then declined; significant decrease in 48 mg group by day 21; no PCA separation of groups at day 35
- **Community shifts**: At genus level, Duncaniella, Lachnospiraceae, Muribaculaceae relative abundances significantly changed; Lactobacillus decreased at 48 mg
- **Pathogen analysis**: 14 potential pathogens identified in top 10% of microbiota via metagenomics; Treponema significantly increased; Prevotella and Oscillibacter significantly decreased
- **Symbiotic networks**: 48 mg group showed highest positive correlation rate (68.94%) compared to control (63.95%), suggesting simplified but more tightly connected microbial communities
- **Metabolic analysis**: KEGG pathway analysis revealed >50% of metabolic functions decreased; carbohydrate and lipid metabolism, fatty acid metabolism, glycosaminoglycan metabolism notably affected
