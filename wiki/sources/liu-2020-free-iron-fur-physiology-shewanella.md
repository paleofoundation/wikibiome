# Liu & Fang 2020 — Free Iron Content Is Critically Linked to Fur Physiology

## Key Findings
- **The Fur regulon responds to labile (free) iron**, not total cellular iron content, meaning bacteria sense and respond to bioavailable metal pools [[liu-2020-free-iron-fur-physiology-shewanella]]
- Total iron measurements can be misleading — a cell loaded with sequestered iron may still behave as iron-starved if the labile pool is depleted
- This distinction explains why host iron-sequestration strategies (nutritional immunity) work even when total tissue iron is high

## Relevance to WikiBiome
- Critical conceptual foundation: total metal measurements in tissue samples do not predict microbial behavior — only the labile fraction matters
- Directly informs how WikiBiome interprets metallomic signatures: elevated total iron in a disease state may coexist with functional iron starvation for the host
