# Presence of the Siderophores Pyoverdine and Pyochelin in the Extracellular Medium Reduces Toxic Metal Accumulation in Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Increases Bacterial Metal Tolerance

## Key Claims

- The siderophores pyoverdine (PVD) and pyochelin (PCH) produced by *[[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]]* sequester toxic metals in the extracellular medium, reducing their diffusion into bacterial cells
- Both PVD and PCH chelate many metals in addition to [[iron]], including Al3+, Co2+, Cu2+, Eu3+, Ni2+, Pb2+, Tb3+ and Zn2+ (PVD) and Al3+, Co2+, Cu2+, Ni2+, Pb2+ and Zn2+ (PCH)
- Only iron is efficiently transported into the cell via PVD/PCH uptake pathways; other metals are chelated extracellularly but not imported
- Siderophore-deficient strains showed lower metal tolerance and higher intracellular metal accumulation
- Cu2+ and Ni2+ specifically induce PVD production, potentially as a defensive response

## Methods/Findings

- **Organism**: *P. aeruginosa* PAO1 (wild type), PAD07 (PVD-/PCH- mutant), CDC5(pPVR2) (PVD+/PCH-), PAO6297 (PVD-/PCH+)
- **Growth Experiments**:
  - PAD07 (siderophore-deficient) was more sensitive to toxic metals than wild type
  - Adding purified PVD or PCH to growth medium restored metal tolerance
  - Only five metals were toxic at 100 microM to PAD07: Co2+, Cu2+, Ga3+, Ni2+, Sn2+
  - PVD and PCH had beneficial effects on growth in presence of Cu2+ and Ni2+ specifically
- **ICP-AES Metal Accumulation** (cells incubated with 5 microM metals +/- siderophores):
  - Metals in first group (Al3+, Eu3+, Fe3+, Pb2+, Tb3+) incorporated at high levels (2.8-4.2 micromol/g cells)
  - Metals in second group (Cd2+, Cr3+, Cu2+, Ga3+, Mn2+, Ni2+, Sn2+, Ti2+, Zn2+) incorporated at lower levels
  - Pyoverdine decreased uptake of Al3+ and Cu2+ by 79% and 85%, respectively
  - PVD was more efficient than PCH for inhibiting uptake of Eu3+, Ga3+, Pb2+, Sn2+, Tb3+
  - PCH was more efficient for Co2+, Fe3+, Ni2+, Zn2+
  - Siderophores reduced Fe3+ accumulation via the TonB-dependent PVD-Fe and FptA pathways, acting as regulated gatekeepers
- **PVD Production Induction**:
  - Cu2+ and Ni2+ (at 10 microM) specifically increased PVD production by 290% and 380%, respectively, under iron-limited conditions
  - Most other metals repressed PVD synthesis at 100 microM
  - PCH production was decreased by Ni2+ and Tb3+ at 100 microM but increased by several metals including Cu2+, Eu3+, Ga3+, Mn2+

## Connections

- Demonstrates extracellular metal sequestration as a bacterial defense against [[nickel]] and other metals -- directly relevant to [[nickel-toxicity]] in microbial contexts
- The siderophore-metal chelation mechanism complements intracellular efflux systems described in [[alquethamy-2021-acinetobacter-cadmium-resistance]] and [[akbari-2022-metal-homeostasis-streptococci]]
- PVD/PCH chelation of cadmium, copper, and nickel connects to the broader topic of [[metal-homeostasis]] and how bacteria manage multi-metal environments
- The principle that bacterial extracellular chelators reduce metal bioavailability parallels how [[probiotics]] may protect the gut from [[dietary-nickel-exposure]] and other [[heavy-metals]] -- binding metals in the gut lumen before absorption
- The EPS production induced by cadmium in [[cheng-2021-cadmium-enterococcus-metabolic]] may serve a similar extracellular sequestration function to siderophores
- Iron-siderophore pathways discussed here relate to the Fe transport systems reviewed in [[akbari-2022-metal-homeostasis-streptococci]] for Streptococcus species
