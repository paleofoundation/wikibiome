# Key Claims

- **Trace elements in maternal hair** affect diversity and composition of infant gut microbiome at 3, 6, and 12 months after delivery
- **Shannon diversity** in 3-month-old infants correlated positively with selenium and negatively with [[copper]]
- **Bifidobacterium** relative abundance increases under high exposure to aluminum and manganese
- **High levels of [[copper]] and arsenic** exposure may induce enrichment of antibiotic resistance genes (ARGs) in infant gut
- **56 differential metabolites** between first and second visits; 515 between second and third
- Mother-infant microbiome compositions gradually converge during first year of life

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Longitudinal study; 146 mother-infant pairs; stool samples at 3, 6, 12 months; amplicon sequencing (N=353), metagenomics (N=65), metabolomics (N=198); maternal hair trace element analysis (N=119)
- **Setting**: Peking University People's Hospital, Beijing, China; published in Nature Communications
