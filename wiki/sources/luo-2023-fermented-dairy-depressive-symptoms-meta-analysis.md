# Key Claims

- **Fermented dairy foods intake** significantly associated with decreased depression risk (OR=0.89, 95% CI: 0.81-0.98) across 8 studies with 83,533 participants
- **Cheese consumption** significantly associated with decreased depression risk (OR=0.91, 95% CI: 0.84-0.98)
- **Yogurt consumption** also protective (OR=0.84, 95% CI: 0.72-0.99)
- **Superabundant intake** did not further decrease risk, suggesting a threshold effect
- Effects likely mediated through [[gut-brain-axis]] via probiotic content of fermented foods

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Meta-analysis of cohort studies; CNKI and PubMed searched 2010-2022; PRISMA guidelines followed
- **Key implication**: Dietary fermented food intake may serve as accessible intervention for depression prevention
