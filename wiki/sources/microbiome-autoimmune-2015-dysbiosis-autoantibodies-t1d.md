# Key Claims

- **Gut microbiome [[dysbiosis]] precedes and promotes** islet autoantibody development in T1D
- **Autoantibody seroconversion** is associated with specific microbiome compositional changes
- **Microbiome-driven immune activation** leads to loss of tolerance to beta-cell antigens

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc-dependent immune tolerance mechanisms may be disrupted by [[dysbiosis]]-driven zinc depletion
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent antioxidant protection of beta-cells may fail when [[dysbiosis]] impairs selenium absorption
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Autoantibody development represents the consequence of [[gut-metal-microbiome]] disruption
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Transition from tolerance to autoimmunity requires [[immune-balance]] disruption via metal-microbiome axis
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Barrier dysfunction allows microbial antigens to cross-react with beta-cell antigens
