# Key Claims

- **Mediterranean diet influences gut microbiota composition** in T1D children
- **Prevotella and other protective taxa** may be enhanced by Mediterranean dietary patterns
- **Dietary intervention may modulate T1D-associated [[dysbiosis]]** through changes in gut microbiota
- **Diet is a modifiable environmental factor** for T1D microbiome management

# Methods & Findings

Study examining relationship between Mediterranean diet and gut microbiota in T1D children:

- Gut microbiota composition compared in T1D vs healthy children
- Dietary patterns assessed for association with microbial community structure
- Mediterranean diet components (fiber, polyphenols, omega-3) linked to beneficial microbiome changes

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Mediterranean diet provides optimal zinc through legumes, nuts, and seafood -- supporting microbiome diversity
- **[[iron]]**: Mediterranean diet iron sources (plant-based, with vitamin C) promote non-heme iron absorption without excess
- **[[selenium]]**: Mediterranean diet selenium from seafood and whole grains supports antioxidant defense
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Mediterranean dietary pattern optimizes metal availability for beneficial gut bacteria
- **[[probiotics]]**: Mediterranean diet naturally provides fermented foods containing [[probiotics]]
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Dietary correction of metal-nutrient imbalances may address upstream drivers of T1D [[dysbiosis]]
