# Overview

Editorial synthesising **32 articles** from a Research Topic on comorbidities associated with [[autism-spectrum-disorder]]. Covers epilepsy, sleep disorders, GI dysfunction, psychiatric comorbidities, and immune abnormalities. Argues that understanding comorbidities is essential for defining ASD subgroups and improving treatment.

# Key Claims

## High Comorbidity Burden

- ~50% of individuals with ASD have 4+ co-occurring medical conditions
- Only 4% of Swedish ASD cohort (born 1992-2001) had no concomitant disorder
- Comorbidities provide additive risk factors for morbidity and mortality

## Five Thematic Categories

1. **Comorbidities and psychiatric manifestations** -- anxiety, depression, ADHD
2. **Anatomical correlates** -- brain structural differences
3. **Specific examples** -- epilepsy (prevalence 21.5% with intellectual disability), GI disorders
4. **Intellectual disabilities** -- large degree of clinical heterogeneity in both ASD and ID
5. **Mechanisms** -- shared genetic and environmental pathways

## GI Comorbidities

- GI disorders present in 30.5% of ASD patients in hospital-based studies
- GI problems significantly associated with sleep disorders and ID
- *Candida albicans* antibodies positive in 36% of ASD children vs 14% of controls, suggesting fungal [[dysbiosis]]

## Immune Dysregulation

- TNF-alpha levels significantly higher in ASD children
- Autoimmune diseases more frequent in family members of autistic children
- Mothers with T1DM, hypothyroidism, RA more likely to have children with ASD
