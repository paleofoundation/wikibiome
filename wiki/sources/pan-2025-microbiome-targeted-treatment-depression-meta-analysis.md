# Key Claims

- **Microbiome-targeted treatment (MTT)** significantly improved depression symptoms overall (SMD -0.26, 95% CI [-0.32, -0.19], I2=54%)
- **Geographic variation**: Effective in Asia (SMD -0.46) and America (SMD -0.33), but not in Europe (SMD -0.07) or Oceania (SMD 0.00)
- **Duration matters**: < 8 weeks (SMD -0.33) and 8-12 weeks (SMD -0.34) effective; > 12 weeks ineffective
- **In perinatal depressed patients, MTT was not effective** (SMD 0.16, 95% CI [0.01, 0.31])
- Effectiveness was poor in patients with digestive disorders (IBS, chronic diarrhea, constipation)
- 34 RCTs included from 51,570 studies identified

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs; PROSPERO registered; PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Web of Science, CNKI searched through October 2023
- **Key implication**: MTT shows overall efficacy for depression but the perinatal subgroup finding of no benefit is critical for PPD research
