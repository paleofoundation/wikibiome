# Pan 2025 — Microbiome-Targeted Treatment for Depression (Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis)

## Key Findings

- **Microbiome-targeted treatment (MTT)** significantly improved depression symptoms: overall SMD −0.26 (95%CI −0.32 to −0.19, I²=54%) across 34 RCTs ([[pan-2025-microbiome-targeted-treatment-depression-meta-analysis]], systematic review/meta-analysis).
- **Geographic variation:** Asia SMD −0.46 (95%CI −0.56 to −0.36, I²=36%); America SMD −0.33 (I²=60%); **Europe NOT effective** SMD −0.07 (95%CI −0.19 to 0.05, I²=55%); Oceania NOT effective ([[pan-2025-microbiome-targeted-treatment-depression-meta-analysis]]).
- **Duration effects:** <8 weeks effective (SMD −0.33), 8–12 weeks effective (SMD −0.34, I²=57%), **>12 weeks NOT effective** (SMD 0.02, 95%CI −0.12 to 0.17, I²=68%) ([[pan-2025-microbiome-targeted-treatment-depression-meta-analysis]]).
- **NOT effective in perinatal depression** (SMD 0.16, 95%CI 0.01 to 0.31, I²=0%) ([[pan-2025-microbiome-targeted-treatment-depression-meta-analysis]]).
- **Not effective in patients with digestive comorbidities** (IBS, chronic diarrhea, chronic constipation) ([[pan-2025-microbiome-targeted-treatment-depression-meta-analysis]]).
- **Effective in depression without comorbidities** (SMD −0.31, 95%CI −0.40 to −0.22, I²=0%) ([[pan-2025-microbiome-targeted-treatment-depression-meta-analysis]]).

## Methods

PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023483649). Searched PubMed, Embase, Cochrane, Web of Science, CNKI through October 2023. Included RCTs of MTT (probiotics, synbiotics, antibiotics, FMT) vs. placebo/no intervention for depression. 51,570 studies screened → 34 met criteria (51 eligible trials in meta-analysis). Random-effects model for I²>50%. Subgroup analyses by geography, duration, MTT type, comorbidities, % female, depression scales.

## Detailed Summary

This is the **largest meta-analysis of microbiome-targeted treatment for depression** to date. The overall effect size (SMD −0.26) represents a small-to-moderate clinical benefit. The geographic variation is striking — Asian studies show 6–7× larger effects than European studies, potentially reflecting baseline microbiome differences, dietary context, or study quality variation. The finding that >12 weeks of treatment is ineffective suggests possible microbiome adaptation or tolerance to probiotic strains. The perinatal depression non-response is clinically critical — hormonal dominance may override microbiome effects in the peripartum period.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Intervention evidence:** MTT is moderately effective for depression but NOT for perinatal depression — important boundary for PPD/PMDD intervention planning.
- **Duration protocol:** 8–12 week treatment window is optimal; >12 weeks shows no benefit.
- **STOP consideration:** MTT should not be primary recommendation for depression with digestive comorbidities based on this evidence.
- **Primitive 5:** ecological engineering via probiotics/synbiotics — effective but geography- and duration-dependent.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Perinatal depression non-response contradicts the general depression efficacy — supports hormonal-pathway dominance in peripartum period.
- >12-week non-response is unexpected — could reflect microbiome adaptation to probiotic strains, or selection bias in longer trials.

## Open Questions

- Would PMDD-specific MTT trials show efficacy, given PMDD's cyclical (not chronic) nature?
- Does the Asian vs. European efficacy difference reflect baseline microbiome composition or dietary context?
- Could cycling MTT (on/off, synchronized to menstrual phase) avoid the >12-week tolerance?
