# Gut Macrobiotic and Its Metabolic Pathways Modulate Cardiovascular Disease

## Summary

Review covering the metabolic pathways through which gut microbiota metabolites -- [[short-chain-fatty-acids]], [[tmao]], [[bile-acid-metabolism]], and LPS -- impact [[cardiovascular-disease]] including hypertension, atherosclerosis, and heart failure. Includes detailed pathway diagrams of healthy vs. impaired gut barrier states.

## Key Findings

### SCFAs and CVD
- Produced mainly from dietary fiber fermentation; acetic acid most abundant
- SCFAs regulate cardiovascular function through GPR41 and Olfr78 receptors
- Butyrate activates a set of G-protein coupled receptors (GPCRs) that affect metabolic processes and GPR43/GPR41 expressed on immune cells
- In healthy gut: butyrate is high, nitrate is low, NO and iNOS maintain normal oxygen levels, beta-oxidation and PPAR-gamma are active
- In impaired gut barrier: butyrate decreases, nitrate increases, iNOS increases, PPAR-gamma is downregulated, leading to inflammation and insulin resistance

### TMAO Pathway
- TMA produced from dietary choline, phosphatidylcholine, L-carnitine
- Oxidized to TMAO by FMO3 in liver
- ApoE-/- mice infused with choline: higher TMAO and increased atherosclerosis
- *Firmicutes* and *Proteobacteria* contain >65% of choline TMA-producing bacteria
- Oral administration of *E. coprostanoligenes* in rabbits: significant decrease in plasma cholesterol lasting >34 days

### Bile Acids
- Primary BAs synthesized in liver via CYP7A1 (rate-limiting enzyme)
- Converted to secondary BAs by gut bacteria via bile salt hydrolase (BSH) and bile acid dehydratase
- BAs act through FXR and TGR5 to regulate cholesterol metabolism, lipid absorption, and inflammatory responses
- FXR activation inhibits cholesterol absorption and increases cholesterol excretion by macrophages
- Research shows BAs can be reduced by regulating bile acid metabolism via FXR transcription factor

### LPS and Inflammation
- LPS activates NF-kB, TLR4, and STAT-1 signaling
- Promotes M1 macrophage polarization leading to TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL-12, IL-1-beta production
- Triggers decreased phagocytosis, decreased iron recycling, and increased foam cell formation

### Gut Microbiota and Specific CVD Conditions
- **Hypertension**: SCFAs regulate blood pressure through Olfr78 and GPR41; Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio imbalanced; *Prevotella* and *Klebsiella* increased
- **Atherosclerosis**: Reduced *Bacteroidetes* and *Prevotella*; increased *Streptococcus* and *Escherichia*; FMT from atherosclerotic mice induces disease
- **Heart failure**: Decreased *Blautia*, *Collinsella*, *Erysipelotrichaceae*, *Ruminococcaceae*; increased *Campylobacter*, *Shigella*, *Salmonella*
