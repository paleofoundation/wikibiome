# Effects of Trace Elements on Endocrine Function and Pathogenesis of Thyroid Diseases - A Literature Review

## Key Claims

- Trace elements (Fe, Cu, Mn, I, Zn, Se) are part of enzymes involved in [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]] reduction, while [[cadmium]], [[mercury]], and [[lead]] can increase ROS production
- [[iron]] deficiency decreases TPO activity, leading to reduced T3/T4 synthesis and increased TSH; 58% of [[hashimotos-thyroiditis]] patients have Fe deficiency anemia and 75% have vitamin D deficiency
- [[copper]] stimulates T4 production and inhibits excessive T4 absorption in blood cells; elevated Cu may be associated with [[hashimotos-thyroiditis|thyroid cancer]] mortality in women
- [[selenium]] builds deiodinases (DIO1, DIO2, DIO3) essential for T4 to T3 conversion; Se is a component of glutathione peroxidases protecting thyrocytes from oxidative damage
- [[iodine]] is essential for thyroid hormone synthesis but excess I can paradoxically inhibit TH synthesis (Wolff-Chaikoff effect) and may promote [[thyroid-autoimmunity|autoimmune thyroiditis]]
- [[cadmium]] inhibits hepatic 5'-monodeiodinase activity, interfering with T4 to T3 conversion; Cd exposure induces UGT activity increasing T3/T4 metabolism
- [[lead]] accumulation disrupts cellular energy production and causes DNA damage in thyroid cells; Pb prevents deiodination and reduces T3 levels
- [[mercury]] interferes with TSH production, inhibits TPO, and both MeHg and inorganic compounds inhibit Tg iodination
- [[cobalt]] can cause hypothyroidism by decreasing I uptake in the thyroid gland; cross-allergy with nickel is common

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive literature review covering 13 trace elements across 7 thyroid disease categories
- **Iron and thyroid diseases**: Fe levels positively correlated with FT3 and FT4; low Fe and Mg are risk factors for autoimmune thyroiditis in childbearing women; Fe facilitates tumor cell proliferation in thyroid cancer via hepcidin; GPX4 identified as ferroptosis regulator in thyroid cancer
- **Copper and thyroid**: Cu/Zn ratio higher in thyroid cancer patients; Cu plays supporting role in TH synthesis via calcium regulation; cuproptosis (Cu-dependent cell death via FDX1) is emerging mechanism in thyroid cancer; DSF/Cu complex inhibits CSC activity in thyroid cancer
- **Iodine complexity**: Excess I activates NLRP3 inflammasome in Hashimoto's; at low concentrations I activates AKT/ERK in thyroid cancer cells promoting growth, while high concentrations inhibit migration; BRAF V600E mutation relationship with I levels is inconsistent
- **Selenium and thyroid**: Se component of GPx, TrxR, and DIOs; Se deficiency is a risk factor for both Hashimoto's and Graves'; Se supplementation in HT reduced anti-TPO antibodies in multiple RCTs
- **Zinc and thyroid**: Zn deficiency affects production of TRH, TSH, T3, and T4; Zn important for thymus function and T cell regulation; insufficient Zn supplementation causes hypothyroidism
- **Manganese**: Elevated Mn levels in autoimmune hypothyroidism; Mn affects DIO activity and T4 to T3 conversion; higher blood Mn in thyroid cancer patients
- **Cadmium**: Decreases thyroid follicle size and epithelial thickness; interferes with 5'-D and UGT activities affecting TH metabolism
- **Lead**: Pb prevents deiodination reducing T3 while T4 and TSH rise
- **Cobalt**: Co irradiation used to treat Graves' ophthalmopathy (96% response rate); Co causes hypothyroidism via decreased I uptake
