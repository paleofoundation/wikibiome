# Key Claims

- High-fiber diet consumption in CD patients is associated with improved clinical remission rates and reduced hospitalizations across 11 included studies (2389 CD patients total)
- Fiber-rich diets are safer than gluten-free diets for CD patients, with fewer disease complications (42.4% vs 60.5%)
- Infliximab combined with a high-fiber vegetarian diet achieved 84% clinical remission with decreased CRP and 46% mucosal healing in a Japanese quasi-experimental trial
- At 4-year follow-up, 52% of CD patients on infliximab plus high-fiber diet were flare-up free, and only 19% required surgery
- Fiber intake generates SCFAs ([[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]], Clostridium leptum, Bacteroides produce acetate, propionate, butyrate) that regulate intestinal [[inflammation]] and immune response

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis; PROSPERO registered (CRD42022372891)
- **Search**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials (677 results -> 11 studies)
- **Study types included**: 2 RCTs, 3 quasi-experimental trials, 3 cohort studies, 3 cross-sectional studies
- **Population**: 5010 total subjects (2389 CD patients); ages 13-77; 44% male, 56% female
- **Key RCT** (Heaton 1979): High-fiber (33.4 g/day) vs normal diet in 64 patients for 5 years; 11 vs 34 hospitalizations (p<0.01); 6 vs 15 median hospital days (p<0.02)
- **Fiber protective effect**: OR of 0.43 for flare-ups in highest fruit/vegetable quartile; OR of 0.47 for CD flare-ups with highest healthy diet quality scores
- **Quality assessment**: Joanna Briggs Institute scales; scores ranged 6/9 to 8/13
