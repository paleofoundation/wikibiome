# Bazzichi 2024 — Environmental Factors and Fibromyalgia Syndrome (Review)

## Key Findings

- **Environmental triggers for FM** include: climatic variations and temperature sensitivity, air pollution, electromagnetic field (EMF) exposure, infections (Lyme, HCV, HIV), vaccinations, physical trauma (accidents, injuries), and psychosocial stress ([[bazzichi-2024-environmental-factors-fibromyalgia-review]], review).
- **FM-long COVID connection:** shared mechanisms (neuroinflammation, fatigue, cognitive dysfunction, HPA axis dysregulation). FM features common in post-COVID syndrome; differential diagnosis is emerging clinical challenge ([[bazzichi-2024-environmental-factors-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- **Small fiber polyneuropathy (SFP)** and nociceptor dysfunction identified in FM patients — peripheral mechanism more complex than previously thought ([[bazzichi-2024-environmental-factors-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- **Nutrient roles:** Mg, Se, and other antioxidants may modulate musculoskeletal pain and protect against oxidative damage; over-exposure to toxic metals associated with worsening symptoms ([[bazzichi-2024-environmental-factors-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- Altered cortisol response in FM patients has been associated with increased vascular resistance — stress biology connects to cardiovascular risk ([[bazzichi-2024-environmental-factors-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- **Psychosocial stress** (childhood adversity, emotional trauma) and PTSD strongly associated with FM development — 42-53% comorbidity rates ([[bazzichi-2024-environmental-factors-fibromyalgia-review]]).
