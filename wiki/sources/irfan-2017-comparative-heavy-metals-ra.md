# Key Claims

- Serum [[lead]], [[cadmium]], [[chromium]], and [[nickel]] levels all significantly differ between RA patients and healthy controls (P < 0.01)
- Pb was elevated in RA (5.73 vs 2.19 ug/L), Cd elevated (1.77 vs 0.28 ug/L), Cr elevated (3.08 vs 0.26 ug/L)
- [[nickel]] was significantly lower in RA patients (41.19 ug/L) compared to controls (112.53 ug/L)
- Gender differences: Cr significantly different between sexes in RA (p=0.009); Ni significantly different between sexes in RA (p=0.018)
- No significant age-related differences for Pb, Cd, or Cr in RA patients across three age groups
- Positive correlations between Cd-Cr, Cd-Pb in RA patients; negative correlation between Cr-Pb

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Case-control study; 100 RA patients and 100 controls from hospitals in Sargodha, Punjab, Pakistan (Dec 2013 - May 2014)
- **Analysis**: Atomic absorption spectrophotometry (AA 6600 Shimadzu); wet acid digestion
- **Metals**: Pb, Cd, Cr, Ni measured in serum
- **Statistics**: One-way ANOVA, two-sample t-test, Pearson's correlation; significance at P < 0.01
- Recovery rates 97.87% to 101.01% (average 99.28%)
- RA males had lower Pb, Cr, Ni but higher Cd compared to RA females
- Correlation matrix showed different metal interaction patterns in RA vs. controls
