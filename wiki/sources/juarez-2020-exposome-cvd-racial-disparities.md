# Use of an Exposome Approach to Understand the Effects of Exposures From the Natural, Built, and Social Environments on Cardio-Vascular Disease Onset, Progression, and Outcomes

## Summary

Review applying the exposome approach to understanding CVD disparities, covering how natural (air, water, land), built (places of living, work, play), and social environments contribute to CVD risk through genomic, epigenomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and metabolomic pathways.

## Key Findings

### Exposome Framework for CVD
- Three biomarker categories: exposure, effect, and susceptibility
- Markers of exposure: Pb, Cd, As, Hg, PAHs directly measured in biological matrix
- Markers of effect: F2 isoprostanes, glutathione (total and oxidized), glutathione peroxidase 3, SOD3, CRP, interleukin 6, defensin, VCAM1
- Markers of susceptibility: glucose, insulin, triglycerides, LDL/HDL cholesterol, apolipoprotein genotyping

### Environmental Exposures and CVD
- Heavy metals (Pb, Cd, As, Hg) are identified as natural environment CVD risk factors
- Particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10) from air pollution strongly associated with CVD morbidity and mortality
- Social environment factors include poverty, segregation, food access, healthcare access

### Epigenomic Mechanisms
- DNA methylation patterns altered by environmental exposures
- LINE-1 hypomethylation associated with ischemic heart disease and stroke risk
- Hypomethylation of LINE-1 found with living in impoverished neighborhoods
- Cadmium and arsenic exposures alter DNA methylation patterns relevant to CVD

### Genomic and Transcriptomic Links
- PAD patients show upregulation of 40 genes (immune response, [[inflammation]], stress response)
- Atherosclerotic plaques show altered gene/pathway expression linked to [[inflammation]]
- Cigarette smoke causes DNA mutations, microsatellite instability in atherosclerotic plaques

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Heavy metals as CVD exposome components** - Explicitly identifies [[lead]], [[cadmium]], arsenic, and mercury as CVD risk factors in the natural environment
2. **[[Oxidative-stress]] markers** - F2 isoprostanes, glutathione, SOD3 as effect biomarkers directly relate to metal-induced oxidative damage and potential [[ferroptosis]]
3. **Epigenetic mediation** - Metal-induced DNA methylation changes provide a mechanism by which [[cadmium]] and [[lead]] exposure can persistently alter CVD risk beyond acute toxicity
4. **Multi-omics integration** - The exposome framework provides the conceptual model for integrating [[gut-metal-microbiome]] data with other -omics layers
5. **Racial disparities** - Environmental justice considerations relevant to understanding differential metal exposure burdens in CVD
