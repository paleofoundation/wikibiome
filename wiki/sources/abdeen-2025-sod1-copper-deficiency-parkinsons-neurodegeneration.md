# Parkinson-like SOD1 Pathology from Copper Deficiency

## Key Findings

- **Copper-deficient SOD1 → PD**: Wild-type superoxide dismutase 1 (SOD1), when **copper-deficient**, misfolds and aggregates in the substantia nigra, causing dopaminergic neuron degeneration that recapitulates Parkinson's disease [[abdeen-2025-sod1-copper-deficiency-parkinsons-neurodegeneration]], animal-model.
- **Non-alpha-synuclein mechanism**: This identifies a **second mis-metallation pathway** to PD — beyond the canonical alpha-synuclein/iron/ferroptosis model. Copper deficiency in SOD1 → protein misfolding → aggregation → neuronal death.
- **Copper-zinc interplay**: SOD1 is a Cu/Zn enzyme. Its copper metalation is essential for function; failure to metalate correctly leads to pathological aggregation — a direct demonstration of [[mis-metallation]] causing neurodegeneration.
