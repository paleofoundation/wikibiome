# Key Claims

- **Prenatal folic acid supplementation** (600 ug) has shown potential beneficial effect on reducing ASD risk, but opposite results also reported
- **Folic acid and/or folinic acid supplementation** in diagnosed ASD children improved neurologic, behavioral symptoms and one-carbon metabolites
- **Increased frequency of serum auto-antibodies against folate receptor alpha (FRAA)** reported in ASD children
- **MTHFR polymorphisms** showed significant influence on ASD risk
- More clinical trials with larger sample sizes and longer observation periods needed

# Methods & Findings

**Systematic review** of folic acid's role in ASD prevention and treatment.

- Reviewed prenatal supplementation studies for ASD risk reduction
- Examined post-diagnosis folic acid/folinic acid supplementation effects
- Assessed MTHFR genetic variants and ASD risk
- Identified folate receptor autoantibodies as mechanism for cerebral folate deficiency in ASD
- Highlighted contradictory findings regarding prenatal supplementation

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Folate-methionine cycle disruption** as metabolic mechanism in ASD etiology
- **FRAA autoantibodies** block folate transport across the BBB, creating cerebral folate deficiency
- **MTHFR variants** as genetic risk factor interacting with folate status
