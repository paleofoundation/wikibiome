# Trace Metals and Contaminants in Commercial Fruit Juices Sold in South Eastern States, Nigeria

## Key Claims

- Commercial fruit juices in Nigeria contain measurable levels of trace metals (As, Sn, Pb, Cu, Zn, Fe) from processing equipment and raw materials
- Arsenic and tin contents were very low (<0.001 mg/L); lead ranged 0.001-0.005 mg/L; copper 0.015-0.22 mg/L; zinc 0.605-1.360 mg/L; iron 0.755-1.532 mg/L
- All lead and zinc values were within acceptable limits (Pb <0.1 mg/kg, Zn <5.0 mg/kg)
- Processing equipment (copper, aluminum, iron, galvanized steel except stainless) is attacked by fruit acids and introduces trace metals
- Calls for tighter regulation and monitoring of metal contamination in commercially sold fruit juices

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Analysis of 130 packaged fruit juice samples from 13 brands purchased in SE Nigeria (Enugu, Awka, Owerri, Abakiliki, Umuara markets)
- **Method**: Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry (AAS) using Solar Unicam 969
- **Brands tested**: Five Alive, Dansa, Frutta, Chivita, Fumman varieties (apple, orange, mango, pineapple, citrus)
- **Metals measured**: As, Sn, Pb, Cu, Zn, Fe
- **Notable**: Nickel was not specifically measured in this study
