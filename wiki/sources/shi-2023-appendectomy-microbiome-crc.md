# Altered Gut Microbiome Composition by Appendectomy Contributes to Colorectal Cancer

## Key Claims

- Appendectomy increased cumulative CRC incidence by 73% over 20 years of follow-up (adjusted SHR 1.730, 95% CI 1.49-2.01, P < 0.001) in a population-based longitudinal study of 129,155 subjects from Hong Kong
- Shotgun metagenomic sequencing of appendectomy subjects revealed significant enrichment of 7 CRC-promoting bacteria (*Bacteroides fragilis*, *B. vulgatus*, *Veilonella dispar*, *Prevotella ruminicola*, *P. fusca*, *P. dentalis*, *P. denticola*) and depletion of 5 beneficial commensals (*Blautia* sp. SC05B48, *Collinsella aerofaciens*, *Enterococcus hirae*, *Lachnospiraceae bacterium Choco86*, *Blautia* sp. YL58)
- Appendectomy-induced microbial network analysis showed *B. fragilis* and *B. vulgatus* as central hubs with the strongest negative correlations to commensal species, particularly in older subjects (>50 years)
- In mouse models, appendectomy promoted colorectal tumorigenesis with significantly higher tumor numbers and sizes (P < 0.05), accompanied by impaired intestinal barrier function (reduced ZO-1, Occludin, E-cadherin; increased serum LPS)
- Functional pathway analysis revealed cancer-promoting nucleotide biosynthesis pathways were enriched in appendectomy subjects while cancer-inhibiting pathways (L-proline biosynthesis, tetrahydrofolate) were depleted

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Population-based cohort study (n = 129,155; 20-year follow-up) combined with shotgun metagenomics (n = 314) and AOM/DSS mouse model
- **Cohort 1**: 43,976 appendectomy cases and 85,179 matched controls from Hong Kong; CRC incidence 73.1 vs. 39.7 per 100,000 person-years
- **Cohort 2**: 157 appendectomy cases and 157 matched controls for metagenomics; average 15 GB per sample
- **Mouse model**: AOM/DSS-induced CRC in C57BL/6 mice with appendectomy vs. sham; microbiome profiling at baseline and 3, 7, 14, 30 days post-surgery
- **Key mechanistic finding**: Hippo signaling pathway activated in post-appendectomy tumors with E-cadherin downregulation
