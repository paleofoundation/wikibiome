# Associations of Serum Short-Chain Fatty Acids with Circulating Immune Cells and Serum Biomarkers in Patients with Multiple Sclerosis

## Summary

This study examined serum SCFA levels in 30 CIS/MS patients and 10 healthy controls from Perth, Australia, correlating them with circulating immune cell subsets, cytokine production, and serum biomarkers including GDF15 and immunoglobulins.

## Key Findings

### SCFA Levels
- Propionate was significantly lower in CIS/MS serum (p=0.0008) compared to healthy controls
- Butyrate and acetate levels were not significantly different, though some CIS/MS patients had very low levels

### T-Cell Associations
- T follicular helper (Tfh) cell frequencies positively correlated with both butyrate (rho=0.41, p=0.049) and propionate (rho=0.42, p=0.046)
- T follicular regulatory (Tfr) cell frequencies correlated with propionate levels (rho=0.40, p=0.049)
- Propionate negatively correlated with Treg:Tfr ratio (rho=-0.43, p=0.03)
- No correlation between SCFAs and conventional Tregs or Th1/Th17 cells

### B-Cell Associations
- Total B cell frequency positively correlated with butyrate (rho=0.56, p=0.008)
- Class-switched memory B cells (Sw-MBC) negatively correlated with butyrate (rho=-0.48, p=0.03)
- Butyrate levels associated with IL-10 production by naive B cells (anti-inflammatory)
- Acetate negatively associated with TNF production by stimulated B cells

### Tryptophan/IDO1 Connection
- IDO1 mRNA levels in PBMCs correlated positively with serum acetate levels
- ARG1 mRNA correlated with serum acetate
- IDO1 and IDO2 mRNA did not correlate significantly with butyrate

### GDF15 and Other Biomarkers
- GDF15 (growth differentiation factor 15) levels higher in CIS/MS (trend, p=0.057)
- GDF15 positively correlated with both acetate and butyrate levels
- No significant correlations between SCFAs and immunoglobulin levels

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This study uniquely connects [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] to specific immune cell populations relevant to [[multiple-sclerosis]]:

1. **Propionate depletion** is the most consistent finding across SCFA-MS studies and connects directly to depletion of propionate-producing Clostridia by [[heavy-metals]]
2. **Tfh/Tfr cell regulation by SCFAs** - these germinal center regulatory cells are critical for antibody production in MS; their SCFA-dependence means metal-induced [[dysbiosis]] could directly impair immune regulation
3. **B cell-butyrate relationship** - butyrate supports anti-inflammatory (IL-10) naive B cells while suppressing class-switched memory B cells, relevant to B cell-targeted MS therapies
4. **IDO1-acetate connection** links gut-derived SCFAs to tryptophan metabolism ([[indoles]]) and AHR signaling

## Limitations

- Very small sample sizes (30 CIS/MS, 10 controls)
- CIS and MS patients combined
- Therapy-naive cohort (early disease)
- Serum SCFAs may not reflect gut production accurately
