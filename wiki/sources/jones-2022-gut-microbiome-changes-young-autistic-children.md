# Overview

Longitudinal study characterising both bacterial and fungal microbiome and **SCFA concentrations** in fecal samples from young children (21-40 months) showing early behavioural signs of autism from the **AICES RCT** in Western Australia. Samples collected at two timepoints (average 24 and 36 months).

# Key Findings

## Bacterial Community

- No significant differences in phyla proportions between ASD, NAASD (non-autistic ASD spectrum), and NDC groups after FDR correction
- **Actinobacteriota** abundance dropped below 4% in NAASD group
- 19 bacterial and 4 fungal ASVs showed significant differential abundance at timepoint B
- Most differentially abundant bacteria classified as Bacteroidia (Bacteroidales) or Clostridia

## Fungal Community

- Fungal communities did not group by diagnosis but clustered by **dominant ASVs** and **stool form**
- *Saccharomyces* and *Debaryomyces* among dominant fungal genera
- Two fungal ASVs significantly enriched in ASD and NDC groups respectively

## SCFA Concentrations

- No significant differences in total or individual SCFA concentrations between groups
- ASD group tended to have **higher acetic acid** and total acetic acid concentrations
- NAASD group had highest butyrate and propionate levels with significantly elevated tetracycline biosynthesis

## Temporal Changes

- Richness, diversity, and phylogenetic diversity higher at timepoint B (36 months) vs A (24 months)
- Core bacteriome of 46 species identified across both timepoints; 11 species lost between timepoints
- *Blautia A sp*, Lachnospiraceae, and *Ruminococcus sp* remained highly prevalent
