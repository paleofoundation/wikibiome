# Key Claims

- Polyphenols synergistically interact with gut microbiota to suppress [[inflammation]] and alleviate IBD symptoms through bidirectional mechanisms
- Polyphenols increase microbiota diversity, boost beneficial bacteria (including [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]]), and inhibit pathogenic species like adherent-invasive [[escherichia-coli]]
- Microbial catabolism of polyphenols in the colon produces metabolites with higher anti-inflammatory activity and bioavailability than precursor compounds
- Polyphenol mechanisms include NF-kappaB suppression, Nrf2 upregulation, TNF-alpha/IL-6/IL-1beta reduction, and gut barrier protection
- IBD dysbiosis is characterized by reduced Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes, decreased SCFA producers (Ruminococcaceae, Roseburia, [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]]), and increased Proteobacteria

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of polyphenol-microbiota-IBD interactions
- **IBD microbiome**: 75% prevalence of adherent-invasive E. coli in CD (vs 6% in healthy); reduced Clostridia, Lactobacillus, Eubacterium, Bacteroides in IBD; 54 ribotypes in CD vs 88 in healthy
- **Polyphenol classes**: Hydroxycinnamic acids, flavonoids (quercetin, flavones, flavan-3-ols, isoflavones), stilbenes (resveratrol), tannins -- all reviewed for IBD effects
- **Key mechanisms**: Reduce oxidative stress via Nrf2; inhibit inflammatory cytokines via NF-kappaB; enhance tight junctions; modulate immune function
- **Microbiota interaction**: Polyphenols serve as prebiotics, selectively promoting beneficial bacteria; microbial metabolism produces bioactive metabolites (urolithins, equol, etc.)
- **230 gene loci** associated with IBD including NOD2, ATG16L1, IRGM, IL-23R, TL1A
