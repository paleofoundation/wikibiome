# Song 2025 -- Targeting Tumor-Associated Microbiome for Cancer Therapy (Editorial)

## Key Claims

- Tumor-associated bacteria (TAB) are mostly localized intracellularly; targeted delivery of antibiotics or agents to kill TAB in the tumor is necessary but not easy, requiring advanced drug delivery methods
- *Fusobacterium nucleatum* resides in colorectal cancer due to direct contact of cancer cells with microbes in the colon; when TAB is killed, the host immune response can be activated to recognize tumor cells and bacteria epitopes for anti-bacteria/tumor vaccine development
- Special issue highlights: (1) progress of tumor-resident intracellular bacteria in cancer therapy, (2) *F. nucleatum* in tumor progression, (3) breast cancer intratumoral microbiota, (4) bacterial metabolites as cancer therapy signals
- Key emerging approaches: cell membrane-coated nanoparticles for targeting carcinogenic bacteria, engineered NPs using bacterial membrane components, microalgae for tumor microbiome modulation, phage-based delivery systems
- Directly targeting and manipulating gut microbiota is complicated by variable abundance of intestinal microbiota and diverse components among individuals; TAB metabolites offer alternative precision targets

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Special issue editorial/overview for Advanced Drug Delivery Reviews
- **Scope**: Six contributions covering intracellular bacteria in cancer, *F. nucleatum* mechanisms, breast tumor microbiota, bacterial metabolites, prebiotics/probiotics/postbiotics, and microalgae approaches
