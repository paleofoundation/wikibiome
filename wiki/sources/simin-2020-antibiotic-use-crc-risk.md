# Key Claims

- Oral [[antibiotic]] use is associated with an increased risk of [[colorectal cancer]] (CRC): pooled effect size ES = 1.17, 95% CI 1.05-1.30 for ever-users vs non-users across 4.1 million individuals and 73,550 CRC cases
- Broad-spectrum antibiotics show a stronger association with CRC (ES = 1.70, 95% CI 1.26-2.30) compared to narrow-spectrum antibiotics (ES = 1.11, 95% CI 0.93-1.32)
- The dose-response analysis shows a non-linear relationship (p for non-linearity < 0.0005): CRC risk increases with antibiotic exposure up to ~30 days, then plateaus
- Antibiotic class-specific risks: penicillins (ES = 1.16), sulfonamides (1.17), quinolones (1.23), cephalosporins (1.33), nitroimidazole/metronidazole (1.28); tetracyclines showed no apparent association (0.97)
- The median time between first antibiotic prescription and cancer diagnosis was 8 years, suggesting a long-latency effect via sustained microbiome disruption

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and dose-response meta-analysis (PRISMA guidelines)
- **Search**: PubMed, Embase, Web of Science; inception to February 2020; no restrictions
- **Included studies**: 10 publications (6483 initially identified); 4.1 million individuals and >73,550 CRC cases
- **Study designs**: 4 nested case-control studies, 5 case-control studies, 1 cohort study; from Netherlands, New Zealand, USA, UK, Taiwan
- **Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale; all studies scored high quality; customized tool for observational studies also applied
- **Colon vs rectal**: Colon cancer ES = 1.06 (95% CI 0.89-1.26, N = 4); rectal cancer ES = 1.01 (95% CI 0.96-1.06, N = 2) -- risk patterns were similar
- **Sensitivity analyses**: Excluding IBD patients strengthened the association (ES = 1.28, 95% CI 1.03-1.63); excluding Crohn's/UC showed no apparent association
- **Dose-response model**: Cubic splines with 3 knots; Akaike Information Criteria for best model fit; risk stabilizes after ~30 days cumulative antibiotic exposure
- **Limitations**: Observational design; confounding by indication possible (antibiotics for infections that may independently promote CRC); recall bias in case-control studies
