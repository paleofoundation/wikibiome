# Key Claims

- **Oral microbiota markedly different in T1D** (elevated Streptococcus abundance) compared to healthy controls
- **Fecal analysis showed decreased butyrate-producing species** in T1D and less butyryl-CoA transferase genes
- **Plasma levels of acetate and propionate lower** in T1D, with similar fecal SCFA
- **Christensenella and Subdoligranulum** correlated with glycemic control, inflammatory parameters, and SCFA
- **53 T1D patients** without complications vs 50 HC examined

# Methods & Findings

Observational study of oral and fecal microbiota in longstanding T1D:

- Oral and fecal microbiota, fecal and plasma SCFA levels measured
- Markers of intestinal inflammation (fecal IgA, calprotectin) and systemic inflammation assessed
- First study to comprehensively compare both oral and fecal compartments in T1D

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Oral Streptococcus enrichment may reflect zinc-dependent antimicrobial defense failure in T1D saliva
- **[[iron]]**: Streptococcus species have efficient iron acquisition systems enabling dominance in iron-dysregulated T1D oral environment
- **[[selenium]]**: Low plasma SCFA in T1D may reflect selenium-dependent fermentative pathway impairment
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Oral-gut microbiome axis transmits metal-selected [[dysbiosis]] from mouth to intestine
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Calprotectin (zinc/calcium-binding) elevation indicates metal-mediated intestinal inflammation
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Concurrent oral and gut [[dysbiosis]] in T1D suggests systemic metal homeostasis disruption
