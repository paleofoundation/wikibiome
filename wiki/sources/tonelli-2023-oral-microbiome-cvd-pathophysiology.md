# The Oral Microbiome in the Pathophysiology of Cardiovascular Disease

## Summary

Comprehensive review in Nature Reviews Cardiology examining the link between the oral microbiome and CVD, covering coronary artery disease, stroke, peripheral artery disease, heart failure, infective endocarditis, and rheumatic heart disease. Discusses mechanisms by which oral [[dysbiosis]] contributes to CVD pathogenesis.

## Key Findings

### Mechanisms of CVD from Oral Dysbiosis
1. **Transmigration** - Oral bacteria seed into systemic circulation through inflamed periodontium, inoculating atherosclerotic plaques
2. **Cytokine release** - Chronic oral [[inflammation]] results in sustained pro-inflammatory molecule release, causing endothelial dysfunction and [[oxidative-stress]]
3. **Molecular mimicry** - Oral bacterial antigens structurally similar to host proteins activate autoimmune B cells targeting atherosclerotic plaques
4. **Toxin release** - Leaky periodontal vasculature allows bacterial toxins into systemic circulation, increasing lipid peroxidation and LDL/HDL modification
5. **Abnormal lipid profile** - LPS-induced lipoprotein metabolism abnormalities and oxidative modification of LDL

### Heart Failure Pathogenesis
- Chronic low-level oral [[inflammation]] seeds cytokines and chemokines into vasculature
- Activates cardiac fibroblasts, promotes extracellular matrix changes and fibrosis
- LPS activates TLR4-dependent macrophage activation and NF-kB-dependent systemic [[inflammation]]
- Oral bacteria translocation induces metabolic endotoxemia, atherosclerosis, and type 2 diabetes

### Infective Endocarditis
- Viridans group streptococci (VGS) and *Staphylococcus aureus* are most common bacterial causes
- VGS express adhesin B, facilitating platelet aggregation on heart valve endothelium
- Dental procedures (scaling, extraction, brushing) can cause bacteremia with VGS

### Rheumatic Heart Disease
- *Streptococcus* M protein in Group A beta-hemolytic streptococci triggers molecular mimicry
- Cross-reactive antibodies target cardiac myosin and valve tissue

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This review contributes to understanding [[cardiovascular-disease]] through:

1. **Oral-systemic translocation** parallels gut-to-blood translocation seen with [[lead]] and [[cadmium]] exposure
2. **LPS-mediated [[inflammation]]** through oral dysbiosis mirrors the gut barrier disruption pathway in metal-exposed individuals
3. **[[Oxidative-stress]]** from chronic oral infection compounds with metal-induced oxidative damage
4. **Multi-site [[dysbiosis]]** - Oral and gut dysbiosis may synergistically amplify CVD risk, especially in metal-exposed populations
