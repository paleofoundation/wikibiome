## Key Claims

- [[aluminum]] was detected in all 41 infant formula samples and 76 baby food products from the Lebanese market, but below the FAO/WHO maximal permissible limit (400 ug/kg for formula)
- Al concentrations: infant formulas 0.317 +/- 0.038 mg/kg; cereals 0.361 +/- 0.049 mg/kg; cornflakes 0.361 +/- 0.049 mg/kg; biscuits 0.357 +/- 0.05 mg/kg; pureed foods 0.362 +/- 0.079 mg/kg
- Total Al exposure through infant formula and complementary food intake was below the JECFA PTWI (2 mg/kg BW/week) at all ages for both genders
- However, hazard quotient (HQ) values exceeded 1 for all age groups, indicating a possible health risk requiring continuous monitoring
- Highest Al exposure was among infants aged 8-10 months through pureed food consumption
- Toxicological contribution to PTWI was <6% for males and <7% for females across all ages
- This is the first study to investigate pediatric Al exposure from infant foods marketed in Lebanon

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: 41 infant formulas and 76 baby food products (16 cereals, 21 cornflakes, 7 biscuits, 32 pureed foods) from Lebanese pharmacies and grocery stores
- **Analysis**: Flame atomic absorption spectrometry (FAAS, Shimadzu AA-6800); LOD 0.04 mg/kg, LOQ 0.12 mg/kg; recovery 88-102%; precision 95.5%
- **Concentrations (Table 3)**: Infant formulas 0.3-0.4 mg/kg (Cv 0.3-0.4); cereals NA (0.3-0.4 range); cornflakes 0.3-0.4 mg/kg; biscuits 0.3-0.4 mg/kg; pureed foods 0.2-0.4 mg/kg
- **EDI (Table 4)**: Infant formula EDI ranged 0.0061-0.0081 mg/kg BW/day for males 0-1 weeks to 0.0017-0.0021 mg/kg BW/day at 10-12 months; pureed food EDI 0.005-0.015 mg/kg BW/day
- **HQ (Table 5)**: Ranged from 14.9 to 40.9 across age groups (using RfD of 4x10^-4 mg/kg/day), all >1
- **% TDI (Table 5)**: Ranged from 2.08-5.73% across ages; % PTWI contribution from infant formula <6% males, <7% females
- **International comparison (Table 6)**: Lebanese EDI (0.01 mg/kg BW/day) lower than Brazil (0.01), Nigeria (0.02), Turkey (0.00603-0.0335), France (0.318), UK (0.1636)

## Connections

- HQ >1 finding contrasts with [[Su et al 2020]] where all THQ/HI <1, highlighting [[aluminum]] as higher-risk contaminant than Cr/As/Cd/Pb in formula
- Complements [[de Paiva et al 2020]] and [[de Paiva et al 2020 cereals]] Brazilian bioaccessibility data
- Lower Al levels than [[Redgrove et al 2019]] prescription formula findings in the UK
- Supports [[Pendergrass 2026]] call for age-specific regulatory limits and continuous monitoring
- PTWI framework consistent with [[Gonzalez-Suarez et al 2022]] and [[Meli et al 2024]] approaches
- First Arab-region data on this topic, filling geographic gap alongside Chinese [[Su et al 2020]], European [[Meli et al 2024]], and Brazilian [[de Paiva et al 2020]] studies
- Relates to [[developmental-metal-vulnerability]] -- critical windows of susceptibility for formula-fed infants
