# Pendergrass 2026 — Endometriosis Conference Presentation (Amsterdam)

> **Note:** This is a conference presentation by the WikiBiome founder, not independently peer-reviewed journal literature.

## Overview

This source is based on Karen Pendergrass's presentation at the Amsterdam Endometriosis Conference (2026). The full transcript is available in `raw/karens-brain/`.

This presentation outlined the **endometriosis disease signature** using all 9 Karen's Brain primitives:

1. Metallomic Profiling
2. Nutritional Immunity as Interpretive Constraint
3. Mis-metallation and Toxic Metal Entry
4. Oxidative Stress Cascade
5. Taxonomic Profiling (Enriched and Depleted Taxa)
6. Interkingdom Relationships and Functional Shielding
7. Virulence Enzyme Mapping
8. Metabolomic Collapse Signature
9. Intervention Design from Signature

The presentation demonstrated that endometriosis is an ecological disease driven by metal-dependent microbial communities, with nickel as the central actionable target. Key findings include 90.3% nickel allergy prevalence in endometriosis patients, the role of Ni-dependent virulence enzymes (urease, hydrogenase, glyoxalase) in pathogen survival, and validated interventions including low-nickel diet and EcN 1917 probiotic.

## Key Claims

- Endometriosis metallomic signature: Ni (77.8%), Fe (70.4%), Pb (59.3%) elevated; glutathione depleted
- All 9 Karen's Brain primitives are active in the endometriosis signature
- Low-nickel diet disables Ni-glyoxalase, urease, and hydrogenase across all glyoxalase-positive pathogens simultaneously
- EcN 1917 achieves competitive exclusion via superior siderophore systems
- Iron and zinc supplementation are counterproductive (STOPs) because hepcidin and calprotectin elevation indicate functional anemia and deliberate metal sequestration, not true deficiency
- Candida albicans is a critical component of the disease ecology, not noise -- missed by 16S studies
