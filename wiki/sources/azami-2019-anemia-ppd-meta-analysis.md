# Key Claims

- **Postpartum anemia significantly increases PPD risk**: RR = 1.887 (95% CI: 1.255-2.838, p=0.002) across 10 studies
- **Anemia during pregnancy** also significantly increases PPD risk: RR = 1.240 (95% CI: 1.001-1.536, p=0.048) across 8 studies
- The association was robust in sensitivity analyses -- omitting any single study did not substantially change the overall estimate
- Subgroup analyses showed the association was **not significantly modified** by geographic region, study design, quality of studies, or timing of depression/anemia assessment
- Evidence quality was graded as **moderate** (GRADE) for both postpartum anemia and anemia during pregnancy
- Low serum ferritin (< 1 ug) was associated with 3.98-fold increased PPD risk in one included study

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis following MOOSE protocol and PRISMA guidelines
- **Search**: Nine English databases (Scopus, PubMed, Web of Science, CINAHL, Cochrane, EBSCO, Google Scholar) through January 2018
- **Included Studies**: 10 studies from 348 initially identified articles (7 from Asia, 1 from America, 2 from Europe)
- **Mean participant age**: 28.16 years (95% CI: 25.30-31.03)
- **Depression tools**: Primarily EPDS (9 studies), CES-D (1 study)

 - By region: Asia RR=1.973 (p=0.009), America RR=5.846 (p=0.019), Europe RR=1.286 (p=0.294)
 - By time of PPD assessment: <= 4 weeks RR=1.392, > 4 weeks RR=2.312 (p=0.004)
 - By time of anemia assessment: <= 2 weeks RR=2.604 (p=0.017), > 2 weeks RR=1.548 (p=0.061)
- **Heterogeneity**: Considerable for postpartum anemia studies (I-squared=74.62%), moderate for pregnancy anemia studies (I-squared=36.42%)
- **Publication bias**: Not significant (Egger p=0.0528, Begg p=0.0736 for postpartum; Egger p=0.826, Begg p=0.999 for pregnancy anemia)
