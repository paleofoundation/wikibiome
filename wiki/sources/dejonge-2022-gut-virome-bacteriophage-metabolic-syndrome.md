# Key Claims

- Metabolic syndrome (MetS) gut viromes exhibit **decreased richness and diversity** compared to healthy controls, enriched in phages infecting *Streptococcaceae* and *Bacteroidaceae* and depleted in those infecting *Bifidobacteriaceae*
- 18 viral clusters (VCs) were identified as significantly associated with either MetS or healthy viromes; a MetS-associated *Roseburia* VC and healthy control-associated *Faecalibacterium* and *Oscillibacter* VCs were key discriminators
- **Candidatus Heliusviridae**, a novel widespread gut phage lineage, was found in >90% of participants and contains nine shared genes; this temperate phage family was previously linked to obesity, NAFLD/steatohepatitis, atherosclerosis, and type 2 diabetes
- Phage richness was significantly negatively correlated with obesity, blood glucose, and triglyceride concentrations, while bacterial richness was not -- suggesting phages may be more sensitive biomarkers than bacteria for MetS
- *Ruminococcaceae* and *Clostridiaceae* bacteria were significantly more abundant in controls, while their bulk phages slightly trended toward MetS, indicating phages may be unevenly predating these host families
- Apparent depletion of *Crassvirales* phages in MetS bulk viromes (but not free-floating VLP viromes) may indicate decreased infectiousness in MetS, representing the first link between this prominent phage order and a disease state

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Case-control study with combined bulk WGS and VLP sequencing
- **Population**: 196 participants from HELIUS cohort (Amsterdam); 97 MetS, 99 controls
- **Methods**: Bulk WGS and VLP sequencing; VirSorter2 and VIBRANT for viral prediction; vConTACT2 for clustering; CRISPR spacer analysis for host prediction; ANCOM-BC for differential abundance
- **Key metrics**: MetS viromes had significantly lower Shannon H' (p=0.02), decreased species richness (p=7.1x10-7), and distinct PCoA separation (Permanova p=0.001)
