# Cai 2022 — Gut Microbiota Supports Male Reproduction via Nutrition, Immunity, and Signaling

## Key Findings

- **Gut-testis nutritional axis:** Gut microbiota synthesize vitamins A, K, folic acid, and regulate calcium absorption — all essential for spermatogenesis and testicular function. Specific taxa are mapped to each nutrient: *Escherichia coli* and *Clostridia* for vitamin A; *Lactobacillus* and *Bifidobacterium* for folic acid; *Bifidobacteria* and *Lactobacillus* for calcium; *[[bacteroides-fragilis]]* for vitamin K ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).
- **H2S as erectile effector:** Sulfate-reducing bacteria (*[[desulfovibrio]]*, *Desulfobacter*, *Desulforomonas*) produce hydrogen sulfide that activates guanylate cyclase to produce cGMP, relaxing cavernosal smooth muscle and inducing penile erection — a parallel gaseous signaling pathway to NO ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).
- **NO from gut bacteria:** *Lactobacillus* spp., *[[bifidobacterium]]* spp., *[[staphylococcus-aureus]]*, and *Bacillus* spp. affect NO production via arginine metabolism. NO activates guanylate cyclase in cavernosal nerve endings, producing cGMP to relax vascular smooth muscle and enable erection ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).
- **GABA signaling:** *[[bacteroides-fragilis]]*, *Parabacter*, and *[[escherichia-coli]]* produce GABA, which promotes sperm capacitation, modulates acrosome reaction, and increases libido and sexual behavior ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).
- **Blood-testis barrier integrity:** Healthy GM promotes BTB formation via androgen-mediated Claudin3 expression in Sertoli cells. Dysbiosis triggers bacterial translocation → LPS/TLR4 activation → NF-kB/MyD88/TRAM pathway → testicular endothelial damage and BTB compromise ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

## Methods

**Narrative review** synthesizing evidence from animal models (germ-free mice, SPF mice, boar studies), in vitro culture systems, and observational human data on the gut microbiota–male reproductive axis. Covers three major pathways: nutritional support (vitamins, minerals), immune regulation (testicular microenvironment, BTB), and signaling molecules (GABA, 5-HT, NO, H2S, sex hormones). No original data; no systematic search protocol described.

## Detailed Summary

### Nutritional Support for Testis via Gut Microbiota

**Vitamin A:** Retinoic acid (RA) drives spermatogonial stem cell differentiation from G0/G1 to meiosis. *[[escherichia-coli]]* proteins (RXRs, farnesoid X receptors) transport vitamin A to intestinal cells. *Clostridia* directly modulates RA concentration. GM inhibits cytochrome P450 degradation of vitamin A. In sheep, diet-induced GM imbalance reduces bile acid production and fat-soluble vitamin A absorption, increasing undifferentiated spermatogonia and decreasing mature sperm count ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Folic acid (B9):** Required for DNA/RNA synthesis and methylation in germ cells. *[[bacteroides-fragilis]]*, *Prevotella copri*, *Clostridium difficile*, *[[lactobacillus-plantarum]]*, *L. reuteri*, *L. delbrueckii*, *Streptococcus thermophilus* (Firmicutes), *Bifidobacterium* spp. (Actinobacteria), *Fusobacterium varium* (Fusobacteria), and *[[salmonella-enterica]]* (Proteobacteria) all participate in tetrahydrofolic acid (THFA) synthesis. Oral *Lactobacillus* or *Acidobacillus* in cadmium-poisoned mice reduced testicular cadmium damage and promoted germ cell formation — paralleling folic acid supplementation effects ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Calcium:** Regulates sperm motility, capacitation, and acrosome reaction via Ca2+ influx through CatSper channels. *Bifidobacteria* and *[[lactobacillus]]* affect calcium absorption. SCFAs produced by GM reduce calcium phosphate formation and promote absorption by lowering intestinal pH. Germ-free mice showed decreased osteoclasts and altered bone calcium homeostasis ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Vitamin K:** *[[bacteroides-fragilis]]* produces vitamin K. MK4 (menaquinone) promotes testosterone synthesis genes. Vitamin K upregulates cholesterol and steroid hormone synthase genes including *Cyp11a*, increasing serum testosterone. In testicular inflammation, vitamin K inhibited NF-kB activation, increased *Cyp11a* expression, and reduced the inhibitory effect of inflammatory stimulation on testosterone synthesis ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

### Immune Regulation of Testicular Microenvironment

**Testicular immune privilege:** Sertoli cells form the BTB via adhesion junctions and tight junction proteins (occludin, claudins, JAM, ZO-1/2/3). In 15-16 day old prepubertal mice, SPF mice showed more complete seminiferous tubule development than germ-free (GF) mice. GF mice had underdeveloped Sertoli cells and atretic seminiferous tubule lumens. Tight junction protein expression (occludin, ZO-2, E-cadherin) was lower in GF mice. Probiotic recolonization improved BTB integrity ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Inflammatory cascades:** *[[bacteroides-fragilis]]* Polysaccharide A activates TLR2 → Foxp3+ Treg cell production → anti-inflammatory IL-10 secretion and inhibition of pro-inflammatory Th17 cells. SCFAs (especially butyrate) facilitate CD4+ T cell differentiation into Foxp3+ Tregs. SCFAs also inhibit NF-kB pathway, reducing lipopolysaccharide-induced pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNFa, IL-1B, IL-6). Dysbiosis increases macrophage and dendritic cell activation in testis, leading to sperm being recognized as non-self ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Testicular immune environment disruption:** Boars with highly abnormal sperm had higher plasma endotoxin, elevated TNF-a and IL-6, lower IL-10, higher fecal branched-chain fatty acids (BCFAs — indicator of increased intestinal permeability), and enrichment of *[[sphingobium]]* (a genus that destroys intestinal integrity). *[[proteobacteria]]* abundance correlated with low semen quality. Increased intestinal permeability → LPS leakage → TLR activation → IL-6 and TNFa production → testicular immune attack → lipid peroxidation of sperm membrane → reduced testosterone synthesis ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

### Signaling Molecules

**GABA:** Produced by *[[bacteroides-fragilis]]*, *Parabacter*, and *[[escherichia-coli]]*. Also isolated from *[[lactobacillus]]* and *[[bifidobacterium]]* cultures. Promotes sperm capacitation (tyrosine phosphorylation), inhibits excessive acrosome activation via GABA receptor antagonism, and regulates libido. In hamsters, GABA reduces excessive sperm activation by inhibiting 5-HT to 5-HT2 receptor binding ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Serotonin (5-HT):** >90% of total body 5-HT is gut-derived. ~10% of ECs synthesize peripheral 5-HT relying on GM. 5-HT balances androgenic/estrogenic effects on testicular weight and volume. 5-HT binding to 5-HT2C and 5-HT1B receptors increases ejaculatory latency. 5-HT binding to 5-HT1A receptor decreases ejaculatory latency. Spore-forming microbes (*Clostridium ramosum*) promote 5-HT synthesis via tryptophan hydroxylase 1 (*Tph1*). *Corynebacterium* spp., *[[streptococcus]]* spp., and *[[escherichia-coli]]* synthesize 5-HT. High-density stress in male Brandt's voles increased *Streptococcus* and *E. coli* abundance, possibly increasing cortisol and 5-HT and making animals more aggressive via hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Nitric oxide (NO):** *[[lactobacillus]]* spp., *[[bifidobacterium]]* spp., *[[staphylococcus-aureus]]*, and *Bacillus* spp. affect NO production via arginine metabolism. NO promotes luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone (LHRH) and GnRH secretion, dilates testicular blood vessels, regulates local temperature, and activates guanylate cyclase in cavernosal nerve endings → cGMP → vascular smooth muscle relaxation → erection. NO synthesizing *Bacillus* and *Paenibacillus* positively correlated with FSH levels in obese girls' GM ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

**Hydrogen sulfide (H2S):** Produced by sulfate-reducing bacteria (*[[desulfovibrio]]*, *Desulfobacter*, *Desulforomonas*) and also by *[[escherichia-coli]]*, *[[salmonella-enterica]]*, *Clostridium* spp., and *[[enterobacter]]* spp. that metabolize sulfur-containing amino acids. H2S activates guanylate cyclase → cGMP → vascular smooth muscle relaxation. H2S injection into penile cavernous body increased penis length — effect similar to 20 ug prostaglandin E1. H2S also acts as physiological vasodilator affecting erectile function ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

### Sex Hormones and Gut Microbiota

Free testosterone and DHT levels were higher in intestinal segments with high microbial density (cecum, colon) vs. low-density segments (proximal small intestine) — free DHT in feces was >70 times higher than serum levels. GF mice had much lower distal intestinal free DHT. Transplantation of high-fat diet fecal microbes increased intestinal *Bacteroidaceae* and *[[prevotellaceae]]*, decreasing Hsd3B1 gene (encoding DHT synthase) expression in testis. Testosterone levels positively associated with *[[prevotellaceae]]*, *Cytophagaceae*, *Sphingobacteriaceae*, *Idomarinaceae* and negatively with *Actinobacteria*, *[[proteobacteria]]*, Firmicutes, *Verrucomicrobia*. GM promotes deglucuronidation of testosterone and DHT via beta-glucuronidase activity, increasing free hormone levels ([[cai-2022-gut-microbiota-male-reproduction]], expert-opinion).

- **Intervention hypothesis 1:** Targeted enrichment of sulfate-reducing bacteria (dietary sulfur amino acids, sulfate) to augment H2S-mediated erection as adjunct to PDE5 inhibitor therapy (primitive 4, 5)
- **Intervention hypothesis 2:** Probiotic recolonization to restore BTB integrity via SCFA/butyrate production and Treg induction — relevant for inflammatory male infertility (primitive 5)
- **Intervention hypothesis 3:** Folic acid via microbial synthesis pathway (primitive 4) — oral *Lactobacillus*/*Acidobacillus* reduced cadmium testicular toxicity, suggesting metal chelation + nutritional support combination
- **STOP signal:** Iron supplementation in male infertility with elevated intestinal permeability markers — LPS/TLR4 cascade already activated; iron would feed siderophore-producing pathogens and worsen testicular inflammation (primitive 2, 8)

- **Nuance:** Excessive *[[desulfovibrio]]* is pro-inflammatory in IBD context but beneficial for erectile function via H2S — therapeutic window and context-dependent interpretation required

## Contradictions / Tensions

- **Desulfovibrio duality:** This review frames *[[desulfovibrio]]* H2S production as beneficial for erectile function, while the IBD literature consistently identifies *Desulfovibrio* as a pathobiont whose H2S damages colonic epithelium. The [[desulfovibrio]] entity page must carry this context-dependent interpretation — the same metabolite (H2S) is therapeutic in one tissue (corpus cavernosum) and toxic in another (colon).
- **Proteobacteria framing:** *[[proteobacteria]]* enrichment is negatively associated with testosterone levels and semen quality in this review, consistent with the dysbiosis marker interpretation across other signatures. However, specific Proteobacteria members (*[[escherichia-coli]]*) are simultaneously cited as beneficial GABA and 5-HT producers — genus-level vs. phylum-level generalizations create tension.
- **Beta-glucuronidase as beneficial:** This review describes GM beta-glucuronidase activity as beneficial (increasing free testosterone/DHT), whereas in [[endometriosis-signature]] the same enzyme activity (increasing free estrogen) is pathological. The enzyme is neutral — the clinical context determines whether hormone recirculation is harmful or helpful.

## Open Questions

- What is the dose-response relationship between H2S-producing taxa abundance and erectile function outcomes in humans?
- Can gut-derived GABA measurements serve as a clinical biomarker for male sexual dysfunction?
- Is there a testicular metallomic signature (zinc, calcium, cadmium, iron tissue levels) that parallels the gut dysbiosis pattern in ED patients?
- What is the safe therapeutic window for *Desulfovibrio* enrichment that supports erectile function without triggering colonic inflammation?
- Does the gut-testis axis involve direct metal transport (e.g., zinc shuttling via calprotectin or metallothionein) or only indirect effects via vitamins and signaling molecules?
- How do cadmium and lead exposure interact with the gut-testis nutritional axis — do they compete with zinc/calcium at the CatSper channel level?
