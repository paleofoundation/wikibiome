# Key Claims

- NAFLD is associated with a significantly higher risk of fatal and non-fatal CVD events (random effect OR 1.64, 95% CI 1.26-2.13) across 16 observational studies with 34,043 individuals
- Patients with more "severe" NAFLD (NASH or advanced fibrosis) had even higher CVD risk (OR 2.58, 95% CI 1.78-3.75), suggesting a dose-response relationship
- NAFLD affects up to 30% of the adult population in the US and Europe, making it the most common cause of chronic liver disease worldwide
- NAFLD is a multisystem disease affecting extra-hepatic organ systems, including the cardiovascular system, independent of traditional cardiometabolic risk factors
- NAFLD was associated with subclinical atherosclerosis markers including increased carotid intima-media thickness, impaired flow-mediated vasodilation, arterial stiffness, and coronary artery calcification
- The mechanisms linking NAFLD to CVD are traced to liver histologic changes, insulin resistance, and oxidative stress

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO registered: CRD42016033481)
- **Search**: PubMed, Google Scholar, Web of Science through January 2016
- **Included studies**: 16 unique observational studies with 17 comparisons; median follow-up 6.9 years; >2,600 CVD outcomes (>70% CVD deaths)
- **Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS); all studies had observational cohort design
- **Statistical methods**: Random effects modelling; sensitivity analyses; funnel plot and Egger's test (no publication bias detected)
- **Study populations**: US, Europe (Denmark, Finland, Germany, Italy, Sweden), Asia (China, South Korea, Turkey, Japan), Africa (Egypt)
