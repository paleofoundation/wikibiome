# Key Claims

- The microbiome and [[endometriosis]] development are bidirectionally related, with dysbiosis both contributing to and resulting from the disease
- Vaginal community state types (CSTs) are relevant to endometriosis: CSTs I-III and V (*Lactobacillus*-dominated) are associated with health, while CST IV (diverse anaerobes) is linked to inflammation and [[dysbiosis]]
- Emerging evidence of urogenital-gastrointestinal crosstalk suggests microbiome changes may serve as non-invasive [[biomarkers]] for endometriosis
- The gut microbiome influences endometriosis through the estrobolome (beta-glucuronidase-producing bacteria that deconjugate estrogens)
- Microbiome-based therapeutics including probiotics, antibiotics, and omega-3 PUFAs show promise for endometriosis management

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Narrative review of recent scientific and clinical findings
- **Key topics covered**:
  - Epidemiology of endometriosis globally (prevalence 6-10% of reproductive-age women)
  - Diagnosis challenges (average 4-12 year delay)
  - Treatment guidelines comparison (ESHRE, TIGE, NICE, OGSM, ASRM)
  - Reproductive tract microbiome in endometriosis (upper vs lower FRT)
  - Gut microbiome and peritoneal microbiome alterations
  - Preclinical studies in mice and non-human primates
  - Potential microbiome-based interventions
