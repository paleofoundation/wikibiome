# Key Claims

- HMs exert a profound influence on [[gut-metal-microbiome|gut microbiota]] composition and function, contributing to [[dysbiosis]] and adverse health effects including cardiovascular disease, neurodegenerative disorders, ulcerative colitis, cirrhosis, allergies, diabetes, and autism
- [[probiotics]] have emerged as a promising strategy to counteract HM-induced dysbiosis through eight key microbial mechanisms: biosorption, bioprecipitation, bioassimilation, bioaccumulation, metal solubilization, biotransformation, bioleaching, and organic acid secretion
- Advanced **biosensor technologies** enable rapid, non-invasive detection of HMs in urine, allowing real-time monitoring of probiotic-mediated detoxification
- Approximately 60% of ingested HMs are absorbed in the intestine causing oxidative stress and intestinal barrier damage
- Common probiotic species showing HM resistance include [[bifidobacterium]], [[lactobacillus]], Streptococcus, Enterococcus, Clostridium, Bacillus, and Escherichia coli
- Limitations of probiotic therapies include strain-specificity, survivability in GI tract, individual variability, regulatory challenges, and safety concerns in immunocompromised populations

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review integrating microbiome disruption, probiotic modulation, and biosensor detection
- Metal-specific microbiota effects reviewed: As (increased pathogenic bacteria, decreased Firmicutes), Cd (barrier integrity loss via ZO-1/ZO-2/occludin/claudin-1 disruption, Akkermansia decrease), Hg (claudin 1/occludin/ZO-1/JAM1 downregulation), Pb (MUC2/tight junction protein decrease)
- Probiotic mechanisms illustrated in Figure 1 showing eight detoxification pathways
- Biosensor classification (Figure 3): electrochemical (voltammetric, amperometric, impedimetric, conductometric, potentiometric), optical (fluorescent, chemiluminescent, colorimetric), and mass-based (piezoelectric, magnetoelastic)
- Table 2 provides comprehensive biosensor specifications for detecting Hg, Pb, Cd, Cu in urine with LODs ranging from 0.01 nM to micromolar levels
- Specific biosensor innovations: DNA biosensors for Hg(II) with 0.11 pM LOD; FRET-based Pb biosensors; genetically engineered bacteria (CadR/MerR regulators) for Cd/Hg detection; colorimetric Cu sensors
