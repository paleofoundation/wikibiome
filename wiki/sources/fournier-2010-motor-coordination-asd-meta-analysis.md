# Overview

Meta-analysis of **83 ASD studies** focused on motor coordination, arm movements, gait, and postural stability deficits. Data extraction from between-group comparisons with ASD and typically developing controls (N = 51 comparisons). Demonstrates that motor impairment is a **cardinal feature** of ASD.

# Key Findings

## Large Overall Effect Size

- Standardized mean difference effect = **1.20** (SE = 0.114, p < 0.0001, Z = 10.49)
- This is a large effect indicating substantial motor coordination deficits across ASD
- 95% confidence interval: 0.973-1.42
- Fail-safe N = 6,114 (very robust against publication bias)

## Subgroup Analyses

- **Upper extremities**: ES = 0.88 (p < 0.0001)
- **Lower extremities**: ES = 1.12 (p < 0.0001)
- **Combined upper and lower**: ES = 2.338 (p < 0.001)
- All three diagnostic categories (autism, ASD, Asperger's) showed significant deficits

## Age Effects

- **Toddler and child**: ES = 1.358 (largest)
- **Child and younger adults**: ES = 1.09
- **Younger adults and adults**: ES = 1.13
- Motor impairments persist across the lifespan but may be most pronounced in early years

## Heterogeneity

- I^2 = 78.05% (substantial variability across studies)
- Variability likely reflects differences in motor tasks, ASD severity, and diagnostic criteria
