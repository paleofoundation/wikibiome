## Key Findings

- **320 natural pyoverdine variants screened** — specific structural variants potently inhibit [[acinetobacter-baumannii]], [[klebsiella-pneumoniae]], and [[staphylococcus-aureus]] by competitive [[iron]] deprivation ([[vollenweider-2024-pyoverdines-antimicrobial-iron-depriving]], in-vitro).
- **Mechanism is iron-dependent with low host toxicity** — pyoverdines sequester iron from pathogens without disrupting host iron metabolism at effective concentrations, demonstrating a therapeutic window.
- **Reduced resistance evolution potential** compared to conventional antibiotics, because the target (iron acquisition) is a fundamental metabolic requirement rather than a single enzyme.
- **Cross-species activity**: Unlike most antibiotics that target narrow taxonomic ranges, pyoverdine variants showed activity against both Gram-negative (*A. baumannii*, *K. pneumoniae*) and Gram-positive (*S. aureus*) pathogens.
- **Natural siderophore diversity** represents an untapped antimicrobial reservoir — the structural variation in pyoverdines across *Pseudomonas* species provides a library of chelators with different iron-binding affinities and selectivities.

## Methods

- **Study design**: In-vitro high-throughput screening
- **Scope**: 320 structurally diverse pyoverdine variants isolated from environmental *Pseudomonas* species, screened against clinically relevant pathogens
- **Key measurements**: Minimum inhibitory concentrations (MICs), iron-dependency of antimicrobial activity (confirmed by iron add-back experiments), host cell cytotoxicity assays, resistance evolution assays under serial passage
- **Target organisms**: *[[acinetobacter-baumannii]]*, *[[klebsiella-pneumoniae]]*, *[[staphylococcus-aureus]]* — all WHO priority pathogens for antibiotic resistance
- **Sample size**: N/A (in-vitro screening library)

## Detailed Summary

This study from eLife represents the largest systematic screen of natural siderophores for antimicrobial activity to date. The authors isolated 320 structurally distinct pyoverdine variants from environmental *Pseudomonas* isolates and tested them against three WHO priority pathogens: *[[acinetobacter-baumannii]]*, *[[klebsiella-pneumoniae]]*, and *[[staphylococcus-aureus]]*. Pyoverdines are fluorescent siderophores produced by *Pseudomonas* species with extremely high iron-binding affinity (stability constant ~10^32). The key finding is that specific pyoverdine structural variants can outcompete target pathogens' own siderophores for iron, creating localized iron deprivation that halts growth and virulence. The iron-dependency of the antimicrobial mechanism was confirmed through add-back experiments: adding excess iron reversed the inhibitory effect, proving the mechanism is competitive chelation rather than direct toxicity. Crucially, at effective antimicrobial concentrations, the pyoverdines showed low toxicity to host cells — the therapeutic window exists because mammalian cells acquire iron through transferrin-receptor pathways that are not affected by siderophore competition. Serial passage experiments revealed that pathogens developed resistance to pyoverdines much more slowly than to conventional antibiotics, consistent with the theory that targeting a fundamental metabolic requirement (iron acquisition) is harder to evolve around than targeting a single enzyme.
