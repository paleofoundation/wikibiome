# Key Claims

- **Dramatic changes in gut microbiota** occur during pregnancy due to hormonal and dietary changes
- **Gut microbiota changes** associated with preeclampsia (PE), gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM), and [[postpartum-depression]]
- **Over-proliferation of Proteobacteria** and progressive decrease of *Firmicutes* linked to GDM development
- **Disturbed gut microbiota** may induce diseases by modulating metabolism of fatty acids, amino acids, and leading to autoimmune/metabolic conditions
- Probiotics proposed as potential treatment for gestational diseases

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Review of gut microbiota-gestational disease associations; mechanisms including LPS, kynurenine, indoles, bile acid hydrolase, SCFAs, serotonin pathways
- **Key figure**: Gut [[dysbiosis]] leading to neuroinflammation and PPD via multiple metabolite pathways

# Connections

- Pregnancy-specific [[dysbiosis]] as precursor to [[postpartum-depression]]
- Metabolite pathways (SCFAs, tryptophan, bile acids) connect [[gut-brain-axis]] to pregnancy complications
- [[inflammation]] via LPS translocation during pregnancy
- Gestational microbiome changes relevant to [[developmental-metal-vulnerability]]
- Mineral homeostasis ([[zinc]], [[iron]], [[copper]], [[magnesium]]) affected by pregnancy microbiome shifts
