# Role of Gut Microbiome in Cardiovascular Events: A Systematic Review

## Summary

Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines examining the role of gut microbiome in cardiovascular events. Searched PubMed and Google Scholar (2017-2021), screening 10,989 references and ultimately including 11 traditional and systematic reviews scored by SANRA (>10/12) and AMSTAR II (>14/16) quality checklists.

## Key Findings

### Atherosclerosis
- LPS concentration leads to vascular endothelial dysfunction via TLR4-mediated NADPH oxidase pathway generating ROS
- Dysbiosis triggers inflammatory pathways including overproduction of CRP, IL-18, IL-1, IL-16, TNF, and VCAM-1
- Neuroendocrine-immune crosstalk enhanced by [[dysbiosis]] leads to disturbed LDL cholesterol, plasma triglycerides, and HDL cholesterol
- [[TMAO]] promotes foam cell formation, endothelial dysfunction, and platelet activation

### Hypertension
- Gut dysbiosis triggers loss of SCFA-producing bacteria leading to decreased butyrate, propionate, and acetate
- Decreased SCFA leads to renal inflammation, elevated salt and water reabsorption
- Reduced propionate decreases GPR41 expression, leading to decreased vasodilation
- Reduced acetate increases Olfr78 activation, causing renin surge and hypertension
- LPS causes endothelial dysfunction through TLR4-mediated oxidative pathway and vascular inflammation through p38MAPK/NF-kB pathway

### Key Studies Summarized (Table 1)
- Firmicutes most critical phylum associated with ischemic stroke; elevated Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio is hallmark of aging
- Higher TMAO levels in ischemic stroke patients with significant decrease at 48 hours
- Butyrate enhances anti-inflammatory effect via *Odoribacter* by lessening LPS-induced NF-kB activation
- People with high circulating TMAO have 12% increased CVD risk vs. low TMAO
- Increased F/B ratio associated with hypertension; *Prevotella* and *Klebsiella* increased

### Gut Microbiome Modification
- Dietary intervention and choline structural analog 3,3-DMB that inhibits TMA lyase are promising therapeutic targets
- *Lactobacillus* spp. enhance insulin secretion, promote incretin secretion in obese glucose-tolerant subjects
- Probiotics can reduce LDL cholesterol, blood pressure, inflammatory mediators, blood glucose levels, and BMI
