# Insight in Schizophrenia: A Meta-Analysis

## Key Claims

- Insight is defined as the patient's awareness of mental disorder, awareness of social consequences, awareness of need for treatment, awareness of symptoms, and attribution of symptoms to disorder
- There was a small negative relationship between insight and global, positive, and negative symptoms of [[schizophrenia]]
- A small positive relationship was found between insight and depressive symptoms -- patients with greater insight experienced more [[depression]]
- Acute status and mean age of onset moderated the relationship between insight and symptoms
- Effect sizes were relatively modest, suggesting insight is a multidimensional construct not fully explained by symptomatology alone

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Meta-analysis (Master's thesis, University of Calgary)
- **Focus**: Relationship between insight and symptom domains across multiple studies
- **Key moderators**: Acuity of illness, age of onset
