# Nakamura 2009 -- I3C and Genistein Synergistically Induce Apoptosis in Colon Cancer

## Key Claims

- Co-treatment with indole-3-carbinol (I3C, 300 microM) and genistein (40 microM) synergistically suppressed viability of HT-29 colon cancer cells at concentrations where each agent alone was ineffective (Combination Index <1 by CalcuSyn analysis)
- The synergistic cell death was caspase-dependent: co-treatment induced cleavage of PARP, caspase-8, and caspase-9, with down-regulation of XIAP and survivin anti-apoptotic proteins
- I3C and genistein combination effectively inhibited Akt phosphorylation (Ser473) by 12 hours after treatment, with subsequent dephosphorylation of mTOR (Ser2448), leading to enhanced autophagosome formation (LC3-II upregulation)
- The combination treatment does NOT induce autophagic cell death: despite enhanced LC3-II positive puncta formation, autophagy inhibitor 5-MA could not rescue cell viability, indicating autophagy is initiated but blocked prior to completion, with cells diverted to caspase-dependent apoptosis
- Both I3C (from cruciferous vegetables) and genistein (from soy) are dietary phytochemicals with established chemopreventive properties; their combination at low doses offers potential for clinical translation

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: In vitro study using HT-29 human colon cancer cells
- **Methods**: Cell viability (CellTiter-Glo), flow cytometry (sub-G1), western blot (PARP, caspases, Akt, mTOR, LC3), fluorescence microscopy (DAPI), z-VAD-fmk caspase inhibition, 5-MA autophagy inhibition, transmission electron microscopy
- **Key mechanistic pathway**: I3C + genistein -> Akt dephosphorylation -> mTOR inhibition -> autophagosome initiation -> blocked completion -> switch to caspase-dependent apoptosis
