# Key Claims

- Concentrations of Cu, Zn, Cr, and [[nickel]] were measured in three leafy vegetable species (Celosia argentea, Talinum triangulare, Vernonia amygdalina) from four market sites in Lagos, Nigeria
- **Cu concentrations exceeded WHO/FAO standards** (0.3 mg/kg) across all sites, ranging from 6.50 to 18.98 mg/g
- **Cr concentrations exceeded WHO/FAO limits** at multiple sites; highest in V. amygdalina from dumpsite (3.90 mg/g)
- **Ni concentrations exceeded the WHO/FAO limit of 2 mg/kg** in Celosia argentea from Site A (2.01 mg/g), posing a serious health risk at that site
- Despite individual metal exceedances, **hazard quotients (HQ) were all below 1** for individual metals, suggesting no immediate carcinogenic risk from single-metal consumption
- However, **continuous consumption** may pose serious health risk due to cumulative effects, with higher HQ values for children compared to adults
- Vegetables from dumpsite locations accumulated the highest concentrations of heavy metals

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional field study with laboratory analysis
- **Location**: Four popular market sites (A, B, C, D) in Lagos, Nigeria including a dumpsite area
- **Samples**: 36 total samples - 3 replicates each of Celosia argentea, Talinum triangulare, and Vernonia amygdalina from all 4 sites; also collected from dumpsite and different farmlands
- **Analysis**: Microwave acid digestion followed by ICP-MS (inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry) for Cu, Zn, Cr, and Ni
- **Cu results**: Highest in V. amygdalina from dumpsite Site A (18.98 mg/g); Cu binds strongly to organic and inorganic soil matter
- **Zn results**: Ranged from 1.33 to 7.30 mg/g; recorded concentrations exceeded regulatory limits though HQ values were below 1
- **Cr results**: Ranged from 1.00 to 3.90 mg/g; Site D V. amygdalina highest; HQ values of Cr indicated negative health effects for consumers at high-Cr sites
- **Ni results**: Ranged from 0.49 to 2.01 mg/g; highest in C. argentea from Site A; Ni is useful for plant growth at low concentrations but leads to reduced nitrogen metabolism efficiency, headache, vertigo, nausea at high levels
- **Hazard Quotients**: Highest single HQ was 0.1537 for Cu in V. amygdalina (children, Site A); Cr HQ approached concern in some combinations; all individual HQs <1 but cumulative risk not assessed
