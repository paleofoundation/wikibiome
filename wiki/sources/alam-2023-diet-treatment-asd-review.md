# Key Claims

- **Ketogenic diet** shows promise in reducing ASD core symptoms and seizures in both animal models and limited human studies
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** may reduce ASD behavioral symptoms but evidence remains limited
- **Prebiotic B-GOS** improved anti-social behavior and GI dysfunction in ASD children on exclusion diets
- **Soy-based infant formula** associated with increased prevalence of epilepsy and ADHD in Fragile X populations
- **Micronutrient deficiencies** (zinc, calcium, iron, selenium, iodine, vitamin A, thiamine) common in ASD children

# Methods & Findings

**Comprehensive review** of nutritional interventions in ASD with emphasis on Fragile X Syndrome (FXS).

**Ketogenic diet:**
- KD improved CARS scores in 18/30 children over 6 months (El-Rashidy et al. 2017)
- Modified KD with MCT improved core autism features in 15 children (Lee et al. 2018)
- KD normalized Akkermansia muciniphila levels in BTBR mouse model
- Mechanisms: improved energy metabolism, anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidative, GABA regulation, mTOR inhibition, microbiota modulation

**Omega-3 fatty acids:**
- Omega-3 supplementation improved social behavior and reduced hyperactivity in Fmr1-KO mice
- Limited clinical evidence; DHA essential for brain development

**Dietary approaches:**
- GFCF diet: reduced opioid system inflammation and ASD symptoms in some studies
- Specific carbohydrate diet (SCD): improved GI and behavioral phenotypes
- Vitamin B6 + magnesium: long history in ASD treatment

**Prebiotic/Probiotic approaches:**
- B-GOS prebiotic improved behavior in ASD children (Grimaldi et al. 2018)
- Bee pollen and propolis reduced neuroinflammation in PPA-treated hamsters
- 10% oligofructose-enriched inulin worsened sociability in BTBR mouse model

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Micronutrient deficiencies as metal dyshomeostasis**: Zinc, calcium, iron, selenium deficiencies in 30-84% of ASD children directly reflect disrupted metal ecology
- **KD-metal axis**: Ketone bodies may alter metal transport across the BBB; KD-induced Akkermansia normalization restores mucin-dependent metal barrier function
- **Soy-metal interaction**: Phytoestrogens in soy formula may disrupt metal-dependent hormone signaling; estrogenic activity via metabotropic glutamate receptor 5
- **FXS-ASD overlap**: FMR1 gene affects gut microbiota composition, suggesting genetic metal-microbiome vulnerability in a significant ASD subpopulation
