# Overview

First bidirectional two-sample MR study to establish **cause-and-effect relationships between 731 immunophenotypes and ASD** using GWAS data from Sardinian (n=3,757) and European (n=10,610 and n=10,263) populations. Identifies 26 immunophenotypes with potential causal effects on ASD across four immunological categories.

# Key Findings

## Immune Cells Increasing ASD Risk (15 positive causal directions)

- **CD4+ %leukocyte** (OR = 1.21, P < 0.001)
- **CD20 on CD24+CD27+** (OR = 1.13)
- **CD28-CD8dim %CD8dim** (OR = 1.31)
- **CD39+secreting Treg AC** (OR = 1.11)
- **Granulocyte %leukocyte** (OR = 1.22)
- Multiple dendritic cell and myeloid cell subtypes also identified

## Immune Cells Protective Against ASD (11 reverse causal directions)

- **CD3- lymphocyte %leukocyte** (OR = 0.69, P = 0.0011) -- strongest protective signal
- **CD45 on HLA DR+ CD4+** (OR = 0.84)
- **CD62L on CD62L+ DC** (OR = 0.86)
- **CD80 on CD62L+ plasmacytoid DC** (OR = 0.78)
- **IgD+CD24+%Bcell** (OR = 0.77)

## Bidirectional Effects

- ASD also showed potential causal impact on 13 immunophenotypes
- Notable: ASD associated with lower CD19 on IgD+CD38-unswitched memory B cells
- Meta-analysis of two IVW estimates confirmed consistency

## No Significant Heterogeneity or Pleiotropy

- All MR analyses showed no indication of effect size heterogeneity
- MR-Egger regression confirmed no horizontal pleiotropy
