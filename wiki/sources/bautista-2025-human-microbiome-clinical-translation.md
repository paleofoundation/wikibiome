# Key Claims

- **Human microbiome** is now recognized as a dynamic and essential determinant of physiology across the lifespan
- **Clinical translation** of microbiome science has begun redefining disease etiology, therapeutic design, and individualized medicine
- **Neonatal microbiome** begins to shape immune development, metabolism, and mucosal integrity from birth
- **Innovative strategies**: Phage therapy, live biotherapeutics, precision nutrition, and microbiota transplantation
- Significant challenges persist including interindividual variability, incomplete functional annotation of "dark matter," and absence of validated biomarkers

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of microbiome clinical translation spanning gut, skin, lungs, oral cavity, and reproductive tract
- **Key neonatal section**: Vaginal delivery facilitates maternal transfer of *Lactobacillus*, *Prevotella*, *Sneathia*; HMOs serve as selective substrates for *Bifidobacterium infantis*
