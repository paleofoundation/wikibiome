# Gut Microbiota in NSAID Enteropathy: New Insights From Inside

## Summary

Review examining the interplay between gut microbiota dysbiosis and [[NSAIDs]]-induced enteropathy. Covers mechanisms by which NSAIDs alter bacterial composition, aggravate bile acid cytotoxicity, impair intestinal barrier via cyclooxygenase inhibition and mitochondrial destruction, and reviews therapeutic strategies.

## Key Findings

### NSAID Effects on Gut Microbiota (Table 1)
- Indomethacin: increased *Prevotellaceae* (female), *Ruminococcus* (male); decreased *Firmicutes* (female)
- Celecoxib: no significant abundance alterations in obese post-menopausal women
- Aspirin: raised *Acidaminococcaceae*, *Propionibacteriaceae*; ibuprofen raised *Pseudomonadaceae*
- Multiple NSAIDs with PPIs: increased *Acidaminococcaceae*, *Enterococcaceae*; decreased *Erysipelotrichaceae*
- Elderly on multiple NSAIDs: decreased *Roseburia*, *Collinsella* spp.; decreased *Ruminococcus*, *Lactobacillus*

### Mechanisms of NSAID Enteropathy
- NSAIDs impair intestinal barrier by inhibiting cyclooxygenase and destroying mitochondria
- Bacterial LPS + TLR4 activation leads to NF-kB pathway, TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6 production
- NSAIDs competitively bind phosphatidylcholine with [[bile acids]], increasing toxic bile salt micelle formation
- ER stress participates in NSAID-induced cell death via NLRP3 inflammasome activation

### Bile Acid Involvement
- Secondary bile acids produced by gut bacteria (*C. perfringens*, *Listeria monocytogenes*, *Bacteroides vulgatus*, *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium*) are pathogenic in intestinal injury
- Ursodeoxycholic acid and taurodeoxycholic acid produce pro-inflammatory NF-kB activation

### Therapeutic Approaches
- [[Probiotics]] (antibiotics, prebiotics, mucosal protective agents, [[FMT]])
- Proton pump inhibitors adversely affect NSAID enteropathy by promoting *Enterococcus*, *Enterobacteriaceae*
- Metronidazole and rifaximin effective for NSAID enteropathy prevention

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **NSAIDs and CVD** - NSAIDs widely used in CVD patients; their gut microbiome effects compound cardiovascular risk via increased intestinal permeability and [[inflammation]]
2. **Bile acid-microbiome axis** - NSAID disruption of bile acid homeostasis intersects with microbiome-mediated bile acid metabolism relevant to [[cardiovascular-disease]]
3. **Drug-microbiome bidirectionality** - Gut microbiome alters NSAID bioavailability while NSAIDs reshape the microbiome
