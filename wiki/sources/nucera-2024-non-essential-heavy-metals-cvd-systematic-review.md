# Nucera 2024 — Non-essential heavy metal effects in cardiovascular diseases: overview of systematic reviews

**Citation:** Nucera S, Serra M, Caminiti R, et al. *Non-essential heavy metal effects in cardiovascular diseases: an overview of systematic reviews.* Frontiers in Cardiovascular Medicine. 2024;11:1332339. DOI: [10.3389/fcvm.2024.1332339](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcvm.2024.1332339)

## Key Findings

- **Cadmium (Cd).** 38 studies, 160,000 subjects across 12 countries. Cd associates with hypertension, atherosclerosis, CHD, stroke, PAD, and myocardial infarction. Mechanism: ROS, TNF-α, NF-kB p65, NLRP3, endothelial damage via reduced NO and increased endothelin-1 (EDN-1). Physiologic plasma range <1 µg/L, pathologic >5 µg/L. Smokers show significantly higher Cd in blood/urine.
- **Mercury (Hg).** 6 studies, 4,848 participants. MeHg exposure in pregnancy associates with higher diastolic BP in 2/6 reviews; perinatal Hg exposure linked to hypertension onset in adolescence. Mechanism: ROS/lipid peroxidation, LDL oxidation, PLA2 activation, PON inactivation (reduced HDL protection), glutathione depletion.
- **Arsenic (As).** Inorganic As (iAs) more toxic than organic. Associated with CHD, PAD, CAD, atherosclerosis, endothelial dysfunction, stroke. EPA drinking water standard 10 µg/L. Mechanism: sICAM-1 and sVCAM-1 elevation (adhesion molecules), NO reduction, PON-1 decrease, oxidative stress.
- **Lead (Pb).** Ubiquitous in soil, food, water, cosmetics, tobacco. >10 µg/dL correlates with hypertension, CAD, PAD, heart failure, stroke. Pb above reference causes 2.24x BP association; mortality increased 5.9x at Pb >10 µg/dL in CKD. Mechanism: H₂O₂ and superoxide generation, eNOS disruption, endothelin-1 elevation, Ca²⁺ transport alteration, elastin synthesis disruption, Cu/Zn homeostasis disruption.
- **Mechanistic convergence.** All four metals drive cardiovascular damage through: (1) ROS/oxidative stress and glutathione/SOD/catalase depletion, (2) endothelial dysfunction via NO reduction and endothelin elevation, (3) lipid peroxidation and LDL oxidation, (4) inflammation (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α, COX-2), (5) displacement of essential metals (Ca²⁺, Zn, Cu) from physiologic binding sites.

## Methods

PRISMA-compliant overview of systematic reviews. PubMed, Medline, Embase, Web of Science searched through March 2023. Inclusion: human systematic reviews on non-essential heavy metals and CVD, last 5 years. 19,484 records identified → 2,400 screened → 1,621 titles → 753 abstracts → 257 full-text → 8 reviews included (total 153 studies, ~348,259 cumulative participants across Cd reviews alone). Qualitative synthesis across metal × CVD pathology × biomatrix (blood, urine, hair).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Review notes that "even at low levels" Cd drives BP elevation, challenging the utility of current regulatory thresholds (EFSA TWI 2.5 µg/kg bw/week for Cd, WHO As 10 µg/L).
- MeHg vs. inorganic Hg distinction is important — some reviews lumped these, obscuring mechanistic differences.
- No direct microbiome data in this review — connection to WikiBiome's metals-microbiome thesis requires cross-referencing with [[zhu-2024-toxic-essential-metals-gut-microbiota]] and [[liu-2023-cadmium-microbiota-metabolome-rats]].

## Open Questions

- Whether gut microbiome modulation (e.g., chelation-competent probiotics) could mitigate the endothelial damage trajectory.
- Whether combined low-level exposure (Cd+Pb+As) acts additively or synergistically in CVD.
- Dose-response curves below current regulatory thresholds remain underpowered.
