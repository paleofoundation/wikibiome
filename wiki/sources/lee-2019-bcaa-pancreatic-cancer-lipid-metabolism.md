# Key Claims

- BCAA metabolism has an important role in PDAC growth by regulating lipogenesis; PDAC cells exhibit significantly elevated BCAA uptake through solute carrier transporters
- BCAT2 knockdown markedly impaired PDAC cell proliferation but not normal HPDE cell proliferation, without significant alterations in glutamate or ROS levels
- BCKDHA knockdown significantly inhibited fatty-acid synthesis, indicating PDAC cells may utilize BCAAs as a carbon source for lipid biosynthesis
- The BCAA metabolic pathway may provide a novel therapeutic target for pancreatic cancer treatment

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: In vitro study using PDAC cell lines (8988T, MIAPACA2) vs. normal human pancreatic ductal (HPDE) cells
- **BCAA uptake**: PDAC cells showed significantly elevated leucine, isoleucine, and valine uptake compared to HPDE cells at 24h and 48h
- **SLC transporter overexpression**: SLC1A5 (p=7.19E-4), SLC3A2 (p=0.003), SLC7A5 (p=4.51E-6) all upregulated in pancreatic cancer tissues (171 tumors vs 179 normal) from GEPIA database
- **BCAT2 essentiality**: BCAT2 knockdown dramatically reduced PDAC cell viability and colony formation; HPDE cells unaffected
- **Lipogenesis connection**: BCKDHA knockdown led to dramatic reduction in free fatty acids and triglyceride accumulation; palmitic acid supplementation rescued proliferation inhibition
- **Glucose independence**: Neither glucose uptake nor lactate production changed upon BCAT2 knockdown; BCAA-driven growth is independent of glycolysis
