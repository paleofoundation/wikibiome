# Key Claims

- **Gut fungal communities are altered** during gestational diabetes
- **Fungal [[dysbiosis]]** may contribute to metabolic disruption during pregnancy
- **Mycobiome changes may serve as biomarkers** for gestational diabetes risk

# Methods & Findings

Study of gut fungal communities in gestational diabetes:

- Fungal community profiling using ITS sequencing
- Comparison between GDM and normoglycemic pregnant women
- Fungal-bacterial interactions assessed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Pregnancy zinc demands create vulnerability to fungal expansion in the gut
- **[[iron]]**: Iron supplementation during pregnancy may inadvertently promote fungal growth
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent antifungal immunity may be strained during pregnancy
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Pregnancy represents a unique metal-microbiome state where fungal [[dysbiosis]] risk is elevated
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Gestational mycobiome [[dysbiosis]] may establish metabolic programming for offspring
