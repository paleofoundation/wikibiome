# Key Claims

- **Insufficient evidence** to determine if omega-3 fatty acids are beneficial for ASD (Evidence Rating = "I")
- **Only one RCT** identified (n = 13): non-significant trend toward benefit in hyperactivity and stereotypy
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** are among the most commonly used CAM therapies for ASD (28.7% prevalence)
- **Three case-control studies** found lower omega-3 levels in ASD children; inconsistent results

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Systematic review of MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane (143 articles identified, 6 met inclusion criteria).

**Included studies:**
1. **Amminger et al. 2007 (RCT):** n=13, 840mg DHA + 700mg EPA daily for 6 weeks. Non-significant trends toward improvement in hyperactivity (effect size 0.71) and stereotypy (effect size 0.72) on ABC
2. **Politi et al. 2008:** n=19 young adults, 0.93g DHA+EPA for 6 weeks. No improvement from baseline
3. **Meguid et al. 2008:** n=30, 240mg DHA + 52mg EPA for 3 months. 20/30 improved on CARS
4. **Patrick and Salik 2005:** n=22, ProEPA Nordic Naturals for 90 days. Significant improvement in ABLLS subscales
5. **Bell et al. 2004:** n=9, 2-4g/day omega-6 with omega-3 for 6 months. Parental reports of improvements
6. **Johnson and Hollander 2003:** Case report, 3g/day EPA eliminated anxiety and agitation in 8 months

**Evidence quality:** Only the Amminger RCT was methodologically sound (Jadad 4/5). All others lacked control groups.

**Side effects:** Generally minimal; some GI complaints, increased activity reported.

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **DHA-metal interaction**: DHA is essential for brain development and its incorporation into neural tissue depends on zinc and selenium-dependent desaturase enzymes
- **Anti-inflammatory-metal axis**: Omega-3 derived resolvins and protectins modulate metal-dependent inflammatory cascades (COX/LOX pathways require iron)
- **Fatty acid deficiency as metallomic marker**: Low omega-3 levels in ASD may reflect impaired metal-dependent fatty acid elongation and desaturation
- **BBB permeability**: DHA maintains BBB integrity; deficiency may compound metal translocation into CNS
