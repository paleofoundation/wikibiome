# Key Claims

- Mannitol (OR 0.97, p=0.006) and methionine (OR 0.97, p=0.006) are causally associated with decreased [[pancreatic-cancer]] (PDAC) risk; stearic acid (OR 0.93, p=0.031) also protective
- Carnitine (OR 1.01, p=0.027), hippuric acid (OR 1.02, p=0.027), and 3-methylhistidine (OR 1.05, p=0.02) are causally associated with increased PDAC risk
- Romboutsia genus (OR 0.87, p=0.004) and Clostridium sensu stricto 1 (OR 0.88, p=0.027) are causally associated with decreased PDAC risk
- This is the largest MR study to date investigating gut bacteria and bacteria-associated metabolites in PDAC, using 8,769 cases and 7,055 controls

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Two-sample MR using MiBioGen consortium (119 genera), Exposome Explorer, and Human Metabolome Database for 109 metabolites; PanScan-III and PanC4 PDAC GWAS
- **Metabolite results**: Mannitol and methionine remained significant after multiple sensitivity analyses; stearic acid supported by alternative IVW methods; carnitine, hippuric acid, 3-methylhistidine associated with increased risk
- **Microbial taxa results**: Romboutsia (OR 0.87) confirmed by multiple methods (IVW, weighted median, Lasso, debiased IVW, radial IVW, RAPS); Clostridium sensu stricto 1 (OR 0.88) also confirmed
- **Sensitivity**: No significant heterogeneity or pleiotropy detected for key findings; however, neither finding survived Bonferroni correction
- **Metabolite identification**: 109 metabolites identified through comprehensive literature search of microbiome-related circulating metabolites including amino acids, vitamins, cofactors, fatty acids, bile acids, sterols
