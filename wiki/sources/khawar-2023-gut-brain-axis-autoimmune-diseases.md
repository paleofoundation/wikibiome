# Key Claims

- **Gut-brain axis (GBA)** is a two-way communication system influenced by nervous system, hormones, metabolism, immune system, and microbes
- **Mounting evidence** connects autoimmune disorders (AIDs) to [[dysbiosis]] and [[gut-brain-axis]] dysfunction
- **Bacterial translocation** and LPS-mediated [[inflammation]] may trigger autoimmune responses through molecular mimicry
- **Dysbiosis** characterized as reduction in diversity and shift toward pathogenic microorganisms
- Dietary changes (prebiotics, probiotics) may prevent or treat AIDs by restoring microbiota composition

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of GBA in autoimmune diseases including IBD, rheumatoid arthritis, and neurological autoimmune conditions
- **Key mechanisms**: SCFAs as immune modulators, intestinal barrier integrity, LPS translocation, molecular mimicry

# Connections

- Autoimmune-microbiome pathways relevant to immune dysregulation in [[postpartum-depression]]
- [[inflammation]] via LPS translocation applicable to perinatal immune shifts
- [[dysbiosis]]-driven molecular mimicry relevant to [[developmental-metal-vulnerability]]
- [[gut-brain-axis]] dysfunction shared mechanism across autoimmune and psychiatric conditions
