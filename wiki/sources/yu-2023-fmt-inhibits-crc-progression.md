# Key Claims

- [[FMT]] from healthy mice to CRC mice significantly reduces tumor number (from 17.6 to 9.8 average), tumor diameter (from 4.4 mm to 2.3 mm), and prolongs survival
- FMT reverses intestinal microbial dysbiosis in CRC mice, restoring diversity and normalizing the Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio
- Massive infiltration of immune cells occurs after FMT: CD8+ T cells and CD49b+ NK cells are significantly increased in FMT-treated CRC mice, enabling direct killing of cancer cells
- FMT downregulates pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1a, IL-6, IL-12a, IL-12b, IL-17a) while upregulating anti-inflammatory IL-10
- Anti-cancer immunoregulatory factors TGFb, STAT3, TNFa, IFNg, and CXCR4 are elevated after FMT, promoting anti-cancer efficacy
- Specific bacteria positively correlated with anti-cancer cytokines include Odoribacter, Lachnospiraceae UCG-006, and Desulfovibrio; negatively correlated: Alloprevotella, Ruminoclostridium, Prevotellaceae UCG-001, and Oscillibacter

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original research, mouse model (Balb/c mice)
- **CRC model**: AOM (10 mg/kg) + DSS (2.5%, 4 cycles) to induce CRC over 10 weeks
- **FMT protocol**: Fecal samples from healthy Balb/c donor mice, freshly collected, mixed in PBS (200 mg/ml), centrifuged; 1 ml microbial solution by enema every 3 days for 9 weeks
- **16S rRNA sequencing**: Full-length 16S rDNA via PacBio platform; 15 phyla, 23 classes, 33 orders, 60 families, 148 genera, and 203 species identified
- **Microbiota restoration**: FMT significantly increased microbial diversity (Shannon index) and restored abundance patterns; Lactobacillus and other beneficial genera increased
- **Immune profiling**: Immunohistochemistry for CD3, CD4, CD8, FOXP3, F4/80, CD49b; PCR array for 84 inflammation/immunity genes; Western blot for cytokine proteins
- **Key immune finding**: CD8+ T cells and CD49b+ NK cells were much more abundant in FMT mice vs CRC mice; Foxp3+ Treg cells were significantly reduced in CRC but partially restored by FMT
