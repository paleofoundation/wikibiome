# Key Claims

- **Exposure to heavy metals disturbs gut microbiota composition** and can lead to [[dysbiosis]]
- **As, Pb, and Hg increased abundance of Collinsella** as pathobionts
- **Heavy metal exposure related to leaky gut**, oxidative stress, IBD, and cancers
- **Probiotic treatment and nutritional strategies** (high fiber, antioxidant-rich diets) should be considered
- **12 studies for As, Pb, Hg, and Cd** included; no eligible study found for aluminium

# Methods & Findings

Systematic review of human observational studies on HM exposure and gut microbiota:

- PubMed, Scopus, ISI Web of Science, Google Scholar searched
- Nutritional approach emphasized for mitigation strategies

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc competes with cadmium and lead for absorption -- adequate zinc protects against heavy metal-induced [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[iron]]**: Iron status modifies heavy metal toxicity through shared absorption pathways
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium directly antagonizes mercury toxicity and protects gut microbiota
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Heavy metals are upstream drivers of disease-associated [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[probiotics]]**: [[probiotics]] as therapeutic agents against heavy metal-induced [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Heavy metal-induced leaky gut links metal exposure to autoimmune disease risk
