# Key Claims

- [[FMT]] attenuates colitis-associated cancer (CAC) progression in AOM/DSS mice by restoring the Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes balance, reducing inflammation, and inducing regulatory T cells ([[Tregs]])
- FMT significantly reduces tumor load (from ~20 to ~7 tumors), tumor size, and colonic shortening while improving body weight and survival
- FMT inhibits canonical NF-kB (p65 phosphorylation) activity and cellular proliferation (Ki67) in colons of CAC mice, but does not affect non-canonical NF-kB (p100) signaling
- CD4+CD25+Foxp3+ Treg cells are significantly increased after FMT treatment in spleen, mesenteric lymph nodes, and lamina propria lymphocytes
- Fecal microbiota or [[butyrate]] can directly induce Treg cell differentiation from splenocytes in vitro, in a dose-dependent manner
- FMT decreases pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, IL-1beta, TNF-alpha) while increasing anti-inflammatory IL-10 and TGF-beta

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original research, mouse model (Balb/c)
- **CAC model**: AOM (7.5 mg/kg, single injection) + DSS (2.0%, drinking water on days 7, 21, 28, 42, 49); FMT given on days 21, 42, 63; sacrificed day 70
- **FMT protocol**: Fresh fecal samples from healthy Balb/c mice homogenized in PBS; administered by oral gavage
- **Microbiota analysis**: 16S rRNA sequencing at days 28, 49, and 70; alpha diversity (species richness, Shannon, PD whole tree, Chao1) and beta diversity (PCA) measured
- **Key microbiota findings**: CAC mice showed unbalanced Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes; FMT partially restored balance; diversity initially decreased in CAC, then restored after FMT
- **NF-kB analysis**: Immunofluorescence showed phospho-p65 markedly elevated in CAC colons, substantially reduced by FMT; phospho-p100 was increased in CAC but not affected by FMT
- **Treg cells**: FACS analysis of CD4+CD25+Foxp3+ cells; significantly upregulated in spleen (P < 0.01), MLN (P < 0.05), and LPL (P < 0.01) after FMT
- **In vitro**: UV-killed fecal microbiota and butyrate both induced Treg differentiation from splenocytes, supporting that both microbial signals and metabolites contribute
