# Key Findings

- **Beta-glucan is buried beneath a mannoprotein coat** in both *C. albicans* and *S. cerevisiae*, rendering the fungal cell wall invisible to Dectin-1-mediated immune recognition ([[wheeler-2006-drug-sensitive-network-masks-fungi-immune]], in-vitro + animal-model).
- **Sub-inhibitory caspofungin unmasks beta-glucan**, exposing the immunostimulatory PAMP and triggering up to **10-fold increases in TNF-alpha** production from macrophages.
- **A conserved genetic network** involving SLT2 (cell wall integrity MAPK), SLA1, and MNN10 is required for maintaining the masking architecture.

# Methods

In-vitro cell wall analysis and macrophage co-culture assays using *C. albicans* and *S. cerevisiae* mutant libraries. Validated in murine infection models (animal-model component).

# Relevance to WikiBiome

- Foundational description of the **beta-glucan masking mechanism** — informs [[biofilm]], [[candida-albicans]], and immune evasion concepts.
- Establishes that masking is a conserved fungal strategy, not unique to *C. albicans*.

# Relevance to Cureva

- Supports **Primitive 6** (Interkingdom Relationships and Functional Shielding): the masking architecture is the molecular basis for why Candida persists despite active immune surveillance.
- Caspofungin unmasking provides a mechanistic rationale for echinocandin-based interventions that go beyond direct antifungal activity.
