# Key Claims

- **T1D patients show distinct gut microbiome profiles** vs healthy controls with altered Prevotella, Faecalibacterium, and Ruminococcaceae abundance
- **HbA1c negatively correlated** with abundance of Prevotella, Faecalibacterium, and Ruminococcaceae
- **Bifidobacteria positively correlated** with abundance of Dorea formicigenerans, Bacteroidetes, Lactobacillales, and Bacteroides
- **Positive correlation** between inflammatory parameters and gut [[dysbiosis]] in T1D
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]** may be increased in T1D contributing to bacterial translocation and inflammation

# Methods & Findings

Systematic review (2003-2023) using Google Scholar, PubMed, Web of Science:

- Patients with T1D may experience leaky gut, bacterial translocation, inflammation, and poor glucose management due to microbiome [[dysbiosis]]
- Direct manipulation of human gut microbiome and its effects on gut permeability and glycemic control not thoroughly investigated
- Correlation between Clostridiaceae and time in range observed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc deficiency impairs tight junction integrity, directly contributing to the [[intestinal-permeability]] documented in T1D -- zinc supplementation may restore barrier function
- **[[iron]]**: Iron overload in T1D gut may select for pathogenic iron-dependent taxa while depleting beneficial [[probiotics]] like Faecalibacterium
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenoproteins protect beta-cells from oxidative damage; dysbiotic microbiome may impair selenium bioavailability
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: The metal environment shapes which taxa thrive -- dysbiotic communities in T1D may reflect altered metal availability
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Microbiome-driven inflammation disrupts immune tolerance to beta-cells
