# Influence of Dietary Habits on Oxidative Stress Markers in Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

## Key Claims

- [[Hashimotos thyroiditis]] (HT) patients reported significantly higher intake frequencies of animal foods (meat, P=0.0001; fish, P=0.0001; dairy, P=0.004) compared to controls, who consumed more plant foods (legumes, P=0.001; fruits/vegetables, P=0.030; nuts, P=0.0005)
- Meat consumption was associated with increased odds ratio of developing [[thyroid autoimmunity]], while [[Mediterranean diet]] traits were protective
- HT subjects had significantly higher serum [[advanced glycation end products]] (AGEs, markers of [[oxidative stress]], P=0.0001) and lower activity of [[glutathione peroxidase]] (P=0.020), [[thioredoxin reductase]] (P=0.023), and total plasma antioxidant activity (P=0.002)
- Stepwise regression models demonstrated a significant dependence of oxidative stress parameters on consumption of animal foods, mainly meat
- Low intake of animal foods had a protective effect toward thyroid autoimmunity and a positive influence on redox balance

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Clinical/basic original study (cross-sectional)
- **Population**: 200 subjects (173 females, 27 males; median age 37 years) with no pharmacological treatment; exclusion of infectious/inflammatory/autoimmune comorbidity, kidney failure, diabetes, and cancer
- **Groups**: 81 euthyroid HT patients (71 females, 10 males) vs. 119 controls (102 females, 17 males)
- **Markers assessed**: Serum TSH, free thyroxine, anti-thyroid antibodies, serum AGEs, glutathione peroxidase, thioredoxin reductase, total plasma antioxidant activity
- **Dietary assessment**: Food frequency questionnaire evaluating intake of food groups and adherence to the [[Mediterranean diet]]
- **Key finding**: HT patients who preferentially consumed poultry instead of red/processed meat were less prevalent than in controls (P=0.0141)
- **Conclusion**: Low animal food intake is protective toward thyroid autoimmunity and positively influences redox balance, potentially impacting oxidative stress-related disorders
