# Key Claims

- **Perinatal depression (PND)** affects approximately 20% of women during the perinatal period and 17% after birth
- **Gut microbiota communicates with the brain** through neural, immune, and endocrine pathways playing critical roles in mood regulation
- **Probiotics and FMT** are increasingly explored for their potential to restore microbial balance and alleviate depressive symptoms
- **Pre-pregnancy microbiota changes**: Reduced *Lactobacillus* and increased *Prevotella* and *Verrumicrobia* during early pregnancy
- **Third-trimester diversity reduction** may set stage for postpartum [[dysbiosis]]

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review of microbiota-gut-brain axis in perinatal depression; PubMed, Web of Science, and Cochrane searches from January 2014 to January 2025
- **Key sections**: Pre-pregnancy, pregnancy, and postpartum microbiota changes; SCFA, neurotransmitter, and immune pathways; therapeutic strategies
- **Key implication**: Microbiota-targeted interventions may complement or replace antidepressants for [[postpartum-depression]]
