# Why This Paper Matters

This comprehensive review covers how Group B Streptococcus (GBS/S. agalactiae) -- a leading cause of neonatal sepsis and meningitis -- survives the triple assault of oxidative stress, nitrosative stress, and metal ion intoxication/mismetallation deployed by the host immune system. For the wiki's [[mis-metallation]] framework, GBS is a particularly informative organism because it is a Gram-positive commensal that transitions to pathogen, meaning it must navigate metal environments ranging from the GI/vaginal tract (variable metals) to the intracellular phagosome (Cu/Zn poisoning). The review explicitly frames metal toxicity as causing "mismetallation and cytotoxicity," directly connecting the Irving-Williams series concept to pathogenesis.

# Key Claims

- GBS colonizes ~10-30% of pregnant women and causes early-onset (first 7 days) and late-onset (1 week to 90 days) neonatal disease including sepsis, meningitis, and pneumonia
- Host phagocytes deploy **three converging killing strategies**: reactive oxygen species (ROS), reactive nitrogen species (RNS), and metal ion mobilization (Cu, Zn poisoning)
- **Metal ion intoxication** causes mismetallation of metalloenzymes, where excess Cu or Zn displaces native metal cofactors following the Irving-Williams series, inactivating essential enzymes
- GBS has evolved specific mechanisms to counter each stress axis: superoxide dismutase (SodA), catalase-like peroxidases, copper efflux (CopA), zinc efflux, and manganese acquisition systems
- **Copper toxicity** in phagosomes involves macrophage Cu-ATPase (ATP7A) pumping Cu+ into the phagolysosome; GBS counters this with CopA (P1B-type ATPase) and CopY (Cu-sensing repressor)
- **Zinc poisoning** is mediated by host zinc transporters flooding the phagosome with Zn2+; excess Zn2+ displaces Mn2+ from Mn-dependent enzymes like SodA
- GBS is a **Mn-centric organism** that relies on Mn for SOD activity and oxidative stress defense; host Mn restriction via calprotectin is therefore a potent anti-GBS strategy
- The **ST-17 hypervirulent lineage** of GBS is particularly associated with neonatal meningitis and shows enhanced resistance to metal stress
- GBS disease in nonpregnant adults is increasing, now representing three-quarters of GBS bacteremia cases

# Methods & Findings

## Oxidative Stress Defense
- GBS lacks catalase but expresses Mn-dependent SodA, alkyl hydroperoxide reductase (AhpCF), and thiol peroxidase
- SodA requires Mn2+ for activity; host Mn restriction directly compromises GBS antioxidant defense
- NADH oxidase (Nox) and glutathione systems provide additional ROS protection

## Nitrosative Stress Defense
- Host macrophages produce NO via iNOS; NO reacts with superoxide to form peroxynitrite (ONOO-)
- GBS defense mechanisms include flavohemoglobin and NO-detoxifying enzymes
- RNS damage Fe-S clusters, heme proteins, and thiol residues

## Metal Stress and Mismetallation
- Cu and Zn are deliberately mobilized by phagocytes to toxic levels inside phagosomes
- Excess Cu displaces Fe from Fe-S clusters and generates hydroxyl radicals via Fenton chemistry
- Excess Zn displaces Mn from SodA and other Mn-dependent enzymes
- GBS metal efflux systems (CopA for Cu, CzcD for Zn) are essential for intracellular survival
- Mn acquisition via PsaBCA is critical for restoring metalloenzyme function under host metal restriction

## Clinical Relevance
- >40,000 maternal invasive GBS episodes annually worldwide
- GBS6 hexavalent conjugate vaccine in development
- Increasing adult GBS disease linked to diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancer
- Understanding metal stress responses may identify new therapeutic targets
