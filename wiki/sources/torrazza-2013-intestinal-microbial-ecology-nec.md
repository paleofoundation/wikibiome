# Torrazza 2013 — Intestinal Microbial Ecology and Environmental Factors Affecting NEC

## Key Findings

- **Proteobacteria bloom precedes NEC.** Proteobacteria reached 61% relative abundance in NEC cases vs. 19% in controls approximately 2 weeks before clinical diagnosis (prospective case-control, n=53).
- **Klebsiella pneumoniae-like signature.** A Klebsiella pneumoniae-like 16S sequence appeared in 11 of 12 NEC cases during week 1 of life — a striking early marker.
- **Bifidobacterial depletion.** Bifidobacteria were significantly lower in NEC cases 2 weeks pre-diagnosis, consistent with the loss of protective SCFA producers before the bloom.
- **Clostridium perfringens** was detected more frequently in NEC stools and fits the anaerobe-dominance-to-facultative-bloom trajectory.

## Methods

Prospective case-control at University of Florida NICU. 53 preterm infants (18 NEC, 35 matched controls). Serial stool samples collected weekly. 16S rRNA sequencing with taxonomic classification. Comparisons at multiple time points before diagnosis.
