# Key Claims

- Gastric cancer is associated with a distinct gastric fungal mycobiome signature characterized by decreased biodiversity and richness and significant differences in fungal composition
- The ratio of Basidiomycota to Ascomycota was significantly higher in GC than healthy controls (p<0.0001), reflecting fungal dysbiosis
- A panel of GC-associated fungi (Cutaneotrichosporon, Rhodotorula) exhibited diagnostic value with mRNA levels of cytokines and chemokines correlated with specific fungal dysbiosis patterns
- Apiotrichum (23.7%), Cutaneotrichosporon, Sarocladium, and Malassezia were significantly enriched in GC tissues

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: GC tissues (n=22), matched para-GC tissues (n=22), and healthy controls (n=11); fungal ITS2 gene sequencing
- **Biodiversity**: Alpha diversity (chao1, observed_species) dramatically reduced in GC vs HC (p<0.0001); PCoA showed distinct separation (Bray-Curtis, p<0.0001)
- **Phylum-level shifts**: Ascomycota dominant in GC; Basidiomycota to Ascomycota ratio increased
- **Genus-level enrichment in GC**: Apiotrichum (23.7%), Cutaneotrichosporon, Sarocladium, Malassezia all significantly increased
- **Genera enriched in HC**: Rhizopus, Rhodotorula, Apodus, Cystobasidium all significantly decreased in GC
- **LEfSe analysis**: 39 differential taxa including 9 classes, 11 orders, and multiple families/genera
- **Functional classification**: FUNGuild analysis predicted specific ecological functions including pathotrophs and saprotrophs
