# Key Claims

- LC-MS metabolomics identified three single metabolite markers discriminating PDAC from chronic pancreatitis: glycocholic acid, N-palmitoyl glutamic acid, and hexanoylcarnitine
- A five-metabolite panel (including phenylacetylglutamine and chenodeoxyglycocholate) further improved discrimination between PDAC and CP
- All three single markers were upregulated in PDAC compared to CP, confirmed across two independent cohorts (German discovery, Swedish validation)
- Phospholipids showed reversed fold-change directions between discovery and validation cohorts, highlighting matrix-dependent effects

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Discovery cohort (PDAC n=44, CP n=23) and validation cohort (PDAC n=20, CP n=31) from independent institutions
- **Analytical platform**: Reversed phase LC-MS (untargeted); 4578 metabolite features after peak filtering
- **Univariate analysis**: 254 features significant by Student's t test; 17 confirmed in validation; 3 single markers remained after quality control
- **Multivariate OPLS-DA**: 5-metabolite panel; refined model R2X(cum) 0.513, Q2(cum) correctly classified 50%
- **Glycocholic acid**: Bile acid metabolite upregulated in PDAC; connects to altered [[bile-acid-metabolism]]
- **Hexanoylcarnitine**: Acylcarnitine reflecting disrupted fatty acid oxidation
- **N-palmitoyl glutamic acid**: Lipid-amino acid conjugate; novel biomarker
