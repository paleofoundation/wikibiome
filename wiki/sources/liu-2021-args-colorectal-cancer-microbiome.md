# Key Claims

- CRC patients harbor significantly enriched [[antibiotic resistance genes]] (ARGs) in their gut microbiota: 25 species and 65 ARGs were significantly enriched compared to healthy controls
- 12 multidrug-resistant genes (MRGs) were enriched in CRC, mainly including acrB, TolC, marA, E. coli acrR mutation, and AcrS, conferring resistance to fluoroquinolones, tetracyclines, cephalosporins, and rifamycin antibiotics
- [[Escherichia coli]] is the primary antibiotic resistance reservoir in CRC patients; 90% of rSGB-sourced ARGs (45 of 50) were found in E. coli
- CRC patients had higher ARG diversity (Shannon-Wiener index H', P < 0.001) and higher beta diversity of ARGs (NMDS, PERMANOVA P = 0.01, R = 0.0058) compared to controls
- A classification model using species and ARG abundance achieved AUC of 0.831 and 0.715 respectively, suggesting potential as CRC biomarkers

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original research, metagenomic analysis
- **Cohort**: 382 CRC patients and 387 healthy controls from 8 published metagenomic datasets (Table 1) across multiple countries
- **Methods**: De novo assembly to obtain representative species-level genome bins (rSGBs) and plasmids; CARD database annotation for ARGs
- **Species enrichment**: 25 species enriched in CRC group including E. coli (GTDB classification), Parvimonas, B. fragilis, Porphyromonas, Alistipes fingegoldii; 35 species enriched in controls including Anaerobutyricum, Bifidobacterium, Faecalibacterium prausnitzii (butyrate producers)
- **ARG mechanisms**: Antibiotic efflux accounted for 53.68% of rSGB-sourced ARG resistance; antibiotic inactivation 21.21%; target alteration 14.49%
- **Top resistance drug types**: Nucleoside antibiotics (17.3%), cephalosporins (16.77%), macrolides (11.64%), phenicol (8.06%), fluoroquinolones (5.97%)
- **Plasmid ARGs**: 23 plasmid-sourced ARGs (39% in E. coli); mainly acrB, msbA, AcrF, E. coli acrR; conferring resistance to 18 antibiotics by 5 mechanisms
- **Validation**: Results confirmed across PRJEB7774 and PRJEB12449 cohorts with 60.9% and 73.9% consistency
