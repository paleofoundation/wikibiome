# Safety and Efficacy of Phage Therapy in Difficult-to-Treat Infections: A Systematic Review

## Summary

Systematic review of clinical data (Jan 2000 - Aug 2021) on phage therapy safety and efficacy for difficult-to-treat bacterial infections. Found 59 eligible studies from 10,785 titles, covering pneumonia, urology, musculoskeletal infections, cardiology/cardiovascular surgery, and dermatology.

## Key Findings

### Cardiology and Cardiovascular Surgery
- Three reports describing phage therapy for difficult-to-treat infections of left ventricular assist device (LVAD, n=2) and aortic graft (n=1)
- Causative pathogens: *P. aeruginosa*, *S. aureus*
- Customised phage cocktails administered via various routes
- Clinical improvement observed in all patients; bacterial eradication not always achieved

### Overall Safety Profile
- Phage therapy well tolerated across all clinical settings
- No adverse events in most cases
- One case of brief hypotension (115 min) after first IV phage administration
- Phage resistance development noted in some cases with relapse after initial improvement
- Neutralising antiphage antibodies detected in some patients

### Efficacy by Indication
- **Pneumonia**: 16 patients; clinical improvement in most; one death from *B. dolosa* sepsis
- **Urology**: 7 reports; clinical improvement and bacterial eradication in most UTI patients
- **Musculoskeletal**: 13 diabetic foot ulcer patients; wound closure in all cases
- **Dermatology**: 224 patients with wound infections; most received topical phage therapy

### Study Quality Limitations
- No randomised controlled trials found
- Most evidence from case reports and case series
- Heterogeneity in phage preparations, doses, and administration routes
- Short follow-up periods in many studies
