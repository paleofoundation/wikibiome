# Key Claims

- **EDC concentrations in breast milk** were not directly associated with maternal [[postpartum-depression]]
- **Phthalate levels** (MEHP, MiNP) in breast milk significantly correlated with infant inattention and hyperactivity
- **PFAS levels** (PFNA, PFHxS) correlated significantly with motor development and total CBCL scores
- **Lactational exposure to EDCs** during the postpartum period can exert negative effects on infant neurodevelopment
- Rates of clinically abnormal infant development ranged from 7.7 to 38.5%

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Korean mother-infant pair cohort study (2018-2022); 221 Korean mothers, 29 EDCs analyzed; EPDS for PPD; developmental scales for infants aged 36-42 months
- **Key implication**: Environmental chemical exposure during lactation affects infant development independent of PPD status
