# Key Claims

- **140 metabolites of gut microbiome origin** identified among 1362 annotated compounds in serum
- **Carbocyclic acids and branched-chain amino acid clusters** at GW 10-13 and unsaturated fatty acids at GW 16-19 positively associated with GDM risk
- **Prediction model combining metabolites with conventional risk factors** significantly outperformed conventional model alone (AUC: 0.884 vs 0.691)
- **Dysbiosis in microbiome-derived metabolites present early in pregnancy** among individuals progressing to GDM

# Methods & Findings

Prospective case-control discovery and validation study:

- PETALS cohort (91 GDM, 180 non-GDM) as discovery, two validation sets
- Untargeted serum metabolomics at GW 10-13 and 16-19
- GC-MS/TOF-MS, LC/quadrupole TOF-MS, and HILIC LC/quadrupole TOF-MS platforms

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Early pregnancy zinc levels may influence microbiome-derived metabolite production relevant to GDM risk
- **[[iron]]**: Iron supplementation timing during pregnancy may alter microbiome metabolite profiles associated with GDM
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent metabolite detoxification may protect against harmful microbiome-derived compounds
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Early pregnancy [[gut-metal-microbiome]] state may be the earliest biomarker for GDM risk
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Metabolite [[dysbiosis]] precedes clinical GDM, enabling early intervention window
