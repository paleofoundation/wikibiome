# Key Claims

- **T2DM is a systemic inflammatory disease** characterized by dysfunctional pancreatic beta-cells and/or peripheral insulin resistance
- **Dietary lipids are significant metabolic modulators** in T2DM and related complications
- **Altered gut microbiota plays an important role** in metabolic health via lipid-microbiota interactions
- **Lipidomics as novel biomarker approach** detected with holistic analytical techniques

# Methods & Findings

Review of dietary lipids and lipidomics in gut-brain axis in T2DM:

- Lipid metabolism and gut microbiota are interconnected through gut-brain axis modulation
- Better understanding of lipid-microbiota interactions will help develop new prevention and treatment strategies
- Nutritional strategies considering lipid-microbiota-gut-brain interactions are needed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc-dependent lipases and phospholipases mediate lipid-microbiome interactions described in this review
- **[[iron]]**: Iron catalyzes lipid peroxidation in the gut, altering lipid profiles and microbiome composition
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent glutathione peroxidase protects polyunsaturated fatty acids from oxidative damage
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Metal availability modulates microbial lipid metabolism and bile acid transformation
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Lipid-driven [[dysbiosis]] may be exacerbated by metal imbalances in the gut environment
