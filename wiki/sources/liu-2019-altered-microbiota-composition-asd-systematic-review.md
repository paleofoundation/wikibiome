# Overview

Systematic review of **16 studies (381 ASD patients, 283 healthy controls)** comparing gut microbiota composition between ASD patients and HCs using culture-independent techniques. Includes functional analysis via PICRUSt. Quality assessed as medium to high via Newcastle-Ottawa Scale.

# Key Findings

## Consistently Altered Taxa

- **Beta-diversity** consistently different in ASD vs HCs
- ***Bifidobacterium*, *Blautia*, *Dialister*, *Prevotella*, *Veillonella*, and *Turicibacter*** consistently **decreased** in ASD
- ***Lactobacillus*, *Bacteroides*, *Desulfovibrio*, and *Clostridium*** **increased** in ASD relative to HCs

## Inconsistent Findings

- Results varied across studies due to differences in enrollment criteria, age ranges, geographic location, and sequencing methods
- Some studies reported no significant alpha-diversity differences
- Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio was inconsistently altered (higher in some Chinese cohorts)

## Functional Predictions

- PICRUSt analysis of 4 studies with available raw data showed altered KEGG pathways
- Differences in functional potential between ASD and HC microbiota

## Study Quality Considerations

- 16 studies from 2005-2018 across USA, Italy, Slovakia, Japan, Australia, China
- Only 6 studies reported GI symptoms; many did not control for diet
- Methodological heterogeneity (different 16S regions, databases, platforms) limits cross-study comparison
