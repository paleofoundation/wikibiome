# Network of Interactions Between Gut Microbiome, Host Biomarkers, and Urine Metabolome in Carotid Atherosclerosis

## Summary

Integrative trans-omics study of 138 treatment-naive Chinese individuals (aged 32-76) combining shotgun fecal metagenomics, 124 blood clinical biomarkers, and urine metabolomics to delineate interactions among biological layers in carotid atherosclerosis. Found that the microbiome alone was a poor biomarker, but integration of all three omics layers revealed novel cross-system interactions.

## Key Findings

### Study Design
- 138 Chinese individuals from Beijing (76 males, 57 females, aged 32-76)
- Recruited during carotid atherosclerosis screening at Chinese PLA General Hospital
- Treatment-naive subjects; multi-omic profiling: shotgun metagenomics, blood biomarkers, urine metabolomics
- Community modeling via non-directional networks using the algorithm by Gerven and Neuman (2021)

### Microbiome and Clinical Parameters
- GGT, serum ferritin, ALT, monocytes, ApoB2 most strongly associated with MGS composition (PERMANOVA)
- Triglycerides (r^2 = 0.020), ALT (r^2 = 0.020), and triglyceride (r^2 = 0.018) had largest effect sizes
- Clinical covariates correlated significantly with diversity and total gene counts
- *Eubacterium*, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*, and *Ruminococcus* associated with healthy liver function
- *Clostridium bolteae*, *Tyzzerella nexilis*, *Ruminococcusgnarvus*, *Blautiahansenji*, and *Atopobiumparvulum* associated with liver disease biomarkers

### Microbiome and CVD Phenotypes
- Higher abundance of *Tyzzerellanexilis* (MGS4:r = 0.30) and *Blautiahansenji* (MGS0607:r = 0.30) positively correlated with GGT
- *Atopobiumparvulum* (MGS0402, r = 0.39) positively correlated with triglyceride levels
- Microbiota poor biomarker for carotid atherosclerosis in functional attributes alone
- Random forest models using MGS relative abundances predicted triglycerides (r^2 = 0.35), LFE (r = 0.28), fluorescence indices (r = 0.21)

### Network Modeling
- Integration of three omics datasets yielded two core clusters: one large gut microbe-urine metabolite close-knit cluster and one triangular microbe-blood-urine cluster
- Demonstrated close inter-system crosstalk especially between gut microbiome and urine metabolome
- Distinct clinical phenotypes manifested in both microbiome and metabolome layers
- Inter-domain connectivity takes the form of high-dimensional networks

### Urine Metabolomics
- 10,890 positively charged and 9,416 negatively charged metabolites detected; clustered into 1,042 modules
- Urine metabolites demonstrated opposing correlation relationships with two sets of blood biomarkers
- Phenols, steroids, and food-derived metabolic intermediates correlated negatively with liver dysfunction markers

## Relevance to the Wiki

Demonstrates the power of integrative multi-omics approaches for understanding [[cardiovascular-disease]] pathogenesis, showing that gut microbiome, blood biomarkers, and urine metabolome form interconnected networks in carotid [[atherosclerosis]]. The finding that the microbiome alone is insufficient as a biomarker but gains power through integration underscores the need for multi-omic strategies.
