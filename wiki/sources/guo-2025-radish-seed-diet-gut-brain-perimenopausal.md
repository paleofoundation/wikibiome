# Guo 2025 — Seed-Rich Vegetable Diet Effects on Gut Microbiota and Brain Activity in Perimenopausal Women

## Key Findings

- **12-week Raphanus sativus L. (RSL, radish seed) enriched diet** (5 g/day mean intake) improved **MoCA visuospatial/executive function** subitem and total score trends in 10 perimenopausal women ([[guo-2025-radish-seed-diet-gut-brain-perimenopausal]], single-arm pilot, n=10).
- **Elevated ALFF** (amplitude of low-frequency fluctuation) post-intervention in left middle occipital gyrus, left precentral gyrus, and left middle cingulum gyrus — brain regions associated with cognitive processing ([[guo-2025-radish-seed-diet-gut-brain-perimenopausal]]).
- **Synergistetes and Verrucomicrobia** phyla abundance positively correlated with ALFF values in left middle occipital, left precentral, and left middle cingulum gyri ([[guo-2025-radish-seed-diet-gut-brain-perimenopausal]]).
- RSL contains **sulforaphane** and other bioactive isothiocyanates with neuroprotective and gut microbiota-modulating properties. RSL has 2.3-fold higher glucosinolate precursors than broccoli ([[guo-2025-radish-seed-diet-gut-brain-perimenopausal]]).
- First study to examine dietary intervention effects on gut microbiota-brain interactions in perimenopausal women ([[guo-2025-radish-seed-diet-gut-brain-perimenopausal]]).

## Methods

Single-arm longitudinal pilot. 24 enrolled, 10 completed. Perimenopausal women (age 49.3±4.1, Guizhou, China). 12-week RSL-rich plant-based diet. Pre/post fecal 16S rRNA sequencing, rs-fMRI (ALFF), MoCA, gastrointestinal symptom assessment. Correlations between microbiota composition and brain ALFF values.
