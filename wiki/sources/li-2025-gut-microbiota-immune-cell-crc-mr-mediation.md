# Gut Microbiota, Immune Cell, Colorectal Cancer Association Mediators: A Mendelian Randomization Study

## Key Claims

- Nine gut microbial taxa showed causal associations with CRC: *Coriobacteriaceae* (OR 0.84), *Sutterellaceae* (OR 0.88), *Eggerthella* (OR 0.91), *Coriobacteriales* (OR 0.84), *Collinsella aerofaciens* (OR 0.85), and *Ruminococcus bromii* (OR 0.91) were protective, while *Lactobacillales* (OR 1.11), *Veillonella* (OR 1.08), and *Bifidobacterium bifidum* (OR 1.05) increased CRC risk
- Mediation analysis revealed that *Collinsella aerofaciens* protects against CRC partly by reducing CD127 on CD28+ CD45RA- CD8br cells (11.30% of effect) and increasing HLA DR on CD33- HLA DR+ cells (6.52% of effect)
- *Ruminococcus bromii*, although protective against CRC overall, was associated with increased IgD- CD38dim %lymphocyte levels, which itself is a CRC risk factor -- demonstrating that immune mediation can involve paradoxical pathways
- 35 types of immune cells had causal relationships with CRC (17 positive, 18 negative), but reverse MR showed CRC did not significantly alter gut microbiota, suggesting unidirectional causality
- The study provides the first MR-based evidence specifically linking *C. aerofaciens* and *R. bromii* to CRC through immune cell mediation

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Bidirectional two-sample MR with two-step mediation analysis using immune cells as mediators
- **Data sources**: Dutch GWAS (207 microbial taxa, 205 functional pathways); IEU OpenGWAS (731 immune cell phenotypes from 3,757 Europeans); Integrative Epidemiology Unit (470,002 participants, 6,581 CRC cases)
- **Methods**: IVW primary analysis; weighted median, simple model, weighted mode, MR-Egger as supplementary; Cochrane Q for heterogeneity; MR-Egger intercept for pleiotropy
- **Immune phenotypes**: Absolute cell counts, relative cell counts, median fluorescence intensity, morphological parameters across B cells, T cells, monocytes, myeloid cells, TBNK panels
