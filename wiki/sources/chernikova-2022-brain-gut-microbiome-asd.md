# Key Claims

- **Gastrointestinal dysfunction is prevalent** in ASD (constipation, diarrhea, reflux, abdominal pain)
- **Dysbiotic microbiota modulates brain function** and social behavior through multiple mechanistic pathways
- **Limited understanding of mechanisms** underlying microbiota-brain effects in ASD—gap between preclinical models and clinical understanding
- **Emerging neuroimaging evidence** linking dysbiosis to altered brain activation patterns and connectivity

# Methods & Findings

Narrative review synthesizing mechanisms by which gut microbiota influence ASD pathogenesis:

**Key mechanistic pathways:**
1. **Neurochemical pathway**: Dysbiotic taxa altered metabolite production (SCFA, secondary bile acids, tryptophan metabolites) → reduced epigenetic remodeling and neurotransmitter synthesis
2. **Immune/inflammatory pathway**: Dysbiotic LPS translocation → TLR4-mediated systemic and CNS inflammation → altered brain function
3. **Vagal afferent pathway**: Dysbiotic-associated metabolites alter signaling via vagus nerve → CNS integration → behavioral changes
4. **Barrier pathway**: Dysbiotic dysregulation of tight-junction proteins → increased intestinal permeability → bacterial translocation → systemic immune activation

**Brain regions implicated:**
- Insula (interoceptive processing, social cognition)
- Amygdala (emotional processing, social behavior)
- Prefrontal cortex (executive function, behavioral control)
- Dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (error monitoring, behavioral adaptation)

**Preclinical evidence:**
- Germ-free mice display autism-like behavioral phenotypes
- Dysbiotic microbiota transfer induces behavioral changes in recipients
- Specific bacteria/metabolites produce behavioral effects
- Reversibility with microbiota restoration demonstrated in some models

**Clinical evidence limitations:**
- Few human neuroimaging studies of microbiota-brain relationships in ASD
- Cross-sectional designs predominate
- Causality inference limited
- Mechanisms largely inferred from preclinical models
