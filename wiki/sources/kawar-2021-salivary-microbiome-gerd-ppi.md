# Salivary microbiome with gastroesophageal reflux disease and treatment

## Key Claims

- Untreated [[gerd]] patients showed multiple differences in [[salivary microbiome]] compared to healthy controls, with 17 species and subgenera at different levels (FDR < 0.1)
- Four taxa were found at lower levels in GERD patients not treated by PPI: *[[Prevotella melaninogenica]]*, *[[Prevotella pallens]]*, *[[Solobacterium moorei]]*, and *[[leptotrichia]]*
- GERD patients chronically using PPI showed salivary microbiome profiles similar to negative controls, suggesting PPI restores oral microbial balance
- The presence of GERD is associated with acidic saliva (pH 4.9) versus near-neutral pH of 6.5 in healthy subjects, driving microbial shifts
- No significant alpha diversity differences were observed between groups, but beta diversity analysis (PCoA with Bray-Curtis) showed subtle clustering differences
- Four periodontal disease-related taxa (*Porphyromonas gingivalis*, *Filifactor alocis*, *Fretibacterium fastidiosum*, *Lachnospiraceae* HMT_500) trended lower in untreated GERD, paradoxically suggesting lower periodontal disease risk

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional observational study
- **Population**: 128 subjects total (92 negative controls, 16 GERD no PPI, 20 GERD+PPI) from University of Illinois at Chicago dental clinics
- **Sequencing**: 16S rDNA HOMD-based taxonomic assignments from stimulated saliva; 4,964,720 reads analyzed via DADA2
- **Matching**: GERD subjects without PPI matched to controls for age, periodontal status, edentulism; all patients edentulous
- **Multivariate analysis**: After adjusting for gender and tobacco use, *Prevotella melaninogenica* (p=0.031), *Prevotella pallens* (p=0.008), *Leptotrichia* (p=0.023), and *Solobacterium moorei* (p=0.047) remained significantly different
- **PPI effect**: GERD+PPI group microbiome was superimposable with negative controls on PCoA, indicating PPI normalizes oral microbiome by restoring pH
