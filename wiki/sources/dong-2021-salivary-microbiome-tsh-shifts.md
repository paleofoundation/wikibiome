# Association Between Serum Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone Levels and Salivary Microbiome Shifts

## Key Claims

- The salivary microbiome in individuals with higher [[TSH]] levels showed significantly higher taxa diversity compared to normal TSH groups
- [[Granulicatella]] was found to possess significantly higher abundance in the Abnormal Group compared to Normal Group (Adonis, p = 0.032)
- Principal coordinates analysis and partial least squares discriminant analysis showed distinct clustering between Abnormal (TSH > 4.2 mIU/L) and Normal Groups
- HOMA-IR (insulin resistance) was significantly elevated in the Abnormal Group (p < 0.05), demonstrating the presence of [[insulin resistance]] with abnormally higher serum TSH levels
- Fasting serum insulin, hemoglobin A1 were elevated in the Abnormal Group, showing the presence of insulin resistance in individuals with higher serum TSH
- Distance-based redundancy analysis revealed the association of this distinctive difference with salivary microbiome composition

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional case-control study
- **Population**: 114 participants from Southeast China; Abnormal Group (n=20, TSH >4.2 mIU/L) and Normal Group (n=20, TSH 0.6-4.2 mIU/L) with case-control matching
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA V3-V4 region, Illumina MiSeq PE300
- **Alpha diversity**: Shannon index significantly higher in Abnormal Group (p < 0.05); Simpson index showed no significant difference
- **Discriminatory taxa**: Lactobacillales (order), Porphyromonadaceae, Carnobacteriaceae, Spirochaetaceae (family), Granulicatella, Treponema, Streptobacillus (genus) -- more prevalent in Abnormal; Rhodococcus and Nocardiaceae more abundant in Normal
- **Functional profiling**: PICRUSt2 analysis; carbohydrate metabolism was the highest functional proportion; no significant difference after multiple testing correction
