# Key Claims

- Circulating cell-free DNA (circDNA) levels were significantly higher in CVD patients (n=80) than healthy controls (n=40), with AUROC values >0.9 for discriminating IHD, CHD, and overall CVD from controls
- The ratio of 16S rRNA to beta-globin gene copy numbers was significantly elevated in CVD plasma, indicating a higher bacterial load in the circulation
- [[Shotgun metagenomics]] of plasma circDNA revealed CVD patients had higher bacterial DNA concentration relative to human DNA compared to controls (68% vs 43% of reads mapped to bacteria)
- At the phylum level, [[Actinobacteria]] dominated in CVD patients while [[Proteobacteria]] dominated in healthy controls, a reversal of the normal pattern
- The circulating [[virome]] in CVD patients was enriched with bacteriophages (Propionibacterium phages, Pseudomonas phages, Rhizobium phages), whereas healthy individuals showed more eukaryotic viruses (Lymphocystis virus, Torque Teno viruses)
- A lesser fraction of viral DNA was observed in CVD patients (2.2%) compared to healthy individuals (6.7%)
- circDNA, 16S rRNA copy number, and beta-globin copy number are statistically dependent on the cardiovascular status of patients

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Case-control study with deep shotgun sequencing of plasma circDNA
- **Population**: 80 CVD patients (24 VHD, 30 IHD, 26 CHD) and 40 healthy blood donors from Government Rajaji Hospital, Madurai, India (July 2010 - March 2011)
- **Key methods**: PicoGreen circDNA quantification; qPCR for 16S rRNA and beta-globin genes; Ion Torrent PGM barcoded shotgun sequencing; MG-RAST annotation; CloVR metagenomics pipeline
- **circDNA concentrations**: CVD patients 1923.64 ng/ml vs healthy 37.30 ng/ml (p<0.0001)
- **Diagnostic performance**: AUROC for circDNA in CHD was 0.989 (96% sensitivity, 100% specificity); for 16S rRNA gene in IHD was 0.925
- **Taxonomic findings**: Significant increase in family Propionibacteriaceae and class alpha-proteobacteria in CVD; Actinobacteria:Proteobacteria ratio increased in CVD (p=0.035)
- **Virome**: Propionibacterium phages predominant in CVD; eukaryotic viruses more abundant in controls
