# Clinical and Metabolic Response to Vitamin D Plus Probiotic in Schizophrenia Patients

## Key Claims

- In 60 chronic [[schizophrenia]] patients randomized to vitamin D3 (50,000 IU/2 weeks) + [[probiotics]] (8x10^9 CFU/day of L. acidophilus, B. bifidum, L. reuteri, L. fermentum) vs placebo for 12 weeks
- Vitamin D + probiotic significantly improved total PANSS scores (-7.4 +/- 8.7 vs -1.9 +/- 7.5, P = 0.01) and general PANSS (-3.1 +/- 4.7 vs +0.3 +/- 3.9, P = 0.004)
- Significantly increased total antioxidant capacity (+51.1 +/- 129.7 vs -20.7 +/- 53.3 mmol/L, P = 0.007) and decreased malondialdehyde (oxidative stress marker, P = 0.01)
- Significantly reduced hs-CRP levels (-2.3 +/- 3.0 vs -0.3 +/- 0.8 mg/L, P = 0.001)
- Significantly reduced fasting plasma glucose (-7.0 +/- 9.9 vs -0.2 +/- 9.9 mg/dL, P = 0.01), insulin concentrations (-2.7 +/- 2.3 vs +0.4 +/- 2.0 microIU/mL, P < 0.001), and HOMA-IR (-0.8 +/- 0.7 vs +0.1 +/- 0.7, P < 0.001)
- Reduced triglycerides (-7.8 +/- 25.2 vs +10.1 +/- 30.8 mg/dL, P = 0.01) and total cholesterol (-4.9 +/- 15.0 vs +5.9 +/- 19.5 mg/dL, P = 0.04)
- 85% of psychiatric subjects had suboptimal vitamin D status; lowest levels found in schizophrenia patients

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial
- **Sample**: 60 chronic schizophrenia patients (30 treatment, 30 placebo), Kashan, Iran
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Key finding**: Combined vitamin D + probiotic produced both psychiatric and metabolic benefits
