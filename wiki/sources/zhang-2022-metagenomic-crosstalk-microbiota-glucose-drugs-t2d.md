# Key Claims

- **Gut microbiota modulates drug efficacy** -- altered gut microbial composition or functional capacity affects glucose-lowering drug responses
- **Both acarbose and vildagliptin** achieved significant HbA1c reductions (~1% reduction) after 6 months
- **Pre-treatment gut microbiota and therapeutic responses** were related -- baseline microbiome predicts drug efficacy
- **Randomised two-arm trial** with 100 treatment-naive T2D patients

# Methods & Findings

6-month RCT: acarbose (n=50) vs vildagliptin (n=50), 92 analyzed:

- Blood, faecal samples, and visceral fat CT data collected before and after treatment
- Metagenomic datasets from previous T2D acarbose/glipizide cohort also analyzed
- Treatment-related changes in clinical variables, gut microbiota, and associations identified

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework
