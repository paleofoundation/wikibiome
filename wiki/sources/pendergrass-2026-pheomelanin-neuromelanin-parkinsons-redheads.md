# Pheomelanin, Eumelanin, and Neuromelanin: A Metal-Linked Hypothesis for Parkinson's Risk in Redheads

## Key Claims

- Proposes a metal-linked mechanism explaining the epidemiologically observed increased Parkinson's disease (PD) risk in redheads (individuals carrying MC1R variants)
- Pheomelanin (the red/yellow pigment dominant in redheads) and eumelanin (the brown/black pigment) differ fundamentally in their metal-binding capacities, particularly for iron and copper
- Eumelanin is a potent metal chelator that sequesters redox-active iron and copper, reducing free radical generation; pheomelanin has weaker metal-binding capacity and may itself generate reactive oxygen species upon UV or metal exposure
- Neuromelanin in the substantia nigra (the brain region most affected in PD) is a mixed polymer of eumelanin and pheomelanin components; its ratio and metal-binding properties may vary by MC1R genotype
- In redheads, a higher pheomelanin-to-eumelanin ratio in neuromelanin could result in reduced iron sequestration in the substantia nigra, leading to greater free iron availability, enhanced Fenton chemistry, and increased dopaminergic neuron vulnerability
- The hypothesis unifies several established PD risk factors: MC1R variants, iron accumulation in substantia nigra, oxidative stress, and ferroptosis-mediated neuronal death
- Dietary and environmental metal exposure may compound the inherent vulnerability in individuals with MC1R-driven pheomelanin dominance

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Hypothesis-driven synthesis integrating melanin biochemistry, metal chemistry, PD neuropathology, and genetic epidemiology
- **Melanin metal-binding biochemistry**: Eumelanin contains catechol and carboxyl groups enabling strong chelation of Fe3+, Cu2+, Zn2+, and other metals; pheomelanin contains benzothiazine units with sulfur groups that bind metals less effectively and can catalyze pro-oxidant reactions
- **MC1R and melanin ratios**: Loss-of-function MC1R variants shift melanogenesis toward pheomelanin production in both skin and brain tissue; MC1R is expressed in dopaminergic neurons of the substantia nigra
- **Epidemiological evidence**: Multiple studies have identified red hair/fair skin phenotypes and MC1R variants as independent risk factors for PD, with risk increases of approximately 50-100% in some cohorts
- **Iron-neuromelanin interactions**: Neuromelanin normally accumulates iron with age in the substantia nigra; when neuromelanin iron-binding capacity is exceeded or reduced, free iron catalyzes lipid peroxidation and ferroptosis
- **Proposed mechanism**: MC1R variants -> increased pheomelanin fraction in neuromelanin -> reduced iron chelation capacity -> greater labile iron pool -> enhanced Fenton reactions -> lipid peroxidation -> dopaminergic neuron death via ferroptosis
- **Testable predictions**: Post-mortem analysis of neuromelanin composition stratified by MC1R genotype should show differences in pheomelanin:eumelanin ratio; in vitro iron-binding assays with synthetic neuromelanin variants should confirm differential chelation capacity
