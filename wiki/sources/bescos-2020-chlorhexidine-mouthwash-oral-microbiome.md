# Key Findings

- **Chlorhexidine (CHX) mouthwash for 7 days** significantly altered oral microbiome: increased Firmicutes and Proteobacteria, decreased Bacteroidetes [[bescos-2020-chlorhexidine-mouthwash-oral-microbiome]], RCT, n=36.
- **Reduced bacterial diversity** (Shannon index decreased) — CHX is a broad-spectrum antiseptic that creates dysbiosis rather than targeted antimicrobial action [[bescos-2020-chlorhexidine-mouthwash-oral-microbiome]].
- **Disrupted oral nitrite/nitrate metabolism**: CHX reduced nitrate-reducing bacteria, impairing oral nitric oxide production with potential cardiovascular implications [[bescos-2020-chlorhexidine-mouthwash-oral-microbiome]].
- Specific genera changes: **Neisseria** increased, **Streptococcus** and **Granulicatella** reduced.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- [[dysbiosis]] — CHX as iatrogenic dysbiosis driver in the oral compartment.
- Oral-systemic connection: CHX-disrupted oral microbiome may affect cardiovascular risk via impaired nitrate metabolism.
- Relevant to the breast cancer-oral microbiome axis — mouthwash-induced oral dysbiosis could alter the oral-breast translocation pathway.
