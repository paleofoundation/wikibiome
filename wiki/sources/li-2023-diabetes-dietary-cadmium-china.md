## Key Findings

- **Blood cadmium in China (1.54 µg/L) is 3-4x higher than in developed European countries** (Italy 0.53 µg/L, Germany 0.38 µg/L). Highest levels in Henan (4.14), Shanxi (2.84), and Jiangxi (2.82 µg/L).
- **Dietary pathway dominates Cd exposure** (59.78%), followed by smoking (37.84%). Grains (1.55 µg/day) and meats (1.10 µg/day) are the largest dietary contributors.
- **Cd is a major driver of diabetes burden**: eliminating cadmium exposure would reduce T2D incidence by ~65% (PAF). Dietary Cd alone contributed 337.86×10^6 DALYs to T2D in 2017.
- **Dose-response**: Filippini 2022 meta-analysis showed linear positive B-Cd/T2D relationship with RR of 1.47 (1.5-2.0 µg/L), 2.43 (2.0-2.5 µg/L), and 4.00 (>2.5 µg/L).
- **Mechanism**: Cd disrupts pancreatic β-cell lipid metabolism, induces pancreatic inflammation, and alters glucose homeostasis. Biological half-life of Cd is 10-30 years; minimal elimination.

## Methods

Top-down burden-of-disease calculation. Systematic literature search (PubMed, Web of Science, CNKI, Wanfang, CBMdisc) 2001-2023 yielded 46 eligible studies (n=56,191). PAF calculated from B-Cd population distribution × RR. DALYs from GBD 2019. Contribution rates calculated from MEP 2013 Chinese Population Exposure Factors Handbook.

## Relevance to Cureva

- Supports elevated-Cd signature in T2D patient populations, particularly where grain/rice-heavy diets dominate.
- Cross-condition: cadmium burden also associated with CVD (Liu 2022), nasopharyngeal cancer (Peng 2015), hypertension, obesity.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Cross-sectional design limits causal inference; reverse causation (T2D altering blood Cd) cannot be excluded.
- RR values from European-population meta-analysis may not translate directly to Chinese populations with higher baseline exposure.

## Open Questions

- Dose-response curve specific to Asian populations with different dietary Cd profiles.
- Contribution of specific grains (rice vs wheat) given regional variation in B-Cd.
- Whether dietary Cd reduction interventions change T2D incidence.
