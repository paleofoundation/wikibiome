# Key Claims

- **Microbial genes exceed human coding potential by ~500-fold**, essential for key metabolic processes including SCFA production, amino acid biosynthesis, and neurotransmitter production
- **16S rRNA and shotgun metagenomics** are the two main approaches for microbiome analysis, each with distinct strengths
- **Stool consistency** has largest effect size on gut microbiota composition; medication, diet, and health variables also contribute
- **Future therapeutic strategies** include personalized nutrition, targeted delivery of propionate, FMT, and genetically modified bacteria

# Methods & Findings

Review of current human evidence and future possibilities for gut microbiota-based diabetes treatment:

- Evidence supports causal role for gut microbiota in glycaemic control
- Metformin effects partly mediated through microbiome changes
- Integration of multi-omic data needed for personalized treatments

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Personalized nutrition strategies must account for zinc status, as zinc modulates both microbiome composition and insulin signaling

- **[[immune-balance]]**: Microbiome manipulation for diabetes prevention must consider metal-dependent immune pathways
