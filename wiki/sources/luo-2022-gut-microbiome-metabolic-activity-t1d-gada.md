# Key Claims

- **T1D progression affected by GADA titers** that persist for many years
- **Gut microbiome differs in diversity, composition, and function** between GADA+ and GADA- T1D patients
- **Alistipes and Ruminococcus abundance significantly increased** in GADA+ vs GADA- patients
- **54 significantly altered serum metabolites** associated with tryptophan metabolism, phenylalanine, and tyrosine biosynthesis in GADA+ vs GADA-
- **Alistipes abundance positively correlated** with altered metabolites involved in tryptophan metabolism

# Methods & Findings

Cross-sectional study of 49 GADA+ and 52 GADA- T1D patients:

- 16S rRNA gene sequencing and GC/MS for gut microbiome and serum metabolites
- Spearman's rank correlation assessed microbiome-metabolite associations
- Tryptophan and tyrosine biosynthesis pathways significantly different between groups

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Tryptophan metabolism to indole derivatives requires zinc-dependent tryptophanase -- GADA status may reflect zinc-microbiome axis
- **[[iron]]**: Alistipes species are iron-requiring anaerobes -- their enrichment in GADA+ patients may reflect iron dysregulation
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent enzymes modulate tryptophan-kynurenine pathway relevant to autoimmune inflammation
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: GADA persistence reflects ongoing autoimmune activity shaped by metal-microbiome interactions
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: GADA-stratified [[dysbiosis]] reveals distinct autoimmune-microbiome-metal phenotypes within T1D
