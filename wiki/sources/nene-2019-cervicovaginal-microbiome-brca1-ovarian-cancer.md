# Cervicovaginal Microbiome, BRCA1 Mutation Status, and Ovarian Cancer Risk

## Key Claims

- Women with ovarian cancer younger than 50 years had significantly higher prevalence of community type O (non-Lactobacillus-dominated) microbiota (OR 2.80, p=0.020)
- BRCA1 mutation carriers younger than 50 also had more community type O microbiota (OR 2.79, p=0.012)
- The younger the participants, the stronger the association between community type O and ovarian cancer or BRCA1 status
- Cervicovaginal microbiome composition could serve as a biomarker for ovarian cancer risk

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Case-control study in Czech Republic, Germany, Italy, Norway, UK
- **Ovarian cancer set**: 176 OC, 115 healthy controls, 69 benign controls (n=360)
- **BRCA set**: 109 BRCA1 carriers, 97 controls, 14 benign controls (n=220)
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA sequencing of cervicovaginal samples; community types L (>50% Lactobacillus) and O (<50%)
