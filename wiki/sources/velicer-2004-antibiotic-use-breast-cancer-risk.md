# Key Findings

- **Antibiotic use associated with increased breast cancer risk**: Women with the highest cumulative antibiotic exposure had elevated breast cancer risk compared to non-users [[velicer-2004-antibiotic-use-breast-cancer-risk]], case-control, n=10,219.
- **Tetracycline** showed statistically significant elevated risk, with dose-response relationship for cumulative days of use [[velicer-2004-antibiotic-use-breast-cancer-risk]].
- Proposed mechanism: antibiotics disrupt the gut [[estrobolome]], altering estrogen metabolism — reduced bacterial [[beta-glucuronidase]] activity from dysbiosis may paradoxically increase circulating estrogens by disrupting normal enterohepatic circulation patterns, or alternatively, other dysbiosis-mediated pathways (inflammation, immune dysregulation) may mediate the association.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

## Relevance to Cureva

- **STOP signal**: Prolonged antibiotic courses may increase breast cancer risk via estrobolome disruption. Practitioners should weigh this risk when prescribing extended antibiotic therapy, particularly tetracyclines.
