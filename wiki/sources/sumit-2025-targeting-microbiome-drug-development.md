# Key Claims

- The human microbiome has emerged as a critical player in health and disease, with disruptions linked to gastrointestinal disorders, autoimmune diseases, metabolic syndrome, and neurological conditions
- Microbiome-based drug development and therapeutic strategies are rapidly evolving, with potential for targeted interventions
- Key mechanisms include immune system regulation, metabolism and nutrient absorption, and pathogen defense through colonization resistance
- Challenges include translating microbiome science into clinical practice, regulatory frameworks for live biotherapeutics, and ensuring long-term safety
- The gut-brain axis represents an emerging area connecting microbiome modulation to neurological and mental health outcomes

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Review of microbiome-based drug development and therapeutic strategies
- **Key therapeutic approaches**: [[probiotics]], prebiotics, synbiotics, FMT, engineered bacteria, phage therapy
- **Immune regulation**: Gut microbiota shapes innate and adaptive immune responses; Tregs promoted by specific commensals
- **Colonization resistance**: Beneficial microbes outcompete pathogens for resources and space
- **Drug-microbiome interactions**: Bacteria can modify drugs, enhance or impair effects, promote toxicity
