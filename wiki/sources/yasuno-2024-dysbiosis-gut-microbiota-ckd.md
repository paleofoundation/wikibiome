## Key Findings

- **Stage-specific Lachnospiraceae decline**: Multiple butyrate-producing genera within [[lachnospiraceae]] — including [[blautia]], [[coprococcus]], [[anaerostipes]], and [[roseburia]] — show significant depletion starting at CKD stage 3b (Yasuno et al. 2024, cross-sectional, n=93).
- **Lachnospira as early biomarker**: [[lachnospira]] is the earliest genus to decline, with significant reduction detectable at stage 3a, suggesting it could serve as an early dysbiosis biomarker.
- **Persistent dysbiosis post-RRT**: Dysbiotic patterns do not resolve after renal replacement therapy (dialysis), indicating the microbial disruption becomes self-sustaining.
- **Enriched taxa**: [[parabacteroides]], [[clostridium]], [[ruminococcus]], and [[lactobacillus]] show relative enrichment in advanced CKD stages.

## Methods

Cross-sectional study of 93 subjects across CKD stages 1-5D (including dialysis patients). 16S rRNA gene sequencing of fecal samples. Alpha/beta diversity analysis with stage-stratified comparisons.

## Detailed Summary

The study mapped gut microbiota composition across the full CKD trajectory. Key structural findings:

- **Alpha diversity** decreased progressively with CKD stage, with the sharpest decline between stages 3a and 4.
- **Beta diversity** showed clear separation between early (stages 1-3a) and advanced (stages 4-5D) CKD.
- The **Lachnospiraceae family** was the most consistently affected taxon, with five member genera showing stage-dependent depletion. [[lachnospira]] declined earliest (stage 3a), while [[blautia]], [[coprococcus]], [[anaerostipes]], and [[roseburia]] declined from stage 3b.
- **Dialysis patients** (stage 5D) maintained the dysbiotic profile seen in stage 5 pre-dialysis, indicating that renal replacement therapy does not restore microbial ecology.
- [[lactobacillus]] enrichment in advanced CKD may reflect pH changes in the intestinal lumen from uremic solute accumulation.

## Relevance to Cureva

The stage-dependent Lachnospiraceae depletion pattern provides a potential monitoring framework for CKD progression. The persistence of dysbiosis post-RRT argues for active microbiome restoration rather than relying on renal function recovery alone. ## Contradictions / Tensions

- [[lactobacillus]] enrichment contradicts its general status as a beneficial genus — in CKD context it may reflect adaptation to altered luminal pH rather than probiotic benefit.

## Open Questions

- Is [[lachnospira]] depletion a driver or consequence of CKD progression at stage 3a?
- Can targeted Lachnospiraceae restoration at stage 3a prevent the cascade of further dysbiosis?
- What environmental factor (uremic toxins, pH, oxygen) is primarily responsible for Lachnospiraceae sensitivity?
