# Key Claims

- **Specific bacterial strains in gut microbiome of one-year-old children** identified as exclusive to subjects who later acquired T1D, celiac disease, hypothyroidism, Crohn's, JIA, or autism
- **1741 Swedish children** followed for 18 years; 96 developed one of five autoimmune diseases or autism
- **Certain class II HLA haplotypes** may serve as bacterial gatekeepers altering microbiome composition
- **Gut microbiome dysbiosis and inflammation during infancy** may be common precedent to all five autoimmune diseases and autism
- **HLA gatekeeping** may prevent gut colonization of beneficial bacteria in genetically at-risk individuals
