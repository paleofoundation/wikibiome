# Key Findings

- **Chronic nickel administration dose-dependently increased anxiety-like behavior** in both open-field and elevated plus maze tests ([[lamtai-2018-chronic-nickel-affective-cognitive-behavior-rats]], animal model, n=24/sex).
- **Depression-like symptoms** (forced swimming test) were significantly increased in Ni-treated rats.
- **Spatial learning and memory** were impaired at 1 mg/kg Ni in males (Morris Water Maze).
- **Oxidative stress markers**: catalase (CAT) and superoxide dismutase (SOD) were significantly decreased in hippocampus; nitric oxide (NO) and lipid peroxidation (LPO) were significantly increased.
- Ni enters the brain via BBB or olfactory pathway and accumulates in cerebral cortex, causing neuronal cytotoxicity.

# Methods

- Male and female Wistar rats (120 ± 20g), 4 groups per sex (n=6 each).
- Daily intraperitoneal injection for 8 weeks: saline control, NiCl₂ at 0.25, 0.5, or 1 mg/kg.
- Behavioral tests: open-field, elevated plus maze, forced swimming test, Morris Water Maze.
- Biochemical: hippocampal CAT, SOD, NO, LPO measured.

# Contradictions / Tensions

- Animal model evidence (IP injection) — route of exposure differs from dietary Ni in humans.
- Doses used (0.25–1 mg/kg/day IP) are much higher than typical dietary nickel exposure.

# Open Questions
