# Key Claims

- **Oral microbiome composition can discriminate ASD from neurotypical siblings** (AUC=0.66) with 108 differentiating species (q<0.005)
- **Relative abundance of differentiating species** highly correlated with cognitive impairment measured by Full-Scale IQ
- **ASD children with IQ<70** exhibit lower microbiome strain sharing with parents (p<10^-6)
- **Functional enrichment analysis** suggests contribution of enzymes from serotonin, GABA, and dopamine degradation pathways
- **Over 7000 whole-genome sequenced salivary samples** from 2025 US families analyzed

# Methods & Findings

**Large-scale cross-sectional study** leveraging whole-genome sequencing of salivary samples.

- 2025 US families with ASD children and neurotypical siblings
- 108 species differentiated ASD from neurotypical siblings
- Cognitive impairment severity correlated with microbial composition
- Serotonin, GABA, and dopamine degradation pathways enriched in ASD oral microbiome
- Restrictive eating and oral hygiene showed relatively minor effects on microbiome composition
