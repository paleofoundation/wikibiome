# Cheng 2025 — Neuroinflammation in PMS and PMDD (Review)

## Key Findings

- **Tryptophan–kynurenine shunt:** IDO upregulation during luteal inflammation diverts tryptophan from serotonin synthesis toward neurotoxic kynurenine metabolites (quinolinic acid) — mechanistic bridge between gut-origin inflammation and mood.
- **GABA-A receptor subunits** altered by TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-17 — cytokine modulation of ALLO-responsive GABAergic signaling.
- **BDNF elevated** in PMDD luteal phase as a compensatory response to neuroinflammation.
- Integrates systemic inflammation, microbiome-derived signaling, and CNS neurosteroid biology.

## Methods

Narrative review on neuroinflammatory contributions to PMS/PMDD pathophysiology and implications for treatment.
