# Bile Acids at the Cross-Roads of Gut Microbiome-Host Cardiometabolic Interactions

## Summary

Review focusing on bile acid-mediated signaling pathways through which the gut microbiome may influence cardiovascular and metabolic disease risk. Covers FXR and TGR5 nuclear/membrane-bound receptor ligands, microbial bile acid modification, and the putative impact on metabolic syndrome phenotypes from obesity to heart failure.

## Key Findings

### Bile Acid Receptors and Cardiometabolic Effects (Table 1)
- **FXR**: Activated by CDCA (most potent), CA, DCA; effects include decreased TAG, decreased insulin, increased RCT, decreased BP
- **TGR5**: Activated by LCA (most potent), TLCA, CA, DCA, CDCA; effects include decreased glucose, decreased insulin, increased NO, decreased monocyte infiltration, decreased foam cell formation, decreased atherosclerosis
- Synthetic FXR agonists (WAY-362450, GW4064, OCA, Fexaramine): Various effects on lipid and glucose metabolism
- FGF15/19 (FGF19 in humans) tightly regulated by FXR; critical intracellular sensor for bile acid and cholesterol homeostasis

### Microbial Bile Acid Modification
- **Bile salt hydrolase (BSH)**: Deconjugates bile acids; found in *Lactobacillus*, *Clostridium*, *Listeria*, and *Bifidobacteria*
- **Coprostanol production**: Microbial conversion of cholesterol to non-absorbable coprostanol; sex-dependent (young women being high converters); limited to *Eubacterium* (*E. coprostanoligenes*) and *Bacteroides* species
- BSH-expressing microbe *Lactobacillus reuteri* NCIMB 30242 demonstrated potential to reduce LDL-C, non-HDL-C, and apo-B100

### Bariatric Surgery and Bile Acids
- Post-bariatric surgery: increased *Proteobacteria*, decreased *Bacteroidetes*; altered serum bile acid profiles
- Vertical sleeve gastrectomy: increased GUDCA with decreased GCA, TCA, DCA
- Bile acid-FXR-FGF19 signaling at core of metabolic improvements after bariatric surgery
- T-beta-MCA (potent endogenous FXR antagonist) modulates bile acid enrichment in germ-free mice

### Vascular Disease and Heart Failure
- Hypertension estimated to be raised by 2-3x in CVD patients with concomitant metabolic disease
- FXR agonist CDCA shown to reduce blood pressure in spontaneously hypertensive rat model through iNOS expression
- FXR agonists PX20606 and OCA: potential to partially reduce portal hypertension
- Altered serum bile acid composition linked to atrial fibrillation in HF patients
- FXR expression downregulated in left ventricle of spontaneously hypertensive rats with end-stage heart failure

### TGRS and Immunomodulation
- OCA: Reduces non-HDL-C and modestly reduces circulating levels of atherogenic lipoproteins
- TGRS activation: antiatherosclerotic via NF-kB pathway inhibition, reduced macrophage recruitment
- 6-EMCA/INT-777: Increases GLP-1, decreases atherosclerosis via TAG/NEFA reduction and decreased foam cell formation
