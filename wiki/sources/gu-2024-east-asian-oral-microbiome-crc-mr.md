# Revealing the Association between East Asian Oral Microbiome and Colorectal Cancer through Mendelian Randomization and Multi-Omics Analysis

## Key Claims

- MR analysis of East Asian oral microbiome data identified 19 bacterial taxa significantly associated with CRC, with protective effects from RUG343 and *Streptococcus_umgs_2425* (OR = 0.817 and 0.797 respectively) and increased risk from HOT-345_umgs_976 and W5053_sp000467935_mgs_712 (OR = 1.210 and 1.183 respectively)
- Single-cell RNA sequencing analysis of CRC tumor vs. normal tissue revealed key pathways including JAK-STAT signaling and tyrosine metabolism as central mechanisms linking oral microbiome to CRC
- Drug prediction using DSigDB identified Menadione Sodium Bisulfite and Raloxifene as potential therapeutics targeting the oral microbiome-CRC axis genes (BCL2, AOX1, CBX4, PHACTR3)
- In tongue samples, *Campylobacter_A_umgs_3358* and *HOT-345_umgs_3064* showed significant negative associations with beneficial health outcomes (OR = 1.614 and 1.242 respectively)
- Gene function enrichment revealed BCL2 and AOX1 connections to JAK-STAT pathway and apoptosis, with CBX4 enriched in SUMO-related pathways involved in protein modification and signal transduction

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Two-sample MR integrating East Asian oral microbiome GWAS with CRC GWAS, followed by single-cell RNA sequencing and drug prediction
- **Data sources**: China National GeneBank (CNGBdb) for oral microbiome (2,948 samples: 2,017 tongue, 1,915 saliva); BioBank Japan (BBJ) for CRC outcomes (6,692 cases, 27,178 controls)
- **Methods**: IVW primary analysis with MR-Egger, weighted median, and weighted mode; Steiger directionality test; scRNA-seq analysis using Seurat; molecular docking via AutoDockTools
- **Novel approach**: First study combining East Asian oral microbiome MR with single-cell transcriptomics and computational drug prediction for CRC
