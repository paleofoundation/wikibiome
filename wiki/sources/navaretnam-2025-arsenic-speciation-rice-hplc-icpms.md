## Key Findings

- **AsIII dominates in rice**, followed by DMA then AsV — AsIII being the most bioactive and toxic species. This confirms that the *form* of arsenic in rice is the most biologically dangerous.
- **Certain rice varieties exceed EU regulatory limits for inorganic As**: MR 27, MR 29 (brown), MR 10, MR 14 (white).
- **100% of samples exceeded THQ=1** and the 10^-3 lifetime cancer risk threshold, indicating consistent risk across commercial rice in Malaysia.
- Method: optimized HPLC-ICP-MS with dynamic reaction cell (DRC) to mitigate 40Ar35Cl+ interference; validated with SRM 1568b Rice Flour. No significant association between As levels and country of origin.
- MMA was excluded from final analysis due to low concentrations and minimal risk contribution.

## Methods

30 rice samples (15 white, 15 brown) purchased from Klang Valley superstores March-April 2022. Heat-enhanced acidic solvent extraction (0.2% HNO3, 90°C). HPLC-ICP-MS on Capcell Pak C18 MG 100 Å column with 1-octanesulfonic acid/malonic acid/tetramethylammonium hydroxide mobile phase. DRC with oxygen gas for AsIII/AsV/DMA/MMA separation.
