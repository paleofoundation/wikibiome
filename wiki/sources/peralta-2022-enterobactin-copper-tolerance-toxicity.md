# Less Is More: Enterobactin Concentration Dependency in Copper Tolerance

## Key Findings

- **Siderophore-copper paradox**: [[siderophores|Enterobactin]] at low concentrations protects *E. coli* from copper toxicity, but at high concentrations **paradoxically increases copper sensitivity** by reducing Cu²⁺ to the more toxic Cu⁺ form [[peralta-2022-enterobactin-copper-tolerance-toxicity]], in-vitro.
- **Iron-copper crosstalk**: This reveals that the iron acquisition system (siderophores) directly participates in copper redox chemistry — siderophore biology cannot be understood in isolation from the broader metal landscape.
- **Therapeutic implications**: Host copper burst (macrophages pump Cu into phagosomes) may be more effective against bacteria with high enterobactin production — a counterintuitive vulnerability.
