# Key Claims

- Dietary administration of oligofructose (Raftilose) and inulin (Raftiline) inhibits the formation of preneoplastic lesions (aberrant crypt foci, ACF) in AOM-induced [[colorectal-cancer]] rat models
- [[prebiotics]] selectively stimulate growth of [[bifidobacterium|Bifidobacteria]], which have tumor inhibitory activity
- Lyophilized cultures of [[bifidobacterium|Bifidobacterium longum]] (BL) strongly suppressed AOM-induced colon tumor development, with a decrease in colonic mucosal cell proliferation and mucosal tumor ODC and ras-p21 activities
- Oligofructose and inulin each significantly reduced total ACF formation (P < 0.02 and P < 0.008, respectively) and the number of multicrypt foci (aberrant crypts per focus >= 3)
- BL dietary administration inhibited colon tumors in both male and female F344 rats, including suppression of IQ-induced (heterocyclic amine) colon and mammary carcinogenesis

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Laboratory animal studies (F344 rats)
- **Prebiotic experiments**: 7-week-old male F344 rats given AOM (carcinogen), then fed diets containing 10% oligofructose or 10% inulin for semipurified diet; ACF scored after 12 weeks
- **ACF results**: Oligofructose reduced total ACF and crypts/focus significantly (P < 0.02); inulin also significantly inhibited ACF (P < 0.008); both reduced multicrypt clusters (>= 3 crypts/focus)
- **Probiotic experiments**: Lyophilized BL cultures (0.5%) in AIN-76A diet fed to AOM-treated F344 rats; BL significantly reduced % animals with tumors (53% vs 77% control), tumor multiplicity (0.83 vs 1.6), and tumor incidence
- **ODC activity**: Elevated in neoplastic tissue; BL cultures significantly decreased ODC activity in both colonic mucosa and tumors of AOM-treated animals
- **ras-p21**: Total ras-p21 expression and mutated ras-p21 were significantly decreased in BL-treated animals
- **IQ carcinogenesis**: BL also suppressed IQ-induced colon tumors and mammary gland tumors in female rats
- **Mechanism**: BL may act as a biological response modifier, modulating O6-methylguanine DNA methyltransferase (suicide enzyme) to restore guanine in DNA via in situ demethylation
