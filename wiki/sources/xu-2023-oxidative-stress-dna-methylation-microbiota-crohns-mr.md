# Key Claims

- Oxidative stress (OS) genes are causally involved in Crohn's disease pathogenesis, with 438 of 817 OS-related genes differentially expressed in CD intestinal tissue (53.61%)
- Five putative CD-causal OS genes identified through three-step SMR: BAD, SHC1, STAT3, MUC1, and GPX3
- Three of the five causal genes (MUC1, CD40, PRKAB1) are involved in gene-microbiota interactions through colocalization analysis
- MUC1 expression regulates microbial metabolic functions (creatinine degradation, myo-inositol degradation pathways) involving [[escherichia-coli]] and Bacillus aciditolerans
- STAT3 upregulation is driven by DNA hypomethylation at an enhancer region and causally increases CD risk, connecting redox signaling to [[inflammation]]

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Multi-omics integrative MR combining GWAS, eQTL, mQTL, and mbQTL data
- **Meta-analysis of DEGs**: 6 intestinal transcriptome datasets (704 CD vs 212 HC); 438 OS-related DEGs (FDR<0.05); top genes by effect size: DUOX2, MMP3, S100A8, MMP1, IL1B
- **Cell type enrichment (CSEA)**: OS DEGs enriched in intestinal enterocytes (FDR=0.002)
- **Three-step SMR from blood**: BAD, SHC1, STAT3, MUC1, GPX3 passed all SMR steps (SNP->methylation->transcription->CD)
- **STAT3**: DNAm probe cg06422947 in enhancer region; lower methylation -> higher STAT3 expression -> increased CD risk
- **GPX3**: Glutathione peroxidase; lower methylation -> higher expression -> protective against CD (catalyzes H2O2 reduction)
- **Colocalization (gene-microbiota)**: MUC1-Bacillus aciditolerans (CRNFORCAT.PWY) and PRKAB1-Escherichia coli (PYRIDNUCSYN.PWY) validated in FAH-SYS IBD cohort
- **External validation**: 88.79% of DEGs replicated in FAH-SYS cohort (46 CD vs 44 HC)

# Connections

- Directly links [[ferroptosis]]-related oxidative stress to Crohn's disease through GPX3 (glutathione peroxidase pathway)
- STAT3 as a CD-causal gene connects to JAK-STAT signaling in [[inflammation]] and complements [[Knights et al. 2014|knights-2014-host-genetics-microbiome-ibd]]
- [[escherichia-coli]] interaction with PRKAB1 demonstrates gene-microbiome causal chains in CD
- MUC1 as barrier gene connecting host genetics to microbial metabolism
- GPX3 protective effect relevant to [[iron]]-dependent oxidative damage and [[ferroptosis]]
- [[copper]] and [[zinc]] as cofactors for antioxidant enzymes (SOD) in the oxidative stress framework
