# Key Claims

- **Perinatal depression prevalence**: 26.3% (SD=11.6, median=23.8%) based on 128 systematic reviews
- Few specific pharmacological treatments exist for breastfeeding mothers concerned about antidepressant safety
- **Saffron and vitamin D** show promise in RCTs but with inconsistent efficacy, limited long-term safety data, and potential interactions with perinatal physiology
- **Herbal remedies** (e.g., St. John's wort) have been used for anxiety and depression but may transfer to breast milk
- Caution warranted until comprehensive studies validate safety and reliability of natural interventions

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Review of pathogenesis (neurotransmitter dysregulation, [[inflammation]], hormonal imbalance, microbiota alterations) and non-pharmacological treatment evidence
- **Key limitation**: "Natural is harmless" misconception requires robust safety evidence for supplementary interventions
