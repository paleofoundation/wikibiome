# Key Findings

- **Different antibiotic classes** produce distinct dysbiosis patterns: broad-spectrum antibiotics cause greater diversity loss and more Proteobacteria overgrowth than narrow-spectrum agents [[bing-2019-antibiotic-induced-dysbiosis-gut-microbiota]], expert-opinion.
- **Proteobacteria overgrowth** after antibiotic treatment increases epithelial permeability, creating a pathway for systemic inflammation and downstream disease risk [[bing-2019-antibiotic-induced-dysbiosis-gut-microbiota]].
- Long-term consequences include loss of colonization resistance, increased susceptibility to pathogenic organisms, and altered metabolic capacity (SCFA production, bile acid metabolism, estrogen metabolism).
