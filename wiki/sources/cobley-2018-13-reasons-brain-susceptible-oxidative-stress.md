# Cobley & Fiorella 2018 — 13 Reasons Why the Brain Is Susceptible to Oxidative Stress

## Key Findings
- **The brain consumes ~20% of total body oxygen** despite being ~2% of body mass, generating proportionally more reactive oxygen species [[cobley-2018-13-reasons-brain-susceptible-oxidative-stress]]
- Iron and copper accumulate in specific brain regions and catalyze Fenton chemistry, producing hydroxyl radicals that damage lipids, proteins, and DNA
- The brain has limited catalase activity, high polyunsaturated fatty acid content (peroxidation substrate), and post-mitotic neurons that cannot dilute damage through division

## Relevance to WikiBiome
- Explains why neurodegenerative diseases (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's) have strong metal signatures — the brain is a pre-loaded system where metal dysregulation has outsized consequences
- Supports the metal-disease connection for neurological conditions without requiring a microbiome intermediary — metals are directly pathogenic in this tissue
