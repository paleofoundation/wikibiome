# Yin 2022 — Alterations of bacteriome, mycobiome and metabolome characteristics in PCOS patients with normal/overweight individuals

**Citation:** Yin G, Chen F, Chen G, et al. *Alterations of bacteriome, mycobiome and metabolome characteristics in PCOS patients with normal/overweight individuals.* Journal of Ovarian Research. 2022;15:117. DOI: [10.1186/s13048-022-01051-8](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13048-022-01051-8)

## Key Findings

- **PCOS bacteriome signature independent of BMI.** Across 88 fecal samples stratified by BMI (LB: normal BMI <24, HB: overweight BMI ≥24) and PCOS status, LEfSe identified PCOS-universal enrichment of **Lachnospira**, **Escherichia-Shigella**, **Erysipelotrichaceae_UCG-003**, and **Hungatella** (blue bars), regardless of BMI. PCOS-universal depletion: Coprobacillus, Slackia, Ruminiclostridium_6, Coprococcus, Desulfovibrio, Lachnospiraceae_UCG-001, Barnesiella, Eubacterium_ventriosum, Coprococcus_3, Alloprevotella, Coprococcus_2, Collinsella, Eubacterium_eligens, Ruminococcaceae_UCG-014, Ruminococcus_1 (red bars).
- **Mycobiome signature partitions PCOS by BMI.** Healthy-HB had significantly higher fungal α-diversity (Chao1, Shannon) than PCOS-LB. Ascomycota, Basidiomycota, Mortierellomycota dominated; **Mortierellomycota was uniquely enriched in Healthy-HB** (and depleted in PCOS-HB). 16 fungal genera differentially abundant between Healthy-LB and PCOS-LB; 75 taxa between Healthy-HB and PCOS-HB. PCOS-characteristic fungi: **Candida, Malassezia, Kazachstania, Microascus, Coniochaeta, Xepicula, Paraphoma, Pyrenochaetopsis, Cephaliophora, Epicoccum, Sclerophora**.
- **Candida positively correlates with HDL-C**; **Mortierella negatively correlated with BMI but adversely correlated with LH, AD, TT, HDL-C, DHEA** — flags Mortierella as a candidate fungal marker of metabolic-reproductive decoupling.
- **Metabolome distinguishes groups best.** 601 metabolites identified; 284 differential in Healthy-LB vs PCOS-LB, 358 in Healthy-HB vs PCOS-HB. Top 10 VIPs included 3-Hydroxy-2-methyl-4H-pyran-4-one, Asp-phe, Furfuryl alcohol, Hydroxyacetone, Iminodiacetic acid, L-ascorbate, L-Dihydroorotic acid, Myoinositol, Phe-Phe, Pyrroloquinoline quinone. **Phe-Phe and Asp-Phe enriched in healthy controls** (dipeptides reflecting protein turnover and muscle metabolism).
- **Random forest diagnostic model.** Metabolome-derived model achieved AUC=1.0 in training and AUC=0.82 in test for PCOS vs Healthy overall; PCOS-LB vs Healthy-LB AUC=1.0 training and 0.75 test. Fungal model AUC=0.84; bacterial model AUC=0.64; combined model AUC=0.85. **Metabolome outperforms microbiome-based classification.**
- **Hyperandrogenism central to PCOS dysbiosis.** PCOS-HB had significantly higher LH, FSH, estrogen, androstenedione, total testosterone, free androgen index, fasting and 120-min glucose, HDL-C-low, SHBG-low, DHEA, FAI vs Healthy-HB (FDR <0.05). FAI and insulin-related parameters most influenced bacterial community structure.

## Methods

Cross-sectional multi-omics at Shantou University (2019 IRB). 88 women, 4 groups: Healthy-LB (BMI<24, n=21), PCOS-LB (BMI<24, n=22), Healthy-HB (BMI≥24, n=20), PCOS-HB (BMI≥24, n=25). PCOS by Rotterdam 2003 criteria. Blood at T0 fasting and 120 min post-OGTT. Fecal: 16S rRNA V3–V4 region (Illumina Novaseq6000) for bacteriome; ITS2 region for mycobiome (E.Z.N.A. kit, BIOZERON Biotech). Serum: LC-ESI-MS/MS untargeted metabolomics (UPLC, ExionLC AD System; Q TRAP 6500 mass spectrometer). Stats: α-diversity (Chao1, Shannon); β-diversity (PCoA Bray-Curtis with PERMANOVA, ANOSIM); LEfSe (LDA>3); Kruskal-Wallis; PLS-DA; random forest classification (80/20 train/test split, 5-fold CV).

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Healthy-HB (overweight healthy) showed *higher* mycobiome diversity than Healthy-LB (normal-BMI healthy) — counterintuitive; suggests BMI alone doesn't predict fungal dysbiosis, and Mortierella in particular may benefit from metabolic substrate availability in overweight but metabolically healthy individuals.
- Candida positively correlates with HDL-C — contradicts intuition that Candida overgrowth signals metabolic dysregulation. Possible that *Candida albicans* strain diversity varies by metabolic state, or that commensal Candida is protective while pathogenic strains are harmful.
- Cross-sectional design cannot distinguish cause from consequence. No intervention arm.

## Open Questions

- Whether Mortierella or Candida strains differ between PCOS and healthy populations (shotgun needed).
- Whether targeting Hungatella (TMA producer) reduces cardiometabolic risk in PCOS.
- Whether the metabolome-based diagnostic model validates in external cohorts (only internal train/test split here).
- How the PCOS-universal enrichment of E. coli-Shigella relates to Candida enrichment — siderophore competition or cooperation?
