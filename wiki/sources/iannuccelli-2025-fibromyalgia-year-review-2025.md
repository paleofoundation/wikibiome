# Iannuccelli 2025 — Fibromyalgia: One Year in Review 2025

## Key Findings

- **Salience network (SN) imbalance model** proposed: FM results from an imbalance between threat and soothing systems, keeping SN in continuous alert ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]], review, ~1000 studies from 2024).
- **Multi-omics diagnostic approach:** Durán-González et al. found **Streptococcus salivarius** and **GAPDH** (glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase) as leading multifactorial actors connecting gut microbiota, mitochondria-derived oxidative stress, and inflammation in FM ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]]).
- **Mitochondrial dysfunction:** SOD1, catalase, NADPH oxidase deficiency confirmed in FM; oxidative stress correlates with pain severity and sensitization ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]]).
- **MRI studies:** altered functional connectivity in default mode network (DMN), SN, and central executive network distinguish FM from healthy controls; MRI + machine learning show diagnostic promise ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]]).
- **FM-long COVID differential diagnosis** emerging: shared mechanisms (neuroinflammation, fatigue, cognitive dysfunction) but distinct trajectories ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]]).
- **Gut-brain axis:** Coprococcus2, Eggerthella, Lactobacillus causally increase FM risk (MR evidence); FamilyXIIIUCG001 and Olsenella protective ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]]).
- **Emerging therapeutics:** non-invasive neuromodulation, psychedelic therapies, VR, AI integration ([[iannuccelli-2025-fibromyalgia-year-review-2025]]).
