# Key Claims

- Prenatal [[lead]] (Pb) exposure during pregnancy is negatively associated with the [[gut-metal-microbiome|gut microbiome]] composition in children aged 9-11 years
- Second trimester Pb exposure shows the strongest negative association with the gut microbiome mixture, supporting the second trimester as a critical window
- Key taxa negatively associated with Pb exposure include Ruminococcus gnavus, Bifidobacterium longum, Alistipes indistinctus, [[Bacteroides caccae]], and Bifidobacterium bifidum
- The consistent association of prenatal Pb with decreased B. caccae across multiple studies and populations is strong evidence for this relationship
- Metagenomic sequencing reveals functional pathway differences, with nucleic acid biosynthesis and amino acid biosynthesis pathways most affected

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Pilot epidemiological study within the PROGRESS birth cohort (Mexico City)
- **Participants**: 123 children aged 9-11 years; 74 male, 49 female; predominantly low-to-middle SES
- **Pb measurement**: Maternal whole blood Pb measured at 2nd trimester (mean 33.6 ug/L) and 3rd trimester (mean 34.9 ug/L) by ICP-MS
- **Microbiome**: Shotgun metagenomic sequencing of child stool samples
- **Alpha/beta diversity**: Slight negative (non-significant) associations with alpha diversity; 3rd trimester Pb associated with beta diversity (Adjusted R^2=1.1%, p=0.066)
- **WQS regression**: Negative association between Pb and gut microbiome mixture for both 2nd trimester (beta=-0.17, 95%CI[-0.46, 0.11]) and 3rd trimester (beta=-0.17, 95%CI[-0.44, 0.10]); 88-89% of repeated holdout estimates below zero
- **Important taxa (WQS weights >0.027)**: Ruminococcus gnavus, Bifidobacterium longum, Alistipes indistinctus, Bacteroides caccae, Bifidobacterium bifidum -- all above importance threshold for both trimesters
- **TWAS individual taxa**: 6 taxa negatively and 1 positively associated with 2nd trimester Pb; 3 negatively and 3 positively associated with 3rd trimester Pb
- **Gene function pathways**: Top 20 pathways from highly weighted taxa differed between trimesters; common pathways involved nucleic acid biosynthesis; trimester-specific pathways involved amino acid biosynthesis and fermentation
