# Overview

Large-scale two-sample MR analysis examining causal relationships between gut microbiota (190 bacterial taxa from MiBioGen, 18,473 individuals) and **15 psychiatric diseases/traits** including ASD, ADHD, depression, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and others.

# Key Findings

## Causal Taxa for ASD

- **Family Prevotellaceae** causally associated with ASD (P_IVW = 5.31 x 10^-4)
- This was the strongest microbiome-ASD signal among all 15 psychiatric conditions
- No reverse causality detected (psychiatric disorders did not alter gut microbiota after FDR correction)

## Broader Psychiatric Associations

- Eight total causal associations identified across 7 bacterial features and 6 psychiatric conditions
- **Class Betaproteobacteria** causally linked to bipolar disorder
- **Class Actinobacteria** linked to schizophrenia
- **Class/Order Bacteroidia/Bacteroidales** linked to Tourette syndrome
- **Species *Clostridium innocuum*** linked to neuroticism

## Sensitivity Analyses

- No evidence of horizontal pleiotropy (MR-Egger intercept, MR-PRESSO)
- No heterogeneity (Cochran's Q)
- Seven of eight causal associations cross-validated by more than two MR methods

## Prevotellaceae Mechanism

- Prevotellaceae produces propionate, which has been linked to ASD-related behavioral changes in animal models
- Propionate administration induces autistic-like behaviors and electrographic changes
- *Bacteroides fragilis* treatment can restore microbiota and decrease ASD-like behaviors in mice
