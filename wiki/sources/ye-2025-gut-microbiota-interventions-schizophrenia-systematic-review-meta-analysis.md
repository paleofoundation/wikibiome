# Effects of Gut Microbiota Interventions on Patients with Schizophrenia: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## Key Claims

- Meta-analysis of 10 RCTs comprising 585 [[schizophrenia]] patients receiving gut microbiota interventions ([[probiotics]], prebiotics, synbiotics, dietary modifications, FMT) as adjunctive therapy
- Interventions significantly lowered total PANSS scores (MD = -5.38, 95% CI -8.70 to -2.06, p = 0.001)
- Substantial metabolic improvements observed: significant reductions in fasting blood sugar, triglycerides, total cholesterol, HOMA-IR, and QUICKI (all p < 0.05)
- No significant effects on HDL-cholesterol, LDL-cholesterol, body weight, BMI, or insulin levels
- Seven of the 10 studies were included in PANSS analysis; intervention durations ranged from 6 to 14 weeks
- Probiotic strains included Lactobacillus species (L. acidophilus, L. reuteri, L. fermentum), Bifidobacterium species (B. bifidum, B. lactis, B. longiformis), and Enterococcus
- Studies were geographically diverse: 5 from East Asia, 2 West Asia, 1 Southeast Asia, 1 Europe, 1 Americas
- Bifidobacterium breve A-1 may alleviate affective symptoms; FMT could restore microbial ecology; dietary fiber may enhance butyrate production
- GRADE evidence quality rated as moderate for some outcomes

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA, PROSPERO registered)
- **Studies included**: 10 RCTs from database search of 1,371 records
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane ROB 2.0; only 2/10 studies had low risk of bias overall
- **Key finding**: First meta-analysis to show significant PANSS improvement alongside metabolic benefits
