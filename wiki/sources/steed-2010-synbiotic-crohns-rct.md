# Key Claims

- Synbiotic consumption (Bifidobacterium longum + Synergy 1 FOS prebiotic) significantly improved clinical outcomes in active [[crohns-disease]] patients
- CDAI activity indices (p=0.020) and histological scores (p=0.018) were significantly reduced in the synbiotic group vs placebo
- Mucosal bifidobacteria proliferated in synbiotic patients, demonstrating successful colonization
- TNF-alpha expression was significantly reduced at 3 months (p=0.041) in synbiotic patients, though this effect was not sustained at 6 months
- IL-18, IFN-gamma, and IL-1beta were not significantly affected by the synbiotic intervention

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (NCT00305409)
- **Participants**: 35 patients with active Crohn's disease
- **Intervention**: Bifidobacterium longum (probiotic) + Synergy 1 (inulin/FOS prebiotic) for 6 months
- **Assessments**: Clinical status scored, rectal biopsies at baseline, 3 months, and 6 months
- **Mucosal analysis**: 16S rRNA gene copy numbers by real-time PCR; transcription levels of immune markers
- **Key result**: Bifidobacteria successfully colonized the mucosa in the synbiotic group

# Connections

- Demonstrates that [[probiotics]] combined with prebiotics can modify mucosal bacterial populations in [[crohns-disease]]
- TNF-alpha reduction connects to anti-[[inflammation]] mechanisms relevant to CD pathogenesis
- Bifidobacterium colonization may compete with adherent-invasive [[escherichia-coli]] for mucosal niches
- Prebiotic FOS component promotes SCFA production by [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] and other commensals
- Addresses [[dysbiosis]] correction at the mucosal level rather than just fecal
