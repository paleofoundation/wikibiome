# Slyepchenko 2021 — Comorbid PMDD and Bipolar Disorder (Review)

## Key Findings

- **10–21% of females with BD** have comorbid PMDD; **54.5% vs. 46.6%** of BD females experience rapid cycling ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]], review).
- **Comorbid BD-PMDD** associated with: more lifetime depressive episodes, earlier age of onset, increased psychiatric comorbidities (anxiety disorders, agoraphobia, alcohol/substance use, eating disorders, ADHD, bulimia, PTSD), and worse clinical course ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]]).
- **Treatment dilemma:** SSRIs are first-line for PMDD but carry risk of treatment-emergent mania in BD patients ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]]).
- **Shared neurobiology:** ALLO sensitivity (GABA_A receptor modulation), serotonergic dysfunction, GABA alterations, BDNF level changes. PMDD may represent a state of differential sensitivity to normal hormone fluctuations ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]]).
- **BD type II** more associated with comorbid PMDD than BD type I ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]]).
- GnRH agonist (leuprolide) eliminated PMDD symptoms in majority of females, but adding back estradiol/progesterone returned symptoms — confirming hormone sensitivity rather than abnormal levels ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]]).
- Vitamin B6 (100mg pyridoxine) recommended in RCOG guidelines; calcium carbonate 1200mg showed benefit comparable to weak fluoxetine effect; Vitex agnus castus (chasteberry) supported by 17-trial meta-analysis ([[slyepchenko-2021-comorbid-pmdd-bipolar-review]]).

## Relevance to Cureva

- **STOP candidate:** SSRIs in comorbid BD-PMDD without mood stabilizer coverage.
- **Intervention candidates:** Vitex agnus castus, calcium carbonate, vitamin B6 as PMDD interventions avoiding BD destabilization.
- **Screening:** All female BD patients should be screened for PMDD.
