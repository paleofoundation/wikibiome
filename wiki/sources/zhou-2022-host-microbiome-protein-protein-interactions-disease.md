# Zhou 2022 -- Host-Microbiome Protein-Protein Interactions in Disease

## Key Claims

- Leveraging interspecies protein-protein interaction (PPI) data, the study identified 1,102 commensal bacterial protein clusters associated with disease across nine metagenomic cohorts for IBD, CRC, obesity, and T2D
- Microbiome broadly targets human proteins involved in immune, oncogenic, apoptotic, and endocrine signaling pathways in relation to disease
- The human protein with the highest degree remaining after filtering was NF-kB p105 subunit (NFKB1), involved in immunodeficiency and bacterial infection, differentially targeted in CRC
- Disease-associated human targets enriched in pathways including IL-12 signaling, clathrin-mediated endocytosis, bile salt metabolism (LXR/RXR, TX/RXR, FXR/RXR activation), and atherosclerosis signaling
- Enriched pathways were not disease-specific but included human proteins across all four disease cohorts; 45.2% of proteins had more than one gene-disease association

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Computational analysis of 15,252 experimentally verified inter-species PPIs mapped to nine metagenomic case-control studies
- **Methods**: Random forest classifier; gene-disease association (GDA) enrichment via DisGeNET; pathway enrichment via Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA)
- **Key result**: 106 bacterial species mapped to 3,056 proteins in 1,698 clusters with homologs detected in patient metagenomes; pathways enriched across diseases include p53, polyamine regulation in colon cancer, glucocorticoid/platelet signaling
