# Key Claims

- [[Amino acids]] are vital nutrients for cancer cells, serving as energy sources, biosynthetic materials, redox balance regulators, and epigenetic/immune modulators
- [[Glutamine]] is the most consumed amino acid in cancer; it fuels the TCA cycle, supports nucleotide biosynthesis, and maintains redox balance via glutathione production
- Branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs: valine, leucine, isoleucine) serve as alternative fuel sources and are elevated in plasma of KRAS-mutant pancreatic cancer patients
- [[Tryptophan]] catabolism via IDO/TDO produces [[kynurenine]], which promotes cancer cell survival by reducing T cell function and inducing immune tolerance in dendritic cells
- Amino acid-derived polyamines (putrescine, spermine, spermidine) promote tumor proliferation and aggressiveness through epigenetic chromatin remodeling
- The system L-type amino acid transporter LAT1 (SLC7A5) is elevated in multiple cancers including colorectal, and represents a therapeutic target
- Serine and glycine metabolism provides one-carbon units critical for nucleotide synthesis, methylation reactions, and redox balance in cancer cells

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review article
- **Amino acids as fuel**: Glutamine and BCAAs provide anaplerotic substrates to the TCA cycle; cancer cells upregulate glutaminase (GLS) and BCAT enzymes
- **Biosynthetic roles**: Asparagine synthetase (ASNS) upregulation is a resistance mechanism to L-asparaginase therapy; serine synthesis pathway (PHGDH) is amplified in breast and CRC
- **Redox balance**: Cysteine, glycine, and glutamate form glutathione (GSH); the xCT antiporter system imports cystine for GSH synthesis; serine contributes via the folate cycle to NADPH
- **Epigenetic regulation**: Methionine cycle provides SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) for DNA and histone methylation; acetyl-CoA from BCAAs/threonine for histone acetylation; CD110/thrombopoietin receptor on tumor-initiating cells activates lysine catabolism for acetyl-CoA
- **Immunosuppression**: IDO1/TDO2 deplete tryptophan in the tumor microenvironment, producing kynurenine that activates AhR in DCs and Tregs; kynurenine shown in Hodgkin lymphoma, lung, and ovarian cancers
- **Transporters as targets**: LAT1 (SLC7A5), ASCT2 (SLC1A5), xCT (SLC7A11) all upregulated in cancers; potential for therapeutic targeting
