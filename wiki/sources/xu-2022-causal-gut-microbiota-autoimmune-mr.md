# Key Claims

- Bifidobacterium genus has a causal relationship with increased risk of type 1 diabetes (OR: 1.605, 95% CI: 1.339-1.922) and celiac disease (OR: 1.401, 95% CI: 1.139-1.722) via Mendelian randomization
- Ruminococcus was causally associated with higher risk of SLE (OR: 5.593) and IBD (OR: 2.141), but not replicated in validation samples
- No evidence of reverse causation from T1D or celiac disease on Bifidobacterium abundance, supporting a causal direction from microbiota to disease
- The causal effects of Bifidobacterium may be species- and strain-specific, with some strains being pro-inflammatory (Th17-inducing) and others protective

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Two-sample Mendelian randomization (MR) across 6 autoimmune diseases (SLE, RA, IBD, MS, T1D, CeD)
- **Exposure data**: GWAS meta-analysis of gut microbiota from 18,340 individuals across 24 cohorts; 211 taxa analyzed at genus level (117 bacterial taxa after filtering)
- **IBD-specific results**: Ruminococcus associated with IBD risk (OR: 2.141, P_FDR = 2.92e-3) in discovery; Bifidobacterium showed OR: 1.182 (P=0.011) but did not survive FDR correction for IBD
- **Replication**: Bifidobacterium on T1D and CeD replicated in UK Biobank samples; IBD associations for Bifidobacterium did not replicate
- **Sensitivity analyses**: MR-Egger intercepts null, no evidence of horizontal pleiotropy; leave-one-out analyses confirmed no single SNP dominated results
- **Reverse MR**: No causal effect of T1D or CeD on Bifidobacterium (all ORs ~1.0, all P>0.05)
