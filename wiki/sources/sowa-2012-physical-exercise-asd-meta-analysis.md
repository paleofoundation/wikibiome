# Key Claims

- **Physical exercise** yields significant progress on social and motor deficiency measures in ASD individuals
- **Individual exercise programmes** elicited significantly more improvement than group interventions in motor and social domains
- **Communication deficits** showed insufficient improvement to permit classification in the meta-analysis
- **16 behavioural studies** evaluated, reporting on 133 children and adults with various ASD variants
- Effects on two of three primary symptom clusters of ASD were normalized to afford quantitative evaluation

# Methods & Findings

**Meta-analysis** of 16 behavioural studies on structured physical activity in ASD.

- Both individual and group exercise contexts evaluated
- All activity programmes yielded significant progress on assessed measures
- Individual programmes superior to group interventions for motor performance and social skills
- Sample sizes were small overall
- Communication deficits less responsive to exercise interventions

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Exercise as behavioral intervention** addresses core ASD symptoms through non-pharmacological means
- **Motor-social connection** in ASD suggests shared neural substrates
- **Individual vs group** format matters for therapeutic outcomes in ASD
