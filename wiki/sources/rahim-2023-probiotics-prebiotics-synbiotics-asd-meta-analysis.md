# Key Claims

- **No significant effect** on autism-related behavioral symptoms (combined SMD = -0.07, 95% CI -0.39 to 0.24, P = 0.65)
- **Significant EEG changes** with probiotics: reduced beta band power (P = 0.043) and gamma spectrum (P = 0.033)
- **Frontopolar gamma coherence** negatively correlated with TNF-alpha (r = -0.30, P = 0.04)
- **Psychobiotics showed no serious side effects**

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Meta-analysis of 16 RCTs (n = 720 children/adults aged 2-55 years) plus umbrella review of prior meta-analyses.

**Included studies:** 15 used probiotics, 1 used prebiotics. From USA (7), China (4), UK (2), Italy, Taiwan, Egypt (1 each).

**Behavioral outcomes (non-significant):**
- 10 studies reported ARBS; random-effects model due to high heterogeneity (P = 0.007, I2 = 62%)
- Combined SMD = -0.07 (95% CI: -0.39 to 0.24, Z = 0.46, P = 0.65)

**EEG/neurophysiological outcomes (significant):**
- Beta band semantic similarity power: baseline 13.09 to endpoint 10.75 (P = 0.043)
- Gamma spectrum: baseline 5.80 to endpoint 4.63 (P = 0.033)
- Frontopolar delta band asymmetry significantly decreased (P = 0.032)
- Frontopolar gamma coherence correlated with lower TNF-alpha

**Table 1 details:** Comprehensive overview of 16 RCTs with probiotic strains, dosages, durations, and outcome measures across multiple countries.

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **EEG as metallomic proxy**: Beta and gamma band changes may reflect altered metal-dependent neurotransmitter synthesis (GABA, glutamate) driven by microbiota shifts
- **TNF-alpha-gamma coherence link**: TNF-alpha reduction via probiotics may restore metal-sequestering acute-phase proteins (ceruloplasmin, metallothionein)
- **Behavioral non-significance**: Confirms that microbiota modulation alone is insufficient for core ASD symptoms; metallomic co-intervention may be required
- **Dose heterogeneity**: Variable probiotic doses (0.5-900 billion CFU) across studies confounds metal-dependent colonization dynamics
