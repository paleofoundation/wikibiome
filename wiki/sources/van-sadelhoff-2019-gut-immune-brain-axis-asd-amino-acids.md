# Key Claims

- **mTOR (mammalian target of rapamycin)** might be a critical factor linking the gut-brain-immune axis in ASD
- **Hyperactivation of mTOR** leads to deficits in communication between neurons in the brain and immune impairments
- **Pharmacological inhibition of mTOR** improves ASD-associated behavior and immune functions but has severe side effects limiting clinical use
- **mTOR activation can be modified by nutritional stimuli**, particularly amino acids
- **Specific amino acids** can inhibit inflammation, improve gut barrier function, and modify microbiota composition

# Methods & Findings

**Review** discussing the gut-immune-brain axis in ASD with focus on amino acid interventions.

- Examined mTOR as central integrator of gut, immune, and brain signaling in ASD
- Reviewed amino acid effects on inflammation, gut barrier, and microbiota
- Proposed amino acids as treatment for ASD via mTOR modulation, immune system, or gut microbiota
- Highlighted the bidirectional nature of the gut-immune-brain axis
- Suggested nutritional approaches as safer alternatives to pharmacological mTOR inhibition
