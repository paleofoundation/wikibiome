# Key Claims

- **Decreased serum zinc levels** were found in postpartum depression cases compared to controls
- Serum zinc in PPD cases was **21.03 +/- 13.73 ug/dL** vs. **54.16 +/- 19.72 ug/dL** in controls -- a statistically significant difference
- There was a **negative correlation** between the severity of postpartum depression (EPDS score) and serum zinc levels (p < 0.05)
- PPD was more prevalent in the **25-30 year age group** (47.5%), among housewives (67.5%), those with cesarean delivery (42.5%), and primiparas (60%)
- Risk factors for PPD included younger age, lower education, vaginal delivery (in this sample), and being primiparous

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Hospital-based case-control study over 3 months (June-August 2018)
- **Sample**: 80 participants -- 40 PPD cases (EPDS > 10) and 40 healthy controls (EPDS < 10) from postnatal mothers aged 20-30 years
- **Setting**: S. Nijalingappa Medical College and Hanagal Sri Kumareshwar Hospital, Karnataka, India
- **Assessment**: EPDS score classification; serum zinc measured by Nitro-PAPS method using semi-auto analyzer
- **Statistical Analysis**: Student's t-test, Pearson correlation, SPSS

 - Mean EPDS in cases: 21.03 +/- 13.73 (zinc); mean EPDS in controls: 54.16 +/- 19.72 (zinc) -- note these are zinc values, not EPDS scores
 - The significant difference (p < 0.01) in serum zinc confirms the association
 - Negative correlation between EPDS score and zinc level was statistically significant
 - Cases: 60% primiparas, 70% had normal vaginal delivery, 50% had cesarean section (among some)
- **Limitations**: Small sample size, single-center study, limited socioeconomic and family-related confounders assessed
