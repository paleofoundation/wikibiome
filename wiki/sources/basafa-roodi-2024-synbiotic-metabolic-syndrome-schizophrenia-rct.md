# Effects of Synbiotic Supplementation on the Components of Metabolic Syndrome in Patients with Schizophrenia

## Key Claims

- Double-blind RCT in 70 [[schizophrenia]] patients with at least 2 criteria of [[metabolic-syndrome]], randomized to synbiotic (n=35) or placebo (n=35) for 8 weeks alongside antipsychotic medication
- Synbiotic significantly decreased waist circumference (-2.66 +/- 4.20 vs 3.03 +/- 4.50, between-group P significant) and HbA1c (-0.26 +/- 0.54 vs 0.20 +/- 0.75)
- BMI did not change significantly in the synbiotic group but increased in the placebo+antipsychotic group (between-group P = 0.01)
- LDL-c and triglycerides decreased significantly in the synbiotic+antipsychotic group (-0.37 +/- 1.00 vs 0.61 +/- 1.09, P < 0.5)
- FBS, HDL-c, systolic and diastolic blood pressure, insulin resistance, and total cholesterol were not significantly different after intervention
- 55 patients completed the study; compliance was >95%
- Synbiotic contained L. rhamnosus, L. casei, L. acidophilus, L. bulgaricus, L. plantarum, L. gasseri, L. helveticus, B. lactis, B. breve, B. longum, B. bifidus, S. thermophilus, and 21 mg fructooligosaccharide

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (IRCT registered)
- **Sample**: 70 patients, 55 completed (27 synbiotic, 28 placebo)
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Key limitation**: Short duration, single-center
