# Towards the Human Colorectal Cancer Microbiome

## Key Claims

- This study provides the first high-resolution map of microbial colonization patterns associated with human CRC, revealing striking differences between on-tumor and off-tumor tissue communities across 6 colon adenocarcinoma patients
- *Coriobacteriaceae* members (*Slackia*, *Collinsella*) and *Roseburia* were consistently overrepresented in CRC tissue, while *Enterobacteriaceae* members (*Citrobacter*, *Shigella*, *Cronobacter*, *Kluyvera*, *Serratia*, *Salmonella*) were underrepresented
- The overrepresentation of butyrate-producing bacteria (*Roseburia*, *Fusobacterium*, *Faecalibacterium*) in tumors suggests these species may be CRC "passengers" recruited by the altered tumor microenvironment rather than drivers
- Table 2 identifies consistent CRC-associated microbiome shifts using a consistency scoring system across all 6 patients, with *Slackia* (-6), *Collinsella* (-5), and *Roseburia* (-4) as the most consistently tumor-enriched
- Metabolomics studies revealed drastic decreases in glucose and pyruvate with increases in lactate, amino acids, lipids, and fatty acids in the CRC tumor microenvironment, explaining the preferential recruitment of specific bacterial species

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Paired on-tumor vs. off-tumor tissue comparison from 6 primary colon adenocarcinoma patients using DGGE fingerprinting, RISA, and 454 pyrosequencing
- **Participants**: 6 patients (5 male, 1 female; ages 49-71) from Radboud University Nijmegen Medical Centre, Netherlands
- **Methods**: DGGE for initial fingerprinting; 454 titanium pyrosequencing of V1-V3 16S rRNA (193,839 reads); MOTHUR for diversity; MEGAN for phylogenetic visualization
- **Consistency scoring**: Novel scoring system (-6 to +6) to quantify how consistently each clade was enriched or depleted across all patients
