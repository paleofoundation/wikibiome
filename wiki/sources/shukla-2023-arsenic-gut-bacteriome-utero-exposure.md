# Analysis of Gut Bacteriome of In Utero Arsenic-Exposed Mice

## Key Claims

- In utero arsenic exposure significantly altered gut microbial community composition in offspring
- Significant decrease in Firmicutes was observed in arsenic-exposed groups
- Functional analysis revealed shifts in genes involved in insulin signaling and NAFLD pathways
- Prenatal arsenic exposure can perturb gut bacterial composition and functional pathways

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Female Balb/C mice exposed to low (0.04 mg/kg) and high (0.4 mg/kg) arsenic from GD-15 to delivery
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA V3-V4 sequencing; PICRUSt functional prediction
