# Key Claims

- **Microbiome changes in composition and function** occur during sub-clinical state prior to disease onset
- **Decrease in Akkermansia muciniphila** suggesting role prior to disease onset
- **Functional changes reflect response to oxidative stress** comparable to that in chronic T2D and IBD
- **Twins demonstrate strain-level differences** in composition despite species-level similarities
- **36 stool shotgun metagenomes** from 20 monozygotic Korean twins spanning sub-clinical range

# Methods & Findings

Twin study of sub-clinical metabolic changes:

- Participants were neither obese nor diabetic but spanned entire range of healthy to near-clinical values
- Microbiome changes detectable before clinical disease onset
- Family-independent changes in microbial composition and metabolic function during sub-clinical state
