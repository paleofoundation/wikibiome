# Overview

Hypothesis paper arguing that three environmental toxins -- **acetaminophen (Tylenol), amoxicillin/clavulanate (Augmentin), and glyphosate (Roundup)** -- share a common mechanism of depleting sulfate and glutathione, disrupting gut bacteria that synthesize key metabolites, and are the primary drivers of increasing US autism prevalence.

# Key Arguments

## Acetaminophen (Tylenol)

- Depletes sulfate and glutathione required to detoxify it
- Kills gut bacteria that make tryptophan (limiting brain serotonin synthesis)
- Kills gut bacteria that synthesize methionine (precursor of DNA methylation via SAMe)
- Replaced aspirin for children after Reye's syndrome concerns (1980s) -- coinciding with autism increase
- By 2008: ~65% of Americans used acetaminophen during pregnancy

## Amoxicillin/Clavulanate (Augmentin)

- Oral antibiotic amoxicillin/clavulanate commonly prescribed for childhood ear infections
- Recognized as enabling *Clostridium* overgrowth in gut -- a pattern common in ASD
- CDC reported increased antibiotic-resistant infections; linked to disruption of protective gut flora

## Glyphosate (Roundup)

- Recently herbicide glyphosate was exponentially implicated
- Disrupts shikimate pathway in plants and gut bacteria that synthesize essential aromatic amino acids (tryptophan, tyrosine, phenylalanine)
- Glyphosate-resistant crops introduced in 1996; usage increased dramatically since
- Swanson and colleagues reported greatly increased chronic disease incidence correlating with US glyphosate usage

## Shared Mechanism

- All three deplete sulfate/glutathione, disrupt methylation, and kill gut bacteria synthesizing tryptophan and methionine
- Stress-induced excess androgens from placental/postnatal estrogen synthesis disruption coherently explains white matter asymmetry, extreme male brain phenotype, and hypersensitivity

## Limitations

- Largely correlational and hypothesis-generating
- Ecological fallacy risk: population-level correlations do not prove individual causation
