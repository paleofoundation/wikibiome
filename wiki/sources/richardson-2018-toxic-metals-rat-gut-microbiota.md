# Key Claims

- Oral gavage of five toxic metals (As, Cd, Co, Cr, Ni) to rats for 5 days triggered metal-specific changes in gut microbiota composition, with nickel showing the most pronounced effects (37 genera affected)
- [[Arsenic Toxicity|Arsenic]], [[Cadmium Toxicity|cadmium]], and nickel all showed significant dose-dependent effects on microbiota composition (PERMANOVA)
- Iron complex transport system genes (ATP-binding protein, permease, substrate-binding protein) were significantly overrepresented after arsenic and nickel treatment, suggesting a shared iron-importing response
- Bacteria with higher numbers of iron-importing gene orthologs were overly represented across arsenic and nickel exposures, suggesting some commonality in the microbial response
- Proteobacteria increased in prevalence across most metals (except cadmium); Verrucomicrobia was a shared candidate for increased abundance

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Original experimental research; 16S rRNA V4 region sequencing
- **Model**: Sprague-Dawley rats (5 per cohort) exposed via oral gavage to 3 doses of sodium arsenite, CdCl2, sodium dichromate, CoCl2, or NiCl2 for 5 days
- **Sequencing**: 14,703,356 reads from one multiplexed run; 11,127,881 sequences after quality filtering; 279 samples analyzed
- **Taxonomic assignments**: 99% of OTUs assigned to phylum, 86% to family, 39% to genus
- **Alpha diversity (Figure 4)**: Reduced observed OTUs and Shannon diversity for arsenic, chromium, and nickel (p < 0.05)
- **Beta diversity (Figure 5, Table 1)**: PERMANOVA significant for As (all doses), Cd (all doses), Ni (all doses) post-exposure vs. controls; effect sizes (R2) up to 0.55 for nickel
- **Differential abundance (Figure 6)**: Heat map showing 47 genera with significantly different abundances; nickel affected 37 genera, arsenic 17, cadmium 5, chromium 10, cobalt 1
- **Gene pathways (Figure 7)**: KEGG iron complex transport orthologs (K02013, K02015, K02016) overrepresented in As and Ni treated samples
- **PICRUSt analysis**: 926 KEGG orthologs significantly altered post-exposure; many metal-specific
