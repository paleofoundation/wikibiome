# Key Claims

- [[Drug repurposing]] identifies new applications for existing clinically approved drugs, offering cost-effective and faster cancer treatment development due to established safety profiles
- Repurposed non-oncology candidates target all 14 hallmarks of cancer: metformin (phenotypic plasticity, metabolism), statins (growth suppressors, ferroptosis), disulfiram (cell death, epigenetics), celecoxib (inflammation), mebendazole (invasion/metastasis)
- Multiple forms of regulated cell death (RCD) can be induced by repurposed drugs: pyroptosis, apoptosis, necroptosis, autophagy, ferroptosis, and cuproptosis
- Organoid models (patient-derived organoids, PDOs) and tumoroids are key platforms for drug repurposing screening, enabling high-throughput assessment with clinical relevance
- Computational approaches (molecular docking, network analysis, machine learning, transcriptional signature matching) are powerful tools for identifying repurposing candidates
- Key databases for cancer drug repurposing include UCSC, GeneBank, KEGG, STRING, BIOGRID, Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia, and DrugBank

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review (Signal Transduction and Targeted Therapy)
- **Hallmarks-based organization**: Systematic review of repurposed drugs mapped to each cancer hallmark
- **Salidroside**: Inhibits nasopharyngeal carcinoma, suppresses proliferation, colony formation, migration via PI3K/AKT; also effective in HeLa cells
- **Triptolide (TPL)**: Induces pyroptosis via GSDME, autophagy, membrane disruption; derivative F60008 in clinical trials for advanced solid tumors
- **Tanshinone IIA**: Induces multiple cell death types in renal cell carcinoma, colon cancer cells
- **Leflunomide**: Anti-rheumatic drug showing anti-cancer properties in melanoma via vancomycin-induced gut Fusobacterium depletion
- **Disulfiram (DSF)**: Alcohol-cessation drug that inhibits glycolysis in cancer cells via copper-dependent mechanism; currently in clinical trials for CRC
- **Statins**: Induce ferroptosis in cancer cells; network-based approaches identified statin targets in gastric cancer
- **Nanotechnology integration**: Discussed as strategy to improve topical drug delivery for repurposed agents
