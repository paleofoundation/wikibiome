# Key Claims

- **ASD is a heterogeneous condition** with multiple etiologies, sub-types, and developmental trajectories
- **High prevalence of comorbid medical/psychiatric conditions**: immune abnormalities, gastrointestinal dysfunction, seizure disorders, ADHD, anxiety
- **Biological heterogeneity** reflects diverse genetic and environmental risk factors
- **Early intervention and behavioral therapies** are primary evidence-based treatments

# Methods & Findings

Review of ASD history, epidemiology, diagnosis, and treatment:
- **Diagnostic evolution**: DSM-III "Infantile Autism" → DSM-5 "Autism Spectrum Disorder"
- **Diagnostic criteria**: Persistent deficits in social communication + restricted/repetitive behaviors
- **Comorbidity landscape**: Immune dysfunction, GI symptoms (constipation, diarrhea, reflux), seizures, intellectual disability, ADHD, anxiety
- **Phenotypic heterogeneity**: Variable severity, developmental trajectories, and symptom profiles

**Key epidemiology:**
- Current prevalence ~1 in 50-88 children (varies by region)
- 3-4:1 male-to-female ratio (likely reflects diagnostic bias and sex-specific presentation)
- Heritability estimated 50-90%

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

This foundational clinical overview identifies **GI dysfunction and immune abnormalities** as core ASD features—without mechanistic explanation. Critical implications:

- **Primitive 2 (Nutritional Immunity)**: Reported immune abnormalities may reflect dysregulated metal sequestration (hepcidin elevation, calprotectin changes) rather than intrinsic immune defect
- **Metal exposure as etiological factor**: Environmental metal burden during critical developmental windows could explain phenotypic heterogeneity (different children, different metal exposures → different symptom profiles)
- **GI dysfunction mechanism**: Dysbiotic microbiota → impaired metal absorption/homeostasis → barrier dysfunction → symptoms
- **Comorbidity clustering**: ADHD, seizures, and GI symptoms all implicate neuroinflammation—potentially stemming from metal-dysbiosis-immune axis rather than independent pathologies

This review establishes heterogeneity as **defining feature**, suggesting need for **patient subtyping based on metallome/microbiome signatures** rather than behavioral criteria alone.
