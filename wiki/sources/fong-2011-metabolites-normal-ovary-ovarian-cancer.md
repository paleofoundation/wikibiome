# Identification of Metabolites in the Normal Ovary and Their Transformation in Primary and Metastatic Ovarian Cancer

## Key Claims

- The human ovarian metabolome contains 364 biochemicals; neoplastic transformation causes changes in energy utilization and altered metabolites associated with glycolysis and beta-oxidation of fatty acids
- Carnitine (1.79 fold in EOC), acetylcarnitine (1.75 fold), and butyrylcarnitine (3.62 fold) were significantly increased in ovarian cancer
- Four metabolites (1-methylimidazoleacetate, taurine, phenol sulfate, 6-phosphogluconate) significantly reduced upon transformation
- Novel metabolites N-acetylaspartate and N-acetyl-aspartyl-glutamate were identified in the ovary

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Metabolomic profiling using GC/MS and LC/MS/MS across normal ovary, primary EOC, and metastatic OC samples
- **Metabolites identified**: 364 biochemicals; 95 significantly different across groups
