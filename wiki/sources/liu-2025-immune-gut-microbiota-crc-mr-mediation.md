# Novel Insights into Immune-Gut Microbiota Interactions in Colorectal Cancer: A Mendelian Randomization Study

## Key Claims

- 13 immune cell phenotypes were significantly associated with CRC (10 protective, 3 risk factors), with CD39+ CD8br %T cell, CD33+ CD8br AC, and CD39+ CD8br %CD8br showing protective effects, while NKT %lymphocyte was a risk factor (OR 1.098)
- 13 gut microbiota showed significant associations with CRC: 8 protective (including order *Francisellales* OR 0.429, class *Peptococcia* OR 0.511) and 5 risk factors (including unclassified GCA-900066135 OR 1.619, genus *Psychroserpens* OR 1.448)
- Mediation analysis revealed 4 gut microbiota mediating immune cell-CRC relationships: unclassified CAG-977 mediated 11% and 12.3% of FSC-A on NK and NKT %lymphocyte effects on CRC respectively
- 22.3% of the protective effect of EM CD8br %CD8br on CRC was mediated through order *Francisellales*, representing the largest mediation proportion identified
- Family *Succinivibrionaceae* partially weakened protective effects of CD39+ CD8br AC, CD39+ CD8br %T cell, and CD39+ CD8br %CD8br with mediation proportions of -10.4%, -9.6%, and -11%

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Three-step MR: (1) immune cells to CRC, (2) gut microbiota to CRC, (3) two-step mediation analysis
- **Data sources**: FinnGen R11 (8,801 CRC cases, 345,118 controls); IEU OpenGWAS (731 immune phenotypes from 3,757 Europeans); EBI gut microbiota GWAS (5,959 Finnish individuals, 473 taxa)
- **Methods**: IVW primary with weighted median, simple mode, weighted mode, MR-Egger; Bonferroni correction; Steiger filtering for reverse causation
- **Reverse MR**: CRC did not significantly impact the 13 identified gut microbiota (all P > 0.05)
