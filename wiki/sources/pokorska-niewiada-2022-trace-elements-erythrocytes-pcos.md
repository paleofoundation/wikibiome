## Summary

This study examined trace element levels in erythrocytes of 47 women with PCOS (23 nonobese, 24 obese) and 16 healthy controls, and correlated these with hormonal parameters. The key finding was that nickel was the only trace element showing significant concentration differences between PCOS groups, with obese PCOS women having significantly higher nickel levels than nonobese women with BMI <30.

## Key Findings

- Nickel was significantly higher in erythrocytes of obese PCOS women compared to nonobese PCOS women (p=0.01782) -- the only trace element to differ significantly between groups
- No significant differences in Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu, or Mg concentrations between PCOS and control groups
- In nonobese PCOS women: positive correlations between testosterone and Zn; estradiol and Ni; LH and Ni; androstenedione and Mn; negative correlation between estradiol and Mg
- In obese PCOS women: positive correlations between prolactin and Zn, testosterone and Mg; negative correlation between TSH and Mn
- The identified increase in nickel in erythrocytes in obese PCOS women may indicate a role in inhibiting folliculogenesis and ovulation

## Methods

- 47 PCOS women (Rotterdam criteria) and 16 healthy controls from Poland
- Erythrocyte trace elements measured by ICP-AES
- Elements analyzed: Zn, Ni, Fe, Mn, Cu, Mg
- LOD/LOQ reported for all elements
- Statistical analysis: Pearson's correlation, Student's t-test, Mann-Whitney U test, ANOVA

## Relevance to Wiki

Provides direct evidence that nickel accumulates preferentially in erythrocytes of obese women with PCOS and correlates with reproductive hormones (estradiol, LH), supporting nickel's potential role as an endocrine disruptor in PCOS pathophysiology.
