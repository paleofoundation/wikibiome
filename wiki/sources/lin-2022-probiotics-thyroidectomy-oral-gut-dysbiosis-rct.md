# Randomized Clinical Trial: Probiotics Alleviated Oral-Gut Microbiota Dysbiosis and Thyroid Hormone Withdrawal-Related Complications in Thyroid Cancer Patients Before Radioiodine Therapy Following Thyroidectomy

## Key Findings

- Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT in post-thyroidectomy differentiated thyroid cancer (DTC) patients undergoing thyroid hormone withdrawal (THW) prior to radioiodine therapy. 23 probiotic / 16 placebo completed 4-week intervention.
- **Probiotic complex**: Bifidobacterium infantis, Lactobacillus acidophilus, Enterococcus faecalis, Bacillus cereus (≥10⁶ CFU each per tablet, 3 tablets BID).
- **Clinical outcomes (probiotic vs placebo, % with severe complications)**: constipation 8.7% vs 62.5% (p=0.004); lack of energy 30.4% vs 62.5% (p=0.047); weight gain 34.8% vs 68.8% (p=0.037); dry mouth 30.4% vs 68.8% (p=0.018).
- **Plasma lipids**: probiotic group showed significantly increased CHOL, TG, LDL, ApoA (p<0.05), reflecting normalization — THW typically causes hyperlipidemia which probiotics *partially prevented* by restoring microbiota.
- **Plasma LPS** significantly decreased — indicating reduced bacterial translocation / leaky gut.
- **Gut microbiome (16S rRNA Illumina HiSeq)**: probiotics restored α-diversity (Sobs, Shannon) reduced by THW. Microbial dysbiosis index (MDI) correlated inversely with α-diversity (r=-0.37 to -0.49).
- **Oral microbiome**: probiotics reduced Prevotella_9, Haemophilus, Fusobacterium, Lautropia — taxa positively correlated with dry mouth severity.
- ClinicalTrials.gov: NCT03574051. Sequencing data deposited NCBI PRJNA784752.

## Methods

Single-center, parallel-group, adaptive-design RCT. Inclusion: 18–65 y, DTC post-total thyroidectomy awaiting radioiodine, TSH > 30 mIU/L after 4-week levothyroxine withdrawal, consented to iodine-deficient diet. Excluded women (sex-homogeneity). Sampling: baseline (2–4 d post-thyroidectomy) and post-4-week intervention. 16S V3–V4 on Illumina HiSeq2500. PICRUSt2 for KEGG functional prediction. TSQ (Thyroid Symptom Questionnaire) for complications.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Direct evidence of **gut-thyroid axis** bidirectionality: thyroid state (THW → iatrogenic hypothyroidism) shifts gut + oral microbiome; probiotic restoration mitigates clinical complications.
- Informs **[[hashimotos-thyroiditis]]** entity: hypothyroid state (whether autoimmune or iatrogenic) perturbs microbiome in consistent direction (loss of diversity, Fusobacterium bloom, Prevotella shifts).
- Supports **[[oral-microbiome]]** concept page: dry mouth → oral dysbiosis → systemic inflammation loop.
- Informs **[[gut-thyroid-axis]]** concept: THW-induced hypercholesterolemia partially microbiome-mediated.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Validated intervention**: Multi-strain probiotic (B. infantis + L. acidophilus + E. faecalis + B. cereus) for **THW-period symptom management** in DTC patients. I→D evidence: RCT (n=39, single-center, moderate bias).
- **Mechanistic triangle**: I→f (probiotics restore α-diversity, reduce Fusobacterium/Prevotella_9); f→D (dysbiosis drives constipation, dyslipidemia, dry mouth via LPS, motility, bile acid pathways); I→D (clinical symptom reduction RCT-graded).
- **Extension hypothesis**: Similar probiotic regimen may benefit **hypothyroid Hashimoto's patients** with constipation and dyslipidemia — not directly tested here but mechanistically plausible.

## Open Questions

- Does probiotic benefit persist post-radioiodine and during lifelong levothyroxine?
- Are female DTC patients (the majority) equally responsive?
- Which specific strain(s) in the 4-strain cocktail drive the effect — dissection not performed.
