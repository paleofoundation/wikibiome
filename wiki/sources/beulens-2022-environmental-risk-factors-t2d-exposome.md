# Key Claims

- **Air pollution, residential noise, area-level socioeconomic deprivation** associated with increased T2D risk
- **Neighbourhood walkability and green space** consistently associated with reduced T2D risk
- **Environmental factors affect T2D risk** mainly through physical activity, diet, microbiome, inflammation, or chronic stress
- **The 'exposome'** represents measurable totality of environmental, nongenetic drivers of health and disease
- **Internal exposome** comprises epigenetic, transcript, proteome, microbiome, and metabolome measurements

# Methods & Findings

Review of environmental risk factors for T2D using exposome framework:

- External exposome: built environment, social environment, physico-chemical environment, lifestyle/food environment
- Internal exposome: biological imprints of external exposures
- Future studies should focus on longitudinal effects and causal assessment

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Environmental zinc exposure (soil, water, air) is part of the exposome affecting T2D risk via microbiome
- **[[iron]]**: Environmental iron exposure (water supply, cookware) contributes to the metal exposome
- **[[selenium]]**: Geographic selenium variation is part of the physico-chemical environment affecting T2D risk
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: The exposome framework should explicitly include metal exposures as mediators of microbiome-disease links
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Environmental exposures drive [[dysbiosis]] through multiple metal-dependent pathways
