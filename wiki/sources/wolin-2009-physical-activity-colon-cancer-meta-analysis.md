# Physical Activity and Colon Cancer Prevention: A Meta-Analysis

## Key Claims

- Physical activity is inversely associated with colon cancer risk with an overall RR of 0.76 (95% CI 0.72-0.81), representing a 24% risk reduction for the most physically active individuals
- For men, the RR was 0.76 (95% CI 0.71-0.82); for women, slightly less protective at RR 0.79 (95% CI 0.71-0.88), though the difference was not statistically significant
- Case-control studies showed stronger associations (RR 0.69, 95% CI 0.65-0.74) compared to cohort studies (RR 0.83, 95% CI 0.78-0.88), potentially reflecting recall bias in case-control designs
- The inverse association has remained consistent over time despite changes in physical activity assessment methods, and was similar when stratified by decade of publication
- Occupational physical activity showed stronger protective effects than recreational/leisure-time activity in some analyses, though both types demonstrated significant risk reduction

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of 52 studies examining physical activity and colon cancer risk
- **Inclusion criteria**: Case-control or cohort studies with colon cancer-specific endpoint (excluding combined colorectal or rectal-only studies); data reported separately for colon cancer
- **Methods**: Random effects models for pooled estimates; heterogeneity assessed by Q statistic; publication bias evaluated by Begg and Mazumdar rank correlation test
- **Studies**: 33 case-control studies and 19 cohort studies published through June 2008
- **Biological mechanisms proposed**: Decreased inflammation, reduced intestinal transit time, decreased insulin-like growth factor levels, reduced hyperinsulinemia, modulated immune function
