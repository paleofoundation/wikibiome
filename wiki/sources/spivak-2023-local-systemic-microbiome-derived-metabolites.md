# Local and Systemic Effects of Microbiome-Derived Metabolites

## Summary

Comprehensive review from the Weizmann Institute examining the differential roles of key microbial metabolites in their local environment of production and their systemic effects via portal and systemic circulation. Covers [[SCFAs]], [[bile-acids]], [[TMAO]], BCAAs, tryptophan metabolites, LPS, flavonoids, and other classes across multiple organ systems including the cardiovascular system.

## Key Findings

### Classification of Microbial Metabolites (Table 1)
- **SCFAs** (butyrate, propionate, acetate): From dietary carbohydrates; implicated in intestinal inflammation, metabolic syndrome, adiposity, hypertension, atherosclerosis, ischemic stroke, CKD, type 1 diabetes
- **Carboxylic acid intermediates** (lactate, succinate): Metabolic syndrome, intestinal epithelial regeneration
- **Amino acids and derivatives** (BCAAs, niacin, nicotinamide, 5-aminovaleric acid): Adiposity, CVD events, atherosclerosis, MI, stroke
- **TMAO**: From host metabolism of microbial TMA; metabolic syndrome, type 2 diabetes, liver steatosis, atherosclerosis, MI, ischemic stroke
- **LPS and PRR ligands**: Intestinal inflammation, infection, carcinogenesis, adiposity, liver steatosis, acute liver failure, thrombosis, metabolic syndrome, type 1 diabetes
- **Tryptophan metabolites** (I3PA, indole-3-aldehyde, indoxyl sulfate, tryptamine, IPA): Intestinal inflammation, fungal infection, hypertension, CKD, type 1 diabetes, atopic dermatitis
- **Secondary bile acids** (LCA, DCA): Liver steatosis, inflammation, fibrosis, metabolic syndrome, adiposity, colon cancer

### Local Effects: Intestinal Barrier Function
- SCFAs maintain barrier stability via HDAC inhibition and GPR activation
- Bile acids and lactic acid contribute to pH maintenance preventing bacterial translocation
- TLR-ligands from bacteria contribute to fortifying intestinal barrier
- Tryptamine can promote intestinal motility through serotonin receptor activation

### Systemic Effects on Cardiovascular System (Figure 2)
- CVS: TMAO, SCFAs, BCAAs, and tryptophan metabolites are key systemic mediators
- Kidneys: SCFAs and indoxyl-sulfate
- Liver: TMAO, SCFAs, bile acids
- Adipose tissue: SCFAs, bile acids, amino acids, TLR/NLR-ligands
- CNS: Amyloids, amino acids, nicotinamide

### Epithelial Cell Renewal and Cancer
- Microbial metabolites can trigger proliferative signals facilitating tumor development
- Deoxycholic acid (bacterial bile acid deconjugation product) can trigger intestinal adenoma-carcinoma pathway
