# Analysis of the gut microbiota in children with gastroesophageal reflux disease using metagenomics and metabolomics

## Key Claims

- Children with [[GERD]] showed significantly different [[gut microbiota]] diversity and composition compared to healthy controls, with decreased Shannon and Simpson indices indicating reduced alpha diversity
- Dominant bacteria in children with GERD were [[Proteobacteria]] and [[Bacteroidota]], with the top three core species being *[[Bacteroides stercoris]]*, *[[Bacteroides vulgatus]]*, and *[[Alistipes putredinis]]*
- LC/MS detected 288 different metabolites between GERD children and controls, mainly involved in [[arachidonic acid]], [[tyrosine]], [[glutathione]], and caffeine metabolism
- At the genus level, *Bacteroides*, *Prevotella-9*, *Escherichia-Shigella*, *Klebsiella*, and *Haemophilus* were significantly higher in GERD children, while *[[Bifidobacterium]]*, *[[Faecalibacterium]]*, and *[[Streptococcus]]* were lower
- Metagenomic functional analysis showed higher energy, amino acid, vitamin, carbohydrate, and lipid metabolic pathway activities in GERD group, while glycan synthesis and metabolic pathways were higher in controls
- Differences in metabolites are related to specific changes in bacterial abundance, suggesting GERD may be treated by targeting specific bacteria related to arachidonic acid metabolism

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional study combining 16S rRNA sequencing, shotgun metagenomics, and nontargeted metabolomics
- **Population**: 30 children with GERD and 30 healthy controls, ages 3-14 years, Beijing Children's Hospital
- **16S rRNA**: 834 OTUs total; 2250 OTUs specific to GERD, 595 specific to controls; significant beta diversity separation (ANOSIM, unweighted UniFrac)
- **Metagenomics**: 10 GERD + 10 HC; 858,667 genes total; significant separation at species level on Bray-Curtis PCoA
- **Metabolomics**: LC/MS on serum samples from 28 GERD + 28 HC children; UHPLC-MS/MS analysis; VIP>1, P<0.05, FC>=2 or FC<=0.5 criteria
- **LEfSe analysis**: *Bifidobacteriales*, *Bifidobacteriaceae*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Lachnospiraceae*, *Blautia*, *Clostridia*, *Firmicutes* enriched in controls; *Bacteroidales*, *Proteobacteria*, *Prevotellaceae*, *Gammaproteobacteria* enriched in GERD
- **Metabolic pathways**: Energy, amino acids, vitamins, carbohydrates, lipids higher in GERD; cell motility, inorganic ion transport, membrane biogenesis more impaired
