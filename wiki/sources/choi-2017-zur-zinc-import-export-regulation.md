# Choi & Lee 2017 — Zur Controls Both Zinc Import and Export Genes

## Key Findings
- **Zur operates as a bidirectional zinc rheostat**, repressing import genes at low zinc (femtomolar sensing) and activating export genes at high zinc (micromolar sensing) [[choi-2017-zur-zinc-import-export-regulation]]
- This dual-phase regulation means a single protein manages the entire zinc window — from starvation to toxicity
- The two sensing thresholds span six orders of magnitude, revealing extraordinary precision in bacterial metal homeostasis

## Relevance to WikiBiome
- Demonstrates that bacterial zinc management is far more sophisticated than simple on/off switches — pathogens can fine-tune responses across a huge concentration range
- Informs Primitive 4: understanding these regulatory systems reveals which concentration windows are vulnerable to therapeutic disruption
