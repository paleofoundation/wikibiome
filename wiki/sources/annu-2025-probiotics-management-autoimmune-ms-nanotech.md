# Probiotics in the Management of Autoimmune Disorders (Multiple Sclerosis): A New Approach

## Summary

This review examines the role of [[probiotics]] in managing autoimmune CNS disorders, specifically [[multiple-sclerosis]], with particular attention to the emerging field of probiotic nanoencapsulation and precision probiotics as next-generation therapeutic approaches.

## Key Concepts

### Probiotics Mechanisms in Autoimmune CNS Disorders
- Probiotics influence immune function by activating signaling pathways that increase anti-inflammatory cytokines and growth factors
- Facilitate Treg differentiation and engage with the [[gut-brain-axis]] through endocrine regulation
- Bacterial (*[[bacillus|Bacillus]]*, *Streptococcus*, *Lactobacillus*, *Enterococcus*, *Pediococcus*) and fungal (*Saccharomyces cerevisiae*, *Kluyveromyces*) probiotic sources are reviewed

### Nanotechnology for Probiotic Delivery
- Clinical effectiveness of probiotics is limited by viability loss during GI transit
- Nanocarrier encapsulation improves absorption and colonization in the gut
- Selenium and gold nanoparticles used for probiotic encapsulation
- Nanofibers emerging as novel delivery techniques due to greater porosity and surface area
- Site-specific delivery could enhance gut-brain communication

### MS-Specific Evidence
- Probiotic supplementation modestly improved bowel symptoms in RRMS patients in open-labeled RCT
- Animal studies show probiotics can reduce EAE severity
- *Prevotella* from human gut suppresses EAE induction
- Changes in intestinal microbiota composition associated with high Th17 levels and enhanced disease activity in MS

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

The nanotechnology approaches described here could be particularly relevant for addressing [[heavy-metals]]-induced [[dysbiosis]] in MS. Nanoencapsulated probiotics with metal-binding properties could simultaneously deliver beneficial bacteria and chelate toxic metals in the gut, providing dual therapeutic benefit. The precision probiotics concept aligns with personalized approaches that account for individual metal exposure profiles and their effects on [[gut-microbiome]] composition.

## Limitations

- Review article from a relatively new journal
- Nanotechnology applications are mostly preclinical
- Limited direct MS clinical trial data presented
