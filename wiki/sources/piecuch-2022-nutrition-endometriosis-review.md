# Key Claims

- Nutrition may be involved in the development and severity of [[endometriosis]], which affects approximately 10% of the female population
- Dietary factors such as polyphenols, vitamins C, D, and E, PUFAs, and [[iron]] play important roles in endometriosis progression
- Alternative diets including Mediterranean, anti-inflammatory, vegetarian, [[low-nickel-diet]], low-FODMAP, and gluten-free diets may have beneficial effects on endometriosis symptoms
- [[nickel]] in foods such as tomatoes, beans, whole grains, nuts, shellfish, garlic, onion, soy, corn, and tea may cause allergic contact mucositis (ACM) with IBS-like symptoms, which overlap with endometriosis GI symptoms
- A [[low-nickel-diet]] might significantly decrease not only gastrointestinal and extraintestinal symptoms but also gynecological symptoms in endometriosis patients

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative research review (non-systematic)
- **Polyphenols and phytoestrogens**: Resveratrol shows pro-apoptotic, anti-invasive, and anti-angiogenic effects on endometriotic cells in vitro; isoflavone and lignan intake inversely correlated with endometriosis risk
- **Vitamins C and E**: Supplementation confirmed to lower systemic indicators of [[oxidative-stress]] in endometriosis patients (randomized triple-blind placebo-controlled trial)
- **Vitamin D**: Supplementation may reduce pelvic pain; linked to inflammation control via 25(OH)D reserve
- **Omega-3 PUFAs**: Higher serum EPA levels associated with 82% less risk of endometriosis development; omega-3 diminished pain in experimental models
- **Red meat**: Consumption of >2 portions/day associated with 56% greater risk of laparoscopically confirmed endometriosis (Nurses' Health Study II)
- **Iron**: Deregulated iron homeostasis may be crucial in endometriotic lesion pathophysiology; iron overload in peritoneal fluid causes embryotoxicity via ferroptosis
- **Low-nickel diet**: Referenced the Borghini et al. 2020 pilot study showing that a [[low-nickel-diet]] significantly improved all GI, extra-intestinal, and gynecological symptoms in endometriosis patients with [[nickel-allergy]] (Ni ACM)
- **Low-FODMAP diet**: May benefit endometriosis patients with gut symptoms but overlaps with low-nickel diet, complicating interpretation
- **Recommended foods**: Vegetables, fruits, spices/herbs, white/green tea, dairy, fish, vegetable oils/nuts/seeds
- **Contraindicated foods**: Processed/unprocessed red meat, zoonotic fats (butter, lard), coffee >300g caffeine/day, highly processed foods

# Connections

- [[nickel]] -- dietary nickel identified as a potential contributor to IBS-like symptoms in endometriosis via allergic contact mucositis
- [[nickel-allergy]] -- Ni ACM prevalence may be high in endometriosis patients, suggesting a causative role in GI symptoms
- [[dietary-nickel-exposure]] -- high-nickel foods (tomatoes, cocoa, beans, whole grains, nuts) overlap with many "healthy" plant-based foods recommended for endometriosis
- [[oxidative-stress]] -- vitamins C and E reduce oxidative stress markers; iron overload promotes ferroptosis in peritoneal fluid
- [[metabolic-syndrome]] -- obesity paradox in endometriosis (inverse BMI relationship); dairy consumption may reduce inflammatory markers
- [[inflammation]] -- anti-inflammatory diet (low DII score) appropriate for endometriosis; polyphenols inhibit COX activation
- [[low-nickel-diet]] -- identified as one of several alternative dietary interventions for endometriosis symptom management
