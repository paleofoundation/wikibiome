# Gut Microbiome and Cardiometabolic Comorbidities in People Living with HIV

## Summary

Review of gut microbiome alterations in people living with HIV (PLWH) and their relationship to cardiometabolic [[comorbidities]] including [[cardiovascular-disease]]. Despite modern ART, PLWH have increased relative risk of inflammatory-driven comorbidities including CVD.

## Key Findings

### HIV-Related Microbiome Dysbiosis
- PLWH have an altered gut microbiome even after adjusting for confounders including sexual preference
- HIV-related microbiome shares features with CVD-related profiles: reduced capacity for [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] generation
- Depletion of *Lachnospiraceae* and *Ruminococcaceae*; enrichment of *Desulfovibrionaceae*
- HIV-associated dysbiosis among strongest disease-associated dysbioses across 20 diseases (American Gut Project analysis)

### Microbial Translocation and CVD
- LPS, CD14, and LBP are prominently elevated in PLWH and serve as markers of microbial translocation
- Microbial translocation triggers [[inflammation]] via TLR4 activation on monocytes
- HIV-associated dysbiosis significantly greater in PLWH who went on to develop CVD vs. those who did not

### Short-Chain Fatty Acids
- SCFAs are key gut microbial metabolites with cardiovascular protective functions
- SCFA production reduced in both HIV and CVD contexts
- Measuring SCFA in feces may be a poor surrogate for microbiome-mediated SCFA production; serum levels may be more informative
- Propionate levels in serum correlate with metagenomic-encoded SCFA production capacity

### Indole-3-Propionic Acid (IPA)
- IPA is a protective metabolite produced by *Peptostreptococcus* and *Clostridium* species
- Diminished in atherosclerosis patients; associated with lower IPA serum levels
- Crucial for reducing atherosclerosis burden via ABCA1 and miR-142-5p levels in macrophages

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Comorbidity model** - HIV-CVD comorbidity provides a framework for understanding how chronic immune activation and [[dysbiosis]] converge to accelerate cardiometabolic disease
2. **Translocation biomarkers** - LPS, CD14, and LBP as markers applicable to metal-induced gut barrier damage scenarios
3. **SCFA measurement** - Highlights limitations of fecal SCFA measurement and importance of serum-based approaches
