# Key Claims

- **PPD affects about 15% of women** within 1 year after giving birth with significant negative consequences for offspring
- **Biological markers** identified using multi-omics: HPA axis dysregulation, reproductive hormone withdrawal, [[inflammation]], tryptophan catabolites, indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase, serotonin, and autoimmunity
- **Strongest biological predictors**: HPA axis dysregulation, inflammatory processes, and genetic vulnerabilities
- **EPDS and PHQ-9** are most widely used screening tools (score >= 10 as cutoff for PPD positive)
- **Biological vs. psychological models** remain largely disconnected in the literature

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Review of PPD diagnosis, etiological models, and biomarker identification approaches
- **Key models**: Withdrawal model (hormone decline), depression model (stress hormones/cortisol), integrated stress-vulnerability model
- **Multi-omics approaches**: Genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics for biomarker discovery
