# Minerbi 2020 — Gut Microbiome: Pertinence in Fibromyalgia (Review)

## Key Findings

- **FM patients have altered gut bacterial community** with specific profiles correlated to symptom severity — multiple studies now confirm this across populations ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]], review).
- **FMT from FM patients to germ-free mice** induced pain hypersensitivity — the strongest evidence for causal directionality from microbiome to FM symptoms ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- **Key depleted taxa in FM:** Lachnospiraceae family (butyrate producers), Faecalibacterium prausnitzii ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- **Putative mechanisms** for gut microbiome → FM symptoms: (1) SCFAs entering host circulation (butyrate as analgesic via pain-related receptors and ion channels); (2) bile acids; (3) neurotransmitters produced by bacteria (GABA, serotonin); (4) bacterial antigens crossing intestinal barrier ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- **Butyrate** exhibits analgesic properties by modulating pain-related receptors and ion channels in sensory neurons ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- Certain gut bacteria produce **GABA and serotonin**, which can influence pain perception and modulate CNS activity ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]]).
- The composition of the gut microbiome in FM is **similar to IBS** (depleted Lachnospiraceae, altered SCFA metabolism) — suggesting shared pathophysiology ([[minerbi-2020-gut-microbiome-pertinence-fibromyalgia-review]]).

## Methods

Review in Clinical and Experimental Rheumatology (Suppl. 123). Synthesizes evidence from the landmark Minerbi 2019 study (n=156 FM + 77 controls), FMT mouse models, and gut-brain axis mechanistic literature.
