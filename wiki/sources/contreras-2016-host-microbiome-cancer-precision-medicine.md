# Contreras 2016 -- Host-Microbiome Interaction and Cancer: Precision Medicine

## Key Claims

- Host-microbial interactions contribute to proper functional activity through signal transduction, immunity, and metabolism; dysbiosis correlates with cancer genesis and evolution
- Ecological interactions with microorganisms have become significant for cancer diagnosis and treatment through systems biology approaches integrating clinical knowledge, HT data, bioinformatics
- The review comprehensively compares four NGS platforms (Illumina, Ion Torrent, PacBio, Oxford Nanopore) for microbiome analysis, with Table 1 detailing template preparation, sequencing chemistry, read length, throughput, advantages, and limitations
- Both 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing and shotgun metagenomics are critical for microbiome profiling; the bioinformatics workflow includes data acquisition, QC, alignment, OTU clustering, diversity assessment, functional assignment, and pathway reconstruction
- Metagenomics provides species-level resolution that 16S cannot achieve; QIIME, MICCA, Phyloseq, MetAMOS, MetaTrans, MOCAT2, COMAN, MEGAN are key analysis tools

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Review of HT sequencing technologies and computational methods for microbiome-cancer research
- **Figure 1**: Complete bioinformatics workflow from data acquisition through microbiome profiling
- **Key comparison**: 16S rRNA (amplicon) vs. shotgun metagenomics approaches, with detailed discussion of OTU clustering algorithms (UPARSE, SLP, bioOTU, UCLUST, DNACLUST)
