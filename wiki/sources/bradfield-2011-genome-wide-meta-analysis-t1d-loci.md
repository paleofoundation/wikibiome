# Key Claims

- **~200 million people affected by diabetes worldwide**, ~10% are type 1
- **GWAS has revealed >40 genetic loci** associated with T1D susceptibility
- **Three new loci with genome-wide significance** identified: rs539514 on 13q22 (LMO7), rs478222 on 2p23 (EFR3B), rs924043 on 6q27
- **Region of linkage disequilibrium** on 2p23 harbors multiple genes including NCOA1, C2orf79, CENPO, ADCY3, DNAJC27, POMC, and DNMT3A
- **Latest associated regions** add to growing repertoire of gene networks predisposing to T1D

# Methods & Findings

Meta-analysis of ~2.54 million SNPs in combined cohort of 9,934 cases and 16,956 controls:

- Targeted follow-up of 53 SNPs in 1,120 affected trios
- Three new loci reached genome-wide significance
- Regions harbor genes involved in immune regulation, beta-cell function

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: DNMT3A (identified near T1D locus) is a zinc-dependent DNA methyltransferase -- zinc availability directly affects epigenetic regulation of immune genes relevant to autoimmunity
- **[[iron]]**: Iron-dependent transcription factors may interact with identified T1D susceptibility loci to modify disease penetrance
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Multiple identified loci encode immune regulators -- their interaction with [[gut-metal-microbiome]] determines whether genetic risk translates to clinical T1D
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenoprotein genes interact with HLA variants to modulate oxidative stress in beta-cells
