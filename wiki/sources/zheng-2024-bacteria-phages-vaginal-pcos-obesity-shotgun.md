# Differential Enrichment of Bacteria and Phages in Vaginal Microbiomes in PCOS and Obesity

## Key Findings

- **Both obesity and PCOS are associated with vaginal microbial dysbiosis**, but with distinct enrichment patterns — obesity and PCOS drive different microbial community shifts rather than a single shared dysbiotic state ([[zheng-2024-bacteria-phages-vaginal-pcos-obesity-shotgun]], cross-sectional).
- **[[lactobacillus]] bacteria, Lactobacillus phage, and Pseudomonas phage** identified as indicators of a healthy vaginal microbiome, suggesting that bacteriophage diversity supports microbial ecosystem stability.
- **Vaginal phage diversity correlates with healthy microbiota** — reduced phage diversity in PCOS and obesity may contribute to bacterial community destabilization.
- **[[pseudomonas-aeruginosa]]-associated phages** were among healthy vaginal indicators, suggesting phages may control potentially pathogenic bacteria in the healthy state.

## Methods

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional observational study
- **Population**: Women with PCOS, women with obesity (without PCOS), and healthy controls
- **Key measurements**: Shotgun metagenomic sequencing of vaginal samples; taxonomic and viral profiling; differential abundance analysis
- **Distinguishing feature**: Simultaneous characterization of bacterial and viral (phage) communities — most vaginal microbiome studies focus only on bacteria

## Detailed Summary

This study applied shotgun metagenomic sequencing to vaginal samples from women with PCOS, obesity, and healthy controls, uniquely characterizing both bacterial and phage communities simultaneously. The key finding is that PCOS and obesity drive distinct vaginal dysbiotic signatures rather than a single shared pattern. **[[lactobacillus]]** dominance in healthy vaginal microbiomes is well established, but this study extends that by showing Lactobacillus phages are also enriched in healthy samples — suggesting a predator-prey dynamic that maintains community stability. The identification of Pseudomonas phages as health indicators is intriguing: these phages may keep potentially pathogenic [[pseudomonas-aeruginosa]] populations in check. In disease states (PCOS and obesity), both bacterial diversity and phage diversity shift, with loss of the Lactobacillus-dominated, phage-rich ecosystem. The distinct patterns between PCOS and obesity suggest different hormonal and metabolic drivers reshape the vaginal microbiome through different mechanisms — hyperandrogenism in PCOS vs. metabolic/inflammatory pathways in obesity.
