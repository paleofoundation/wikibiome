# The Blood Microbiome and Its Association to Cardiovascular Disease Mortality: Case-Cohort Study

## Summary

Case-cohort study based on the Oslo II study (9.5-year follow-up) analyzing bacterial DNA in blood samples from 227 men who died from CVD and 178 controls. Identified specific blood bacterial genera associated with CVD mortality using 16S rRNA sequencing.

## Key Findings

### Blood Bacterial Genera and CVD Mortality
- Bacterial DNA identified in 372 (82%) of blood samples across 78 genera from six phyla
- Three genera significantly associated with CVD mortality after Bonferroni correction:
  - *Kocuria* (skin/oral): HR 8.50 (95% CI 4.05-17.84) - increased risk
  - *Enhydrobacter* (skin): HR 3.30 (95% CI 2.01-5.57) - increased risk
  - *Paracoccus* (soil): HR 0.29 (95% CI 0.15-0.57) - decreased risk
- *Stenotrophomonas* (HR 0.35) and *Bacteroides* (HR 0.22) also negatively associated

### Other Risk Factors
- Diabetes (HR 3.10), history of heart failure (HR 2.30), and history of myocardial infarction (HR 2.20) were significant predictors
- Excessive coffee consumption (>3 cups/day) increased risk (HR 1.70)
- Subjective bad health was inversely related to all mortality levels

### Blood Microbiome Characteristics
- Most prevalent genus: *Streptococcus* (42.3% cases vs. 49.5% controls)
- *Staphylococcus* second most common (38.3% vs. 38.8%)
- *Enhydrobacter* significantly more prevalent in cases (34.8% vs. 13.1%)
- Periodontal-related genera (*Porphyromonas*, *Tannerella*, *Prevotella*) found in both groups

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Blood microbiome as CVD biomarker** - Circulating bacterial DNA levels and specific genera may serve as non-invasive [[microbial-biomarkers]] for cardiovascular mortality risk
2. **Bacterial translocation** - Supports the concept that gut barrier dysfunction permits bacterial migration into circulation, contributing to [[atherosclerosis]]
3. **Comorbidity interactions** - Demonstrates the interplay between [[comorbidities]] (diabetes, heart failure) and blood microbial burden in CVD
