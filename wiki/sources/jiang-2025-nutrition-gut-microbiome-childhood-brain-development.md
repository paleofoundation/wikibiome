# Key Claims

- **Gut-brain axis** mediates the relationship between nutrition, gut microbiome, and brain development through microbial modulation of inflammation, neuroactive compounds, and blood-brain barrier integrity
- **Healthy dietary patterns** (whole foods, high-fiber, minimally processed) play a crucial role in shaping gut microbiome and promoting microbial diversity
- **Disruptions in gut microbiota composition** have been linked to ASD, ADHD, and anxiety in children
- **Early life nutrition** particularly during prenatal and early postnatal periods is critical for neurodevelopment
- **First 1,000 days** marked by rapid brain growth with brain doubling in first year and growing 15% in second year

# Methods & Findings

**Review** summarizing current advances on how early-life nutrition and gut microbiota interaction influence brain development and childhood behaviors.

- Integrated findings from animal models, clinical trials, and epidemiological studies
- Highlighted the first 3 months as critical period of rapid metabolite changes in brain development
- N-acetyl-aspartate, creatine, and glutamine increase rapidly from birth to 3 months
- Breast milk and formula provide essential nutrients shaping early brain development
- Dietary interventions proposed to promote optimal neurodevelopmental health

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Critical windows** for nutritional influence on neurodevelopment relevant to ASD prevention
- **Maternal diet** as modifiable risk factor through microbiome-mediated mechanisms
- **Microbiome-nutrient-brain axis** as integrated framework for understanding ASD etiology
