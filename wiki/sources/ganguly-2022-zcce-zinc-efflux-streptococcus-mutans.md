# Ganguly & Peterson 2022 — ZccE Zinc Efflux Pump in Streptococcus mutans

## Key Findings
- **ZccE** is a novel P-type ATPase that confers zinc resistance in S. mutans by actively exporting intracellular zinc [[ganguly-2022-zcce-zinc-efflux-streptococcus-mutans]]
- Loss of ZccE renders S. mutans hypersensitive to zinc stress, confirming the pump is essential for surviving host nutritional immunity zinc bombardment
- The discovery reveals a pathogen counter-strategy against host zinc intoxication — the arms race between host metal flooding and bacterial metal efflux

## Relevance to WikiBiome
- Illustrates the host-pathogen metal arms race: the host floods infection sites with zinc (nutritional immunity, Primitive 2), and S. mutans evolved ZccE to survive it
- Identifies ZccE as a potential drug target — blocking the efflux pump would restore zinc toxicity against the pathogen (Primitive 4)
