# Investigation of the gut microbiota and adverse reactions associated with the combined bifidobacterium, lactobacillus, enterococcus and bacillus cereus tablets and rabeprazole in patients suffering from gastroesophageal reflux

## Key Claims

- Combined [[bifidobacterium]] quadruplex viable tablets with [[rabeprazole]] significantly improved [[gut-microbiome]] balance in [[gerd]] patients compared to rabeprazole alone
- After treatment, *Bifidobacterium* and *Lactobacillus* counts in the experimental group were significantly higher than the control group (P<0.05), while *Saccharomyces* and *Enterococcus* showed no significant difference
- GerdQ scores decreased significantly in both groups (from ~10.5 to ~6.5, P<0.01), but the experimental group showed a slightly lower post-treatment score (6.53 vs 6.43)
- Inflammatory markers (IL-6, IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, [[inflammation|CRP]]) all decreased after treatment in both groups, with CRP showing a linear correlation with GerdQ scores (R^2=0.65)
- The total incidence of adverse reactions (enteritis, abdominal pain, loss of appetite) was significantly decreased in the probiotic group (P<0.01 vs control), demonstrating improved medication safety
- CRP levels in the experimental group decreased from 10.23 to 3.54 pg/ml, more than the control group (8.92 to 3.73 pg/ml)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Randomized controlled trial
- **Population**: 60 GERD patients randomized to control (rabeprazole alone, n=30) and experimental (rabeprazole + CBLEB probiotic, n=30) groups; treated for 8 weeks
- **Probiotic**: Combined *Bifidobacterium*, *Lactobacillus*, *Enterococcus*, and *Bacillus cereus* tablets (Hangzhou Yuanda Biopharmaceutical), 0.5g, 1.5g three times daily
- **PPI**: Rabeprazole sodium enteric-coated capsules (Jizhun Pharmaceutical), 10mg, specification 20mg, once daily 30 minutes before meals
- **Outcomes**: Gut microbiota counts (lg CFU/g), GerdQ questionnaire scores, inflammatory markers (IL-6, IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, CRP), adverse reactions
- **Microbiota results**: Experimental group Bifidobacterium increased from 6.3 to 9.2 (lg CFU/g); Lactobacillus from 6 to 7.6; control group showed smaller increases
