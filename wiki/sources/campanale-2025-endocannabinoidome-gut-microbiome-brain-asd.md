# Overview

Review examining therapeutic targets within the **endocannabinoidome (eCBome)-gut microbiome (GM)-brain axis** for ASD. The eCB system -- comprising endocannabinoids (anandamide, 2-AG), CB1/CB2 receptors, and over 100 mediators -- is bidirectionally linked to the gut microbiome and offers novel intervention strategies.

# Key Findings

## eCBome Alterations in ASD

- Reduced levels of **oleoylethanolamide** and **anandamide** and altered CB1/CB2 receptor activity may contribute to ASD symptoms
- FAAH and MAGL inhibition may also induce **ADHD-like behaviors** reversible by CB1 inverse agonists
- Targeting metabotropic glutamate receptor 5 via eCBome mediator **2-AG** may benefit ASD-related behaviors

## Therapeutic Targets

- **FAAH inhibitors**: may improve repetitive, stereotypical, and sensory behaviors; alleviate sociability impairments, depression, and anxiety
- **CB1/CB2 modulation**: 2-AG, 1/2-palmitoylglycerol, and palmitoylethanolamide may manage GI and systemic inflammation
- **Cyclooxygenase-2/prostaglandin E2 pathway**: may address hyperactivity and repetitive behaviors
- **Mucin-degrading genera** (*Akkermansia*, *Ruminococcus*): may improve ASD-related GI hypersensitivity

## Gut Microbiome-eCBome Interactions

- GM strains affect eCB system; some reported to influence eCB levels and exert anti-inflammatory effects
- Selective antibiotics against specific *Clostridium* strains may improve irritability and aggression
- Modulating CB1 and CB2 receptors and bacterial families like **Ruminococcaceae** and **Lachnospiraceae** may offer therapeutic value

## Animal Model Evidence

- Germ-free mice show absence of microbiome affects brain function and behavior
- FMT from healthy donors to GF mice: improved social behaviors
- Mono-colonization experiments: single bacterial species can pinpoint effects on brain function
