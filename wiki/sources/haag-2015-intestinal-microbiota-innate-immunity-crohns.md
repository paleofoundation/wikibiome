# Key Claims

- Chronic intestinal [[inflammation]] in Crohn's disease results from a dysregulated immune response toward commensal intestinal microbiota in genetically susceptible individuals
- NOD2 mutations (the strongest CD genetic risk factor) lead to deficient muramyl dipeptide sensing, reduced alpha-defensin production, and impaired bacterial clearance by Paneth cells
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] is consistently depleted in CD patients' mucosa, and its restoration after surgery is associated with maintained remission
- Adherent-invasive [[escherichia-coli]] (AIEC) is found in ileal mucosa of CD patients; it invades epithelial cells, replicates in macrophages, and induces granuloma-like structures
- A "predysbiosis" state may precede clinical manifestation of CD, as unaffected relatives of CD patients show subclinical dysbiosis

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of microbiota-innate immunity crosstalk in CD pathogenesis
- **Microbiota composition**: CD patients show reduced Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes, increased adherent bacteria; reduced SCFA-producing capacity; increased sulfate-reducing bacteria
- **Innate immune defects**: NOD2 frameshift mutations impair alpha-defensin secretion; ATG16L1 and IRGM variants impair autophagy in Paneth cells; FUT2 non-secretor status alters mucosal glycan landscape
- **Barrier dysfunction**: Reduced mucus layer thickness; increased bacterial adherence to mucosal surface; impaired tight junctions; decreased antimicrobial peptides (defensins, REG3gamma)
- **Clinical evidence table** (Table 2): Fistulae, mucosal translocation, altered mucosal microbiota, higher mucosa-associated bacteria all documented in CD
- **SCFAs and metabolites**: Butyrate from [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] suppresses NF-kappaB and increases anti-inflammatory IL-10; its depletion contributes to mucosal inflammation
- **AIEC mechanisms**: LF82 reference strain adheres via CEACAM6 receptor upregulated in inflamed ileum; triggers TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL-8 from macrophages

# Connections

- Core mechanistic link between [[dysbiosis]] and [[inflammation]] through innate immune defects in Crohn's disease
- [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii]] as keystone commensal -- its loss directly reduces anti-inflammatory butyrate
- [[escherichia-coli]] AIEC as a pathogenic trigger requiring host genetic susceptibility (NOD2, ATG16L1)
- Paneth cell dysfunction links to [[zinc]] and other metal-dependent antimicrobial mechanisms (defensins require zinc)
- [[iron]] availability in the gut may influence AIEC colonization capacity
- Connects to [[gut-metal-microbiome]] through nutrient competition at the mucosal-luminal interface
