# Key Claims

- **No significant association** was found between maternal serum [[magnesium]] levels and postpartum depression risk (OR: 0.29, 95% CI: 0.04-1.77)
- PPD was significantly associated with **poorer communication skills** in offspring at 12 months (beta: 1.81, 95% CI: 0.32-3.30, p=0.02) after controlling for confounders
- No significant differences in infant **growth parameters** (weight-for-height, weight-for-age, height-for-age) were found between depressed and non-depressed mothers at birth, 6 months, or 12 months
- PPD prevalence in the study was **22.3%**, lower than prior Iranian studies but higher than North American rates

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Longitudinal sub-study of the PERSIAN Birth Cohort ("Isfahan Birth Cohort"), 2019-2020
- **Sample**: 224 mother-infant pairs from Isfahan, Iran
- **Exposure**: Maternal serum Mg levels measured at 38 weeks gestation (mean: 1.95 +/- 0.23 mg/dL)
- **Outcomes**: PPD assessed by EPDS (cutoff >= 12) at 4 weeks postpartum; infant growth at birth, 6, and 12 months; ASQ neurodevelopmental status at 6 and 12 months
- **Key Findings**:
  - Mean Mg was similar between depressed (1.91 mg/dL) and non-depressed (1.97 mg/dL) mothers (p=0.64)
  - Lower SES (p=0.02) and lower maternal education (p=0.004) were significantly associated with PPD
  - A significant depression-by-time interaction was found for communication skills (p=0.02), with depressed mothers' infants showing poorer progression
  - No significant associations in other ASQ domains (gross motor, fine motor, problem-solving, personal-social)
- **Response rate**: 93.4%

# Connections

- Contrasts with [[etebary-2010-ppd-serum-trace-elements]] which found roles for multiple trace elements including [[magnesium]] in PPD pathophysiology
- Supports findings from a Fard et al. RCT showing Mg supplementation did not reduce PPD symptoms
- The communication skills finding relates to broader literature on [[postpartum-depression]] effects on child development
- Relevant to [[aoki-2022-zinc-supplementation-ppd-anemia]] which examined another trace element intervention for PPD
- The null Mg finding may reflect that Mg levels within normal range are not a risk factor, consistent with [[magnesium]] deficiency rather than sufficiency being the concern
