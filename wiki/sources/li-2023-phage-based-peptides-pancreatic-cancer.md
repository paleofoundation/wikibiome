# Key Claims

- [[Phage therapy]] display technology offers a promising approach for developing novel peptide-based therapeutics for [[pancreatic cancer]] diagnosis and treatment through high-affinity target-binding peptides
- M13 and T7 phages are the most commonly used vectors for phage display, with M13 using non-lytic infection of *E. coli* and T7 using lytic infection allowing display of larger proteins
- Phage display biopanning can select peptides from libraries of up to 10^13 clones, enabling identification of tumor-targeting peptides with high selectivity and specificity
- Applications include targeted drug delivery, diagnostic imaging, early detection biomarkers, and cancer cell-specific therapeutic peptides

# Methods/Findings

- **Phage biology**: M13 phage -- rod-shaped, ~1000nm length, 5nm width, non-lytic; displays peptides on pIII (5 copies) or pVIII (~2700 copies); T7 phage -- icosahedral, lytic, displays on 10B capsid protein
- **Biopanning process**: 3-5 rounds of phage library incubation with target, washing, elution of bound phages, amplification in *E. coli*, and enrichment; affinity validated by ELISA
- **Screening methods**: In situ (plate/bead-based), in vitro cell selection, in vivo biopurification in live animals, ex vivo organ perfusion, and human screening approaches
- **Key advantages**: High selectivity, chemical modifiability, good tissue permeability, easy metabolism and excretion, cost-effectiveness
- **Challenges**: Optimizing selection process, improving pharmacokinetic properties, addressing immunogenicity, scale-up manufacturing
