# Association Between Serum Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone Levels and Salivary Microbiome Shifts

## Key Claims

- Higher serum TSH levels are associated with significantly greater salivary microbiome diversity and distinct community composition compared to normal TSH levels
- Granulicatella was identified as a discriminative genus for the Abnormal (high TSH) group, distinguishing it from the Normal group via LDA effect size analysis
- Participants with abnormally higher serum TSH levels showed insulin resistance (elevated fasting serum insulin, HOMA-IR, and hemoglobin A1C; all p<0.05), linking thyroid dysfunction to metabolic disturbance
- The salivary microbiome in high-TSH individuals showed higher taxa diversity (Shannon index significantly elevated, p<0.05) with distinct PCoA clustering (p=0.0320)
- Shifts in salivary microbial profile were observed in individuals with different serum TSH levels, suggesting the oral microbiome may play an important role in biochemical and microbial alterations associated with thyroid dysfunction

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Case-control 16S rDNA sequencing study
- **Participants**: 40 participants (aged 45-60, Southeast China health census): 20 Abnormal Group (TSH >4.2 mIU/L) and 20 Normal Group (TSH 0.6-4.2 mIU/L), case-control matched by sex and age
- **Methods**: Unstimulated saliva collected 8-10am; Illumina MiSeq PE300 16S rDNA sequencing (V3-V4 region)
- **Key results** (Table 1):
  - TSH: Abnormal 5.55 vs Normal 2.36 (p<0.0001)
  - TPOAb: Abnormal 16.79 vs Normal 5.73 (p=0.0256)
  - TG: Abnormal 2.22 vs Normal 1.57 (p=0.0373)
  - FSI (fasting serum insulin): Abnormal 5.31 vs Normal 3.76 (p=0.0447)
  - HOMA-IR: Abnormal 1.22 vs Normal 0.83 (p=0.0322)
- **Microbiome findings**:
  - 1,216,520 high-quality sequences; 21 phyla, 258 genera, 514 species, 978 OTUs identified
  - Abnormal Group: significantly greater community richness (Chao index, p<0.05)
  - Top phyla (>99%): Firmicutes, Bacteroidetes, Proteobacteria, Actinobacteria, Fusobacteria, Saccharibacteria
  - PCoA showed separation on PC1 axis (20.98% variance, p=0.0320)
  - PLS-DA confirmed distinct clustering between groups
