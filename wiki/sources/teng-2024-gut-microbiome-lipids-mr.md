# Causality of the Gut Microbiome and Atherosclerosis-Related Lipids: A Bidirectional Mendelian Randomization Study

## Summary

Bidirectional two-sample MR study examining causal relationships between 211 intestinal flora taxa and blood lipid traits (ApoA, ApoB, LDL-C, HDL-C, TG) using GWAS data from 441,016 UKBB participants. Found 19 intestinal flora with causal links to lipids.

## Key Findings

### Significant Causal Relationships
- **ApoA**: Genus *Ruminococcaceae* causally associated with higher ApoA levels
- **ApoB**: Genus *Christensenellaceae*, *Parasutterella*, *Terrisporobacter* positively correlated; family *Desulfovibrionaceae* and genus *Oscillospira* showed negative association
- **LDL-C**: *Oscillospira* associated with lower LDL-C; *Parasutterella* and *Terrisporobacter* with higher LDL-C
- **HDL-C**: Class *Erysipelotrichia*, family *Erysipelotrichaceae*, genus *Parabacteroides*, *Ruminococcaceae UCG010* associated with higher HDL-C
- **TG**: Genus *Dorea* associated with lower TG

### Bonferroni-Corrected Results
- Only *Desulfovibrionaceae* exhibited a stable inverse causality with ApoB (Estimate=-0.0418, P=0.0007)
- Reverse MR analysis showed no causal effect of lipids on intestinal flora

### Key Mechanistic Discussion
- *Desulfovibrio* binds human colonic mucin, enriched on mucosal surface
- Reduction of H2S is associated with atherosclerosis occurrence
- *Desulfovibrionaceae* can produce acetic acid; associated with lower BMI and waist circumference
- *Ruminococcaceae* and *Christensenellaceae* associated with favorable metabolic profiles

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Lipid-mediated CVD risk** - Metal-induced changes in *Desulfovibrionaceae* and *Ruminococcaceae* could causally affect lipid profiles and atherosclerosis risk
2. **H2S signaling** - [[cadmium]] and [[lead]] may reduce sulfate-reducing bacteria, diminishing cardioprotective H2S production
3. **ApoB as risk marker** - The causal link between gut flora and ApoB provides a measurable biomarker for microbiome-mediated [[cardiovascular-disease]] risk
