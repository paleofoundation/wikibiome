# Microbiome-Targeted Therapies for Parkinson's Disease

## Summary

This comprehensive review evaluates the current landscape of microbiome-targeted therapeutic strategies for [[parkinsons-disease]], covering [[probiotics]], prebiotics, synbiotics, [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]], postbiotics, phage therapy, and dietary interventions. It assesses the evidence base, mechanisms of action, and clinical translation potential for each approach.

## Key Findings

- [[probiotics]]: Multiple RCTs show improvements in constipation, some motor symptoms, and inflammatory markers
- Prebiotics: Promote SCFA production and selectively enrich depleted beneficial taxa
- [[fmt-intervention]]: Most comprehensive approach; early clinical trials show safety and feasibility
- Postbiotics (heat-killed bacteria, metabolite preparations): Offer standardized, safer alternatives to live organisms
- Phage therapy: Targeted elimination of specific pathogenic bacteria without disrupting commensals
- Dietary interventions: Mediterranean and high-fiber diets reshape PD microbiome toward healthier profiles
- Combination strategies may be needed for optimal therapeutic effect

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This therapeutic review maps the full intervention landscape for the gut-metal-microbiome axis. Each therapeutic modality addresses the axis differently: [[probiotics]] directly supply beneficial organisms that produce metal-regulatory metabolites ([[butyrate]], siderophores); prebiotics feed endogenous metal-handling commensals; FMT transplants the entire metal-metabolic ecosystem; phage therapy selectively removes metal-scavenging pathogens (Enterobacteriaceae). The postbiotics concept is particularly interesting for the metal axis, as heat-killed bacterial preparations may retain metal-binding capacity without the variability of live organisms. The combination approach -- addressing [[dysbiosis]] from multiple angles simultaneously -- is most likely to restore the complex metal homeostatic network disrupted in PD, involving [[iron]], [[nickel]], [[lead]], [[manganese]], [[short-chain-fatty-acids]], and [[neuroinflammation]].

## Limitations

- Review article synthesizing evidence of varying quality
- Most evidence from small trials or preclinical studies
- Head-to-head comparisons of different microbiome therapies lacking
- Personalized approaches likely needed but framework undefined
