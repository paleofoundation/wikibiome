# Lu 2021 — Maternal AHR Activation Protects Offspring from NEC

## Key Findings

- **AHR is reduced in NEC epithelium** across mouse, piglet, and human samples — identifying an epigenetically relevant target.
- **Maternal indole-3-carbinol (I3C)** administration activates fetal/neonatal AHR, suppresses TLR4 signaling, and **prevents experimental NEC** in offspring.
- **Breast milk itself activates AHR** on neonatal intestinal epithelium, providing mechanistic grounding for the protective effect of human milk.
- Cross-species validation strengthens translational relevance.

## Methods

Multi-model study: mouse NEC model, neonatal piglet model, human intestinal tissue analysis. I3C administered to dams; offspring challenged with experimental NEC.
