# Overview

Systematic review of **28 studies** (published 1966-July 2019) examining the gut microbiota in children with ASD compared to controls. Found that **Clostridium, Sutterella, Desulfovibrio, and Lactobacillus** were most consistently reported as elevated in ASD, but findings are heterogeneous across studies. Methodological variability in ASD diagnosis, gut site sampled, and laboratory methods precluded formal meta-analysis.

# Key Findings

## Study Characteristics

- 28 studies included from 898 initial records (PRISMA)
- Sample sizes: 12-104 participants per study
- Ages: 2-18 years across all studies
- 24 studies used fecal samples; 4 used mucosal biopsies
- Majority from USA (n = 11), Europe (n = 6), Asia (n = 8), Australia (n = 3)

## Consistently Reported Taxa

### Higher in ASD
- **[[clostridium]]**: most frequently reported as elevated
- **[[sutterella]]**: consistently higher in ASD
- **[[desulfovibrio]]**: multiple studies show enrichment
- **[[lactobacillus]]**: reported higher in several studies

### Inconsistent Findings
- Findings for each taxon "are however inconsistent across studies"
- No single taxon universally confirmed as ASD-associated
- Meta-analysis deemed inappropriate due to heterogeneity

## Quality Assessment (Newcastle-Ottawa Scale)

- Scores ranged from 1 to 8 (out of 9)
- Quality varied significantly across studies
- Selection, comparability, and exposure domains assessed

## Confounding Factors Identified

### Antibiotics
- >50% of studies excluded recent antibiotic users
- Duration of exclusion: 15 days to 3 months
- 8 studies did not address antibiotic use at all

### GI Symptoms
- Most studies collected GI symptom data via parent questionnaires
- Few used standardized assessment (ROME III, 6-GSI)
- 2 studies excluded all subjects with gut problems

### Diet
- Majority of studies did not adequately assess diet
- Some noted children had specific diets but effect not controlled
- Diet as major confound "due to generally poor assessment of these factors"

### Diagnostic Criteria for ASD
- Varied: DSM-IV, DSM-V, CARS, ADI-R, ADOS, ICD-10
- Some relied on psychiatrist/psychologist evaluation without standardized tools
- Heterogeneity in ASD subtypes included (Asperger's, PDD-NOS, etc.)

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**Moderate relevance** — identifies key taxa with metal metabolism implications:

- **[[clostridium]]**: produces [[p-cresol]], [[propionic acid]] — affects metal-dependent enzyme function
- **[[desulfovibrio]]**: sulfate-reducing bacteria that sequester [[iron]] and produce H2S
- **[[sutterella]]**: associated with intestinal inflammation that alters metal absorption
- **[[lactobacillus]]**: binds [[heavy metals]] on cell surfaces — may reflect compensatory response
- Methodological heterogeneity caution: no single taxon confirmed — need standardized protocols to assess metal-microbiome interactions

# Methodological Notes

- MEDLINE and EMBASE databases searched
- Quality assessment by 2 independent researchers
- Highlights critical need for standardized protocols
- Published 2020 — does not include more recent large-scale studies (e.g., Taroncher-Oldenburg 2023)
- UK-based research team (NHS Grampian, Glasgow Children's Hospital)
