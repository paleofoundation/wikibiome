# Proton pump inhibitors induced fungal dysbiosis in patients with gastroesophageal reflux disease

## Key Claims

- [[Mycobiota]] dysbiosis was present in [[GERD]] patients in both gastric mucosal and fecal mycobiota, regardless of [[proton pump inhibitor|PPI]] use
- [[Candida]] colonization of gastric mucosa was significantly increased after PPI treatment, with higher relative abundance in both short-term and long-term PPI users compared to untreated GERD patients
- LEfSe analysis identified *Candida* at the genus level as a biomarker for the short-term PPI group compared to untreated GERD
- *[[Alternaria]]*, *[[Aspergillus]]*, *Monographella*, *Phallus*, and *Podospora* were significantly more abundant in long-term PPI users compared to untreated GERD patients
- PPI treatment may provide a suitable environment for *Candida* growth in gastric mucosa, and its enrichment may be a [[biomarker]] of PPI use in GERD patients
- The gastric mucosal fungi were mainly composed of Ascomycota, Basidiomycota, Chytridiomycota, and Glomeromycota at the phylum level

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Cross-sectional study
- **Population**: 65 participants in 4 groups: healthy controls (HC, n=12), untreated GERD (nt-GERD, n=21), short-term PPI users (s-PPI, n=14, 2 months to 1 year), long-term PPI users (l-PPI, n=18, >1 year average 2 years)
- **Sequencing**: ITS (Internal Transcribed Spacer) ITS1F-ITS2 region on Illumina MiSeq; UPARSE-OTU clustering at 97% identity
- **Gastric mucosal mycobiota**: 3,544,805 tags from gastric mucosa; 2,091 OTUs; HC and l-PPI had greater fungal richness than s-PPI
- **Fecal mycobiota**: 3,790,351 tags yielded 2,399 OTUs; significant differences in fecal mycobiota between HC and all GERD groups
- **Beta diversity**: Bray-Curtis PCoA showed significant separation (R=0.08, p=0.022); weighted UniFrac confirmed (R=0.12, p=0.004)
- **No difference** in *Candida* colonization between patients with and without endoscopically visible esophageal lesions
- **Fecal findings**: *Alternaria*, *Aspergillus*, *Mycenella*, *Exserohilum*, and *Clitopilus* enriched in s-PPI group fecal samples
