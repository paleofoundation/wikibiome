## Summary

A selective literature review examining the health effects of aluminum exposure, covering environmental/occupational exposure, neurotoxicity, Alzheimer's disease, and breast cancer. The review establishes reference values and threshold concentrations for aluminum toxicity, finding that the proven neurotoxic effect is the critical adverse endpoint.

## Key Findings

- Internal aluminum load reference values: <15 ug/L urine, <5 ug/L serum for general population
- EFSA tolerable weekly intake (TWI): 1 mg aluminum/kg body weight
- Only 0.1% of orally ingested aluminum is absorbed; bioavailability is limited
- Early signs of neurotoxicity appear at ~13 ug/L plasma and ~120 ug/L urine (occupational)
- Dialysis encephalopathy occurs at ~100 ug/L plasma
- Aluminum welders showed declining neuropsychological performance at concentrations post-shift of 120 ug/L
- Elevated aluminum content found in brains of Alzheimer's patients, but unclear whether cause or effect
- Meta-analysis of 8 cohort/case-control studies: OR 1.71 (95% CI: 1.35-2.18) for Alzheimer's disease with chronic aluminum exposure
- No consistent epidemiological evidence linking aluminum-containing antiperspirants to breast cancer
- Currently no definitive evidence for association between aluminum exposure and breast cancer or Alzheimer's disease

## Relevance to Wiki

Provides comprehensive toxicological reference values for aluminum and establishes the neurotoxicity threshold, relevant to understanding aluminum as a co-contaminant alongside nickel and other heavy metals in food and environmental exposures.
