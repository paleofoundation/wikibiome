# Overview

Cross-sectional study combining **fecal metabolomics, task-based fMRI, and behavioral assessments** in **43 ASD and 41 neurotypical children** (ages 8-17). Found that fecal levels of specific **tryptophan-related metabolites** (kynurenate, indolelactate, tryptophan betaine) were significantly lower in ASD and were associated with **alterations in insular and cingulate cortical activity** — brain regions critical for interoception. Activity in the mid-insula and mid-cingulate significantly **mediated the relationship between indolelactate/tryptophan betaine and ASD severity**.

# Key Findings

## Tryptophan Metabolite Differences

- **Kynurenate (KA)**: significantly lower in ASD (q = 0.02, two-tailed)
- **5-Hydroxytryptophan**: lower in ASD (Cohen's d = 0.49)
- **Indolelactate**: lower in ASD (Cohen's d = 0.41)
- **Indole-3-carboxylate**: lower in ASD (Cohen's d = 0.39)
- No significant group differences in overall SCFA or bile acid levels

## Brain Activity-Metabolite Associations (fMRI)

### Facial Expression Processing
- Higher **anthranilate** levels → increased right IFGop activity
- Higher **tryptophan betaine** → decreased right mid-cingulate (MCC) activity
- Higher **N-acetyltryptophan** → increased right MCC hand actions activity
- Higher **c-glycosyltryptophan** → increased right MCC non-emotional faces activity

### Disgust Processing
- Lower **indolelactate** → increased right mid-insula activity when viewing disgusting foods
- Higher **kynurenate (KA)** → increased left dorsal anterior insula activity during disgust facial expressions

### Somatosensory Processing (Touch)
- Higher **indole-3-carboxylate** → increased activity in right S1 and dorsal medial prefrontal cortex during object touch
- Decreased **indole-3-carboxylate** → activity in left posterior insula during social touch

## Mediation Analysis

- **Mid-insula activity during disgust processing** mediated the relationship between:
 - **Indolelactate** and ASD severity (ADOS RRB score): indirect effect Std. beta = 0.341, SE = 0.227, 95% CI [0.016, 0.967]
 - **Indolelactate** and ADOS total score: indirect effect Std. beta = 0.440, SE = 0.585, 95% CI [0.011, 2.26]

## Brain-Behavioral Correlations in ASD

- **Tryptophol**: significantly correlated with sensory sensitivities (vestibular/proprioception, social, non-social)
- **Anthranilate**: higher levels correlated with greater quality of sleep
- Right mid-insula activity during disgust: significantly associated with autism severity (ADOS-2 RRB, ADOS-2 total)

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**Very high relevance** — tryptophan pathway metabolites are produced by metal-dependent enzymes:

- **Kynurenate** depletion in ASD: kynurenine aminotransferase (KAT) is a [[tryptophan|PLP]]/[[zinc]]-dependent enzyme; reduced KA may reflect cofactor deficiency
- **Indolelactate** production: bacterial aromatic lactate dehydrogenase often requires metal cofactors
- **Indole-3-carboxylate**: product of bacterial indole oxidation — [[iron]]-dependent oxidases
- **AhR signaling** (discussed in paper): regulated by indole derivatives; AhR-mediated neuroprotection is [[iron]]-sensitive
- **Insular cortex** (key finding): hub for interoception — visceral sensation impaired when gut-derived metabolite signals disrupted
- Provides human neuroimaging evidence linking microbial metabolites to specific brain circuits affected in ASD

# Methodological Notes

- Published in Nature Communications — high-impact human neuroimaging study
- Task-based fMRI using validated socio-emotional and sensory paradigms
- Fecal metabolomics (untargeted) — comprehensive tryptophan pathway coverage
- Age range 8-17 — older than most ASD microbiome studies
- Cross-sectional — cannot establish causality
- Higher female ratio in ASD group than typical (important limitation noted)
- COVID-19 pandemic affected in-person data collection (fMRI limited)
