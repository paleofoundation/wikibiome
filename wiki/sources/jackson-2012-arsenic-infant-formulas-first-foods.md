## Key Claims

- [[arsenic]] is detectable in all infant formulas tested, with total As ranging from 2.2 to 12.6 ng/g (n=15)
- Non-dairy formulas were significantly higher in As than dairy-based formulas
- Arsenic in formula was almost exclusively [[inorganic arsenic]], the more toxic form
- Rice-fortified foods had significantly higher total As concentrations than non-rice-based foods
- Pear-containing fruit purees had notably high As concentrations (mean 16.6 ng/g), likely source-related rather than a property of pears generally
- Even ostensibly low As concentrations in food can exceed the WHO/EPA safe drinking water limit (0.17 ug/kg/d) when expressed on a per-kg body weight basis for infants
- The US has no regulations governing As concentration in foodstuffs, while China set a limit of 150 ng/g inorganic As for rice

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Measured total As and As speciation in 15 infant formulas (5 brands), 41 fruit/vegetable purees, and 18 stage 2/3 foods purchased from supermarkets in Hanover, NH
- **Analytical methods**: Total As by ICP-MS (7700x, Agilent) after acid digestion; speciation by anion-exchange chromatography coupled to ICP-MS (HPLC-ICP-MS) using 1% HNO3 extraction
- **Formula results**: Total As 2.2-12.6 ng/g; non-dairy > dairy (nested ANOVA F=13.3, P=0.003); speciation almost exclusively inorganic with major species As(V)
- **Puree results**: Total As 0.3-22 ng/g across 41 purees; fruit purees marginally higher than vegetable; pear-containing products drove differences (F=32.3, P<0.0001); inorganic As predominant (76-83% as As(III)+As(V))
- **Stage 2/3 foods**: Total As ranged from below detection to 22 ng/g; rice-containing and meat-containing foods had highest As; predominantly inorganic (>70%)
- **Exposure estimate**: A 3.5-month-old consuming 6 x 120-mL bottles daily would be exposed to 0.036-0.21 ug As/kg/d solely from formula, with the higher range exceeding the 0.17 ug/kg/d safe adult drinking water limit
- **Exposure from purees**: A 7-month-old eating 1.5 jars/day of pears at median concentration would be exposed to 0.2 ug/kg/d, above the safe adult level
