## Key Findings

- **Salivary pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1beta, IL-6, IL-8) and anti-inflammatory IL-10 were all elevated** in CP children with constipation, indicating both oral and systemic inflammatory activation.
- **GABA-ergic antiepileptic drugs were associated with increased constipation prevalence and elevated cytokine levels**, suggesting medication-driven GI dysfunction compounds the neurological condition.
- **Strong correlation between oral and systemic IL-1beta (R=0.720)** establishes the oral cavity as a window into systemic inflammatory status in CP — salivary biomarkers may serve as non-invasive monitoring tools.
- The oral-gut inflammation axis suggests CP involves multi-compartment immune dysregulation, not just neurological impairment.
