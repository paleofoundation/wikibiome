# Key Claims

- Adjuvant chemotherapy provides a 25% significant reduction in risk of death (HR = 0.75, 95% CI: 0.64-0.90, P = 0.001) for resected [[pancreatic-cancer]]
- Chemoradiation showed no significant difference in risk of death (HR = 1.09, 95% CI: 0.89-1.32, P = 0.43) compared to observation
- Median survival with chemotherapy was 19.0 months (95% CI: 16.4-21.1) vs 13.5 months (95% CI: 12.2-15.8) without
- Chemoradiation was more effective in patients with positive resection margins but chemotherapy was less effective in this subgroup

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Meta-analysis of 5 RCTs (GITSG, Norway, EORTC, Japan, ESPAC1) with individual patient data from 875 patients
- **Chemotherapy pooled analysis**: 2- and 5-year survival rates were 38% and 19% with chemotherapy vs 28% and 12% without
- **Chemoradiation pooled analysis**: 2- and 5-year survival rates 30% and 12% with CRT vs 34% and 17% without (excluding GITSG)
- **Patient characteristics**: 58% male, 55% age >60, 68% negative resection margins, 53% regional lymph node involvement
- **Trial heterogeneity**: Significant heterogeneity in chemotherapy trials (chi-squared = 11.7, P = 0.009) due to Japanese trial inclusion
