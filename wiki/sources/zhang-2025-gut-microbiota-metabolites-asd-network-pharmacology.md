# Overview

Network pharmacology and molecular docking study identifying **51 core target genes** through which gut microbiota-derived metabolites regulate ASD. Identified **AKT1** and **IL6** as key pivotal genes via protein-protein interaction network analysis, with key metabolites linked to the **PI3K/Akt** and **IL-17 signaling pathways**.

# Key Findings

## Target Identification

- Retrieved 278 gut microbial metabolites from gutMGene v2.0 database; predicted 755 metabolite target genes
- Intersected with 4,576 ASD-related genes from GeneCards and OMIM: **399 overlapping genes**
- Further cross-referenced with 238 human intestinal targets: **51 ASD-related host genes of gut microbial metabolites (AHGGMM)**

## Hub Gene Analysis

- PPI network: 114 nodes and 144 edges; top genes by four topological algorithms: **AKT1** and **IL6** had highest scores across Degree, EPC, MCC, and MNC
- AKT1: core component of PI3K/Akt pathway; impairment may lead to defective synaptic pruning and abnormal neuronal connectivity
- IL6: neuroinflammatory mediator; elevated in ASD, contributes to immune dysregulation

## Metabolite-Target Signaling Network

- Constructed MMTS network: 72 gut microbes, 12 metabolites, and 30 signaling pathways centered on AKT1 and IL6
- IL6 directly associated with 6 gut microbes and 5 gut-derived metabolites
- AKT1 associated with 3 metabolites: **vancomycin, glycocholic acid, and indole**

## Molecular Docking Results

- **Glycocholic acid** exhibited strongest binding to AKT1 (-10.2 kcal/mol)
- **3-indolepropionic acid** showed strongest binding to IL6 (-4.9 kcal/mol)
- Eight key metabolites (SCFAs and indole derivatives) linked to AKT1/IL6 regulation
- Drug similarity assessments confirmed safety of acetate, butyrate, propionate, and indole derivatives

## Enrichment Analysis

- GO enrichment: stress response, chemical stimulus response, organic substance response
- KEGG pathways: **PI3K-Akt**, **IL-17**, AGE-RAGE, Toll-like receptor signaling enriched
