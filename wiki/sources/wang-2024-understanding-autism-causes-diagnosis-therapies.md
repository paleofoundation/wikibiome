# Overview

Comprehensive review of **ASD etiology, biomarkers, and emerging therapeutic strategies**. Emphasizes rising prevalence, genetic-environmental interactions, current diagnostic limitations, and next-generation biomarker approaches and therapies.

# Epidemiology

- **Rising prevalence**: 1 in 36 children diagnosed in recent U.S. surveys
- **Critical public health issue** with substantial healthcare and societal burden
- Increasing trend suggests **combination of diagnostic improvement and true prevalence increase**

# Etiology: Genetic + Environmental Model

## Genetic Factors

- Multiple genetic loci associated with ASD
- **No single Mendelian mutation** for typical ASD
- **Polygenic risk scores** emerging but limited explanatory power
- **Most cases lack clear monogenic explanation**

## Environmental Factors (Key Findings)

- **Advanced parental age** (maternal, paternal)
- **Maternal diabetes during pregnancy** (metabolic dysregulation)
- **Prenatal infections** (viral, bacterial → inflammatory response)
- **Hormonal imbalances** (maternal androgens, estrogens)
- **Medication exposure** (some antiepileptic drugs, valproate)
- **Air pollution exposure** (environmental xenobiotics, oxidative stress)
- **Nutritional deficiencies** (prenatal and early postnatal)

## Gene-Environment Interactions

- Genetic predisposition + environmental trigger model
- **Developmental windows of vulnerability** (prenatal, early postnatal)
- Suggests **preventive strategies** may be possible with environmental modification

# Biomarker Categories

## Current Diagnostic Limitation

- **No objective molecular biomarkers** for ASD diagnosis
- Diagnosis relies on **behavioral assessment alone**
- **Heterogeneity** makes single biomarker unlikely
- **Early detection delayed** because behavioral signs emerge gradually

## Emerging Biomarker Approaches

### Neuroimaging Biomarkers
- Brain structure/connectivity changes
- Brain activation patterns in response to social stimuli
- Neuroimaging classifiers showing promise in some studies

### Neurophysiological Biomarkers
- **Electroencephalography (EEG) patterns** — altered spectral power, event-related potentials
- **Eye-tracking metrics** — reduced social attention, predictable gaze patterns
- **Electroculography** — pupil response abnormalities

### Digital Analytics
- Movement patterns, coordination measures
- Speech/language acoustics
- Social behavior quantification

### Genomic Biomarkers
- **Gene expression profiles** — cell-type specific dysregulation
- **Copy number variants** (CNVs) as genetic risk markers
- **Epigenetic modifications** (DNA methylation, histone marks)

### Inflammatory/Immune Biomarkers
- **Cytokine profiles** (elevated pro-inflammatory cytokines)
- **Chemokine markers** (immune cell recruitment signals)
- **Immune cell composition** shifts
- Suggests **neuroinflammation as mechanism**

### Proteomic Biomarkers
- Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)
- Synaptic markers (synaptophysin, PSD-95)
- Neuroinflammation-related proteins

### Metabolomic Biomarkers
- **Metabolite profiles** (amino acids, lipids, energy metabolism markers)
- **Tryptophan metabolism** dysregulation (reduced metabolites)
- **Energy metabolism** abnormalities (suggestive of mitochondrial dysfunction)

### Microbiota Biomarkers
- **Dysbiosis patterns** (taxonomic composition)
- **Metabolite-producing capacity** of microbiota
- Microbiota diversity metrics

# Therapeutic Approaches Under Investigation

## Pharmacological Therapies

- **Antioxidants** (reduce oxidative stress)
- **Oxytocin** (social behavior modulation)
- **AVPR1a antagonists** (vasopressin signaling modulation)
- **PPAR agonists** (metabolic, immune, epigenetic effects)
- **mTOR inhibitors** (growth signaling dysregulation)

## Non-Pharmacological Therapies

- **Non-invasive brain stimulation** (rTMS, tDCS, tACS)
- **Behavioral interventions** (ABA, social skills training)
- **Environmental modification** (dietary, microbiota, sensory)

## Microbiota-Targeted Therapies

- Probiotics (limited but promising)
- Prebiotics (emerging)
- FMT/MTT (experimental)
- **Dietary modification** to support dysbiosis reversal

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**High relevance** — paper identifies **multiple metal-dependent pathways** as putative mechanisms in ASD, though not explicitly framed as metal-related:

### Implicit Metal Components

- **Neuroinflammation**: mediated by [[iron]]-dependent microglial PRRs, [[zinc]]-dependent NF-κB signaling
- **Oxidative stress**: [[iron]]-dependent Fenton chemistry, [[zinc]]-dependent SOD dysfunction
- **Mitochondrial dysfunction**: [[iron]]-dependent cytochrome c oxidase, [[zinc]]-dependent dehydrogenases
- **BDNF/synaptic function**: [[zinc]]-dependent synaptic zinc signaling, [[iron]]-dependent mitochondrial ATP production
- **Microbiota dysbiosis**: [[iron]]-, [[zinc]]-, [[nickel]]-dependent taxa metabolism

### Environmental Factors Analysis

- **Maternal infections** → systemic [[iron]] sequestration (hepcidin upregulation), oxidative stress
- **Air pollution** → oxidative stress, [[iron]]-catalyzed ROS production
- **Medications (valproate)** → [[zinc]] depletion (known side effect)
- **Nutritional deficiencies** → zinc, iron directly
- **Advanced parental age** → oxidative stress (age-related mitochondrial dysfunction)

### Testable Hypothesis

Environmental triggers (infections, pollution, dietary deficiency) → **systemic [[iron]]/[[zinc]] dysregulation** → dysbiosis + neuroinflammation + developmental vulnerability = ASD symptom emergence
