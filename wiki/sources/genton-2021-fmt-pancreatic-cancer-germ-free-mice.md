# Key Claims

- [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]] from [[pancreatic-cancer]] patients into germ-free mice was associated with significantly decreased visceral fat compared to FMT from healthy volunteers (coeff -0.19, 95% CI: -0.38 to -0.02, p=0.035)
- *Escherichia coli* and *Streptococcus salivarius* were remarkably more abundant (>147-fold and >31-fold respectively) in fecal microbiota of PC patients compared to controls
- Microbiota from PC patients clustered separately from healthy volunteers in PCoA, and this pattern was maintained in transplanted mice
- 65-99% of recipient mice microbiota was of human donor origin, confirming successful human-to-mouse microbiota transfer

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Pilot study; feces from 5 PC patients and 5 healthy volunteers transplanted into 2 GF mice each (n=10 per group); 16S rRNA sequencing on days 5, 10, 15, 30
- **Body composition**: Visceral fat significantly lower in PC-FMT recipients (0.72g vs 0.92g); subcutaneous, brown adipose tissue, and skeletal muscle mass not significantly different
- **Metabolic parameters**: No significant differences in oGTT, insulin tolerance, or plasma cytokines between groups
- **Microbiota transfer fidelity**: PCoA showed mouse samples clustered according to cancer/healthy status of their human donors; Shannon diversity higher in human (3.59-4.30) than mouse (2.87-3.92) samples
- **Differentially abundant taxa**: PC patients enriched in *E. coli*, *S. salivarius*, *Clostridium bolteae*, *Clostridium_g24*; depleted in *Lachnospiraceae* PAC000196_s, *Coriobacteriaceae_unclassified*
- **Intra-group similarity**: Bray-Curtis similarity lower in PC recipients (median 47.53) than volunteer recipients (median 56.76, p=0.01)

# Connections

- Visceral fat reduction by PC-derived microbiota links to [[inflammation|cachexia]] and [[fermentative-metabolism|metabolic pathways]] in pancreatic cancer
- *E. coli* enrichment connects to intratumoral bacteria findings in [[dysbiosis|microbiome composition]] and [[dysbiosis|microbiome and disease progression]] studies
- Successful human-to-mouse microbiota transfer validates [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]] as translational model for pancreatic cancer research
- *Lachnospiraceae* depletion parallels SCFA-producing bacteria loss reported in [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] and [[short-chain-fatty-acids|microbial metabolites]] studies
