# The Gut Microbiota (Microbiome) in Cardiovascular Disease and Its Therapeutic Regulation

## Summary

Comprehensive review covering the role of the gut microbiota in [[cardiovascular-disease]], including mechanisms of TMAO production, SCFA regulation, bile acid metabolism, and the gut microbiota as a therapeutic target. Covers atherosclerosis, heart failure, coronary artery disease (CAD), and stroke.

## Key Findings

### Molecular Functions of Gut Microbiota in CVD
- Microbiota sends signals via LPS and peptidoglycans to host mucosal cells through pattern recognition receptors (PRRs)
- PRR signaling stimulates host immune system, including TLRs, C-type lectins, Dectin-1, and cytosolic NLR/NOD-like receptors
- Gut microbiota utilizes the bioactive metabolite system via bile acid route and trimethylamine

### TMAO Pathway
- Gut microbiota metabolizes lecithin (phosphatidylcholine), choline, and carnitine into TMA
- L-carnitine is oxidized to TMAO in the liver
- High circulating TMAO linked to increased CVD and mortality risk
- CutC/D is an effective TMAO inhibitor in animals

### Microbiome Composition in CAD
- Patients with CAD show reduced *Bacteroidetes* and higher *Firmicutes* (Cui et al., 2017)
- *Roseburia intestinalis* and *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* decreased in CAD
- Butyrate producers (*Faecalibacterium*, *Roseburia*, *Eubacterium rectale*) depleted
- *Escherichia-Shigella* and *Enterococcus* enriched in CAD (Zhu et al., 2018)

### Heart Failure Microbiome
- Consistent findings of depleted butyrate producers in HF
- Decreased *Coriobacteriaceae*, *Erysipelotrichaceae*, *Ruminococcaceae*, *Blautia*
- Increased *Prevotella*, *Hungatella*, *Succinclasticum* in some HF cohorts
- Butyrate production genetic potential is lower in HF microbiomes

### Dietary and Therapeutic Interventions
- Mediterranean diet reduces CVD prevalence and mortality
- Probiotics and prebiotics regulate glycemia and plasma lipid profiles
- SCFAs and butyrate help in lowering diastolic BP
- Fiber intake helps decrease BP (-9 SBP, -7 DBP)
- Probiotics and genetically engineered prebiotics can improve cardiovascular health

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This review connects to the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] model by:

1. **Butyrate-producing bacteria depletion** - The consistent loss of *Roseburia*, *Faecalibacterium*, and *Eubacterium rectale* in CAD mirrors the taxa most vulnerable to [[cadmium]] and [[lead]] exposure
2. **TMAO as metal-microbiome mediator** - Since heavy metals promote [[dysbiosis]] favoring TMA-producing bacteria, the TMAO-CVD pathway may be a key mechanism by which metal exposure increases cardiovascular risk
3. **LPS translocation** - Metal-induced intestinal barrier damage would amplify the LPS-TLR4-NF-kB [[inflammation]] cascade described as central to atherosclerosis
4. **[[Metabolic-syndrome]] connection** - The F/B ratio disruption seen in CVD parallels patterns observed with metal exposure
