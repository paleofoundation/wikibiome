# The Gut Microbiome and Multiple Sclerosis

## Key Claims

- The gut microbiome is proposed as the major environmental risk factor for CNS demyelinating disease
- Gut dysbiosis can alter both the effector and regulatory phases of [[multiple-sclerosis|MS]] demyelination
- [[Bacteroides fragilis]] polysaccharide A (PSA) is a potent immunomodulatory symbiont factor that protects against EAE via TLR2-dependent CD4+ T-cell activation and induction of IL-10-producing [[Tregs]]
- Fecal transplantation from MS patients into germ-free mice produces more severe EAE than transplants from healthy donors, providing causal evidence for the MS microbiome's functional role
- Segmented filamentous bacterium (SFB) exacerbates EAE via induction of Th17 cells; *Porphyromonas gingivalis* LPS promotes neuroinflammation via TLR2
- CD39+ Tregs are reduced in frequency and function in RRMS patients; PSA treatment promotes CD39 expression and restores Treg function in EAE mice
- The gut-associated lymphoid tissue (GALT) is the reservoir for many lymphoid cells, and the 10^13 gut microbes functionally affect both local and systemic immune balance
- Fingolimod (S1P1 receptor antagonist) enhances CD39 mRNA levels in MS-treated patients, connecting DMT mechanisms to Treg biology

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review synthesizing EAE animal model data and preliminary human MS microbiome studies
- **EAE model**: Characterized by CD4+ T helper 1 (Th1) and Th17 cell activation; germ-free mice are resistant to EAE disease
- **Key animal experiments**: Monocolonization with SFB restores EAE susceptibility via Th17 induction; *B. fragilis* colonization restores immune balance via PSA-driven Tregs
- **Fecal transplant studies**: Berer et al. (2017) and Cekanaviciute et al. (2017) both showed MS patient fecal content transferred to germ-free mice produced more severe disease than healthy donor content
- **Treg biology in MS**: Reduced Foxp3 expression and suppressive function in RRMS; nTregs and iTregs show different dysfunction patterns across MS subtypes (RRMS vs SPMS)
- **PSA mechanism**: TLR2-dependent, MHC class II presentation to CD4+ T cells; promotes CD39+ Foxp3+ Treg phenotype with enhanced expression of PD-1, GITR, CTLA-4, ICOS
- **Blood-brain barrier**: Dysbiosis compromises BBB integrity; gut microbes maintain BBB via SCFAs and other metabolites
- **Limitations**: Review notes that causative associations between specific microbes and MS have not been established; most human data is cross-sectional
