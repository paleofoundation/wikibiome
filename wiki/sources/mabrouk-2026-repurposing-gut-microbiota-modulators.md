# Overview

Review of the therapeutic versatility of **gut microbiota modulators (GMMs)** -- probiotics, prebiotics, synbiotics, and antibiotics -- and their potential to be repurposed beyond GI disorders to target neurological, metabolic, and immune-related diseases including ASD and ADHD.

# Key Findings

## GMMs for GI Disease Treatment

- **Salmonella Typhimurium**: probiotics like *Lactobacillus* and *Bifidobacterium* reduce severity and length of infections; butyrate-producing probiotics inhibit *C. difficile* infection (CDI)
- CDI: broad-spectrum antibiotic use exacerbates gut dysbiosis; narrow-spectrum or targeted microbiome therapies preferred
- *Saccharomyces boulardii* reduced antibiotic-associated diarrhea incidence

## GMMs for Neurodegenerative Disorders

- Microbiota-gut-brain axis (MGBA) links gut dysbiosis to **Alzheimer's disease (AD)** and **Parkinson's disease (PD)**
- In AD mouse models: probiotics reduced amyloid plaque formation and improved cognitive function
- In PD models: probiotics reduced alpha-synuclein aggregation and protected dopaminergic neurons
- Prebiotics like **inulin and FOS** promote SCFA-producing beneficial bacteria, reducing neuroinflammation
- SCFAs maintain gut barrier integrity and reduce systemic inflammation -- key contributors to neurodegeneration

## GMMs for ASD and ADHD

- Neurotransmitter balance modulation (serotonin, dopamine, GABA) supports ASD/ADHD applications
- Integration of GMMs into drug delivery systems improves targeted therapeutic delivery
- Repurposing approach bypasses lengthy de novo drug development, proceeding directly to clinical trials

## Limitations

- Personalized approaches needed due to variability in human responses
- GI stability challenges require encapsulated formulations
- Large-scale clinical trials still required to validate efficacy and clarify safety
