# Identification of Novel Non-Myelin Biomarkers in Multiple Sclerosis Using an Improved Phage-Display Approach

## Summary

This study uses antibody and cDNA phage display libraries to identify new non-myelin autoantigens in [[multiple-sclerosis]]. A human brain cDNA phage display library was constructed and screened against pooled immunoglobulins from CSF and sera of MS patients.

## Key Findings

- 14 antigens identified by biopanning from CSF B cell-derived scFv antibody library
- **DDX24** protein was present in all selections -- a novel candidate autoantigen
- Two novel candidate autoantigens validated serologically: **DDX24** and **TCERG1**
- Both are implicated in RNA modification and regulation, which can be altered in neurodegenerative processes
- DDX24 and TCERG1 could discriminate between MS patients and those with other neurological diseases
- Proposed as potential markers of disease activity state

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

The identification of non-myelin autoantigens (DDX24, TCERG1) involved in RNA processing expands the understanding of what drives the autoimmune response in MS beyond classical myelin targets. RNA-binding proteins can be modified by oxidative stress -- a consequence of both [[heavy-metals]] exposure and [[gut-microbiome]] [[dysbiosis]]-driven inflammation. If metal-induced oxidative damage creates neoantigens from RNA-processing proteins, this could represent a mechanism by which the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] axis drives autoimmunity through molecular modification of self-proteins.

## Limitations

- Relatively small sample sizes for validation cohorts
- Phage display selects for binding affinity, not necessarily disease relevance
- Cross-reactivity with other neurological diseases not fully excluded
