# Key Claims

- **Bile acids activate FXR, TGR5, and other nuclear/membrane receptors** to regulate glucose metabolism
- **Bile acids substantially lower blood glucose** through multiple pathways: BA-FXR-SHP, BA-FXR-FGFR15/19, BA-TGRS-GLP-1, BA-TGRS-cAMP
- **Bile acids impact blood glucose regulation** by influencing intestinal flora, enteroendocrine signaling, and bitter taste receptors
- **Bile acid-microbiota crosstalk** is bidirectional -- microbiota transforms bile acids and bile acids shape microbiota

# Methods & Findings

Comprehensive review of bile acid metabolism in T2DM:

- Primary regulatory pathways detailed (FXR, TGR5)
- Insulin sensitivity, secretion, and energy expenditure mechanisms reviewed
- Research limitations identified and future therapeutic directions proposed

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: FXR activation is zinc-dependent (zinc-finger nuclear receptor) -- zinc deficiency impairs bile acid signaling
- **[[iron]]**: Bile acid-iron interactions in the gut affect both iron absorption and microbial bile acid transformation
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium modulates bile acid detoxification pathways through selenoprotein activity
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Bile acid-microbiome crosstalk is fundamentally shaped by metal cofactor availability
- **[[dysbiosis]]**: Bile acid dysmetabolism and [[dysbiosis]] create a metal-dependent vicious cycle
