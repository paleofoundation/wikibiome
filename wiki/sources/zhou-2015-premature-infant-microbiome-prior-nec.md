# Zhou 2015 — Premature Infant Microbiome Prior to NEC

## Key Findings

- **Two distinct microbial trajectories** precede NEC, separated by timing of onset:
  - **Early-onset NEC** (<3 weeks): *Clostridium sensu stricto* dominated at 56.4% relative abundance by week 2.
  - **Late-onset NEC** (≥3 weeks): Gammaproteobacteria (Enterobacteriaceae, Klebsiella, Enterobacter) bloomed 1–3 days before clinical diagnosis.
- **Shared early signature.** A Klebsiella pneumoniae-like OTU appeared in 11/12 NEC cases during week 1, corroborating Torrazza 2013.
- **Dense longitudinal sampling** (312 samples across 38 infants) establishes temporal sequencing of dysbiosis.

## Methods

Prospective case-control nested within preterm NICU cohort. 38 infants (12 NEC, 26 controls). 312 serial stool samples. 16S rRNA V3–V5 sequencing. Stratified analysis by age of NEC onset.

- Timing: 1–3-day pre-diagnosis Gammaproteobacteria spike suggests a narrow intervention window.
