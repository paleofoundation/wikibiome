# Overview

Narrative review of **18 randomized, placebo-controlled trials** evaluating immunoregulatory and/or anti-inflammatory agents for ASD management. Found beneficial effects of (add-on) treatment on **stereotyped behavior** as well as associated symptoms (irritability, hyperactivity, lethargy).

# Key Findings

## Corticosteroids

- **Prednisolone** (2 RCTs): improved irritability (p=0.026), stereotypies (p=0.026), hyperactivity (p=0.039), lethargy (p<0.001) on ABC and CARS scores
- **Pregnenolone** (1 RCT, n=59): improved irritability (p=0.025), stereotypies (p=0.029), hyperactivity (p=0.004)
- Mechanisms: suppress microglial/monocyte proinflammatory activation, restore T regulatory/Th17 imbalances

## Celecoxib (COX-2 Inhibitor)

- 1 RCT (n=40): significant improvement in irritability (p<0.001), lethargy/social withdrawal (p<0.001), stereotypy behavior (p<0.001)
- COX-2 inhibition suggests neuroinflammation as therapeutic target

## Minocycline

- 1 RCT (n=46): improved irritability (p=0.003), hyperactivity/non-compliance (p=0.002) on ABC
- Anti-inflammatory tetracycline with neuroprotective properties

## N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)

- 5 RCTs with mixed results: 3 showed improvements in irritability, stereotypy, social cognition
- 2 showed no placebo- or NAC-specific improvement
- Doses ranged from 60 mg/kg/day to 2700 mg/day

## Sulforaphane

- 2 RCTs: improvements in irritability, hyperactivity
- One study (n=29): significant but modest effects

## Omega-3 Fatty Acids

- 3 RCTs: 2 showed specific improvement; 1 showed no effect
- Add-on to risperidone showed benefits

## Key Points

- All agents may suppress microglial/monocyte activation and restore immune cell imbalances
- Decrease proinflammatory cytokines (IL-6, IL-17A) in blood and brain
- Small sample sizes and short follow-up periods limit conclusions
