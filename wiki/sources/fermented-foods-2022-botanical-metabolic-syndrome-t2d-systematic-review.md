# Key Claims

- **Botanical fermented foods provide combined probiotic and bioactive compound benefits** for metabolic syndrome and T2D
- **Fermentation enhances bioavailability** of polyphenols and other plant-derived compounds
- **Living microorganisms in fermented foods** contribute to gut microbiome diversity and function

# Methods & Findings

Systematic review of RCTs on botanical fermented foods and metabolic outcomes:

- Fermented foods evaluated for effects on glucose metabolism, lipid profiles, and inflammation markers
- Mechanisms include probiotic effects, enhanced polyphenol bioavailability, and prebiotic fiber content

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Fermentation can enhance zinc bioavailability from plant foods by reducing phytate content
- **[[iron]]**: Fermentation increases non-heme iron absorption through organic acid production
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-enriched fermented foods could provide combined selenium-probiotic benefit
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Fermented foods directly introduce metal-tolerant beneficial bacteria into the gut
