# Long 2025 — Combination metformin and liraglutide in PCOS: clinical efficacy + gut microbiome modulation

**Citation:** Long XF, Fang YQ, Li YH, Li JY, Wang XP, Wang XL, Zhang L, Liu Y. *Combination metformin and liraglutide in PCOS: clinical efficacy in women and preclinical insights from gut microbiome modulation in rats.* Frontiers in Endocrinology. 2025;16:1599879. DOI: [10.3389/fendo.2025.1599879](https://doi.org/10.3389/fendo.2025.1599879)

## Key Findings

- **Clinical RCT (n=60, 12 weeks):** Overweight/obese Rotterdam-diagnosed PCOS women randomized 1:1:1 to metformin 0.85 g BID (MET, n=19 completed), liraglutide 1.2 mg SC QD (LIRA, n=18), or combination (COM, n=17).
  - Weight loss: COM 7.42 kg > LIRA 5.19 kg > MET 3.87 kg (COM vs MET p=0.002).
  - ≥5% weight reduction achieved in 94.1% COM, 77.8% LIRA, 52.6% MET (p=0.025).
  - Visceral fat area reduction: COM -27.11 cm² > LIRA -19.53 > MET -7.96 (COM vs MET p=0.002).
  - Free testosterone reduction: COM -4.81 > LIRA -2.51 > MET -1.12 pmol/L (COM vs MET p=0.012).
  - FAI (free androgen index) normalized (≤6.4) in 3/3 groups; LH/FSH normalized (<2) in COM 8→2, LIRA 10→2.
  - HbA1c: COM reduction significant vs MET (p=0.03).
  - HOMA-IR: insulin resistance improvement effective in 35.71% COM, 12.5% LIRA, 0% MET (χ² p=0.033).
- **Preclinical rat study (letrozole-induced PCOS, 4-week treatment):**
  - All three drug groups recovered estrous cycle vs. PCOS controls.
  - Testosterone: MET, LIRA, COM all reduced vs. PCOS; only LIRA and COM significantly (p<0.01, 0.05 respectively).
  - Ovarian morphology: reduced cystic follicles, increased corpora lutea in MET, LIRA, COM.
  - Gut microbiome (16S V3–V4): β-diversity significantly differed between NC and PCOS (ANOSIM R²=0.379, p=0.003); α-diversity (ACE, Chao) reduced in PCOS vs NC, restored in LIRA and COM.
- **Taxa shifts** (genus level):
  - PCOS vs. NC: ↓ Muribaculaceae (unranked), ↓ Lactobacillus, ↓ Dubosiella; ↑ Lachnospiraceae, Prevotella_1, Prevotella_9, Prevotellaceae_Ga6A1, Escherichia-Shigella, Parabacteroides.
  - MET/LIRA/COM vs. PCOS: ↑ Lactobacillus; ↓ Prevotella_9, Prevotella_1, Prevotellaceae_Ga6A1, Escherichia-Shigella.
  - MET unique: ↑ Akkermansia.
  - LIRA unique: ↑ Phascolarctobacterium, ↑ Akkermansia.
  - COM unique: ↑ **Alloprevotella**, ↑ **Parasutterella** (both negatively correlated with testosterone in correlation analysis).
- **Microbe-testosterone correlations.** Unclassified Porphyromonadaceae positively correlated with testosterone; Alistipes, Alloprevotella, Eubacterium, and Parasutterella negatively correlated. COM's unique superiority attributable in part to Alloprevotella/Parasutterella expansion.

## Methods

- **Clinical.** Open-label 12-week RCT at Union Hospital, Wuhan. 60 premenopausal, overweight/obese PCOS women per Rotterdam criteria, BMI ≥24. Registered ChiCTR1800017538. Primary outcomes: weight, BMI, FSH/LH, testosterone, insulin resistance, lipid profile, OGTT. Efficacy of insulin resistance defined as HOMA-IR <2.69 after treatment.
- **Preclinical.** Letrozole 1 mg/kg orally induced PCOS in female SD rats (weeks 5–8). Treatment weeks 9–12: NC (no treatment), PCOS (letrozole only), MET (letrozole + metformin 200 mg/kg PO), LIRA (letrozole + liraglutide 0.2 mg/kg SC), COM (both). 16S rRNA V3–V4 Illumina sequencing; PCoA weighted UniFrac; LEfSe; Spearman correlation.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- LIRA alone was less effective for insulin resistance (12.5%) than COM (35.71%); this contradicts some GLP-1 monotherapy reports in diabetes but is consistent with PCOS's metabolic heterogeneity.
- MET monotherapy had 0% HOMA-IR effectiveness in this cohort — surprisingly poor, possibly reflecting dose (0.85 g BID, lower than standard 1 g BID) or the overweight/obese patient subgroup.
- Rat microbiome findings from letrozole-PCOS model may not fully translate to human PCOS endocrine milieu.

## Open Questions

- Whether Alloprevotella/Parasutterella colonization via probiotic delivery could achieve similar testosterone-suppressive effects without the GLP-1 agonist.
- Whether the combination's microbiome signature persists after drug discontinuation.
- Whether the bile acid profile changes parallel the microbiome shifts (not measured here but implicated by Alloprevotella).
- Long-term fertility outcomes of COM vs. MET (not measured at 12 weeks).
