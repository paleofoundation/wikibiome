# Exploratory Characterization of Gut Microbiota and Cognitive Profiles in Adolescents with Subthreshold Depression

## Key Claims

- In 38 adolescents with subthreshold [[depression]] (SD) vs 139 clinically well controls, significant differences in beta-diversity (p < 0.001) but not alpha-diversity were found
- SD group showed significantly increased Spirochaetes, Spirochaetia, Spirochaetales, and Spirochaetaceae at multiple taxonomic ranks (r = -0.218, p = 0.039 at phylum level)
- Synergistetes (r = -0.204, p = 0.027) was also significantly higher in SD
- Higher abundance of Spirochaetes, Spirochaetia, and Spirochaetales was the best predictor of SD in adolescents (AUC 0.76)
- KEGG pathways for neurodegenerative diseases and translation were depleted in SD microbiome
- Intracellular trafficking secretion and vesicular transport pathways negatively correlated with spatial span cognitive scores
- C-PHQ-9 depression scores significantly higher in SD group (5.87 vs 0.12, p < 0.001)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Case-control with shotgun metagenomics
- **Sample**: 38 SD + 139 CW adolescents (12-14 years), Yunnan, China
- **Cognitive assessment**: MATRICS Consensus Cognitive Battery (MCCB)
- **Machine learning**: Random Forest classifier for predicting SD status from microbiome
