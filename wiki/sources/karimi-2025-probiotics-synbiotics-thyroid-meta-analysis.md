# Effects of Probiotics and Synbiotics Oral Supplementation on Thyroid Function in Adults: A Grade-Assessed Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## Key Claims

- [[Probiotics]] and [[synbiotics]] oral supplementation led to a significant reduction in TSH (SMD: -1.10, 95% CI: -1.96, -0.23, p=0.013) and significantly increased free T3 (SMD: 0.98, 95% CI: 0.10, 1.85, p=0.029) and free T4 (SMD: 1.04, 95% CI: 0.04, 2.05, p=0.042)
- Subgroup analyses showed interventions lasting 8 weeks or less had a stronger effect, and patients with thyroid disorders showed higher responsiveness than non-thyroid groups
- Shorter intervention durations (<=8 weeks) exhibited larger, near-significant effect sizes for T4 (SMD=1.75, p=0.055), while longer durations had negligible impact (SMD=0.10)
- Probiotics significantly reduced TSH (SMD=-0.76, p=0.036) while synbiotics did not; populations with thyroid disorders experienced larger TSH reductions (SMD=-1.10, p=0.047)
- This is the most comprehensive meta-analysis to date with 9 RCTs (395 participants total) using GRADE assessment

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA guidelines)
- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, ISI Web of Science, Scopus; all available literature up to April 2025
- **Included studies**: 9 RCTs from Iran, Italy, China, South Korea, Ukraine, and Poland; published 2014-2025; sample sizes 11-80; durations 2-24 weeks
- **PICO**: Adults receiving probiotics/synbiotics oral supplementation vs. placebo/no supplementation; outcomes = T3, T4, TSH
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB-2); 5/9 studies low overall risk, 4 had some concerns; GRADE assessment for certainty of evidence
- **T3 effect**: Significantly increased (SMD=0.98, p=0.029); high heterogeneity (I-squared=91%); interventions <=8 weeks showed stronger effect (SMD=1.08) vs. >8 weeks (SMD=0.67)
- **T4 effect**: Significantly increased (SMD=1.04, p=0.042); high heterogeneity (I-squared=93%); shorter durations (<=8 weeks) showed larger effect (SMD=1.75) vs. longer (SMD=0.10)
- **TSH effect**: Significantly decreased (SMD=-1.10, p=0.013); high heterogeneity (I-squared=93%); probiotics alone significantly reduced TSH, synbiotics alone did not; larger studies (>50 participants) showed significant reduction (SMD=-1.54, p=0.042)
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Removal of high-bias studies altered some effect estimates; high heterogeneity limits certainty
- **Studies included**: Narimani-rad 2014, Spaggiari 2017, Talebi 2020, Huo 2021, Lin 2022, Kong 2022, Ramezani 2023, Salamadze 2023, Szydlowska 2025
