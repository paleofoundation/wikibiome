# Effects of Probiotics and Synbiotics Oral Supplementation on Thyroid Function in Adults: A Grade-Assessed Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## Key Findings

- **Significant TSH reduction**: Probiotics and synbiotics led to significant reduction in TSH (SMD: -1.10, 95% CI: -1.96 to -0.23, p=0.013; I^2=93%) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, n=395).
- **Significant fT3 increase**: Free T3 significantly increased (SMD: 0.98, 95% CI: 0.10 to 1.85, p=0.029; I^2=91%) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).
- **Significant fT4 increase**: Free T4 significantly increased (SMD: 1.04, 95% CI: 0.04 to 2.05, p=0.042; I^2=93%) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).
- **Duration effect**: Interventions lasting <=8 weeks showed stronger effects for T3 (SMD=1.08 vs. 0.67), T4 (SMD=1.75, p=0.055 vs. SMD=0.10), and TSH (SMD=-1.87, p=0.009 vs. SMD=-0.20) compared to >8 weeks ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).
- **Probiotics vs. synbiotics**: Probiotics alone significantly reduced TSH (SMD=-0.76, p=0.036) while synbiotics did not (SMD=-1.33, p=0.139); larger studies (>50 participants) showed significant TSH reduction (SMD=-1.54, p=0.042) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).

## Methods

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA guidelines; PICOS framework; published online August 1, 2025)
- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed (n=310), ISI Web of Science (n=635), Scopus (n=1,211); all relevant literature to April 2025; no language/time restrictions
- **Screening**: 2,156 records → 1,566 unique → 1,546 excluded → 20 full-text assessed → 11 excluded → 9 RCTs included
- **Quality assessment**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB-2) — 5/9 studies low overall risk, 4 had some concerns (3 with high risk in D1 randomization, 2 with high risk in D2 deviation); GRADE assessment for certainty of evidence
- **Statistical analysis**: Stata v17; random-effects model (SMD with 95% CI); heterogeneity by Cochran's Q and I^2; subgroups by duration (<=8 vs. >8 weeks), intervention type, thyroid status, geography, sample size; sensitivity analysis by sequential removal; publication bias by funnel plots and Egger's test
- **Studies included**: Narimani-rad 2014 (Iran), Spaggiari 2017 (Italy), Talebi 2019 (Iran), Huo 2021 (China), Lin 2022 (China), Kong 2022 (South Korea), Ramezani 2023 (Iran), Salamadze 2023 (Ukraine), Szydlowska 2025 (Poland)

## Detailed Summary

**T3 effect** (7 studies): Overall SMD=0.98 (p=0.029), with high heterogeneity (I^2=91%). Subgroup by duration: <=8 weeks SMD=1.08 (I^2=93.6%) vs. >8 weeks SMD=0.67 (I^2=74.3%). Probiotics subtotal SMD=0.94 (I^2=88.5%) vs. synbiotics SMD=1.02 (I^2=94.3%). Asian studies showed significant effect (SMD=0.88, p=0.005); European studies did not (SMD=1.01, p=0.494). Smaller studies (<=50) significant (SMD=1.05, p=0.019) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).

**T4 effect** (7 studies): Overall SMD=1.04 (p=0.042), I^2=93%. Shorter durations (<=8 weeks) showed SMD=1.75 (p=0.055) vs. >8 weeks SMD=0.10 (p=0.681). Thyroid disorder populations had higher effect (SMD=0.78) than non-thyroid (SMD=2.29). Sample size <=50: SMD=0.97 (p=0.133); >50: SMD=1.07 (p=0.229) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).

**TSH effect** (9 studies): Overall SMD=-1.10 (p=0.013), I^2=93%. Shorter durations: SMD=-1.87 (p=0.009) vs. longer: SMD=-0.20 (p=0.594). Probiotics: SMD=-0.76 (p=0.036); synbiotics: SMD=-1.33 (p=0.139). Thyroid disorders: SMD=-1.10 (p=0.047); non-thyroid: SMD=-1.13 (p=0.206). Larger studies (>50): SMD=-1.54 (p=0.042) ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).

**GRADE assessment** (Table 3): All three outcomes rated high quality of evidence. T3: SMD=0.98, 306 participants (160 int./146 cont.). T4: SMD=1.04, 306 participants. TSH: SMD=-1.10, 405 participants (207 int./198 cont.). Serious limitations noted for risk of bias and inconsistency (heterogeneity I^2>80%) but no concerns for indirectness, imprecision, or publication bias ([[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).

**Sensitivity analysis**: Removal of high-bias studies (Narimani-rad, Talebi, Salamadze, Huo) altered some effect estimates. For T3, removal led to CI range of (95% CI: -0.08, 2.16). For TSH, analysis remained within (95% CI: -2.23, 0).

**Publication bias**: Funnel plots appeared symmetric; Egger's test non-significant for T3 (p=0.307), T4 (p=0.255), TSH (p=0.288).

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Updates and supersedes [[shu-2024-probiotics-prebiotics-thyroid-function-meta-analysis]] with additional studies and GRADE assessment
- The duration effect (shorter interventions more effective) is a novel finding suggesting acute microbiome modulation may trigger lasting thyroid effects
- High heterogeneity across studies reflects the strain/dose variability problem in probiotic research — relevant to concept pages on [[probiotics]]

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Clinically actionable**: Probiotics as adjunctive intervention for thyroid disorders supported with GRADE-high evidence for TSH reduction and T3/T4 increases
- **Duration paradox**: <=8 weeks showing stronger effects suggests an early microbiota-mediated modulation of nutrient absorption (iodine, selenium) and immune signaling — clinically, short courses may be more effective than chronic supplementation
- **Probiotics alone > synbiotics for TSH**: Suggests the prebiotic component may not add benefit or may introduce confounding substrate effects
- **Population specificity**: Thyroid disorder patients respond more than healthy populations — intervention should be targeted

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Directly conflicts with [[shu-2024-probiotics-prebiotics-thyroid-function-meta-analysis]] null findings on TSH/fT4/fT3 — the discrepancy likely driven by inclusion of Narimani-rad 2014 and Szydlowska 2025, use of change scores, and analytical choices
- Very high heterogeneity (I^2=91-93% for all outcomes) despite GRADE rating of "high" quality — the GRADE rating may be generous given the inconsistency
- The short-duration advantage is counterintuitive if the mechanism is chronic microbiome remodeling; may instead reflect transient metabolic effects

## Open Questions

- Whether the duration effect is real or an artifact of the specific studies in the <=8 week subgroup (which included the most extreme effect sizes)
- Which specific probiotic strains drive the TSH reduction effect
- Whether the T3/T4 increases translate to clinically meaningful improvement in hypothyroid symptoms
- Whether standardized probiotic protocols could reduce the heterogeneity seen across these studies
