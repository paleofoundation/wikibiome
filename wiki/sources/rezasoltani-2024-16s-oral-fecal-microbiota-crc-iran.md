# 16S rRNA Sequencing Analysis of the Oral and Fecal Microbiota in Colorectal Cancer Positives Versus Negatives in Iranian Population

## Key Claims

- This is the first integrated microbiome study of both stool and saliva from CRC patients vs. healthy controls in an Iranian population, revealing distinct microbial profiles at phylum, family, and species levels
- In saliva, *Calothrix parietina*, *Granulicatella adiacens*, *Rothia dentocariosa*, and *Rothia mucilaginosa* were among the top 10 CRC-associated microbes, with none present in healthy controls
- In stool, *Lachnospiraceae* and *Prevotellaceae* were markedly higher in CRC patients, while the genus *Akkermansia* and species *Akkermansia muciniphila* were significantly more abundant in the cancer-negative group
- Support vector machine models achieved the best diagnostic performance for stool-based CRC prediction (AUC 0.97, sensitivity 92%, specificity 93%, accuracy 90%)
- Saliva-based logistic regression achieved AUC of 0.91 for CRC detection using *Porphyromonadaceae*, *Fusobacteria*, *Actinobacteria*, and *Streptococcus infantis* as key predictors

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Case-control comparison of oral and fecal microbiota using 16S rRNA sequencing
- **Participants**: 25 CRC-positive and 15 CRC-negative individuals from Taleghani Hospital, Shahid Beheshti University, Tehran, Iran
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA sequencing using Nextera XT Index Kit on Illumina NovaSeq; multiple ML classifiers (logistic regression, SVM, naive Bayes, neural network) for CRC prediction
- **Key findings**: Smoking and physical inactivity were significantly more prevalent among CRC patients; family history was a significant risk factor (P = 0.036)
