# Opportunities and Challenges in Phage Therapy for Cardiometabolic Diseases

## Summary

Opinion article discussing the potential of bacteriophage (phage) therapy as a precision tool for modulating the gut microbiota in cardiometabolic diseases (CMD) including metabolic syndrome, T2D, and [[cardiovascular-disease]].

## Key Findings

### Phages in Health and CMD
- Phages are viruses specifically targeting bacteria; mucus layer enriched in phages contributing to reduced pathogenic colonization
- Phage:bacteria ratio in gut estimated 1:1 to 10:1
- Phage life cycles: lytic (kills host), lysogenic (integrates), chronic (reduces growth without killing)
- Gut virome perturbations associated with T2D, hypertension, metabolic syndrome
- Healthy individuals predominantly harbor integrated prophages; diseased individuals show elevated extracellular phage virions

### Potential for Phage Therapy in CMD
- Previous studies used phages against cytolysin-positive *Enterococcus faecalis* to reduce liver disease in humanized mice
- *K. pneumoniae* targeted by phages to reduce steatohepatitis inflammation without altering broader microbiota
- *E. coli*-targeting commercial cocktail in overweight humans: reduced *E. coli* but no overall microbiome composition changes
- **Fecal virome transplantation (FVT)**: Transfer of phage-containing fecal filtrate; shown to alter gut microbiota similar to FMT

### Challenges
- Identifying consistent bacterial targets causally contributing to CMD is difficult
- Phage resistance development; potential for altering trophic networks
- Individual-specific gut ecosystems complicate standardized phage formulations
- Functional redundancy of gut microbes makes this endeavor highly challenging

### Temperate Phages as Novel Approach
- Temperate phages can alter bacterial function via lysogenic conversion rather than killing
- Could shift metabolite production from harmful to beneficial (e.g., increased SCFA)
