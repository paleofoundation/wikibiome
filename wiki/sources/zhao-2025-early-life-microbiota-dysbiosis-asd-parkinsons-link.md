# Key Claims

- **ASD predisposes to neurodegeneration** including Parkinson's disease later in life
- **Population-based cohort** of 2,278,565 individuals found ASD in 0.05% with relative risk of 4.43 (95% CI 2.92-6.72) for PD
- **Similar dysbiosis-driven immune and metabolic disturbances** reported in both ASD and PD, converging on shared pathways including alpha-synuclein misfolding
- **FMT from ASD donors** transfers social deficits to germ-free mice, illustrating dysbiosis-driven neurodevelopmental changes
- **Microbiota-based interventions** (FMT, prebiotics, synbiotics) promising for both ASD and PD

# Methods & Findings

**Commentary** proposing a mechanistic link between early-life gut microbiota dysbiosis in ASD and later PD risk.

- Examined epidemiological data linking ASD to subsequent PD diagnosis
- Reviewed shared biological pathways: SCFA production, alpha-synuclein propagation, synaptic pruning
- Discussed maternal glyphosate exposure as shared risk factor for both conditions
- Proposed disease-specific developmental windows for microbiome intervention
- Called for longitudinal studies tracking ASD patients for neurodegenerative outcomes

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Lifespan perspective** on ASD gut dysbiosis with implications for long-term neurodegeneration
- **Shared microbiome-immune-neural pathways** between neurodevelopmental and neurodegenerative conditions
- **Alpha-synuclein gut-to-brain propagation** as mechanism linking gut dysbiosis to neurodegeneration
