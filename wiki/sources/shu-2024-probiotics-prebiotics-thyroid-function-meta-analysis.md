# Effect of Probiotics or Prebiotics on Thyroid Function: A Meta-Analysis of Eight Randomized Controlled Trials

## Key Claims

- [[probiotics]] and [[prebiotics]] supplementation does not significantly influence [[gut-thyroid-axis|thyroid hormone]] levels (TSH, fT4, fT3) but may modestly reduce TRAb levels in patients with [[graves-disease]]
- Meta-analysis of 8 RCTs showed no significant alterations in TSH (SMD: -0.01, 95% CI: -0.21, 0.20, P=0.93) or fT4 (SMD: 0.04, 95% CI: -0.29, 0.21, P=0.73) or fT3 (SMD: 0.45, 95% CI: -0.14, 1.03, P=0.43; I-squared=78%)
- A significant reduction in TRAb levels was observed (SMD: -0.85, 95% CI: -1.54, -0.15, P=0.02; I-squared=18%) following probiotic/prebiotic supplementation, with no indication of publication bias
- Various regimens were tested across 3 countries (China, Iran, Italy): [[lactobacillus]], [[bifidobacterium]], Streptococcus, Enterococcus, Bacillus probiotics; fructo-oligosaccharides, black bean, Elaeagnus angustifolia prebiotics; berberine; and synbiotics

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA 2020)
- **Search**: MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Social Sciences Citation Index, Embase up to April 1, 2023; no language/year restrictions
- **Included studies**: 8 RCTs published 2017-2023 from China, Iran, and Italy; subjects randomized to placebo, control, or intervention groups; ages 18-65 years
- **Interventions**: Probiotics (Lactobacillus, Streptococcus, Bifidobacterium, Enterococcus); prebiotics (FOS, black bean, Elaeagnus angustifolia L., berberine, silymarin, green tea essence); synbiotics (probiotics + prebiotics); duration 4 weeks to 12 months
- **Populations**: Overweight/obese, GD patients, postmenopausal women, patients with hypothyroidism, DTC patients
- **Quality**: Jadad scale assessment; 3 studies scored >=4 (high quality), 5 studies scored <4 (low quality)
- **Key study findings**: (1) Lin 2022 - probiotics reduced complications post-thyroidectomy; (2) Huo 2021 - methimazole + black bean reduced FT3/FT4; (3) Spaggiari 2017 - multistrain probiotic did not alter LT4 therapy; (4) Han 2022 - berberine restored TSH and FT3 in GD; (5) Jalalvand 2021 - E. angustifolia increased TSH within-group only
- **Limitations**: Small sample sizes, heterogeneous populations, inconsistent probiotic strains and dosages
