# Effect of Probiotics or Prebiotics on Thyroid Function: A Meta-Analysis of Eight Randomized Controlled Trials

## Key Findings

- **No significant effect on thyroid hormone levels**: Probiotics/prebiotics supplementation showed no significant alterations in TSH (SMD: -0.01, 95% CI: -0.21 to 0.20, P=0.93; I^2=0%), fT4 (SMD: 0.04, 95% CI: -0.29 to 0.21, P=0.73; I^2=0%), or fT3 (SMD: 0.45, 95% CI: -0.14 to 1.03, P=0.43; I^2=78%) ([[shu-2024-probiotics-prebiotics-thyroid-function-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis, n=367).
- **Significant TRAb reduction in GD**: A significant reduction in TRAb levels was observed (SMD: -0.85, 95% CI: -1.54 to -0.15, P=0.02; I^2=18%) following probiotic/prebiotic supplementation, with no indication of publication bias (Begg's p=0.296, Egger's p=0.362) ([[shu-2024-probiotics-prebiotics-thyroid-function-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).
- **Subgroup analysis**: Subgroup analyses by intervention type (probiotics/prebiotics vs. synbiotics) did not identify significant changes in TSH, fT4, or fT3 between groups. The TRAb finding was based on 3 studies of GD patients only ([[shu-2024-probiotics-prebiotics-thyroid-function-meta-analysis]], systematic-review-meta-analysis).

## Methods

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA 2020; published January 11, 2024)
- **Search strategy**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Scopus, Web of Science, Social Sciences Citation Index, Embase up to April 1, 2023; no language or year restrictions; also searched medRxiv, Research Square, CINAHL, China National Knowledge Internet databases, Wangfang Database; ClinicalTrials.gov, Cochrane Central
- **Screening**: 693 records identified → 676 after duplicates → 17 screened → 16 full-text assessed → 8 RCTs included
- **Eligibility**: Adult participants (18-65 years), assessed effects on TSH, fT4, fT3, TT3, TT4, TRAb; excluded cell/animal studies, editorials, reviews, non-RCTs
- **Quality assessment**: Jadad scale (3 studies scored >=4 = high quality; 5 scored <4 = low quality); Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0
- **Statistical analysis**: STATA v.17.0 and Review Manager v.5.4; random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird); heterogeneity assessed by chi-squared and I^2 (low <30%, moderate 30-60%, high >60%); publication bias by funnel plots, Begg's rank correlation, Egger's regression; trim-and-fill for imputed studies

## Detailed Summary

**Included studies** (Table 1): 8 RCTs published 2017-2023 from 3 countries:
1. **Lin 2022** (China, n=39): Bifidobacterium Tetravaccine in post-thyroidectomy DTC patients, 4 weeks — reduced complications; Jadad >=4
2. **Huo 2021** (China, n=25): Methimazole + black bean or + B. longum in GD patients, 6 months — methimazole+black bean reduced FT3/FT4, methimazole+probiotics reduced TRAb; Jadad <4
3. **Spaggiari 2017** (Italy, n=80): Multistrain probiotic (B. breve, B. longum, B. infantis, L. acidophilus, L. plantarum, L. paracasei, L. bulgaricus, S. thermophilus) in primary hypothyroidism patients on LT4, 4 months — no change; Jadad <4
4. **Han 2022** (China, n=18): Berberine + methimazole in GD patients, 6 months — berberine restored TSH and FT3; Jadad <4
5. **Jalalvand 2021** (Iran, n=58): Elaeagnus angustifolia in postmenopausal women, 10 weeks — TSH increase significant within-group only; Jadad >=4
6. **Kong 2022** (China/Taiwan, n=40): Synbiotic (L. johnsonii No.1088, B. subtilis BS139, fermented sake lees, green tea essence) in overweight/obese subjects, 12 weeks — TSH and T4 significantly higher; Jadad >=4
7. **Ramezani 2023** (Iran, n=51): Synbiotic (L. casei, L. acidophilus, L. rhamnus, L. bulgaricus, B. breve, B. longum, S. thermophilus + FOS) in hypothyroidism patients, 10 weeks — TSH not changed, FT4 increased in both groups; Jadad >=4
8. **Talebi 2019** (Iran, n=56): Synbiotic (7 probiotic strains + FOS) in hypothyroidism patients, 8 weeks — TSH significantly decreased; Jadad >=4

**Subject characteristics** (Table 2): 194 (52.86%) assigned to intervention, 173 (47.14%) to control. Hypothyroidism (48.45% intervention, 53.76% control), DTC (11.86%), postmenopausal (15.46%), obese (10.31%), GD (13.92%). Iran (36.08%), China (43.81%), Italy (20.10%).

**Risk of bias**: 1 study high ROB overall, 4 had some concerns, 3 had low ROB across all domains. Blinding of participants/personnel and outcome assessment were the most commonly flagged domains.

**Publication bias**: Trim-and-fill method imputed 0 studies for TSH, 2 for fT4, 2 for fT3, and 2 for TRAb. Sensitivity analyses stable after imputation.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- First meta-analysis dedicated to probiotics/prebiotics and thyroid function — establishes current evidence baseline
- Null findings on thyroid hormone levels temper overenthusiastic claims about probiotic effects on thyroid function
- Minerals (selenium, iron, zinc) mentioned as mediators between microbiota and thyroid function inform entity pages

## Relevance to Cureva

- **TRAb reduction signal in GD**: The significant TRAb finding (P=0.02) suggests probiotics may have immunomodulatory effects specifically on thyroid autoantibodies, potentially useful as adjunct therapy in [[graves-disease]]
- **Null thyroid hormone result**: Important for clinical reasoning — probiotics alone are unlikely to replace levothyroxine or antithyroid drugs
- **Strain heterogeneity**: The wide variation in probiotic formulations across studies limits strain-specific intervention recommendations
- All TRAb studies were from Chinese groups — geographic/population bias needs consideration

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The null findings on TSH/fT4/fT3 conflict with [[karimi-2025-probiotics-synbiotics-thyroid-meta-analysis]] which found significant effects on all three endpoints with a larger, overlapping study set (9 RCTs, 395 participants)
- The difference likely driven by the additional study (Szydlowska 2025) and different inclusion of Narimani-rad 2014, plus analytical differences (SMD of change scores vs. final values)
- TRAb reduction based on only 3 studies with 47 total participants — clinically uncertain significance despite statistical significance

## Open Questions

- Whether TRAb reduction translates to meaningful clinical improvement in GD outcomes
- Whether strain-specific meta-analyses would reveal hidden effects masked by pooling heterogeneous formulations
- Whether duration effects exist (the 4-week studies may be too short to observe thyroid hormone changes)
- Why Lactobacillus-based probiotics increased orbital CD25+ Treg cells and promoted Graves' orbitopathy in one mouse model — potential STOP signal
