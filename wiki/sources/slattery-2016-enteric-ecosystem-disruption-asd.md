# Overview

Review of three prominent theoretical models -- **Biome Depletion Theory (BDT)**, **Propionic Acid (PPA) model**, and **Cell Danger Response (CDR)** -- that frame ASD as resulting from disruption of the enteric ecosystem, including both microbiota and macrobiota (helminths, fauna).

# Key Findings

## Biome Depletion Theory (BDT)

- Alterations in microbiome and macrobiome are consequences of post-industrial society: eradication of helminths, reduced exposure to symbiotic organisms
- Loss of biome organisms that co-evolved with humans destabilizes immune modulatory activity
- "Three-hit paradigm": (1) biome depletion, (2) environmental stimulus (acetaminophen, antibiotics, vitamin D deficiency), (3) genetic/epigenetic predisposition
- Reconstitution through probiotics, FMT, helminth reintroduction proposed

## Propionic Acid (PPA) Model

- PPA produced abundantly by *Clostridia*, *Bacteroides*, and *Desulfovibrio* species enriched in ASD
- ICV PPA infusions in rats produce **ASD-like behaviors**: repetitive behaviors, object preference, impaired social interaction, metabolic changes
- PPA affects: CREB activation (memory), histone deacetylase inhibition, gene expression alteration, and mitochondrial function
- Broad physiological effects: fatty acid oxidation, immune modulation, gap junction function, neurotransmitter synthesis

## Cell Danger Response (CDR)

- CDR proposes extracellular ATP as purinergic danger signal when cells are damaged
- Metabolic mismatch between cellular physiology and modern environment
- Suramin (anti-parasitic drug) reversed ASD-like behaviors in mouse models by antagonizing purinergic signaling
- Both behavioral and metabolic abnormalities corrected in poly(I:C) and Fragile X mouse models

## Potential Triggers for ASD

- **Acetaminophen**: mitochondrial toxin implicated in oxidative stress
- **Antibiotics**: disrupt gut microbiome during critical developmental windows
- **NSAIDs, antimotility agents, statins, proton pump inhibitors**: various classes of drugs affect mitochondrial function
- **Environmental toxicants**: heavy metals, pesticides, industrial chemicals
