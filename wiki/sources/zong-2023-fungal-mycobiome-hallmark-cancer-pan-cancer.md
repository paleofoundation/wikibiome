# Zong 2023 -- Fungal Mycobiome as New Hallmark of Cancer

## Key Claims

- Pan-cancer analysis by Narunsky-Haziza et al. characterized tumor mycobiome across 35 cancer types from four independent cohorts, finding fungi significantly enriched in all cancer types using internal transcribed spacer sequencing and whole-genome sequencing
- In colon cancer, *Candida* and *Saccharomycetes* are the dominant cancer-associated fungi; specific fungal signatures vary by body site
- Cancer-specific fungi identified: *Malassezia* in head-neck and colon; *Candida* in stomach and head-neck; unique mycobiome signatures and immune responses detected using TCGA data from various cancer samples
- The study provides direct evidence that fungal DNA is present in most human cancer tissues, reinforcing the concept that the tumor microbiome is composed of tumor type-specific intracellular bacteria and fungi
- These findings support reconsidering the 'Hallmarks of Cancer' to include polymorphic microbiomes as a component of the immuno-oncology microbiome axis

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Research highlight/commentary on Cell 185 (2022) pan-cancer mycobiome analysis
- **Figure 1**: Cancer-associated fungi in different body sites with potential prognostic markers highlighted; unique mycobiome signatures and immune responses across different cancers from TCGA data
- **Key implication**: Fungal 'mycotypes' may predict cancer diagnosis, prognosis, and guide drug development
