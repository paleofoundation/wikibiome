# Key Claims

- Stage 3/4 [[endometriosis]] patients have lower alpha diversity and a higher Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio in gut microbiota
- Serum estradiol (E2) was significantly higher in endometriosis (74.7 vs 47.9 pg/L, p=0.002) and IL-8 levels were significantly elevated
- Seven gut bacteria (*Blautia*, *Dorea*, *Bifidobacterium*, *Streptococcus*, *Bacteroides*, *Subdoligranulum*, *Phascolarctobacterium*) showed significant abundance differences
- *Blautia* and *Dorea* abundance correlated positively with estradiol levels; *Subdoligranulum* correlated with IL-8 abundance
- The gut microbiota of endometriosis patients was enriched for environmental information processing, endocrine system, and immune system KEGG pathways

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Case-control, Changhai Hospital, Shanghai
- **Participants**: 12 patients with stage 3/4 endometriosis and 12 healthy controls
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA V3-V4 sequencing of stool, serum hormone and cytokine panel (Bio-Plex)
- **Key results**:
  - 3D-PCoA showed significant separation (R=0.2616, P=0.001)
  - LH, FSH, PRL not significantly different; E2 significantly higher in EM group
  - *Bifidobacterium* significantly increased (p=0.01529); *Blautia* significantly increased (p=0.000308)
  - *Lachnospira* significantly decreased (p=0.00007631)
  - Correlation heatmaps showed *Blautia* and *Dorea* positively correlated with E2 and PRL

# Relevance to Wiki

This study directly links gut [[dysbiosis]] with hormonal and inflammatory profiles in [[endometriosis]], supporting the estrobolome hypothesis. The correlation between *Blautia*/*Dorea* (bacteria with beta-glucuronidase activity) and estradiol levels suggests these microbes contribute to estrogen recirculation, potentially worsening endometriosis. The IL-8 correlation with *Subdoligranulum* connects microbial shifts to the inflammatory milieu. These findings are relevant to understanding how [[iron]]-related [[oxidative-stress]] and [[metalloestrogens]] may compound estrogen-driven disease progression.
