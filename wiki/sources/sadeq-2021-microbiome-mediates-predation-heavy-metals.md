# Key Claims

- The gut microbiome of Daphnia pulex mediates the interaction between copper (heavy metal) stress and predation risk stress, providing evidence for a causal role of the microbiome in multi-stressor responses
- Antibiotic manipulation of the microbiome reversed 7 of 12 trait responses across life history, morphology, and body condition, demonstrating the microbiome's causal role
- Each stressor (copper, predation risk) and their combination was associated with distinct microbiome community composition changes
- Sub-lethal copper concentrations influence foraging and assimilation of nutritional resources, leading to increased metabolic cost and reduced energy acquisition
- Digestive physiology represents a shared mode-of-action for the response to metals and predation risk, with the gut microbiome as a key mediator

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Fully factorial experimental design with Daphnia pulex (clone LD33) evaluating copper x predation risk x antibiotic treatment
- **Copper exposure**: Sub-lethal concentrations used to mimic environmentally relevant freshwater contamination
- **Predation risk**: Chemical cues from fish predators to simulate natural predation pressure
- **Antibiotic manipulation**: Used to disrupt the gut microbiome and test whether trait responses require intact microbiota
- **Key finding**: Without antibiotics, copper and predation risk interacted (non-additive effects) on body condition, life history (size at maturity, growth rate, clutch size), and morphology (induced defence)
- **Reversal by antibiotics**: 7/12 interactions between copper and predation risk were reversed when microbiome was manipulated, supporting causal mediation by gut microbiota
- **Community composition**: Distinct microbial community patterns associated with each stressor and their combination, with clear taxonomic and functional diversity shifts
- **Theoretical framework**: Copper affects digestive physiology similarly to starvation (resource limitation), while predation risk affects life history through size-selectivity -- the microbiome connects these pathways
