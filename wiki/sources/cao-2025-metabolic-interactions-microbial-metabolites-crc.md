# Metabolic Interactions: How Gut Microbial Metabolites Influence Colorectal Cancer

## Key Claims

- SCFAs (butyrate, propionate, acetate) are closely linked to CRC inhibition through maintaining intestinal barrier integrity, but under dysbiotic conditions their production or function may be impaired, diminishing anti-tumorigenic effects
- Bile acids, particularly secondary bile acids like deoxycholic acid (DCA), promote CRC through DNA damage via FXR signaling disruption; the ratio of butyrate to secondary bile acids (DCA/butyrate) offers superior diagnostic specificity for distinguishing early CRC from adenomas (AUC = 0.92)
- Tryptophan metabolites operate through three main pathways: kynurenine (accounting for >90% of metabolism), serotonin, and indole pathways, with indole derivatives having anti-inflammatory effects while kynurenine pathway activation promotes immune evasion
- Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) exhibits a biphasic effect in cancer biology: at low concentrations it enhances mitochondrial function and promotes antitumor immunity, while at high concentrations it inhibits cancer cell proliferation via ferroptosis and H2S-mediated protein persulfidation
- Table 1 summarizes 10 dietary patterns targeting CRC through microbial metabolite modulation, with fiber-rich/plant-based diets increasing beneficial SCFAs and high-fat diets increasing harmful DCA

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Comprehensive review of microbial metabolites in CRC covering SCFAs, bile acids, amino acid metabolites (tryptophan, polyamines), H2S, formate, and TMAO
- **Key metabolite-biomarker applications**: Stool metabolite profiling for non-invasive CRC diagnosis; exhaled breath VOCs from microbial metabolism; combining metabolite markers with qFIT for improved screening
- **Therapeutic strategies**: Dietary modulation, FMT, phage therapy, combination with chemotherapy/immunotherapy
