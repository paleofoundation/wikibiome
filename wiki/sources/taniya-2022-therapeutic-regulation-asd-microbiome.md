# Key Claims

- **Microbiota dysbiosis** is integral to ASD pathogenesis, not merely associated
- **Multiple dysbiosis mechanisms**: taxa composition shifts, functional gene loss, metabolite dysregulation, barrier disruption, immune activation
- **Therapeutic targets span entire microbiota-host interface**: microbe selection (FMT, probiotics), metabolite restoration (prebiotics), dietary modification, pharmaceutical adjuncts
- **Emerging evidence** for reversibility of dysbiosis-driven ASD symptoms

# Methods & Findings

Comprehensive review of microbiota-ASD axis and therapeutic interventions:

**Dysbiosis signatures in ASD:**
- Reduced microbial diversity (alpha diversity ↓)
- Altered community structure (beta diversity shifts)
- Enriched pathogenic taxa (e.g., [[clostridium]], [[desulfovibrio]])
- Depleted protective taxa (e.g., [[faecalibacterium-prausnitzii|faecalibacterium]])
- Dysregulated metabolic pathways (SCFA, tryptophan metabolism, bile acid transformation)

**Mechanisms linking dysbiosis to ASD symptoms:**
1. **Barrier disruption**: Loss of tight-junction-supporting bacteria → intestinal permeability
2. **Immune activation**: Pro-inflammatory taxa lipopolysaccharide → TLR4 signaling → systemic inflammation
3. **Metabolite loss**: Depleted SCFA/secondary bile acids → histone deacetylase dysfunction → epigenetic remodeling failure
4. **Neuroactive compound deficit**: Loss of GABA-, serotonin-, dopamine-producing bacteria
5. **Amino acid dysmetabolism**: Altered tryptophan/tyrosine metabolism → neurotransmitter precursor scarcity

**Therapeutic targets:**
- Dysbiotic taxa suppression (antibiotics, competitive exclusion via probiotics)
- Protective taxa restoration (FMT, targeted probiotics)
- Metabolite supplementation (prebiotic, exogenous SCFA, butyrate)
- Barrier support (glutamine, L-arginine, zinc)
- Immune modulation (probiotics, dietary factors)

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

This comprehensive review maps dysbiosis mechanisms but underemphasizes **metal as ecological driver**:

- **Primitive 1 (Metals as Selective Pressures)**: Each dysbiosis mechanism could originate from **metal burden selecting dysbiotic taxa**
 - High [[nickel]] → enrichment of [[clostridium]] species with nickel-dependent hydrogenases
 - High [[cadmium]] → loss of cadmium-sensitive protective taxa
 - High [[lead]] → selection for lead-tolerant pathogens
 
- **Primitive 3 (Mis-metallation)**: [[cadmium]], [[lead]] mis-metallation into zinc-binding sites → Loss of zinc-dependent tight junction proteins (claudins) → barrier disruption

- **Primitive 2 link (Nutritional Immunity)**: Dysbiosis reflects failed antimicrobial defense. Restore protective microbiota = restore metal sequestration capacity = strengthen barrier = reduced systemic inflammation

- **SCFA production requires metal cofactors**: [[butyrate]] synthase and other fermentation enzymes require [[iron]], [[nickel]], [[manganese]] cofactors. Dysbiotic shift may reflect **metal limitation** preventing protective taxa from thriving

- **Zinc depletion signal**: Many barrier-support interventions (glutamine, arginine, zinc) address consequences of dysbiosis. **Root cause**: metal burden → dysbiosis → barrier failure. Without metal correction, interventions may be insufficient
