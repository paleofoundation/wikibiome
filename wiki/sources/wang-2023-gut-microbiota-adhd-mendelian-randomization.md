# Key Claims

- **Potential causal relationship** between gut microbiota and ADHD demonstrated via MR analysis
- **Eubacteriumhallii group** (p=0.013) and **Ruminococcaceae UCG013** (p=0.049) correlated with ADHD risk
- **Butyricicoccus** (p=0.009), **Roseburia** (p=0.009), **Desulfovibrio** (p=0.015), **Lachnospiraceae UC2004group** (p=0.026), **Romboutsia** (p=0.028), and **Oxalobacteraceae** (p=0.048) were protective
- **Ruminococcuscustorquesgroup** was a risk factor for ADHD (p=0.003)
- Reverse MR showed ADHD may lead to increased **Roseburia** abundance (p=0.020)

# Methods & Findings

**Two-sample Mendelian randomization** using 211 gut microbiota taxa from GWAS.

- PGC GWAS (20,183 ADHD cases and 35,191 controls) and FinnGen GWAS (830 ADHD cases and 215,763 controls) used
- IVW, MR-Egger, and weighted median methods applied
- Multiple taxa identified as risk or protective factors
- Bidirectional MR performed
- Sensitivity analyses (heterogeneity, pleiotropy) confirmed robustness

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **ADHD-ASD comorbidity** -- many identified taxa are also implicated in ASD microbiome studies
- **Shared microbial mechanisms** between ADHD and ASD through gut-brain axis
- **Roseburia and Butyricicoccus** as protective butyrate-producing taxa relevant to both conditions
