# Key Claims

- **Probiotic supplementation** may improve ASD symptoms by rebalancing gut microbiota composition
- **Bifidobacterium abundance** was associated with ASD severity in prior work by the same group
- **Gut-brain axis communication** through neurotransmitters (serotonin, GABA, dopamine, melatonin) provides mechanistic basis for probiotic therapy
- **Sleep disturbances and GI dysfunction** in ASD may be modifiable through microbiota intervention

# Methods & Findings

**Study design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel group trial (n = 160 children aged 3-12 years with ASD).

**Intervention:**
- Lyophilized powder containing [[Lactobacillus]], [[Bifidobacteria]], and [[Streptococcus thermophilus]] (10 billion CFU twice daily for 3 months)
- Placebo: maltodextrin
- 3-month washout, then 6-month follow-up
- Add-on therapy irrespective of ABA and TEACCH maintenance

**Outcome measures:**
- Primary: ATEC, CARS, social responsiveness scale, children's sleep health questionnaire, GI assessment, Bristol stool chart
- Secondary: Immunity and inflammation markers, metabolism, metagenome analysis

**Key mechanistic observations from introduction:**
- [[Akkermansia]], [[Coprococcus]], and [[Ruminococcus]] elevated in ASD children
- [[Collinsella]] and [[Clostridium]] at higher levels produce propionic acid and butyric acid with neurotoxic effects
- Probiotics may restore neurotransmitter production via [[Streptococcus thermophilus]] and indigenous spore-forming bacteria

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

This protocol bridges microbiota dysbiosis with neurodevelopmental dysfunction through metabolic intermediaries:

- **Microbial metal dependencies**: Probiotic strains such as Lactobacillus and Bifidobacteria have specific metal cofactor requirements (zinc, manganese) that influence colonization success
- **SCFA-metal axis**: Propionic acid and butyric acid production by dysbiotic taxa may alter metal bioavailability and BBB permeability
- **Neurotransmitter synthesis**: GABA, serotonin, and dopamine production by gut bacteria depends on metal-dependent enzymatic pathways
- **Immune modulation**: Cytokine changes (IL-2, IL-4, IL-13, IL-17A, IFN-gamma) mediated by probiotics may restore nutritional immunity metal-sequestration capacity
