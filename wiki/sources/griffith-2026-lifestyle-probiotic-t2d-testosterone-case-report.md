## Key Findings

- **HbA1c reduction from 7.2% to 5.9% in 3 months** using a 5-strain probiotic (Pendulum Glucose Control, WBF-038) combined with dietary modification and exercise in a 52-year-old male with longstanding T2D who refused pharmacological antidiabetic therapy ([[griffith-2026-lifestyle-probiotic-t2d-testosterone-case-report]], case report, n=1).
- **Triglycerides dropped from 667 to 113 mg/dL** at 3 months, alongside total cholesterol reduction from 213.5 to 177.8 mg/dL, attributed to the combined lifestyle and supplementation intervention ([[griffith-2026-lifestyle-probiotic-t2d-testosterone-case-report]], case report, n=1).
- **Body composition improvements over 6 months**: 13.2 lb decrease in fat mass (48.9 to 35.7 lb), 6.1% reduction in body fat percentage (24.3% to 18.2%), 5.1 lb increase in skeletal muscle mass (87.3 to 92.4 lb), and visceral fat score decrease from 10 to 7 — concurrent with testosterone replacement therapy ([[griffith-2026-lifestyle-probiotic-t2d-testosterone-case-report]], case report, n=1).
- **Hypothesized probiotic mechanism**: [[akkermansia-muciniphila]] produces propionate and its P9 protein increases IL-6 to augment GLP-1 secretion via ICAM-2 on L-cells; [[clostridium-butyricum]] produces butyrate from dietary fiber; [[bifidobacterium-infantis]] produces acetate that cross-feeds butyrate producers; butyrate acts as endogenous agonist of GPR41 and GPR43 on L-cells to further stimulate GLP-1 ([[griffith-2026-lifestyle-probiotic-t2d-testosterone-case-report]], case report).
- **Conflict of interest**: four of five authors are employees of Pendulum Therapeutics Inc., the manufacturer of the probiotic product described. The study was supported by Pendulum Therapeutics.

## Methods

**Study design:** Single-patient case report with clinical follow-up at 3 and 6 months.

**Patient:** 52-year-old Caucasian male, BMI 28.9, presenting with low libido and poor sleep quality. Medical history: T2D diagnosed at age 40, hypertension (on amlodipine 5 mg/day), metabolic syndrome (increased waist circumference, hyperglycemia, hypertension). SHIM score 24 (no erectile dysfunction). ADAM questionnaire consistent with low testosterone. Family history negative for T2D.

**Interventions (initiated simultaneously, May 2024):**
- Testosterone cream 100 mg/day scrotal application (increased to 150 mg/day at 3 months)
- Pendulum Glucose Control probiotic (WBF-038): 2 billion AFU/day containing *Clostridium beijerinckii*, *Clostridium butyricum*, *Akkermansia muciniphila*, *Anaerobutyricum hallii*, *Bifidobacterium infantis*, plus prebiotic chicory inulin
- Omega-3 supplementation (1.3 g/day)
- Vitamin D3 (5000 IU/day)
- Sublingual Vitamin B12 + folate
- Dietary targets: increased fiber, 1 g protein/lb body weight, reduced simple sugars and processed foods
- Exercise: 30 min/day, 5+ days/week (aerobic + resistance)
- Creatine added at 3-month visit (sporadic compliance)

**Measurements:** Plasma glucose, HbA1c, lipid panel, testosterone (total and free), body composition via bioelectrical impedance (InBody), vitamin D, PSA, hematocrit/hemoglobin, estradiol, FSH, LH, prolactin.

## Detailed Summary

The patient had T2D for 12 years with suboptimal control (HbA1c 7.2%) and no current antidiabetic medication. He had previously used metformin with lifestyle changes, achieved normoglycemia, discontinued metformin, then regained weight and returned to hyperglycemia. He presented primarily for low testosterone symptoms, not diabetes management.

**Baseline labs:** total testosterone 232 ng/dL (lower limit of normal), free testosterone 4.99 ng/dL, plasma glucose 150 mg/dL, triglycerides markedly elevated at 667 mg/dL, total cholesterol 213.5 mg/dL, LDL direct 143 mg/dL, HDL 38.6 mg/dL (low), vitamin D 18.7 ng/mL (deficient).

**3-month results:**
- HbA1c: 7.2% to 5.9% (into prediabetes range)
- Plasma glucose: 150 to 113 mg/dL
- Triglycerides: 667 to 113 mg/dL (83% reduction)
- Total cholesterol: 213.5 to 177.8 mg/dL
- HDL: 38.6 to 34.5 mg/dL (slight decrease)
- Total testosterone: 232 to 479 ng/dL
- Weight: 201.7 to 197.5 lb (4.2 lb loss)
- Body fat: 48.9 to 42.1 lb (6.8 lb loss), 24.3% to 21.3%
- Skeletal muscle: 87.3 to 88.6 lb (+1.3 lb)
- Hematocrit: 46.9% to 53.8% (expected TRT effect)

**6-month results (body composition only; no metabolic labs drawn):**
- Weight: 196.2 lb (5.5 lb total loss from baseline)
- Body fat: 35.7 lb (13.2 lb loss from baseline), 18.2%
- Skeletal muscle: 92.4 lb (+5.1 lb from baseline)
- Visceral fat score: 7 (from 10)
- Total testosterone: 845 ng/dL, free testosterone: 22.6 ng/dL (above reference range upper limit of 15.6)
- Hematocrit: 56% (above reference range of 39.8-52%)

The probiotic's mechanism of action centers on GLP-1 stimulation through two pathways: (1) [[akkermansia-muciniphila]] P9 protein increases IL-6, augmenting GLP-1 secretion via ICAM-2 on intestinal L-cells, and (2) butyrate produced by [[clostridium-butyricum]] (from dietary fiber) and cross-fed by acetate from [[bifidobacterium-infantis]] acts as an endogenous GPR41/GPR43 agonist on L-cells, further stimulating GLP-1. The prior RCT of this formulation (Perraudeau et al. 2020) showed a placebo-adjusted A1c reduction of -0.6% over 12 weeks in T2D patients inadequately controlled on metformin.

**Limitations acknowledged by authors:** single patient, no control, multiple simultaneous interventions prevent attribution of individual contributions, unquantified fiber intake, potential confounding from testosterone-driven body composition changes and weight loss.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- **Attribution challenge:** The simultaneous introduction of diet changes, exercise, probiotic, omega-3, vitamins, AND testosterone replacement makes it impossible to isolate the probiotic's contribution. The authors acknowledge this but attribute metabolic improvements primarily to the probiotic + lifestyle, citing the Perraudeau 2020 RCT as supporting evidence.
- **Conflict of interest:** Four of five authors are Pendulum Therapeutics employees, and the study was funded by Pendulum. This is a significant commercial conflict for a case report promoting their product.
- **HDL paradox:** HDL cholesterol decreased from 38.6 to 34.5 mg/dL at 3 months despite overall metabolic improvement — this is the opposite direction expected with metabolic improvement and exercise.
- **Hematocrit safety concern:** At 6 months, hematocrit was 56% (reference max 52%), representing a polycythemia risk from TRT that was not addressed in the discussion.
- **No 6-month metabolic labs:** Glucose and lipid parameters were not measured at the 6-month visit, weakening the durability claim.

## Open Questions

1. What is the independent contribution of the probiotic versus lifestyle changes and testosterone replacement to the metabolic improvements observed?
2. Does [[akkermansia-muciniphila]] P9-mediated GLP-1 stimulation have measurable effects on testosterone metabolism or erectile function independent of glycemic control?
3. Would this probiotic formulation improve metabolic parameters in hypogonadal men without concurrent lifestyle changes?
4. What is the long-term durability of these metabolic improvements beyond 6 months?
5. Does the butyrate-GPR41/43-GLP-1 axis interact with the testosterone-gut microbiome axis, and if so, what are the metal dependencies of this cross-talk?
