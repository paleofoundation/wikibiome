# Bioavailability of Dietary Polyphenols and the Cardiovascular Diseases

## Summary

Review examining the bioavailability of different classes of dietary [[polyphenols]] and their relationship to [[cardiovascular-disease]] prevention, focusing on LDL oxidation inhibition, vasodilation, and platelet aggregation effects.

## Key Findings

### Polyphenol Bioavailability
- Only 5% of dietary polyphenol absorbed in duodenum; 95% reaches colon for microbial fermentation
- Bioavailability ranking (Figure 2): isoflavones (highest) > gallic acid > flavanols > flavanones > flavonol > proanthocyanidins/anthocyanins (lowest)
- Colonic microflora cleave esters and ferment polyphenols to release phenolic acids and other metabolites
- Microbial metabolites of quercetin include glucuronic and sulfate conjugates of isorhamnetin
- Individual variation in absorption rates due to microbial degradation differences

### LDL Oxidation Inhibition
- Quercetin glucuronides and sulfates equally or more active than free aglycone in inhibiting LDL oxidation
- Small intestine metabolites (quercetin-7-glucuronide, quercetin-3-glucuronide) more efficient antioxidants than subsequent liver metabolites
- Fruit and vegetable polyphenols, beverages (coffee, tea, red wine) shown to inhibit LDL oxidation
- Cocoa products particularly effective (20 cocoa consumption significantly increased microbial and plasma (-)-epicatechin)

### Vasodilation Effects
- Grape polyphenols caused significant improvement in brachial artery flow-mediated dilation
- Red wine polyphenols enhanced vasodilation by increasing NO synthase activity and expression
- Tea and coffee polyphenols: conflicting results; cafestol implicated in negative effects
- Cocoa high-flavonoid consumption improved endothelium-dependent FMD by 40% independent of plasma antioxidant activity

### Platelet Aggregation
- Grape juice and red wine consumption decreased platelet aggregation and suppressed thromboxane production
- Polyphenol supplementation inhibited platelet aggregation in animals and humans
- Olive oil with higher polyphenol content associated with reduced platelet aggregation markers

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Microbiota-dependent bioactivation** - Gut microbiota essential for converting [[polyphenols|dietary-polyphenols]] to bioavailable cardioprotective metabolites
2. **Individual variation** - Microbial composition differences explain variable polyphenol responses across individuals
3. **Multi-pathway cardioprotection** - Polyphenols and their microbial metabolites protect against [[cardiovascular-disease]] via LDL oxidation, vasodilation, and anti-platelet mechanisms simultaneously
