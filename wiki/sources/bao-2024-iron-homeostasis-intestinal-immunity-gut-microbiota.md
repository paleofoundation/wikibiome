# Key Claims

- [[iron]] is a vital trace element that plays an active role in growth, development, and reproduction of bacteria such as [[bifidobacterium|Bifidobacteria]]; both deficiency and excess negatively affect bacterial hosts
- Iron homeostasis is regulated primarily by the **hepcidin-ferroportin axis**: hepcidin controls iron absorption from dietary sources, macrophages, and body stores
- Iron imbalance leads to two categories of disorders: iron deficiency diseases (anemia, fatigue) and iron overload diseases (hereditary hemochromatosis, liver disease)
- **Ferroptosis** (iron-dependent programmed cell death) is emerging as a therapeutic target in cancer treatment by inducing oxidative stress via the Fenton reaction
- Iron deficiency reduces commensal beneficial bacteria (Lactobacillus) while iron excess increases harmful bacteria (Bacteroides, E. coli); maintaining balanced iron levels is essential for gut health
- Gut microbiota influence the host via metabolites: [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] (butyrate enhances epithelial barrier, reduces inflammation), indole derivatives (anti-inflammatory via AhR), bile acids (immunomodulation), and TMAO/choline metabolites (cardiovascular risk)
- Gut microbiota can regulate the CNS via the gut-brain axis through serotonin (5-HT), dopamine, GABA, norepinephrine production

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Review article covering iron homeostasis, intestinal immunity, and gut microbiota remodeling
- **Iron transport**: Four main cell types regulate iron cycling - intestinal cells (duodenum), red blood cell precursors (bone marrow), macrophages (iron recycling), liver cells (storage as ferritin)
- **Key transport proteins**: SLC11A2 (DMT1) for iron import; SLC40A1 (FPN/ferroportin) for export; hepcidin as negative regulator; iron regulatory proteins IRP1/2 sense cellular iron
- **Iron and microbiota**: Iron deficiency anemia (IDA) reduced Bacillota abundance, increased Bacteroidota, Pseudomonadota, Patescibacteria; iron-deficient women had lower fecal Lactobacillus
- **Iron supplementation effects**: Increased Enterobacteriaceae and decreased Lactobacillus in infants; iron fortification increased Bacteroidetes in African children
- **Immune regulation**: Gut microbiota shapes innate (macrophages, DCs, neutrophils) and adaptive immunity (T cells, B cells); SCFAs bind GPR41/43/109A receptors on epithelial and immune cells
- **Probiotics and immunity**: Lactobacillus acidophilus and Streptococcus faecalis reduce Clostridium jejuni colonization; Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium eliminate Chlamydomonas from immunodeficient mice
- **Gut-brain axis** (Figure 4): SCFAs activate FFARs and TPH1 for 5-HT production; metabolites influence neuroendocrine-HPA axis pathway and CNS
