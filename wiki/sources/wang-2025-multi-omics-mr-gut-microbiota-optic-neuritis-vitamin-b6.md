# Multi-omics Mendelian Randomization Reveals Immunometabolic Signatures of the Gut Microbiota in Optic Neuritis and the Potential Therapeutic Role of Vitamin B6

## Summary

This multi-omics MR study integrates data on 473 gut microbiota taxa, 731 immune cell phenotypes, 1,400 metabolites, and vitamin B6 to assess causal relationships with optic neuritis (ON), a condition frequently associated with [[multiple-sclerosis]] and NMOSD. The study identified 27 GM taxa with direct causal relationships to ON risk.

## Key Findings

- **27 gut microbiota taxa** causally associated with ON risk
- Three taxa had particularly strong associations with notable interactions with vitamin B6 metabolism
- Mediation analysis revealed specific metabolic and immune pathways through which gut taxa exert effects
- **Vitamin B6** could be a potential therapeutic method by acting on the gut microbiome in ON
- The study highlights the potential of the [[gut-microbiome]] as a target for therapeutic strategies in ON

## Study Design

Three-step approach:
1. Two-sample MR to assess causal relationships between 473 GM taxa and ON (951 cases, 307,092 controls from FinnGen)
2. Mediation analysis with 731 immune cell phenotypes and 1,400 metabolites
3. Exploration of vitamin B6's therapeutic potential via the gut microbiome

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

ON is frequently the first clinical manifestation of [[multiple-sclerosis]], making these findings directly relevant to early MS. The identification of 27 causal gut taxa and the mediation through immune cells and metabolites provides mechanistic evidence for the [[gut-brain-axis]] in demyelinating disease. The vitamin B6 connection is notable because B6 metabolism is influenced by both the [[gut-microbiome]] and [[heavy-metals]] exposure (metals can deplete B vitamins through oxidative stress and enzyme inhibition). This suggests that metal-induced [[dysbiosis]] could impair B6 availability and exacerbate ON/MS risk.

## Limitations

- ON is not synonymous with MS (also occurs in NMOSD and other conditions)
- European ancestry population only
- Vitamin B6 therapeutic potential requires clinical validation
