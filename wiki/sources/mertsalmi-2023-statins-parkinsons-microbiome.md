# Statins and Gut Microbiome in Parkinson's Disease

## Summary

This study examines the relationship between statin use and gut [[gut-microbiome|microbiome]] composition in [[parkinsons-disease]], investigating whether the epidemiologically observed neuroprotective effects of statins in PD may be partially mediated through microbiome modulation.

## Key Findings

- Statin use is associated with distinct gut microbiome profiles in PD patients
- Statin users show increased abundance of specific beneficial taxa compared to non-users
- The statin-associated microbiome changes may contribute to the drug's anti-inflammatory effects
- Cholesterol metabolism alterations in the gut microbiome are modulated by statin use
- Bile acid profiles are influenced by statin-microbiome interactions
- The findings suggest a gut-mediated mechanism for statins' potential neuroprotective effects
- Statin-microbiome interactions may help explain variable statin effects across PD cohorts

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

Statins interact with the metal-microbiome axis through multiple pathways. Bile acid metabolism, altered by both statins and gut microbiome composition, regulates [[iron]] absorption through FXR signaling. Statin-driven anti-inflammatory effects would reduce IL-6-driven [[hepcidin]] upregulation, potentially normalizing [[iron]] distribution. The cholesterol-lowering effect of statins also affects membrane composition, potentially modifying susceptibility to [[ferroptosis]] (which requires specific lipid membrane compositions). Statin-induced shifts in gut microbiome composition -- if they restore SCFA-producing bacteria -- could re-engage [[butyrate]]-mediated [[iron]] regulation. This positions statins as a drug repurposing candidate that addresses the PD metal-microbiome axis through both direct pharmacological and indirect microbiome-mediated mechanisms.

## Limitations

- Observational study; cannot establish causation
- Statin users differ systematically from non-users (healthy user bias)
- Multiple statin types may have different microbiome effects
- Microbiome changes may be confounded by comorbidities requiring statin use
