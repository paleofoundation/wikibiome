# Exploring the Mycobiota in Multiple Sclerosis: Its Influence on Disease Development and Progression

## Key Claims

- MS patients show higher fungal diversity and richness compared to controls, with significant enrichment of *Saccharomyces*, *Torulaspora*, and *Malassezia*
- *Malassezia* demonstrates a strong association with increased disability, aligning with its previous identification in neurodegenerative conditions
- HLA-DRB1*1501 carriers have a distinct mycobiota composition characterized by *Debaryomyces* and *Talaromyces*; these carriers exhibit more severe MS (worse MSFC scores, higher EDSS)
- DMF (dimethyl fumarate) treatment significantly reduces mycobiota presence: from 79.2% detection in naive patients to 42.3% in DMF-treated patients (p = 0.008)
- Dietary fats show greater impact on mycobiota composition in MS patients than controls, highlighting altered lipid metabolism in MS
- *Cladosporium* and *Meyerozyma* combination differentiates MS from controls with AUC 0.723; Shannon diversity alone achieves AUC 0.700
- Plasma chitotriosidase and calprotectin show disease-specific correlations with specific fungal groups

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Prospective case-control; 50 MS patients (24 naive, 26 DMF-treated) and 25 controls; Basque Country, Spain (Dec 2021 - Jun 2023)
- **Methods**: ITS1/ITS2 amplicon sequencing (Ion Torrent PGM); HLA-DRB1*15 genotyping; FFQ (142 food items); calprotectin and chitotriosidase ELISAs; MSFC assessment
- **Demographics**: MS median age 45.5 (naive) and 44.5 (DMF); controls 48.0; significant sex difference (p = 0.001)
- **Key fungal findings**: Ascomycota predominant; *Saccharomyces* characteristic of controls; *Torulaspora* and *Debaryomyces* associated with MS; DMF reduced *Meyerozyma*, *Saccharomycetaceae*, and *Sclerotiniaceae*
- **Severity correlations**: *Torulaspora* in lower disability; unassigned fungi in higher disability; *Aspergillus*, *Nectriaceae*, *Malassezia* correlated with EDSS and MSSS
- **Dietary correlations**: Cholesterol intake higher in MS (p = 0.029); cholesterol correlated with *Talaromyces*, *Nectriaceae*, *Fusarium* in MS but not controls
- **Limitations**: Small sample; ITS sequencing biases; cross-sectional; Basque population may limit generalizability
