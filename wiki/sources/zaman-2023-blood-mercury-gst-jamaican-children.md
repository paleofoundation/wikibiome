## Key Findings

- **Canned fish (sardine, mackerel) consumption** showed a significant interaction with *GSTP1* genotype in predicting blood mercury: children with *GSTP1* Ile/Ile or Ile/Val had 59% and 46% higher mean Hg with canned fish consumption; no effect in Val/Val (P=0.02, P=0.01).
- **Dietary sources associated with higher BHgC**: saltwater fish, canned fish (sardine, mackerel), canned tuna, canned shrimp, string beans, grains, starches (pasta, macaroni, noodles).
- **Gene-environment interaction framework**: GSTT1, GSTM1, GSTP1 polymorphisms modify Hg conjugation to glutathione and downstream elimination. Null (DD) GSTT1 and GSTM1 genotypes impair detoxification capacity.
- Children aged ≥72 months had 25% higher mean BHgC than younger children (accumulation over time).

## Methods

375 TD children (Jamaican ERAJ study, matched controls for ASD case-control). Blood Hg measured by PerkinElmer Elan DRC II ICP-MS at CDC certified lab (MDHHS Lansing MI). TaqMan genotyping for *GSTT1*, *GSTM1* (deletion status) and *GSTP1* Ile105Val (rs1695). Multivariable GLMs for BHgC with dietary/SES/genotype predictors.

## Relevance to Cureva

- Supports assessment of GST polymorphism status alongside blood Hg in high-fish-consuming populations.
- Clinical implication: fish-exposure + GSTP1 Ile/Ile carriers are a high-risk subgroup for Hg accumulation.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Cross-sectional; cannot establish causality between fish consumption and neurodevelopmental outcomes.
- GSTT1/GSTM1 recessive genetic model used given I/I vs I/D ambiguity in TaqMan assay.

## Open Questions

- Whether GSTP1 Val/Val is protective via enhanced or different conjugation, or simply less active with MeHg.
- Role of selenium co-exposure (fish contains both Hg and Se) in modifying toxicity by genotype.
