# Key Claims

- The gut microbiota can directly affect hypercholesterolemia and CAD development via metabolite production (bile acids, coprostanol, SCFAs, TMAO) and indirectly via the immune system
- Bile acid modulation by gut microbiota is a key mechanism: bacteria with bile salt hydrolase (BSH) convert primary to secondary bile acids, activating FXR and TGR5 receptors that regulate cholesterol, lipid, glucose, and energy metabolism
- Coprostanol production by gut bacteria (limited to *Eubacterium* and *Bacteroides* genera) represents an understudied cholesterol-lowering mechanism; conversion rates are bimodal in human populations
- SCFAs regulate gut barrier integrity through tight junction protein expression, activate GPR41/43 receptors, inhibit NF-kB, and serve as HDAC inhibitors; decreased SCFAs may promote CVD
- Gut microbiota LPS activates TLR4 on immune cells, triggering pro-inflammatory cytokines (Th1, CD4, T cells) and downregulating Treg cells, which contributes to atherosclerosis
- Demographic factors including age, sex, ethnicity, and diet significantly modulate both gut microbiome composition and CAD risk -- these confounders complicate causal inference
- Sex differences in microbiome relate to estrogen production cessation at menopause shifting lipoproteins toward LDL; aging decreases CYP7A1 bile acid synthesis and increases NPC1L1 cholesterol absorption

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review with focus on direct/indirect gut microbiota-CAD mechanisms
- **Direct mechanisms**: Bile acid modulation (BSH, FXR, TGR5, FGF19 signaling), coprostanol production, SCFA production, TMAO pathway
- **Indirect mechanisms**: Immune system modulation via LPS/TLR4/NF-kB, T-cell differentiation, Treg suppression, pro-inflammatory cytokine cascades
- **Demographics**: South Asians have higher CAD risk and lower PA rates; African Americans have different dietary patterns; rural populations have greater microbial diversity than urban; Dutch population showed greatest diversity, South Asians the smallest
