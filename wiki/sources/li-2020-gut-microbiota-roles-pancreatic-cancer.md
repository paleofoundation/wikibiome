# Key Claims

- Gut microbiota influence pancreatic cancer through multiple mechanisms: persistent low-grade inflammation via LPS and SCFAs activating NF-kB and MAPK signaling, and modulation of immune responses
- Several bacterial species are directly associated with PC carcinogenesis: H. pylori increases risk via endogenous nitrosation; Fusobacterium spp. has 8.8% malignant potential; Porphyromonas gingivalis associated with higher PC risk
- Risk factors for PC (obesity, diabetes, chronic pancreatitis) all involve gut microbiota alterations that may mediate their carcinogenic effects
- Bifidobacteria combined with anti-PD-L1 immunomodulator induced tumor-specific T cell and CD8+ T cell responses, facilitating anti-PD-L1 effects in melanoma with implications for PC immunotherapy

# Methods/Findings

- **Review scope**: Mini-review covering gut microbiota roles in PC initiation, risk factors, and treatment
- **Bacterial metabolites table**: Comprehensive catalog of LPS, lipoteichoic acid, deoxycholic acid, SCFAs, cytolethal distending toxin, cyclomodulins, and cytotoxic necrotizing factor -- each with specific mechanisms in PC
- **Specific bacteria table**: H. pylori, P. gingivalis, Fusobacterium spp., Bifidobacteria, Bacteroides, E. faecalis, Enterobacteriaceae -- each with distinct roles in PC pathogenesis
- **Obesity connection**: Obese mice show decreased GM diversity with increases in Firmicutes and reductions in Bacteroides, generating procarcinogenic microbial metabolites
- **Diabetes connection**: T2DM associated with moderate and profound intestinal dysbiosis; causal relationships between GM, SCFAs, and T2DM via GPR-41/43 stimulating gut peptide secretion
- **Immunotherapy**: GM has been recognized as modulator influencing immune responses; PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint inhibitors more efficacious in cancers with Kras mutations
