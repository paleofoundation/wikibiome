# The Role of Sex Hormones in Multiple Sclerosis

## Key Claims

- MS has a 3:1 female-to-male ratio, suggesting hormonal associations; pregnancy (particularly the third trimester) reduces relapse rate by 70%, linked to estriol-mediated immunomodulation
- Estrogen prevents changes in gut microbiota composition and cross-talk between gut microbiota and sex hormones may mediate neuroprotection in MS
- Estriol (pregnancy estrogen) has the most clinical evidence: shown to reduce gadolinium-enhancing lesions in a pilot study of 10 RRMS women; induces tolerogenic dendritic cells; prevents hippocampal changes in EAE
- Progesterone demonstrates neuroprotective effects: recovered neurosteroidogenesis in EAE; reduced demyelination; promotes remyelination through progesterone receptor-dependent pathway in cuprizone model; decreases amyloid precursor protein formation
- Testosterone is significantly lower in men with MS correlating with disability; EAE is less severe in female mice pretreated with dihydrotestosterone and worse in castrated mice; testosterone administration restores excitatory synaptic transmission in EAE hippocampus
- Estradiol and progesterone may reduce central immune responses in the brain and regulate oligodendrocyte, astrocyte function; individuals with high estrogen/progesterone ratio had more active MRI lesions
- Vitamin D and sex steroids share nuclear receptor signaling; functional synergy between vitamin D3 and 17-beta estradiol offers new RRMS treatment options

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Review article (2018) covering sex hormone-specific immunomodulatory effects and remyelination potential in MS
- **Estrogen mechanisms**: Shifts Th1 to Th2 response; promotes OPC proliferation; reduces MMP-9 levels; increases oligodendrocyte generation during pregnancy; acts via ER-alpha and ER-beta receptors with potentially different effects
- **Progesterone mechanisms**: Increases MBP expression; reduces demyelination area; acts through progesterone receptor; combination with estrogen in EAE inhibits major neurochemical abnormalities
- **Testosterone mechanisms**: Neuroprotective in RRMS; increases brain-derived neurotrophic factor and platelet-derived growth factors; increases gray matter in frontal cortex
- **Pregnancy MS Study**: Prospective study of 254 women with 269 pregnancies; relapse rate fell 70% during third trimester; relapse rate increased by 70% in first 3 months postpartum
- **Limitations**: Most evidence from animal models (EAE); limited human clinical trials; complex interactions between multiple hormones not well studied; estriol vs estradiol comparative data limited
