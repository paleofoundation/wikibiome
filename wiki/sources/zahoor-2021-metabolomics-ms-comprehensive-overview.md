# An Emerging Potential of Metabolomics in Multiple Sclerosis: A Comprehensive Overview

## Summary

This comprehensive review surveys metabolomics studies in [[multiple-sclerosis]] across CSF, blood, and urine samples. The commonly perturbed metabolic pathways include lipid metabolism (alpha-linoleic acid), nucleotide metabolism, amino acid metabolism, tricarboxylic acid cycle, D-ornithine and D-arginine pathways.

## Key Findings

### CSF Metabolomics
- Higher acetate and lower formate levels in MS CSF, reflecting perturbed energy metabolism
- Increased lactate and fructose, decreased creatinine and phenylalanine
- Beta-hydroxyisobutyrate (BHIB) elevated in patients with active plaques vs. inactive
- Increased sorbitol, fructose, and lactate in SPMS vs. RRMS, correlating with neurologic disability
- Kynurenine pathway metabolites altered: decreased kynurenic acid (KYNA), increased 3-hydroxyanthranilic acid (3HAA) in MS

### Blood/Serum Metabolomics
- Lower levels of phospholipids, with changes correlating with disease activity, duration, and disability
- CSF metabolome can predict stage-specific disease pathology
- Tryptophan metabolism significantly altered: kynurenine/tryptophan ratio changes discriminate MS subtypes
- Kynurenic acid and quinolinic acid levels distinguish MS subtypes

### Urine Metabolomics
- Altered levels of tryptophan and amino acid metabolites
- Potential for non-invasive disease monitoring

### Key Metabolic Pathways
- **Lipid metabolism**: alpha-linoleic acid pathway, phospholipid alterations
- **Amino acid metabolism**: tryptophan/kynurenine pathway, arginine metabolism
- **Energy metabolism**: TCA cycle disruption, increased anaerobic markers
- **Nucleotide metabolism**: altered purine and pyrimidine pathways

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This review supports the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] model through:

1. **Tryptophan/kynurenine pathway disruption** - this pathway is heavily dependent on gut microbial metabolism, and [[heavy-metals]] disrupt tryptophan-metabolizing bacteria, connecting to [[indoles]] and AHR signaling
2. **SCFA-related metabolite changes** - acetate alterations in CSF may reflect gut-derived [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] crossing the BBB
3. **[[oxidative-stress]] markers** - metabolomic evidence of mitochondrial dysfunction and oxidative damage aligns with metal-induced ROS generation and [[ferroptosis]]
4. **Lipid peroxidation products** - phospholipid changes may reflect metal-catalyzed lipid oxidation

## Limitations

- Heterogeneous study designs and metabolomics platforms across reviewed studies
- Small sample sizes in most individual studies
- Limited integration with microbiome data
