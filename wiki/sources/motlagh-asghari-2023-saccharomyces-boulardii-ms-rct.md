# The Effect of Probiotic Supplementation on the Clinical and Para-clinical Findings of Multiple Sclerosis: A Randomized Clinical Trial

## Key Findings

- Prospective, randomized, double-blinded, placebo-controlled 4-month trial in relapsing-remitting MS (RRMS, 2017 McDonald criteria; EDSS ≤ 4.5; n=40).
- **Intervention**: BioDigest® (250 mg/day *Saccharomyces boulardii* ≥10¹⁰ CFU + lactose filler + magnesium acetate).
- **Inflammation**: hs-CRP significantly decreased in probiotic group (p<0.001) vs placebo.
- **Oxidative stress**: Total antioxidant capacity (TAC) significantly increased in probiotic group (p=0.004). Malondialdehyde (MDA) decreased in both groups (p=0.004 and p=0.01) but without between-group difference.
- **Clinical outcomes**: Pain (VAS, p=0.004), fatigue (Fatigue Severity Scale, p=0.01) significantly improved. Quality of life (SF-36 somatic and social dysfunction subscales) improved (p=0.01). General Health Questionnaire (GHQ-28) improved.
- **Design strength**: block randomization stratified on age, sex, BMI; allocation concealment; identical probiotic/placebo capsules; 1,000 IU vitamin D3 background in both groups.
- Registration: IRCT20161022030424N1.

## Methods

Convenience sample of RRMS outpatients, Emam Reza Hospital, Tabriz. Inclusion: 18–55 y, EDSS ≤ 4.5, no relapse 3 months prior. Exclusion: pregnancy, breastfeeding, probiotic/yogurt consumption, active GI disease. hs-CRP by turbidimetry; TAC and MDA by ELISA kits. 2-week washout preceded intervention. Sample size based on GHQ-28 effect (n=20/arm, power 90%, α 0.05, β 0.1).

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Promising intervention (single RCT)**: *S. boulardii* 10¹⁰ CFU/day × 4 months in stable RRMS for symptomatic improvement (pain, fatigue, QoL) and hs-CRP reduction. Triangle: I→f (probiotic alters microbiome — not sequenced here); f→D (inflammatory/oxidative cascade → MS symptoms — supported by broader literature); I→D (RCT-graded).
- **Distinguishing feature**: Unlike bacterial probiotics, *S. boulardii* is a fungus unaffected by antibiotics — potentially useful during antibiotic courses.
- **Caveat**: Single-center, small sample, no microbiome sequencing, 4-month duration — classify as *promising* not *validated*.

## Open Questions

- Durability of effects beyond 4 months.
- Microbiome compositional shifts during *S. boulardii* intervention.
- Whether effect extends to progressive MS subtypes.
- Interaction with disease-modifying therapies (patients in this trial continued routine DMT).
