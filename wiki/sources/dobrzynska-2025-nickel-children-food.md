# Nickel Exposure from Food and Levels in Children's Blood and Tissues: Health Implications

## Overview

Narrative review synthesizing 21 studies on [[dietary-nickel-exposure]] and tissue levels in children aged 0-18 years. Establishes that children are a particularly vulnerable population for [[nickel]] toxicity due to immature detoxification systems, higher food intake relative to body weight, and high consumption of nickel-rich foods.

## Regulatory Thresholds

- **TDI** (EFSA): 13 μg/kg body weight
- **LOAEL**: 4.3 μg Ni/kg body weight
- **Critical acute effect**: eczematous flare-up in nickel-sensitized individuals (~15% of the general population), linking to [[nickel-allergy]]

## Nickel-Rich Food Sources (mg Ni/kg)

| Food Category | Nickel Content (mg/kg) |
|---|---|
| Cocoa | 8.2-17.1 |
| Grains, legumes, cereals | 0.3-9.8 |
| Nuts | 0.2-6.7 |
| Soy | 0.1-5.1 |
| Mushrooms | 1.5-5.0 |

## Children's Dietary Intake Exceeds Safety Thresholds

- A French study (children 1-36 months) found **7.9%-37.9%** exceeded TDI under lower bound assumptions
- Under upper bound assumptions, **up to 98%** exceeded TDI
- Chocolate and cocoa products accounted for **10% of mean daily nickel intake** in French children
- These findings highlight the importance of [[dietary-nickel-exposure]] monitoring in pediatric populations

## Infant Formula Concern

Soy-based infant formulas had higher nickel concentrations than cow's milk formulas:
- With iron fortification: **0.05 mg/kg**
- Without iron fortification: **0.02 mg/kg**

## Biomonitoring Data

- Nickel concentrations measured across multiple countries in urine, blood, hair, and toenails
- Children living near industrial areas (e.g., petrochemical facilities) had elevated urinary nickel correlated with markers of [[oxidative-stress]]
- High nickel in placenta was negatively correlated with birth weight, consistent with findings in [[nickel-reproductive-toxicity]]

## Endocrine Disruption

Nickel affects multiple endocrine organs:
- Hypothalamus
- Pituitary gland
- Adrenal gland
- Thyroid
- Gonads

This positions nickel as a potential endocrine disruptor with implications for child development.

## Connections

This review integrates data relevant to [[nickel]], [[dietary-nickel-exposure]], and [[nickel-allergy]]. The findings on pediatric vulnerability complement clinical perspectives from [[tuchman-2015-nickel-dermatitis-children|Tuchman et al. (2015)]] on nickel contact dermatitis in children.
