# Maternal Probiotic L. rhamnosus HN001 Alters Postpartum Anxiety and Cortical Monoamines

## Key Findings

- **Peripartum mood and anxiety disorders (PMADs) affect 15-20% of peripartum women**, representing a major unmet therapeutic need where microbiome-based interventions could offer prevention without psychotropic medication exposure during pregnancy and lactation (Lonstein et al. 2024, animal model)
- **L. rhamnosus HN001 treatment from pregnancy day 1 through postpartum day 10** altered postpartum anxiety-like behavior in rats, providing preclinical evidence for the same strain used in the human PiP trial
- **Cortical monoamine concentrations were modulated by probiotic treatment**, including changes in serotonin, dopamine, and/or norepinephrine levels in brain regions associated with anxiety and maternal behavior
- **Probiotic effects were examined against a background of chronic variable pregnancy stress**, modeling the real-world context where peripartum mood disorders develop in stressed individuals
- **Gut microbiome composition was altered by probiotic treatment**, confirming that L. rhamnosus HN001 engrafts and modifies the maternal gut ecology during the peripartum period

## Methods

- **Design**: Controlled animal model study with 2x2 factorial design (probiotic vs. vehicle x stressed vs. unstressed)
- **Subjects**: Female Sprague-Dawley rats during pregnancy and early postpartum
- **Intervention**: Lactocaseibacillus rhamnosus HN001 administered in drinking water from pregnancy day 1 through postpartum day 10
- **Stress model**: Chronic variable pregnancy stress (randomized daily stressors) applied to half the animals
- **Behavioral assessments**: Elevated plus maze (anxiety), open field test, maternal caregiving behaviors (pup retrieval, nursing posture, nest building)
- **Neurochemistry**: Cortical monoamine levels (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine and metabolites) measured by HPLC
- **Microbiome**: Fecal microbiome composition assessed by 16S rRNA sequencing

## Detailed Summary

Lonstein et al. provide the mechanistic preclinical foundation for the L. rhamnosus HN001 strain that has shown promise in human peripartum mental health trials. The study uses a chronic variable pregnancy stress paradigm to model the stress exposure that typically precedes peripartum mood and anxiety disorders, then evaluates whether concurrent probiotic treatment can buffer against stress-induced anxiety and neurochemical changes. The L. rhamnosus HN001 strain is the same strain used in the PiP (Probiotics in Pregnancy) study and in [[rauniyar-2023-maternal-mental-health-probiotics-dietary-fibre]], making this animal study a mechanistic companion to the human clinical data. The cortical monoamine findings are significant because they provide a plausible neural mechanism for probiotics' anxiolytic effects: the gut microbiome influences central neurotransmitter systems through the vagus nerve, microbial metabolites (SCFAs, tryptophan metabolites), and immune signaling pathways. By demonstrating that a specific probiotic strain alters both behavior and brain neurochemistry during the peripartum period, the study supports the concept that the [[gut-brain-axis]] is a tractable therapeutic target for [[postpartum-depression]] and related conditions. The caregiving behavior assessment adds ecological validity, as impaired maternal behavior is a clinically relevant consequence of peripartum mood disorders.

## Open Questions

- Which specific cortical monoamine changes (serotonin vs. dopamine vs. norepinephrine) are most affected by L. rhamnosus HN001, and do these correlate with specific behavioral improvements?
- Does the probiotic effect on anxiety persist beyond the supplementation period, or is continued treatment needed?
- How does the chronic stress context modify the probiotic's neurochemical effects -- does stress create a different microbiome landscape that alters probiotic engraftment?
- Can the cortical monoamine changes be mapped to human neuroimaging findings in peripartum mood disorders to validate translational relevance?
- What role does iron status during pregnancy play in modulating both gut microbiome composition and probiotic efficacy?
