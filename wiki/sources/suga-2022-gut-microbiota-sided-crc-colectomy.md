# The Gut Microbiota Composition in Patients with Right- and Left-Sided Colorectal Cancer and After Curative Colectomy

## Key Claims

- Gut microbial composition differs significantly between right-sided colon cancer (RCC) and sigmoid colon cancer (SCC) patients, with RCC showing higher diversity (Chao1 index P < 0.05) and distinct beta diversity clustering (Bray-Curtis ANOVA P < 0.01)
- *Firmicutes* was significantly dominant in the RCC group while the SCC group had higher abundance of *Verrucomicrobia*; at the genus level, LEfSe revealed enrichment of *Ruminococcaceae*, *Streptococcaceae*, *Clostridiaceae*, and *Gemellaceae* in RCC
- Post-colectomy microbiota changes were site-dependent: sigmoid colon resection (SCR) group was more diverse than right colon resection (RCR) group (P < 0.01), and *Clostridia* increased in post-operative SCR
- T-RFLP analysis showed reduced *Clostridium cluster XIVa* in SCC and reduced *Clostridium cluster IX* in RCC patients compared to controls
- Cholesterol transport- and metabolism-related enzymes were specifically upregulated in RCC, while cobalamin metabolism was downregulated in SCC, suggesting distinct metabolic functional shifts by tumor location

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Cross-sectional comparison of gut microbiota by tumor location and post-colectomy changes
- **Participants**: 41 subjects total (10 controls, 10 RCC, 6 SCC, 9 right colon resection, 6 sigmoid colon resection) from Mie Prefectural General Medical Center, Japan
- **Methods**: T-RFLP profiling and 16S rRNA V3-V4 amplicon sequencing on Illumina MiSeq; PICRUSt2 functional prediction; LEfSe for differential abundance
- **Post-operative follow-up**: Average 37 months (range 13-90 months) after colectomy
