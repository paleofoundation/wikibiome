# Key Claims

- **PPD prevalence** is 10-15% globally, with about 80% of cases going undiagnosed and untreated
- **Strongest risk factors**: Previous history of depression/psychiatric illness, depressive symptoms during pregnancy, gestational diabetes, and lack of spousal/social support
- **Other significant factors**: Complications during pregnancy, low socioeconomic status, stressful life events, vitamin D deficiency, diet and nutrition, postpartum anemia
- **PPD hampers** mother-child bonding, infant feeding practices, and child's emotional, intellectual, and social development
- Genetic, epigenetic, cultural factors, and vitamin D insufficiency require further investigation

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Literature review; PubMed search yielding 77 articles, 58 included after screening
- **Risk factor categories**: Sociodemographic, biological, psychological, obstetric, pediatric, and cultural
