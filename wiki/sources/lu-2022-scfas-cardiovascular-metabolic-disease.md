# Microbiota-Derived Short-Chain Fatty Acids: Implications for Cardiovascular and Metabolic Disease

## Summary

Comprehensive review of [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] (SCFAs) in [[cardiovascular-disease]], covering their production, signal transduction through GPCRs and HDAC inhibition, and roles in atherosclerosis, hypertension, myocardial infarction, and stroke.

## Key Findings

### SCFA Production and Signaling
- Acetate, propionate, and butyrate comprise >95% of SCFAs in a 3:1:1 ratio in intestinal lumen
- Signal through GPCRs: GPR41 (propionate > butyrate > acetate), GPR43 (propionate = butyrate = acetate), GPR109A (butyrate only)
- HDAC inhibition: butyrate is the most potent HDACi, followed by propionate

### Atherosclerosis
- Butyrate lowers lipogenic gene levels (Acot1, Acot2, Perilipin 2, Plin5) and fatty acid degradation genes
- Butyrate inhibits HDAC3, induces FGF21, promotes lipid oxidation and ketogenesis
- SCFAs suppress macrophage [[inflammation]] via NF-kB pathway and NLRP3 inflammasome
- Butyrate and propionate stimulate Treg cell generation, modulate Th1/Th17 balance
- SCFAs reduce VCAM-1, MCP-1 expression and monocyte recruitment

### Hypertension
- Fecal SCFA levels correlate with blood pressure in cross-sectional studies
- Butyrate lowers BP via PRR-mediated intrarenal renin-angiotensin system suppression
- Propionate modulates renin release via Olfr78 receptor in juxtaglomerular apparatus
- GPR41 and Olfr78 play opposing roles in BP regulation, creating a "buffering" effect
- Acetate supplementation downregulates RAAS in kidney

### Myocardial Infarction
- SCFAs modulate inflammatory response post-MI by promoting M2 macrophage polarization
- Propionate restores myeloid cells and CX3CR1+ monocytes in antibiotic-treated MI mice
- Butyrate administration down-regulates TNF-alpha, IL-1beta and up-regulates IL-10

### Stroke
- SCFAs cross blood-brain barrier and influence neuroinflammation
- Butyrate reduces ROS and improves gut barrier after stroke

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Metal-induced SCFA depletion** - [[cadmium]] and [[lead]] exposure depletes SCFA-producing Clostridia, directly reducing the cardioprotective effects described here
2. **[[Inflammation]] amplification** - Without SCFA-mediated NLRP3 inflammasome suppression, metal-exposed individuals face amplified cardiovascular [[inflammation]]
3. **Blood pressure dysregulation** - Metal-induced loss of SCFA producers disrupts the GPR41/Olfr78 balance, potentially contributing to hypertension
4. **[[Oxidative-stress]] vulnerability** - Reduced butyrate means less ROS scavenging and more [[oxidative-stress]] damage to vasculature
5. **[[ferroptosis]] connection** - SCFA depletion reduces antioxidant defenses that may normally protect against [[iron]]-mediated lipid peroxidation in cardiac tissue
