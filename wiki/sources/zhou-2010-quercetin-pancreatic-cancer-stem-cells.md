# Key Claims

- [[Quercetin]], a major dietary [[polyphenol]] found in fruits and vegetables, diminishes self-renewal of [[pancreatic-cancer]] stem cells (CSCs) as measured by spheroid and colony formation assays
- Combination of quercetin with [[sulforaphane]] (from broccoli) had synergistic effects, eliminating the pro-proliferative NF-kB binding feature while preventing EMT
- Quercetin overcomes apoptosis resistance of CSCs through caspase-dependent mechanisms and PARP cleavage
- In vivo, quercetin and sulforaphane combination strongly reduced growth of CSC-enriched xenografts without toxic side effects

# Methods/Findings

- **Cell models**: MIA-PaCa2 (CSC-high) and BxPc-3 (CSC-low) pancreatic cancer cell lines; CSC-high characterized by 82% spheroid-forming capacity, high ALDH activity, CD44+/CD24- expression
- **Self-renewal inhibition**: 200 microM quercetin eliminated most CSC-high tumor cells within 48h; combination with 10 microM sulforaphane completely abolished spheroid formation
- **ALDH activity**: Quercetin reduced ALDH-positive cells from 25% to 8% alone and to 3% with sulforaphane combination
- **NF-kB effects**: Quercetin enhanced DNA binding of NF-kB (unexpected) but sulforaphane markedly reduced it; combination eliminated NF-kB-induced DNA binding in CSC-high cells
- **EMT prevention**: Quercetin prevented expression of EMT-associated proteins; effect stronger with sulforaphane co-treatment
- **In vivo**: Daily quercetin + sulforaphane for 3 consecutive days, 7 days after tumor cell implantation; strongly reduced CSC-xenograft growth with reduced Ki-67 proliferation and decreased CD31-positive endothelial cells
- **Apoptosis**: Combination induced caspase-3 activation, caspase-8 and -9 involvement, and PARP cleavage in CSC-high cells; Z-VAD-FMK confirmed caspase-dependent mechanism
