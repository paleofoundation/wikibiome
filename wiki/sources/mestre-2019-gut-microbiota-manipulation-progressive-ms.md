# Manipulation of Gut Microbiota Influences Immune Responses, Axon Preservation, and Motor Disability in a Model of Progressive MS

## Key Claims

- ABX treatment (day 55-70 post-TMEV infection) during the pre-symptomatic phase prevents motor dysfunction and limits axon damage in a progressive MS model, with fewer CD4+ and CD8+ T cells in the CNS
- Gut microbiota recolonization after ABX cessation worsened motor function and disease severity, demonstrating that the restored microbiota has pathogenic potential
- Probiotic supplementation (Vivomixx) after ABX cessation partially modifies microbiota recolonization but does not prevent motor disability worsening
- ABX-treated TMEV mice show predominantly Proteobacteria (71.9% vs ~40% control) with significant decreases in Bacteroidetes, Firmicutes, Actinobacteria, and Cyanobacteria
- ABX treatment increases CD39+ T cells and CD5+CD1d+ B cells in spleen, suggesting promotion of regulatory immune populations
- Microglia adopt a round ameboid morphology with anti-inflammatory gene profile in ABX-treated TMEV mice; recolonization disrupts this beneficial phenotype
- Recolonization increases plasma acetate and butyrate levels, yet motor function worsens, suggesting complex SCFA effects or that specific microbial taxa are more important than total SCFA levels
- KEGG pathway analysis: ABX-treated mice enriched for infectious disease, energy metabolism, amino acid metabolism; TMEV-only mice enriched for neurodegenerative disease pathways

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Experimental; SJL/J female mice; TMEV infection at day 0; ABX (ampicillin, metronidazole, neomycin sulfate, vancomycin) days 55-70; assessment at days 70 and 85
- **Motor assessments**: Horizontal activity (HACTV), vertical activity (VACTV), and rotarod performance; ABX treatment significantly improved all three measures
- **16S rRNA sequencing**: Metagenomic predictions via PICRUSt; significant compositional differences between all treatment groups
- **Recolonization effects**: After ABX cessation, *Bacteroidetes* dominated recolonization; *Proteobacteria* reduced; *Ruminococcus* and *Mucispirillum* significantly changed
- **Vivomixx probiotic effects**: Modified recolonization pattern with increased *Proteobacteria* and *Actinobacteria* but did not prevent motor worsening
- **Immunology**: ABX increased CD39+ T cells (regulatory phenotype); flow cytometry for CD4, CD8, CD39, CD5, CD1d subsets
- **Axon preservation**: Neurofilament H immunostaining in spinal cord showed better axon preservation with ABX; increased Iba1+ microglia with CD39 expression
- **Limitations**: Animal model; ABX cocktail non-specific; short treatment window; Vivomixx not sufficient to prevent recolonization-associated worsening
