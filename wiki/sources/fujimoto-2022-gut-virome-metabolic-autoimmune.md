# Key Claims

- The intestinal [[virome]], primarily composed of bacteriophages, plays a pivotal role in disease pathogenesis through trans-kingdom interactions with bacteria, yet has received far less attention than bacterial microbiome research
- In CVD, limited case-control studies show: percentage of *Virgaviridae* (rod-shaped plant viruses) was higher and *Microviridae* family lower in CHD patients compared to controls; in ACVD patients, several bacteriophages were more or less abundant than healthy controls
- In hypertension, two viral types predominate in fecal samples: *Erwinia* phage phiEaH2 and *Lactococcus* phage 1706; certain viruses (*Cnaphalocrocis medinalis* granulovirus, *Cronobacter* phage CR3, *Streptococcus* virus phiAbc2) could distinguish healthy from hypertensive individuals with superior resolution compared to bacteria
- In NAFLD, lower phage diversity and bacteriophage number were associated with disease severity; *Lactococcus* and *Leuconostoc* phages inversely correlated with fibrosis while *Lactobacillus* phages correlated positively
- In T2DM, specific phageome changes (four *Siphoviridae*, two *Podoviridae*, one unclassified family) could not be explained by bacterial co-variation alone, suggesting independent viral roles
- The vast majority of sequence reads are "viral dark matter" -- they do not align with known viral sequences, representing a major obstacle to comprehensive virome characterization
- Phage therapy is proposed as an alternative to antibiotics for controlling dysbiosis and eliminating pathogens

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review of intestinal virome in metabolic and autoimmune diseases
- **Table 1**: Human studies of gut virome in metabolic disorders (obesity, MetS, NAFLD, T2DM, CVD, hypertension)
- **Table 2**: Human studies of intestinal virome in autoimmune diseases (islet autoimmunity, RA, T1DM)
- **Key challenge**: No universally conserved gene in viruses equivalent to 16S rRNA, requiring whole metagenome shotgun sequencing
- **Autoimmune findings**: crAss-like phages are a primary component of healthy gut virome; they significantly decreased in RA and SLE patients
