# Key Claims

- **Updated global PPD prevalence**: 19.18% (95% CI: 18.02-20.34%) across 412 studies of 792,055 women from 46 countries
- **Sugar-sweetened beverage (SSB) consumption** at the national level predicted higher PPD rates (Coef.=0.325, p=0.044)
- When SSB consumption increased within a country over time, PPD prevalence correspondingly increased (Coef.=0.129, p=0.026)
- **Seafood consumption** may be protective against PPD through omega-3 fatty acids and anti-[[inflammation]]atory properties
- Diet contributes to cross-national variation in [[postpartum-depression]] prevalence

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis using EPDS; random effects meta-analysis and meta-regression with Global Dietary Database data
- **Update**: Extends [[hahn-holbrook-2018-economic-health-predictors-ppd-prevalence]] with 121 additional studies and dietary factor analysis
