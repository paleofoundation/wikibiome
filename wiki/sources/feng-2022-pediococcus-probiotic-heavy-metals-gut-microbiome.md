# Key Claims

- **Probiotic yogurt with Pediococcus acidilactici GR-1** reduced copper (34.45%) and nickel (38.34%) in blood after 12 weeks
- **Conventional yogurt also reduced** these metals but less effectively (16.41% and 27.57%)
- **Metagenomic and metabolomic studies** identified close correlation between gut microbiota and host metal metabolism
- **Enriched members of Blautia and Bifidobacterium** correlated positively with antioxidant capacities
- **Probiotic yogurt consumption** is effective and affordable for combating toxic metal exposure

# Methods & Findings

Randomized double-blind controlled trial in 152 occupational workers from metal industry in Pakistan:

- Two groups: probiotic yogurt (with HM-resistant P. acidilactici GR-1) vs conventional yogurt for 12 weeks
- Blood metal content, gut microbiome, and metabolome analyzed
- Protective effect of GR-1 on intestinal antioxidative capacity demonstrated
