# Key Findings

- **Oral microbiota alpha-diversity inversely associated with breast cancer**: OR 0.86 per SD increase in diversity — lower oral diversity = higher breast cancer risk [[wu-2022-oral-fecal-microbiome-breast-cancer-ghana]], case-control, n=881.
- **Porphyromonas** inversely associated with breast cancer (protective); **Propionibacterium** positively associated (risk) [[wu-2022-oral-fecal-microbiome-breast-cancer-ghana]].
- **Oral-fecal correlations**: Strong correlations between oral and fecal microbiota composition, supporting an **oral-gut-breast axis** where oral dysbiosis influences gut microbiome and systemic cancer risk [[wu-2022-oral-fecal-microbiome-breast-cancer-ghana]].
- Fecal Bacteroidetes abundance lower in breast cancer cases compared to controls.
- Many oral microbial metrics associated with both breast cancer AND non-malignant breast disease but not controls — suggesting microbiome changes precede malignant transformation.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- [[porphyromonas]] — Inverse association with breast cancer is notable given P. gingivalis's role as a periodontal pathogen. Different Porphyromonas species may have opposing effects.
- [[gemella]] — Listed among discriminating oral taxa in breast cancer.
- Establishes the oral-gut-breast translocation axis as a measurable epidemiological association.
- Connects to the estrobolome hypothesis: oral dysbiosis → gut microbiome alteration → estrogen metabolism disruption → breast cancer risk.

## Relevance to Cureva

- Oral microbiome profiling could serve as a **non-invasive breast cancer risk biomarker**.
- Interventions targeting oral microbiome restoration (beyond dental hygiene) may have cancer-preventive implications.
