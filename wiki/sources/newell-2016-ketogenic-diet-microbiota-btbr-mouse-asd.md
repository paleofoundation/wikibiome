# Key Claims

- **KD triggers gut microbial compositional remodeling** in BTBR ASD mouse model
- **KD caused anti-microbial-like effect**: significantly decreased total host bacterial abundance in cecal and fecal matter
- **KD counteracted the common ASD phenotype** of low Firmicutes:Bacteroidetes ratio in BTBR mice
- **KD reversed elevated Akkermansia muciniphila** content in BTBR mice to levels comparable to control B6 mice

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Juvenile male C57BL/6 (B6, n=21) and BTBR T+Itpr3tf/J (BTBR, n=25) mice fed standard chow (13% kcal fat) or ketogenic diet (75% kcal fat) for 10-14 days, sacrificed at 7 weeks.

**Key findings (Table 2 - comprehensive):**

**Cecal microbiota (16S rRNA qRT-PCR):**
- [[Akkermansia muciniphila]]: BTBR-chow 1011 +/- 153 vs. BTBR-KD 0.2 +/- 0.02 (dramatically reduced by KD)
- [[bifidobacterium]]: reduced by KD in both genotypes
- [[Clostridium coccoides]]: BTBR-chow 46,568 vs. BTBR-KD 11,157 (reduced by KD)
- [[Clostridium leptum]]: relatively maintained in BTBR-KD
- [[lactobacillus]]: dramatically reduced by KD in both genotypes
- Total Firmicutes: reduced by KD
- Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio: BTBR-chow 6.9 vs. BTBR-KD 32.5 (reversed by KD)

**Fecal microbiota:**
- Similar patterns as cecal but with some differences in magnitude
- [[Enterobacteriaceae]]: reduced from 116 in BTBR-chow to 36 in BTBR-KD

**PLS-DA analysis (Figure 1):**
- Clear separation between all four groups (B6-chow, B6-KD, BTBR-chow, BTBR-KD) in both cecal and fecal samples
- VIP scores identified Akkermansia muciniphila as the top discriminating taxon

**Anti-microbial effect:**
- KD reduced total bacterial content by 78% (cecal) and 28% (fecal) in BTBR animals

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Akkermansia normalization**: BTBR mice have elevated Akkermansia (a mucin-degrading bacterium); KD normalization may restore mucin barrier-dependent metal permeability control
- **Total bacterial reduction**: KD's "anti-microbial" effect reduces overall microbial metal demand, potentially redistributing metals to host
- **Clostridium reduction**: Decreased Clostridium coccoides reduces cobalt-dependent propionic acid production, a known neurotoxic metabolite in ASD
- **Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes reversal**: KD shifts this ratio in BTBR mice, altering the community-level metal metabolism profile (Firmicutes vs. Bacteroidetes have distinct metal requirements)
- **Translational limitation**: Short intervention (10-14 days) in mice; human KD studies needed with metal biomarker tracking
