# Key Claims

- **Maternal valproate use during pregnancy** is associated with significantly increased risk of ASD and childhood autism
- **508 children exposed to valproate** had absolute risk of 4.42% (95% CI 2.59-7.46%) for ASD vs 2.50% (95% CI 1.30-4.81%) for unexposed
- **Adjusted HR of 2.9** (95% CI 1.7-4.9) for ASD and 5.2 (95% CI 2.7-10.0) for childhood autism
- **Risk persists even after adjusting** for maternal epilepsy
- Among 432 children born to women with epilepsy, absolute ASD risk was 4.15% for valproate-exposed vs 2.44% for unexposed

# Methods & Findings

**Population-based study** of all 655,615 children born alive in Denmark from 1996-2006.

- National registers used to identify valproate exposure during pregnancy
- 5,437 identified with ASD, including 2,067 with childhood autism
- Cox regression adjusting for maternal age at conception, paternal age, gestational age, birth weight, sex, congenital malformations, parity, psychiatric history
- Follow-up from birth until ASD diagnosis, death, emigration, or December 31, 2010
- Risk remained significant when restricting to children of women with epilepsy

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Valproic acid** is the most widely used animal model for ASD, and this population study confirms human risk
- **Dose-dependent teratogenic effects** relevant to understanding environmental contributions to ASD
- **Treatment decisions** must balance epilepsy control against offspring ASD risk
