# Epidemiology of Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease in Asia: A Systematic Review

## Key Claims

- Symptom-based [[gerd]] prevalence in Eastern Asia was 2.5-4.8% before 2005 and 5.2-8.5% from 2005 to 2010, showing increasing trends
- In Southeast and Western Asia, GERD prevalence was 6.3-18.3% after 2005, much higher than Eastern Asia
- Endoscopic reflux esophagitis prevalence in Eastern Asia increased from 3.4-5.0% before 2000 to 4.3-15.7% after 2005
- Barrett's esophagus prevalence remained low at 0.06-0.84% in health check-up participants and 0.31-2.00% in referral hospital settings
- Extra-esophageal symptoms were more prevalent in GERD groups than controls, though only limited studies were available
- Risk factors included old age, night-shift work, heavy work burdens, greasy/sweet food intake, and excessive eating

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review
- **Search**: PubMed (January 1995 - October 2010); 3,440 papers searched; 70 studies included
- **Regions**: Eastern Asia (China, Japan, Korea, Taiwan), Southeastern Asia (Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand), South Central Asia (India, Iran, Pakistan), Western Asia (Israel, Turkey)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Epidemiologic studies with >=200 subjects, population-based or medical check-up; detailed GERD definition; any sample type
