## Key Claims

- Dietary nickel can induce dyshidrosiform pemphigoid (a rare localized variant of bullous pemphigoid simulating pompholyx) in nickel-sensitized patients
- The patient achieved complete clinical clearance on a [[low-nickel-diet]] within 15 days, after failing to respond to conventional treatments (dapsone, prednisolone)
- Oral nickel challenge (6 mg elemental nickel as NiSO4) induced mild itching and new bullae within 24 hours, confirming dietary nickel as the trigger
- This case represents one of only about 9 previously reported cases of dyshidrosiform pemphigoid in the literature

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Case report from Hacettepe University, Ankara, Turkey
- **Patient**: 23-year-old woman with recurrent itchy vesicular eruption of hands for 5 years; hemorrhagic bullae on palms and soles; 6-year history of metal allergy
- **Diagnosis**: Punch biopsy showed subepidermal bulla with eosinophils and small round cells; DIF revealed linear IgG and C3 along basement membrane zone (BMZ); patch test 3+ to 5% nickel sulphate; diagnosis: localized bullous pemphigoid (LBP)
- **Treatment course**: Dapsone 50mg 3x daily - only transitory improvement; prednisolone 60mg - improved in 10-11 days but relapsed when tapered to 35mg; low-nickel diet - complete resolution in 15 days
- **Oral challenge**: After clearance, 6 mg elemental nickel (NiSO4.7H2O) administered; mild itching and few bullae within 24 hours
- **Follow-up**: At 6 months on low-nickel diet only, patient is blister-free with no recurrence
