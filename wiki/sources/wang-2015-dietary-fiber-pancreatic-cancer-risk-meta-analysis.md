# Key Claims

- High [[dietary fiber]] intake is significantly associated with reduced [[pancreatic-cancer]] risk in case-control studies (OR = 0.54, 95% CI: 0.44-0.67, P = 0.043) but not in the single cohort study included
- The overall summary risk estimate across 14 studies (3,287 PC cases) showed a 46% risk reduction (OR 0.54, 95% CI: 0.44-0.67)
- Both soluble fiber (OR 0.52) and insoluble fiber (OR 0.49) showed protective associations, with no significant difference between fiber types
- The protective association was consistent across geographic regions (North America OR 0.57, Europe OR 0.43, Asia OR 0.57)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Meta-analysis of 13 case-control studies and 1 cohort study; 3,287 pancreatic cancer cases
- **Case-control summary**: OR 0.53 (95% CI: 0.46-0.62, P for heterogeneity = 0.114) for highest vs lowest fiber intake
- **Cohort study**: HR 1.01 (95% CI: 0.59-1.74) -- not significant
- **Subgroup by fiber type**: Soluble fiber OR 0.52 (0.37-0.73); insoluble fiber OR 0.49 (0.36-0.66); crude fiber OR 0.55 (0.30-1.02)
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Summary risk estimates ranged from 0.53-0.57 with sequential study exclusion, demonstrating robustness
- **Publication bias**: No evidence (Egger's test P = 0.495; Begg's test P = 0.787)
- **Proposed mechanisms**: Fiber may lower circulating inflammatory markers; improve insulin metabolism; inositol hexaphosphate (in high-fiber foods) inhibits pancreatic cancer cell growth
