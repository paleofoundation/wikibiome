# Brawley & Lindahl 2021 — Labile Metal Pools in Escherichia coli

## Key Findings
- **The labile metal pool** — metal ions loosely bound to small molecules like citrate, glutathione, and phosphate — is what metalloregulators actually sense and what enzymes draw from [[brawley-2021-labile-metal-pools-ecoli]]
- In E. coli, labile iron is predominantly Fe²⁺ bound to low-molecular-mass thiols, while labile zinc exists at femtomolar free concentrations despite micromolar total zinc
- The gap between total and labile metal concentrations can span 4-6 orders of magnitude, meaning standard metal measurements dramatically overestimate bioavailable metal

## Relevance to WikiBiome
- Foundational for interpreting metallomic signatures: a tissue with elevated total zinc may have virtually no labile zinc available to bacteria
- Pairs with [[liu-2020-free-iron-fur-physiology-shewanella]] to establish that labile pools, not total concentrations, drive microbial ecology and host-pathogen metal competition
