# The Gut Microbiome in Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease

## Summary

Landmark metagenome-wide association study on stools from 218 ACVD patients and 187 healthy controls. Found that ACVD gut microbiome deviates from healthy status by increased *Enterobacteriaceae* and *Streptococcus* spp., with functional enrichment in TMA lyase genes. Drug treatment was the major confounding factor.

## Key Findings

### Taxonomic Changes
- ACVD enriched: *Enterobacteriaceae*, *Streptococcus* spp., *E. lenta*, *A. parvulum*, *R. gnavus*, *L. salivarius*, *E. coli*, *Lachnospiraceae*, *Clostridium nexile*
- ACVD depleted: Butyrate-producing *Roseburia* spp., *F. prausnitzii*, *Clostridiales* in untreated individuals
- 47 MLGs selected for ACVD classifier with AUC of 86% (83-90%)

### Functional Alterations
- ACVD enriched in TMA lyase genes (CutC/D, YeaW/X) for TMAO production
- Higher transport of simple sugars (PTS system) and amino acids
- Lower potential for vitamin biosynthesis
- Enriched virulence factors and LPS biosynthesis modules
- Reduced potential for SCFA butyrate synthesis

### Drug Confounding Effects
- Drug use was the major distinguishing feature in this cohort
- Fondaparinux, acarbose, metoprolol, atorvastatin all significantly influenced gut microbial genes
- Classification AUC was lower between ACVD patients with drugs vs. controls compared to ACVD without drugs vs. controls
- Highlights need to account for polypharmacy in microbiome-CVD studies

### Cross-Disease Comparisons
- Common features with liver cirrhosis, obesity, T2D, and RA
- *Streptococcus* spp. enriched in ACVD and cirrhosis
- *E. coli* MLGs showed unique features in ACVD vs. other diseases
- ACVD-enriched bacteriophages mainly targeted *Enterobacteriaceae* and *Streptococcus*

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **TMA lyase enrichment** - Metal-induced [[dysbiosis]] that expands *Enterobacteriaceae* would increase TMA/TMAO production, directly linking [[lead]] and [[cadmium]] exposure to atherosclerosis
2. **Butyrate producer loss** - Depletion of *Roseburia* and *F. prausnitzii* in ACVD parallels metal-sensitive taxa losses
3. **LPS pathway activation** - Enriched LPS biosynthesis in ACVD mirrors the endotoxemia pathway activated by metal-induced gut barrier disruption
4. **Drug confounding** - Critical consideration for [[gut-metal-microbiome]] studies, as CVD patients on multiple medications may have drug-altered rather than disease-altered microbiomes
