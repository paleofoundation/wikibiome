# Key Claims

- Coxsackievirus B3 (CVB3) infection in mice caused significant reduction in gut microflora richness and diversity, with increased *Proteobacteria* and decreased *Cyanobacteria* and *Desulfobacterota* at the phylum level
- Key genera enriched in AVMC (acute viral myocarditis) mice: *Escherichia-Shigella*, *Lactobacillus*, *Clostridium_sensu_stricto_1*, *Prevotellaceae_UCG-001*, and *Odoribacter*; depleted genera included *Alistipes*, *Bacteroides*, and *Lachnospiraceae_NK4A136*
- 198 differential metabolites (DMs) were identified between control and AVMC groups, with 79 upregulated and 119 downregulated; lipids and lipid-like molecules accounted for the largest proportion
- Lipid metabolism pathway was significantly enriched: **Traumatic acid (TA)**, **Alpha-Linolenic acid (ALA)**, **Eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA)**, and **Docosahexaenoic acid (DHA)** were key differential metabolites
- Significant positive correlations between EPA levels and *Clostridium_sensu_stricto_1* abundance, and between DHA levels and *Escherichia-Shigella* abundance, linking gut bacteria to omega-3 fatty acid metabolism
- CVB3 infection altered 44 statistically different functional KEGG pathways between groups, with lipid metabolism, xenobiotics biodegradation, membrane transport, and signal transduction enriched in AVMC
- The gut-heart axis is the primary pathway through which CVB3 infects the host; the gut microbiome plays a critical but understudied role in viral myocarditis progression

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Animal model study (BALB/c mice) with 16S rRNA sequencing and untargeted metabolomics
- **Design**: Control (n=7) vs AVMC (n=9) groups; CVB3 intraperitoneal injection; sacrifice at day 7
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA V3-V4 sequencing (Illumina NovaSeq 6000); UHPLC-MS/MS metabolomics; QIIME (v1.9.1); USEARCH OTU clustering; LEfSe analysis; OPLS-DA metabolomics
- **Cardiac confirmation**: HE staining showed significant cardiac pathological changes; CVB3 RNA significantly elevated in AVMC hearts
