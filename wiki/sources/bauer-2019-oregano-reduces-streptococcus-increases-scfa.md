# Bauer 2019 — Oregano Powder Reduces Streptococcus and Increases SCFA in Mixed Bacterial Culture

## Key Findings

- **1% (w/v) oregano powder** had a mild but significant effect on complex caecal microbiota: increased **Enterococcus faecium** rearranging ratios, significantly reduced **Streptococcus** (p=1.6e-3) ([[bauer-2019-oregano-reduces-streptococcus-increases-scfa]], in vitro).
- **Beneficial SCFAs** — acetic acid and butyric acid — were significantly increased in oregano-supplemented cultures ([[bauer-2019-oregano-reduces-streptococcus-increases-scfa]]).
- Oregano powder as a **phytobiotic replacement for antibiotic growth promoters (AGPs)** — selectively suppresses pathogenic taxa while preserving beneficial community and enhancing SCFA production ([[bauer-2019-oregano-reduces-streptococcus-increases-scfa]]).
- This is the **first comprehensive 16S rRNA sequencing study** of oregano effects on complex mixed bacterial cultures from caecal microbiota ([[bauer-2019-oregano-reduces-streptococcus-increases-scfa]]).
- Active compounds: **carvacrol (CAR)** and **thymol** equate to ~80% of oregano essential oil content. Known to reduce cell membrane potential leading to cell death at low doses ([[bauer-2019-oregano-reduces-streptococcus-increases-scfa]]).

## Methods

Mixed bacterial culture from chicken caeca (3 roosters, 6 months old). Modified LYHBHI media enriched with chicken feed extract, vitamins, trace elements. Anaerobic incubation (37°C, mixed gas). 1% oregano powder vs. control. 16S rRNA gene amplicon sequencing (MG Rast). SCFA analysis.
