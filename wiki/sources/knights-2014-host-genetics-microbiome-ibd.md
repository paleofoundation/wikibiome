# Key Claims

- NOD2 risk allele count is significantly associated with increased relative abundance of Enterobacteriaceae in IBD patients, confirmed across two independent cohorts
- Forty-eight additional IBD-related SNPs show conserved directionality of bacterial associations across cohorts, implicating genes enriched for innate immune response, inflammatory response, and JAK-STAT cascade
- Host genetic factors are part of a complex web influencing microbiome composition, but their overall effect is moderate compared to environmental factors (antibiotics, biopsy location, disease phenotype)
- Gene-gene interaction network of host factors with conserved microbiome associations identifies STAT3, IL12B, PTPN2, and TAB1 as key nodes linking innate immunity to microbial composition
- Antibiotics and duration of disease are additional risk factors for IBD-related [[dysbiosis]], independent of genetic effects

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Multi-cohort genotype-microbiome association study; 474 IBD subjects across 3 independent cohorts (Boston, Toronto, Groningen)
- **Genotyping**: Illumina Immunochip; 163 published IBD risk loci tested; 16S rRNA gene sequencing from intestinal biopsies
- **NOD2 findings**: Aggregated 6 causal NOD2 variants; NOD2 associated with first principal axis of taxonomic variation (FDR<0.05); increased Enterobacteriaceae with higher risk allele dosage (Figure 2b)
- **Pathway enrichment**: 49 genes with conserved microbiome associations enriched for innate immune response (FDR=2.31e-6), inflammatory response (FDR=7.43e-6), JAK-STAT cascade (FDR=2.04e-4)
- **Top gene-bacteria associations** (Figure 4): IL12B with Deltaproteobacteria and Alphaproteobacteria; PTPN2 with Desulfovibrionaceae and Collinsella aerofaciens; TAB1 with Lactobacillus
- **Host factor network** (Figure 5): Age, sex, antibiotics, immunosuppressants, biopsy location, disease phenotype, NOD2 all independently associated with IBD microbiome; effects are overlapping
- **Dysbiosis index**: Confirmed antibiotics usage (within 1 month) increases microbial dysbiosis index (MDI) even after controlling for NOD2
