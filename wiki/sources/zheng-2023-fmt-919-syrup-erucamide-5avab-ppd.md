# Key Claims

- **919 Syrup** alleviates [[postpartum-depression]] in mice and inhibits elevated erucamide levels in depressive hippocampus
- **FMT from 919 Syrup-treated mice** effectively improved depressive behaviors, upregulated 5-AVAB, and downregulated erucamide in hippocampus of recipient mice
- **Antibiotic-treated mice** were not sensitive to the antidepressant effect of 919 Syrup, confirming microbiome-dependent mechanism
- **Erucamide** negatively correlated with increased *Bacteroides* after 919 Syrup or FMT treatment
- *Ruminococcaceae UCG-014* increased in feces of PPD mice and positively correlated with erucamide
- **Bacteroides, Lactobacillus, and Ruminiclostridium** increases after FMT positively correlated with 5-AVAB

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Mouse model of PPD; forced swimming test (FST) and tail suspension test (TST); non-target metabolomics and 16S rRNA sequencing; FMT validation experiment
- **Setting**: Jinshan Hospital, Fudan University, Shanghai, China
- **Key implication**: 919 Syrup acts through gut microbiome modulation to alter hippocampal metabolite ratios; microbiome-dependent antidepressant mechanism confirmed by FMT
