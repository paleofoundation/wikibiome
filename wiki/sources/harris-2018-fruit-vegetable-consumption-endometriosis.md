# Key Claims

- Higher intake of fruits, particularly citrus fruits, is associated with a lower risk of laparoscopically confirmed [[endometriosis]]
- Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts) are associated with a 13% higher risk of endometriosis, possibly due to GI symptoms mimicking endometriosis presentation
- Beta-cryptoxanthin intake (from citrus fruits) was the only nutrient significantly associated with lower endometriosis risk
- The protective effect of fruit consumption was stronger among ever-smokers, possibly due to higher oxidative stress burden
- Cruciferous vegetables are high in fermentable oligo-, di-, and monosaccharides and polyols (FODMAPs), which may exacerbate IBS-like symptoms in endometriosis

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Prospective cohort study using Nurses' Health Study II (NHSII) data
- **Participants**: 70,835 premenopausal women followed from 1991 to 2013 (22 years)
- **Cases**: 2,609 laparoscopically confirmed endometriosis cases (incidence rate = 311 per 100,000 person-years)
- **Dietary assessment**: Validated food frequency questionnaire (FFQ) every 4 years, covering >130 food items
- **Key findings -- Fruit**:
  - Non-linear inverse association between fruit consumption and endometriosis (P_nonlinearity = 0.002, P_significance of curve = 0.005)
  - Women consuming >=1 serving citrus fruits/day had 22% lower risk (95% CI 0.69-0.89; P_trend = 0.004)
  - Citrus fruits: oranges showed strongest individual association (HR 0.73 for >=4/week, P_trend = 0.005)
  - Rosaceae fruits (>=1/day): HR 0.91 (P_trend = 0.04)
- **Key findings -- Vegetables**:
  - No overall association between total vegetable intake and endometriosis risk
  - Cruciferous vegetables (>=1 serving/day): HR 1.13 (95% CI 0.95-1.34; P_trend = 0.03) -- increased risk
  - Raw cabbage/coleslaw: HR 1.26 (P_trend = 0.02); Brussel sprouts: HR 1.30; cauliflower: HR 1.16 -- all increased risk
  - Corn and peas/lima beans also associated with higher risk
  - Romaine/leaf lettuce associated with lower risk (HR 0.85, P_trend = 0.09)
- **Nutrient analyses**: Only beta-cryptoxanthin showed significant inverse association (RR 5th quintile 0.88, P_trend = 0.02); no significant associations for RAE, beta-carotene, alpha-carotene, lycopene, or lutein/zeaxanthin
- **Smoking interaction**: Protective effect of fruit stronger among ever-smokers (P_nonlinearity = 0.009) vs. never-smokers
- **Temporal consistency**: Inverse associations consistent across multiple time lag analyses (baseline, cumulative average, 4-6 year lag, 6-8 year lag)

# Connections

- [[nickel]] -- cruciferous vegetables, corn, peas/lima beans, and legumes (all associated with increased endometriosis risk in this study) are known to be high in [[nickel]] content; this raises the question of whether [[dietary-nickel-exposure]] from these foods could partly explain the observed increased risk
- [[dietary-nickel-exposure]] -- the paradox of some vegetables increasing endometriosis risk while fruits decrease it may relate to differential [[nickel]] content; cruciferous vegetables, legumes, and corn are high-nickel foods
- [[oxidative-stress]] -- beta-cryptoxanthin's protective effect may relate to antioxidant activity; smoking interaction suggests oxidative stress mediates the fruit-endometriosis relationship
- [[inflammation]] -- cruciferous vegetables may exacerbate GI symptoms through FODMAP content, leading to increased endometriosis detection rather than true causation
- [[low-nickel-diet]] -- findings support the concept that certain plant foods may worsen symptoms in endometriosis patients, consistent with the rationale for a [[low-nickel-diet]] in susceptible individuals
