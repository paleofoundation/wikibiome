# Multiple Sclerosis and the Hygiene Hypothesis: From the Outdoor Exposure to the Indoor Effector

## Summary

This doctoral thesis from the University of Ferrara investigates [[multiple-sclerosis]] through the lens of the hygiene hypothesis, combining epidemiological surveys (EnvIMS study) on allergy and sibling effects with an experimental study on gut microbiota interactions with oral DMTs and environmental factors in Italian MS patients.

## Structure and Scope

### Part 1: Epidemiological Evidence (EnvIMS Study)
- Study A: MS and history of allergy
- Study B: MS and the sibling effect
- Explores the "When-What-How much-Old Friends" (WWHOLF) methodological framework

### Part 2: Experimental Study
- Interactions between [[gut-microbiome]], oral DMTs, and environmental factors in MS patients
- Examines how DMTs affect microbiota composition
- Assesses whether microbiota mediates DMT side effects

## Key Context

- Italy MS prevalence: 208/100,000 (Sardinia: 330/100,000)
- MS pathogenesis: discusses both "outside-in" (immune activation in periphery) and "inside-out" (primary CNS degeneration) models
- Emphasizes role of MAIT cells in MS pathogenesis -- mucosal-associated invariant T cells bridge gut microbiota and CNS
- Environmental prognostic factors reviewed: smoking, air pollution, sun exposure/vitamin D, diet/BMI, physical activity

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This thesis directly links environmental exposures (including air pollution containing [[heavy-metals]]) to [[gut-microbiome]] composition and MS outcomes. The hygiene hypothesis framework -- that reduced microbial exposure in childhood predisposes to autoimmunity -- has clear implications for the [[gut-metal-microbiome]] axis: both reduced microbial diversity and increased toxic metal exposure in modern environments may synergistically drive MS risk. The experimental study on DMT-microbiota interactions provides mechanistic data on how MS treatments may work partly through [[gut-microbiome]] modulation.

## Limitations

- Doctoral thesis; experimental study section would need peer review
- Italian population specific
- Environmental exposure data largely based on questionnaires
