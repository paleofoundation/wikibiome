# Key Claims

- Specific well-studied oral, gastrointestinal, and intrapancreatic microbes have potential etiological roles in [[pancreatic-cancer]] carcinogenesis
- *Porphyromonas gingivalis* is the periodontal pathogen most strongly associated with elevated PDAC risk, with a 59% greater risk of development and 2-fold higher risk based on antibody levels
- The microbiota influences PDAC through five key mechanisms: persistent inflammation, immune modulation, metabolism regulation, tumor [[microenvironment]] alteration, and bacteria-virus interaction
- Hepatotropic viruses (HBV, HCV) have potential oncogenic roles, with *H. pylori* infection linked to increased PDAC risk

# Methods/Findings

- **Oral microbiota**: *P. gingivalis*, *Fusobacterium*, *N. elongata*, and *S. mitis* are keystone pathogens; PAD enzymes degrade arginine causing p53 and K-ras mutations
- **Fusobacterium**: Paradoxical effects -- potentiates tumorigenesis in colorectal cancer but some studies show reduced PDAC risk; promotes chemoresistance via reactive oxygen species and inflammatory cytokines
- **N. elongata and S. mitis**: Lower in saliva post-PC diagnosis; combination biomarker yielded 96.4% sensitivity and 82.1% specificity
- **H. pylori**: IgG antibody levels higher in PDAC; associated with increased risk through chronic inflammation; detection of *H. pylori* DNA in pancreatic cancer tissues
- **Intrapancreatic microbiota**: Dominated by Gammaproteobacteria; *Enterobacter* and *Enterococcus* species found; intratumoral bacteria conferred gemcitabine resistance
- **Inflammation mechanisms**: NF-kB activation, TLR signaling (MyD88, TRIF), MAPK pathways; dual role in promoting immune suppression or antitumor activation
- **Metabolic regulation**: Obesity-associated [[dysbiosis]] linked to oncogenic KRAS activation; microbiota serves as critical regulator of metabolism in PDAC
