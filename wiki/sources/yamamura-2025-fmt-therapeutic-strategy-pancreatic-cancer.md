# Key Claims

- [[FMT]] holds considerable promise as adjunct treatment for PDAC but is hindered by challenges in donor selection, microbiome engraftment, and suboptimal delivery methods
- Long-term survivors (LTS) of PDAC exhibit higher intratumoral microbial diversity with specific beneficial genera (*Bacillus clausii*, *Pseudoxanthomonas*, *Saccharopolyspora*, *Streptomyces*) that promote CD8+ T cell activation
- FMT from LTS donors into tumor-bearing mice showed slower tumor growth, higher survival, and higher interferon-gamma and IL-2 levels compared to short-term survivor (STS) FMT
- An early Phase I clinical trial for FMT in patients with PDAC is ongoing at M.D. Anderson Cancer Center (NCT04975217)

# Methods/Findings

- **Tumor microbiome in LTS vs STS**: LTS tumors had higher alpha diversity; *Aggregatibacter* and *Neisseria* negatively associated with improved OS
- **Gut microbiome in PDAC**: Reduced diversity, decreased Clostridiales, Faecalibacterium, Ruminococcaceae; increased *Akkermansia*, *Porphyromonas*, *Prevotella*, *Streptococcus*, *Veillonella*, *Veillonellaceae*
- **Preclinical FMT evidence**: Mice receiving LTS feces showed distinct gut microbiome community and antitumor effect mediated by CD8+ T cells; antibiotic treatment attenuated the effect
- **Antibiotics and PDAC**: Retrospective studies showed antibiotic use in metastatic PDAC patients was associated with improved OS; concurrent antibiotic with gemcitabine improved OS
- **Delivery methods**: Capsules most common; upper endoscopic delivery potentially more effective for targeting pancreas via duodenum; colonoscopic option available
- **Donor options**: Healthy individuals, autologous (pre-disease), or from treatment responders
- **REBYOTA precedent**: FDA-approved live biotherapeutic for recurrent *C. difficile* provides regulatory pathway model
