## Key Findings

- **Large-scale synthesis**: Meta-analysis and meta-regression of 412 studies from 46 countries examining nutritional factors associated with postpartum depression prevalence (systematic review-meta-analysis level).
- **Micronutrient associations**: Deficiencies in key micronutrients (iron, zinc, magnesium, B vitamins, vitamin D, polyunsaturated fatty acids) associated with increased PPD risk (systematic review level).
- **National variation**: PPD prevalence correlates with population-level nutritional status, suggesting nutritional factors are modifiable risk factors for PPD (systematic review level).

## Methods

Comprehensive systematic review and meta-regression analysis of 412 studies from 46 countries examining associations between maternal nutritional status/intake and postpartum depression prevalence. Analysis controlled for economic, healthcare access, and social factors to isolate nutritional contributions.

## Detailed Summary

The study establishes nutritional factors as significant modifiable contributors to postpartum depression:

- **Iron**: Low maternal iron stores associated with increased PPD risk; iron essential for monoamine synthesis and HPA axis function
- **Zinc**: Zinc deficiency associated with elevated inflammatory markers and impaired immune tolerance; zinc essential for metalloproteases and transcription factors
- **Magnesium**: Mg deficiency associated with elevated stress response and neuroinflammation
- **Vitamin D**: Low vitamin D associated with increased inflammation and immune dysregulation; D-dependent regulatory T cell differentiation
- **Omega-3 fatty acids**: DHA/EPA deficiency associated with altered monoamine signaling and membrane rigidity
- **B vitamins**: Deficiencies in B6, B12, folate associated with elevated homocysteine and impaired monoamine synthesis
- **Selenium**: Selenoprotein synthesis dependent on selenium; role in antioxidant defense and immune tolerance

The review highlights that nutritional status is modifiable and should be assessed and supported in postpartum women at risk for or experiencing depression.
