# Key Claims

- Urinary VOC metabolites AMCC, CEMC, CYMC, DHBC, HPMC, and MB3C are independently associated with increased RA risk after adjusting for covariates
- Mixed VOC exposure (WQS model) associated with RA: OR 1.39 (95% CI 1.07-1.80) in total population
- CEMC, CYMC, and AMCC are the major contributors to RA risk in the 20-59 age group
- Dose-response relationship exists between CEMC, CYMC, AMCC concentrations and RA risk in adults aged 20-59
- Smoking and alcohol are potential effect modifiers for CYMC-RA relationship
- No significant association in adults over 60

# Methods/Findings

- **Data**: NHANES 2011-2018, n = 3,390 participants (204 with RA)
- **Methods**: Logistic regression, WQS, qgcomp, BKMR models, restricted cubic splines (RCS)
- **15 urinary VOC metabolites** analyzed; detection rates > 80% for most (except CYMC at 78.97%)
- AMCC, CEMC, DHBC, MB3C, PHGA, and PMMC significantly higher in RA vs. non-RA
- WQS OR for age 20-59: 1.70 (95% CI 1.1-2.62); qgcomp OR: 1.91 (95% CI 1.33-3.47)
- BKMR showed increased RA risk below 50th percentile of VOC concentration, declining above
- **Key VOC parent compounds**: CEMC from acrolein, CYMC from acrylonitrile, AMCC from N,N-dimethylformamide

# Connections

- Relevant to [[environmental pollutants]] and [[autoimmune disease]]
- Acrolein (parent of CEMC) is a combustion product found in vehicle exhaust and cigarette smoke -- links to [[smoking]] and RA
- Acrylonitrile (parent of CYMC) is a known carcinogen used in plastics manufacturing
- VOCs may cause RA through [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]], reduced GSH, and [[immune dysregulation]]
- Complements heavy metal studies using [[NHANES]] data
- Related to [[inflammatory cytokines]] (IL-12, IL-23) and T cell/dendritic cell activation pathways
