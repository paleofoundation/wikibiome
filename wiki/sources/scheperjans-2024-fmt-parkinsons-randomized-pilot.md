# Fecal Microbiota Transplantation in Parkinson's Disease: A Randomized Repeat-Dose, Placebo-Controlled Clinical Pilot Study

## Summary

This landmark randomized, placebo-controlled pilot study evaluates the safety and efficacy of repeat-dose [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]] (FMT) in [[parkinsons-disease]]. It is among the first rigorous clinical trials of FMT specifically designed for PD, using repeated doses to improve engraftment.

## Key Findings

- FMT was safe and well-tolerated in PD patients with no serious adverse events
- Repeat dosing improved donor microbiota engraftment compared to single-dose FMT
- GI symptoms, particularly constipation, improved in the FMT group versus placebo
- Microbiome composition shifted toward donor profiles in the FMT group
- Motor symptom improvements were modest and did not reach statistical significance in this small trial
- Fecal SCFA profiles trended toward normalization in the FMT group
- The study established feasibility for larger phase II/III trials

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

This pilot trial provides the first controlled human evidence for [[fecal-microbiota-transplant]] efficacy in PD, validating the approach for future trials incorporating metallomic endpoints. The improvement in constipation is directly relevant to metal exposure, as reduced colonic transit time decreases contact time for metal absorption. The microbiome shift toward donor profiles suggests that the entire metabolic network -- including metal-handling pathways like siderophore production, [[iron]] sequestration, and [[butyrate]]-mediated barrier function -- may be partially restored. Future trials should incorporate metallomic profiling (serum [[iron]], [[manganese]], [[nickel]], [[lead]]) alongside microbiome analysis to test whether FMT normalizes the metal-microbiome axis in PD.

## Limitations

- Small pilot study with limited statistical power
- Short follow-up period
- Motor symptom endpoints did not reach significance
- Optimal donor selection criteria for PD not yet determined
- Long-term engraftment durability unknown
