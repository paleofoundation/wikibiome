# Key Claims

- PDAC patients have significantly increased fungal abundance (p<0.001) and significantly decreased fungal diversity (p<0.001) in saliva compared to healthy controls
- Higher abundance of Basidiomycota and unclassified Ascomycota was associated with increased PDAC risk (OR 1.359 and 1.260 per unit increase respectively)
- Aspergillus (AUC 0.983) and Cladosporium (AUC 0.969) achieved high classification power for distinguishing PDAC from healthy controls
- Oral salivary mycobiota profiling via ITS sequencing offers a rapid, inexpensive, non-invasive biomarker approach for early PDAC detection

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Prospective case-control; 34 PDAC patients and 35 healthy controls from West China Hospital; salivary ITS1 sequencing
- **Fungal diversity**: PDAC patients had higher Chao index (p<0.001), ACE index (p<0.001), lower Shannon index (p<0.001), and lower Simpson index (p<0.001) at OTU level
- **Phylum-level**: Basidiomycota significantly decreased in PDAC; unclassified fungi significantly different
- **Genus-level**: Candida, Malassezia, Peniophora, Aspergillus, Cladosporium all significantly differentially abundant (corrected p<1e-15 for most)
- **PCoA analysis**: Clear separation between PDAC and HC groups (Bray-Curtis ANOSIM R=0.695, p=0.001)
- **Risk prediction**: Random forest and ROC analysis; Aspergillus and Cladosporium as top predictive biomarkers
- **5022 unique OTUs in PDAC vs 830 in HC**: Dramatic expansion of fungal diversity in disease
