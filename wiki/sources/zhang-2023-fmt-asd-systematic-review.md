# Key Claims

- **FMT significantly improved ASD symptoms** in observational studies but no RCTs exist
- **CARS score decreased significantly** from baseline post-treatment (P < 0.0001)
- **ABC score decreased significantly** post-treatment (P < 0.01)
- **SRS improvements reversed** after 8-12 weeks without further FMT

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Systematic review of 5 studies (2 prospective open-label, 2 retrospective observational, 1 case report). No RCTs identified.

**Study characteristics (Table 1):**
- Kang et al. 2017 (USA): n=18, ages 7-17, 10-week FMT + 8-week follow-up using vancomycin + SHGM
- Li et al. 2021 (China): n=40, ages 3-17, 4-week FMT + 8-week follow-up using oral capsule + rectal
- Zhang et al. 2022 (China): n=49, ages 3-14, two consecutive WMT courses via transendoscopic tubing
- Pan et al. 2022 (China): n=42, ages 3.75-8.25, at least 2 WMT courses via transendoscopic tube
- Huang et al. 2022 (China): n=1, age 18, 3-week FMT via transendoscopic enteral tubing

**Key outcomes:**
- Kang et al.: CARS decreased from 34.06 to 39.67 (P < 0.001); ABC from 63.58 to 47.75 (P < 0.01); SRS from 97.78 to 47.86 (P < 0.001); VABS-II improved by 1.4 years
- Li et al.: CARS, BC, SRS all decreased relative to baseline; effects reversed after 8-12 weeks
- Both oral and rectal routes showed similar efficacy

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Donor microbiota engraftment**: FMT restores complete microbial ecosystem including metal-metabolizing taxa absent in ASD dysbiosis
- **Transient benefits**: Reversal of SRS improvements after FMT cessation suggests need for sustained metal-ecological support
- **Vancomycin pretreatment**: Antibiotic clearance before FMT eliminates metal-resistant pathobionts, creating niche space for donor taxa with healthy metal metabolism
- **Comprehensive ecosystem transfer**: Unlike probiotics (which supply limited strains), FMT transfers entire metal-metabolizing community including fungi and bacteriophages
