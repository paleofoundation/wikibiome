# Hou 2018 -- Phage Display Peptide for Colon Cancer Imaging

## Key Claims

- A peptide termed CBP-DWS (DWSSWVYRDPQTGGGS) was identified through phage display screening that specifically binds to human colon cancer cells (COLO320HSR, HCT116, HT29, SW480, LoVo) with virtually no binding to normal intestinal epithelial cells (NCM460)
- After four rounds of biopanning on COLO320HSR cells plus negative selection with NCM460, four positive phage clones (C2, C5, C8, C19) showed >10x higher recovery compared to controls
- Bioinformatics analysis revealed CBP-DWS targets human Glypican-3, which is involved in important cellular functions in multiple cancer types; Glypican-3 was confirmed expressed on colon cancer cell surfaces
- The FITC-CBP-DWS peptide demonstrated strong fluorescent signal on all four CRC cell lines and on fresh human CRC tissue specimens, with significantly higher IOD compared to scrambled control peptide
- Competitive inhibition assays confirmed specificity: unlabeled CBP-DWS decreased binding with increasing concentrations on HCT116 and LoVo cells

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Phage display screening using M13 filamentous phage library (Ph.D.-12) against COLO320HSR cells with NCM460 negative selection
- **Methods**: Cell-based ELISA, confocal immunofluorescence, FACS analysis, DNA sequencing, fluorescence imaging on fresh tumor specimens from 6 CRC patients
- **Validation**: CBP-DWS bound to human CRC tissue but not adjacent normal tissue; potential clinical application for early CRC detection
