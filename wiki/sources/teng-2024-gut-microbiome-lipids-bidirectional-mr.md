# Key Claims

- After Bonferroni correction, only family **Desulfovibrionaceae** exhibited a stable and significant inverse causality with serum ApoB levels (Estimate=-0.0418, 95% CI 0.9362-0.9826, p=0.0007), suggesting higher Desulfovibrionaceae abundance protects against atherogenic lipoproteins
- 19 intestinal flora taxa showed causal relationships with blood lipids across ApoA, ApoB, LDL-C, HDL-C, and triglyceride outcomes
- **ApoA**: Genus *Ruminococcaceae* was causally associated with higher ApoA (p=0.0003)
- **ApoB**: *Christensenellaceae*, *Parasutterella*, *Terrisporobacter* positively associated; *Oscillospira*, *Peptococcus*, *RuminococcaceaeUCG010/011*, Desulfovibrionaceae negatively associated
- **LDL-C**: *Oscillospira* associated with lower LDL-C; *Parasutterella* and *Terrisporobacter* with higher LDL-C
- **HDL-C**: Class Erysipelotrichia, *Parabacteroides*, *RuminococcaceaeUCG010* associated with higher HDL-C
- **TG**: Only genus *Dorea* was associated with lower triglycerides
- Reverse MR analysis found no causal effect of lipids on intestinal flora, supporting unidirectional causality

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Bidirectional two-sample MR
- **Exposure**: MiBioGen consortium (18,340 individuals, 211 taxa)
- **Outcome**: Serum lipids from IEU Open GWAS (441,016 UK Biobank participants)
- **Methods**: IVW (primary), weighted median; Bonferroni correction for multiple testing (genus p=3.8x10-4, family p=1.4x10-3)
- **Key mechanistic insight**: *Desulfovibrio* performs dissimilatory sulfate reduction producing H2S; H2S may serve as a vasodilator and influence lipid metabolism
