# Overview

Comprehensive JAMA clinical review of ASD covering epidemiology, pathophysiology, diagnosis, and clinical management. Based on 591 publications (11 RCTs, 6 cohort studies, 5 systematic reviews, 24 meta-analyses). ASD affects **2.3% of US children** aged 8 years and **2.2% of adults**.

# Key Findings

## Epidemiology

- US prevalence increased from **1.1% in 2008 to 2.3% in 2018** -- partly attributed to broadened diagnostic criteria
- Higher prevalence in males: 3.7% vs 0.9% in females (3.7:1 to 4.2:1 ratio)
- Heritability estimated at **81%** (74-85%); environmental factors contribute 14-22%
- Key genetic risk: approximately 100 rare gene variants (e.g., *KMT2A*, *NRXN1*, *SHANK3*)
- Advanced paternal age: 21% increase in ASD per 10-year increase

## Diagnosis

- DSM-5: persistent deficits in social communication + restricted/repetitive behaviors
- ADOS-2 and ADI diagnostic tools: sensitivity 91%/80%, specificity 76%/72%
- American Academy of Pediatrics recommends screening at 18 and 24 months
- Early behavioral signs detectable in first 2 years of life

## Treatment

- **Early intensive behavioral intervention** (EIBI): small to medium effect sizes for language (0.29), cognitive ability (0.38), adaptive behavior (0.40)
- **Risperidone and aripiprazole**: FDA-approved for irritability/aggression (SMD ~1.1 for risperidone)
- **Psychostimulants**: effective for co-occurring ADHD (SMD ~0.6)
- No pharmacotherapy for core ASD symptoms exists

## Comorbidities

- Depression (20% vs 7%), anxiety (11% vs 5%), sleep difficulties (13% vs 5%), epilepsy (21% with co-occurring ID vs 0.8%)
- GI conditions (37% vs 5%), constipation (22%)
