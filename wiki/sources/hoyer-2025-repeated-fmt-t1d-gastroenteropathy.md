## Key Findings

- **First direct FMT trial in T1D**: This is the only study to date performing fecal microbiota transplantation directly in T1D patients for gastrointestinal symptoms, rather than in animal models or T2D patients (Hoyer et al. 2025, case series, n=17)
- **Symptom improvement**: Gastrointestinal Symptom Rating Scale for IBS (GSRS-IBS) scores decreased from a mean of 60 to 35 after FMT. Stool frequency normalized in the majority of patients
- **Transient effect requiring repetition**: Symptom relief waned over 3-6 months, requiring repeated FMT courses. This positions FMT as a maintenance therapy rather than a curative intervention in T1D
- **GI comorbidity in T1D**: Highlights the underrecognized burden of diabetic gastroenteropathy in T1D patients, with 40-50% experiencing significant GI symptoms

## Relevance to WikiBiome

Supports entity page content on T1D as a disease with significant GI involvement beyond glycemic control. The transient FMT effect is relevant to concept pages on **microbiome resilience** and **colonization resistance**.

## Relevance to Cureva

Direct clinical evidence for FMT as a T1D intervention, but with the critical caveat that effects are transient. Informs STOP considerations — FMT is not a standalone cure. The maintenance requirement has implications for clinical protocol design and patient expectations.

## Open Questions

- Would autologous FMT (from pre-disease or healthy-period stool) produce more durable engraftment?
- What specific taxa from the donor fail to engraft long-term?
- Does FMT improve glycemic control (HbA1c, C-peptide) in addition to GI symptoms?
