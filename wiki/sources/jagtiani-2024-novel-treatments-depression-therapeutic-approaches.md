# Key Claims

- Current monoaminergic treatments often fail to achieve full remission; a substantial proportion of patients do not respond
- **Novel treatment avenues** include glutamatergic modulation (ketamine/esketamine), brain stimulation, anti-[[inflammation]]atory agents, [[gut-brain-axis]] interventions, GABA modulation, and psychedelic-assisted therapy
- **Ketamine** rapidly alleviates depression through NMDA antagonism and AMPA receptor activation
- **Gut-brain axis interventions** recognized as emerging therapeutic strategy for depression
- Side effects of traditional antidepressants (weight gain, sexual dysfunction, GI disturbances) limit long-term adherence

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Narrative review of novel depression treatments; PubMed scoping search of treatment strategies from last 10 years
