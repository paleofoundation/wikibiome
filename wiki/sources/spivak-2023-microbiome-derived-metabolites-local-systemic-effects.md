# Local and Systemic Effects of Microbiome-Derived Metabolites

## Key Claims

- Commensal microbes exert numerous biochemical reactions synthesizing or modifying multiple bioactive small molecules (metabolites) that impact host physiology in a variety of contexts
- [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] (butyrate, propionate, acetate) and [[bile-acid-metabolism]] are mostly linked to beneficial effects on host health; different end-products of microbial [[tryptophan-metabolism]] metabolism can have either beneficial or harmful impact
- [[short-chain-fatty-acids]] are critical for intestinal barrier function by providing energy to intestinal epithelial cells (IECs), regulating metabolic circuits in gluconeogenesis and ceramide synthesis, and maintaining barrier stability
- [[proteobacteria]] and related pattern receptor recognition (PRR) ligands from structural components of microbes can trigger intestinal inflammation, infection, and carcinogenesis
- Microbiome-derived metabolites have systemic impacts via portal and systemic circulation affecting liver (lipogenesis, gluconeogenesis), muscle (glucose uptake, fatty acid oxidation), CNS (neurodegeneration, behavioral disorders), kidneys (CKD), and cardiovascular system (atherosclerosis)
- Table 1 categorizes metabolite classes: SCFAs, carboxylic acid intermediates, amino acids, tryptophan metabolites, flavonoids, secondary bile acids, and other classes (tetrahydrobiopterin, folate, amyloids)

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Comprehensive review
- **Scope**: Local (gut barrier) and systemic effects of microbially produced/modified metabolites
- **Key metabolite classes**: SCFAs (butyrate, propionate, acetate); bile acids (LCA, DCA); tryptophan metabolites (I3PA, indole-3-aldehyde, tryptamine); amino acids (BCAAs, TMAO); LPS and PRR ligands
- **Local effects**: Barrier stability, tolerogenic signaling, regeneration/proliferation, contractile/secretory signals, oncogenesis
- **Systemic effects**: CNS (amyloids, amino acids, nicotinamide), liver (TMAO, SCFAs, bile acids), muscle (SCFAs, bile acids, amino acids), adipose (SCFAs, bile acids, TLR/NLR-ligands), CVS (TMAO, SCFAs, BCAAs)
- **Postbiotic concept**: ISAPP definition of postbiotic includes inactivated microorganisms; remains controversial
