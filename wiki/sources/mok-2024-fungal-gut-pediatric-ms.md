# The Fungal Gut Microbiota in Pediatric-Onset Multiple Sclerosis

## Key Claims

- First study investigating the gut mycobiome in pediatric-onset MS (POMS), comparing to monophasic acquired demyelinating syndrome (monoADS) and unaffected controls
- Beta-diversity (PERMANOVA) shows significant difference among three groups (p = 0.02), specifically between POMS and monoADS (p = 0.005 adjusted), but not between monoADS and controls
- Shannon diversity index differs across groups (p = 0.041, Kruskal-Wallis ANOVA); unaffected controls show highest diversity, monoADS lowest
- *Saccharomyces* and *Candida* dominate all groups (top 2 genera); *Agaricus* (primarily *A. bisporus*, white button mushroom) is uniquely abundant in POMS -- likely diet-derived
- Predictive modeling identifies *C. albicans*, *Cyberlindnera jadinii*, and *Fusarium poae* as markers specific to POMS
- Only 6 fungal species identified at species level: *Saitoella complicata*, *Aspergillus fischeri/Neosartorya fischeri*, *Aspergillus flavus*, *Chytriomyces hyalinus*, *Rhizophagus irregularis*, and *Candida apicola*
- Gut mycobiome in POMS may comprise largely transient (diet-derived) fungi, but rare/under-detected markers may have pathogenic importance

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Cross-sectional; 46 participants from Canadian Pediatric Demyelinating Disease Network (18 POMS, 13 monoADS, 15 unaffected controls); age <= 24 at stool collection
- **Methods**: ITS2 amplicon sequencing (Illumina MiSeq); DADA2 processing; UNITE database; LEfSe and ALDEx2 for differential abundance; random forest classifiers
- **Demographics**: POMS 83% female, mean onset 15.3 years; monoADS 54% female, onset 5.2 years; 14/18 POMS on DMT
- **Key results**: Ascomycota dominated (>80% all groups); Basidiomycota higher in POMS (6.62%) vs monoADS (0.622%) and controls (0.465%)
- **Alpha diversity**: No significant difference in observed species (p = 0.08); Shannon (p = 0.041) and inverse Simpson (p = 0.034) differed; females had higher diversity index than males
- **Limitations**: Very small sample sizes (especially POMS male n = 3); ITS2 sequencing has taxonomic resolution limits; most detected fungi may be dietary transients; single timepoint
