# Song 2023 -- Anti-PD-L1 and Estradiol Effects on MC38 Colon Tumor Microbiome

## Key Claims

- At the taxonomic family level, *Muribaculaceae* was enriched only in the MC38 male control group; anti-PD-L1 and/or E2 treatment induced changes in four microorganisms commonly regulated in single and combination treatment
- Anti-PD-L1 treatment in males showed a decrease in *PAC001068_g_uc* and *PAC001070_s* (family Muribaculaceae) and an increase in *PAC001716_s* and *PAC001785_s* (family Ruminococcaceae)
- In the anti-PD-L1 plus E2 group, decreased opportunistic pathogens (Enterobacteriaceae) and increased commensal bacteria (*Lactobacillus murinus* and *Parabacteroides goldsteinii*) were observed
- The abundance of *Parabacteroides goldsteinii* was increased in both males and females in the anti-PD-L1 group, suggesting it may serve as a biomarker for immunotherapy response
- Sex-specific microbiome differences were observed: F/B ratio was not significantly different between treatment groups in males or females

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Syngeneic MC38 colon tumor mouse model; C57BL/6 male and female mice; MC38 injection + E2 (10 mg/kg daily) and/or anti-PD-L1 antibody treatment
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA metagenomics sequencing, LEfSe analysis for species-level effect sizes, PCoA and UPGMA for beta diversity
- **Groups**: Male (control, anti-PD-L1, E2, anti-PD-L1+E2) and Female (control, anti-PD-L1); n=5-7 per group
