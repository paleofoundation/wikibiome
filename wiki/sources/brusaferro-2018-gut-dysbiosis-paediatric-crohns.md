# Brusaferro 2018 — Gut dysbiosis in paediatric Crohn's

## Key Findings

- **Paediatric CD has distinct natural history.** 25% of CD cases are diagnosed in children. Paediatric CD is characterized by panenteric phenotype at onset in 43% of cases vs 3% of adults. Isolated ileal (2% vs 31%, p<0.0001) and colonic-only (15% vs 36%, p<0.0001) disease are significantly less common in paediatric CD. Children have more severe course, impaired linear growth, and pubertal development disruption.
- **Genetic susceptibility converges on innate immunity and autophagy.** Most frequent CD-associated variants: NOD2, IL23R, ATG16L1, IRGM, IL10, NKX2-3, ORMDL3. These genes regulate innate immunity and autophagy, altering response to microbiota challenge. The altered response drives persistent bacterial infection, defective mucosal barrier, and loss of immune tolerance.
- **Dysbiosis is the dominant environmental trigger.** The review concludes that gut microbiota are primary actors in CD pathogenesis; industrialization, smoking (in adults), and dietary shifts modulate disease risk via microbiota; restoring normal microbiota via probiotics, FMT, diet modification, and exclusive enteral nutrition (EEN) shows therapeutic promise in paediatric CD.

## Methods

PubMed search of studies from January 2008 to June 2018 using "Crohn's disease" AND ("gut dysbiosis" OR "microbiota" OR "microbioma" OR "probiotic") AND ("children" OR "paediatric"). 100+ articles reviewed, selected by language (English) and evidence-based content.
