# The Serum Trace Metal Signature Distinguishes Patients with Psychiatric Disorders from Healthy Controls

Squitti et al. (2025). *Biometals* 38:1601-1613.

## Overview

This study evaluated serum levels of zinc (Zn), copper (Cu), iron (Fe), magnesium (Mg), transferrin (TF), ferritin (F), and derived ratios (Cu/Zn, Cu/Mg, Fe/Cu) in 168 patients with mental health disorders (MHD) -- including mood spectrum disorders (MSD, n = 107), schizophrenia spectrum disorders (SSD, n = 20), and personality disorders (PD, n = 41) -- compared to 61 healthy controls (HC). The study demonstrates that trace metals, particularly the Cu/Zn ratio, can serve as adjunctive biomarkers for psychiatric diagnosis.

## Key Findings

### Metal Alterations Across Psychiatric Groups
- **Zn**: Significantly lower in all psychiatric groups vs HC (HC: 18.2 uM vs MHD: 13.4 uM; p < 0.001), with the sharpest decrease in PD patients
- **Cu**: Higher in MHD patients (14.7 uM vs HC 13.4 uM; p = 0.016), but only significantly elevated in women
- **Fe**: Lower in MHD (MHD: 81.8 ug/dL vs HC: 108.0 ug/dL; p < 0.001), with SSD showing greatest decrease
- **Mg**: Lower in patients overall, with PD showing significant reduction vs HC
- **TF**: Lower in MHD (2.2 mg/dL vs HC 2.5 mg/dL; p < 0.001)
- **Cu/Zn ratio**: Significantly elevated in all patient groups (MHD: 1.16 vs HC: 0.75; p < 0.001)
- **Cu/Mg ratio**: Elevated in patients; linked to 84% increased odds of having MHD per unit increase

### The Cu/Zn Ratio as Psychiatric Biomarker
- Cu/Zn ratio was the most consistent discriminator across all psychiatric subgroups
- Higher Cu/Zn recognized as a biomarker of systemic inflammation and oxidative stress
- Mechanistically: excess Cu displaces Zn from metallothionein, reducing Zn-dependent protein function while simultaneously generating oxidative stress via Fenton-type reactions
- Cu/Zn ratio may serve as a dynamic indicator for monitoring therapeutic responses

### Sex Differences
- Cu elevation significant only in women (16.3 vs 14.5 uM; p = 0.026)
- Zn decreased in both sexes but more sharply in men
- Fe and TF-sat differed significantly from HC only in women

### PCA and Logistic Regression
- Six PCA components retained, accounting for 98% of total variance
- **Cu component** (Cu, Cu/Zn, Cu/Mg): OR = 1.84 (p = 0.047) -- 84% increased odds of psychiatric disease per unit increase
- **Zn component**: OR = 0.27 (p < 0.001) -- protective
- **TF component**: OR = 0.22 (p < 0.001) -- protective
- **Fe component** (Fe, %TF-sat, Fe/Cu): OR = 0.50 (p = 0.002) -- protective
- Combined ROC model (Zn, TF, Fe, Cu PCA components + age/sex): **AUC = 0.92**

### Individual Metal ROC Performance
| Metal/Ratio | AUC |
|-------------|-----|
| Cu/Zn | 0.77 |
| Cu/Mg | 0.71 |
| Zn | 0.82 (highest individual) |
| TF | 0.81 |
| Fe/Cu | 0.79 |

## Methods

- **Subjects**: 168 psychiatric patients (recruited 2017-2019, IRCCS Brescia, Italy) + 61 HC
- **Diagnosis**: DSM-5 criteria; severity assessed by BPRS, CGI, GAF scales
- **Metals**: Cu and Zn by colorimetric assay (validated by GF-AAS); Fe, Mg, TF, ferritin by automated biochemical analyzer
- **Statistics**: MANOVA controlling for age; PCA with Varimax rotation; stepwise binary logistic regression; ROC analysis

## Relevance to Wiki

This study provides strong evidence that the Cu/Zn ratio is a transdiagnostic biomarker across psychiatric conditions, not limited to any single disorder. The combined AUC of 0.92 for the multi-metal model suggests clinical utility as an adjunctive diagnostic tool. The study reinforces the pattern seen in neurodegenerative diseases: Cu elevation, Zn depletion, and Fe dysregulation as a common metallomic signature of brain pathology. The sex-specific findings highlight the importance of stratifying metal analyses by sex.
