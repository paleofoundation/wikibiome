# Key Claims

- **Significant microbiota compositional differences** between children with ADHD/ASD and neurotypical controls
- **Probiotic supplementation** shows promise in modifying dysbiotic profiles in neurodevelopmental disorders
- **Microbiota changes** correlate with reductions in core behavioral symptoms in ASD/ADHD
- Probiotics target specific dysbiotic taxa implicated in both conditions

# Methods & Findings

This was a **randomized controlled trial** examining:
- Gut microbiota composition in children with ADHD, ASD, and comorbid ADHD/ASD
- Effects of probiotic supplementation on microbiota structure and neurobehavioral outcomes
- Fecal microbiota profiling via 16S rRNA sequencing
- Assessment of clinical behavioral outcomes before/after intervention

**Key microbiota findings:**
- ASD/ADHD groups show reduced microbial diversity and altered taxa abundance
- Specific pathogenic taxa enriched in neurodevelopmental disorders
- Probiotic administration shifts microbiota composition toward neurotypical profiles
- Correlations between microbiota changes and symptom improvement
