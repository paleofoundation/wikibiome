# Overview

First systematic meta-analysis to derive a **pooled male-to-female odds ratio (MFOR)** in ASD from 54 prevalence studies (13,784,284 participants; 53,712 with ASD). Challenges the widely cited 4:1 male-to-female ratio.

# Key Findings

## True Sex Ratio Closer to 3:1

- Overall pooled MFOR = **4.20** (95% CI: 3.84-4.60), but with very substantial heterogeneity (I^2 = 90.9%)
- **High-quality studies**: MFOR = **3.32** (95% CI: 2.88-3.84)
- **Active case-finding studies** (screening general population): MFOR = **3.25** (95% CI: 2.93-3.62)
- Studies ascertaining only pre-diagnosed individuals: MFOR = 4.56 (95% CI: 4.10-5.07)

## Diagnostic Gender Bias

- Girls who meet criteria for ASD are at **disproportionate risk of not receiving a clinical diagnosis**
- The difference between active (3.25) and passive (4.56) ascertainment methods suggests systematic under-identification of females
- Study quality was significantly associated with MFOR -- lower quality studies yielded higher ratios

## Moderator Variables

- IQ did not significantly affect the MFOR (contra common assumption)
- No significant effect of participant age or date of study
- Case ascertainment method was the only significant moderator
