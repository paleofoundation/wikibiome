# Key Claims

- **Low dietary fibre intake** was related to higher EPDS and STAI-6 scores (higher [[postpartum-depression]] and anxiety), though this did not remain significant after adjusting for confounders
- **Probiotic-fibre interaction** was not significant; probiotic (*Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* HN001) treatment had no effect on EPDS or STAI-6 vs. placebo
- **Over 50% of mothers** internationally experience symptoms of PPD and anxiety
- Study addresses a significant gap in current PPD research by integrating biological, social, and psychological perspectives

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Secondary analysis of the Probiotics in Pregnancy (PiP) Study; double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT; 423 pregnant women in New Zealand
- **Intervention**: *L. rhamnosus* HN001 (6x10^9 CFU) vs. placebo from 14-18 weeks gestation to 12 months postpartum
- **Diet assessment**: 3-day food diaries at 24-26 weeks gestation
- **Key implication**: Need for further research on prebiotic-probiotic synergistic effects in perinatal period
