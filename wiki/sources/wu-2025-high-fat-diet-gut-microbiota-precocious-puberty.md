## Key Findings

- **HFD disrupts gut microbiota composition within 24 hours**, elevating Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio, increasing bile-resistant genera (e.g., *[[alistipes]]*, *[[bacteroides]]*) and decreasing plant polysaccharide-degrading Firmicutes (e.g., *[[roseburia]]*, *[[ruminococcus]]*) ([[wu-2025-high-fat-diet-gut-microbiota-precocious-puberty]], expert-opinion, narrative review).
- **Gut microbiota-derived metabolites mediate HFD's effect on puberty** through four key pathways: short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs), bile acids (BAs), lipopolysaccharides (LPS), and neurotransmitters (GABA, dopamine, serotonin) ([[wu-2025-high-fat-diet-gut-microbiota-precocious-puberty]]).
- **Beta-glucuronidase produced by *Clostridium* species prevalent in HFD activates estrogen by deconjugation**, enhancing its bioavailability and absorption in gut and peripheral tissues — a direct estrobolome mechanism linking diet to hormonal disruption ([[wu-2025-high-fat-diet-gut-microbiota-precocious-puberty]]).
- **FMT from precocious puberty (PP) rats to healthy rats induced accelerated puberty**, accompanied by elevated serum LH, FSH, E2, and upregulated hypothalamic *Kiss1* and *GnRH* gene expression, confirming a causal role for gut microbiota in HPG axis activation ([[wu-2025-high-fat-diet-gut-microbiota-precocious-puberty]]).
- **SCFAs exert protective effects against HFD-induced PP** by reversing pubertal symptoms through the Kiss1-GPR54-protein kinase C (PKC)-ERK1/2 signaling pathway ([[wu-2025-high-fat-diet-gut-microbiota-precocious-puberty]]).

## Methods

This is a **narrative review** synthesizing preclinical (animal model) and clinical (observational, cross-sectional) evidence on the relationship between high-fat diet (HFD), gut microbiota (GM), and precocious puberty (PP). The authors reviewed studies spanning epidemiological data on dietary patterns and pubertal timing, 16S rRNA gene sequencing studies of gut microbiota in PP cohorts, animal models of HFD-induced PP, metabolomic analyses of SCFAs and bile acids, FMT experiments, and intervention studies (dietary, probiotics, traditional Chinese medicine). No original data were collected. Received 22 January 2025; accepted 19 May 2025; published 4 June 2025.

## Detailed Summary

### HFD Directly Promotes Precocious Puberty

The review identifies five direct mechanisms by which HFD promotes PP independent of gut microbiota:

1. **Hypothalamic microglial activation**: Long-chain saturated fatty acids activate hypothalamic microglia, which release prostaglandins and pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1-beta, IL-6, NO, TNF-alpha, ROS) that stimulate GnRH neurons and accelerate puberty.
2. **Phoenixin upregulation**: This reproductive peptide is elevated in response to fatty acids (palmitate, oleate), accelerating puberty through GnRH activation via kisspeptin signaling. Phoenixin also promotes follicular growth and enhances estradiol secretion in a dose-responsive manner.
3. **Transcription factor p53 upregulation**: HFD upregulates hypothalamic p53 expression, which positively correlates with pubertal onset timing. p53 acts through the Lin28/let-7 system to activate Kiss1/GPR54 and PI3K/mTOR signaling pathways.
4. **Metabolic hormone disruption**: HFD elevates leptin, insulin, and IGF-1 (which stimulate HPG axis) while reducing adiponectin and ghrelin (which normally inhibit it).
5. **Reproductive programming**: Maternal HFD during pregnancy and lactation accelerates offspring pubertal timing by disrupting developmental programming of the HPG axis.

### HFD Disrupts Gut Microbiota Balance

Short-term HFD exposure induces dysbiosis characterized by elevated Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio, increased bile-resistant genera (*[[alistipes]]*, *[[bacteroides]]*), and decreased populations of plant polysaccharide-degrading Firmicutes (*[[roseburia]]*, *[[ruminococcus]]*). Long-term HFD in children produces a *Bacteroides*-predominant enterotype contrasting with the microbial profiles of children on traditional carbohydrate-rich diets.

Elevated n-6 PUFA levels promote proliferation of pro-inflammatory species such as *[[mucispirillum-schaedleri]]* and *Lactobacillus murinus*. Increased saturated fatty acids facilitate entry of Gram-negative bacterial LPS into the bloodstream.

### Gut Microbial Imbalance Promotes PP

Multiple 16S rRNA gene sequencing studies reveal altered gut microbiota in girls with PP compared to healthy controls, with altered abundances of *[[ruminococcus]]*, *[[bifidobacterium]]*, *[[bacteroides]]*, and increased butyrate-producing bacteria. Cross-sectional studies cannot distinguish causative "driver" microorganisms from secondary "passenger" species. However, FMT experiments from PP donors (human patients or PP model rats) to recipient female rats demonstrated accelerated pubertal onset with elevated serum LH, FSH, E2, and upregulated hypothalamic *Kiss1* and *GnRH* expression.

### Metabolite-Mediated Mechanisms

**SCFAs**: HFD suppresses SCFA-producing bacteria while fostering pathogenic growth. A metabolomic study established negative correlations between fecal butyrate, isovalerate, and caproate levels and early puberty onset. A 2022 animal study demonstrated SCFAs reverse HFD-induced PP through the Kiss1-GPR54-PKC-ERK1/2 signaling pathway. However, paradoxically, 16S rRNA studies in PP girls showed increased SCFA-producing bacteria — possibly reflecting fundamental differences between experimental and physiological conditions.

**Bile acids**: HFD boosts BA synthesis and secretion, particularly hydrophobic secondary BAs. The relationship between BAs and puberty was first noted by Bergmann et al. in 1986, who found elevated BA saturation during female puberty. BAs exert diverse effects via TGR5, FXR, and other receptors, with hypothalamic *Sirt1/Kiss1* signaling implicated. Glycodeoxycholic acid (GDCA) supplementation ameliorated PP symptoms through modulation of the hypothalamic Sirt1/Kiss1 pathway.

**LPS**: HFD increases LPS-producing bacteria (S24-7 family, [[enterobacteriaceae]], [[desulfovibrionaceae]]) and exacerbates intestinal permeability, leading to elevated intestinal LPS. LPS activates TLR4 receptors on glial cells, inducing inflammatory mediators and potentiating oxidative stress. LPS stimulates inducible NO synthase, increasing NO levels involved in regulating female reproduction and GnRH secretion.

**Neurotransmitters**: HFD diminishes populations of *[[lactobacillus]]* (which produces GABA via glutamate decarboxylase) and *[[bifidobacterium]]*, reducing GABA production. GABA acts as a critical inhibitory modulator of hypothalamic GnRH neurons. HFD also augments bacteria linked to intestinal inflammation (e.g., *[[bilophila]]*, *[[desulfovibrio]]*, *[[escherichia]]*) which synthesize dopamine and serotonin (5-HT), both critical regulators of puberty onset. Dopamine activates GnRH through cAMP-mediated mechanism; 5-HT exerts a biphasic effect on GnRH neurons.

### Interaction Between GM and Sex Hormones

**Beta-glucuronidase** secreted by *[[clostridium]]* species prevalent in HFD activates estrogen by deconjugation, enhancing its bioavailability and absorption in gut and peripheral tissues — a key [[estrobolome]] mechanism. Conversely, sex hormone levels shape GM composition: premenopausal healthy women had lower *[[bacteroides]]* and *[[prevotella]]* abundances than age-matched men. Direct evidence shows E2-induced GM changes include increases in *Dubosiella*, *[[faecalibacillus]]*, and *[[bifidobacterium]]*.

### Therapeutic Strategies

**Dietary interventions**: Plant-based high-fiber diets may diminish estrogen levels by inhibiting dissociation of estrogen-binding proteins. Decaffeinated green tea polyphenol delayed puberty onset in obese girls (RCT). Intermittent fasting showed positive outcomes.

**Probiotics**: Regimens containing *[[bifidobacterium]]* longum, *[[lactobacillus-rhamnosus]]*, and *[[lactobacillus-helveticus]]* have demonstrated potential in delaying pubertal onset in female mice.

**FMT**: Two animal studies showed FMT from HFD-induced PP rats to healthy rats accelerated puberty, and conversely, FMT could serve as a viable treatment.

**Traditional Chinese medicine (TCM)**: Anemarrhena, berberine, and poria show efficacy in modulating gut dysregulation.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- **SCFA paradox**: Animal studies show SCFAs are protective against HFD-induced PP, yet 16S rRNA studies in PP girls show paradoxically *increased* SCFA-producing bacteria. The authors acknowledge this discrepancy may reflect differences between supraphysiological SCFA doses in animal models versus complex gut-brain communication in humans, or confounding by physiological barriers (intestinal absorption, hepatic metabolism, blood-brain barrier permeability).
- **Bile acid paradox**: Van den Brink et al. showed hypothalamic TGR5 receptor activation accelerated puberty in female rats through kisspeptin-dependent GnRH secretion, yet GDCA supplementation *ameliorated* PP symptoms. The authors attribute this to rodent-specific differences in BA metabolism, context-dependent effects (HFD vs. normal diet), and non-native forms of GDCA.
- **Milk controversy**: Milk's effects on pubertal timing remain controversial — some studies report negative associations, some positive, some null — potentially due to genetic backgrounds, study design, dietary intake assessment methods, and temporal context.

## Open Questions

1. **Are SCFA-producing bacteria drivers or passengers in PP?** Cross-sectional studies cannot establish causality for the paradoxical increase of SCFA producers in PP girls.
2. **What are the precise molecular mechanisms by which specific microbial taxa and their metabolic products regulate pubertal timing?** The causal chain from individual species to HPG axis activation remains incompletely mapped.
3. **Do the animal model findings translate to humans?** Most mechanistic data on PP comes from rodent models; species differences in BA metabolism, hormonal regulation, and microbiome composition limit direct translation.
4. **What is the role of integrated multi-omics (metagenomics, metabolomics, epigenomics, proteomics) in elucidating HFD-GM-PP relationships?** Current evidence relies primarily on low-resolution 16S rRNA sequencing.
5. **Can FMT be safely and effectively used in children with PP?** Only two animal studies exist; safety and mechanism data in pediatric populations are absent.
6. **How do the estrobolome mechanisms described here interact with metal-dependent virulence in estrogen-driven conditions like [[endometriosis]]?** The beta-glucuronidase pathway overlaps with WikiBiome's existing estrobolome framework but metal dependencies of the producing organisms are not addressed.
