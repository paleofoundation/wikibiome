# Turrini 2024 -- M13 Phage Nanovector for Photodynamic Treatment of CRC

## Key Claims

- Engineered M13 phage (M13cc) displaying a colon cancer-targeting peptide (CPDIERPMG) was conjugated with Rose Bengal (RB) photosensitizer; the resulting M13cc-RB bioconjugate showed specific targeting and photodynamic anticancer effects on CRC cells
- M13cc-RB enhanced generation and intracellular accumulation of reactive oxygen species (ROS) compared to unmodified M13 phage, triggering cancer cell death
- Photodynamic effects were demonstrated on 3D colon cancer spheroids for the first time, proving the ability of engineered M13 phage conjugates to deeply penetrate multicellular spheroids with significant spheroid disruption and cytotoxicity at picomolar concentrations
- M13cc-RB targets CC cells through pentavalent display of targeting peptide on minor coat protein pIII, with specificity confirmed by immunoblotting and immunocytochemistry
- Cell viability was impaired at RB equivalents of 1 microM with 30 min light exposure; significant photodynamic effects readily triggered at picomolar phage concentrations

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Bioengineering study using M13 filamentous phage modified for CC-specific targeting
- **Methods**: Phage modification (pSEX81 vector, pPK24 phagemid), RB conjugation via EDC/NHS chemistry, fluorescence/confocal microscopy, cell viability (MTT), spheroid generation and penetration assays, ROS detection (ABMDMA)
- **Cell lines**: HT29 (mismatch repair), DLD1 and HCT116 (MMR deficient); plus non-CC A431 and BT474 controls
