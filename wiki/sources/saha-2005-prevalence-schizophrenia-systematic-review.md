# A Systematic Review of the Prevalence of Schizophrenia

## Key Claims

- A total of 1,721 prevalence estimates from 188 studies across 46 countries were identified, based on an estimated 154,140 potentially overlapping prevalent cases
- Median values per 1,000 persons for point, period, lifetime, and lifetime morbid risk were 4.6, 3.3, 4.0, and 7.2 respectively
- No significant difference in combined prevalence estimates between males and females, or between urban, rural, and mixed sites
- Prevalence of [[schizophrenia]] in migrants was higher compared to native-born individuals (migrant-to-native-born ratio median 1.8)
- Prevalence estimates from "least developed" countries were significantly lower than those from "emerging" and "developed" sites (p = 0.04)
- Males were more likely to develop schizophrenia than native-born individuals (risk ratio = 1.4); urban settings had higher risk than mixed suburban settings

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Search period**: Studies published 1965-2002
- **Included studies**: 188 studies with independent prevalence rates (132 core, 41 special groups, 15 migrant)
- **Analytic approach**: Cumulative plots, between- and within-study filters, log-transformed distributions
