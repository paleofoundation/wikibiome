## Key Findings

- **Mean heavy metal concentrations in Guizhou dried chili**: Cr 0.954 mg/kg, As 0.329 mg/kg, Pb 0.895 mg/kg, Cd 0.0627 mg/kg — ranking Cr > Pb > As > Cd by concentration (but exceedances driven by Cr, Pb, Cd).
- **Regional exceedances of GB 2762-2022 limits**:
  - Cr (limit 0.5 mg/kg for chili): exceeded in QDN (Qiandongnan, 2.166 mg/kg), AS (Anshun, 1.325 mg/kg), BJ (Bijie, 1.907 mg/kg)
  - Pb (limit 1.0 mg/kg): exceeded in QDN (1.894 mg/kg, 33-89% exceedance range)
  - Cd (limit 0.05 mg/kg): exceeded in BJ (0.064), QN (0.115), QDN (0.157) regions by 28-214%
  - As (limit 0.5 mg/kg): within limits but high baseline from coal combustion in high-arsenic coal areas
- **Mining and industrial activities drove the contamination pattern** — coal combustion in As-rich coal regions, metal mining (lead, zinc, copper), and agricultural practices.
- **Children at elevated risk per body weight** (18.74 kg vs 64.3 kg adults); TCR (target cancer risk) values below 10^-4 but present.

## Methods

130 samples from 9 districts. HNO3/H2O2 microwave digestion (Ethos A Milestone) at 200°C. ICP-MS (Thermo Fisher iCAP-Q). Spiked recoveries 90.8-115%. EDI, THQ, TTHQ, and TCR calculated per IR (adult 1.71 g/day; child 0.85 g/day) and body weight.
