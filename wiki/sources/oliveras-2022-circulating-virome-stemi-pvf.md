# Circulating Virome and Inflammatory Proteome in Patients with STEMI and Primary Ventricular Fibrillation

## Summary

Pilot study evaluating the circulating virome and systemic inflammatory proteome in patients with ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI) with and without primary ventricular fibrillation (PVF) at the time of primary PCI. Used VirCapSeq-VERT platform targeting 342,438 viral taxa.

## Key Findings

### Circulating Virome Composition
- No significant differences in viral species richness or diversity between PVF and non-PVF STEMI patients
- Predominant OTU: *Human endogenous retrovirus K113* (NC_022518.1)
- Most frequent genus: *Lymphocryptovirus* (belonging to *Herpesviridae* family)
- Complete genomes detected: *Human gammaherpesvirus 4* (EBV), *Human herpesvirus 4 type 2*, *Macacine gammaherpesvirus 4*
- *Anelloviridae* family (Torque teno viruses) represented by 34 species across multiple genera

### PVF vs. Non-PVF Differences
- *Human herpesvirus 4 type 2* significantly less frequent in PVF patients (p = 0.024)
- *Macacine gammaherpesvirus 4* significantly less frequent in non-PVF patients (p = 0.042)
- No significant difference in *Human gammaherpesvirus 4* frequency
- EBV viral load (RT-PCR) showed no significant differences between groups

### Inflammatory Proteome
- CDCP1 and IL18-R1 significantly higher in PVF patients compared to healthy subjects
- No significant differences in inflammatory profiles between PVF and non-PVF STEMI patients
- Inflammatory proteins associated with cell adhesion, chemotaxis, and immune response elevated in all STEMI patients

## Relevance to Gut-Metal-Microbiome Axis

1. **Circulating virome in acute CVD** - Blood virome characterization during acute [[cardiovascular-disease]] events represents emerging field
2. **Herpesvirus-CVD link** - Herpesvirus detection in STEMI patients supports viral involvement in acute coronary events
3. **Inflammatory mediators** - CDCP1 and IL18-R1 as potential biomarkers linking viral burden to cardiac arrhythmia risk
