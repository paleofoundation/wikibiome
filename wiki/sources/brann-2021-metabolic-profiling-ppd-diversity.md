# Key Claims

- **Metabolomic profile variations** identified among women with PPD symptoms, revealing diversity in metabolic physiologies
- **Two metabolic clusters** identified: one showing altered kidney function, the other a metabolic syndrome profile previously associated with depression
- **Five metabolites** (glycerol, threonine, 2-hydroxybutanoic acid, erythritol, phenylalanine) showed higher abundance in PPD women, indicating perturbations in serine/threonine and glycerol lipid metabolism and oxidative stress
- **Plasma metabolic profiling** could aid PPD diagnosis and pathophysiological investigation

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Substudy within BASIC cohort; 24 women with PPD symptoms (PPDS) at 6 weeks postpartum; blood plasma at 10 weeks analyzed by GC-MS metabolomics
- **Setting**: Uppsala University, Sweden
- **Key implication**: Metabolic heterogeneity in PPD suggests need for subgroup-specific treatment approaches
