## Key Findings

- Prospective PROGRESS birth cohort from Mexico City, 123 mother–child pairs with shotgun metagenomic stool sequencing at 9–11 years old. Prenatal blood Pb measured by ICP-MS in 2nd and 3rd trimesters (mean 33.6 and 34.9 µg/L).
- Weighted quantile sum regression (WQS) showed consistent negative association between prenatal Pb and the overall gut microbial mixture for both trimesters (2T β=-0.17, 95%CI [-0.46, 0.11]; 3T β=-0.17, 95%CI [-0.44, 0.10]); ~88–89% of repeated holdouts were below zero.
- **Ruminococcus gnavus, Bifidobacterium longum, B. bifidum, Alistipes indistinctus, and Bacteroides caccae** all exceeded the WQS importance threshold in ≥80% of repeated holdouts for BOTH trimesters — i.e., the most reproducibly Pb-depleted taxa.
- Taxa-wide association analysis (TWAS) identified **A. putredinis, R. gnavus, B. caccae, B. intestinalis, Coprococcus catus, A. indistinctus** as negatively associated with 2nd-trimester Pb, and **B. bifidum, B. longum, A. indistinctus** with 3rd-trimester Pb. **Bacteroides coprocola** was positively associated in both trimesters; **Eubacterium eligens, B. finegoldii** were positively associated with 3rd-trimester Pb.
- Shared microbial gene pathways across Pb-affected taxa clustered on nucleic acid biosynthesis and essential housekeeping; trimester-specific pathways involved amino acid biosynthesis (histidine, methionine, isoleucine, lysine) and thiamin/peptidoglycan/folate metabolism — suggesting Pb differentially shapes specialized metabolic capacity.
- Second-trimester exposure showed the strongest signal, supporting 2nd trimester as a critical window.

## Relevance to WikiBiome

- Strengthens the [[lead]] entity page: human evidence that prenatal Pb exposure has persistent negative effects on gut microbiome composition 9–11 years later, even without elevated childhood Pb.
- Refines [[bacteroides-caccae]], [[bifidobacterium-longum]], [[ruminococcus-gnavus]], [[alistipes]] entity pages: these taxa are specifically vulnerable to prenatal Pb; the B. caccae depletion replicates Sitarik 2020's findings from a Detroit cohort (cross-population replication).
- Supports primitive #1 (Metals as Selective Pressures) as operating developmentally/transgenerationally — maternal Pb shapes offspring microbial ecology through birth transfer, breastmilk, and/or immune programming.
- Contributes to the public-health framing on [[prenatal-metal-exposure]] as an early determinant of microbial trajectory.

## Relevance to Cureva

- For practitioners evaluating children with atypical microbial depletion of Bifidobacterium/Alistipes/B. caccae, prenatal Pb exposure history (maternal environment during pregnancy) is a relevant exposure variable.
- Reinforces the STOP logic against viewing low beneficial taxa as intrinsic — historical metal exposure is a recoverable input variable.

## Tensions / Open Questions

- Pilot sample size (n=123). Confidence intervals cross zero for the overall mixture estimates.
- Functional pathway inference is limited to potential, not active transcription.
- Mechanism (direct fetal exposure vs. maternal microbiome transfer vs. breastmilk Pb vs. immune programming) remains unresolved.
