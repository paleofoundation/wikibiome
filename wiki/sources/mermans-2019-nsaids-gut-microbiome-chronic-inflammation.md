# Mermans 2019 -- NSAIDs as Therapeutic Allies of the Gut Microbiome

## Key Claims

- NSAIDs and gut microbiome have a bidirectional relationship: gut microbiota transforms NSAIDs into metabolites via direct and indirect mechanisms, while NSAIDs alter microbial community composition and function
- COX-2 is the inducible form upregulated in inflammatory conditions; COX-2 expression is found to be increased in colorectal cancer, showing enhanced prostaglandins, enhanced angiogenesis, and suppressed apoptosis
- Gut bacteria can degrade certain NSAID compounds and alter their bioavailability; bacterial beta-glucuronidase can reactivate glucuronidated NSAIDs in the gut, leading to local toxicity
- Pharmacomicrobiomics -- the impact of human microbiome variations on systems pharmacology -- is an emerging field; NSAIDs may be used to modulate the inflammatory response in colon-targeted drug delivery systems for CRC and IBD
- Interest in developing colon-targeted drug delivery systems has increased, as differences in microbiome composition may ultimately impact drug metabolism between subjects

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Invited review article
- **Figure 1**: COX-1 and COX-2 isoforms with their homeostatic vs. inflammatory functions
- **Key pharmacological insight**: COX-1 is primarily constitutive; COX-2 is considered a "housekeeping" enzyme with context-dependent expression; both catalyze arachidonic acid to PGH2
