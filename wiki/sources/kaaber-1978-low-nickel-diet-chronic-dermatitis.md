## Key Claims

- Dietary nickel reduction can improve chronic nickel dermatitis of dyshidrotic morphology in nickel-sensitive patients
- 17 of 28 nickel-sensitive patients experienced aggravation after oral ingestion of 2.5 mg nickel (but not placebo), confirming a link between dietary nickel and dermatitis flares
- 9 of 17 patients who followed a 6-week [[low-nickel-diet]] showed improvement; 7 of these 9 relapsed when resuming normal diet
- Urinary nickel excretion was reduced during dieting but no significant difference was found between those who improved and those who did not

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Blind oral challenge followed by open diet trial at the Finsen Institute, Copenhagen, Denmark
- **Population**: 28 patients (26 females, 2 males) with hand eczema of dyshidrotic morphology and positive patch test to 3.5% nickel sulphate
- **Protocol**: Blind challenge with nickel tablet (2.5 mg) vs. placebo; those reacting to nickel but not placebo followed a low-nickel diet for 6 weeks; re-examined at 2, 4, and 6 weeks
- **Diet**: Permitted foods included all meats, poultry, fish (except herring), eggs, milk, yoghurt, butter, margarine, cheese, polished rice, fresh fruits (except pears), flour (except whole-grain); prohibited foods included canned/acid foods cooked in stainless steel, herring, oysters, beans, mushrooms, onions, corn, spinach, tomatoes, peas, tea, cocoa/chocolate, coffee, rhubarb, baking powder, whole-grain flour, pears
- **Nickel excretion**: Measured by atomic absorption spectrophotometry; mean pre-diet urinary nickel 1.47 ug/24h; reduced during diet in 11/14 patients; 6/16 patients remained low after resuming normal diet
- **Limitations**: Moderate strength corticosteroid cream used during study; spontaneous variation could partially explain improvement

## Connections

- One of the earliest clinical studies of [[low-nickel-diet]] for dermatitis treatment, preceding [[veien-1993-low-nickel-diet-trial]]
- Diet instructions adapted by many subsequent studies including [[braga-2013-snas-nosologic-framework]]
- Nickel excretion methodology relates to later biomarker studies
- Confirmed the concept that dietary nickel provokes [[systemic contact dermatitis]], foundational for the [[systemic nickel allergy syndrome]] concept
