# Key Claims

- Urine [[metallomics]] can non-invasively discriminate pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) from healthy controls with high sensitivity (99.5%) using a combined analysis of Ca, Mg, Cu, and Zn concentrations
- PDAC patients show significantly lower urinary [[calcium]] and [[magnesium]] and increased urinary [[copper]] and [[zinc]] compared to healthy controls
- Natural stable Zn isotope composition in urine is significantly different in PDAC, with preferential excretion of isotopically light zinc, supporting dysregulation of metalloproteins
- The combination of trace element concentrations and isotope ratios represents a powerful new approach for pancreatic cancer biomarker discovery

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Discovery study analyzing urine from 46 healthy controls and 21 PDAC patients
- **Analytical methods**: ICP-MS for trace element analysis; MC-ICP-MS for Zn isotope analysis; microwave digestion for sample preparation

 - Urinary Mg significantly lower in PDAC (p < 0.0001) -- reflects disrupted cell proliferation and protein synthesis
 - Urinary Ca significantly lower in PDAC (p < 0.0001)
 - Urinary Zn significantly higher in PDAC (p = 0.02) -- correlates with disrupted Zn transporters (ZnT, SLC30A, ZIP3)
 - Urinary Cu significantly higher in PDAC (p = 0.015) -- mirrors plasma/serum Cu elevation previously reported
- **ROC analysis**: Individual AUCs -- Mg 0.783, Ca 0.796, Zn 0.685, Cu 0.671; combined Mg+Ca+Zn+Cu AUC = 0.99 (sensitivity 0.952, specificity 0.978)

 - PDAC urinary delta-66/64-Zn ranged from -0.33 to +0.15 per mille (median -0.15 per mille)
 - Healthy controls ranged from -0.26 to +0.67 per mille (median +0.02 per mille)
 - Significant difference (p = 0.002) between groups
 - 75% of PDAC samples showed preferential excretion of isotopically light Zn, while healthy controls excreted predominantly isotopically heavy Zn
 - Disrupted Zn isotope balance reflects metalloprotein dysregulation in PDAC
- **Zn homeostasis disruption**: In healthy controls, strong correlation between urinary Zn concentration and Zn/Cu ratio (r2 = 0.66); this correlation is absent in PDAC (r2 = 0.0002), indicating fundamental disruption of Zn regulation

# Connections

- [[copper]] elevation in PDAC urine parallels Cu increases found across multiple cancer types in blood/serum, reinforcing Cu as a universal [[metal-carcinogenesis]] biomarker
- [[zinc]] dysregulation through disrupted ZnT and ZIP transporters (particularly RREB1, ZIP3, SLC30A) connects to broader Zn-dependent metalloenzyme impairment in cancer
- Zn isotope fractionation as a biomarker represents a novel dimension of [[metallomics]] beyond concentration-based analysis
- The non-invasive urine-based approach addresses a critical need for early PDAC detection, one of the deadliest cancers with poorest prognosis
- ATP7A Cu transporter overexpression in PDAC connects [[copper]] metabolism to cancer-specific metalloprotein biology
- Mg and Ca decreases may relate to disrupted cell signaling and S100 protein dysregulation, connecting to [[calcium]] and [[magnesium]] roles in cellular homeostasis
- The combined biomarker panel approach mirrors multi-element strategies used in other cancers and cardiovascular disease [[metallomics]]
