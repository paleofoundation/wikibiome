# Why This Paper Matters

This is the first study to demonstrate that **fecal microbiota transplantation from ketogenic diet-fed mice** can attenuate schizophrenia-like sensorimotor gating deficits in a preclinical NMDA receptor antagonist model. While ketogenic diets (KDs) have shown promise for schizophrenia in case studies and open trials, their mechanisms of action remain unclear. This paper provides direct evidence that some beneficial effects of KDs are **mediated by the gut microbiota** rather than ketone bodies alone, since transferring the microbial community from KD-fed donors to standard diet-fed recipients reproduced behavioral improvements.

# Key Claims

- **Ketogenic diet-derived FMT** significantly improved prepulse inhibition (PPI) deficits induced by the NMDA receptor antagonist MK-801 (dizocilpine) in C57BL/6 mice
- FMT improved PPI in an **interstimulus interval (ISI)-dependent manner**: improvement at ISI 30 ms but NOT at ISI 100 ms, suggesting it strengthens gating of stimuli arriving in the immediate temporal proximity
- This ISI specificity is **distinct from chronic BHB (beta-hydroxybutyrate) treatment**, which improves PPI at ISI 100 but not ISI 30 -- suggesting KD-derived FMT and BHB have different biological pathways
- MK-801 significantly reduced average PPI (F = 10.044, P = 0.001), and the interaction between FMT and MK-801 approached significance at ISI 30
- **Ketogenic diet-derived FMT did NOT alter** startle amplitude, indicating the effect is specific to sensorimotor gating rather than general arousal
- The ketogenic diet used was very low carbohydrate (~0.6%), high fat (~67%) and medium protein (~17.3%)
- Fecal samples were collected from male C57BL/6 mice fed KD for 4 months; standard diet-fed mice received 3 inoculations every other day
- Behavioral testing was performed 1 week after the last FMT inoculation, with MK-801 (0.2 mg/kg) administered acutely before testing

# Methods & Findings

## Experimental Design
- Four groups (n = 8-9/group): (1) saline-saline, (2) saline-MK-801, (3) KD-FMT-saline, (4) KD-FMT-MK-801
- Fresh fecal filtrate (2.5 mL, 40 um nylon filter, 300 uL/mouse) administered by oral gavage
- Behavioral testing 1 hour after MK-801/saline injection

## Prepulse Inhibition Results
- MK-801 reduced PPI across all prepulse intensities (2, 4, 8, 16 dB above background)
- KD-derived FMT significantly improved MK-801-induced PPI deficit at ISI 30 ms
- At ISI 100 ms, KD-derived FMT did NOT improve PPI
- Startle reactivity (baseline acoustic startle response) was not affected by FMT

## Comparison with BHB
- Chronic BHB (3 weeks, 2 mmol/kg): normalizes PPI at ISI 100, no change at ISI 30
- KD-derived FMT: improves PPI at ISI 30, no change at ISI 100
- This complementary pattern suggests distinct mechanisms for metabolic vs. microbial effects of KD
- ISI 30 ms is associated with preconscious processing (brainstem-level); ISI 100 ms is associated with conscious information processing

## Mechanistic Implications
- FMT may work through epigenetic regulation of gene expression
- Gut microbiota can influence brain function through vagal nerve signaling, immune modulation, and metabolite production
- KD rapidly shifts gut microbial composition; the transplanted community may produce neuroactive metabolites
- The different ISI sensitivities suggest FMT and BHB target different neural circuits
