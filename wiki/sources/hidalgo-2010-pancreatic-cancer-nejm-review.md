# Key Claims

- Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma results from successive accumulation of gene mutations progressing from PanIN to invasive cancer, with [[metal-carcinogenesis|KRAS]] mutation in 90% of tumors
- The characteristic dense [[metal-carcinogenesis|stroma]] (desmoplastic reaction) formed by pancreatic stellate cells is a dynamic compartment critically involved in tumor formation, progression, invasion, and metastasis
- CA 19-9 is the only biomarker with demonstrated clinical usefulness for monitoring but is not specific for [[pancreatic-cancer]] and fails in ~10% of Lewis antigen-negative patients
- Cancer stem cells (1-5% of tumor) are resistant to chemotherapy and radiation, which may explain treatment failure and disease recurrence

# Methods/Findings

- **Staging**: TNM classification -- Stage IA (T1N0M0) median survival 24.1 months; Stage IV (any T, any N, M1) median survival 4.5 months
- **Surgery**: Only curative modality; only 10-20% of patients are candidates; 5-year survival approximately 20% after resection
- **Genetic alterations**: Inactivation of CDKN2A (95%), TP53 (50-75%), DPC4/SMAD4 (50%); average 63 abnormalities per tumor in 12 core pathways
- **Stroma biology**: Stellate cells activated by TGF-beta1, PDGF, FGF express cyclo-oxygenase-2, PDGF receptor, VEGF, SPARC, and hedgehog pathway elements
- **Angiogenesis**: Controversial role -- angiogenesis inhibitors have failed; targeting stromal hedgehog pathway may increase chemotherapy delivery
- **Diagnosis**: Contrast-enhanced helical CT predicts surgical resectability with 80-90% accuracy; EUS useful when CT equivocal

# Connections

- [[metal-carcinogenesis|Tumor microenvironment]] and stellate cell activation link to potential [[gut-metal-microbiome|microbiome-host interactions]] in shaping immune responses
- CA 19-9 elevation also occurs in benign conditions including [[pancreatitis]], cholangitis, and [[inflammatory-bowel-disease]]
- Hedgehog pathway targeting represents a potential [[probiotics|microbiome-targeted therapy]] approach through modulation of the tumor stroma
