# Key Claims

- **Exogenous hydrogen sulfide affects diabetic metabolic disorders** in association with gut bacterial and fungal microbiota changes
- **H2S treatment modulates both bacterial and fungal gut communities** in diabetic mouse models
- **Gut fungal-bacterial interactions** are relevant to metabolic outcomes in diabetes

# Methods & Findings

db/db diabetic mouse model treated with exogenous H2S:

- Both bacterial and fungal microbiota composition assessed
- Metabolic parameters including glucose and lipid profiles measured
- H2S-microbiota-metabolism axis characterized

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: H2S interacts with zinc-dependent metalloenzymes, potentially modulating their activity
- **[[iron]]**: H2S binds iron in iron-sulfur clusters, affecting mitochondrial and microbial metabolism
- **[[selenium]]**: H2S and selenium share pathways in redox biology and gasotransmitter signaling
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: H2S is a gasotransmitter that links metal biology, microbiome function, and host metabolism
