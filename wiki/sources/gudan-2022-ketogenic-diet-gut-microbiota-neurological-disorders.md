# Key Claims

- **Animal-product based diets** (high saturated fat) increase bile-resistant microorganisms including Bilophila and increase Alistipes and Bacteroides
- **Long-term consumption of animal-based foods** contributes to formation of the enterotype described as Bacteroides type
- **Ketogenic diet** has shown protective effects in neurodegenerative and metabolic diseases, but is highly dependent on gut microbiota
- **Adverse changes in intestinal microbiota** from KD can significantly deteriorate intestinal barrier function and cause inflammation
- Review focuses on the correlation between KD influence on intestinal microbiota changes observed in epilepsy, Alzheimer's disease, ASD, and multiple sclerosis

# Methods & Findings

**Literature review** of papers from March 2020 to May 2020 on ketogenic diet and gut microbiota.

- Examined KD effects on intestinal barrier and transmetabolomics
- Reviewed microbiome changes across multiple neurological conditions
- Highlighted that dietary fat composition shapes microbial communities
- Noted both beneficial and detrimental effects of KD depending on context
- Emphasized the role of intestinal barrier integrity in mediating KD effects

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **KD-microbiome-ASD axis** -- ketogenic diet modifies the same microbial taxa altered in ASD
- **Intestinal barrier** integrity as mediating factor between KD effects and neurological outcomes
- **Disease-specific microbiome responses** to KD relevant to personalized dietary interventions in ASD
