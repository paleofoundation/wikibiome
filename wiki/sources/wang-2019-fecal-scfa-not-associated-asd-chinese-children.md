# Overview

Case-control study of **45 ASD children and 90 age-sex-matched controls** (ages 6-9) from Guangzhou, China, measuring **10 fecal SCFA subtypes** by HPLC. Found **no significant association between fecal SCFA concentrations and ASD risk** after adjustment for confounders. A notable **negative result** challenging the hypothesis that fecal SCFA levels are directly linked to ASD.

# Key Findings

## SCFA Concentrations (ASD vs Control)

- **Total SCFAs**: 111.7 vs 106.2 umol/g (not significant)
- **Acetic acid**: 56.3 vs 53.1 umol/g (p = 0.459)
- **Propionic acid**: 25.0 vs 22.8 umol/g (p = 0.346)
- **Butyric acid**: 19.9 vs 19.9 umol/g (p = 0.965)
- Percentages of acetate, propionate, and butyrate: 50.8%, 22.2%, and 19.9% in ASD vs 49.9%, 21.4%, 19.9% in controls — no significant differences

## Conditional Logistic Regression

- After adjustment for age, sex, BMI, breastfeeding, delivery mode, parental education, daily energy/protein/fat/fiber intake:
- **No significant associations** between any SCFA subtype (absolute or percentage) and ASD risk
- ORs across tertiles: all non-significant
- Both crude and fully adjusted models showed no association

## Minor SCFAs Also Not Associated

- Crotonic acid, isobutyric acid, 2-methylbutyric acid, isovaleric acid, valeric acid, 4-methylvaleric acid, hexanoic acid — all not significantly different

## Population Characteristics

- ASD children significantly younger (7.67 vs 7.73 years, p = 0.008)
- Higher breastfeeding rate in ASD (77.8% vs 87.8%)
- Lower maternal education in ASD
- Fat intake significantly higher in ASD group (64.5 vs 67.7 g/d, p = 0.003)

# Context of Prior Studies

- **Inconsistent prior results**: some studies found elevated SCFAs in ASD, others found decreased
- Wang et al. (2012, Australia): elevated fecal acetic, propionic, butyric acids in ASD
- Kang et al. (2018, USA): no significant group differences in fecal SCFAs
- Adams et al. (2011): decreased SCFAs in ASD
- Differences may reflect population genetics, diet, microbiome variation, measurement timing

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

**Moderate relevance** — negative result has implications for metal-SCFA hypothesis:

- If fecal SCFA levels are not consistently altered in ASD, the SCFA-mediated pathway to metal absorption changes may be less direct
- [[Butyrate]] regulates [[iron]] transporter expression — but fecal butyrate may not reflect colonic epithelial exposure
- Single-timepoint fecal SCFA measurement may miss temporal dynamics
- Suggests that **microbial SCFA production capacity** (from metagenomics) may be more informative than actual fecal SCFA levels

# Methodological Notes

- Relatively large sample (45 ASD + 90 controls) for SCFA study
- HPLC quantification of 10 SCFA subtypes — comprehensive
- 3-day food diary + food frequency questionnaire for dietary adjustment
- Matched controls (age and sex)
- Single stool sample at single timepoint — may not capture variability
- Chinese population — dietary context (high rice/fiber) may differ from Western studies
- 3-month antibiotic exclusion period
