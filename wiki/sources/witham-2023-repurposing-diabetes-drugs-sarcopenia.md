# Key Claims

- **T2DM and sarcopenia share common mechanisms**: advanced glycation end products, mitochondrial dysfunction, chronic inflammation, insulin signalling changes
- **GLP-1 receptor agonists and SGLT2 inhibitors** show most promise for sarcopenia benefit
- **Metformin has mixed evidence** for skeletal muscle effects
- **Clinical trials needed** enrolling people with sarcopenia specifically

# Methods & Findings

Narrative review of diabetes drugs for sarcopenia:

- Pathophysiological overlap between diabetes and sarcopenia reviewed
- Preclinical, observational, and interventional data for each drug class assessed
- Not all diabetes treatments safe for non-diabetic use due to variable side effects

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc deficiency contributes to both sarcopenia and diabetes -- co-addressing zinc status may enhance drug repurposing outcomes
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent selenoprotein P influences both muscle and metabolic function
- **[[iron]]**: Muscle iron homeostasis is disrupted in both diabetes and sarcopenia -- shared metal-dependent pathology
- **[[immune-balance]]**: Chronic inflammation linking diabetes and sarcopenia is metal-cofactor dependent
