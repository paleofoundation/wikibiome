# Key Claims

- Environmental agents (mercury, silica, trichloroethylene, pesticides, smoking) can trigger [[autoimmune diseases]] in genetically susceptible individuals
- [[Oxidative stress]] is the most common shared mechanism by which environmental agents contribute to autoimmune disease pathogenesis
- [[Gut microbiome]] dysbiosis is a significant contributing factor to autoimmune diseases, though whether it is causal or consequential remains unclear
- Environmental chemical exposures can cause gut microbiome dysbiosis, which may alter intestinal barrier function and mucosal immunity, promoting systemic autoimmune responses
- [[probiotics]], [[antibiotics]], and [[fecal microbiota transplantation]] show therapeutic potential in autoimmune disease models

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review
- Reviews epidemiological and mechanistic evidence linking environmental/occupational exposures to autoimmune diseases, with emphasis on [[systemic lupus erythematosus]]
- **Mercury**: Exposure leads to lupus-like syndrome in autoimmune-prone mice; Hg-induced autoimmunity (HgIA) is a type 1 IFN-independent model; Hg alters phosphorylation of cytoskeletal proteins and B cell receptor signaling
- **Pesticides**: Chlordecone accelerates SLE in mouse models; organochlorine insecticides associated with higher ANA levels in farmers
- **Silica**: Exposure linked to SLE, RA, systemic sclerosis; lupus-prone NZ-2410 mice show increased autoantibodies and immune complex deposits after silica exposure; mechanism involves NLRP3 inflammasome and STING activation
- **Trichloroethylene (TCE)**: Causes autoimmunity via oxidative stress and lipid peroxidation-derived aldehydes; alters gut microbiome in autoimmune-prone mice
- **Smoking**: Induces oxidative stress, causes OS-mediated autoimmunity, and alters gut microbiome composition
- **Gut microbiome in SLE**: Patients show lower Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio, reduced Lactobacillaceae, increased Lachnospiraceae; increased serum LPS suggests leaky gut
- **Probiotics**: Oral Lactobacillus in MRL/lpr mice restored intestinal barrier, suppressed pro-inflammatory cytokines, improved Treg/Th17 ratio
- **Oxidative stress link**: NADPH oxidase Nox2 catalyzes oxidative burst; Lactobacilli have greater potential to induce ROS with enhanced ability to penetrate mucus layer
- **Fecal microbiota transplantation**: Fecal microbiome from SLE mice induces autoimmune responses in germ-free mice
