# Nutritional and Physio Chemical Analysis of Commercial Apple Juices and Natural Apple Juices Available in Pakistan

## Key Claims

- Fresh natural apple juices have higher mineral and organic contents than commercial packed juices
- Nickel was detected in both fresh (0.04 ppm) and commercial (0.02 ppm) apple juices -- relatively low levels
- Manganese was comparatively high in fresh natural juices (0.55 ppm) vs commercial (0.38 ppm)
- Chromium levels were higher in commercial juices (1.62 ppm vs 0.51 ppm in fresh), suggesting processing-related contamination
- Fresh apple juice had higher vitamin C (1.88 mg/100mL vs 0.76 mg/100mL), supporting its nutritional superiority
- Processing (clarification, pasteurization) may reduce some beneficial compounds while introducing contaminants

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Comparative analysis of fresh natural and commercial apple juices from Pakistani markets
- **Brands**: Pure Heaven, Lacnor, Mezan, Slice, Nestle, Country, and 11 natural varieties (Carrot Apple, Swat Apple, Golden Apple, etc.)
- **Methods**: AAS for minerals (Ca, Mn, Zn); modified AAS for macroelements; UV-VIS for phosphorus; AOAC methods for proximate analysis
- **pH range**: 1.7-4.7 across all varieties; fresh juices had higher pH (3.56) vs commercial (3.01)
- **TSS**: Fresh juices higher (8.38 Brix) vs commercial (8.22 Brix)
- **Protein**: Slightly higher in fresh (0.08%) vs commercial (0.06%)
