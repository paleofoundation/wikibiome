# Key Claims

- **Oral lyophilized FMT improved GI, ASD, and sleep symptoms** in 38 ASD children
- **Both bacterial and fungal microbiota** altered by FMT (16S rRNA + ITS sequencing)
- **ABC scores decreased 23%**, CARS 10%, SRS 6%, SDSC 10% from baseline
- **Fungal genera** Blautia, Sellimonas, Saccharomycopsis, Cystobasidium more abundant in ASD pre-FMT

# Methods & Findings

**Design:** Open-label clinical trial, 38 ASD children (ages 3-14) + 30 healthy controls. 12-week FMT + 8-week follow-up. Trial: ChiCTR2200055943.

**FMT protocol:**
- Oral lyophilized capsules every 4 weeks for 12 weeks (3 courses)
- Dose: 1g donor stool per 1kg recipient body weight
- No vancomycin or PPI pretreatment

**Clinical outcomes at follow-up (week 20):**
- GSRS: decreased 51% from baseline, reversed to 32% decrease by week 20
- ABC: decreased 23% at week 12, persisted at 18% at week 20
- CARS: decreased 10% at week 12
- SRS: decreased 6% at week 12
- SDSC (sleep): decreased 10% at week 12, persisted at week 20

**Bacterial microbiota:**
- Significant differences in beta diversity between ASD and HC
- [[blautia]], [[Sellimonas]] more abundant in ASD pre-FMT; decreased after treatment
- [[dorea]] less abundant in ASD; increased after FMT

**Fungal microbiota (ITS sequencing):**
- [[Saccharomycopsis]], [[Cystobasidium]] more abundant in ASD; decreased after FMT
- [[Fusicatenibacter]], [[Erysipelotrichaceae_UCG-003]], [[saccharomyces]], [[Rhodotorula]], [[Cutaneotrichosporon]], [[Zygosaccharomyces]] negatively correlated with ASD core symptom scores

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **Fungal-bacterial-metal interplay**: Saccharomycopsis and Cystobasidium are metal-accumulating fungi; their elevation in ASD may reflect compensatory metal sequestration in a dysbiotic environment
- **Sleep improvement**: SDSC changes suggest FMT modulates melatonin pathways that require tryptophan and metal-dependent enzymatic conversion
- **Lyophilized oral route feasibility**: Standardized oral delivery enables consistent dosing for metal-ecological restoration without endoscopic intervention
- **Transient benefits pattern**: Consistent with other FMT studies, benefits fade post-cessation, reinforcing need for sustained metal-ecological support
