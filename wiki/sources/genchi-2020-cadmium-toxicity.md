# The Effects of Cadmium Toxicity

## Key Claims

- [[cadmium]] is a toxic non-essential transition metal with a half-life of 25-30 years in humans, posing long-term accumulation risks
- Occupational and environmental cadmium exposure is linked to cancers of the breast, lung, prostate, nasopharynx, pancreas, and kidney
- The liver and kidneys are extremely sensitive to cadmium due to their capacity to synthesize [[metallothioneins]] (MT), which bind toxic Cd ions
- [[Mitochondria]] are key intracellular targets for cadmium; Cd inhibits respiratory chain complexes II and III, reduces ATP synthesis, generates [[reactive oxygen species]] (ROS), and induces apoptosis via caspase-8 and caspase-9 activation
- Cadmium induces [[epigenetic changes]] including alterations in DNA methylation, histone modifications, and micro RNA expression
- Cadmium is classified as a proven human carcinogen (IARC Group 1)
- Cadmium acts as an [[endocrine disruptor]], binding estrogen receptor alpha and affecting MAPK signaling pathways
- Environmental cadmium may be a risk factor for [[osteoporosis]], rheumatoid arthritis, and osteoarthritis

## Methods/Findings

- **Document type**: Comprehensive narrative review (24 pages)
- **Exposure routes**: Food (rice, leafy vegetables, shellfish), cigarette smoke (a cigarette contains ~1 ug Cd; 40-60% absorbed via inhalation), contaminated water, occupational dust
- **Dietary sources**: Vegetarians and shellfish consumers face higher cadmium intake than omnivores; rice consumption is a major route in Asia
- **Chelation therapy**: EDTA, BAL (dimercaprol), DMSA, DMPS, and lipophilic analogs (MiADMSA, MmDMSA, MchDMSA) are discussed; combination of DMPS and NAC (N-acetyl cysteine) reduces hepatic and renal metallothioneins
- **Protective compounds**: Polyphenols (cloves 15,200 mg/100g; cocoa powder 3,450 mg/100g), melatonin, selenium, vitamin E show protective effects; caution needed as vitamin C in presence of Cd/Ni causes Fenton-type DNA breaks
- **Metallothionein biology**: MT proteins (MW 7-8 KDa) contain 61-68 amino acids with 18-23 cysteines; can complex 7 divalent cations; half-life of Cd-MT complex is 25-30 years vs. free Cd half-life of only a few hours
- **Mitochondrial damage pathway**: Cd inhibits electron transport complexes II and III, collapses membrane potential, opens permeability transition pore, releases cytochrome C, activates caspase cascade
- **Phytoremediation**: Sunflower, Indian mustard, river red gum can remove Cd from polluted soil; TiO2 and Al2O3 nanoparticles used for wastewater remediation
