# Thyroid Hormones and Minerals in Immunocorrection of Disorders in Autoimmune Thyroid Diseases

## Key Findings

- **No effective pharmacological therapy exists for AITD** (Hashimoto's thyroiditis, Graves' disease, Graves' ophthalmopathy); current treatment is symptomatic with synthetic thyroid hormones or thyrostatics, with 13.3% treatment failure and 36.7% relapse among GD patients after drug discontinuation ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).
- **Selenium supplementation reduces anti-TPO antibodies** and increases GPx and thioredoxin reductase (TR) enzyme activity. A **meta-analysis by Zheng et al. (2018) of 10 randomized placebo-controlled trials (n=796)** showed short-term efficacy of Se on fT4 and fT3 levels at 3 and 6 months, with TRAb levels decreasing more often after 6 months but not after 9 months ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).
- **Very low serum magnesium** is associated with a 4.5-fold increased risk of TgAb positivity, HT, and hypothyroidism (OR = 4.482-4.971 for lowest Mg group; p < 0.01), and lower Mg and Fe concentrations are found in TPOAb- and TgAb-positive HT patients ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).
- **Iron deficiency impairs thyroid peroxidase (TPO) activity** — TPO is a glycosylated hemoenzyme requiring Fe^2+ — and modulates macrophage polarization from anti-inflammatory M2 toward pro-inflammatory M1, increasing IL-12/IL-10 ratio ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).
- **Zinc deficiency impairs thymulin activation**, T cell differentiation (Th1, Th2, Th17, Treg), and melatonin receptor expression; intracellular free Zn is important for Treg maintenance, and Zn deficiency promotes a pro-inflammatory immune response ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

## Methods

- **Study design:** Critical narrative review analyzing immunotherapeutic use of dietary supplements (I, Se, Zn, Fe, Cu, Mg, Ca) in AITD, published in Frontiers in Endocrinology (received 19 May 2023, published 30 August 2023).
- **Scope:** Examines the immunomodulatory role of thyroid hormones (TG) and seven minerals on both innate and adaptive immunity in AITD, with comprehensive Table 1 mapping each mineral's immunological targets under normal and AITD conditions.
- **AITD epidemiology cited:** TPOAb prevalence 11.3%, TgAb 2.0% in euthyroid subjects (15.3% of individuals); AITD incidence 27-273 cases per 100,000/year; hyperthyroidism prevalence 1.2-1.6%, of which 0.5-0.6% overt; GD age-adjusted incidence 24.8 per 100,000/year in Sheffield, UK.

## Detailed Summary

This comprehensive review systematically examines how thyroid hormones and seven essential minerals participate in immunomodulation relevant to AITD pathogenesis and treatment.

**Thyroid hormone immunology:** T3 promotes phenotypic maturation of dendritic cells by regulating MHC-II and costimulatory molecules; triiodothyronine promotes Th1/Th17 development and reduces Treg function. Hyperthyroid states activate the immune system while hypothyroidism suppresses it. TG effects are mediated through NF-kB, protein kinase C, and beta-adrenergic receptor signaling pathways, operating through both genomic (nuclear receptors) and non-genomic (membrane) mechanisms including mTOR ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

**Iodine:** Both deficiency and excess serve as risk factors for AITD. More than 2 billion people are at risk of insufficient iodine supply. Excess I increases prevalence of autoimmune thyroiditis after salt iodization programs. At low concentrations, I activates thyroid cancer cell proliferation via AKT/ERK; at excess, it inhibits via the Wolff-Chaikoff effect. In vivo and in vitro, excess iodine increases Th17, TRAIL, ROS, and apoptosis while decreasing TGF-beta1 and Treg populations ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

**Selenium:** Selenoproteins (GPx, TR) play a central role in antioxidant defense and anti-inflammatory processes. Treatment of HT patients with Se yeast for 6 months significantly increased Se, GPx3, and selenoprotein P1 levels. In primary hypothyroidism patients, Se decreased malondialdehyde (MDA), an oxidative stress marker. Combined selenomethionine and myoinositol reduced TSH and antithyroid antibodies after 6 and 12 months. The EUGOGO clinical trial confirmed beneficial effects of Se for moderate Graves' ophthalmopathy over a 6-month course. Se supplementation is appropriate only when Se deficiency is confirmed; in selenium-sufficient GD patients treated with methimazole, no beneficial effect was observed ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

**Iron and copper:** Fe deficiency impairs TPO activity (TPO requires Fe^2+ as prosthetic group), is associated with higher prevalence of TPOAb (not TgAb) in pregnant and non-pregnant women with subclinical hypothyroidism, and results in autoimmune gastritis which further reduces Fe absorption. Fe modulates macrophage polarization: elevated Fe increases M2 phenotype and reduces M1 response. Cu binds to ceruloplasmin stimulating immune function; patients with GD/GO conversely had low serum Cu and high serum Se. The ratio of Cu and Se in blood is significantly related to TSH and fT4. Cu serves as cofactor for CuSOD, cytochrome C oxidase; ZnSOD protects against ROS ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

**Zinc:** Zn homeostasis is regulated by Zn transporters and metallothioneins. Zn is necessary for thymulin (Zn-FTS) activation — a peptide synthesized by thymus epithelial cells that plays key roles in T cell differentiation, apoptosis, and reactivation. Zinc participates in immunosenescence through age-related decrease in Zn bioavailability due to metallothionein increase. Zn deficiency leads to impaired immune response, altered signal transduction, and increased autoimmune susceptibility. Cross-sectional studies show relationship between Zn deficiency and TG levels. Zn also acts as antioxidant by inhibiting oxidation of macromolecules and reducing ROS production ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

**Magnesium:** Low serum Mg is associated with low-grade chronic inflammation via NF-kB, IL-1, IL-6, E-selectins, ICAM-1, VCAM-1, and CRP. In patients with latent HT (high titer TPOAb with mild iodine deficiency), Ca, Mg, and Zn content in blood serum decreases. The risks of hypothyroidism and subclinical hypothyroidism in the lowest Mg group (less than or equal to 0.55 mmol/L) were significantly higher (p < 0.01, OR = 4.482-4.971). Mg regulates proper functioning of other nutrients including vitamin D, Ca, and P homeostasis, and is necessary for H2O2 production and TG synthesis ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

**Calcium:** Intracellular Ca^2+ is key regulator of cell apoptosis; Ca activates mTOR pathway and B-lymphocyte proliferation. Ca regulation of BAFF (B-cell activating factor) is important in autoimmunity. Ca/Mg ratio is approximately 2.0 and ratios less than 1.7 or greater than 2.8 may be harmful. In hypothyroidism, serum Ca and Mg decrease while P levels elevate. In hyperthyroidism, prevalence of hypercalcemia is 38% ([[kravchenko-2023-thyroid-hormones-minerals-AITD]], expert-opinion).

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Selenium supplementation as intervention for HT/GO:** Triangle evidence: I_to_f (Se increases GPx/TR, reduces OS), I_to_D (meta-analysis of 10 RCTs shows antibody reduction), f_to_D (OS drives thyrocyte damage in AITD). BUT Se supplementation should only be offered when Se deficiency is confirmed — no benefit in Se-sufficient patients.
- **Iron supplementation as potential STOP in AITD:** If hepcidin elevation indicates functional anemia (host defense via nutritional immunity), iron supplementation may feed siderophore-producing pathogens. This paper's data on Fe-TPO dependency creates tension with the nutritional immunity framework — requires careful distinction between true deficiency and functional sequestration.
- **Magnesium supplementation hypothesis:** OR data for low Mg and AITD risk suggests Mg supplementation as candidate intervention, but evidence is observational only.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- **Se supplementation timing:** Beneficial at 3-6 months but benefit disappears by 9 months in the Zheng 2018 meta-analysis. This raises questions about sustained efficacy vs. transient effect.
- **Se in GD vs. HT:** Beneficial in moderate GO (EUGOGO), but no benefit in Se-sufficient GD patients treated with methimazole. Disease-specific effects complicate universal mineral supplementation recommendations.
- **Iodine paradox:** Both deficiency and excess cause AITD, making optimal dosing a narrow therapeutic window rather than a simple supplementation target.
- **Iron as double-edged sword:** Fe deficiency impairs TPO and increases autoimmune susceptibility, but Fe supplementation could feed pathogenic organisms in the gut-thyroid axis — a direct tension with the nutritional immunity framework (Karen's Brain Primitive 2).
