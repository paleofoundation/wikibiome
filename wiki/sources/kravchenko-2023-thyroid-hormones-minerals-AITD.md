# Thyroid hormones and minerals in immunocorrection of disorders in autoimmune thyroid diseases

## Key Claims

- There is currently no effective pharmacological therapy for [[autoimmune thyroid disease]] (AITD); treatment remains symptomatic with synthetic TG or thyrostatics
- Thyroid hormones (TG) and minerals play important immunomodulating roles in both innate and adaptive immunity relevant to AITD
- [[selenium]] supplementation reduces anti-TPO antibodies, increases GPx and TR enzyme activity, and decreases oxidative stress markers in [[Hashimoto's thyroiditis]]
- [[iodine]] deficiency and excess both serve as risk factors for AITD; careful monitoring of iodine prophylaxis is essential
- [[zinc]] is necessary for thymus hormone thymulin activation, T cell differentiation, and melatonin receptor expression; Zn deficiency impairs immune function
- [[iron]] deficiency decreases TPO activity and is associated with increased autoantibodies (TPOAb, TgAb) in autoimmune thyroiditis
- [[magnesium]] exhibits immunomodulating effects through NF-kB, IL-6, TNF-a regulation and is important for mTOR pathway regulation
- [[Calcium]] influences CD8+ T cell apoptosis, Treg regulation, and BAFF/B-cell activation via mTOR pathway
- The immunomodulating targets of minerals in AITD include normalization of neutrophil function, reduction of ROS, and suppression of pro-inflammatory cytokines

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Critical review analyzing immunotherapeutic use of dietary supplements in AITD
- **AITD prevalence**: TPOAb in 11.3%, TgAb in 2.0% of euthyroid subjects (15.3% of individuals); AITD incidence 27-273 cases per 100,000/year
- **Thyroid hormone immunology**: T3 promotes phenotypic maturation of DCs; triiodothyronine promotes Th1/Th17 development; hyperthyroid state activates immune system while hypothyroidism suppresses it
- **Iodine**: Excess I increases prevalence of autoimmune thyroiditis after salt iodization programs; I at low concentrations activates thyroid cancer cell proliferation via AKT/ERK; excess I inhibits via Wolff-Chaikoff effect
- **Selenium**: Treatment of HT patients with Se yeast for 6 months increased Se, GPx3, and selenoprotein P1; in patients with primary hypothyroidism, Se decreased MDA (oxidative stress marker); combined selenomethionine and myoinositol reduced TSH and antithyroid antibodies after 6 and 12 months
- **Iron and copper**: Fe deficiency impairs TPO activity; Fe has immunomodulating effect on M1/M2 macrophage polarization; Cu binds to ceruloplasmin stimulating immune function; patients with GD/GO had low serum Cu and high serum Se levels
- **Zinc**: Zn homeostasis regulated by Zn transporters and metallothioneins; Zn necessary for thymulin activation; deficiency leads to impaired T cell function and increased autoimmune susceptibility; age-related Zn decrease contributes to immunosenescence
- **Magnesium**: Low serum Mg associated with increased risk of TgAb, HT, and hypothyroidism (OR = 4.482-4.971 for lowest Mg group); Mg supplementation may improve thyroid function
- **Calcium**: Intracellular Ca2+ is key regulator of cell apoptosis; Ca activates mTOR pathway and B-lymphocyte proliferation; Ca regulation of BAFF is important in autoimmunity
