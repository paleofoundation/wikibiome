# Safety and Efficacy of Ketogenic Diet in the Management of Multiple Sclerosis: A Systematic Review

## Key Claims

- Six studies (2017-2024, sample sizes 21-65) met inclusion criteria from 132 initial records, all focusing predominantly on relapsing-remitting MS
- KD consistently demonstrated: normalization of [[gut-microbiome]] composition, reduced pro-inflammatory markers (ALOX5, COX1, COX2), improved body composition (decreased fat mass, increased lean mass), and enhanced clinical outcomes (fatigue, depression, sleep quality, quality of life)
- Microbiome-specific finding (Swidsinski et al. 2017): KD effects were biphasic -- initial short-term reduction in bacterial concentrations and diversity, followed by recovery exceeding baseline values after 23-24 weeks, demonstrating that microbiome alterations in MS are not inherent and can be corrected through dietary intervention
- Adherence rates were notably high (83% in Brenton et al. 2022; 58% maintained strict or liberalized KD 3 months post-trial in Wetmore et al. 2024), and 23% of participants reduced or discontinued medications during KD
- EDSS improvements were observed (2.3 +/- 0.9 to 1.9 +/- 1.1, p < 0.001 in Brenton et al.) along with improved 6-minute walk test and Nine-Hole Peg Test performance

## Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Systematic review following PRISMA 2020 guidelines; searched PubMed, Scopus, EMBASE, Web of Science, Hinari through April 2025
- **Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale scores ranged 6-8/9 (satisfactory to good); main limitations were absence of control groups and short follow-up periods
- **Key mechanisms**: Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) inhibits NLRP3 inflammasome; ketone bodies provide alternative neuronal energy substrates; increased BDNF production; enhanced mitochondrial function; reduced ROS
- **Limitations identified**: Small sample sizes (21-65); heterogeneity in KD protocols (classical 4:1 to modified Atkins); short intervention periods (predominantly 6 months); most studies lacked control groups; focus on RRMS limits generalizability to progressive forms
