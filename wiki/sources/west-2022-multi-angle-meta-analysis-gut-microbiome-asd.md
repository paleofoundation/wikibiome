# Key Claims

- **Differentially abundant taxa** in ASD vs neurotypical children are dependent upon age, sex, and bowel function -- marking these as potential confounders
- **Largest ASD gut microbiome meta-analysis** to date (n=690) compiled 10 publicly available datasets with standardized pre-processing
- **Several taxa** including Bacteroides stercoris, Clostridium M bolteae, Granulicatella elegans, and Massilioclostridium coli showed differential abundance only after removing subjects with bowel dysfunction
- **Adjusting for age, sex, and bowel function** resulted in adding or removing significantly differentially abundant taxa
- Mitigating confounders identified **robust microbial signatures across cohorts**

# Methods & Findings

**Comprehensive meta-analysis** of 10 amplicon and metagenomic sequencing datasets.

- Screened 52 metadata variables in multiple patient subsets
- Standardized pre-processing across all datasets
- Identified age, sex, and bowel function as key confounders in ASD microbiome studies
- Demonstrated that controlling for these confounders changes which taxa appear significant
- Emphasized importance of metadata collection and reporting in ASD microbiome research

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Confounders explain inconsistent findings** across ASD microbiome studies
- **Patient subgroup identification** through careful metadata analysis
- **Methodological standard** for future ASD microbiome research
