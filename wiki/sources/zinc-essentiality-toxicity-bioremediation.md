# Zinc — Essentiality, Toxicity, and Bacterial Bioremediation

## Key Findings
- **Zinc is a cofactor in ~10% of the bacterial proteome**, making it the most widely required transition metal in biology [[zinc-essentiality-toxicity-bioremediation]]
- At excess concentrations, zinc displaces other metal cofactors (mismetallation), inhibits enzymes, and disrupts membrane integrity
- Bacteria deploy zinc-specific importers (ZnuABC), exporters (ZntA, CzcD), and metallochaperones to maintain zinc within a narrow functional window
