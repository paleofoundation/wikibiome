## Key Findings

- **Consistent cross-study pattern**: Shift from *Streptococcus*-dominated (healthy) to Gram-negative anaerobe-enriched (*Prevotella*, *Veillonella*, *Leptotrichia*, *Fusobacterium*) microbiome in GERD and Barrett's esophagus across 11 studies spanning 2015–2025.
- **Mucosal barrier mechanism (Chen et al. 2024)**: Gram-negative bacteria increased 35% in GERD; TLR2 expression elevated 2.1-fold; claudin-1 (tight junction protein) decreased 47%. This provides a direct molecular mechanism: LPS from gram-negative dysbiosis → TLR2-IL-6 activation → claudin-1 degradation → compromised mucosal barrier → perpetuating reflux symptoms even with acid suppression.
- **SIBO-GERD link (Wang et al. 2025, n=394)**: SIBO prevalence significantly higher in GERD patients (P=0.007). *Bacteroides uniformis* (28%) and *B. stercoris* (22%) dominated; ABC transporter-related metabolites upregulated in SIBO-GERD overlap.
- **Barrett's/EAC progression**: Lopetuso et al. (2020, n=26) documented microbial transition from BE to EAC: *Streptococcus* ↓45%, *Prevotella* ↑60%, *Veillonella* ↑52%, *Leptotrichia* ↑48%; *Leptotrichia* identified as key EAC biomarker.
- **PPI effects on infants (Francis et al. 2023)**: PPI-treated infants showed *Firmicutes* increase to 65% vs. *Bifidobacterium* dominance (72%) in controls; PPI duration positively correlated with α-diversity (r=0.42, P=0.01).
- **Pediatric GERD (Ye et al. 2023, n=60 children)**: Proteobacteria and Bacteroidota dominated GERD children's microbiota; 288 differential metabolites by LC-MS, particularly in arachidonic acid metabolism.
- **Oral microbiome**: Martins et al. (2024, n=266) found *Prevotella*, *Haemophilus* dominant in GERD oral samples — consistent with oral-esophageal translocation hypothesis.
- **Quality**: Of 11 studies, 5 low-risk, 4 moderate-risk, 2 high-risk (small samples or limited confounder adjustment).

## Methods

Systematic review following PRISMA 2020 guidelines. Search across PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Embase, Google Scholar (January 2015–October 2025). Eligible studies: human participants with GERD, NERD, BE, or EAC; microbiome assessment by 16S rRNA sequencing, shotgun metagenomics, metabolomics, or molecular culture-independent techniques; healthy controls for comparison. Total: 83 records identified → 67 after duplicates → 18 full-text reviewed → 11 included. Narrative synthesis used due to heterogeneity in study designs, sample types, and outcome measures.

## Detailed Summary

This review is notable for synthesizing across multiple anatomical compartments (esophageal, gastric, intestinal, oral) and disease stages (GERD → NERD → BE → EAC), providing a unified picture of progressive microbiome disruption.

**The Gram-negative shift as central mechanism**: The review converges on the interpretation that GERD involves a fundamental ecological shift from gram-positive aerobes (*Streptococcus*, *Lactobacillus*) to gram-negative anaerobes (*Prevotella*, *Fusobacterium*, *Leptotrichia*). This shift activates innate immune TLR4/TLR2 pathways, IL-6 production, and NF-κB signaling, which simultaneously impairs epithelial tight junctions (claudin-1 degradation) and promotes mucosal inflammation. This creates a cycle: acid reflux → barrier disruption → gram-negative colonization → TLR activation → further barrier compromise.

**SIBO as systemic component**: The GERD-SIBO association expands the disease beyond the upper GI tract. ABC transporter metabolism (lipid/bile acid transport) is altered in GERD+SIBO patients, suggesting systemic metabolic consequences extending to hepatic and cardiovascular risk.

**PPI paradox**: PPI therapy in infants increases *Firmicutes* dominance at the expense of *Bifidobacterium*, potentially programming long-term microbiome composition during the critical first-year window when colonization establishes baseline immune tolerance.

# Examining the Microbiome Composition in Patients with Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease: A Systematic Review

## Key Claims

- Microbial [[dysbiosis]] is central to [[gerd]] pathogenesis, influencing mucosal integrity, [[inflammation]], and symptom persistence across 11 included studies (2015-2025)
- Most studies demonstrated decreased [[streptococcus]] abundance and enrichment of [[prevotella]], [[veillonella]], and [[leptotrichia]] in GERD and [[barretts-esophagus]] patients
- Dysbiosis was consistently associated with altered [[fermentative-metabolism|metabolic pathways]], notably involving [[inflammation|arachidonic acid]], [[glutathione]], and ABC transporter pathways
- [[pharmacomicrobiomics|Proton pump inhibitor]] therapy, [[dysbiosis|small intestinal bacterial overgrowth]], and psychological stress were identified as key modulators of microbial imbalance
- [[mendelian-randomization]] studies supported a genetic causal relationship between [[gut-microbiome]] and GERD risk
- The transition from *Streptococcus*-dominated microbiome to one enriched with Gram-negative species such as *Prevotella* and *Fusobacterium* may promote proinflammatory cytokine-mediated immune response, increasing [[gerd|esophageal inflammation]]

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Systematic review following PRISMA 2020 guidelines
- **Included studies**: 11 peer-reviewed studies (2015-2025) using 16S rRNA sequencing, shotgun metagenomics, or metabolomics
- **Sample types**: Esophageal, gastric, intestinal, oral, and salivary microbiota profiles
- **Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for observational studies; Cochrane RoB 2 for RCTs; STROBE-MR for MR studies; 5 low-risk, 4 moderate-risk, 2 high-risk

 - Ye et al. (2023): Proteobacteria and Bacteroidota dominated GERD; 288 differential metabolites via LC-MS
 - Wang et al. (2025): Positive GERD-SIBO correlation (P=0.007); *Bacteroides uniformis* (28%) and *Bacteroides stercoris* (22%) dominant
 - Chen et al. (2024): Gram-negative bacteria increased 35% in GERD; TLR2 expression elevated 2.1-fold
 - Lopetuso et al. (2020): Microbial transitions from *Streptococcus* to *Prevotella* to *Leptotrichia* across BE to EAC progression
 - Francis et al. (2023): PPI-treated infants showed *Firmicutes* increase to 65% vs *Bifidobacterium* dominance (72%) in controls
- **Gut-esophageal axis**: Changes in gut microbial communities can influence esophageal inflammation through microbial metabolites, immune signaling, and altered bile acid profiles
