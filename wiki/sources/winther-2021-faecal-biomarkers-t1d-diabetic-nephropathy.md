# Key Claims

- **Faecal propionate and butyrate concentrations lower** in T1D subjects vs healthy controls (p=0.04 and p=0.03)
- **Branched SCFA (BCFA) correlated positively** with albuminuria level (isobutyrate p=0.03; isovalerate p=0.005)
- **Macroalbuminuric T1D subjects** exceeded faecal calprotectin threshold more frequently than controls (p=0.02)
- **Fatty acid metabolism altered** among T1D subjects and those with albuminuria vs controls
- **GI dysbiosis** may reflect gastrointestinal imbalances associated with T1D and renal complications

# Methods & Findings

Cross-sectional study: 159 T1D subjects + 50 healthy controls:

- T1D stratified by albuminuria: normo (n=159), micro (n=49), macro (n=50)
- Faecal calprotectin, IAP, SCFA, and immunoglobulins measured
- Christensenella and Subdoligranulum correlated with glycemic control, inflammatory parameters, and SCFA

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Calprotectin (S100A8/A9) is a zinc/calcium-binding protein -- elevated faecal calprotectin reflects zinc-mediated innate immune activation in the gut
- **[[iron]]**: Calprotectin also sequesters iron as antimicrobial defense -- elevated levels indicate [[intestinal-permeability]] and bacterial translocation
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium deficiency may contribute to oxidative renal damage that worsens nephropathy alongside gut [[dysbiosis]]
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: SCFA depletion reflects loss of metal-dependent fermentative bacteria
- **[[intestinal-permeability]]**: Elevated calprotectin directly marks gut barrier dysfunction in T1D
