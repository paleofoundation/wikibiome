# Relationships Between Biological Heavy Metals and Breast Cancer: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## Key Claims

- In all biological specimens, [[copper]] (Cu), [[cadmium]] (Cd), and [[lead]] (Pb) concentrations were **higher** in breast cancer patients than in non-BC controls
- [[zinc]] (Zn) and [[manganese]] (Mn) concentrations were **lower** in breast cancer patients
- No significant differences were found for [[nickel]] (Ni), [[chromium]] (Cr), [[cobalt]] (Co), or [[iron]] (Fe) in plasma/serum between breast cancer and control groups
- Cu, Cd, Pb, Zn, and Mn may lead to [[breast-cancer]] through different molecular modifications
- The transport of heavy metals is regulated by divalent metal transporter 1 (DMT1), which can raise the uptake of Fe, Pb, and Cd in the duodenum

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis registered in PROSPERO (CRD42020176934)
- **Search**: PubMed, Web of Science, and Embase databases through September 2020
- **Included studies**: 36 case-control studies with 4,151 individuals (1,996 cases and 2,155 controls) from five continents
- **Quality assessment**: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) and Critical Appraisal Skills Program (CASP); all studies scored 5+ points
- **Copper findings**: Significantly higher in plasma/serum and tissue of BC patients; SMD (95% CI): 2.44 (1.80, 3.09) in Africa and Europe; Cu is preserved by ATP7A and ATP7B transporters
- **Cadmium findings**: Significantly higher in plasma/serum of BC patients in Asia; SMD (95% CI): 2.55 (1.16, 3.94); Cd mimics estrogen and activates ERa
- **Zinc findings**: Significantly lower in plasma/serum; SMD (95% CI): -2.09 (-3.27, -0.91); also lower in hair in Asia; Zn deficiency disrupts antioxidant defense via Cu/Zn SOD pathway
- **Manganese findings**: Significantly lower in plasma/serum in Asia; SMD (95% CI): -2.95 (-4.26, -1.64); Mn deficiency disrupts MnSOD antioxidant function
- **Lead findings**: Significantly lower in hair; non-significant in plasma/serum; Pb activates ERa and Ras/Raf/MEK/ERK pathway
- **Nickel findings**: Non-significant in plasma/serum and hair; limited number of studies
- **Signaling pathways**: Figure 6 maps Zn (ZIPx/MMP pathway), Cu (copper-binding protein/PLCb/EMT), Cd (P53/integrin/b-catenin/ROS), Mn (MnSOD/SESTRIN), Pb (GPER/ER/PI3K/AKT/mTORC)
- **Regional variation**: Associations varied by geographic region, suggesting ethnic and genetic differences in metal metabolism

## Connections

- Results largely consistent with [[ali-2024-heavy-metals-breast-cancer-review]] narrative review on direction of metal changes
- Cu and Cd elevations support mechanistic evidence in [[tarhonska-2022-cadmium-breast-cancer-mechanisms]]
- Contrasts with [[niehoff-2021-metals-breast-cancer-toenail]] which found no associations using toenail biomarkers (different biomarker type may explain discrepancy)
- Nickel null findings consistent with [[niehoff-2021-metals-breast-cancer-toenail]] and partly with [[aquino-2012-cadmium-nickel-metalloestrogens]]
- Zn and Mn depletion connects to [[oxidative-stress|oxidative stress]] through SOD enzyme disruption
- [[DMT1]] transporter biology relevant to understanding metal uptake and [[metal-homeostasis]]
- Regional variations connect to [[Environmental Exposure]] patterns and genetic polymorphisms in metal transporters
