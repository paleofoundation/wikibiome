## Key Findings

- **Urinary nickel in the highest quartile associates with 3.57-fold increased CVD risk** based on NHANES population data ([[liu-2025-nickel-cardio-metabolic-effects]], expert-opinion).
- **Nickel exposure correlates with metabolic syndrome features**: elevated fasting glucose, hypertension, and dyslipidemia.
- **Mechanism involves oxidative stress** through depletion of superoxide dismutase (SOD) and reduced glutathione — consistent with nickel's pro-oxidant role seen across conditions.
