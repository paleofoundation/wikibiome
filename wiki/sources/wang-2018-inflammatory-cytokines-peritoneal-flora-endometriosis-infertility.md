# Key Claims

- Inflammatory factors IL-6, IL-10, IL-13, and TNF-alpha in peritoneal fluid are significantly higher in endometriosis patients with infertility compared to controls
- Peritoneal fluid bacteria were mainly *Proteobacteria* and *Firmicutes* in both endometriosis and control groups, with no significant difference in bacterial levels
- Logistic regression showed IL-6 (OR=4.260), IL-10 (OR=5.736), IL-13 (OR=4.261), and TNF-alpha (OR=8.346) significantly correlated with endometriosis-related infertility
- TNF-alpha had the highest diagnostic AUC (0.903) followed by IL-6 (0.873) for predicting endometriosis with infertility
- White blood cell counts (monocytes, neutrophils, eosinophils, basophils) were significantly increased in peritoneal fluid of endometriosis patients

# Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: Observational study, Beijing/Shandong hospitals
- **Participants**: 55 endometriosis patients with infertility and 30 non-endometriosis controls with infertility
- **Methods**: Peritoneal fluid bacterial DNA extraction, Ion Torrent PGM sequencing, ELISA for cytokines

 - IL-6: 48.15 vs 18.14 pg/mL (p<0.05); TNF-alpha: 87.29 vs 37.06 pg/mL (p<0.05)
 - Peritoneal fluid dominated by Proteobacteria, Actinobacteria, Bacteroidetes, Fusobacteria, Tenericutes
 - No significant bacterial differences between groups
 - ROC analysis: TNF-alpha AUC=0.903; IL-6 AUC=0.873; IL-10 AUC=0.685; IL-13 AUC=0.661

# Relevance to Wiki

While this study found no significant differences in peritoneal fluid bacterial composition between [[endometriosis]] and controls, the dramatically elevated inflammatory cytokines support the immune-inflammatory pathogenesis of [[endometriosis]]. The elevated TNF-alpha and IL-6 are consistent with the [[bacterial-contamination-hypothesis]] where LPS-driven TLR4 activation promotes cytokine production. The inflammatory milieu may influence [[iron]] metabolism and contribute to [[oxidative-stress]] in the peritoneal cavity.
