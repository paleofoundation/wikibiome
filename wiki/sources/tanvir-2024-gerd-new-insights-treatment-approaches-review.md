# Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease: New Insights and Treatment Approaches

## Key Claims

- [[GERD]] pathophysiology is complex and multifactorial, involving LES dysfunction, transient lower esophageal sphincter relaxations (TLESRs), esophageal motility disorders, [[hiatal hernia]], and impaired mucosal barrier function
- Up to 70% of patients with typical GERD symptoms have normal endoscopic findings, highlighting the diagnostic challenge and need for advanced techniques
- The microbiome has emerged as a potential target in GERD pathogenesis and as a therapeutic target, connecting [[dysbiosis]] to disease development
- Novel treatment strategies include potassium-competitive acid blockers, endoscopic therapies, and minimally invasive surgical techniques beyond traditional PPIs
- Ineffective esophageal motility (IEM) is prevalent in up to 50% of GERD patients, including fragmented peristalsis and absent contractility
- Non-acid reflux and mixed reflux events contribute significantly to symptom generation, particularly in PPI-refractory patients, underscoring the importance of impedance-pH monitoring
- Annual direct and indirect costs of GERD in the US exceed $9 billion, with 73% of patients reporting impaired sleep and 65% impaired work productivity

## Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Narrative review
- **Scope**: Pathophysiology, diagnosis, clinical presentation, complications, and treatment of GERD
- **Diagnostic tools reviewed**: High-resolution manometry (HRM), combined multichannel intraluminal impedance-pH monitoring (MII-pH), wireless pH capsule (Bravo), mucosal impedance testing, functional lumen imaging probe (FLIP)
- **Complications**: Erosive esophagitis (30-70% prevalence, LA grades A-D), Barrett's esophagus (8th most common malignancy), peptic strictures, esophageal adenocarcinoma
- **Treatment landscape**: Lifestyle modifications, PPIs, H2 receptor antagonists, potassium-competitive acid blockers, mucosal protectors, endoscopic techniques, anti-reflux surgery (fundoplication, magnetic sphincter augmentation)
