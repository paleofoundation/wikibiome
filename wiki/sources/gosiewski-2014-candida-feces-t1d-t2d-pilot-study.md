# Key Claims

- **Candida spp. populations larger in both T1DM and T2DM** vs controls (p=0.017 and p=0.037)
- **No difference found between T1DM and T2DM** in Candida quantity
- **No association between fungal quantity and clinical/biochemical features** except negative correlation with blood lipids in T2DM
- **Candida fungi more prevalent** in feces of diabetic patients -- may be useful for developing therapeutic measures

# Methods & Findings

Pilot study: 27 T1DM + 17 T2DM + 17 healthy controls:

- DNA isolated from fecal and blood samples
- qPCR applied to determine fungal cell numbers
- Statistical associations with clinical and biochemical features examined

# Relevance to Metallomic Framework

- **[[zinc]]**: Zinc-dependent antimicrobial peptides (defensins) normally control Candida -- deficiency permits expansion
- **[[iron]]**: Candida is a highly efficient iron scavenger -- diabetic iron dysregulation creates permissive environment
- **[[selenium]]**: Selenium-dependent neutrophil killing of Candida may be impaired in diabetes
- **[[gut-metal-microbiome]]**: Candida-metal interactions are central to understanding fungal [[dysbiosis]] in diabetes
- **[[probiotics]]**: Probiotic strains competing with Candida for metal resources could reduce fungal burden
