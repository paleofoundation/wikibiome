# Key Findings

- **Sex-specific fungal exposure signature**: C. albicans seropositivity conferred **increased odds of schizophrenia in males** (OR range 2.04-9.53, P<=0.0001), but not in females. This is one of the clearest demonstrations of sex-dimorphic fungal-psychiatric risk in a well-powered cohort (n=947 total across two cohorts).
- **Cognitive deficits in seropositive females**: C. albicans IgG-positive females with schizophrenia showed significantly lower scores on RBANS immediate memory (t=2.29, P=0.01) and delayed memory (t=3.27, P=0.0007-0.001) modules. Similar deficits seen in BPD females (P=0.007-0.033). Males showed no cognitive differences.
- **Antipsychotic medications do NOT reduce C. albicans IgG levels** in either sex, implying the fungal exposure signal is **not a consequence of treatment** and may be a persistent etiological factor. GI disturbances were significantly associated with elevated C. albicans IgG in male SCZ patients (P=0.009) and female BPD patients (P=0.02).

# Methods

Case-control serological analysis of two cohorts. Cohort 1: 261 SCZ + 270 BPD + 277 non-psychiatric controls (total n=808). Cohort 2: 139 first-episode schizophrenia (FES) patients with 78 antipsychotic-naive. C. albicans IgG and S. cerevisiae IgG quantified via ELISA with dichotomous cutoffs (90% female/male control values) and continuous values. Multinomial logistic regression adjusted for antipsychotic medication, homelessness, socioeconomic status. RBANS (Repeatable Battery for the Assessment of Neuropsychological Status) Form A for cognition.

# Relevance to Cureva

- **Schizophrenia signature page** Layer 2 (taxonomic) + Layer 3 (immune): Candida albicans seropositivity as a diagnostic and **sex-stratified** biomarker. Confidence: high — large sample (n=947), two independent cohorts, replicated across FES and chronic SCZ.
- **Intervention triangle** for antifungal strategies in SCZ: I -> f (antifungals reduce Candida); f -> D (seropositivity correlates with SCZ diagnosis); I -> D (needs RCT — authors specifically call for trials of yeast exposure as a **modifiable risk factor**).
- **Sex-stratified treatment**: cognitive function interventions in female SCZ/BPD should consider Candida burden; risk reduction in males should target the seropositivity signal.
- **STOP candidate**: the paper shows antipsychotics do NOT reduce C. albicans IgG — implying that antipsychotic monotherapy is insufficient when there is active fungal exposure, and adjunctive antifungal strategies may be warranted.
