# Key Claims

- A catered high-fiber (14g/1000 kcal), low-fat (20% calories) diet (Mi-IBD diet) is well-tolerated by CD patients including those with fibrostenotic disease, contrary to clinical dogma
- Catering the diet achieved high adherence (96%) and marked dietary changes (increased fiber, decreased fat), while counseling alone was insufficient
- CD symptoms improved (PRO2 and sCDAI scores) in the catered diet group
- Proteomic analysis revealed higher baseline serum amyloid A (SAA) and CRP in CD patients vs healthy controls; these decreased with the catered diet
- Fecal metabolomics showed improved markers of protein and energy metabolism as well as markers of oxidative stress and [[inflammation]]

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Longitudinal patient-preference clinical trial (NCT04213729); 73 CD patients in 3 groups
- **Groups**: Group 1 (n=23, counseling only), Group 2 (n=26, catered Mi-IBD diet), Group 3 (n=24, catered diet + healthy household control dyads)
- **Duration**: 8 weeks of dietary intervention
- **Key dietary change**: CD patients had low-fiber, high-fat diets at baseline; catering successfully shifted intake (all p<0.001)
- **Symptom improvement**: Group 3 showed improvements in PRO2 and sCDAI scores
- **Biomarkers**: SomaScan Discovery v4.1 proteomic assays; stool metabolomics analyzed
