# Differential Distribution of Bacteria Between Cancerous and Noncancerous Ovarian Tissues

## Key Claims

- Bacterial LPS was present in both ovarian cancer and noncancerous tissue, confirming bacteria exist in ovarian tissues
- At the phylum level, cancerous ovarian bacteria showed increased Aquificae and Planctomycetes and decreased Crenarchaeota compared to controls
- Genes associated with pathogenicity and oxidative stress tolerance were enriched in ovarian cancer tissues
- Forty-six significantly different KEGG pathways existed between cancer and control groups
- Challenges the longstanding assumption that the upper female reproductive tract is sterile

## Methods/Findings

- **Study design**: 16S rRNA sequencing of ovarian tissue from 6 ovarian cancer patients and 10 controls with noncancerous conditions
- **Methods**: Immunohistochemistry for LPS antibody staining; 16S rRNA V3-V4 sequencing on Illumina MiSeq; BugBase and PICRUSt for functional prediction
- **Key phyla**: Increased Aquificae and Planctomycetes in cancer; decreased Crenarchaeota
- **Functional analysis**: Pathogenicity and oxidative stress tolerance genes enriched in cancer group
