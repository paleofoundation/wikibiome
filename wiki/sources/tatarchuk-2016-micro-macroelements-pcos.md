# Key Claims

- PCOS women (with and without pelvic inflammatory disease) had **increased levels of nickel, zinc, and manganese** compared to healthy controls
- **Nickel** levels were notably elevated: Group I (PCOS only) median 0.046 g/l vs Group III (healthy controls) median 0.014 g/l (p < 0.05)
- **Manganese** was significantly elevated in PCOS: Group I median 0.0098 g/l vs Group III 0.0056 g/l (significant difference)
- **Copper** levels showed no significant differences across groups
- **Zinc** levels were increased in PCOS women with and without PID (Group I median 0.659 g/l, Group II median 0.648 g/l) compared to controls (median 0.442 g/l, p < 0.05)
- No significant differences in magnesium, chromium, selenium, or vanadium across groups
- Nickel at elevated concentrations can damage cellular membranes, increase lipid peroxidation, cause vasoconstriction, disrupt DNA/RNA metabolism, and induce acute/chronic inflammation
- Manganese excess can damage cellular membranes, alter mitochondrial function, and disrupt energy metabolism
- The authors recommend dietary intake of manganese-rich and nickel-containing foods be monitored, and that complex multivitamin preparations be considered for PCOS women with inflammatory diseases

# Methods/Findings

- **Design**: Cross-sectional study with 3 groups
- **Participants**: Group I -- 30 women with PCOS without PID; Group II -- 22 patients with PCOS and PID; Group III -- 25 healthy women
- **Elements measured**: Magnesium (Mg), copper (Cu), manganese (Mn), nickel (Ni), zinc (Zn), chromium (Cr), selenium (Se), vanadium (V) in serum
- **Method**: Microwave-based mineralization with inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry (ICP-MS), performed at accredited laboratory
- **Statistical analysis**: Software Statistica 6.0; Median, 95th percentile, mean values; Mann-Whitney U test; Kolmogorov-Smirnov test
- **Key quantitative results** (medians, g/l):
  - Nickel: I = 0.046, II = 0.032, III = 0.014 (I vs III p < 0.05; II vs III p < 0.05)
  - Manganese: I = 0.0098, II = 0.0094, III = 0.0056
  - Zinc: I = 0.659, II = 0.648, III = 0.442 (I vs III p < 0.05)
- **Limitations**: Relatively small sample sizes; Ukrainian population; serum only (no urine or whole blood); published in Ukrainian journal with English abstract
