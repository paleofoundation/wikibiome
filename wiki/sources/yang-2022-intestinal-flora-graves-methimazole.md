# Preliminary Observation of the Changes in the Intestinal Flora of Patients With Graves' Disease Before and After Methimazole Treatment

## Key Findings

- **Bifidobacterium abundance is positively correlated with thyroid autoantibodies**: TRAb (r=0.588, P=0.002), TGAb (r=0.463, P=0.023), and TPOAb (r=0.578, P=0.002), suggesting Bifidobacterium may be involved in the autoimmune process of GD ([[yang-2022-intestinal-flora-graves-methimazole]], cross-sectional, n=39)
- **Collinsella and Bifidobacterium were significantly higher in untreated GD patients** compared to both healthy controls and post-methimazole patients, while Dialister and Roseburia were significantly reduced ([[yang-2022-intestinal-flora-graves-methimazole]], cross-sectional, n=39)
- **Post-methimazole treatment significantly improved gut microbiota diversity**: observed OTUs increased (P=0.002), Shannon index improved (P=0.016), indicating the relationship between thyroid function and microbiome composition is bidirectional ([[yang-2022-intestinal-flora-graves-methimazole]], cross-sectional, n=39)
- **Synergistetes was strongly negatively correlated with all three thyroid autoantibodies**: TRAb (R=-0.702), TGAb (R=-0.624), TPOAb (R=-0.711), suggesting a potential protective role ([[yang-2022-intestinal-flora-graves-methimazole]], cross-sectional, n=39)
- **Lactobacillus was positively correlated with TRAb (R=0.489) and TPOAb (R=0.607)**, paralleling the Bifidobacterium pattern and contradicting the common assumption that Lactobacillus is uniformly beneficial ([[yang-2022-intestinal-flora-graves-methimazole]], cross-sectional, n=39)

## Methods

- **Study type**: Observational cohort with pre/post methimazole treatment comparison (cross-sectional design with three groups)
- **Population**: Shanghai Fifth People's Hospital; 18 newly diagnosed untreated GD patients (UGD), 10 GD patients post-methimazole treatment with normal thyroid function (TGD), 11 healthy controls (NC)
- **Inclusion criteria**: UGD — newly diagnosed, never treated, no other autoimmune diseases; TGD — receiving methimazole ≥6 months with normal thyroid function; NC — age/sex matched, no thyroid or autoimmune disease
- **Exclusion criteria**: Other GD/non-GD related treatments, antibiotics/probiotics/prebiotics in past month, history of GI surgery, acute GI infection, pregnancy, lactation, GI disorders
- **Sequencing**: 16S rRNA gene V3-V4 region; Illumina platform; Greengenes database for OTU classification at 97% sequence similarity
- **Total sequences**: 1,562,445 high-quality sequences (NC: 477,940; UGD: 722,725; TGD: 361,780); ~40,063 +/- 6,355 valid sequences per sample
- **Statistical analysis**: SPSS 22.0; Kruskal-Wallis H test for alpha diversity; NMDS with unweighted UniFrac; Spearman rank test for correlations; Bonferroni correction (P<0.017 for pairwise comparisons); MetaStats for differential abundance

## Detailed Summary

**Clinical features (Table 1).** TSH was significantly lower in UGD (0.005 mIU/L) vs. NC (1.890 mIU/L) (P<0.017). FT3 and FT4 were significantly elevated in UGD (FT3: 30.80 pmol/L vs. 5.00; FT4: 77.40 pmol/L vs. 16.90) (P<0.017). TRAb in UGD: 17.82 IU/L (5.27-29). TGAb in UGD: 464.9 IU/L (10.1-4000). TPOAb in UGD: 107.2 IU/L (13.5-6000). After methimazole treatment, TSH normalized (3.670 mIU/L) and FT3/FT4 returned to reference range, though TGD values for FT3 (5.05) and FT4 (14.90) were still within but at the upper end of normal. No significant difference in sex, age, or BMI among the three groups.

**Diversity analysis.** Alpha diversity (Chao1, ACE, Shannon, Simpson) showed no statistically significant difference among the three groups (P>0.05), though UGD trends showed reduced diversity. Rarefaction curves plateaued, confirming adequate sequencing depth. NMDS with unweighted UniFrac separated the three groups: NC in upper left, UGD in lower left, TGD in upper right, with some overlap. Venn analysis: 2,177 OTUs shared among all groups; NC had 409 unique OTUs, UGD had 525, TGD had 191.

**Phylum-level changes.** In UGD vs. NC: Actinobacteria, Cyanobacteria, TM7 significantly increased (P<0.017); Firmicutes and [Thermi] significantly decreased (P<0.017). In TGD vs. NC: [Thermi] and TM7 significantly different. In UGD vs. TGD: Actinobacteria significantly higher in UGD (P<0.017); Proteobacteria and TM7 showed gradual increasing trends from NC to UGD to TGD.

**Genus-level changes.** UGD vs. NC: Collinsella and Bifidobacterium significantly higher; Parabacteroides, Butyricimonas, Anaerostipes, Roseburia, Dialister, Phoscolarctobacterium, and Desulfovibrio significantly lower. UGD vs. TGD: Ruminococcus, Streptococcus, Blautia decreased in TGD; Phascolarctobacterium increased in TGD. LEfSe analysis (LDA >2): Bifidobacteriaceae, Clostridiaceae, and Carnobacteriaceae were predominant in UGD; Porphyromonadaceae, Rikenellaceae, Paraprevotellaceae, Veillonellaceae, and Desulfovibrionaceae were predominant in NC.

**Correlation analysis (Spearman).** Bifidobacterium: positively correlated with TRAb (r=0.588, P=0.002), TGAb (r=0.463, P=0.023), TPOAb (r=0.578, P=0.002); not correlated with FT3, FT4, or TSH. Synergistetes: negatively correlated with TRAb (R=-0.702), TGAb (R=-0.624), TPOAb (R=-0.711). Lactobacillus: positively correlated with TRAb (R=0.489) and TPOAb (R=0.607). Phascolarctobacterium: no significant correlations reported.

**Roseburia significance.** Roseburia was significantly reduced in untreated GD and slightly increased after methimazole. In UC, butyrate-producing Roseburia is also decreased. Roseburia produces acetic acid, butyric acid, and other SCFAs, which induce Treg production, enhance IL-10, and strengthen the intestinal mucosal barrier. Reduced Roseburia in GD may contribute to Th17/Treg imbalance and reduced anti-inflammatory signaling.

- Synergistetes-enhancing interventions could be explored for GD immunomodulation

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Bifidobacterium is traditionally considered a beneficial probiotic, but its positive correlation with TRAb (r=0.588) and TGAb in GD patients challenges this; aligns with [[chen-2024-gut-microbiota-six-thyroid-diseases-mr]] MR findings but contradicts [[han-2022-berberine-methimazole-graves-microbiome]] where Bifidobacterium longum improved GD outcomes
- Lactobacillus positively correlated with TRAb and TPOAb — another traditionally "beneficial" genus showing unexpected associations in GD
- Small sample size (n=39 total, smallest group n=10) limits generalizability; alpha diversity differences did not reach significance despite visible trends
- Cross-sectional design cannot determine causality — does dysbiosis cause GD, or does GD cause dysbiosis? The post-treatment improvement suggests bidirectional causation

## Open Questions

- Is the Bifidobacterium-TRAb correlation causal or a consequence of the altered immune environment in GD?
- Which specific Bifidobacterium species drive the positive antibody correlation — is it species-specific?
- Would Synergistetes-enriching interventions reduce thyroid autoantibody levels in early GD?
- Does the post-methimazole microbiome recovery reflect direct drug effects on the microbiome or indirect effects via thyroid function normalization?
- Can microbiome profiling at GD diagnosis predict treatment response or relapse risk?
