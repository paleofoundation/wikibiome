# Synbiotic as an Ameliorating Factor in the Health-Related Quality of Life in Women with Polycystic Ovary Syndrome

## Key Findings

- **12-week synbiotic supplementation significantly improved PCOSQ-26 scores** across four of five domains via ANCOVA analysis adjusted for baseline value, age, marital status, PCOS duration, and changes in BMI/WC/HC: emotional (P = 0.044), body hair (P = 0.016), weight (P = 0.033), and infertility (P = 0.027). Menstrual problems domain was not significantly different (P = 0.389) ([[hariri-2024-synbiotic-pcos-quality-of-life-rct]], RCT, n=52).
- **SF-12 physical component score** was significantly enhanced in the synbiotic group vs. placebo (P = 0.035 between groups). Mental component score showed no significant inter-group difference (P = 0.224) ([[hariri-2024-synbiotic-pcos-quality-of-life-rct]], RCT, n=52).
- **Synbiotic formulation:** 2-gram daily sachets containing *Bacillus coagulans* GBI-30 (10^9 colony-forming units), *Lactobacillus rhamnosus*, *Lactobacillus helveticus*, *Lactobacillus acidophilus*, and 0.75 g fructooligosaccharide prebiotic with 0.75 g natural orange flavoring ([[hariri-2024-synbiotic-pcos-quality-of-life-rct]], RCT).
- **No significant improvement in perceived stress** (PSS-10 total score) after 12 weeks — neither within-group nor between-group changes were significant ([[hariri-2024-synbiotic-pcos-quality-of-life-rct]], RCT, n=52).
- **No significant anthropometric changes** (weight, BMI, waist circumference, hip circumference) in either group pre-to-post or between groups ([[hariri-2024-synbiotic-pcos-quality-of-life-rct]], RCT, n=52).

## Methods

- **Study design:** Randomized, triple-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial. Registered at IRCT (IRCT20211108053007N1; date: 14/02/2023). Conducted February to May 2023.
- **Population:** 56 women with newly diagnosed PCOS (Rotterdam criteria), aged 18-45, BMI 18.5-35, recruited from obstetrics/gynecology clinic of Ayatollah Taleghani Hospital and Shahid Beheshti University of Medical Sciences, Tehran, Iran. Participants assigned to intervention were identified by the main researcher.
- **Exclusion criteria:** Antibiotics or probiotics/synbiotics in past month, pregnancy, heart/kidney/liver disease, hypothyroidism/hyperthyroidism, malignancy, hyperprolactinemia, infectious or inflammatory disease, weight loss diet, special diet.
- **Randomization:** Block randomization. Sachets were identical in packaging, texture, taste, and color, labeled A and B by the researchers. Triple-blind (participant, investigator, statistician).
- **Intervention:** Daily 2-gram synbiotic or placebo sachet for 12 weeks. Placebo contained 0.75 g fructooligosaccharide and 0.75 g natural orange flavoring (matching the prebiotic component but without live bacteria). Participants advised not to change diet or exercise.
- **Outcomes measured:** PCOSQ-26 (26-item PCOS-specific quality of life questionnaire with 5 domains: emotional, body hair, weight, infertility, menstrual problems; 7-point Likert scale, scores converted to 0-100), SF-12 (12-item Short-Form Health Survey with physical and mental component scores), PSS-10 (Perceived Stress Scale, 10 items).
- **Sample size calculation:** Based on assumed score difference of 9 in emotional domain of PCOSQ-26, alpha = 0.05, type two error beta = 0.20, power = 80%, yielding n = 28 per group (including 10% dropout).
- **Statistics:** Paired t-tests (within-group), independent t-tests (between-group), ANCOVA (adjusted for confounders). SPSS version 26. Cronbach's alpha calculated for internal consistency (PCOSQ-26 pre: 0.857, post: 0.906 in synbiotic group; PSS-10 pre: 0.828/0.889, post: 0.847/0.894).
- **Completion rate:** 52 of 56 completed (93%); 4 dropouts in placebo group (1 changed drug type, 3 withdrew).

## Detailed Summary

This triple-blind RCT from Tehran assessed whether synbiotic supplementation could improve quality of life in PCOS women, reasoning that gut microbiome modulation may influence the hormonal, metabolic, and psychological disturbances underlying PCOS symptoms.

**PCOSQ-26 results (Table 3):** Within the synbiotic group, significant improvements were seen in emotional (P = 0.029), body hair (P = 0.016), weight (P = 0.016), and infertility (P = 0.023) domains. Between-group ANCOVA (adjusted) showed significance for emotional (P = 0.044), body hair (P = 0.016), weight (P = 0.033), and infertility (P = 0.027). Menstrual problems showed within-group improvement in the synbiotic group (trending P = 0.164) but no between-group significance (P = 0.389). Notably, the control group also showed significant within-group improvement in body hair (P = 0.014) and weight (P = 0.014) domains, suggesting some placebo effect.

**SF-12 results (Table 4):** Physical score increased in synbiotic group (66.75 to 68.95, change +2.19, P = 0.291 within; P = 0.035 ANCOVA between groups after adjustment). Mental score showed no significant change. Internal consistency was good (pre: 0.806/0.903, post: 0.882/0.940).

**PSS-10 results (Table 5):** Total stress scores showed no significant change in either group. Individual PSS-10 items showed no between-group differences for any of the 10 items. Cronbach's alpha was adequate.

**Anthropometric results (Table 2):** No significant changes in weight, BMI, waist circumference, or hip circumference — the synbiotic did not produce measurable body composition changes at 12 weeks.

**Mechanism discussion:** The authors reason that altered gut microbiota in PCOS reduces SCFA production, endangers gut barrier integrity, triggers systemic inflammation and insulin resistance via endotoxemia. SCFAs function as gut-brain axis mediators and regulate androgen secretion. By restoring gut microbial balance, synbiotics may address multiple symptom pathways simultaneously. The *Bacillus coagulans* GBI-30 component survives gastric acid (spore-forming), while *Lactobacillus* strains provide direct mucosal effects.

**Limitations identified:** Type and severity of PCOS not controlled; physical activity not measured as confounding factor; participants' life change events during 12-week period not captured; relatively small sample size.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- **No metabolic or hormonal endpoints measured:** This is a QoL-only study. Without measuring fasting glucose, insulin, HOMA-IR, testosterone, LH/FSH, or gut microbiome composition, the mechanistic pathway remains assumed rather than demonstrated.
- **Placebo also contained prebiotic:** Both groups received 0.75 g fructooligosaccharide, meaning the placebo was not inert — it was a prebiotic-only control. Any prebiotic-mediated effects would be shared across groups, potentially underestimating the synbiotic benefit.
- **No stress improvement:** The null PSS-10 result contradicts the hypothesis that gut-brain axis modulation would improve perceived stress in PCOS. This may reflect insufficient intervention duration, inadequate microbial colonization, or the possibility that PCOS-related stress is primarily psychosocial rather than physiological.
- **Small sample size (n=52 analyzed):** Power was calculated for the emotional domain (primary outcome) but may be underpowered for secondary outcomes including menstrual problems and stress measures.

## Open Questions

1. Which specific microbial changes (if any) occurred during the 12-week intervention — was the synbiotic actually altering gut microbiome composition?
2. Does synbiotic supplementation affect androgen levels, insulin resistance, or inflammatory markers in PCOS?
3. Would a longer intervention period (6-12 months) produce improvements in menstrual regularity and perceived stress?
4. How does this synbiotic compare to the specific probiotic strains shown to modulate hormones in PCOS (e.g., *Bifidobacterium lactis* V9 for sex hormone regulation)?
5. Would combining this synbiotic with metformin produce additive benefits on both metabolic and QoL endpoints?
