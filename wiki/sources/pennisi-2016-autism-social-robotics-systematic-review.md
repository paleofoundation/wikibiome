# Key Claims

- **ASD subjects often performed better with a robot partner** than a human partner in social interaction tasks
- **ASD patients showed social behaviors toward robots** that TD patients had toward human agents
- **Reduced repetitive and stereotyped behaviors** observed during robotic sessions
- **Social robots improve spontaneous language** during therapy sessions
- Robots provide therapists and researchers a means to **connect with autistic subjects** in an easier way

# Methods & Findings

**Systematic review** of studies on social robotics in ASD therapy published in the last 10 years.

- Followed PRISMA guidelines; protocol registered within PROSPERO (CRD42015016158)
- Found many positive implications for social robot use in ASD therapy
- ASD subjects showed better engagement with robots than with human therapists
- Robot design features (physical appearance, functionality, autonomy) need optimization for ASD
- Studies are still insufficient in area; need to clarify effects of sex, IQ, and age on outcomes
- Questions remain about whether benefits transfer outside clinical/experimental context

# Relevance to ASD Framework

- **Technology-based intervention** for core social communication deficits in ASD
- **Robot preference** in ASD may relate to reduced social anxiety and predictable interaction patterns
- **Complementary to biological interventions** targeting gut-brain-immune axis
