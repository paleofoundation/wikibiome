# Overview

Pilot randomized double-blind crossover trial (NCT04209075) examining whether a **prebiotic fiber supplement** (BiomeBliss -- inulin, beta-glucan, and polyphenols from blueberry pomace) could reduce metformin-induced GI side effects in **6 youth** (aged 17.2 +/- 1.7 years) with youth-onset type 2 diabetes (Y-T2DM).

# Key Findings

## GI Symptoms

- No differences in stool frequency, stool consistency, or composite GI symptom scores between metformin/placebo and metformin/prebiotic groups at 1 week
- No carryover effect; no difference between Phase 1 and Phase 2 outcomes
- Study provides **proof-of-concept** for feasibility of exploring prebiotic-metformin-microbiome interactions

## Microbiome Changes

- Principal coordinate analyses revealed overall trends for **shifts in beta diversity** during both Phase 1 and Phase 2
- Distinct shifts in microbiome signatures associated with metformin/prebiotic intervention at 1 week and 1 month
- After 1 week (Phase 1): *Proteobacteria*, *Enterobacteriaceae*, and *Enterobacteriales* identified as candidate biomarkers of metformin effects

## Metabolic Outcomes

- No changes in glycemia (fructosamine or glucose AUC), markers of inflammation (hsCRP/ESR), or lipid panel
- Trend toward lower mean average glucose with Phase 1 metformin/prebiotic supplementation (not significant)
- HbA1c: 6.4 +/- 0.6% at baseline; BMI: 42 +/- 9 kg/m2

## Rationale for ASD Relevance

- Metformin affects gut microbiota composition; GI side effects (bloating, diarrhea, nausea) are common barriers to adherence
- Prebiotics with polyphenols would promote SCFA-producing bacteria and limit overgrowth of metformin-induced *Escherichia* spp
- Proof-of-concept for prebiotic adjunct to metformin therapy relevant to metabolic comorbidities in ASD

## Limitations

- Very small sample size (n=6) due to COVID-19 enrollment disruption
- Short intervention period (1 week crossover + 1 month extension)
- Cannot generalize to broader populations
