# Key Claims

- **12 bacterial taxa** identified with causal associations to [[postpartum-depression]] risk
- **Protective taxa**: *Veillonellaceae*, *Ruminococcaceae UCG 011*, *Bifidobacterium adolescentis*, *Paraprevotella clara*, *Clostridium leptum*, *Eubacterium siraeum*, *Coprococcus catus* (inversely associated with PPD)
- **Risk-increasing taxa**: *Alphaproteobacteria*, *Roseburia*, *FamilyXIIIAD3011group*, *Alistipes onderdonkii*, *Bilophila wadsworthia* (positively associated with PPD)
- MiBioGen consortium, DMP, and FinnGen data used; primarily European populations

# Methods & Findings

- **Design**: Two-sample MR using GWAS summary-level data from MiBioGen and Dutch Microbiome Project; PPD data from FinnGen (57,604 cases, 596,601 controls)
- **Primary method**: IVW as cornerstone; sensitivity analyses via MR-Egger, weighted median, weighted mode
- **Key implication**: Causal evidence supports combined microbial interventions and biotherapeutics for PPD treatment

# Connections

- Largest PPD case-control dataset among MR studies; confirms causal [[dysbiosis]]-PPD link
- Protective role of *Bifidobacterium* consistent with [[zhang-2024-gut-microbiota-ppd-mendelian-randomization]] and [[cui-2024-gut-microbiota-blood-metabolites-ppd-mr]]
