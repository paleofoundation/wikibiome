# Key Claims

- AminoIndex Cancer Screening (AICS) uses multiple plasma [[tryptophan|amino acid]] concentrations to calculate cancer risk scores and may detect early-stage [[colorectal-cancer]] with high sensitivity
- For AICS Rank C (colorectal cancer), sensitivities are 55% for Stage 0 (carcinoma in situ), 30% for Stage I, 43% for Stage II, 42% for Stage III, and 62% for Stage IV
- AICS has a specificity of 95% and sensitivity of 41% for colorectal cancer, with an estimated positive predictive value of 0.67%
- This is the first reported case in which a curable cancer was detected using AICS: a 62-year-old Japanese man with ascending colon carcinoma in situ
- High plasma isoleucine is a prominent characteristic of patients with colorectal cancer
- Six amino acids (serine, proline, valine, methionine, isoleucine, lysine) are used in the colorectal cancer AICS score calculation

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Case report
- **Patient**: 62-year-old Japanese male with family history of colorectal cancer; fecal occult blood test negative; AICS flagged Rank C for colorectal cancer (score 8.3)
- **Colonoscopy**: Revealed a 10-mm adenoma-like lesion in ascending colon; biopsy confirmed carcinoma in situ (carcinoma in adenoma)
- **Treatment**: Endoscopic mucosal resection; complete resection confirmed; no recurrence at 6-month follow-up colonoscopy
- **AICS methodology**: 5 mL fasting blood; 19 amino acids measured by HPLC-ESI-MS; scores range 0-10; Rank C (>=8.0) indicates approximately 10-fold cancer risk
- **Key advantage**: AICS may detect proximal colon cancers that fecal occult blood testing misses (FOBT sensitivity for ascending colon tumors is only 11.2%)
- **Limitation**: Studies performed only in Japanese subjects; applicability to other populations untested
