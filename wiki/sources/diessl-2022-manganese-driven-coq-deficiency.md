# Manganese-Driven CoQ Deficiency

## Key Findings

- **Manganese destroys CoQ biosynthesis**: Manganese overexposure selectively disrupts **coenzyme Q (ubiquinone) biosynthesis** — a critical component of the mitochondrial electron transport chain — causing mitochondrial dysfunction and cell death [[diessl-2022-manganese-driven-coq-deficiency]], in-vitro.
- **Cross-species conservation**: This toxicity mechanism is conserved across yeast, bacteria, and human cells — indicating a fundamental vulnerability to manganese excess.
- **Connects to neurodegeneration**: Manganese toxicity (manganism) causes Parkinson's-like symptoms. CoQ deficiency in mitochondria provides a mechanistic link between manganese exposure and dopaminergic neuronal death.
