# Siderophore-Mediated Zinc Acquisition Enhances Enterobacterial Colonization of the Inflamed Gut

## Key Findings

- **Yersiniabactin is a zincophore, not just a siderophore**: The metallophore yersiniabactin (Ybt), classically known as an iron-chelating siderophore, **also acquires zinc** under zinc-limited conditions. This dual metal-acquisition function enables Enterobacteriaceae to resist host [[calprotectin]]-mediated zinc sequestration [[behnsen-2021-siderophore-zinc-acquisition-enterobacteria-inflamed-gut]], animal-model.

- **pH-dependent metal switching**: Yersiniabactin's relative affinity for iron vs. zinc changes with pH — at higher pH (characteristic of the inflamed gut), zinc binding increases relative to iron. The local environment determines which metal the metallophore captures [[behnsen-2021-siderophore-zinc-acquisition-enterobacteria-inflamed-gut]].

- **E. coli Nissle 1917 probiotic mechanism**: The probiotic *E. coli* Nissle (EcN) uses yersiniabactin to acquire zinc even when calprotectin is actively sequestering it. When conventional zinc transporters (ZnuABC, ZupT) are deleted, EcN still grows in zinc-limited media because of Ybt-mediated zinc import. This partly explains **why EcN is an effective probiotic** — it outcompetes pathogens for zinc in the inflamed gut [[behnsen-2021-siderophore-zinc-acquisition-enterobacteria-inflamed-gut]].

- **Colonization advantage in inflamed gut**: In mouse colitis models, yersiniabactin-producing strains had a significant colonization advantage over non-producers, specifically under calprotectin-mediated zinc restriction. The siderophore/zincophore enables gut colonization during inflammation [[behnsen-2021-siderophore-zinc-acquisition-enterobacteria-inflamed-gut]].

## Why This Is a Keystone Paper

1. **Cross-Domain Bridge**: Connects siderophore chemistry (metallomics) to probiotic mechanism (microbiology) to gut inflammation (immunology) — three fields that don't usually intersect.
2. **Signature Layer Dependency**: The zinc layer of every Enterobacteriaceae-enriched disease signature now must account for siderophore-mediated zinc acquisition, not just iron.
3. **Cross-Condition Pattern Enabler**: Calprotectin-mediated zinc sequestration is a universal host defense across IBD, CRC, NEC, and other inflammatory conditions — this paper explains how pathobionts circumvent it.
4. **Mechanistic Linchpin**: Transforms the understanding of siderophores from "iron-only" to "dual metal" — yersiniabactin is a zincophore, not just a siderophore.

## Relevance to Cureva

- **Intervention insight**: EcN's probiotic mechanism is zinc-dependent. In zinc-deficient patients, EcN may be less effective (less zinc to scavenge). Conversely, in inflammation (high calprotectin), EcN's Ybt gives it a specific competitive advantage.
- **STOP consideration**: Zinc supplementation during active gut inflammation might counterproductively feed Ybt-producing pathobionts as well as probiotics — the benefit depends on which organism has superior zinc acquisition.

## Open Questions

1. Do other probiotic strains use similar dual-metal siderophores?
2. Does dietary zinc intake modulate the EcN probiotic effect via this mechanism?
3. Can Ybt-mediated zinc acquisition be therapeutically exploited — or blocked — in IBD?
4. Does calprotectin resistance via Ybt explain why Enterobacteriaceae bloom specifically during inflammation?
