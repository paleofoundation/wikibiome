# Iodine

An essential halogen required for thyroid hormone synthesis (T3 and T4). The thyroid gland is the primary iodine-concentrating organ, containing 70-80% of total body iodine. Deficiency causes goiter, hypothyroidism, and neurodevelopmental impairment; excess can trigger autoimmune thyroiditis in susceptible individuals.

## Relevance to This Wiki

- **Thyroid-metal interactions**: Iodine status modulates the toxicity of [[selenium]] (required for deiodinase enzymes that convert T4 to T3), [[iron]] (required for thyroid peroxidase), and heavy metals ([[cadmium]], [[mercury]], [[lead]]) that compete with iodine uptake via the sodium-iodide symporter (NIS).
- **Selenium-iodine axis**: Combined deficiency of both elements produces more severe thyroid dysfunction than either alone; selenoprotein-dependent deiodinases are the critical link.
- **Microbiome connections**: Thyroid hormone status affects gut motility and microbiome composition; [[dysbiosis]] may impair enterohepatic recycling of thyroid hormones.
