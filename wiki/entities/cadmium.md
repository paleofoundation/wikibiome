# Cadmium (Cd)

A toxic heavy metal included in this wiki as a comparator to [[nickel]]. While not the primary focus, the cadmium-probiotic study provides a model for how gut barrier protection could potentially apply to nickel exposure reduction.

## Toxicity Overview

- Environmental pollutant from food, drinking water, and industrial sources.
- Causes hepatic, renal, skeletal, reproductive, and cardiovascular dysfunction.
- **Intestinal tract is the first organ** susceptible to Cd contamination via food chain.
- Cd disrupts tight junctions → increases gut permeability → amplifies its own absorption.

## Probiotic Protection [[[zhai-2016-probiotics-cadmium]]]

*L. plantarum* CCFM8610 demonstrated four protective mechanisms:
1. **Intestinal Cd sequestration**: bacterial binding of Cd before absorption.
2. **Oxidative stress alleviation**: reduced ROS, MDA in intestinal cells.
3. **Tight junction protection**: restored ZO-1, ZO-2, occludin, claudin-1 expression.
4. **Immune modulation**: restored sIgA, modulated cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8, IL-10).

Key insight: **strains with both metal-binding AND antioxidative capacity** were superior to strains with only one property.

## Relevance to Nickel

- The probiotic gut barrier protection model could potentially apply to reducing dietary [[nickel]] absorption.
- Both metals disrupt tight junctions and induce oxidative stress in intestinal epithelium.
- Cd and Ni often co-occur in environmental exposures and may have synergistic toxicity.
- Cd, Ni, and lead have been reported to compete for absorption.
