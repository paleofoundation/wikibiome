# Bifidobacterium infantis

*Bifidobacterium longum* subsp. *infantis* (commonly called *B. infantis*) is the premier human milk oligosaccharide (HMO)-utilizing bacterium and the dominant species in the breastfed infant gut. It drives immune maturation, Treg induction, and pathogen exclusion in early life.

## Clinical Evidence

- Thyroidectomy: RCT showing probiotic (including B. infantis) reduces oral-gut dysbiosis post-thyroidectomy [[lin-2022-probiotics-thyroidectomy-oral-gut-dysbiosis-rct]].
- Hypothyroidism: Component of probiotic formulation tested alongside levothyroxine [[spaggiari-2017-probiotics-hypothyroid-levothyroxine-rct]].
- T2D/testosterone: Part of multi-strain probiotic in case report [[griffith-2026-lifestyle-probiotic-t2d-testosterone-case-report]].
- GDM: Depleted in gestational diabetes [[wei-2022-16s-rrna-gut-microbiota-gestational-diabetes]].
- Ovarian cancer: Microbiome context [[asangba-2023-microbiome-ovarian-cancer-diagnostic-prognostic]].
