# Key Claims

- Non-PVF and PVF STEMI patients only significantly differed in the frequencies of viruses in the *Gamma-herpesvirinae* and *Anelloviridae* families; most viruses showed higher relative frequency in non-PVF controls
- The predominant viral OTU was **Human endogenous retrovirus K113**; the most frequent genus was *Lymphocryptovirus* (belonging to *Herpesviridae* family)
- **Human herpesvirus 4 type 2** was significantly higher in non-PVF patients (p=0.042); **Macacine gammaherpesvirus 4** was significantly less frequent in non-PVF (p=0.024)
- **Torque teno virus 18** and **Torque teno virus 8** showed significantly different frequencies between PVF and non-PVF patients, suggesting potential viral biomarkers for ventricular fibrillation risk
- Circulating virome and systemic inflammation were **not associated** with increased risk of PVF development in acute STEMI -- novel strategies beyond virome needed to elucidate PVF triggers
- Inflammatory proteomic analysis (92 proteins, Olink panel) showed PVF patients had significantly higher CDCP1 and IL18-R1 compared to healthy subjects, but no differences between PVF and non-PVF STEMI groups
- 94.8% of the analyzed population had IgG-class antibodies to Epstein-Barr nuclear antigen

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Pilot case-control study from RUTI-STEMI-PVF cohort
- **Population**: 20 STEMI patients (11 PVF, 9 non-PVF); mean age 60, 85% male; blood sampled at primary PCI
- **Methods**: VirCapSeq-VERT platform (342,438 representative viral sequences); Olink Inflammation panel (92 proteins); Illumina HiSeq/NovaSeq
- **Viral taxonomy**: 51 different genome entries identified; dominant families were Herpesviridae and Anelloviridae
- **Alpha diversity**: No significant differences in richness (Chao1) or diversity (Shannon, Simpson) between PVF and non-PVF groups
