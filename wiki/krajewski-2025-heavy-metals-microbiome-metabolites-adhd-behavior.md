# Krajewski 2025 -- Heavy Metals, Microbiome Metabolites, ADHD Symptoms, and Social Behaviour in Children

## Key Claims

- Heavy metal load (As, Cd, Pb, Hg in urine), microbiome-associated metabolites, and noradrenaline/adrenaline ratio together predict 32% of social behaviour variance, 17% of ADHD symptoms, and 11% of executive function variance in typical school children
- Children with the most reduced social behaviours were the same individuals with the highest microbiome-associated metabolites, lowest noradrenaline/adrenaline ratio, OR the highest heavy metal load
- Heavy metal overload occurred 6 times more frequently in children with the most reduced social behaviours (OR = 6.04) compared to those with better social skills
- [[arsenic]] (r = -0.43), [[cadmium]] (r = -0.38), and [[lead]] (r = -0.33) each showed significant negative correlations with social behaviour

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Longitudinal pilot study (2 measurement points, 3 months apart)
- **Population**: 87 typically developing primary school children (49 boys, 38 girls), ages 8-10, Germany
- **Methods**: Urine analysis for heavy metals (As, Cd, Pb, Hg) and microbiome-associated metabolites (phenylalanine, tyrosine, L-dopa derivatives); parent/teacher questionnaires for behaviour
- **Urinary biomarkers measured**:
  - Heavy metal load: Sum of standardized z-scores for As, Cd, Pb, Hg
  - Microbiome metabolites: 3-phenylpropionic acid, p-OH-phenylacetic acid, 4-hydroxyphenylpropionic acid, 3,4-dihydroxyphenylpropionic acid
  - Noradrenaline/adrenaline ratio
- **Key correlations**:
  - Social behaviour vs heavy metal load: r = -0.43, p < 0.001
  - Social behaviour vs microbiome metabolites: r = -0.43, p < 0.001
  - ADHD symptoms vs microbiome metabolites: r = 0.41, p < 0.001
  - Executive dysfunction vs social behaviour: r = -0.45, p < 0.001
  - Microbiome metabolites vs heavy metal load: r = 0.33, p = 0.004
- **Path model results**:
  - Microbiome-associated metabolites: 10.9% shared variance with social behaviours, 16.8% with ADHD, 10.9% with executive dysfunction
  - Heavy metal load: 12.3% of reduced social behaviours, but did NOT predict executive dysfunction or ADHD symptoms directly
  - Noradrenaline/adrenaline ratio: 9.0% of social behaviours
- **Classification analysis**: Heavy metal overload was most predictive of reduced social behaviours (OR = 6.04); microbiome metabolite overload predicted ADHD symptoms (OR = 2.02) and executive dysfunction (OR = 3.02)
