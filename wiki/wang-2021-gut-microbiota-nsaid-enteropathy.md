# Wang et al. 2021 -- Gut Microbiota in NSAID Enteropathy

## Key Claims

- [[NSAIDs]] impair the intestinal barrier by inhibiting cyclooxygenase and destroying mitochondria, leading to bacterial translocation, [[LPS]] release, and TLR4/NF-kappaB-mediated inflammation
- Chronic NSAID medication changes gut bacterial composition and aggravates bile acid cytotoxicity; aspirin raises *Prevotella*, *Bacteroides*, *Ruminococcaceae*, and *Barnesiella* while abundance of *Collinsella*, *Actinobacteria*, and *Lactobacilli* was reduced
- NSAIDs competitively bind to phosphatidylcholine (PC) with bile acids, increasing formation of toxic bile salt micelles that destroy phospholipid membranes
- Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) is associated with severe NSAID-induced small intestinal damage; LHBT-positive patients had obviously more severe injury
- A cocktail of antibiotics including neomycin, polymyxin B, and bacitracin can reduce mortality and severity of intestinal ulcers caused by indomethacin

## Methods/Findings

- **Type**: Comprehensive review
- **Table 1 -- NSAID-induced microbiota changes**:
  - Indomethacin: increased *Prevotellaceae* (female), *Firmicutes* (male); decreased *Bacteroidetes* (female), *Ruminococcus* (female)
  - Celecoxib: no significant abundance alterations in obese postmenopausal women
  - Ibuprofen: increased *Propionibacteriaceae*, *Pseudomonadaceae*
  - Multiple NSAIDs: decreased *Roseburia*, *Collinsella spp.* (elderly); increased *Ruminococcus*, *Lactobacillus*
  - Aspirin: increased *Akkermansia*, decreased *Bifidobacterium*
- **Key mechanisms**:
  - NSAIDs inhibit COX -> reduced prostaglandin synthesis -> impaired mucosal defense
  - NSAIDs uncouple oxidative phosphorylation in mitochondria -> reduced ATP -> increased intestinal permeability
  - ER stress participates in NSAID-induced cell death via NLRP3 inflammasome activation
  - Enterohepatic circulation of NSAIDs with bile acids amplifies small intestinal damage
- **Therapeutic strategies**: Antibiotics, [[probiotics]] (*Bifidobacterium*, *Lactobacillus*), mucosal protective agents, [[fecal microbiota transplant]], proton pump inhibitors (adverse effects noted: increased *Streptococcus*, decreased microbial diversity)
