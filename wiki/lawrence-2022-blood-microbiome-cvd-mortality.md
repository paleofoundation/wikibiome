# Key Claims

- Bacterial DNA was identified in 372 (82%) of blood samples from the Oslo II study, encompassing 78 bacterial genera from six phyla
- Three genera were significantly associated with CVD mortality after Bonferroni correction: **Kocuria** (HR 8.50, 95% CI 4.05-17.84) and **Enhydrobacter** (HR 3.30, 95% CI 2.01-5.57) with increased risk, and **Paracoccus** (HR 0.29, 95% CI 0.15-0.57) with decreased risk
- The most prevalent genera among cases were *Streptococcus* (42.3%) and *Staphylococcus* (38.3%), but these did not reach significance in multivariate analysis
- *Kocuria* (skin and oral origin) and *Enhydrobacter* (skin origin) and low levels of *Paracoccus* (soil) were associated with CVD mortality independent of known risk factors
- Significant predictors of CVD mortality included diabetes (HR 3.10), history of myocardial infarction (HR 2.20), hypertensive medication use (HR 2.20-3.20), and excessive coffee consumption >3 cups/day (HR 1.70)

# Methods/Findings

- **Study type**: Case-cohort study nested within Oslo II longitudinal study (9.5-year follow-up from 2000)
- **Population**: 227 men who died from CVD and 178 randomly selected controls, born 1923-1932
- **Methods**: 16S rRNA gene amplification (V3-V5 regions) from stored frozen blood samples; Roche Junior Sequencer; Cox proportional hazard regression with inverse probability weighting
- **Mortality causes**: 113 ischaemic heart disease, 84 acute/subsequent MI, 83 cerebrovascular disease, 21 aortic aneurysm
- **Key covariates**: Diabetes more common in cases (17.3% vs 6.8%), antihypertensive use (53.2% vs 25.0%), history of MI (39.7% vs 10.1%)
- **Bacterial associations**: 484 different bacteria from 88 genera identified across 372 blood samples; periodontal-related genera (*Porphyromonas*, *Tannerella*, *Prevotella*) found in both groups but lower than *Bacteroides*
