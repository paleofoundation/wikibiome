# WikiBiome

> The microbiome-metallomics encyclopedia. WikiBiome is a peer-reviewed knowledge base exploring the intersection of heavy metal exposure, microbiome disruption, and chronic disease.

WikiBiome is maintained by the Paleo Foundation and represents the most comprehensive public resource on microbial metallomics — the study of how metals shape microbial communities and how those communities drive disease.

## What WikiBiome covers

WikiBiome provides authoritative, citation-backed articles on:

- **Heavy metals**: Nickel, cadmium, lead, mercury, arsenic, chromium, copper, zinc, iron, manganese, aluminum, selenium — their toxicology, environmental exposure routes, and biological mechanisms
- **Microorganisms**: Pathogens and commensals whose virulence or survival depends on metal cofactors — H. pylori, E. coli, S. aureus, Candida albicans, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and more
- **Mechanisms**: Nutritional immunity, mis-metallation, siderophore competition, epigenetic modifications, ferroptosis, oxidative stress, hypoxic signaling, metalloestrogens, interkingdom relationships
- **Host defenses**: Calprotectin, lactoferrin, hepcidin, lipocalin-2, transferrin, glutathione — how the body withholds metals from pathogens
- **Disease associations**: How metal-microbiome disruption connects to cancer, autoimmune disease, neurodegeneration, reproductive disorders, and metabolic conditions

## Key concepts

- **Microbial metallomics**: The study of how metal availability shapes microbial community structure and virulence
- **Nutritional immunity**: The host strategy of withholding essential metals from pathogens — low serum iron or zinc may represent host defense, not nutritional deficiency
- **Mis-metallation**: Toxic metals (cadmium, lead) displacing correct cofactors (zinc, iron) in enzyme active sites through molecular mimicry
- **Metal-dependent virulence**: Many pathogens require nickel (urease, hydrogenase, glyoxalase), iron (siderophores), or zinc (metalloproteases) for virulence — restricting these metals disables pathogenic mechanisms
- **Microbiome signatures**: Disease-specific patterns of enriched and depleted microbial taxa explained by their metal dependencies and ecological interactions

## Site structure

- `/` — Home page with featured articles and category navigation
- `/article/{id}` — Individual encyclopedia articles with inline citations, cross-references, and structured data
- `/category/{id}` — Browse articles by category (Heavy Metals, Microorganisms, Mechanisms, Diseases, Host Defenses)
- `/explore` — Interactive knowledge graph showing relationships between entities
- `/search?q={query}` — Full-text search across all articles

## Citation policy

All claims on WikiBiome are attributed to peer-reviewed sources with inline superscripted citations linking to full reference data (author, year, title, journal, DOI). When citing WikiBiome content, please reference the specific article URL and the underlying primary sources listed in its References section.
