Purpureocillium

A filamentous fungus in the order Hypocreales, historically studied as an insect pathogen (P. lilacinum, formerly Paecilomyces lilacinus), now emerging as a clinically significant member of the gut mycobiome linked to cognitive impairment in schizophrenia. Its enrichment correlates with depletion of neuroprotective metabolites through disruption of amino acid metabolism.

Metabolite Depletion Mechanism

The cognitive impact appears to operate through depletion of three key metabolites yuan 2025 purpureocillium amino acid metabolism cognitive schizophrenia:

  1. Ergothioneine — A potent antioxidant and cytoprotectant synthesized exclusively by fungi and certain bacteria. Purpureocillium enrichment paradoxically depletes ergothioneine, possibly by competing with ergothioneine-producing species or by enzymatic degradation.
  2. N-acetylserotonin — A melatonin precursor with independent neuroprotective and neurotrophic (BDNF-inducing) properties. Its depletion disrupts both serotonergic signaling and circadian regulation.
  3. 2-oxoarginine — An arginine metabolite involved in nitric oxide signaling and neural plasticity.

The convergence on amino acid metabolism suggests Purpureocillium reshapes the metabolic landscape of the gut in ways that specifically compromise neuroactive compound availability.
